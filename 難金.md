## Notes
**Form.** 難金 is 難 “difficult; hard to obtain” + 金 “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymology.** _Dysprosium_ derives from Greek δύσπροσιτος (_dysprositos_) “hard to get at; difficult to obtain.” The name reflects the historical difficulty of isolating the element from its ores.

**Motivation.** 難 is a direct, economical rendering of this core meaning:

- “difficult” (難) ↔ _dys-_ (“bad, hard”) + _prositos_ (“approachable, obtainable”)

This is one of the clearest one-character matches in your system.

**Type of formation.**  
A **direct semantic calque** of the Greek etymon.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, no phonetic compromise.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a metal.
3. Provides a simple, high-frequency, unambiguous symbol (難).
4. Aligns with other property/behavior-based names (硬金, 吸金), but with stronger etymological fidelity.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 镝 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ジスプロシウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 디스프로슘 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: dysprosi (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 難金 restores the original meaning.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 難 is broad (“difficult”), but this matches the Greek precisely.
- The name is unusually transparent compared to most lanthanides.

**Conclusion.**  
難金 is an exact and efficient semantic rendering of _dysprosium_, fitting your system with no compromise.
