### Basics
Looking up at the night sky, we see an entire "culture", called <ruby>еӨ©ж–Ү<rt>г„Ҡг„қг„Ӣг„Үг„Ёг„Ӣ</rt></ruby>.  The formal study of these celestial objects is <ruby>еӨ©ж–ҮеӯҰ<rt>г„Ҡг„қг„Ӣг„Үг„Ёг„Ӣг„Ҹг„ҡг„Һ</rt></ruby>.  This is a true modern science, and not the ancient practice of divination, called <ruby>еҚ жҳҹиЎ“<rt>г„җг„қг„Үг„ҷг„қг„Ҙг„ҷг„Ёг„Ҡ</rt></ruby>.  (Notice that the first two-characters are the ancient verb "to divine by looking at star signs", <ruby>еҚ жҳҹ<rt>г„җг„қг„Үг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>.).  The underlying cognitive metaphor is that we are looking up at a house, with "eaves" and "roof beams", which is why the 'universe/cosmos/space-time' is <ruby>е®Үе®ҷ<rt>г„Ёг„җг„ЁЛҮ</rt></ruby>.  A 'spaceship' is a <ruby>е®Үе®ҷиҲ№<rt>г„Ёг„җг„ЁЛҮг„җЛҮг„қг„Ӣ</rt></ruby> and an alien or astronaut is an <ruby>е®Үе®ҷдәә<rt>г„Ёг„җг„ЁЛҮг„Ёг„Ӣг„§г„Ӣ</rt></ruby>.

### Solar System
The basic object in the sky is a "star" <ruby>жҳҹ<rt>г„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>, though this also includes planets and moons.  To match the English "star", you would need to say 'fixed star', i.e. <ruby>жҒ’жҳҹ<rt>г„Ҹг„Ёг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>.  The big ones are the sun - <ruby>еӨӘйҷҪ<rt>г„Ҡг„ҡЛҷВ·Лҷг„ҡг„Ҙ</rt></ruby> - and moon - <ruby>еӨӘйҷ°<rt>г„Ҡг„ҡЛҷВ·г„§г„Ү</rt></ruby>.  Notice the connection to Yin and Yang.  Today, we distinguish planets from stars, so planet is <ruby>иЎҢжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҹг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>.  We know that our home is in the solar system <ruby>еӨӘйҷҪзі»<rt>г„Ҡг„ҡЛҷВ·Лҷг„ҡг„Ҙг„Ҹг„қЛҷ</rt></ruby>.  It is made up of
- Mercury - <ruby>ж°ҙжҳҹ<rt>г„ҷг„Ёг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>
- Venus - <ruby>йҮ‘жҳҹ<rt>г„Қг„§г„Үг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby> (ancient people were unaware that this was the same as the Morning Star <ruby>е•“жҳҺ<rt>г„Һг„қЛҷг„ҮЛҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby> and the Evening Star <ruby>еӨӘзҷҪжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҡг„ҡЛҷг„…г„ҡг„Һг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>)
- Earth - <ruby>ең°зҗғ<rt>г„үг„§Лҷг„ҚЛҷг„Ё</rt></ruby>
- Mars - <ruby>зҒ«жҳҹ<rt>г„ҸЛҮг„ҡг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>
- Jupiter - <ruby>жңЁжҳҹ<rt>г„Үг„ӣг„Һг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>
- Saturn - <ruby>еңҹжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҡг„ӣг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby> (notice that, skipping the Earth, this order is ''not'' the same as the days of the week)
- Uranus - <ruby>еӨ©зҺӢжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҡг„қг„ӢВ·ЛҮг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>
- Neptune - <ruby>жө·зҺӢжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҹг„ҡЛҷВ·ЛҮг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>
- Pluto - <ruby>еҶҘзҺӢжҳҹ<rt>г„Үг„қг„ҘВ·ЛҮг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby> (now a dwarf planet (<ruby>зҹ®иЎҢжҳҹ<rt>ЛҮг„ҡЛҷг„Ҹг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>) because of the discovery of Eris <ruby>дҰ§зҘһжҳҹ<rt>г„Ҹг„қг„Һг„ҷг„§г„Ӣг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>)
- 
Between Mars and Jupiter is the Asteroid Belt <ruby>е°ҸиЎҢжҳҹеёҜ<rt>г„ҷг„ӣг„Ҹг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙг„үг„ҡЛҷ</rt></ruby> (which is made out of asteroids <ruby>е°ҸиЎҢжҳҹ<rt>г„ҷг„ӣг„Ҹг„ҡг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>), and the region after Neptune is now called the Oort Cloud В·г„ӣг„Ңг„ңг„Ҡг„ң<ruby>йӣІ<rt>В·г„Ёг„Ӣ</rt></ruby>.  It is believed that most comets (<ruby>еҪ—жҳҹ<rt>г„Ҹг„қг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>) come from there.  If one of these or some object enteres our atmosphere, it burns up and becomes as 'shooting star' <ruby>жөҒжҳҹ<rt>г„ҢЛҷг„Ёг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>.

Our entire solar system is orbiting (<ruby>и»ҢйҒ“<rt>г„Қг„ЁВ·г„үг„ҡЛҮ</rt></ruby>) within a galaxy (<ruby>жҒ’жҳҹзі»<rt>г„Ҹг„Ёг„Ҙг„ҷг„қг„Ҙг„Ҹг„қЛҷ</rt></ruby>), specifically, the Milky Way <ruby>йҠҖжІізі»<rt>г„§г„Ӣг„Ҹг„ҡг„Ҹг„қЛҷ</rt></ruby>.  (This is a bigger word than way we see up the sky, the "silver river" <ruby>йҠҖжІі<rt>г„§г„Ӣг„Ҹг„ҡ</rt></ruby>.) Looking out, we see nebulae <ruby>жҳҹйӣІ<rt>г„ҷг„қг„ҘВ·г„Ёг„Ӣ</rt></ruby> and novae <ruby>ж–°жҳҹ<rt>г„ҷг„§г„Ӣг„ҷг„қг„Ҙ</rt></ruby>.  For millennia, humans have grouped these stars into constellations <ruby>жҳҹеә§<rt>г„ҷг„қг„Ҙг„җЛҮг„ҡ</rt></ruby>.  They are established, so if you invent your own, it's just an 'asterism' <ruby>жҳҹзҫӨ<rt>г„ҷг„қг„Ҙг„Қг„Ёг„Ӣ</rt></ruby>.
