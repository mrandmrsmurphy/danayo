## Notes
- 会意: triplication of [金](Radical%20167) ("gold, metal"), symbolizing abundant wealth. A modern/folk coinage absent from classical texts — used almost exclusively in Sinophone personal names and business names (41st most common character in Chinese newborn given names, 2018 PRC household-registration data), and separately registered as a South Korean name-use hanja.
- [SKIP-2-8-16](../lookup/SKIP/SKIP-2/SKIP-2-8-16.md) ([Stroke 24](../lookup/Stroke/Stroke%2024.md))
- No Middle Chinese reconstruction is attested for 鑫 itself. Its modern readings (Mandarin xīn, Cantonese jam1/colloquially ham1, Korean 흠, Japanese kin/kon, Vietnamese hâm) align closely with [[歆]] (Ancient [[Lookup/CC/initials/聲 曉|x]] + [[Lookup/CC/finals/韻 侵B|ɣiɪm]]), whose attested Middle Chinese is recorded here as a plausible borrowed ancestry rather than 鑫's own. The Dan'a'yo syllable itself was fixed directly from 鑫's own modern Cantonese/Korean readings — independently of 歆's already-established [ㄏㄨㄇ](../syllables/ㄏㄨㄇ.md) — since 鑫's naming pedigree stands on its own → [ㄏㄜㄇ](../syllables/ㄏㄜㄇ.md)
