## Notes
- 形声 (OC \*ɡaːl): semantic [[Radical 085|水]] ("water") + phonetic [[何 (char)|何]] (OC \*ɡaːl, \*ɡaːlʔ) — originally denoted the Yellow River specifically, later extended to rivers generally.
- [SKIP-1-3-5](../lookup/SKIP/SKIP-1/SKIP-1-3-5.md) ([Stroke 08](../lookup/Stroke/Stroke%2008.md))
- 265th most used character in Classical Chinese. Ancient [[Lookup/CC/initials/聲 匣|ɣ]] + [[Lookup/CC/finals/韻 歌|ɑ]] → [ㄏㄚ](../syllables/ㄏㄚ.md)
- [Grade 2](../lookup/Grade%202.md), [HSK Beginner](../lookup/HSK/HSK%20Beginner.md), [Jōyō - Kyōiku](../lookup/Japanese/Jōyō%20-%20Kyōiku.md), [Korean MS](../lookup/Korean/Korean%20MS.md)

Mainly used in place names, chiefly in China. Not the primary Dan'a'yo word for "river," but universally present on important character lists.
