## Notes
- [List of 指事](../lookup/List%20of%20指事.md): depicts a person kneeling (卩) beneath a cliff ([[Radical 027|厂]]), with a small stroke added to accentuate the knee — later reinterpreted as marking hardship, being "brought to one's knees" by misfortune. (An alternative, Shuowen-derived analysis instead treats it as 形声: phonetic 厂 + semantic 卪 "knot," but the vault follows the ideogram reading.)
- [SKIP-3-2-2](../lookup/SKIP/SKIP-3/SKIP-3-2-2.md) ([Stroke 04](../lookup/Stroke/Stroke%2004.md))
- 2206th most used character in Classical Chinese. Ancient [[Lookup/CC/initials/聲 影|ʔ]] + [[Lookup/CC/finals/韻 麥開|ɣɛk]] → [ㄜㄎ](../syllables/ㄜㄎ.md)
- [Grade Advanced](../lookup/Grade%20Advanced.md), [HSK No](../lookup/HSK/HSK%20No.md), [Jōyō - Kōtō](../lookup/Japanese/Jōyō%20-%20Kōtō.md), [Korean HS](../lookup/Korean/Korean%20HS.md)
- 厄 is generally absent from modern Mandarin except as a bound/phonetic element; for "calamity, disaster" use [[災害]], and for "trouble, burden" use [[災厄]] (the Dan'a'yo equivalent of Japanese 厄介事).
