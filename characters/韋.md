## Notes
- 会意 of [[Radical 136|舛]] ("footsteps, walking") and [[Radical 031|囗]] ("enclosure") — footsteps circling an enclosure; originally meant "to surround," later phonetically borrowed for its modern primary sense, "tanned leather." Note the reversed role: 囗 usually *is* the surrounding shape (e.g. 国, 囲/圍 itself), but here it's the object being surrounded — 舛's footprints do the surrounding, walking the perimeter of the settlement 囗 depicts.
