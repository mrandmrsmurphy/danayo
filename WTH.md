### Definitely not transitive

1. 剰余, 
	*Ancient meanings:* 剰 (MC dʑ+ɨŋ, `mc_id: 0` — unattested in the CC corpus) — "surplus, remainder," phonetic tied to 乗/乘 ("to ride/multiply"). 余 (MC j+ɨʌ) — this character has a tangled history: its own graphemic note says the *pictograph* 余 originally meant "thatched house" (now written 舍), later phonetically borrowed for the pronoun "I; me" (OC *la) — but the modern "surplus" character here is actually simplified 餘 (a completely different original character, listed as 余's own alias), which collided with the old 余 pictograph in simplification.
	*Candidate words:* 余(=餘) is extremely productive already: 余弦 (cosine), 余接 (cotangent), 余割 (cosecant), 余波 (aftermath/fallout), 余震 (aftershock) — all real vault words, all cleanly "residual/remaining-after" in sense. 剰 has zero other compounds.
	*Should either get the word:* 余 doesn't need 剰余 at all — prime solo candidate (same shape as 自/体/机), or could anchor on 余波 as a general "remainder/aftermath" sense if solo feels too disruptive. 剰 has nowhere else to go.
	*Revise the meaning?* No collapse — 余's productivity is real and vault-attested, not just outside knowledge. Definitely-not-transitive stands, and 余 in particular looks over-qualified to still be sharing a compound with 剰.
2. 収集, 
	*Ancient meanings:* 収 (MC ɕ+ɨu, phonetic 丩) — "to gather in, collect, take in," semantic 又 ("hand"). 集 (MC dz+iɪp) — "to collect, assemble," graphemically birds (隹) gathering on a tree (木).
	*Candidate words:* Both massively productive already. 収: 収入 (income), 収穫 (harvest), 収拾 (tidy up/bring under control), 収蔵 (store/collection) — 4 real compounds plus 収集. 集: 集団 (group/collective), 蒐集 (collect/gather — near-synonym doubling with 集 itself), 募集 (recruit/recruitment), 召集 (convene/summon), 採集 (collect, as in specimens), 編集 (edit/compile), 集合 (assemble/set, math term) — 7+ real compounds.
	*Should either get the word:* No — both are prime solo candidates, easily as productive as 自/体/机/机/余/保/護. Neither needs 収集 specifically.
	*Revise the meaning?* No — "collect/gather" is alive and well for both, richly attested across the vault's own data. Definitely not transitive; if anything this is one of the clearest "should just go solo" cases in the whole list.
3. 呪詛 (呪 independently huge: 呪文/呪術), 
	*Ancient meanings:* 呪 (MC tɕ+ɨu, phonetic 祝 "to pray/invoke") — "spell, incantation," `japanese_native` まじな(う) ("to cast a spell/charm" — magic-adjacent, not inherently malicious), `korean_native` 빌 ("to pray/beseech"). 詛 (MC tʃ+ɨʌ, phonetic 且) — "curse," `japanese_native` のろ(う) ("to curse" — specifically malicious ill-wishing), `korean_native` 저주할 ("to curse," matches directly).
	*Candidate words:* None in the vault — neither character appears in any other compound here.
	*Should either get the word:* No redirect available in this vault's current data.
	*Revise the meaning?* This is the same shape as 抵抗: the original vote's "呪 independently huge: 呪文/呪術" is true in the real world (both are extremely common, well-attested Japanese/Chinese words — 呪文 "incantation," 呪術 "sorcery," 呪縛 "spellbound," 呪い "a curse") but **neither exists in this vault yet**. By the vault's own data alone, this would look collapsible (nothing else attests either character) — but the native-reading split (まじなう "cast a spell/pray" vs. のろう "curse maliciously") is real and matches a genuine registers distinction that's very likely to resurface the moment 呪文 or 呪術 get created. Flagging exactly like 抵抗: transitive by current vault data, but fragile — recommend holding off or noting the risk explicitly if tagged now.
4. 呻吟 (吟 independently means "chant/recite poetry," unrelated to "moan"), 
	*Ancient meanings:* 呻 (MC ɕ+iɪn, phonetic 申) — "to groan, moan," `korean_native` 읊조릴 ("to recite/chant in a low murmuring voice"). 吟 (MC ŋ+ɣiɪm, phonetic 今) — "to hum, moan," `korean_native` 읊을 (same root 읊- "to recite/chant"). 	*Should either get the word:* No redirect available in vault data.
	*Revise the meaning?* Same tension as 呪詛 and 抵抗: vault-internal evidence (zero other compounds, shared 읊- root in Korean) leans toward collapse. But real-world knowledge says 吟's true, independent life — chanting/reciting poetry (吟遊詩人 "bard," 詩吟 "poetry recitation," 独吟 "solo recitation") — is a distinct, still-living register from "moan in pain," just not vault-documented yet. Recommend treating like 呪詛/抵抗: plausible collapse today, flagged as fragile against future word creation.

	*Should either get the word:* 楽 doesn't need 娯楽 at all — one of the strongest solo candidates on this whole list (24 total word-file hits, though many are surely false positives from "楽" as a common substring — worth a tighter check, but even the confirmed real compounds are plenty). 娯 has nowhere else to go.
	*Revise the meaning?* No collapse — 楽's productivity and semantic range (music AND joy AND ease/comfort) is real, rich, and vault-attested. Definitely not transitive; 楽 is significantly bigger than "entertainment" alone.

Not a collapse; a real split with 宗 turning out bigger than expected, same shape as 机/機.
