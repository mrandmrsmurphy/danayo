## <ruby>概要<rt>ㄍㄚㄧ·˙ㄛㄨ</rt></ruby>
<ruby>単亜語<rt>ㄉㄚㄋㄚㄧㄛ</rt></ruby> sounds somewhat like older Korean.  It is written in <ruby>新字体<rt>ㄙㄧㄋㄐㄧㄊㄚㄧ</rt></ruby>, with <ruby>注音符号<rt>ㄐㄨˇㄇㄅㄨㄏㄚㄨ</rt></ruby> above as the phonological alphabet.  Grammar is somewhere between Classical topic-comment, and modern <ruby>日本語<rt>ㄋㄧㄊㄅㄛㄋ·˙ㄛ</rt></ruby>/Korean forced into SVO word-order.

In **<ruby>単亜語<rt>ㄉㄚㄋㄚㄧㄛ</rt></ruby>**, expression is shaped by two organizing principles: **three modes of style** and **three meta-parts of speech**.

### Modes
1. **<ruby>古風体<rt>ㄍㄛㄆㄨㄥㄊㄝㄧ</rt></ruby>** — terse, topic–comment, copulas often omitted, written chiefly in <ruby>漢字<rt>ㄏㄚㄋㄐㄧ</rt></ruby>.
2. **<ruby>書面体<rt>ㄙ˙ㄛㄇ˙ㄝㄋㄊㄝㄧ</rt></ruby>** — explicit and conservative, requires copulas and nominalizers where needed, always written with <ruby>漢字<rt>ㄏㄚㄋㄐㄧ</rt></ruby> plus ruby.    
3. **<ruby>網語体<rt>ㄇㄚㄥ·˙ㄛㄊㄝㄧ</rt></ruby>** — clipped and context-driven, often omitting particles, written primarily in <ruby>注音符号<rt>ㄐㄨ·ㄨㄇㄅㄨㄏㄚㄨ</rt></ruby>.

## Meta-parts of speech
1. **<ruby>実詞<rt>ㄙㄧㄊㄙㄚ</rt></ruby>** — nouns, verbs, adjectives; any of these may serve as topic or comment.
2. **<ruby>助詞<rt>ㄐㄛㄙㄚ</rt></ruby>** — case markers, connectives, pronouns, aspect/degree markers, sentence-final particles.
3. **<ruby>特詞<rt>ㄉㄨㄎㄙㄚ</rt></ruby>** — numerals, classifiers, determiners, interjections, ideophones; independent but never predicative.
