武明帥 (Dan'a'yo handle) is the creator and sole maintainer of *単亜語* (Dan'a'yo), a constructed zonal auxiliary language for the East Asian cultural sphere (Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese), built out as an Obsidian vault with a deeply systematic data model (characters/words/chengyu/syllables, cross-linked, all derived from a coherent internal phonology traced back to Middle Chinese readings).

**Background and goal:** a hobbyist conlanger (not a professional linguist) — Dan'a'yo is a serious long-running passion project, years deep at this point (hence the ~3,300-character database). The end goal is an *actually usable* auxiliary language: something real people could learn and speak for cross-cultural communication across the Mandarin/Cantonese/Japanese/Korean/Vietnamese sphere — not just a documented artifact. Started as a collaboration; the other person eventually moved on to other projects (no falling-out), and 武明帥 has carried it solo since.

**Personal background — why this project, specifically, from him:** white American by birth, but moved to Korea at two months old and grew up multilingual — elementary school in Japan, high school in Korea, with a father fluent in Japanese, Korean, Chinese, and more. He's also taught many Chinese students as a teacher over the years. Zonal CJKV thinking isn't an academic exercise he adopted — it's *how he already thinks*, from lived experience straddling these languages and cultures since childhood. Dan'a'yo is, in his words, "the overlap of the Venn diagrams of CJKV" — something he's seen in his mind for as long as he can remember, and is now building into existence.

**Interests visible in the work:** historical Chinese phonology (Middle Chinese initials/finals, Old Chinese reconstructions), comparative CJKV vocabulary and semantic drift across the five languages, classical/literary registers, and etymology — he wants Notes sections to read as genuine encyclopedia entries, not glosses (see [[feedback_notes_style]]).
