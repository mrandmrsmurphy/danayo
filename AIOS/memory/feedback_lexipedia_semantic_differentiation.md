Lexipedia pages (`lexipedia/*.md`) are not just a static semantic-field index of existing words — they are the site where Dan'a'yo's own semantic ranges get worked out. Word page Notes ([[feedback_notes_style]]) survey the *existing* CJKV landscape: etymology, historical usage, cross-linguistic drift within the source languages. Lexipedia pages do the complementary work: they are where D, as a functioning zonal IAL, has to decide what its *own* words cover, independent of any single source language's boundaries.

**Why:** D lacks some native-vocabulary distinctions that exist in individual source languages, and simultaneously needs to draw its own lines between near-synonymous D words that no single source language distinguishes the same way. That differentiation can't be decided in the abstract, word-by-word — it emerges from seeing which D words get used for which entries as adjacent lexipedia pages (or adjacent entries within one page) get filled in. A semantic boundary only becomes legible when two D words are placed side by side against a shared conceptual field. As a regional IAL, D should never stray far from its sources, but some divergence is inevitable and should be allowed to happen organically rather than be designed up front.

**How to apply:**
- When filling in a lexipedia entry, it's fine — expected — for the chosen D word to not map 1:1 onto the English gloss or any one source language's word. Feel free to note when a single D word is doing double duty across entries that a source language keeps separate, or conversely when two D words split a range one source language treats as a single word.
- Don't force premature disambiguation. Let the pattern emerge as more of a semantic field gets filled in; only worth acting on once a real contrast is visible (e.g. two D words consistently landing on opposite ends of a lexipedia field).
- Once a D-specific semantic boundary is legible from lexipedia usage, backfill it into the relevant word page(s): add a short D-centric definition — distinct from the existing cross-linguistic-survey Notes — stating what the word covers *in Dan'a'yo specifically* and how it contrasts with its near-synonym(s). Cross-link near-synonyms to each other, similar to how homophones are cross-linked ([[feedback_homophone_warning]]).
- This is a slow, organic process, not a task to force through the whole lexipedia in one pass. Let it happen as lexipedia coverage grows naturally, not as a deliberate disambiguation sweep.
