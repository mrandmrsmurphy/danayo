## Notes
- [攻 (char)](../characters/攻%20(char).md) "attack" + [防](../characters/防.md) "defend, prevent"

攻防 pairs two antonyms into a single compound denoting the complete cycle of armed contest: offense (攻) and defense (防) as a unified dynamic. The compound is common in military writing, sports commentary, and strategic analysis across all CJKV languages. In Chinese (gōngfáng) and Japanese (こうぼう, kōbō), it functions as both noun and verb — "the offense and defense," "to attack and defend," "to contest." In Japanese, 攻防 is particularly alive in sports reporting (攻防戦, "battle of offense and defense") and in go and shogi commentary.

The compound captures a specifically East Asian analytical framing of conflict as a reciprocal exchange rather than a one-sided application of force — the same logic underlying concepts like 兵法 (military strategy) and 太極 (the interplay of opposing forces). In Korean (공방), the word is used in both military and figurative contexts; 공방전 (攻防戰) denotes a prolonged back-and-forth exchange of argument or action.
