## Notes
- [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "when" + [候](../characters/候.md) "climate; expectation" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (時's own points to bare `時`, 候's own to `気候`); 時候 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/時 (char).md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/候.md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **Corrected gloss and readings**: `english` previously listed only "season and climate" — but in Mandarin/Cantonese, 时候/si4 hau6 is one of the most common HSK-1 words, meaning "time, moment, when" (什么时候 "when," 有时候 "sometimes"), essentially a grammaticalized time-word, not "season/climate." Japanese 時候 jikō, by contrast, genuinely does mean "season; the time of year" in a formal register (時候の挨拶, seasonal greeting phrases used to open formal letters) — the old gloss appears to have described only the Japanese sense while presenting it as the word's primary meaning. Rewrote to document both senses. Also fixed `mandarin` (dropped a spurious redundant "shíhòu" variant) and `cantonese` (`si2 hou4`→`si4 hau6`, matching `characters/時 (char).md`'s own `si4` and `characters/候.md`'s own `hau6`).
