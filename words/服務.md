## Notes
- [服](../characters/服.md) "clothes, wear, dress" + [務](../characters/務.md) "serve, do one's duty" — `characters/務.md`'s own `stand_in` points here, meaning 務 does not stand independently outside this compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/服.md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/務.md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- A clean, directly parallel compound across the sphere in meaning — Mandarin fúwù, Cantonese fuk6 mou6, Korean 복무 (also specifically "term of duty/service," e.g. military service), and Vietnamese phục vụ all mean "to serve." Japanese ふくむ (a direct on'yomi concatenation of FUKU + MU) is a coincidental homophone with the entirely unrelated native verb 含む ("to contain, include") — the recurring Sino-Xenic collision-with-an-unrelated-word pattern seen elsewhere in this vault, not an error.
