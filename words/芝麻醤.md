## Notes
- [芝](../characters/芝.md) "sesame" + [麻](../characters/麻.md) "hemp, sesame" + [醤](../characters/醤.md) "paste, sauce"

芝麻醤 (or its traditional form 芝麻醬) is sesame paste — a thick, oily condiment made from ground toasted sesame seeds, used across Chinese, Japanese, and broader East Asian cuisines. The first two characters, 芝麻 (zhīma), form the standard Mandarin term for sesame; 醤/醬 denotes a fermented or ground paste.

Sesame paste is a key ingredient in dishes including 涼麵 (cold noodles), 麻醬拌麵, hot pot dipping sauces, and various cold vegetable dishes. It differs from Middle Eastern tahini in typically being made from toasted (rather than raw) seeds, giving it a darker color and stronger roasted flavor. A shorter variant, 麻醤 (májiàng), drops the 芝 and is common in northern Chinese cooking. In Japanese contexts (しまじょう, shima-jō), the term appears in Chinese-cuisine writing; Japanese cooking more commonly uses 練りごま (neri goma, "kneaded sesame") or ごまだれ (sesame sauce).
