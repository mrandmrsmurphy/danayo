## Notes
- [医](../characters/医.md) "doctor, medicine" + [院](../characters/院.md) "institution, courtyard, public building" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (医's is 医生, 院's is 院落). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/医.md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/院.md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- **Genuine cross-linguistic sense-size difference, not an error**: Mandarin yīyuàn/Cantonese ji1 jyun6-2 are the ordinary, general-purpose words for "hospital" (any size). Japanese いいん and Korean 의원, however, specifically denote a small private clinic or practice — legally distinguished from 病院/병원 ("hospital," 20+ inpatient beds) — verified via search. Vietnamese y viện is a real, if less common, Sino-Vietnamese word for "hospital" (the everyday word is bệnh viện, native-adjacent 病院-calque), confirmed on Vietnamese Wiktionary rather than fabricated.
