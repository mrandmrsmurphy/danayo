## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; object; material result"

其物 is the medial correlative in the object column: it picks out a thing in the addressee's sphere or in the common ground between speaker and listener — "that thing (of yours), that object we both know about." As with 其事 in the action column, the character 其 carries both a deictic function (medial demonstrative) and a possessive/genitive one in classical Chinese; in Dan'a'yo only the deictic reading is operative. 其物 accordingly means "that thing there (on your side or in our shared context)."

In Mandarin, 其物 (qí wù) appears in classical and formal prose meaning "the thing in question" or "that object" — typically in contexts of property, ownership, or reference to a previously mentioned item. Japanese そのもの (sono mono, lit. "that [adnominal] thing") is the natural spoken equivalent, with の nominalization distinguishing it from the bare adnominal その; Korean 그것 (geugeot) is the standard medial object pronoun. Vietnamese *vật đó* or *cái đó* (that thing) covers the same function.

A distinctive feature of Dan'a'yo 物 is the preservation of the Middle Chinese final stop *-t: the MC form of 物 was \*mɨt or \*mjɨt (物韻), giving Dan'a'yo romanisation *mud* (with lenis final, written ㄊ in 注音 per Dan'a'yo convention). The Sino-Korean reading is 물 (mul, with sonorant final), which shows the standard Korean development of MC final stops to /l/; Dan'a'yo preserves the stop, yielding 묻 rather than 물 — hence kwin: false across the entire 物 row.
