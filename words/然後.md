## Notes
- [然 (char)](../characters/然%20(char).md) "so; thus" + [後 (char)](../characters/後%20(char).md) "after; behind"

然後 joins 然 — a demonstrative particle meaning "so; it being thus" — with 後 ("afterward") to create a conjunction of contingent sequence: "it being so, afterward" → "then; after that." The construction appears throughout the classical canon with this causally loaded sense. Confucius uses it in the Analects to express prerequisite logic (先正其名，然後可行 — "First rectify names; only then can action proceed"), and Mencius and the Zhuangzi use it in the same way. Unlike the neutral temporal marker 後 alone, 然後 signals that the second event is contingent on the first: the prior condition must obtain before the next step follows.

Modern Mandarin preserves 然後 (ránhòu) as one of its most common connectives, used equally in formal prose and casual speech. Japanese has largely abandoned the Sino-Japanese compound in favour of それから (sore kara, "from that") or そして (soshite) for sequence and その後 (sono go) for temporal reference; the cognate 然後 (zengō) survives only in literary or archaic contexts. Korean 연후 (yeon-hu) retains the classical form but restricts it to formal, legal, or literary registers — everyday Korean strongly prefers 그 다음에 or 그러고 나서. Vietnamese nhiên hậu is similarly archaic, displaced in spoken usage by rồi, sau đó, or xong.

In Dan'a'yo, 然後 functions as the standard coordinating connective for ordered sequences, carrying over its classical role. The kwin=false marking reflects systematic phonological divergence: Dan'a'yo 년훗 (nyen + huo, concatenated from the sinographic readings) differs from Korean 연후 in vowel quality, final consonant, and syllable structure — the two forms have evolved independently, even though they derive from the same Middle Chinese source.
