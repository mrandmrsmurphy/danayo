## Notes
- [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small" + [学](../characters/学.md) "learn" — literally "lesser learning."

1. In the traditional Confucian education system, 小学 names the elementary stage of schooling — basic literacy, etiquette, and moral grounding for children — contrasted with [[太学]] (the Imperial Academy), the highest tier reserved for advanced classical study and civil-service preparation. This is the sense intended in [[歴史綱要]]'s vocabulary list.
2. 小学 is also the traditional name of a specific scholarly discipline in Chinese learning: the philological study of characters, their forms, sounds, and meanings, comprising what later scholarship divides into 文字學 (graphology), 音韻學 (phonology), and 訓詁學 (exegesis). It was called "lesser" learning not because it was considered unimportant, but because it was foundational and preparatory to the "greater learning" of classical interpretation and statecraft.
3. 小學 is also the title of a specific book: a Confucian primer compiled by Zhu Xi (朱熹) at his disciple Liu Qingzhi's direction, gathering essential moral precepts and exemplary conduct for children. It became required reading throughout the Sinosphere's Confucian school systems — in Korea it was central to Joseon-period education at every level, from village schools (書堂) up through provincial academies, and the Korean reading 소학 refers to this text as readily as to elementary education in general.
4. In modern usage, 小学 is simply "elementary school." This is in fact the ordinary Mandarin word for the institution — Mandarin says 小学 on its own, without needing a further "school" suffix, unlike Japanese, which normally appends 校 to form [[小学校]].
