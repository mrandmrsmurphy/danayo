## Notes
繭/견/kén stay close across the family as the ordinary word for a silkworm's cocoon, but Japanese marks a real distinction the others don't: the native kun'yomi まゆ (mayu) is the everyday word for "cocoon" itself, while the on'yomi ケン (ken, this character's Sino-Japanese reading) shows up mainly in compounds like 繭糸 (kenshi, "cocoon thread/raw silk") rather than standing alone. Vietnamese kén, despite ultimately deriving from the same Sino root as the more literal Hán Việt reading kiển, has fully naturalized into ordinary folk vocabulary — used the way a native word would be, including the extended sense "to be picky/choosy" (kén chọn) that has nothing to do with cocoons.
