## Notes
- [帝](../characters/帝.md) "emperor" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank, throne"

帝位 names the imperial throne specifically — the "position of the 帝," as distinct from the more general [[王位]] ("throne" of a king). 帝 is a 象形 pictograph of a flower's calyx, the structure that joins blossom to stem; the image of the calyx as both the apex of the stalk and the support of the flower naturally extended to "supreme arbiter, the one above and supporting all" — hence "emperor, God" (上帝). The character's other compound, [[帝王]] ("emperor," cross-linked from [[帝]]'s Words section), names the *person*, while 帝位 names the *office* or *seat* he occupies.

The distinction between 帝 (emperor, the title above "king") and 王 (king) reflects the historical escalation of Chinese royal titulature: after Qin Shi Huang unified China in 221 BC, he judged the old title 王 insufficient and combined 三皇 (Three Sovereigns) and 五帝 (Five Emperors) into the new title 皇帝 ("emperor"), establishing 帝 as the title for a ruler over multiple kingdoms — exactly the hierarchy reflected in 帝位 standing one tier above 王位 in this list.
