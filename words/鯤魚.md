## Notes
- [鯤](../characters/鯤.md) "mythical fish" + [魚 (char)](../characters/魚%20(char).md) "fish" — stand-in for [[鯤]], which cannot appear independently

鯤 (kūn) is the name of the colossal fish described in the opening passage of the Zhuangzi (莊子·逍遙遊). The text describes it as so vast that its back spans thousands of li; when it transforms (化) into the giant bird Peng (鵬), it fills the sky. The name 鯤 originally meant "fish roe" or "small fry" in earlier texts, making the Zhuangzi's usage a deliberate paradox: the tiniest thing becomes the most enormous.

鯤魚 pairs the mythical creature name 鯤 with the plain classifier 魚 ("fish") to produce the compound "the kūn fish", making 鯤 usable in discourse without relying on the bare character. In classical citations and literary allusion 鯤 alone is conventional, but 鯤魚 appears where a fuller nominal form is needed. The 鯤–鵬 transformation is one of the most celebrated images in Daoist literature, symbolising the transcendence of ordinary limitations and the relativity of scale.
