## Notes
- [即 (char)](../characters/即%20(char).md) "to approach, namely" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank"

即位 is the standard term for a monarch's accession — the moment of "approaching the [throne] position." 即 is a 会意 compound of 皀 (a round-mouthed food vessel) and 卩 (a kneeling person), originally depicting someone drawing near to eat; from "to approach, to come near" it generalized to the grammatical sense of "namely, that is to say" that dominates 即's modern usage (as in 即時, 即興). 即位's literal sense — to draw near to, and take up, the ruler's seat — preserves the character's older, more concrete meaning, a trace of which the [[即 (char)|character page]] notes survives in Western Zhou inscriptions where 立 was sometimes written for 位 in exactly this phrase.

This is the first of ten words in this batch built on [[位]] ("position, rank, throne") — itself a 会意 of 人 ("person") + 立 ("stand"), the image of a person standing in their proper place. Across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean, 位 contributes a near-identical reading (wèi/wai6/i/위) to each compound, while the first character supplies the specific kind of "position" — here, the ruler's.
