## Notes
- [宿](../characters/宿.md) "lodging, long-standing" + [敵](../characters/敵.md) "enemy, foe"

宿敵 denotes an enemy of long standing — a rival whose antagonism is rooted in history rather than recent events. The character 宿 contributes the sense of "old" or "of long duration" (as in 宿命, 宿題), framing the enmity as something that has been carried for a long time, perhaps across careers, institutions, or generations. This distinguishes 宿敵 from 敵人 (a current opponent) or 世仇 (a feud inherited across bloodlines): 宿敵 is specifically the opponent you have faced repeatedly over time, whose rivalry has become definitional.

The word is fully alive in modern Japanese (しゅくてき, shukuteki), where it is the standard term for "archenemy" or "nemesis" in both historical and contemporary contexts — sports rivalries, corporate competition, and narrative antagonism alike. In Chinese, it appears in literary and historical registers; modern spoken Mandarin often prefers 死對頭 or 老對手 for the same sense. In Korean (수적), it is encountered chiefly in literary or formal contexts.
