## Notes
- [目 (char)](../characters/目%20(char).md) "eye" + [的](../characters/的.md) "target, objective, -ish" — literally "the eye's target"; stand-in for [[的]], which cannot appear independently.

目的/목적/もくてき/mục đích all stay consistent as the ordinary, everyday word for "purpose, goal, objective" across the family, with no notable drift. One fact worth flagging about 的 itself: in this compound it's read dì (fourth tone, "target"), a completely different reading from its far more common use as de (neutral tone) — the single most frequent possessive/attributive particle in modern Mandarin (我的, "my/mine"; 红色的, "the red one"). The two readings share a character but not a function: dì is the older, semantically "full" reading meaning "target, mark, bullseye" (目的, 標的), while de is a grammaticalized particle with no lexical meaning of its own.
