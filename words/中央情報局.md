## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "central" + [央](../characters/央.md) "middle" + [情](../characters/情.md) "intelligence, information" + [報 (char)](../characters/報%20(char).md) "report" + [局 (char)](../characters/局%20(char).md) "bureau, agency" — Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)

中央情報局 is the standard Chinese/Japanese translation of the CIA. The five-character compound works transparently: 中央 (central — the two characters together emphasize the center from different angles, 中 naming the middle position and 央 the point of convergence) + 情報 (intelligence — information gathered about others' intentions and activities, from 情 "feeling, condition" + 報 "report") + 局 (bureau).

情報 is itself a compound worth noting: it names not raw data but actionable information about the state of affairs — originally used in military contexts for battlefield intelligence, later generalized to any information gathered for strategic or analytical purposes. 情報局 alone means intelligence agency; 中央 specifies that this is the central, coordinating intelligence body rather than a departmental or field unit.

The CIA was established in 1947 under the National Security Act, succeeding the wartime OSS (Office of Strategic Services). Its Chinese translation 中央情报局 uses the simplified form of 報 (报); the Japanese 中央情報局 uses the full form. Korean 중앙정보국 (KCIA, Korea Central Intelligence Agency, 1961–1981) borrowed both the name and the organizational model, making the compound historically significant on the Korean peninsula as well.
