## Notes
- [艱](../characters/艱.md) "difficult" + [難](../characters/難.md) "difficult" — stand-in for [[艱]], which cannot appear independently

艱難 is a near-synonymous compound in which both characters mean "difficult" or "hard": 艱 (hardship, adversity) and 難 (difficulty, obstacle). The doubling intensifies the sense, producing a word that conveys gruelling, sustained difficulty rather than a momentary obstacle — the kind of hardship endured over time, such as poverty, war, illness, or the toil of a long journey.

The compound is classical in register and appears throughout Chinese historical and literary writing to describe the ordeal of a dynasty's founding, the sufferings of the people during wartime, or the hardship of the scholar's path. In Japanese かんなん (艱難) retains this elevated tone and is common in proverbs — most famously 艱難汝を玉にす (kannan nanji wo tama ni su, "adversity makes a jewel of you"), the Japanese rendering of the European adage that hardship builds character. In Korean 간난 is similarly literary and often appears in the compound 간난신고 (艱難辛苦, "hardship and bitterness"), a four-character idiom for extreme suffering.
