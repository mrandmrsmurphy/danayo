## Notes
- [諸 (char)](../characters/諸%20(char).md) "various, all, sundry" + [語](../characters/語.md) "language" — the languages, collectively, of some family or region: "the X languages."

諸 is a classical/literary plural-collective prefix attested productively across the vocabulary's languages of origin — Mandarin keeps it alive in everyday set phrases like 诸位 ("everyone present") and 诸如 ("such as"), and it appears bound into many academic X諸語 formations (印欧諸語 "the Indo-European languages," ロマンス諸語 "the Romance languages" in Japanese scholarly writing) of exactly the kind this term names. The grammar book itself uses 諸語 in just this sense, in the phrase 東亜諸語 ("the languages of East Asia"). One caution: in real Korean, 諸語 and the everyday word [[制御]] (not yet created, "control") happen to share the identical reading 제어 — an accident of the two languages' own phonology, unrelated to Dan'a'yo's own spelling of either word, which keeps them entirely distinct (자요 vs. a hypothetical 제어 reading for 制御). Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
