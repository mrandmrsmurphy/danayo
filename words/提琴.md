## Notes
- [提](../characters/提.md) "to hold, raise" + [琴 (char)](../characters/琴%20(char).md) "zither, stringed instrument"

提琴 names a "held/raised stringed instrument" — historically a term for traditional Chinese bowed instruments like the 二胡-family 提琴 of southern China, and in modern Mandarin extended to the entire Western violin family (小提琴 "violin," 大提琴 "cello," 低音提琴 "double bass"). 琴, a 象形 character depicting an ancient zither, anchors a large family of names for stringed instruments across East Asia, and is itself the name of the venerable seven-stringed 古琴 (guqin) — the instrument whose neglect in favor of crude pleasures gives [[焚琴煮鶴]] (cross-linked from [[琴 (char)]]'s Chengyu section) its force as an image of philistinism.

Unlike 古琴, which is held horizontally on a table or the lap, the 提琴 family is literally "held up" (提) against the body or shoulder while played — the compound's name directly describes the playing posture, distinguishing it from older zither-type instruments. 提琴 thus sits alongside [[提供]], [[提出]], and [[提督]] as another compound built on 提's core image of "raising, holding up," here applied quite literally to an instrument's posture rather than metaphorically to offering or directing.
