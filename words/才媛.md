## Notes
- [才](../characters/才.md) "talent; ability" + [媛](../characters/媛.md) "beautiful woman; accomplished woman"

才媛 describes a woman whose distinction rests on intellectual and artistic gifts rather than birth or rank alone. The compound sits in a formal, literary register — the kind of phrase found in classical essays, biographies of accomplished women, and formal modern usage when the intent is respectful rather than merely decorative.

In Chinese literary tradition, the 才媛 appears alongside the 才子 ("talented man") as the female counterpart to the scholar-poet ideal. Women like the Song dynasty poet Li Qingzhao (李清照) and the Tang poetess Yu Xuanji (魚玄機) are canonical examples. The compound retains this classical resonance in modern Chinese, Japanese さいえん (regular on-yomi: SAI + EN), and Korean 재원.
