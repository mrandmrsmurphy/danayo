## Notes
- [体](../characters/体.md) "group, body, form" + [系](../characters/系.md) "system" — a synonym-doubling of two "structure/framework" roots into one word. This word is the viable form of [体](../characters/体.md): 体 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 体系 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 体's sense the abstract structural/organizational one — as seen across its other compounds 体制 ("governing structure"), 全体 ("the whole"), and the 液体/気体/固体 family ("liquid/gas/solid," 体 as a general suffix for "physical state/form") — in contrast to [[身体]] (the concrete personal body that [[身]] stands in for).

Mandarin tǐxì and Cantonese tai2hai6 are both standard, everyday words for "system" in the abstract sense — 教育体系 (education system), 生態体系 (ecosystem). Japanese taikei and Korean 체계 (chegye) both use the same compound just as productively — 体系的 (systematic), 分類体系 (classification system). Unlike 身体, which names a concrete physical object, 体系 names an abstract organizing structure — the same "form" sense 体 carries in 体制 and 全体, just generalized to any structured whole rather than a governing or bodily one specifically.
