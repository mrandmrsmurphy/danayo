## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "to receive, accept" + [賞 (char)](../characters/賞%20(char).md) "reward, prize"

受賞 means "to win a prize, to be awarded" — receiving (受) a prize or honor (賞) such as a literary award, a medal, or an academic prize (ノーベル賞を受賞する, "to be awarded the Nobel Prize"). 受, a 形声 character built around the phonetic 舟 ("boat" — the image of an object passed from hand to hand across a boat), is the basic verb "to receive, to accept, to undergo," and is the first word in a five-word run in this batch (受賞, [[受難]], [[受理]], [[受胎]], [[受託]]) — all sharing 受's core sense of "receiving" something, whether an honor, a hardship, a case, a pregnancy, or a commission.

賞, a 形声 character (phonetic 尚 over semantic 貝 "shell/money" — money being the original form of many rewards), means "reward, prize, to bestow a reward" — its only frontmatter entry beyond this is its stand_in role for the character itself, making 受賞 likely 賞's first cross-linked compound in the vault. 受賞 forms a natural pair with 賞's other implied compound 授賞 ("to bestow a prize") — 受賞 from the recipient's perspective, 授賞 from the awarding body's, the two verbs describing the same ceremony from opposite ends.
