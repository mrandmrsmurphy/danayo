## Notes
- [滑 (char)](../characters/滑%20(char).md) "slippery" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

**Real bug corrected**: `korean` was `골` (gol), which is a genuinely different reading of 滑 belonging to its other, unrelated sense — "chaotic, cunning" (滑稽, 골계, "comical") — rather than this word's "slippery" sense; corrected to `활` (hwal), matching `characters/滑 (char).md`'s own stored value exactly. Also aligned `注音` (`ㄏㄨㄚㄊ`→`ㄏ⺢ㄊ`) and `vietnamese` (`gột`→`hoạt`) to match the character's own authoritative stored fields, since word and character are identical here. `kwin` corrected `false`→`true` to match.

Mandarin huá, Cantonese waat6, Korean 활 (hwal), and Vietnamese hoạt all use this character productively and independently for "slippery, smooth" (滑走路/활주로, "runway"; linh hoạt, "flexible, agile"). Japanese uses the native kun'yomi verb 滑る (すべる) for the everyday adjective/verb rather than a bare on'yomi citation form.
