## Notes
- [刺 (char)](../characters/刺%20(char).md) "stab; pierce" + [客](../characters/客.md) "guest; traveller"

**刺客** is the classical East Asian word for an assassin — literally a "stabbing traveller" or "piercing guest", the image being of a wandering agent who arrives, kills, and departs. The compound is ancient: 客 in this context carries the older sense of an itinerant person operating outside ordinary society, a stranger whose business is deadly.

The word is inseparable from its literary heritage. The 刺客列傳 ("Biographies of Assassins"), a celebrated chapter of Sima Qian's *Shiji* (史記, c. 94 BC), chronicles five famous assassins of the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods. Their stories — marked by loyalty, sacrifice, and often deliberate self-destruction after completing a mission — gave 刺客 its enduring romantic and tragic valence, distinct from the more neutral or criminal connotations of Western "assassin" or "hitman".
