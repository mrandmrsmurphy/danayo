## Notes
- [倦](../characters/倦.md) "weary, tired of" + [嫌](../characters/嫌.md) "detest, hate" — stand-in for [[倦]], which cannot appear independently.

嫌 here stands in for its alias 厭 ("to be sick of; to loathe; surfeit"), not for its primary sense of "detest, hate." 倦厭 (juànyàn) describes a state of weary disgust — being so tired of something that one has come to loathe it, a combination of fatigue and aversion. It is often used of long-running situations, relationships, or routines that have worn a person down past mere tiredness and into resentment.

Because 倦 and 嫌 are the vault's canonical bearers of Dan'a'yo phonology, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 here are mechanically concatenated from their own fixed Dan'a'yo values (권험 / gwenhem / ㄍ⼔ㄋㄏㄝㄇ). The mandarin, cantonese, japanese, korean, and vietnamese fields instead reflect the real-world readings of 倦厭, using 厭's own attested reading (yàn / jim3 / EN / 염 / yếm) rather than 嫌's (xián / jim4 / KEN / 혐 / hiềm). This is the same divergence pattern seen in 波蘭, 六楽, and 魏峨.
