## Notes
- [比 (char)](../characters/比%20(char).md) "than; to compare"

比 is the comparative case particle — 比較格, marking the standard of comparison: "than; compared to." It attaches to the entity being compared against: 君比我高 (you are taller than me, lit. you 比 me tall), 今日比昨日冷 (today is colder than yesterday). In the comparison chart, 比 parallels Mandarin 比 (bǐ, than — the same character), Cantonese 比 (bei2), Japanese より (yori, than; more than), Korean 보다 (boda, than; compared to), Vietnamese hơn (more than; than). Uniquely, the character 比 doubles as its own Mandarin equivalent — the same character that means "to compare" becomes a productive case particle in Dan'a'yo and is retained as the comparative preposition in modern Mandarin. 比 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[比]].

Mandarin 比 (bǐ): the standard comparative construction A比B+adjective means "A is more [adjective] than B" — 她比我高 (she is taller than me), 今天比昨天冷 (today is colder than yesterday). This preserves the classical Chinese construction in modern Mandarin almost unchanged. 比賽 (bǐsài, competition; match), 比例 (bǐlì, ratio; proportion), 比率 (bǐlǜ, rate; ratio), 比較 (bǐjiào, to compare; comparative; relatively). Cantonese 比 (bei2): 佢比我高 (he is taller than me). Japanese より (yori): 君より大きい (bigger than you), 以前より上手 (more skilled than before). Korean 보다 (boda): 나보다 키가 크다 (taller than me), 어제보다 춥다 (colder than yesterday). Vietnamese hơn (than; more): lớn hơn (bigger than), tốt hơn (better than), đẹp hơn (more beautiful than).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 븨 differs from Korean 비. 比 reads 븨 (biǝ/ㄅㄧㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 並母 (b) + 脂A三開韻 (iɪ) gives Dan'a'yo a compound vowel -iǝ (ㄧㄜ) — while Sino-Korean gives 비 (bi), using the plain -i vowel. Dan'a'yo preserves the compound-vowel quality of 脂A韻 where Sino-Korean monophthongizes to -i, following the same pattern as 至 (즤 vs 지) in the same rhyme class.
