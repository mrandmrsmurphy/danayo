## Notes
- [憧](../characters/憧.md) "restless, flickering" reduplicated — a classical ideophone for unsettled, wavering motion. This word is the viable form of [憧](../characters/憧.md): 憧 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 憧憧 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to anchor 憧's precise Dan'a'yo sense in its *older* classical meaning — "flickering, wavering, undecided" — rather than the later "yearn, adore" sense that 憧 alone has narrowed to in modern usage (see [[憧憬]]).

憧憧 is first attested in the *Yijing* (易經), 咸卦: 憧憧往來,朋從爾思 ("restless and wavering, coming and going — friends follow your thoughts"), describing an unsettled, undecided state of mind. It survives into modern literary Mandarin describing flickering or restlessly moving shadows and figures — 人影憧憧 ("figures flickering restlessly," a real if literary phrase still used in descriptive prose). This is squarely a Classical Chinese-attested sense rather than a pan-CJKV one: Japanese and Korean don't preserve an independent "flicker/restless" life for 憧 at all — in both, the character's entire modern identity is あこがれ/동경, "yearn," with nothing left over once 憧憬 is accounted for. The likely path from here to there: a heart restlessly wavering (憧) toward something → a heart persistently reaching for something → longing.
