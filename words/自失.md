## Notes
- [自](../characters/自.md) "self" + [失](../characters/失.md) "to lose" — literally "to lose oneself," i.e. to lose one's composure, bearings, or presence of mind.

自失 (zìshī) describes the collapse of a person's mental orientation under shock, grief, or sudden realization — not a permanent loss of identity, but a temporary blankness in which one "loses oneself" and does not know how to react. It pairs the reflexive 自 with 失, "to lose" (cf. [[喪失]], [[損失]], [[失業]]), in a verb-object structure parallel to compounds like 自制 ("self-control," literally "to control oneself") on [[自]]'s own Words list.

The compound is the second half of the Tang-period idiom [[茫然自失]], "vaguely bewildered, having lost oneself," where [[茫然]] (a haze of confusion) sets the scene and 自失 names the resulting loss of bearings. The chengyu page notes that the construction is descriptive rather than moralizing — it records a psychological state rather than condemning a fault, which made it a useful phrase in narrative prose for depicting a character's reaction to the unexpected.

Japanese じしつ (jishitsu) is independently attested as a noun/する-verb meaning "to forget oneself, to become vacant-minded" (我を忘れ、茫然とすること), confirming that the compound stands on its own outside the four-character idiom in at least one CJKV tradition. Sino-Korean 자실 (jasil) and Sino-Vietnamese tự thất both follow the expected compositional readings, matching the corresponding segments of [[茫然自失]]'s own pronunciation fields. Note the divergence between the Dan'a'yo 諺文 transcription 즤싣 and the standard Sino-Korean 자실 — these differ enough that `kwin` is `false`, reflecting the same kind of orthographic split seen elsewhere between Dan'a'yo phonology and standard Sino-Korean spelling.

For usage in context, see [[茫然自失]].
