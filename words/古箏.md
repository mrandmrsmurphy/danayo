## Notes
- [古](../characters/古.md) "ancient; old" + [箏](../characters/箏.md) "zither; plucked string instrument" — stand-in for [[箏]], which cannot appear independently
- 古箏 names the long, multi-stringed plucked zither at the root of an entire family of East Asian instruments — the "old 箏," distinguished by name from later derivatives once they had drifted far enough to need their own identities. Its descendants went separate ways and took separate names with them: Korea's 가야금 (gayageum) and Japan's 箏/琴 (こと, koto) are close cousins that emerged from the same lineage but are never called 古箏 in their own languages, leaving the term itself — read gǔzhēng in Mandarin and gu2 zang1 in Cantonese — to function almost as a proper name for the specifically Chinese instrument, the way a loanword keeps its foreign shape rather than being translated.
