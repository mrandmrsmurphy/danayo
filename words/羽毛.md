## Notes
- [羽](../characters/羽.md) "feather; plume" + [毛 (char)](../characters/毛%20(char).md) "fur" — stand-in for [[羽]], which cannot appear independently

羽 alone already names a bird's feather specifically, while 毛 broadens the compound to body-covering hair/fur in general — together, "feathers" as the covering of a bird's whole body, plumage.

Mandarin yǔmáo, Cantonese jyu5 mou4, Japanese うもう (umō), and Korean 우모 (umo) are all the standard word for "feathers, plumage" in formal/written registers across the sphere. Vietnamese vũ mao is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound — interestingly also found in the reversed character order, 毛羽 (mao vũ), in Vietnamese-Chinese dictionaries, the same free word-order flexibility seen in [[介紹]]/紹介 and [[草花]]/花草 earlier in this sweep.
