## Notes
- [慣](../characters/慣.md) "habitual, accustomed" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use" + [句](../characters/句.md) "phrase, clause" — a phrase whose meaning has become fixed by habitual use, rather than by the literal sum of its words.

This is a precise technical sibling of [[成語]] and its subtype [[四字成語]]: where 成語 specifically denotes a phrase with a literary or classical pedigree (often, though not always, four characters drawn from a canonical source), 慣用句 is the broader linguistic category for any expression — of any length, any register, any origin — that speakers reproduce as a fixed unit rather than generate freely, such as English "kick the bucket" or "by and large." [[熟語]] overlaps with both terms and is sometimes used as a near-synonym of either, depending on the tradition. 慣用句 is well attested in exactly this technical sense in Japanese linguistics (慣用句) and Korean linguistics (관용구), but Mandarin and Cantonese description of fixed expressions instead favors 惯用语 (with 語/语 rather than 句) or simply 成语, so the precise three-character form 慣用句 is a Japanese–Korean coinage rather than pan-Sinitic. Attested across Japanese and Korean.
