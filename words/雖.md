## Notes
- [雖 (char)](../characters/雖%20(char).md) "although; even though; even if; despite"

雖 is the concessive linker in Dan'a'yo — "although; even though; even if" — introducing a clause whose truth does not prevent the consequence in the following clause: 雖A，猶B ("although A, still B"). This distinguishes 雖 sharply from [[若]] (conditional, where B depends on A): with 雖, B holds *regardless* of A — the concessive premise is acknowledged and overridden. Classical Chinese 雖 is grammatically strict in this role, typically followed by 猶 (yóu, "still; yet"), 然 (rán, "even so"), or 亦 (yì, "also; still"). Vietnamese tuy — the everyday word for "although; even though" — is the direct Sino-Vietnamese reflex of 雖, surviving so robustly that it has displaced any native equivalent: tuy nhiên ("however; nevertheless"), tuy vậy ("even so; despite that"), tuy... nhưng... ("although... but...").

雖 (ㄙㄨㄧ/sui/쉬 — Mandarin suī, Cantonese seoi1, Korean 수, Japanese SUI/すい, Vietnamese tuy): "although; even though; even if" — MC 心母 (s) + 脂B韻 (iuɪ). The character 雖 is a complex graph traditionally parsed as 口 (mouth) + 虫 (creature), originally a phonetic borrowing for the concessive particle with no semantic connection to its graphic elements. MC 心母 (s, voiceless dental sibilant) is stable across all five languages: Dan'a'yo ㄙ (s-), Korean ㅅ (s-), Mandarin s-, Cantonese s-, Vietnamese t- (a regular shift of the MC s-series in Sino-Vietnamese). The MC 脂B韻 (iuɪ, a close diphthong) → Dan'a'yo -ui (ㄨㄧ, diphthong preserved) vs Korean -u (수, simplified to monophthong). Mandarin suī and Cantonese seoi1 preserve the front-rounded vowel quality from the original diphthong; Japanese すい (SUI) gives the standard Sino-Japanese rendering.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 쉬 does not match Korean 수. MC 心母 (s-) is not the issue — both give s- initially. The divergence is in the rhyme: MC 脂B韻 (iuɪ) is preserved as the diphthong -ui (ㄨㄧ) in Dan'a'yo, giving 쉬 (swi), while Korean simplifies it to plain -u (수, su). This diphthong-simplification is the same pattern as [[未]] (Dan'a'yo 뮈 vs Korean 미, MC 微韻 ʉi): Dan'a'yo preserves the MC diphthong quality that Korean collapses into a monophthong.
