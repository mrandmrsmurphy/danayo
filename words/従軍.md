## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, obey" + [軍](../characters/軍.md) "armed forces, army"

従軍 means to join the military or to follow an army on campaign — literally, to go along with the armed forces. The compound is used both as a verb (従軍する, to serve in the military) and as a prefix in compounds such as 従軍記者 (war correspondent) and 従軍看護婦 (military nurse). In Chinese (从军, cóngjūn), the simplified form is standard and conveys the same sense of enlisting or going on campaign. In Korean (종군), the word appears in historical writing and journalism, including in the charged context of 종군 위안부 (戦時性暴力被害者, wartime comfort women) — a phrase whose political weight is significant in Korean-Japanese relations.
