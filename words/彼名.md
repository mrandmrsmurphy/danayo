## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name; honorific person classifier"

彼名 is the distal honorific correlative — "that person (polite, distal)" — the respectful counterpart to 彼人. It refers to a person of elevated status who is further removed: spatially distant, less directly in the shared discourse space, or simply more socially remote and elevated. The combination of 彼 (distal) and 名 (honorific) makes 彼名 the most socially "far" of the proximal-medial-distal polite triad: 此名 (this honoured person, near me), 其名 (that honoured person, near you or in our shared context), 彼名 (that honoured person, further removed).

Chinese 那位 (nà wèi) covers both 其名 and 彼名 since Mandarin has collapsed the medial/distal distinction; 那位先生 means simply "that gentleman" without specifying deictic tier. Japanese あの方 (ano kata) is the distal polite person demonstrative, commonly used in formal speech when referring to a respected person at some remove: あの方はどなたでしょうか ("Who might that distinguished person be?"). Korean 저분 (jeo-bun) uses the distal demonstrative 저 with the polite suffix 분, parallel to 이분/그분/저분 as a three-way polite deictic set.

In addition to spatial distance, 彼名 can encode social distance or elevation: a speaker might use 彼名 for a highly revered person even when that person is physically present, marking the gulf of social status. This parallels how Japanese あの方 is sometimes preferred over その方 for a very senior or distinguished person — the distal form implying respectful distance rather than closeness. This "social-deictic" use of the distal-polite form appears across CJKV honorific systems and is captured neatly by 彼名 in Dan'a'yo.
