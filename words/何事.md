## Notes
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what; which; interrogative" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

何事 is the interrogative correlative in the action column, asking for the identification of an event or matter: "what act? what is going on? what has happened?" Unlike the other correlatives in the 事 row, 何事 is not a Dan'a'yo neologism — it is an ancient compound that exists independently in Chinese, Japanese, and Sino-Korean contexts. The character 何 is the classical Chinese interrogative pronoun for objects, identity, and category (何人, 何処, 何事) and has been a productive interrogative prefix across CJKV for over two millennia.

In Mandarin, 何事 (héshì) survives in formal and literary registers meaning "what matter, what affair" (何事如此急迫? "What matter is so urgent?"); the colloquial equivalent is 什麼事 (shénme shì). Cantonese ho4 si6 exists in formal written registers; spoken Cantonese uses 乜事 or 咩事. Japanese なにごと (何事, nani-goto) is common and vivid, especially in exclamations of alarm or surprise: 何事だ! ("What on earth is going on!"); 何事もなく (何事も無く, "without incident, safely"); 何事か ("something or other, I wonder what"). Korean 무슨 일 or 어떤 일 are the natural equivalents; Sino-Korean 何事 (하사) is archaic.

The interrogative column is the only column of Dan'a'yo's correlative table that is not fully neological: 何事, 何物, 何人, 何名, 何処, 何時, 何様 all have classical Chinese antecedents. This reflects the historical depth of the 何-series as interrogative pronouns in classical Chinese, which spread throughout CJKV literary culture. Dan'a'yo regularises these into a single systematic column, favouring the classical sinographic forms over colloquial replacements (什麼, 誰, どこ, 무슨) for cross-linguistic accessibility and compactness.
