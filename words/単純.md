## Notes
- [単](../characters/単.md) "single, simple" + [純](../characters/純.md) "pure, unmixed" — having no extra parts or complications; simple in the sense of "nothing more than this."

単純 sits beside [[簡単]] and [[簡略]] in the vocabulary's small family of "simple" words, but with its own shade of meaning: where 簡単 emphasizes ease (a 簡単な task is not hard to do) and 簡略 emphasizes brevity (trimmed down from something longer), 単純 emphasizes a lack of internal complexity or mixture — a 単純な explanation has no hidden factors, and 単純 as used in the grammar book's own description of Dan'a'yo's number-marking (a [[単純]] [[複数]] marker, i.e. a plain plural marker with no extra connotation) is exactly this sense of "bare, unembellished." The Vietnamese reflex đơn thuần preserves the same "merely, purely" nuance. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
