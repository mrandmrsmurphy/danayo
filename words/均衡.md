## Notes
- [均 (char)](../characters/均%20(char).md) "even, equal" + [衡](../characters/衡.md) "scale, weighing-beam" — literally "evened on the scale," and so a state where opposing forces or quantities are matched.

衡 originally named the horizontal crossbar of a balance scale (the part a weight hangs from), which is why 均衡 still carries a slightly more physical, mechanical flavor than its near-synonym 平衡 (also "balance," not yet in the vocabulary, built from 平 "level" instead of 均 "even"): 均衡 leans toward economic and statistical equilibrium (供需均衡, "supply-demand balance"), while 平衡 leans toward bodily or literal balance. Note that Dan'a'yo's own reading of 衡 (Korean-bound-form 항) differs from the real Sino-Korean reading of this exact word, 균형 — the character's everyday Korean reading 형 only surfaces in this and a handful of other compounds. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
