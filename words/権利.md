## Notes
- [権](../characters/権.md) "power, authority" + [利](../characters/利.md) "benefit, profit" — stand-in for [[権]], which cannot appear independently

権利 is the standard East Asian compound for "right(s)" or "privilege" in the legal and political sense — an entitlement recognized and enforceable by law or social convention. The pairing of 権 (power, authority) with 利 (benefit, gain) captures the dual nature of a right as both a claim against others and a benefit accruing to the holder.

The compound gained its modern political sense during the Meiji period in Japan, when jurists and translators were rendering Western legal terminology into Chinese characters. 権利 became the canonical translation of German *Recht* and French *droit* in their subjective sense — a right held by an individual, as opposed to objective law (法律). From Japan the term spread back into Chinese and Korean legal vocabulary, making it one of the clearest examples of a Meiji-era translation compound that circulated across the entire Sinosphere.

In contemporary usage 権利 (quánlì / けんり / 권리) appears in constitutional law (基本権利, fundamental rights), labour law (労働権利, labour rights), and everyday speech wherever a person asserts an entitlement. It is contrasted with 義務 (obligations) in the paired formula 権利と義務 / 权利与义务.
