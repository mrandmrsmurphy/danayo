## Notes
- doubling of [母](../characters/母.md) "mother" — a childish, affectionate term of address, "mommy."

Dan'a'yo builds this word by doubling 母, the formal character for "mother" — real Mandarin instead doubles a different character, 媽 (a character whose own core meaning is "maidservant," extended informally to "mom"), giving 妈妈/媽媽, kept here as an alias for that real-world spelling. This vault also has a separate word, [[媽媽]], independently built by doubling 媽 itself — but Dan'a'yo assigns it a different, more literal gloss ("sweet little maidservant") rather than reusing it for "mommy," so the two pages don't overlap in meaning despite the coincidental resemblance to real Mandarin. Japanese ママ is itself a 20th-century Western loanword (from English "mama"), not a native or Sino-Japanese formation; はは (the native, neutral word for "mother") is what 母 alone means in Japanese, but doubling it the way Mandarin/Dan'a'yo does isn't how Japanese forms its childish "mommy" term. Korean 엄마 and Vietnamese mẹ are both fully native words, unrelated to any Sino root at all — every language in the family reaches for a different strategy to produce the same childish "mommy" register.
