## Notes
- [五 (char)](../characters/五%20(char).md) "five" + [方](../characters/方.md) "direction"

The Five Cardinal Directions of East Asian cosmology: the four compass points plus the center. The addition of Center (中) as a fifth direction is the defining feature of this system and has no equivalent in most other traditions, which count four directions.

| Direction | Color | Element | Season | Guardian creature |
|-----------|-------|---------|--------|-------------------|
| 東 East | 青 blue-green | 木 Wood | Spring | 青龍 Azure Dragon |
| 南 South | 赤 red | 火 Fire | Summer | 朱雀 Vermilion Bird |
| 西 West | 白 white | 金 Metal | Autumn | 白虎 White Tiger |
| 北 North | 黒 black | 水 Water | Winter | 玄武 Black Tortoise |
| 中 Center | 黄 yellow | 土 Earth | Midsummer | 黄龍 Yellow Dragon |

The Five Directions interlock with the Five Colors ([[五色]]), Five Elements ([[五行]]), Five Grains ([[五穀]]), and the five seasons (the Chinese calendar adds a short midsummer period to the standard four). Together these correspondences form the backbone of Chinese cosmological thought, informing traditional medicine, geomancy (風水), divination, military strategy, and imperial ritual.

The four outer guardian creatures — the Azure Dragon (East), Vermilion Bird (South), White Tiger (West), and Black Tortoise (North) — are among the most pervasive symbols in East Asian visual culture, appearing on Han dynasty tomb murals, Tang dynasty roof tiles, Korean tomb paintings at Goguryeo, Japanese textiles, and contemporary pop culture. The center Yellow Dragon, by contrast, was exclusively associated with the Emperor and appears less frequently in public art.

五方 is also used in a secondary sense meaning "everywhere; in all directions; from all quarters," as in 五方雜處 (people from all five directions living together, i.e. a mixed or cosmopolitan population).
