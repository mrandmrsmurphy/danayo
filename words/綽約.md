## Notes
- [綽](../characters/綽.md) "graceful" + [約](../characters/約.md) "bind; restrain" (here used for sound rather than sense, in a binome describing graceful bearing) — stand-in for [[綽]], which cannot appear independently
- 綽約 is a literary word reserved almost exclusively for describing women: a soft, willowy elegance of bearing and movement, the kind of grace that 19th-century English might have called "willowy" or "lissome." Like many such binomes it is built less from the plain meanings of its two characters than from their sounds — a 聯綿詞 (lianmianci) whose halves rhyme and alliterate (chuòyuē, coek3 joek3) to produce an impressionistic, almost onomatopoeic effect of softness, much as English "wishy-washy" or "higgledy-piggledy" lean on sound over sense. Sino-Korean 작약 (jagyak) and Sino-Japanese しゃくやく (shakuyaku) both preserve this literary register intact — and in Korean, 작약 happens to be a perfect homophone of 芍藥, the peony, a flower whose name in turn derives from 婥約, "beautiful in form." The coincidence draws the two words into the same orbit of meaning: a peony in bloom and a woman of 綽約 grace are, by the logic of the language itself, scarcely distinguishable images.
