## Notes
- [制](../characters/制.md) "regulate, control" + [約](../characters/約.md) "bind, agree, restrict" — a condition that binds or hems in some other action or possibility; a limiting factor.

制 is the regulatory root behind 制度 ("system, institution") and 制限 (also "restriction," not yet created, with a narrower legal-procedural flavor), while 約 carries the core sense of "tying down" seen in 約束 ("promise," literally "bound and bundled," not yet in the vocabulary). 制約 sits between the two: it names not a rule itself but the constraining effect of circumstance, resources, or law on what is otherwise possible — a budget's 制約, a treaty's 制約 on sovereignty, grammar's 制約 on word order. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese (chế ước).
