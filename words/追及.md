## Notes
- [追 (char)](../characters/追%20(char).md) "follow; hunt" + [及 (char)](../characters/及%20(char).md) "reach out to"

To follow until reaching; to pursue to the point of catching. The compound embeds the logic of completed pursuit: 追 (to chase, to follow behind) + 及 (to reach, to arrive at the target). To 追及 is not merely to give chase but to successfully close the distance — the 及 specifies that the following has reached its object.

In **Chinese**, 追及 is used both literally (chasing and overtaking a person, vehicle, or competitor) and in the abstract sense of catching up to a level or standard. 追及問題 in mathematics refers to "catch-up problems" — the classic word problem in which Object A pursues Object B at different speeds and one must calculate when A overtakes B. This pedagogical usage keeps the word alive in school mathematics.

In **Japanese**, **ついきゅう** (tsuikyū) has developed a primary modern meaning distinct from the Chinese: **to investigate and hold accountable; to demand responsibility.** 責任を追及する (sekinin wo tsuikyū suru, "to pursue accountability") is a stock phrase of political journalism, Diet questioning, prosecutors pressing suspects, and reporters demanding answers from officials who deflect. The word appears daily in political coverage: opposition parties 追及する government ministers; prosecutors 追及する corporate fraud; journalists 追及する cover-ups. The structural logic — following behind until you reach and catch the person responsible — maps perfectly onto this prosecutorial sense.

In **Vietnamese**, the compound 追及 has been reinterpreted as **truy cập** — now the standard term for "to access" in the digital age, particularly accessing websites, files, databases, and network resources. 追 (truy, to pursue/follow) + 及 (cập, to reach) = to follow a path until reaching information. This is a striking semantic evolution: an ancient pursuit compound has become the Vietnamese word for internet access, the everyday act of following links until you reach the content you seek.
