## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

其事 is the medial correlative in the action column: it picks out a matter in the addressee's sphere or in the shared common ground between speaker and listener, corresponding to Japanese それ/その in the three-way deictic system. The character 其 was the standard third-person pronoun in classical Chinese ("its, their") and secondarily a medial demonstrative; in Dan'a'yo it is standardised as the medial demonstrative contrasting with 此 (proximal) and 彼 (distal). 其事 accordingly means "that matter which you raised" or "the thing in question between us."

In Mandarin, 其事 (qí shì) appears in classical and semi-classical prose meaning "that affair, the matter in question" (明其事 "clarify that matter"; 成其事 "accomplish the thing"). Japanese そのこと is the natural spoken equivalent — その (adnominal medial demonstrative) + こと (nominaliser, matter). Korean 그 일 (geu il) uses the medial native demonstrative 그 + 일 (matter, work); Vietnamese *việc đó* (that matter). As with 此事, spoken East Asian languages tend toward analytic demonstrative + noun, while Dan'a'yo preserves the compact classical compound form.

The medial demonstrative 其 also carries a genitive/possessive function in classical Chinese (其 = "its"), which gives 其事 an occasional secondary reading of "its affair, his business" in classical texts — blurring topic-referential and discourse-deictic uses. In Dan'a'yo, only the deictic reading (medial demonstrative) is operative; the possessive function is handled by the genitive particle 之. This disambiguation — assigning 其 exclusively to the deictic column and 之 exclusively to possession — is one of the ways Dan'a'yo regularises classical Chinese polysemy into a unambiguous grammatical system.
