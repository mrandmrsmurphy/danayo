## Notes
- [憤](../characters/憤.md) "resent, indignant" + [慨 (char)](../characters/慨%20(char).md) "sigh, lament"

This word is the stand-in for [憤](../characters/憤.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 憤慨 carries the same sense of righteous anger and resentment.

憤慨 describes the emotional state of indignation — anger provoked by something perceived as unjust, improper, or outrageous. The compound pairs 憤 (pent-up resentment, indignation — the feeling pressing against the chest) with 慨 (a deep sigh or lament at something lamentable), producing a compound that captures both the hot emotional energy of outrage and the bitter resignation it entails. One is 憤慨 not merely by random anger but specifically at injustice, incompetence, or moral failure.

In Japanese (ふんがい, fungai), 憤慨する is a common suru verb for expressing strong indignation: 彼の態度に憤慨した ("I was outraged at his attitude"). The word is more formal and elevated than the colloquial 腹を立てる (to get angry). In Chinese (fènkǎi), the compound appears in political commentary, journalism, and literary writing to describe righteous anger. In Korean (분개), 분개하다 is used in both formal and everyday speech to express indignation.
