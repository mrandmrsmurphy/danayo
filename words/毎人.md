## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each; distributive" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human being"

毎人 is the distributive correlative in the person column — "per person; for each person individually." As a 副用名詞 (adverbial noun), it functions as a frame-setting expression that distributes a predicate across each individual person in a group, without a case particle: 毎人、役割を持つ ("each person has a role"). This patterns exactly like 毎日 (every day), 毎月 (every month), and the other 毎- compounds — applying the predicate to each instance of the nominal one by one rather than treating the group as a collective whole.

Japanese 一人当たり (hitori atari, "per person, per head") is the natural equivalent and is widely used in quantitative contexts: 一人当たりGDP ("GDP per capita"), 一人当たりの費用 ("cost per person"). Korean 사람마다 (saram-mada, "person by person, each person") uses the distributive suffix -마다 which attaches to nouns to mark individualised distribution across their members — semantically equivalent to 毎 in Dan'a'yo. Mandarin 每人 (měirén, every person) or 每個人 (měi ge rén, each person) are the nearest equivalents.

The distributive/collective contrast (毎人 vs 皆人) in the person column is practically important: 皆人が参加する ("all the people participate," understood as a group) vs 毎人が参加する ("each person participates," individually accounted for). 毎人 implies that the predicate applies to each person separately and can be checked or applied one at a time; 皆人 treats the group holistically. This distinction matters in distributional contexts like resource allocation (毎人に配布 = distribute to each person) vs universal statements (皆人にとって = for everyone).
