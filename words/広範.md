## Notes
- [広 (char)](../characters/広%20(char).md) "wide; broad" + [範](../characters/範.md) as 代用字 for 汎/泛 "extensive; wide-ranging"

The four aliases reflect two independent variables across the region's writing systems:

| | 泛/汎 | 範 (substitute) |
|---|---|---|
| 廣/広 (traditional/Dan'a'yo) | 廣泛, 広汎 | 廣範, **広範** |
| 广 (simplified) | 广泛 | — |

The substitution of 範 for 汎 is not a Dan'a'yo invention: Wiktionary explicitly identifies 範 as the *daiyōji* (代用字) for 汎 within the Japanese Jōyō kanji framework, where 汎 was dropped from the standard list and 範 designated its official substitute. Dan'a'yo inherits and extends this decision. 広 covers 廣 and 广 through its own aliases.

The word describes scope or reach that is genuinely broad — the spatial metaphor of 広/廣 ("wide") applied to the extent or coverage of something: a wide-ranging survey, extensive influence, broad support. Korean 광범 (廣範) and the set phrase 광범위하다 (廣範圍하다, "wide-ranging") confirm the compound's reach across the peninsula.
