## Notes
- [由 (char)](../characters/由%20(char).md) "from; since; ablative"

由 is the ablative/source case particle — 離格 (奪格), marking the point of origin or departure: "from; since; starting from." It attaches to a source noun to indicate the starting point of motion or the origin of a cause: 由東來 (come from the east), 由古至今 (from ancient times until now), 由此推斷 (infer from this). In the comparison chart, 由 parallels Mandarin 从 (cóng, from; since), Cantonese 從 (cung4), Japanese から (kara, from; since), Korean 에서 (eseo, from), Vietnamese từ (from; since). Dan'a'yo distinguishes 由 (source, moving away from) from 向 (allative, moving toward) and 至 (terminative, the endpoint reached). 由 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[由]].

Mandarin 从/從 (cóng): 從北京來 (come from Beijing), 從古至今 (from ancient times to the present), 從此以後 (from this point on; henceforth). In classical Chinese, 由 (yóu) and 從 (cóng) both expressed source and route; modern Mandarin uses 从 almost exclusively for the ablative, while 由 appears in compounds: 由來 (origin; background), 原由 (reason; cause), 自由 (freedom; liberty — lit. "from oneself"). Cantonese 從 (cung4): 從香港嚟 (come from Hong Kong). Japanese から (kara): 東京から来る (come from Tokyo), 朝から夜まで (from morning to night), 子供の頃から (since childhood). Korean 에서 (eseo): 서울에서 왔어요 (I came from Seoul), 여기에서부터 (starting from here). Vietnamese từ (from; since): từ Hà Nội đến Sài Gòn (from Hanoi to Saigon), từ xưa đến nay (from ancient times to now).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 윳 differs from Korean 유. 由 reads 윳 ('yuo/⼜ㄛ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 以母 (j) + 尤韻 (ɨu) gives Dan'a'yo a compound diphthong -yuo (⼜ㄛ), written 윳 in Hangul — while Sino-Korean gives 유 (yu), using a plain monophthong. Dan'a'yo preserves the compound-vowel structure of 尤韻 where Sino-Korean monophthongizes.
