## Notes
### Form
莱金 is [莱](characters/莱.md) + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following the rule for transition elements.

### Motivation
The international name _rhenium_ derives from Latin _Rhenus_ “the Rhine.” In CJKV, the Rhine is 萊茵河 (also commonly 莱茵河 in simplified forms). Selecting 萊 as the elemental sign is thus a **toponymic abbreviation** from an established river name.

### Type of formation
- source: 莱茵河 (Rhine)
- reduction: 莱 (first graph retained)
- suffixation: 金

This parallels the other place-derived elements (法素, 波素, 露金).

### Fit within the system
1. Avoids phonetic borrowing while preserving the naming origin.
2. Maintains one-character uniqueness for symbol use.
3. Uses 金 appropriately for a d-block element.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 铼 (phonetic) __Notice the similar component__
- Japanese: レニウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 레늄 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: rheni (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 萊金 restores a graph-based link to the Rhine.

### Clarity and issues
- 萊 in isolation ordinarily refers to a plant (a kind of weed) or appears mainly in proper names; its association with the Rhine is conventional rather than transparent.
- Within a chemical register, reserving 萊 as an element symbol removes ambiguity.

### Conclusion
萊金 is a consistent toponymic formation aligned with the system. It preserves the Rhine-based origin of _rhenium_ while maintaining the one-character symbol constraint and overall internal coherence.
