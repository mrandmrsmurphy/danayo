## Notes
渇/갈/khát all stay remarkably consistent across the family as the plain, everyday word for physical thirst — khát nước ("thirsty for water") is exactly as ordinary in Vietnamese as 喉が渇く (nodo ga kawaku, "the throat gets thirsty") is in Japanese, with no register split the way some body-sensation words show. All four languages also extend the same root metaphorically to non-physical craving or yearning (渇望/갈망/khát vọng, "aspiration, yearning"), a semantic extension that runs in parallel rather than diverging.
