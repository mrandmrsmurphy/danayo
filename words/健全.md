## Notes
- [健](../characters/健.md) "strong, robust" + [全 (char)](../characters/全%20(char).md) "whole, complete, intact" — sturdy in every part, sound through and through. This word is the viable form of [健](../characters/健.md): 健 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 健全 is its `stand_in` compound, keeping 健's sense the "sturdy, robust" one — distinct from [[康寧]] (the "at ease, well-being" sense that [[康]] stands in for instead) — splitting apart the two flavors the collapsed compound [[健康]] used to blur into one generic "healthy."

健全 is arguably the more central, more frequently invoked term of the two across all four daughters, and it extends readily beyond bodies to institutions and finances: Mandarin jiànquán and Cantonese gin6cyun4 both cover "sound" in the sense of a well-functioning system (健全的制度, "a sound system"), not just physical health. Japanese けんぜん (kenzen) is everyday vocabulary for both a healthy body and a wholesome, upstanding character (健全な精神, "a sound mind"), and Korean 건전 (geonjeon) follows the identical pattern (건전한 사회, "a sound/wholesome society"). 健康 stays the word for ordinary physical health; 健全 is what you reach for when soundness itself — of body, mind, or system — is the point.
