## Notes
- [思](../characters/思.md) "think" + [考](../characters/考.md) "consider, regard" — stand-in for [[思]], which cannot appear independently

**Small fix**: `cantonese` was missing a vowel (`s1 haau2` → `si1 haau2`, matching `characters/思.md`'s own stored `si1`).

**The richest homophone cluster found this entire sweep**: Korean 사고 (sago) is not just a two-way collision like the several found earlier this sweep, but a documented five-way homophone cluster with its own Korean Wikipedia disambiguation page — alongside this word's own 思考 ("thinking"), the identical spelling also covers 事故 (an accident — by far the most common everyday sense), 史庫 (a historical archive), 社告 (a company announcement), and 四苦 (the Buddhist "four sufferings"). Japanese しこう is similarly known for unusually dense homophony in its own right (志向, 指向, 試行, 至高, among others), though not independently re-verified here in the same depth as the Korean cluster.

Vietnamese tư khảo is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (confirmed via search), though the everyday native word for "to think" is simply suy nghĩ.
