## Notes
- [無 (char)](../characters/無%20(char).md) "lack; without" + [色](../characters/色.md) "color; form; appearance"

**無色** (*wúsè*) denotes the absence of color, and by extension, the absence of form or material substance in the Buddhist sense of 色 (*rūpa*). In everyday usage across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean, 無色 is the straightforward adjective/noun "colorless" or "transparent" — describing a liquid, gas, or material with no hue, such as a 無色 gas or 無色透明 ("colorless and transparent") glass.

In **Buddhist philosophy**, 無色 is the standard rendering of Sanskrit/Pali *arūpa*, negating 色 (*rūpa*, "form" — one of the five aggregates, 五蘊, that constitute a being). The **無色界** (*arūpa-dhātu*, the "formless realm") is the highest of the three realms (三界) of Buddhist cosmology, above the 欲界 (desire realm) and 色界 (form realm): a plane of existence inhabited by beings who have transcended physical form entirely and exist as pure consciousness in states of profound meditative absorption (the four 無色定, *arūpa-jhāna*). This sense of 無色 is closely related to but narrower than [[無形]]: where 無形 broadly denotes "without shape" across Daoist, Buddhist, and everyday registers, 無色 in its technical Buddhist sense specifically negates the aggregate of *rūpa* — material form as one of the constituents of conditioned existence — rather than shape or outline in general.

In **Japanese**, むしょく (*mushoku*) carries an additional figurative sense beyond "colorless": describing a person, opinion, or institution as 無色 means "unbiased, neutral, not colored by any particular ideology or affiliation" — analogous to English "colorless" in the sense of "lacking a distinctive character," but used positively to mean impartial or non-partisan, as in 無色透明な立場 ("a colorless and transparent — i.e., neutral — position"). Korean 무색 (*musaek*) and the Sino-Vietnamese *vô sắc* pattern closely with the Mandarin and Japanese literal senses; the Japanese figurative "unbiased" extension is not as productive in Korean or Vietnamese, which prefer native or other Sino-Xenic compounds for "neutral, impartial."
