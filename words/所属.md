## Notes
- [所](../characters/所%20(char).md) "place; -ee (nominalizer)" + [属](../characters/属.md) "belong"
- This word is the stand-in for [[属]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- Affiliation or organizational membership — the institution, group, or body to which one belongs. In Japanese, しょぞく (shozoku) is one of the most common administrative words: 所属部署 "assigned department," 所属チーム "team affiliation," 所属不明 "affiliation unknown." Chinese 所属 (suǒshǔ) functions similarly in bureaucratic and organizational language: 所属部门 "the department it belongs to." Korean 소속 (所属) is ubiquitous in workplace and institutional contexts: 소속사 "management agency/talent agency," 소속감 "sense of belonging." The morpheme 所 acts as a nominalizer here — 所属 is "the place/thing belonging to (something)" → "affiliation."
