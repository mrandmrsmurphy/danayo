## Notes
- [荘](../characters/荘.md) "villa; village" + [子](../characters/子.md) "son; master"

**荘子** (*Zhuāngzǐ*, "Master Zhuang") refers both to the Taoist philosopher Zhuang Zhou (荘周, c. 369–286 BC) and to the text attributed to him, [[translation/荘子 (book)]]. A contemporary of Mencius (孟子), he is one of the two foundational figures of classical Taoism alongside Laozi (老子). His name follows the same classical honorific pattern seen in Confucius (孔子 *Kǒngzǐ*), Mencius (孟子 *Mèngzǐ*), and Sun Tzu (孫子 *Sūnzǐ*).

The *荘子* text is celebrated for its literary imagination, its paradoxes, and its sustained use of allegory to dissolve distinctions between dreaming and waking, self and other, life and death. The seven inner chapters (内篇) are generally considered authentic; the outer and miscellaneous chapters may incorporate later material.

As a common noun, 荘子 also means hamlet or small village — 荘 "villa; settlement" with the diminutive suffix 子.
