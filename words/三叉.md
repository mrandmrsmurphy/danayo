## Notes
- [三 (char)](../characters/三%20(char).md) "three" + [叉 (char)](../characters/叉%20(char).md) "fork, prong" — literally "three-forked, tripartite"; used as a bound descriptive root more often than as a free standalone word for the weapon (the actual word for a trident is 三叉戟/삼지창, adding 戟 "halberd").

三叉/삼차/さんさ/tam xoa are all identical roots, but their single most common real-world appearance across the whole Sinosphere is medical, not martial: 三叉神経/삼차신경 (trigeminal nerve, the fifth cranial nerve, named for its three major branches) is genuinely shared vocabulary in Chinese, Japanese, and Korean medicine. Vietnamese medical terminology breaks from that pattern entirely — the trigeminal nerve is dây thần kinh sinh ba, a native/hybrid descriptive phrase ("nerve born in three"), not built on tam xoa at all.
