## Notes
- [所 (char)](../characters/所%20(char).md) "-ee (nominalizer)" + [有 (char)](../characters/有%20(char).md) "have" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (所's own points to bare `所`, 有's own to bare `有`); 所有 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/所 (char).md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/有 (char).md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- **A genuine grammaticalization divergence, documented rather than forced into one gloss**: in Mandarin and Cantonese, 所有/so2 jau5 has grammaticalized into an everyday determiner meaning "all, every" (所有人 "everyone," 所有的东西 "everything"), placed before a noun and usually marked with 的 — the literal "possess/own" sense survives only in formal, legal, or written registers (为本公司所有 "owned by this company"). Japanese しょゆう (verified via search), Korean 소유, and Vietnamese sở hữu, however, never developed the "all" determiner sense at all — they remain squarely in the formal "possession; ownership" register (所有権/소유권/quyền sở hữu "ownership rights"; 所有者/소유자 "owner"), functioning as a noun (optionally verbed with する/하다) rather than a quantifier. A learner moving between Mandarin and the other three languages should not expect 所有 to mean "all" outside Mandarin/Cantonese.
