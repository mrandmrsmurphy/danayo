## Notes
- [家](../characters/家.md) "house, family" + [禽](../characters/禽.md) "fowl" — domesticated birds raised for meat, eggs, or feathers; a bird-specific subset of livestock (家畜, which covers mammals too).

家禽/가금/gia cầm/かきん all keep the same precise scope across the family — birds only, distinct from four-legged livestock. Japanese carries one register note: かきん reads as a bit formal or technical in casual conversation, appropriate for writing and formal explanation, while everyday spoken Japanese more often names the specific bird (鶏, "chicken") or says 養鶏 ("poultry farming") instead of the general category term.
