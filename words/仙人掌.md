## Notes
- [仙](../characters/仙.md) "immortal, fairy" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" + [掌](../characters/掌.md) "palm"

仙人掌 is the standard Sino-Japanese/Sino-Korean term for "cactus," literally "immortal-person's palm" — a vivid image of the flat, hand-shaped, spine-covered pads of a prickly-pear (Opuntia) cactus, the variety most familiar in East Asia before the modern globalization of houseplants. 仙, a character built around the 山 ("mountain") component, denotes the Daoist immortals (仙人, cross-linked from [[人 (char)]]'s Words section) traditionally imagined dwelling in remote mountains; 掌 here returns to its literal anatomical sense of "palm" (as opposed to its extended "to manage" sense seen in [[掌管]], the previous word in this batch).

Despite being a transparent Sino-Chinese/Sino-Korean compound (fully productive in Mandarin xiānrénzhǎng and Korean 선인장), Japanese famously uses the native loanword サボテン (saboten, ultimately from Portuguese via the soap-like sap of certain species) for everyday speech, with 仙人掌 surviving mainly as the kanji spelling of that native word — a relatively rare case in this vault of a kanji compound whose pronunciation is not derived from its components' on'yomi at all.
