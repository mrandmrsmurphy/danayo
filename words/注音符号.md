## Notes
- [注](../characters/注.md) "annotate; pour" + [音](../characters/音.md) "sound; phonetic" + [符](../characters/符.md) "symbol; token" + [号](../characters/号.md) "mark; designation"

注音符号 is the formal name for Bopomofo — the phonetic script devised in China in 1913 to transcribe Mandarin syllables, and adapted in Dan'a'yo as its own phonological notation system. The name compounds two sub-words: [[注音]] (phonetic annotation; to mark the pronunciation of a character) and [[符号]] (symbol; mark), giving "phonetic notation symbols." Dan'a'yo's 注音符号 extends the original 37 Mandarin symbols with additional glyphs for sounds absent from Mandarin — retroflexes, the Dan'a'yo mid-central vowel ㄜ, the nasal coda ㄫ, and others. Colloquially called <ruby>ㄅㄆㄇㄈ<rt>ㄅㄛㄆㄛㄇㄛㄈㄛ</rt></ruby> from its first four symbols, as in Mandarin. Traditional-form aliases: 注音符號 (with 號 for 号) and 註音符號 (with both 註 for 注 and 號 for 号).

注 (ㄐㄨ/ju/주 — Mandarin zhù, Cantonese zyu3, Japanese CHUU/ちゅう, Korean 주, Vietnamese chú): "pour; annotate; comment" — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 虞韻 (ɨo); originally "to pour water," extended to "to annotate; to give a phonetic gloss." 音 (ㄨㄇ/'um/움 — Mandarin yīn, Cantonese jam1, Japanese ON・IN/おん・いん, Korean 음, Vietnamese âm): "sound; phonetics; tone" — MC 影母 (ʔ) + 侵B韻 (ɣiɪm); the core character for sound in all CJKV phonological terminology. 符 (ㄅㄨ/bu/부 — Mandarin fú, Cantonese fu4, Japanese FU/ふ, Korean 부, Vietnamese phù): "token; tally; symbol; sign" — MC 奉母 (v) + 虞韻 (ɨo); depicts bamboo tallies used for verification, generalized to any written sign or symbol. 号 (ㄏㄚㄨ/hau/핫 — Mandarin hào, Cantonese hou6, Japanese GOU/ごう, Korean 호, Vietnamese hiệu): "number; mark; designation" — MC 匣母 (ɣ) + 豪韻 (ɑu); a 会意 of 口 (mouth) + 丂 (exhaled breath), depicting a call or cry, generalized to "designation; label; symbol."

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 주움부핫 does not match Korean 주음부호. Two of the four syllables diverge. 注 (주=주) and 符 (부=부) match, but 音 and 号 do not. 音 gives Dan'a'yo 움 (MC 侵B韻 ɣiɪm → ㄨㄇ, -u vowel/ㅜ) vs Korean 음 (same MC final → -eu vowel/ㅡ) — the same MC syllable yields -u in Dan'a'yo but -eu in Korean. 号 gives Dan'a'yo 핫 (MC 豪韻 ɑu preserved as diphthong, -w coda written ᆺ in Hangul) vs Korean 호 (-ho, MC diphthong simplified to plain -o). The 豪韻 diphthong-retention pattern in Dan'a'yo — writing the -w coda as ᆺ — also appears in [[少]] (솟), [[鳥]] (촛), and [[草]] (찻).
