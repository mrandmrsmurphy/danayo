## Notes
Japan alone shifted this character to mean simply "princess" as an ordinary word — ひめ (hime) is the everyday term used in fairy tales and historical titles (白雪姫, "Snow White"; かぐや姫, "Princess Kaguya"). Mandarin, Korean, and Vietnamese all keep 姬/희/cơ closer to its classical Chinese range: originally the surname of the Zhou royal house (姬姓), then by extension an honorific for a noblewoman or a beautiful woman generally, and — perhaps its most persistent classical sense — a term for a palace lady, concubine, or courtesan-entertainer (寵姬, "favored concubine"; 歌舞姬/가무희, "singing-and-dancing girl"). "Princess" appears in these three languages mainly bound inside specific royal titles (帝姬/제희, "imperial princess") rather than as the plain, standalone word the way ひめ is in Japanese.
