## Notes
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what; which; interrogative" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

何多 is the interrogative quantity correlative — "how much; how many; to what degree?" As a 副用名詞, it questions the extent or quantity of a predicate: 何多必要か ("how much is needed?"); 何多の人が来るか ("how many people will come?"). The quantity interrogative completes the "how?" axis of the correlative table: 何処 asks where, 何時 asks when, 何様 asks in what manner, and 何多 asks to what degree or in what quantity.

Cross-linguistic equivalents: Mandarin 多少 (duōshǎo, "how much/many" — quantity interrogative, literally "more-less") is the standard everyday form, used both for countable (多少人, how many people) and uncountable (多少水, how much water) quantities. Cantonese 幾多 (gei2 do1, "how many/much") is the colloquial Cantonese quantity interrogative. Japanese いくら (ikura, "how much" — especially for price or uncountable amount) and いくつ (ikutsu, "how many" — for discrete countable items) cover the two sub-senses. Korean 얼마나 (eolmana, "how much/many; to what extent") is the all-purpose quantity-degree interrogative.

The original file had japanese and korean as comma-separated strings ("幾,何,いくつ" and "얼마,얼마나,몇") rather than YAML lists. The original 注音 ㄏㄚㄉㄚ also carried the systematic ㄉㄚ error for the 多 character; the correct reading is ㄏㄚㄉㄜ.
