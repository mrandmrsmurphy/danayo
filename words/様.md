## Notes
- [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "-ly (adverbializer)"

様 is "-ly" — in Dan'a'yo, 様 functions as the manner case particle: attached as a suffix to a noun or stative it derives an adverb meaning "in the manner/way of X." As a correlative row heading, 様 generates 此様 (in this way; thus; like this), 其様 (in that way; so; thus), 彼様 (in that distant/other way), 何様 (how?; in what manner?), 某様 (in some way; somehow). The character 様 (traditional 樣, simplified 样) has an original meaning of "appearance; form; shape; style" — and Dan'a'yo extends this to the grammatical function of deriving manner adverbials: the way or form in which something is done. 様 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[様]].

Mandarin 樣 (yàng): 樣子 (yàngzi, appearance; shape; way), 這樣 (zhèyàng, in this way; like this), 那樣 (nàyàng, like that; in that way), 同樣 (tóngyàng, in the same way; likewise), 多樣 (duōyàng, diverse; varied). The Mandarin adverbializer 地 (de) corresponds to Dan'a'yo 様 in function. Cantonese 樣 (joeng4): 呢樣 (ni1 joeng4, this kind), 咁樣 (gam2 joeng4, in that way; like that), 多樣性 (do1 joeng4 sing3, diversity). Japanese 様 (よう, yō / さま, sama): 様子 (ようす, yōsu, appearance; situation; state of affairs), ～様 (-さま, -sama, the respectful address suffix — 田中様, Dear Mr. Tanaka), ように (yō ni, in the manner of; so that). Korean 양 (様) in Sino-Korean: 이 모양으로 (in this manner; in this form); the adverbial particle in Korean is -게 (-ge) or 처럼 (-cheoreom, "like"). Vietnamese dạng (樣): dạng thức (form; format), đa dạng (diverse; lit. "many-formed").

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 양 exactly matches Korean 양. Both read 'yang/양 — MC 疑母 (ŋ) + 陽開韻 (ɨɐŋ) gives a vowel-initial syllable with a y-glide onset in both systems: Dan'a'yo renders 疑母 as an apostrophe (glottal-y onset) and the -ang vowel with -ng final produces 양 in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean identically.
