## Notes
- [左](../characters/左.md) "left" + [学](../characters/学.md) "learn" — the Left School, the lower of a pair of state schools recorded in Shang-dynasty education and described in the *Book of Rites* (禮記·王制): "殷人養國老於右學，養庶老於左學" ("the Yin [Shang] people cared for their state elders at the Right School, and their common elders at the Left School"). 左學 trained and housed commoners (庶老) rather than the hereditary elite, making it the functional predecessor of the later [[小学]] tier of education, while its counterpart [[右学]] anticipated [[太学]].

The Shi ji and other early histories preserve at least one concrete record of the institution in use: in the sixth year of King Wu Ding's reign, the minister Fu Yue was ordered to inspect both the Left and Right Schools and see to the care of the elders housed there, suggesting the schools functioned partly as state-supported homes for retired officials and respected commoners as much as as places of instruction for the young — a blending of educational, ceremonial, and welfare functions characteristic of early Chinese state institutions before the clearer separation of these roles in later dynasties.
