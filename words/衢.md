## Notes
- [衢 (char)](../characters/衢%20(char).md) "crossroads, thoroughfare"

衢 names a crossroads or thoroughfare specifically as a *branching point* — a road that splits into several directions — rather than a road or street in general. The character's own phonetic component (瞿, itself built on 目 "eye" doubled, plus 隹 "bird," evoking a startled bird's darting glance) is unattested elsewhere in this vault, leaving 衢 without any sibling characters sharing its phonetic family here. Classical usage often paired it with numbers to specify how many ways a road split — 三衢 "a three-way fork," 九衢 "a nine-fold crossroads" (used hyperbolically of a great capital's web of avenues) — a sense still faintly present in the modern word's connotation of a busy, many-branched urban thoroughfare rather than a simple street.
