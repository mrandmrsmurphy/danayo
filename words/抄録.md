## Notes
- [抄](../characters/抄.md) "copy; abridge; excerpt" + [録](../characters/録.md) "record; write down" — stand-in for [[抄]], which cannot appear independently
- 抄録 names the practice — and the product — of picking out the essential parts of a longer text and setting them down on their own: an excerpt, a digest, an abstract. In modern academic registers across the Sinosphere this is precisely the word for the abstract that opens a research paper — Sino-Korean 초록 (chorok) is the standard term students learn for "abstract," and Japanese しょうろく (shōroku) carries the same sense of "summary; abridged extract." The Korean reading produces an unrelated but charming homophone: 초록 is also 草綠, "grass-green," the color — so that in spoken Korean alone, "please write the 초록" could momentarily suggest either an academic summary or a shade of green, disambiguated only by the hanja that the ear cannot hear.
