## Notes
- [折](../characters/折.md) "break, bend" + [衷](../characters/衷.md) "inner feeling, center" — literally "to bend toward the center," and so to settle on a compromise between competing positions by taking a bit of each.

衷 on its own denotes the innermost, most sincere part of a person's feelings (as in 衷心, "heartfelt," not yet created), so 折衷 has the literal flavor of "bending [opposing claims] until they meet at the heart of the matter." The word is especially well known as the description of 折衷主義 (eclecticism), the practice — in philosophy, architecture, or design — of combining elements from multiple schools or traditions rather than adhering strictly to one. It is a near match for [[簡略]] in register (both are somewhat formal, written-style vocabulary) but unrelated in meaning. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese (chiết trung).
