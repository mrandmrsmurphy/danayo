## Notes
- [狂](../characters/狂.md) "wild; crazy" + [風 (char)](../characters/風%20(char).md) "wind"

> [!Warning] Anagram of [[風狂]] — same characters, reversed order, entirely different meaning. 狂風 is a violent wind; 風狂 is madness.

**狂風** (*kuángfēng*) is a raging wind — a gale, storm-wind, or tempest. The compound applies 狂 (*kuáng*, "wild; uncontrolled; frenzied") to 風 (*fēng*, "wind"), yielding the image of wind gone feral: untethered, destructive, irresistible. It appears across East Asian literature as a stock term for fierce storms, from Tang poetry to modern meteorological writing.

Though it shares characters with [[風狂]] (*fēngkuáng*, "madness; insanity"), the two compounds are wholly unrelated in meaning. 風狂 is an abstract psychological state — the "wind of madness" in the Classical sense, where 風 carries the older sense of affliction or influence (cf. 風邪, "cold; illness from wind-evil"). 狂風 is purely meteorological, grounded in physical phenomenon. In Dan'a'yo the two form a minimal pair by reversal: 狂風 (*gwangfung*, ㄍ⺢ㄫㄈㄨㄫ) vs. 風狂 (*funggwang*, ㄈㄨㄫㄍ⺢ㄫ) — same elements, different order, different worlds.

The Sino-Korean 광풍 (狂風) survives in modern Korean in both literal and figurative senses, frequently appearing in headlines and political writing to describe a whirlwind of social or political upheaval — *a storm of change sweeping through*. The Vietnamese *cuồng phong* is a direct Sino-Vietnamese compound carrying the same meteorological and figurative range.
