## Notes
- [清](../characters/清.md) "pure, clear" + [楚](../characters/楚.md) "clear" — `characters/楚.md`'s own `stand_in` points here, meaning 楚 does not stand independently outside this compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/清.md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/楚.md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- **A genuine register/scope divergence, documented rather than forced into one gloss**: in Mandarin and Cantonese, 清楚/cing1 co2 is a general-purpose word for "clear, distinct, understandable" — applicable to explanations, vision, sound, or thinking (说清楚 "explain clearly," 看不清楚 "can't see clearly"). Japanese せいそ, however, narrows the sense specifically to describe a person's neat, modest, unaffectedly elegant appearance or demeanor (清楚系, a fashion/style category) — not general "clarity." Korean 청초 similarly leans toward this narrower "neat and graceful" sense (often describing a person or flower) rather than Mandarin's broad "clarity."
