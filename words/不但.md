## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [但](../characters/但.md) "only; but" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (不's is bare `不`, 但's is bare `但`)

**A genuinely Mandarin-specific grammatical particle, left honestly incomplete rather than fabricated**: 不但……而且…… ("not only… but also…") is a common Mandarin correlative conjunction pair (interchangeable with 不僅/不只), but searches turned up no evidence that Japanese or Korean use this compound at all — both languages express "not only… but also…" with entirely different, unrelated constructions (Japanese 〜だけでなく/〜のみならず; Korean -뿐만 아니라), built from different roots rather than a 不+但-style calque. Left `japanese`/`korean`/`vietnamese` blank rather than inventing an unattested compositional reading, since even a "compositional, not a real word" disclosure (the pattern used elsewhere this sweep) would still overstate the case for a pure grammatical particle with no cross-linguistic parallel at all.
