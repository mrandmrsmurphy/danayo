## Notes
- [榜](../characters/榜.md) "posted list, notice board" + [文](../characters/文.md) "writing, text"

This word is the stand-in for [榜](../characters/榜.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 榜文 carries the same sense of a publicly posted written notice.

榜文 is the term for an official proclamation or public notice — specifically the kind posted in a conspicuous location for the populace to read. Historically, 榜文 were large boards or sheets displayed at city gates, yamen entrances, or market squares bearing imperial edicts, examination results, military orders, or criminal sentences. The character 榜 originally denoted a flat plank of wood (semantic component 木 + phonetic 旁), and the noticeboards of classical China were literally that: planed boards of wood bearing painted or brush-written text.

In Chinese classical fiction and historical drama, the 榜文 is a recurring plot device — the notice that announces examination rankings, declares a person wanted, or proclaims a reward. 張榜 (zhāngbǎng, to post a notice) is the verbal form. In Korean (방문), the Sino-Korean reading is a near-homophone of 訪問 (방문, "to visit, to call upon"), one of the most common Korean words; context always distinguishes them.
