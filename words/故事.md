## Notes
- [故](../characters/故.md) "happening" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "thing; action" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (故's own points to `緣故`, 事's own to bare `事`); 故事 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/故.md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/事 (char).md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **A genuine register/scope divergence, documented rather than forced into one gloss**: Mandarin gùshi (and Cantonese gu3 si6) is the broad, everyday word for "story" in general — a children's story, a movie's plot, 讲故事 "to tell a story," 故事书 "storybook." Japanese こじ, Korean 고사, and Vietnamese cố sự (all verified via search), however, narrow the sense to specifically an *old or classical* anecdote — a historical precedent, a story from antiquity cited as an example (Japanese 故事成語/Korean 고사성어, idioms coined from such classical anecdotes) — not a general-purpose word for "story" (which Japanese and Korean instead express with native words, 話/이야기). A learner should not expect 故事 to mean "story" in the everyday Mandarin sense outside Mandarin/Cantonese.
