## Notes
- [至 (char)](../characters/至%20(char).md) "to; until; terminative"

至 is the terminative case particle — 終止格, marking the endpoint or limit of motion or extent: "to; until; up to; as far as." It attaches to the endpoint noun to indicate where something reaches or stops: 行至城門 (walk to the city gate), 至今 (up to now; until now), 由春至夏 (from spring to summer). In the comparison chart, 至 parallels Mandarin 到 (dào, to; until; arrive at), Cantonese 到 (dou3), Japanese まで (made, until; up to; as far as), Korean 까지 (kkaji, until; up to; as far as), Vietnamese đến (to; until; to arrive). 至 marks the point reached, in contrast with 由 (source, starting point) and 向 (allative, direction of movement). 至 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[至]].

Mandarin 到 (dào): 到北京 (arrive at/go to Beijing), 從這裡到那裡 (from here to there), 直到現在 (until now; right up to the present). The character 至 itself appears in compounds: 至今 (zhìjīn, until now; to this day), 至於 (zhìyú, as for; regarding; to go so far as to), 至少 (zhìshǎo, at least), 由古至今 (from ancient times to the present). Cantonese 到 (dou3): 去到香港 (go to Hong Kong), 等到天光 (wait until dawn). Japanese まで (made): 東京まで行く (go as far as Tokyo), 5時まで (until 5 o'clock), 子供まで知っている (even children know it — "up to" in scope). Korean 까지 (kkaji): 서울까지 가다 (go as far as Seoul), 지금까지 (until now; up to now), 끝까지 (until the end). Vietnamese đến (to; until): đến Hà Nội (to Hanoi), từ sáng đến tối (from morning to evening), đến bây giờ (until now).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 즤 differs from Korean 지. 至 reads 즤 (jiǝ/ㄐㄧㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 脂A三開韻 (iɪ) gives Dan'a'yo a compound vowel -iǝ (ㄧㄜ), producing 즤 — while Sino-Korean gives 지 (ji), using the plain -i vowel. Dan'a'yo preserves the compound-vowel quality of 脂A韻 where Sino-Korean monophthongizes to -i, the same pattern seen in 比 (븨 vs 비) in the same rhyme class.
