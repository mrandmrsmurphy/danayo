## Notes
- [瞭](../characters/瞭.md) "clear, clear-sighted" + [然 (char)](../characters/然%20(char).md) "so"

瞭然 describes something being immediately obvious or transparent — perceivable at a glance without effort. It is most commonly encountered as the second half of the four-character idiom 一目瞭然 ("clear at a single glance"), one of the most widely used chengyu in modern Chinese and Japanese. The compound is literary in register across all five CJKV languages.

In Japanese (ryōzen), the word functions as a na-adjective and is chiefly literary; it rarely appears independently in modern speech, but is well-known through 一目瞭然 (ichimokuryōzen). In Vietnamese, neither 瞭 nor 然 has a standard Hán-Việt compound reading recorded for this pairing, so Vietnamese renders the sense through native vocabulary (rõ ràng).

The character 瞭 (Dan'a'yo alias 了) carries the core sense of clarity of vision or understanding; 然 functions as an adjectival suffix meaning "having the quality of," a grammatical pattern that generated dozens of literary adjectives in Classical Chinese (e.g. 突然, 確然, 漠然).
