## Notes
- [軽 (char)](../characters/軽%20(char).md) "light" + [重 (char)](../characters/重%20(char).md) "heavy" — literally "lightness and heaviness"; by extension, the relative importance of things, or the proper weighing of priorities against one another.

軽重 functions as a noun naming the relationship between things of differing significance: 軽重を知る (to know what matters more), 軽重をつける (to assign relative priority), 軽重を誤る (to misjudge the relative importance). The compound is deeply embedded in classical Chinese strategic and economic thought: the statesman Guan Zhong's *Guanzi* (管子) contains an extended section titled 輕重篇 which treats the balancing of economic and political forces — supply and demand, fiscal policy, the weight of commodities against currency — as a science of statecraft. This classical resonance gives 軽重 a richer connotation than simple "importance"; it invokes the image of a scale, and the wisdom to read it correctly.

Korean 경중 is the standard equivalent and appears in the same contexts: 경중을 따지다 (to weigh relative importance), 경중에 따라 (according to relative gravity). Vietnamese *khinh trọng* follows the Sino-Vietnamese compound form, though everyday Vietnamese more often uses analytic constructions.
