## Notes
- [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small"

小 is "small," and by natural extension "young" or "junior" — a very productive character that appears as a modifier across a huge range of Dan'a'yo compounds, from the concrete ([[小雨]] "drizzle", [[小指]] "pinky finger", [[小鼠]] "mouse") to institutional terms ([[小学]] "elementary school") to the abstract ([[微小]] "microscopic", [[杪小]] "insignificant"). The character is 指事 (an indicative symbol): three small dots representing smallness directly, rather than depicting a specific object.

Mandarin 小 (xiǎo). Cantonese 小 (siu2). Japanese has the on'yomi SHOU (小学校 shōgakkō "elementary school") alongside the everyday native adjective ちいさい (chiisai, "small") — the reading stored on this word page — plus a cluster of rarer native readings (こ-, お-, ささ-, etc.) used as prefixes in names and set compounds. Korean recites this character by its traditional hun-eum mnemonic "작을 소" (jageul so) — 작을, the meaning ("small"), paired with 소, the sound — the same two-part naming convention Korean applies to every hanja; only the sound half, 소, carries over into Sino-Korean compounds. Vietnamese tiểu is the Sino-Vietnamese reading, common in compounds (tiểu học "elementary school," mirroring 小学) and titles, while everyday speech more often uses the native nhỏ.

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 소 (so) exactly matches Sino-Korean 소, both descending from the same Middle Chinese source (s + iᴇu, 心母 + 宵A韻) with no divergence in either initial or final.
