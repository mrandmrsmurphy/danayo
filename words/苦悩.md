## Notes
- [苦 (char)](../characters/苦%20(char).md) "suffering, bitter" + [悩 (char)](../characters/悩%20(char).md) "anguish, troubled mind" — inner torment; mental anguish; existential suffering

Where 苦難 (hardship, tribulation) names suffering that is imposed by external circumstances — war, poverty, persecution — 苦悩 names the suffering that takes place inside the mind: the anguish of an unresolved dilemma, the torment of grief or guilt, the existential distress of a person who cannot find peace. Both components point inward: 苦 here is less the bitterness of physical pain than the bitterness of an afflicted consciousness, and 悩 is specifically the disturbed, tormented heart.

In Japanese, 苦悩 (くのう) is particularly associated with Meiji and Taishō-era literature — the 苦悩 of the intellectual torn between tradition and modernity, between individual desire and social obligation. The word carries a certain literary weight: Natsume Sōseki (夏目漱石) and Mori Ōgai (森鷗外) use it to name the distinctive inner conflict of the modern Japanese subject confronting an alien world.

The Buddhist resonance is present throughout: 苦 as dukkha — not merely pain but the fundamental unsatisfactoriness of existence — means that 苦悩 can shade into a philosophical or spiritual register, naming not a temporary distress but a structural condition of conscious life.
