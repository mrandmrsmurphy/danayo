## Notes
- [利](../characters/利.md) "profit" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use"

利用 is "to use, utilize, make use of" — literally "to use for profit/benefit," combining 利 "profit, gain" with 用 "use." Unlike the plain, neutral [[使用]] (simply "to use, employ"), 利用 carries a purposive edge — using something *to one's advantage* — which in many contexts tips into a negative sense: "to exploit, take advantage of (a person)," as opposed to straightforwardly using a tool or resource.

Mandarin 利用 (lìyòng, "to use; to exploit" — 利用機会 "seize the opportunity" vs. 他利用了我 "he took advantage of me," the same word covering both the neutral and the exploitative sense, disambiguated only by context). Cantonese 利用 (lei6jung6). Japanese 利用 (りよう, riyō) stays closer to the neutral "use, utilize" sense in everyday register (図書館を利用する, "to make use of the library") and is less prone to the negative "exploit a person" reading than Mandarin. Korean 리용/이용 (Dan'a'yo's own `korean` field records the North Korean 문화어 form 리용, since South Korean 두음법칙 would shift the same word to 이용) patterns similarly to Japanese, common in everyday phrases like 대중교통 이용 ("public transit use"). Vietnamese lợi dụng has drifted furthest: in everyday Vietnamese it leans heavily toward the *negative* sense, "to exploit, take advantage of (unscrupulously)" — lợi dụng lòng tin ("to abuse someone's trust") — with the neutral "use, utilize" sense more often expressed by sử dụng instead.

kwin is false: 利 alone doesn't match its own Sino-Korean reading (Dan'a'yo 릐 vs Korean 리 — a vowel mismatch), even though 用 does (용=용), so the compound's `kwin` — true only when every constituent character matches — comes out false.
