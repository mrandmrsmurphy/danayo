## Notes
- [君 (char)](../characters/君%20(char).md) "you; second-person pronoun"

君 is the second-person pronoun in Dan'a'yo — "you" — the standard polite second-person address: 君食 (you eat; you are eating), 君去否 (are you going? — lit. "you go-or-not?"). 君 is the Swadesh 2 word, indicating its fundamental status alongside [[我]] (Swadesh 1). In classical Chinese, 君 (OC *kun) was primarily a title of nobility ("lord; sovereign; ruler") that also served as a respectful second-person address; Dan'a'yo retains only the pronominal "you" function, with the politeness suffix role delegated to [[公]]. 君 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[君]].

Mandarin 你 (nǐ, you — standard), 您 (nín, you — respectful), 汝 (rǔ, thou — classical/archaic). Cantonese 你 (nei5, you — general), 您 (nei5/lam5, you — formal/respectful). Japanese あなた (anata, you — neutral/polite), 君 (kimi, you — casual or literary; also used as a respectful address), お前 (omae, you — masculine rough), 貴方 (anata — formal). Korean 당신 (dangsin, you — formal/distant; literary), 너 (neo, you — informal/intimate), 자네 (jane, you — senior speaking to junior respectfully). Vietnamese bạn (you — peer/neutral), anh/chị/em (you — role-relative, based on social register and relation to addressee), ông/bà (you — respectful elder address).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 군 exactly matches Korean 군. 君 reads 군 (gun/ㄍㄨㄋ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 見母 (k, velar stop) + 文韻 (ɨun). Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map MC 見母 to a plain velar initial (ㄍ/ㄱ) and MC 文韻 (ɨun) to plain -un (군). The 文韻 is among the most consistently preserved MC finals in both systems — a simple nasal coda -n with no medial complications — producing identical 군 forms. A phonologically parallel stable kwin: true case is [[公]] (gong/공), another 見母 initial with a simple nasal-final rhyme mapping identically across both systems.
