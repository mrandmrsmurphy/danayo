## Notes
- [検](../characters/検.md) "examine; check" + [査](../characters/査.md) "investigate"

検査 is "to inspect; to examine; to check" — 検 (to examine, to check, to look over carefully for defects or compliance) + 査 (to investigate, to look into, to audit). The compound describes systematic examination: verifying whether something meets a standard, assessing its condition, or investigating for problems. 検査 is broad in application — medical examinations, quality inspections, customs checks, official document reviews — anywhere something must be scrutinised against a criterion.

Mandarin 检查 (jiǎnchá; traditional 檢查) is the standard term across inspection contexts: 身體檢查 (physical examination/health check), 安全檢查 (safety inspection), 海關檢查 (customs inspection), 質量检查 (quality check). Japanese 検査 (けんさ, kensa) is the standard across medical, industrial, and official use: 健康検査 (health check), 品質検査 (quality inspection), 税関検査 (customs inspection); 車検 (vehicle inspection) is a well-known abbreviation. Korean 검사 (檢查/檢事, gumsa) is the standard word for inspection — but note a notable homophony: 검사 (檢事) also means "public prosecutor," giving the same pronunciation two unrelated meanings distinguished only in writing. Vietnamese kiểm tra is the standard everyday term for check and inspection.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 검자 differs from Korean 검사 in the second syllable. 査's Middle Chinese initial is 崇 (d͡ʒ), which Dan'a'yo maps to the affricate ㄐ (j-), giving 자 — while Sino-Korean maps the same initial to s-, giving 사. This 자/사 split is a regular sound correspondence between Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean for characters with MC 崇 initials. The alias 檢查 is the traditional-character form (檢 is the traditional version of 検, simplified in Japan).
