## Notes
- [飛 (char)](../characters/飛%20(char).md) "to fly; to soar; flying" + [行 (char)](../characters/行%20(char).md) "to go; to travel; to proceed"

飛行 is "to fly; flight" — 飛 (to fly, to soar) + 行 (to go, to travel, to proceed). The character 行 adds directionality and intentionality to 飛's raw aerial motion: flight as purposeful travel through the air, not mere flapping or hovering. This makes 飛行 the appropriate compound for aviation and directed airborne movement — 飛行機 (aeroplane), 飛行士/飛行員 (aviator/pilot), 飛行時間 (flight time) — in contrast to, say, 飛翔 (soaring, circling flight) which is more poetic and less directional.

Mandarin 飛行 (fēixíng) is the standard term for flight and aviation: 飛行員 (pilot), 飛行器 (aircraft, flying vehicle). Cantonese 飛行 (fei1 hang4) carries the same meanings. Japanese 飛行 (ひこう, hikō) is the formal aviation term: 飛行機 (hikōki, aeroplane), 飛行場 (hikōjō, airfield), 飛行時間 (flight time). Korean 비행 (bihaeng) is standard: 비행기 (aeroplane), 비행사 (aviator), 비행 금지 구역 (no-fly zone). Vietnamese bay is the native word for "to fly" — the Sino-Vietnamese compound phi hành is more formal/technical. 飛行 appears on the Swadesh list at item 120, confirming "fly" as core vocabulary.

The alias 飞行 is the simplified-character form (飛→飞). In Dan'a'yo, 飛 reads ㄈㄝㄧ and 行 reads ㄏㄚㄫ, giving 注音 ㄈㄝㄧㄏㄚㄫ and Hangul 피항. The ㄈ (labiodental fricative) initial of 飛 is transcribed in Dan'a'yo Hangul as ㅍ (aspirated bilabial) — the conventional adaptation since Korean lacks a labiodental fricative — and in ROmaji as "p", giving pihang rather than fihang.
