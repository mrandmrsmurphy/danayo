## Notes
- [自](../characters/自.md) "self" + [閉](../characters/閉.md) "shut"

自閉 is a back-formation from [[自閉症]] (autism). That compound was coined in twentieth-century Japanese psychiatry to translate Eugen Bleuler's 1911 term *Autismus* — itself from Greek *autos* (αὐτός, "self") — by rendering the "self" component as 自 and the "closed off" component as 閉. The base word 自閉 then came to stand independently for the state of being closed off from others, carrying both a clinical sense (autistic) and a broader colloquial one (withdrawn, self-isolating).

In Chinese the word functions as a stative verb or adjective: one can 自閉 (withdraw socially) or describe a condition as 自閉. In Japanese じへい is used almost exclusively in the compound 自閉症; the bare form is rare. Korean 자폐 (Sino-Korean 자=自, 폐=閉) follows the same pattern, appearing mainly in 자폐증 (autism).
