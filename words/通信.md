## Notes
- [通 (char)](../characters/通%20(char).md) "pass through, communicate" + [信](../characters/信.md) "trust, message, letter"

通信 covers the full range of communication by transmission — correspondence by letter, signaling by telegraph or radio, and modern telecommunications. The compound is productive across all CJKV languages in technical and everyday usage: 通信衛星 (communications satellite), 通信網 (communications network), 無線通信 (wireless communication), 通信社 (news agency, wire service).

In Japanese (つうしん, tsūshin), the word is among the most common in media and technology contexts. 通信教育 (correspondence education) and 通信販売 (mail-order sales) show its older correspondence-based sense still active alongside its modern telecommunications meaning. In Chinese (tōngxìn), 通信 is the standard term for telecommunications and correspondence alike. In Korean (통신), the same range applies: 통신사 (news agency, also telecommunications carrier), 한국통신 (Korea Telecom). The Vietnamese thông tin leans more toward "information" in modern usage, appearing in công nghệ thông tin (information technology, IT).
