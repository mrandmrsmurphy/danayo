## Notes
- [提](../characters/提.md) "to raise, present" + [供](../characters/供.md) "to supply, provide for" — stand-in for [[供]], which cannot appear independently

提供 is the standard verb "to provide, to offer, to supply" — used for everything from a company providing a service to a witness providing testimony. 提 is a 形声 character (semantic 手 "hand" + phonetic 是) whose core meaning is "to raise up, to bring forth, to present" — already seen in [[前提]] ("premise, prerequisite," literally "that which is raised before") and [[提示]] ("to present, to show"), both cross-linked from [[提]]'s own Words section.

供, a 形声 character built on 人 ("person") with phonetic 共 ("together"), means "to supply, to furnish, to provide for" — most familiar from its religious sense of making offerings (供物, 供養) to the dead or to deities, extended in modern usage to any act of supplying something needed. 提供 combines these two near-synonymous verbs of "bringing something forward for someone's use" into the single, slightly more formal compound that dominates business, legal, and media registers across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean alike.
