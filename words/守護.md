## Notes
- [守](../characters/守.md) "guard, keep watch over" + [護](../characters/護.md) "safeguard, protect" — to keep watch over and shield from harm; also, as a noun, one who does so. This word is the viable form of [護](../characters/護.md): 護 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 守護 is its `stand_in` compound, paired with 守 (already independently legitimized elsewhere via [[守衛]]) rather than with [[保]] — leaving the collapsed compound [[保護]] to plain synonym-compound status, with 保's own precise sense now living in [[保持]] instead.

守護 is a genuinely major word across all three East Asian daughters, not a marginal alternative to 保護. Japanese しゅご (shugo) names guardian deities and spirits (守護神, 守護霊) and, historically, the provincial military governors of the Ashikaga shogunate (守護大名, shugo daimyō) — a specific institutional title, not just a generic gloss. Korean 수호 (suho) is equally central — 수호신 ("guardian deity") is everyday vocabulary, and 수호천사 ("guardian angel") is the standard Korean rendering of that concept. Mandarin shǒuhù and Cantonese sau2wu6 stay in the same "watch over and protect" register, though 保護 remains the more common everyday verb for "to protect" in both.
