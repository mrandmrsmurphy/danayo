## Notes
- [王 (char)](../characters/王%20(char).md) "king" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank, throne"

王位 is "the throne" in the most general sense — the seat and authority of a king (王), as opposed to [[帝位]] (specifically an emperor's) or [[爵位]] (a noble's title below the crown). 王 is a 象形 pictograph of a ceremonial axe, a symbol of martial authority kept near the ruler's seat and used in state rituals — an origin shared with [[全 (char)]], listed as one of 王's derived characters.

王位 appears throughout East Asian historiography and folklore wherever succession, usurpation, or abdication is at stake — "to ascend to 王位" ([[即位]]), "to vacate 王位," "to contest 王位." Together with [[王冠]] ("crown") and [[王妃]] ("queen consort"), already cross-linked from [[王 (char)]]'s Chengyu/Words sections, 王位 completes the basic vocabulary cluster of monarchy: the person (王), the regalia (王冠), the consort (王妃), and the office itself (王位).
