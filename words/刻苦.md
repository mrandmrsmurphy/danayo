## Notes
- [刻](../characters/刻.md) "carve, strict, harsh" + [苦 (char)](../characters/苦%20(char).md) "bitter, suffering" — assiduous; bearing hardship with strict discipline; working hard despite difficulty

刻 contributes the sense of precision and stringency — the careful cutting of a carver who tolerates no excess. 苦 contributes the endurance of hardship. Together they describe a quality of character: not merely working hard (努力) but working with an austere, almost punishing self-discipline that refuses ease and comfort. 刻苦 is a moral-aesthetic term as much as a descriptive one.

The compound is characteristically Confucian in its valorization of effort over endowment. A student who is not naturally gifted but studies with 刻苦 diligence is more admirable than a talented one who coasts. The Four-character phrase 刻苦耐労 (bearing hardship with perseverance) makes the ethical dimension explicit.

Korean 각고 retains this moralized register: 각고의 노력 (刻苦の努力, effort with austere diligence) is a set phrase used particularly to describe the preparation behind an achievement — the years of hidden, unglamorous work that preceded a visible success.
