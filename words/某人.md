## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human being"

某人 is the indefinite correlative in the person column — "someone; a certain person whose identity is withheld." Of all the 某-column entries, 某人 is perhaps the most widely attested across CJKV as a natural compound: in Chinese, 某人 (mǒurén) appears everywhere from classical texts to modern journalism as the standard way to anonymise or leave unspecified a human referent. It sits between 其人 (a specific known person in the discourse) and 誰 (asking for identification) — 某人 refers to a person the speaker is deliberately not naming.

In Mandarin, 某人 is used pervasively in legal documents (某人侵害了原告的权利 "a certain person infringed the plaintiff's rights"), journalistic anonymisation (消息人士某人表示… "according to a certain person with knowledge of the matter…"), and everyday speech (是某人告訴我的 "it was someone who told me"). Japanese 某 (ぼう, bō) in compound form (某氏 "Mr./Ms. So-and-so"; 某氏の話によると "according to a certain person") is formal; Japanese also uses だれか (dareka, "someone") and 何者 (nanimono, "some unknown person/entity") for the indefinite-person function. Korean 어떤 사람 (eotteon saram, "a certain person") or 누군가 (nugunga, "someone") are the natural equivalents.

某人 is particularly useful in contexts where anonymisation is legally or socially required: when the identity of a referent must remain unspecified even though the speaker knows it, 某人 provides a formally neutral, widely understood placeholder. This distinguishes it from 誰 (interrogative — asking who) and from 彼人 (distal deictic — a known but distant person). In Dan'a'yo's correlative system, 某人 is the person-row analog of 某事 and 某物, all sharing the deliberate-vagueness semantics of the 某 column.
