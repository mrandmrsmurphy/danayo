## Notes
凜/름/lẫm all carry the same double meaning, moving in parallel from a literal sense — biting, freezing cold, the kind that makes one shiver — to a figurative one: a stern, awe-inspiring dignity that commands respect, as in Mandarin 凜然 (lǐnrán, "stern, awe-inspiring") and 威風凜凜 (wēifēng lǐnlǐn, "an imposing, dignified bearing"). This character is almost always bound rather than standing alone in all four languages — Vietnamese lẫm survives specifically in the idiom oai phong lẫm liệt ("a dignified bearing that commands universal respect and fear"), the exact same "biting cold → stern dignity" shift as its Mandarin cognate, packaged into a fixed four-character phrase rather than free use.
