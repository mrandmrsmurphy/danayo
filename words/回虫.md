## Notes
- [回 (char)](../characters/回%20(char).md) "return, coil" (Dan'a'yo stand-in for 蛔, roundworm) + [虫](../characters/虫.md) "insect, worm"

In Dan'a'yo, 回 serves as the graphemic stand-in for 蛔 (the dedicated roundworm character), which shares its pronunciation; the alias 蛔虫 is the standard Chinese simplified form, and 蛔蟲 the traditional form.

回虫 / 蛔蟲 is the roundworm — specifically *Ascaris lumbricoides*, a large parasitic nematode that infects the human small intestine. It is among the most prevalent parasitic infections globally, historically common across the CJKV region before modern sanitation. The character 蛔 (Dan'a'yo 回) depicts a 虫 (worm/insect) with the 回 phonetic component, encoding both meaning and sound in a typical 形声 structure. The character 回 in Dan'a'yo covers both the "return/cycle" sense and, through the alias 蛔, the roundworm sense.

In Japanese (かいちゅう, kaichū), the word appears in medical writing and is also commonly written in katakana (カイチュウ) in biological contexts. In Korean (회충), the word is the standard medical and everyday term for roundworm.
