## Notes
- [刺 (char)](../characters/刺%20(char).md) "stab; prick; sting" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin cì, Cantonese ci3, and Korean 자 (ja) all use this character productively and independently — 刺 alone means "to stab, prick" in ordinary Mandarin speech, and appears bound in common compounds across the sphere (刺客 "assassin," 刺激 "stimulate/provoke," 名刺/名刺 "business card" — originally a card presented on a pointed stick). Japanese さす (sasu) is likewise the everyday native verb for "to stab, to pierce." Vietnamese keeps chích, one of three attested readings on `characters/刺 (char).md` (alongside thứ and thích) — chích specifically survives in the everyday verb for a sting or injection (chích thuốc, "to give/get a shot"), a narrower sense than the general "stab" this character covers elsewhere.
