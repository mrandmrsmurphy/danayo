## Notes
- [覚](../characters/覚.md) "wake, perceive" + [悟](../characters/悟.md) "realize"

覚悟 carries two related but distinct senses depending on the language. In Mandarin/Cantonese (覺悟/juéwù), the compound keeps the literal "waking up to realize" sense — **awareness, enlightenment**, often in a Buddhist or ideological context (becoming conscious of a truth). In Japanese (かくご) and Korean (각오), the same compound has shifted to mean **resolve, mental preparedness, readiness to face something difficult** — having "woken up" to a hard reality and steeled oneself for it. Both senses are kept here since both are in active use across the Dan'a'yo cultural sphere.
