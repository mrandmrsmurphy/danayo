## Notes
- [貿](../characters/貿.md) "to exchange goods; to trade" + [易](../characters/易.md) "to exchange; to change"

貿易 is a semantic doublet: both 貿 and 易 mean "to exchange," and their pairing creates the standard word for trade and commerce across the CJKV sphere. 貿 (semantic 貝, the cowrie shell that served as East Asia's earliest currency) specifically denotes the exchange of goods with a commercial connotation; 易 is more general — "to exchange, to change, to transform" — but participates in the commercial context here. The doubling of synonyms is a characteristic classical Chinese device for creating unambiguous compound nouns from characters that are individually polysemous.

易 is famously polysemous: in modern Mandarin (yì), it covers "easy/simple," "to change/exchange," and the divination tradition of the Yijing (Book of Changes). The 易 in 貿易 is the exchange sense, distinct from both divination and ease, though the polysemy generates occasional false conceptual connections. 貿易 appears in ancient Chinese texts describing market exchange, and by the Tang and Song dynasties it was established for long-distance commerce — including Silk Road and maritime trade. It entered formal bureaucratic vocabulary to describe the tribute-trade system (朝貢貿易) that structured relations between China and neighboring states.

In modern usage, 貿易 (Mandarin màoyì, Japanese bōeki, Korean 무역 muyeok, Vietnamese mậu dịch) is the dominant formal term for international trade in all four writing-using languages. It appears in free trade agreement names (自由貿易協定, FTA), government ministry names (Ministry of Trade, in various forms), and economic statistics. The kwin=false marking reflects the divergence between Dan'a'yo 못역 (mou + 'yeg) and Korean 무역, where 貿 maps to 무 in Korean but to 못 in Dan'a'yo.
