## Notes
- [計](../characters/計.md) "measure; plan; count" + [数](../characters/数.md) "count; number" — stand-in for [[数]], which cannot appear independently

Japanese けいすう (keisū) genuinely means "counting, calculation, tallying" — matching this word's own sense directly. But it is a real homophone of a different compound, 係数 (also read けいすう), "a mathematical coefficient" (as in the "2" in 2x²) — same reading, unrelated first character (係 "relate/connect," not 計 "measure/plan"). Korean 계수 (gyesu) carries this collision even further: per Wiktionary, 계수 is a genuine multi-way homophone covering 計數/係數 ("calculation" / "coefficient," etymologically tied to Japanese 係数), plus entirely unrelated hanja for "cinnamon" and "sister-in-law" — a cluster reminiscent of [[思考]]'s five-way 사고 collision earlier this sweep. Mandarin jìshù is the more straightforward, unambiguous term for "counting, tallying." Vietnamese kế số is an honest compositional reading; the everyday native verb for "to count" is simply đếm.
