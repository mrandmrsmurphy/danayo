## Notes
- [装 (char)](../characters/装%20(char).md) "dress up; clothe; adorn; pretend"

装 is "to dress up; to adorn; to pretend; to feign" — a versatile character covering both physical adorning (dressing, clothing, decorating) and figurative pretending (affecting a false appearance). The semantic thread is the outer surface: whether clothing a body, decorating an object, packing goods into a container, or constructing a false front. As a single-character word in Dan'a'yo, 装 carries all these senses; compound words like [[装飾]] (decoration) and [[装置]] (equipment/device) extend the physical-adorning branch, while bare 装 alone often carries the feigning sense.

Mandarin 装 (zhuāng; traditional 裝) covers the full semantic range: 装扮 (to dress up; to disguise oneself), 装饰 (to decorate), 装载 (to load/pack), 安装 (to install), and bare 装 meaning "to pretend; to feign" — 别装了 ("stop faking it"). Cantonese 裝 (zong1) covers dressing, decorating, and installing similarly. Japanese 装う (よそおう, yosoou) is the native verb for "to dress; to adorn oneself; to put on an appearance of"; Sino-Japanese compounds include 装置 (sōchi, equipment), 装飾 (sōshoku, decoration), 服装 (fukusō, attire). Korean 장 (裝, jang) appears in compounds: 포장 (包裝, packaging), 무장 (武裝, armament), 복장 (服裝, attire). Vietnamese trang is the Sino-Vietnamese reading: trang phục (clothing/attire), trang trí (to decorate).

The "pretend" sense is particularly productive in modern colloquial Mandarin — 装 alone, or in internet-slang formations, describes someone performing a false persona or affecting innocence. This derives from the same covering metaphor: just as you dress a surface to change its appearance, you can 装 your behavior to present a false front. The kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 諺文 좡 does not match Korean 장 — the medial ⺢ (wa) in Dan'a'yo's 注音 ㄐ⺢ㄫ yields 좡, while Sino-Korean drops the medial, giving the plain 장. The alias 裝 is the traditional-character form (衣 radical visible in the lower half); 装 is the Japanese-simplified form used as the Dan'a'yo headword.
