## Notes
- [代](../characters/代.md) "substitute; replace" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use" + [字 (char)](../characters/字%20(char).md) "character; glyph"

A 代用字 is a character pressed into service in place of another — either because the intended character is absent from the vault, too rare to include, or unavailable in the script register being used. The substitution is sanctioned by listing the replaced character as an alias on the stand-in's character page: [[包]] carries 雹 as an alias, authorising [[雨包]]; [[厳 (char)]] carries 儼, authorising [[厳然]]; [[井]] carries 丼, authorising the whole donburi family.

The term is borrowed from Japanese linguistic terminology, where 代用字 (だいようじ, *daiyōji*) describes a character used in place of the standard one. The Dan'a'yo pronunciation ㄉㄚㄧ⼄ㄫㄐㄧ (*dai'yongji*) closely parallels だいようじ — 代 (ㄉㄚㄧ, *dai*) and 字 (ㄐㄧ, *ji*) are identical — but 用 diverges: Japanese reads it as よう (*yō*), a long vowel with no final consonant, while Dan'a'yo preserves the Middle Chinese nasal coda as ⼄ㄫ (*'yong*). The -ŋ that Japanese elided into vowel length is still present in the Dan'a'yo phonology of 用, so *dai'yongji* and *daiyōji* are near-cognates separated by one coda.
