## Notes
- [因](../characters/因.md) "cause, reason" + [由 (char)](../characters/由%20(char).md) "from, since" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (因's is `原因`, 由's is bare `由`)

**Real content bug corrected**: `english` was `since, therefore, because`, describing a conjunction — but 因由 is actually a noun meaning "the reason, the cause" (verified via search: a synonym of 原因/理由/緣故, "cause of a matter," sometimes with a Buddhist "predestined connection" sense), matching `characters/因.md`'s own gloss directly. Corrected `pos` from blank to `名詞` accordingly.

**Real bug corrected**: `korean` was `때문에`, the fully native Korean phrase for "because of," not a reading of 因由 at all — corrected to `인유` (inyu), matching the word's own already-stored `諺文`/`羅馬字`.

Mandarin yīnyóu, Cantonese jan1 jau4, and Vietnamese nhân do (verified via search, a real synonym alongside lý do, nguyên nhân, duyên cớ) all name "the reason, the cause" as a noun. A cluster of close synonyms exists across the sphere built from overlapping roots — 因為, 由於/由于 (Mandarin, more conjunction-like "because"), 原因 (nguyên + nhân), 理由 (lý + do) — 因由 itself sits closest to 原因/緣故 in register and function, a plain noun rather than a connective.
