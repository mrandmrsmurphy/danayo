## Notes
- [宗](../characters/宗.md) "sect, lineage; ancestral" + [徒](../characters/徒.md) "disciple, follower" — the Roman Catholic term for "apostle," as opposed to [[使徒]], used by Protestants and the Eastern Orthodox.

The split between **宗徒** and **使徒** is a denominational translation choice rather than a difference in underlying meaning — both render the Greek ἀπόστολος ("one who is sent"). 使徒 (lit. "envoy-disciple," emphasizing the apostle as one *sent out* on a mission) became the standard term in Protestant Bibles across China, Japan, and Korea, and in Eastern Orthodox usage. 宗徒 (lit. "lineage-disciple," emphasizing the apostle's place within the foundational succession of the Church) is the term preferred in Chinese-language Catholic translations and liturgy — e.g. 宗徒信經 ("Apostles' Creed," vs. Protestant 使徒信経) and 宗徒大事錄 ("Acts of the Apostles," vs. Protestant 使徒行傳/[[行傳|行伝]]).

The Vietnamese Catholic tradition shows the same split: **tông đồ** (宗徒) is the term used in Vietnamese Catholic Bibles and prayers (e.g. Kinh Tin Kính các Tông Đồ, the Apostles' Creed), parallel to **sứ đồ** (使徒) in Protestant translations. The twelve apostles themselves are 十二宗徒 in Catholic usage, 十二使徒 in Protestant usage — the same individuals, named differently according to the translating tradition.
