## Notes
- [立 (char)](../characters/立%20(char).md) "stand" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin lì, Cantonese lap6, Korean 립 (rip, preserving the vault's standing North Korean 문화어 form — South Korean would apply 두음법칙 and drop the initial entirely to 입), and Vietnamese lập all use this character productively and independently or in extremely common compounds across the sphere (獨立/独立/독립, "independence"; 成立/성립, "come into existence"). Japanese, by contrast, keeps 立 mostly bound in on'yomi compounds (独立, どくりつ) while the standalone verb "to stand" uses the native kun'yomi 立つ (たつ) — the character itself, not a separate word, but conjugated rather than read as a bare noun-like citation form.
