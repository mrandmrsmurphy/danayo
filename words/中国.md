## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "center; middle" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation; state; kingdom"

中国 is "China" — the Chinese state, and historically the Chinese cultural-geographic region. 中 (middle) + 国 (nation, kingdom). The name encodes the classical Chinese self-conception: China as the central state (中央之国, the nation at the center of the world), surrounded by peripheral polities and peoples. The name 中国 appears in texts from at least the Western Zhou period (1046–771 BCE), where it referred not to a unified empire but to the central states of the Yellow River basin — the culturally and politically dominant heartland as opposed to peripheral tribal territories. In modern usage, 中国 primarily denotes the People's Republic of China (中华人民共和国, founded 1949), though the [[中華民国]] (Republic of China, founded 1912) also uses 中国 in its historical self-designation.

Mandarin 中国 (Zhōngguó; traditional 中國) is the standard name — the full official name of the PRC is 中华人民共和国 (Zhōnghuá Rénmín Gònghéguó). Cantonese 中國 (zung1 gwok3). Japanese 中国 (ちゅうごく, Chūgoku) means "China" but also independently names the Chūgoku region of western Honshū — an ambiguity that occasionally arises in Japanese geographic writing; Japan typically uses 中国 for both, relying on context. Korean 중국 (中國, Jungguk) is the standard term. Vietnamese Trung Quốc (Sino-Vietnamese: 中=Trung, 国=Quốc) is the standard Vietnamese name for China.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 중곡 differs from Korean 중국 in the second syllable — 国 reads 곡 (gog/ㄍㄛㄎ) in Dan'a'yo but 국 (guk) in Sino-Korean. This 곡/국 divergence is systematic across all 国-containing Dan'a'yo compounds: [[中国人]] (중곡닌 vs 중국인), [[中国語]] (중곡요 vs 중국어), [[中華民国]] (중화민곡 vs 중화민국). The alias 中國 is the traditional-character form (國 vs 国 — 国 is the Japanese-simplified and PRC-simplified form; 國 is used in Taiwan, Hong Kong, and classical contexts).
