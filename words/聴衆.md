## Notes
- [聴 (char)](../characters/聴%20(char).md) "listen" + [衆](../characters/衆.md) "crowd"

In Buddhism, 聴衆 specifically denotes the assembly gathered to hear a dharma teaching — not a passive crowd but active receivers of the Teaching. The act of listening (聴) is

  itself the first of the three wisdoms in Buddhist practice: 聞慧 (wisdom through hearing), 思慧 (wisdom through reflection), 修慧 (wisdom through meditation). So 聴衆 in a sutra

  context carries the implication that the listeners are engaged in a spiritual act, not merely attending an event.

  

  The word appears constantly in sutra preambles — the assembly present when the Buddha speaks is 聴衆. It's the crowd at Vulture Peak, so to speak.
