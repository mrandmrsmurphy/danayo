## Notes
- [画](../characters/画.md) "draw; plan; scheme" + [策](../characters/策.md) "scheme; plan; stratagem" — stand-in for [[策]], which cannot appear independently
- 画策 fuses two characters that both already mean "to plan" into a compound that leans toward the conspiratorial end of planning — not an open strategy but a scheme hatched behind closed doors, the maneuvering and string-pulling of court intrigue or backroom politics. Japanese かくさく (kakusaku) keeps this edge sharply: a politician 画策する is not "planning" in any neutral sense but actively scheming, angling, plotting an outcome others aren't meant to see coming. The Korean cognate is usually written 劃策 rather than 画策 — 劃, 畫, and 画 being three faces of the same etymon, all carrying the Sino-Korean reading 획 — and 획책 (hoekchaek) preserves precisely the same shade of "scheme, contrivance, often with a sly or underhanded cast" that the word carries in every register of the Sinosphere it touches.
