## Notes
- [文](../characters/文.md) "culture, writing" + [科](../characters/科.md) "branch, section" — the humanities/liberal-arts track, as opposed to 理科 (the sciences track).

文科/문과/ぶんか all name the same real institution: the arts-vs-sciences fork built into China's, Japan's, and Korea's secondary and university admissions systems (文科生/理科生 in China, 文系/理系 in Japan, 문과/이과 in Korea), a genuinely shared piece of educational infrastructure across the three, not just a cognate vocabulary item. Vietnamese breaks the pattern here: modern Vietnamese schooling doesn't sort students into a 文科-named track at all — it uses native khối xã hội ("social sciences block") versus khối tự nhiên ("natural sciences block") instead. Văn khoa does exist in Vietnamese, but as a more dated, institutional term — historically the name of university Faculties of Letters/Humanities (Đại học Văn Khoa) — rather than the operative term for today's track system.
