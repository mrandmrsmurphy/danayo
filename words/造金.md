## Notes
### Form
造金 is [造](characters/造.md) “to make; to fabricate; to create” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal.” The suffix follows the rule for transition metals.

### Motivation
The international name _technetium_ derives from Greek **τεχνητός** (_technētos_) “artificial, man-made,” from **τέχνη** (_technē_) “art, craft, skill.” The element was the first to be **artificially produced** (1937), not found naturally at the time of discovery. 造 directly captures this defining feature.

### Type of formation
This is a **semantic calque of the core etymon** (artifice, manufacture), rather than a phonetic borrowing. It parallels other meaning-based reconstructions (e.g., 青素, 重素).

### Choice of 造
- Conveys deliberate human manufacture, not merely occurrence.
- More concrete than 工 (“work”) or 技 (“skill”), which point to craft but not necessarily the act of bringing something into existence.
- Avoids overlap with other elements that might plausibly use 工/技.

### Fit within the system
1. Meaning-based, preserving the Greek naming logic.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a d-block element.
3. Provides a distinctive leading graph for symbol use.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 锝 (phonetic) ,  鎝
- Japanese: テクネチウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 테크네튬 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: techneti (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 造金 restores semantic transparency tied to the element’s artificial origin.

### Clarity and ambiguity
- 造 is common and polysemous (“make; build; create”), but within a chemical register the “artificially made” sense is strongly cued.
- The association is historically accurate, even though trace natural technetium is now known; the naming reflects the discovery context.

### Conclusion
造金 is a precise semantic reconstruction of _technetium_, emphasizing its defining historical property as an artificial element, and it fits cleanly within Dan'a'yo.
