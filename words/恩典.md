## Notes
- [恩](../characters/恩.md) "kindness; mercy" + [典](../characters/典.md) "canon; authoritative text; standard" — grace as a doctrinal category; the formal theological principle that God gives freely without merit

Among the Dan'a'yo 恩- words, 恩典 is the most distinctly theological and doctrinal. 典 connotes canon and authoritative rule — the standard by which things are measured. 恩典 thus frames grace not as a warm personal gesture (like [[恩寵]]) or an act (like [[恩施]]), but as a foundational doctrinal principle: the teaching that God's beneficence operates freely, outside the economy of merit.

In Chinese Protestant Christianity, 恩典 is the dominant translation for the Greek χάρις (charis) and the Latin gratia. It appears in the formulas of Reformed theology: 唯獨恩典 (sola gratia), the principle that salvation comes through grace alone. The Korean 은전 and Vietnamese ân điển follow the same reading. In Eastern Orthodox tradition, the Greek term is usually rendered 恩寵 rather than 恩典.
