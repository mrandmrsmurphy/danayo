## Notes
- [居](../characters/居.md) "reside" + [所 (char)](../characters/所%20(char).md) "place" — the place one currently occupies, not the act of residing there. This word is the viable form of [居](../characters/居.md): 居 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 居所 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 居's sense the noun "dwelling/whereabouts," in contrast to [[居住]] (the plain verb "to reside" that [[住]] stands in for) — splitting apart what the collapsed compound 居住 had blurred into one generic "reside."

The split isn't arbitrary — Japanese and Korean civil law already draw exactly this line. 住所 (jūsho/주소) names a person's *permanent legal domicile*, built on 住; 居所 (kyosho/거소) names their *current whereabouts or temporary residence*, built on 居, and is specifically the term used when someone has no fixed 住所. Japanese legal documents pair the two directly: 住所又は居所 ("domicile or whereabouts"). Korean civil law preserves the identical 주소/거소 distinction. 居's other compounds support the same reading — 起居 (daily life/routine), 故居 (former residence), 新居 (new home), 隠居 (retirement/seclusion) — all naming a state or place one currently occupies, never the bare action of residing.
