## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "to receive, accept" + [理](../characters/理.md) "to manage, to put in order"

受理 is a bureaucratic and legal term meaning "to accept [a submitted document, application, or legal case] for processing" — the formal moment at which an authority takes responsibility for handling something that has been [[提出]] (submitted, the word from earlier in this project) to it. 理's core sense is "to put in order, to manage, to govern" — visible in [[理想]] ("ideal") and [[理論]] ("theory," both cross-linked from [[理]]'s own Notes) as well as [[理屈]] ("reasoning, logic") and [[原理]] ("principle," from [[理]]'s Words section); the character's name component 里 ("village, unit of distance") suggests an original sense of "the grain/pattern in jade or wood," extended to "underlying order, reason."

受理 is the third of five 受-compounds in this batch, and forms a natural procedural pair with [[提出]]: a citizen 提出s (submits) a form, and an office either 受理s (accepts it for processing) or — if incomplete or improperly filed — rejects it (不受理). The compound's bureaucratic precision — marking the exact legal moment at which a submission becomes "in process" — makes it a fixture of administrative and legal Japanese, Chinese, and Korean alike.
