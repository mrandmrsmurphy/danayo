## Notes
- [楼](../characters/楼.md) "watchtower, multistory building" + [閣](../characters/閣.md) "chamber, pavilion"

楼閣 refers to traditional East Asian elevated architecture — multistory towers and pavilions built of timber, associated with scenic overlooks, temples, and palace complexes. Both constituent characters denote structures raised above ground level: 楼 (lóu) a multi-story building or watchtower, 閣 (gé) a pavilion or elevated chamber often built on stilts or over water. Together they evoke the graceful, layered towers of classical Chinese and Japanese architecture — structures like the Yellow Crane Tower (黄鶴楼) or the various 閣 of Kyoto.

The compound is best known in the fixed phrase 空中楼閣 ("castle in the air"), where the beauty and grandeur of real 楼閣 architecture ironically underscores the unreality of things that exist only in imagination — the more magnificent the vision, the more revealing its lack of foundation. In Korean, 누각 (nugak) applies the initial sound rule (두음법칙), changing initial 루 to 누, and remains the standard term for traditional Korean elevated pavilions.
