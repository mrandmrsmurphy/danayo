## Notes
- [正](../characters/正%20(char).md) "correct" + [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "great"

正大 compounds 正 (correct, upright, orthodox) with 大 (great, grand), yielding something like "righteously great" — behaviour that is not merely correct but openly, unambiguously so. The word implies transparency as much as virtue: nothing hidden, nothing underhand. It appears most prominently in the chengyu [[公明正大]] (open, fair, and above board), where it reinforces the sense of acting without concealment.

In Dan'a'yo 正大 is used as a 性詞 (stative adjective). Note that the reversed form 大正 is reserved for the Japanese imperial era (大正, 1912–1926) and should not be used in this adjectival sense.
