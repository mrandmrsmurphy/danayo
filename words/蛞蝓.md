## Notes
- [蛞](../characters/蛞.md) "slug, snail" + [蝓](../characters/蝓.md) "slug" — stand-in for both [[蛞]] and [[蝓]], neither of which can appear independently.

蛞蝓 itself is the formal, written/biological term across Mandarin, Cantonese, and Japanese alike — everyday spoken usage in all three tends toward a vivid descriptive name instead: Cantonese and colloquial Mandarin both favor 鼻涕蟲/鼻涕虫 ("snot bug/worm," from the slug's mucus trail, kept here as an alias), while Japanese なめくじ is itself a native compound rather than a Sino-Japanese reading of the characters. Korean 민달팽이 breaks from the Sino root entirely — a fully native compound meaning "shell-less snail" (민-, "bare/without" + 달팽이, "snail"), with no Sino-Korean reading of 蛞蝓 in ordinary use at all. Vietnamese sên trần ("naked sên") follows the same "shell-less snail" logic as Korean — sên alone already covers snails and slug-like creatures, and trần ("bare, naked") marks the absence of a shell; a shorter form sên (or the variant sên lãi) is also used colloquially.
