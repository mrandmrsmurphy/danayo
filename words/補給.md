## Notes
- [補](../characters/補.md) "mend, repair, supplement" + [給](../characters/給.md) "supply, give" — to top something back up to its full level. This word is the viable form of [給](../characters/給.md): 給 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 補給 is its `stand_in` compound, kept deliberately general (matching 給's own broad "supply" gloss) in contrast to [[俸給]] (which narrows to wages specifically) or [[供給]] (the collapsed compound this split apart from, shared with [[提供]]).

補給 is the standard word across Mandarin (bǔjǐ) and Japanese (hokyū) for resupplying or replenishing something already in use — military logistics (補給線, supply line; 補給部隊, supply corps), refueling (燃料補給), or restocking depleted provisions generally. Korean 보급 covers the same ground, though it also carries a secondary, very common sense "to popularize, to spread" (普及 in hanja, a homophone collision with a different character pair entirely — 보급 from 普及 means "widespread adoption," e.g. of a technology, and is unrelated to 補給's "resupply" despite sounding identical in Korean).
