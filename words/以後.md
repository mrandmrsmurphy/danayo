## Notes
- [以 (char)](../characters/以%20(char).md) "by means of" + [後 (char)](../characters/後%20(char).md) "after; behind" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

Mandarin yǐhòu, Cantonese ji5 hau6, Japanese いご (igo), and Korean 이후 (ihu) are all the standard, everyday word for "after that, from then on" across the sphere — used both after a specific reference point (三点以後/삼시 이후, "after three o'clock") and standalone at the start of a clause ("from now on..."). Vietnamese dĩ hậu is a real, attested classical/formal Sino-Vietnamese phrase (verified via search, e.g. từ nay về sau, "from now on"), though modern everyday Vietnamese more often uses native sau đó/sau này for the same idea.
