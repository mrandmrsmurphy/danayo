## Notes
- [生](../characters/生.md) "life" + [日 (char)](../characters/日%20(char).md) "day, sun" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (生's own points to `生活`, 日's own to bare `日`); 生日 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/生.md` and `characters/日 (char).md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **Content removed**: `japanese: たんじょーび` and `aliases: [誕生日]` — 誕生日 (tanjōbi) is a genuinely different, longer three-character Japanese compound (誕 + 生 + 日, "birth-life-day"), not an alternate spelling of the two-character 生日. Japanese does not actually use the bare two-character 生日 for "birthday" in ordinary usage; no attested on'yomi reading of it (せいにち/しょうじつ, mechanically concatenated from `characters/生.md`'s SEI/SHOU and `characters/日 (char).md`'s NICHI/JITSU) is a real word, so `japanese` is left blank rather than fabricated.
