## Notes
- [肆](../characters/肆.md) "four (anti-forgery form)" + [拾](../characters/拾.md) "ten (anti-forgery form)" — the formal, fraud-resistant way of writing the numeral [[四十]] ("forty"), exactly as [[参拾]] is to [[三十]].

肆 stands in for 四 in the same "大寫數字" (formal/anti-forgery numeral) system as 參/参 for 三 — characters chosen because their complexity makes after-the-fact alteration of a written amount difficult or impossible. **肆拾** therefore carries the same meaning and reading as [[四十]]; the two differ only in which register of numeral character is used, with 肆拾 reserved for checks, contracts, ledgers, and other documents where a number's integrity matters.

[[四十]]'s own Notes observe that it is "an anagram of [[十四]]" (14) — the same is naturally true of 肆拾 relative to [[拾肆]] (a hypothetical formal writing of 14), though the formal numerals are far less often used for teen-numbers, where ambiguity and forgery risk are lower.
