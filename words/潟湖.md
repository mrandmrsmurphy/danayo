## Notes
- [潟](../characters/潟.md) "tidal flat, lagoon" + [湖](../characters/湖.md) "lake"

潟 denotes a tidal flat or shallow body of brackish water cut off from the open sea by a sandbar or reef — the character is built from the water radical 水 plus 舄 (a magpie, here purely phonetic), and survives in Japanese most visibly in place names like 新潟 (Niigata, "new lagoon/tidal flat"). 湖 is the general word for "lake," cross-linked from [[湖]]'s own compound [[湖水]].

潟湖 names the lagoon specifically as a coastal landform: a shallow lake-like body of water separated from the sea by a barrier island, sandbar, or coral reef, often connected to the ocean by a narrow inlet. According to Wiktionary, the Mandarin and Cantonese terms here are themselves orthographic borrowings from the Japanese geographical term 潟湖 (せきこ) — an example of a modern scientific/geographical vocabulary item that, unusually, flowed from Japanese into Chinese rather than the more typical reverse direction (as with most Sino-Japanese technical terms coined during the Meiji era).
