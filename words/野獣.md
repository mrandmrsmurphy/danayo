## Notes
- [野](../characters/野.md) "wild, countryside" + [獣](../characters/獣.md) "beast, mammal" — stand-in for [[獣]], which cannot appear independently

野獣 is "wild beast, savage animal" — pairing 野 "wild, untamed" with 獣 "beast, mammal" for a specifically undomesticated, often menacing animal, as opposed to the neutral general term [[動物]] "animal." 獣 cannot appear as an independent vault entry on its own, so 野獣 is what legitimizes it.

Mandarin 野獸/野兽 (yěshòu). Cantonese 野獸 (je5sau3). Japanese 野獣 (やじゅう, yajū) is a real, everyday word, used both literally (wild animals) and figuratively for a brutish or feral person — the character page's own Notes on 獣 record exactly that double sense ("beast with hair, mammal" / "brutish person, beastly person"). Korean 야수 (yasu) carries the same literal/figurative double sense. Vietnamese dã thú likewise covers both a literal wild beast and, figuratively, a brutal or savage person.

kwin is false: 野 alone matches its own Sino-Korean reading exactly (야=야), but 獣 does not (Dan'a'yo 슈 vs Korean 수 — a glide mismatch), so the compound comes out false under the AND-rule established earlier in this sweep.
