## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, obey" + [者 (char)](../characters/者%20(char).md) "-er, person"

従者 denotes a person who follows and serves another — an attendant, retainer, or member of a lord's entourage. In Japanese (じゅうしゃ, jūsha), the word covers the range from personal servants to feudal retainers to the modern sense of bodyguards or accompanying staff. It appears in historical fiction and role-playing contexts describing samurai retainers and noble attendants. In Chinese (从者, cóngzhě), the compound is classical and literary, appearing in historical texts to describe the followers accompanying a person of rank on a journey or in ceremony.
