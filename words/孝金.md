## Notes
**孝金** is a binomial neologism for the chemical element **Niobium** (Atomic Number: 41). It utilizes the character for **"Filial Piety"** or **"Child of"** to calque the Greek mythological relationship between [Niobe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/niobium#Etymology) and her father [Tantalus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/niobium#Etymology).

### 1. The Mythological Calque: The Daughter of Tantalus
As noted in the [Wiktionary etymology for Niobium](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/niobium#Etymology), the element was named after [Niobe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niobe), the daughter of Tantalus in Greek mythology.

- **The Reason:** This naming choice was made because Niobium is chemically almost identical to Tantalum and is [nearly always found alongside it](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niobium#History) in nature.
- **The Translation:** By using **孝** (Mandarin: _xiào_; Japanese: _kō_; Korean: _hyo_; Vietnamese: _hiếu_), which represents the virtue of **filial piety** or the "duty of a child to a parent," we are identifying Niobium as the "Child Metal" of Tantalum.

### 2. Physical and Chemical Affinity
Niobium and Tantalum are often referred to as "chemical twins."

- **The Property:** Their [atomic radiǝ are almost identical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niobium#Physical_properties) due to the lanthanide contraction, making them extremely difficult to distinguish or separate.
- **The Logic:** **孝金** captures this "closeness." Just as a child bears a strong resemblance to their parent, Niobium mirrors the properties of Tantalum (refractoriness, corrosion resistance) but is slightly lighter.

### 3. Single-Character Symbol: 孝
In the abbreviated periodic table, **孝** serves as a unique and culturally resonant symbol:

- **The Symbol:** **孝** (X)
- **The Formula:** A Niobium-Tantalum alloy—often found in the mineral [columbite-tantalite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coltan) (coltan)—could be rendered simply as **$孝渇$**. This formula elegantly expresses the "Child and Parent" or "Niobe and Tantalus" relationship present in the rock itself.
