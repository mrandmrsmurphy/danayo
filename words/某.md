## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; certain"

某 is "some; certain" — the indefinite quantifier in Dan'a'yo's correlative paradigm, generating 某事 (some matter; a certain thing), 某人 (someone; a certain person), 某処 (somewhere; a certain place), 某時 (at some time; once). 某 marks entities that exist but are deliberately left unspecified or unnamed — indefinite reference without the universal force of 毎 (every, each) or 皆 (all). In classical Chinese, 某 serves as a placeholder for a person or place whose identity the speaker withholds: 某人 (so-and-so; a certain person), 某地 (a certain place). 某 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[某]].

Mandarin 某 (mǒu): 某人 (mǒurén, a certain person; someone), 某處 (mǒuchù, somewhere), 某天 (mǒutiān, one day; some day). 某 also appears in classical Chinese as a self-deprecating first-person pronoun: "I, so-and-so" — the speaker anonymizes themselves before a superior. Cantonese 某 (mau5): literary; rarely used in colloquial speech. Japanese 某 (ぼう, bō; それがし, soregashi in archaic speech): used as an archaic self-reference ("I, the unnamed one") in classical or samurai-era literature; modern usage is confined to 某氏 (bōshi, a certain Mr. X) and formal or legal contexts. Korean 모 (某) in Sino-Korean: 모 씨 (a certain Mr. X; so-and-so), 모처 (某處, somewhere). Vietnamese mỗ (某): like Japanese それがし, used as an archaic self-deprecating pronoun in classical Vietnamese literature.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 못 differs from Korean 모. 某 reads 못 (mou/ㄇㄛㄨ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 明母 (m) + 侯韻 (əu) gives Dan'a'yo a diphthong -ou (ㄛㄨ), written 못 in Hangul — while Sino-Korean gives the plain vowel 모 (mo), without the -u offglide. The Dan'a'yo form preserves the MC compound-vowel quality of 侯韻 where Sino-Korean monophthongizes to -o.
