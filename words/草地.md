## Notes
- [草 (char)](../characters/草%20(char).md) "grass" + [地 (char)](../characters/地%20(char).md) "land" — a smaller-scale grassy area (lawn, meadow), as opposed to [[草原]], the large-scale grassland/prairie/steppe formed with the same first root plus 原 ("field, plain").

草地/초지/くさち all carry the same small-vs-large distinction against their 草原-type sibling — Japanese explicitly marks this as a matter of scale, not a strict category boundary (草地 for smaller patches, 草原 for large plains), and the pattern holds loosely across the family. Vietnamese đồng cỏ (native, "grass field") is the everyday word for this general concept in Vietnamese, paralleling the same scale distinction against thảo nguyên ("grassland, prairie"); the literal Sino-Vietnamese reading thảo địa exists but isn't the term reached for in ordinary speech.
