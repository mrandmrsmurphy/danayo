## Notes
- [太 (char)](../characters/太%20(char).md) "great; extreme" + [陽 (char)](../characters/陽%20(char).md) "shine" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters).

Mandarin tàiyáng, Cantonese taai3 joeng4, Japanese たいよう (taiyō), Korean 태양 (taeyang), and Vietnamese thái dương are all the standard, everyday word for "the sun" across the sphere — the literal "great yang/sunshine," 太 emphasizing the sun's supremacy among heavenly bodies. Vietnamese thái dương additionally survives in the compound thái dương hệ, "solar system," and in anatomical use (thái dương, "temple," the side of the head) — a coincidental match to the same 太陽穴/たいようけつ ("temple") usage of these characters elsewhere in the sphere.
