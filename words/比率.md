## Notes
- [比 (char)](../characters/比%20(char).md) "to compare; than" + [率](../characters/率.md) "rate; ratio; to lead"

比率 pairs 比 (to compare, to put side by side — graphemically two people standing parallel to each other) with 率 (rate, ratio, proportion — the measure of one thing against another) to express the mathematical concept of ratio: a comparison of two quantities expressed as a fraction or proportion. The compound is transparently compositional: 比 names the act of comparison, and 率 names the quantitative expression of its result.

率 is notable for its multiple readings in modern Chinese (lǜ for ratio/rate; shuài for to lead, a general, or a fraction of troops) and in Sino-Japanese (RITSU for ratio, SOTSU for leadership/general). These two readings stem from distinct original meanings that happened to be written with the same character, creating the unusual situation where 比率 (bǐlǜ) and 将率 (jiàngshuài, commander) use the same character differently. In mathematical vocabulary, 率 appears widely: 比率 (ratio), 確率 (probability), 効率 (efficiency), 利率 (interest rate), 税率 (tax rate), and 成長率 (growth rate) all use 率 in this sense.

In Japanese, ひりつ (hiritsu) is standard for mathematical ratio; everyday expressions of proportion often use the native suffix 割 (-wari, tenths: 七割 = 70%). Korean 비율 (biyul) is standard in mathematics and statistics. Vietnamese tỉ lệ uses Sino-Vietnamese forms. The kwin=false marking reflects the divergence between Dan'a'yo 븨룯 (biǝ + lud) and Korean 비율 (biyul) — 比 maps to 비 in Korean but to 븨 in Dan'a'yo, a consistent vowel difference.
