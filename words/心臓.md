## Notes
- [心 (char)](../characters/心%20(char).md) "heart" + [臓](../characters/臓.md) "organ"

Vietnamese shows a real, interesting doublet from the same character 心: tim (this word's field, the concrete anatomical "heart" as an organ) and tâm (the more abstract "heart/mind," as in tâm hồn "soul") both descend from 心, split by register/sense rather than being alternate spellings of the same thing — `characters/心 (char).md`'s own `vietnamese` field lists both. Mandarin xīnzàng, Cantonese sam1 zong6, Japanese しんぞう (shinzō), and Korean 심장 (simjang) are all the standard clinical/everyday term for the heart as an organ across the sphere, with 臓/臟 marking it specifically as an internal organ (compare 内臓, 五臓六府).
