## Notes
- [懐 (char)](../characters/懐%20(char).md) "to miss, hold dear in one's heart" + [愁](../characters/愁.md) "worry, sorrow, melancholy"

A classical compound for the wistful ache of missing someone or something distant in time or space. 懐 carries the sense of cherishing something inward — holding close in the breast — while 愁 is the specific coloring of sorrowful anxiety. Together they describe a pining grief, the emotion one feels gazing at a familiar landscape or reading an old letter. The compound appears throughout Tang and Song poetry.

The two CJKV traditions that most use this word have an unfortunate homophone collision: in both Japanese and Korean, かいしゅう (kaishū) and 회수 (hoisu) are perfect homophones of 回収 (かいしゅう / 회수, "collection, retrieval, reclaiming"). A sentence like 彼への懐愁 could be heard as "nostalgic grief for him" or "the collection of him" — both phonetically identical. This collision likely explains why 懐愁 is confined to literary and classical registers in both languages, rather than entering everyday speech.

In Chinese, 懷愁 (traditional form; 懐 = 懷 + 愁) avoids the collision since Mandarin huáichóu shares no overlap with 回收 (huíshōu), making the compound more viable in Chinese literary usage.
