## Notes
- [六 (char)](../characters/六%20(char).md) "six" + [楽](../characters/楽.md) "music; entertainment" — the Six Musics, the second of the Six Arts (六芸) of Zhou-dynasty noble education.

六楽 (liùyuè) is the second item in the **《周禮·地官司徒·保氏》** curriculum, immediately following [[五礼]]. According to **《周禮·春官宗伯·大司樂》**, the Grand Music Master taught six ceremonial dance-and-music suites, traditionally enumerated as:

1. **雲門大卷** — performed in sacrifices to Heaven
2. **大咸** (also given as 大韶 in some lists) — performed in sacrifices to Earth
3. **大磬** (大韶) — associated with the sage-king Shun
4. **大夏** — associated with Yu the Great and the Xia dynasty
5. **大濩** — associated with Tang of Shang
6. **大武** — associated with King Wu of Zhou's conquest

The first four are traditionally classed as "civil" (文) dances commemorating peaceful succession, while 大濩 and 大武 are "martial" (武) dances commemorating conquest.

A crucial point of polysemy: the character 楽 has two unrelated Middle Chinese readings — *lè* "joy, entertainment" (Dan'a'yo's fixed reading for this character, 諺文 락, as in [[娯楽]]) and *yuè* "music" (as in 音樂/音楽). [[六楽|六楽]] belongs to the *yuè* reading, not the *lè* reading that the character page records as 楽's primary Dan'a'yo identity. This word therefore deliberately uses the *yuè*-family readings — Sino-Japanese がく (GAKU), Sino-Korean 악 (ak), Sino-Vietnamese nhạc, Cantonese ngok6 — for its `mandarin`/`cantonese`/`japanese`/`korean`/`vietnamese` fields, while its `諺文`/`羅馬字`/`注音` fields remain mechanically tied to 楽's fixed Dan'a'yo phonology (룩락/luglag/ㄌㄨㄎㄌㄚㄎ). This is the same kind of reading-divergence noted on [[波蘭]], where a word's CJKV pronunciation can diverge from a constituent character's fixed Dan'a'yo transcription.

As one of the Six Arts, 六楽 sits alongside [[五礼]], [[五射]], [[五馭]], [[六書]], and [[九数]].
