## Notes
- [交 (char)](../characters/交%20(char).md) "mix, exchange" + [友](../characters/友.md) "friend" — to mix with and become friends; the narrower, more everyday member of the pair.

交友 names the individual friendship itself — the bond of trust and closeness between people — and is the ordinary, unmarked word for "making friends" in all four modern languages. Its near-homophone [[交遊]] describes the broader activity of socializing across a wide circle rather than any one bond; the two are close enough in sense that they're often glossed identically in bilingual dictionaries, but 交遊廣闊 ("having vast social connections") could not be written with 交友 instead — that fixed phrase specifically needs the network sense, not the friendship sense.

The collision between them is not a Dan'a'yo artifact: 友 and 遊 are true Middle Chinese homophones (以母尤韻), separated only by tone in their daughter languages (Mandarin yǒu/yóu, Cantonese jau5/jau4). Japanese never preserved that tone distinction, so 交友 and 交遊 are already homophones there too — both こうゆう — meaning Dan'a'yo inherits a real historical collision rather than manufacturing a new one. Korean keeps 우 and 유 distinct, so 교우 and 교유 remain separable there.
