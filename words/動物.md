## Notes
- [動 (char)](../characters/動%20(char).md) "move" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing" — literally "moving thing," the natural criterion distinguishing animal life from plant life in the compound's own construction.

動物 is "animal" — the general biological category, as opposed to 植物 "plant" (built the same way, from 植 "plant, grow" + 物). Unlike several of the family/kinship words earlier in this sweep, 動物 has no competing native word crowding it out — it's the plain, everyday, unmarked term in every language here.

Mandarin 動物/动物 (dòngwù). Cantonese 動物 (dung6mat6). Japanese 動物 (どうぶつ, dōbutsu) is equally everyday — 動物園 (dōbutsuen, "zoo"), 動物病院 (dōbutsu byōin, "veterinary clinic"). Korean 동물 (dongmul) is likewise the standard word — 동물원 (dongmurwon, "zoo") mirrors the Japanese compound exactly. Vietnamese động vật is the everyday Sino-Vietnamese term, alongside the semantically narrower native con vật ("creature, beast," often with a slightly more informal or diminutive flavor).

kwin is true: both 動 and 物 individually match their own Sino-Korean readings exactly (동=동, 물=물), so the compound comes out true.
