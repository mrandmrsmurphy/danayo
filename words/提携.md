## Notes
- [提](../characters/提.md) "to raise, hold" + [携](../characters/携.md) "to carry, take with"

提携 means "to form an alliance, to cooperate, to join hands" — most often in business contexts (業務提携, "business tie-up") but extendable to any partnership between individuals, organizations, or nations. 携, a 形声 character (semantic 手 "hand" + phonetic 巂), means "to carry, to take along, to lead by the hand," already seen in its only other cross-linked compound, [[携帯]] ("to carry with one; portable; mobile phone" — the word that gave Japanese ケータイ, "cell phone," cross-linked from [[携]]'s Words section).

The compound's literal sense — "to raise [hands] and carry [together]" — paints a vivid image of two parties literally joining hands, a gesture of partnership that maps neatly onto the abstract "alliance" or "tie-up" meaning dominant in modern usage. 提携 thus completes a small cluster of 提-compounds in this batch ([[提供]], [[提出]], [[提督]], [[提琴]]) that move from 提's literal "to raise with the hand" toward increasingly abstract senses of presenting, directing, and — here — joining forces.
