## Notes
- [玉](../characters/玉%20(char).md) "jade; ball; sphere" + [葱](../characters/葱.md) "scallion; onion; leek" — stand-in for [[葱]], which cannot appear independently
- 玉葱 is, at heart, a Japanese coinage: たまねぎ (tamanegi) names the onion as a "ball-shaped scallion," its round bulb (玉, "jade; sphere") set against the long green leek-family stalk (葱) it visibly grows from. The plant itself arrived comparatively late and from far away — native to Persia/Central Asia, it reached Japan only in the early Meiji period — and the descriptive, transparent compound 玉葱 was coined fresh to name an unfamiliar vegetable in familiar terms, rather than inherited as an old Sino-Japanese loan. That novelty shows in how differently the rest of the Sinosphere names the same plant: Mandarin 洋葱 (yángcōng) and Korean 양파 (yangpa) both foreground 洋/양 "foreign; Western," marking the onion explicitly as an outsider crop rather than describing its shape — two cultures naming the same import by where it came from, and one naming it by what it looks like.
