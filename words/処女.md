## Notes
- [処 (char)](../characters/処%20(char).md) "place; to dwell" + [女 (char)](../characters/女%20(char).md) "woman"

処女 means "virgin, maiden" — an unmarried woman, by extension the constellation/zodiac sign Virgo (処女座), and attributively "maiden, inaugural" (処女作, "a debut work"; 処女航海, "maiden voyage"). The compound is built here on the canonical character 処 — itself an alias-cluster covering 処/处/處, all variants of the same morpheme meaning "place, to reside, to be situated" — with the more common real-world spellings 处女 (simplified) and 處女 (traditional) recorded as word-level aliases, following the same alias-parent pattern used for [[坐位]] earlier in this project.

女, a 象形 pictograph of a kneeling figure with folded arms (detailed in [[女 (char)]]'s own Notes), is one of the most productive characters in the script, anchoring [[魔女]] ("witch"), [[女優]] ("actress"), and the [[妓女]]/[[舞妓]] pair later in this batch. 処女's literal sense — "a woman dwelling [at home, unmarried]" — reflects an etymology shared with many world languages' words for "maiden," where unmarried women are characterized by their continued residence in the parental household (cf. English "maiden" itself, related to "maid" and household service).
