## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big" + [笑 (char)](../characters/笑%20(char).md) "laugh, smile"

大笑 denotes laughter that is loud, unrestrained, and whole-hearted — a belly laugh or guffaw, rather than a polite chuckle. The compound is used both as a verb (to burst out laughing) and as a noun (uproarious laughter) across the CJKV languages.

In Japanese, two readings exist: たいしょう (taishō, on'yomi) is the standard literary compound, while おおわらい (ōwarai, kun'yomi, "big laugh") is the colloquial and comedic form — the latter now strongly associated with the Osaka entertainment industry, where 大笑い (ōwarai) is the umbrella term for stand-up comedy and comedic performance. In Korean (대소), the word appears in literary and formal writing; everyday speech more often uses 폭소 (poksso, "explosive laughter") or 웃음 (usŭm). In Mandarin (dàxiào), it is the natural verb for uncontrolled laughter and appears widely in narrative prose and spoken language.
