## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

某時 is the indefinite temporal correlative — "sometime; at a certain unspecified point in time." Like all 某-column entries, it designates a referent whose identity is deliberately withheld: an event happened (or will happen) "at some time," but the speaker cannot or will not specify when. This distinguishes 某時 from the interrogative 何時 (asking when) and the distal 彼時 (pointing to a known but remote past time). 某時 covers both forward-looking indefiniteness (sometime in the future) and backward-looking vagueness (at some point in the past).

Mandarin 某時 (mǒu shí, "at a certain time") is attested in formal Chinese: 案件某時發生 ("the incident occurred at a certain time"); 請在某時出席 ("please attend at a specified time"). Japanese いつか (itsuka, "sometime; someday") is the natural spoken equivalent: いつかまた会いましょう ("let's meet again sometime"); いつか分かる日が来る ("a day will come when you understand"). Korean 언젠가 (eonjenga, "someday; at some point") covers both future and past indefiniteness: 언젠가 그때가 올 거야 ("someday that time will come"); 언젠가 그런 일이 있었어 ("there was such a thing at some point in the past").

The original Korean field 때때로 (ttaettaero, "sometimes, occasionally") was a mistranslation — 때때로 implies recurrence at multiple occasions (sometimes = on some occasions), while 某時 is a single indefinite occasion (sometime = at one unspecified point). The correct equivalent is 언젠가 (a single indefinite point in time) or 어떤 때 (a certain time). 某時 belongs with the other single-referent indefinite correlatives (某人, 某物, 某処) rather than with frequentative adverbs.
