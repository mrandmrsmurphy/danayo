## Notes
- [昨 (char)](../characters/昨%20(char).md) "last, previous" + [日 (char)](../characters/日%20(char).md) "day, sun" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 昨日 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/昨 (char).md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/日 (char).md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **Corrected two wrong-word conflations**: `mandarin`/`cantonese` had held the readings of the sibling word 昨天 (zuótiān/zok3 tin1 — using 天 "day," not 日 "sun/day") rather than 昨日's own; corrected to `zuórì`/`zok6 jat6`, matching `characters/日 (char).md`'s own stored `jat6`. `korean` had held `어제`, the native Korean word for "yesterday" (also stored on `characters/昨 (char).md` as its own `korean_native` gloss) rather than the genuine Sino-Korean compound reading; corrected to `작일` (verified via search — a real, attested, if formal/literary word for "yesterday," distinct from everyday native 어제).
- Japanese きのう is the standard, everyday word for "yesterday" — correct as-is. Vietnamese does not use a Sino-Vietnamese calque of 昨日 at all; the real, standard modern term is the native hôm qua. No attested reading of 昨日 itself is in actual use, so `vietnamese` is left blank rather than fabricated.
