## Notes
- [消 (char)](../characters/消%20(char).md) "extinguish, dissolve" + [防](../characters/防.md) "prevent, defend" + [局 (char)](../characters/局%20(char).md) "bureau, department" — fire department; the institutional body responsible for firefighting and fire prevention

消防 pairs the two complementary responses to fire: 消 (to extinguish what has already ignited) and 防 (to prevent ignition and contain spread). Adding 局 designates this activity as an organized government bureau. The compound is used across the Dan'a'yo sphere with consistent meaning, though institutional names vary: Japan distinguishes 消防庁 (Fire and Disaster Management Agency, the national body) from 消防局 (city-level fire service organizations) from 消防署 (individual fire stations). Korean uses 소방서 (消防署) for the local fire station. Dan'a'yo 消防局 covers the institution at any organizational level.

The 消防 compound itself is a Meiji-era Japanese coinage that was adopted into Chinese and Korean, a common pattern for institutional vocabulary in the modern Sinitic sphere.
