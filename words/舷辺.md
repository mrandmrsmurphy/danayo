## Notes
- [舷](../characters/舷.md) "side of a boat, gunwale" + [辺](../characters/辺.md) "side, edge, border"
- This word is the stand-in for [[舷]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A compound describing the gunwale — the upper edge of a ship's side where hull meets deck. 舷 (ふなばた funanabata in Japanese) is the specific nautical term for the ship's side, and figures in directional compounds: 左舷 (さげん sagen, port) and 右舷 (うげん ugen, starboard). In Chinese, 舷 similarly appears in 舷窗 (xiánchuāng "porthole") and 舷梯 (xiántī "gangway"). The addition of 辺 "edge, side" reinforces the boundary sense — the margin where boat ends and sea begins. Korean 현변 is the hanja reading; the native term is 뱃전 (baetjeon "ship's side").
