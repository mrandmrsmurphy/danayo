## Notes
- [只 (char)](../characters/只%20(char).md) "only; just; merely"

只 is the exclusive topic-retaining particle in Dan'a'yo — "only; just; merely; simply" — suffixing to a noun phrase to restrict the predicate to that noun exclusively: 我只 (only I; it's only me), 一冊只 (just one volume; only one book). Among the Dan'a'yo topic-retaining particles (関詞), 只 marks exclusivity — narrowing the scope to precisely and only the marked noun — contrasting with [[且]] (additionally), which broadens scope by inclusion. In classical Chinese, 只 (OC *tɕiʔ) was originally a demonstrative or terminal particle; its exclusive adverbial meaning "only; merely" is the dominant function in Dan'a'yo. 只 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[只]].

The comparison chart: 只 parallels Mandarin 只/仅 (zhǐ/jǐn, only), Cantonese 淨/只 (zing6/zi2), Japanese だけ (dake, only; just), Korean 만 (man, only), Vietnamese chỉ (only; just). Mandarin 只 (zhǐ): 只有你 (only you), 只是 (it's just that; only), 只好 (can only; have no choice but to); 仅 (jǐn) is a more literary synonym. Cantonese 淨係 (zing6 hai6, only is): 淨係佢 (only he/she), 只係 (zi2 hai6, it's only). Japanese だけ (dake): 私だけ (only I; just me), それだけ (only that; that's all); のみ (nomi) is the literary equivalent. Korean 만 (man): 나만 (only I), 이것만 (only this one), 한 번만 (just once). Vietnamese chỉ: Chỉ tôi thôi (only I, that's all), chỉ có một (there is only one).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 처 does not match Korean 지. 只 reads 처 (ce/ㄑㄝ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 支三開韻 (iᴇ) gives an aspirated palatal affricate with the -e vowel. Dan'a'yo maps MC 章母 to ㄑ (aspirated ch-, ㅊ in Hangul) and MC 支三開韻 to the mid -e vowel (ㄝ/처), while Sino-Korean 지 (ji) maps the same MC initial to ㅈ (unaspirated j-) and the same MC final to the high -i vowel. Both the initial (aspirated ㅊ vs unaspirated ㅈ) and the vowel (-e vs -i) differ — the same double divergence seen in [[是]] (ㄙㄝ/서 vs 시), which shares the same MC final 支三開韻.
