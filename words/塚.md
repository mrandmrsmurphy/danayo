## Notes
塚/총/trủng name a raised burial mound, but only Vietnamese trủng has visibly slipped into archaic/poetic register in ordinary usage — it survives mainly in classical verse (Nguyễn Du's 青塚, "green grave-mound," referring to Wang Zhaojun's legendary tomb), with modern Vietnamese reaching for mộ or the native gò mả instead. Mandarin, Cantonese, and Korean all keep 塚/총 in active, if somewhat literary, use — most visibly in 古塚 ("ancient burial mound") and 貝塚 (kaizuka in Japanese, "shell mound," a standard archaeological term for a midden of discarded shells).
