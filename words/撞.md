## Notes
- [撞 (char)](../characters/撞%20(char).md) "hit; bump; collide" — 撞 stands alone as a Dan'a'yo verb, the same way it functions as an ordinary transitive verb in Mandarin (撞車 "to crash a car," 撞見 "to run into someone by chance," 撞倒 "to knock down"). It is common, everyday vocabulary in Chinese (HSK 2) but comparatively marginal in Japanese and Korean, where it survives mainly in a handful of Sino-xenic compounds (Japanese 撞着 dōchaku "contradiction," 撞球 dōkyū "billiards") rather than as productive native vocabulary — a lopsided distribution that still argues for treating it as real 先進-level vocabulary rather than a name-only character, given how central it is on the Chinese side.
