## Notes
- [詔](../characters/詔.md) "imperial edict; proclaim" + [書](../characters/書.md) "document; book" — stand-in for [[詔]], which cannot appear independently
- The compound denotes a written imperial edict or proclamation, and the bare character carries its full classical meaning only here. The same compound, with the same sense, is shared across Mandarin (zhàoshū), Cantonese, Japanese (しょうしょ), Korean (조서/joseo), and Sino-Vietnamese (chiếu thư), making it an unusually stable pan-Sinospheric term for a now-historical institution: the formal written proclamations issued by an emperor, traditionally counted with the measure word 道.
