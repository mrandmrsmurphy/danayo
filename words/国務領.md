## Notes
- [国](../characters/国.md) "nation" + [務](../characters/務.md) "affairs; duty" + [領](../characters/領.md) "lead; territory"

**国務領** (Korean: 국무령, *Gungmuryeong*) was the title of the head of state of the [[大韓民国]] 臨時政府 (Provisional Government of the Republic of Korea) during its [[臨時]] constitutional period of 1925–1927. The position was created by a constitutional revision that moved the exile government away from the presidential 大統領 model toward a less personalised executive, amid factional tensions within the independence movement. The most notable holder was Kim Gu (金九, 1876–1949), who later served as Chairman (主席) of the provisional government and became the dominant figure of the Korean independence movement.

The title was short-lived: the system reverted to a collective council structure in 1927. As a result, 国務領 stands as a historically unique office — held by only two or three individuals during the three years it existed — and is rarely encountered outside Korean historiography of the provisional government period.

The CJKV readings above are reconstructed from the component characters; 국무령 is the attested Korean form and the word's natural home.
