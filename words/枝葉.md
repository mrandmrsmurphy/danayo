## Notes
- [枝](../characters/枝.md) "branch; bough" + [葉 (char)](../characters/葉%20(char).md) "leaf" — stand-in for [[枝]], which cannot appear independently
- Literally "branches and leaves," the compound carries the same double life across the Sinosphere: a plain botanical description of a tree's outer growth, and by extension the parts of any matter that are secondary, peripheral, or merely decorative — the things that grow off the trunk rather than forming it. Japanese keeps both the on'yomi しよう (shiyō) and a native kun'yomi reading えだは (edaha) for the literal sense, while Mandarin zhīyè, Cantonese zi1 jip6, and Sino-Korean 지엽 (jiyeop) lean toward the figurative "nonessential details" — as in the common derived term 지엽적, "peripheral, beside the point."
