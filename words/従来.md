## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, continuing from" + [来 (char)](../characters/来%20(char).md) "come"

従来 describes what has been the case up to now — the existing state of affairs, the conventional approach, the way things have been done. It is used attributively (従来の方法, "the conventional method"; 従来通り, "as before, in the same way as always") and as a standalone adverb ("hitherto"). The compound implies continuity from the past to the present, with an implicit contrast to some new or proposed change: 従来の技術では不十分だ ("the existing technology is insufficient").

In Chinese (从来, cónglái), the simplified form is common and means "from beginning to end, always, at any time" — often in negative constructions (从来没有, "has never"), emphasizing the totality of a time span. This is somewhat different from the Japanese sense of "conventional, as before." In Korean (종래), the word appears in formal and academic writing with the Japanese-influenced sense of "up to now, conventional."
