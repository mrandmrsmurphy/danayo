## Notes
- [傾](../characters/傾.md) "lean; incline" + [向 (char)](../characters/向%20(char).md) "toward; direction"
- This word is the viable form of [傾](../characters/傾.md): the character's meaning (to lean, to incline) is identical, but 傾 alone is too bounded to stand as a Dan'a'yo word. 傾向 is its `stand_in` compound.

The compound pairs the action of leaning (傾) with its target direction (向) to produce the abstract noun "the way something leans" — a tendency, an inclination, a drift. What began as a concrete physical image has fully grammaticalised into the everyday vocabulary of preference, politics, and personality.

傾向 is a core term across the CJKV sphere. In Chinese it describes political leanings (政治傾向), ideological drift, and personal predispositions. In Japanese けいこう is equally ubiquitous — 〜する傾向がある ("there is a tendency to...") is a fixed analytical frame in academic and journalistic writing. Korean 경향 (傾向) appears in the same contexts, and 경향신문 ("Gyeonghyang Sinmun") is one of South Korea's major national newspapers, named for the idea of inclination or orientation. Vietnamese khuynh hướng preserves the Sino-Vietnamese reading and is used in the same registers as its cognates.

The simplified Chinese alias is 倾向, reflecting the simplified form of 傾.
