## Notes
- [猿](../characters/猿.md) "monkey" + [楽](../characters/楽.md) "entertainment" — sarugaku, a form of Japanese theater popular in the 11th–14th centuries, ancestor of Noh and Kyōgen. A Japanese cultural concept, but the characters are read with their own native pronunciation in Mandarin (yuányuè) and Cantonese (jyun4 lok6) — not a phonetic transliteration of the Japanese word — making it an internationally readable term, the same naturalization pattern as 浴衣.
