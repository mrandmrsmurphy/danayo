## Notes
- [有 (char)](../characters/有%20(char).md) "have" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name" — literally "to have a name [that people know]." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 有名 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/有 (char).md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/名 (char).md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- A clean, directly parallel compound across the whole sphere — Mandarin yǒumíng, Cantonese jau5 meng4-2 (名 takes a colloquial changed-tone reading in this compound, a recognized Cantonese tone-sandhi pattern, not an error), Japanese ゆうめい, and Korean 유명 all preserve the Sino compound directly. Vietnamese danh tiếng ("fame; renowned") is the everyday idiomatic choice; the fully compositional hữu danh (有 hữu + 名 danh) also exists and is attested (e.g. in the idiom 有名無實/hữu danh vô thực, "famous in name only") but is comparatively more formal/literary.
