## Notes
- [平](../characters/平.md) "even; peaceful" + [凡](../characters/凡.md) "ordinary; simple" — stand-in for [[凡]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.

平凡 pairs two near-synonymous characters: 平 "even, level" and 凡 "ordinary, common." Both can independently carry the sense of "commonplace," and the compound doubly reinforces ordinariness. It is attested in Classical Chinese and has spread evenly across the Sinosphere.

凡 is among the oldest Chinese characters — its oracle-bone and bronze-inscribed form depicted a flat tray or basin, the concrete embodiment of "flat and ordinary" (象形). That physical origin still faintly shapes the word's feel: 平凡 connotes not just "not special" but a kind of groundedness, a flatness of circumstance.

In Modern Mandarin, 平凡 (píngfán) is neutral-to-humble, often used without judgment. Its antonym 非凡 (fēifán) "extraordinary" — built on the same 凡 — highlights how central 凡 is to the entire axis of ordinary/extraordinary in Chinese. In Japanese, 平凡 (へいぼん heibōn) skews slightly negative: 平凡な人 is a person of no particular distinction, and the word frequently appears in self-deprecating or resigned contexts. In Korean, the compound reads 평범 (pyeong-beom) — a standard Sino-Korean reading, neutral in register, with no strong pejorative edge. The divergence between the Dan'a'yo bound form 병봄 and the Korean 평범 accounts for kwin: false.
