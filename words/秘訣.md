## Notes
- [秘](../characters/秘.md) "secret, mysterious" + [訣](../characters/訣.md) "secret, trick" — stand-in for [[訣]], which cannot appear independently

秘訣 denotes a secret formula or method that produces excellent results: the private recipe of a craftsman, the technique behind a great cook's dish, the mental habits of an expert. It differs meaningfully from [[秘密]] (a hidden fact kept from others) in that 秘訣 is practical and generative — not merely withheld information but a key that unlocks a skill. The word is built from 秘 (secret, mysterious) and 訣 (a concise formula or mnemonic rule), the latter a character that already implies a distilled body of knowledge passed privately between teacher and student.

In classical Chinese usage 訣 referred to brief mnemonic verses used in medicine, martial arts, and Daoist practice — hence 口訣 (oral formula). When compounded with 秘, the emphasis shifts from the form (a mnemonic) to the epistemic status (closely guarded). The compound appears in texts from the Tang dynasty onward as a polished literary term.

In Japanese ひけつ carries the same practical nuance and is the normal literary term for "secret of success" or "the trick to doing something well." In Korean 비결 is similarly the standard word for a secret method or recipe — notably used in cooking, martial arts, and business contexts. Vietnamese bí quyết, borrowed as a Sino-Vietnamese compound, is fully integrated into everyday speech and is the standard translation for English "secret" in the sense of "the secret to X."
