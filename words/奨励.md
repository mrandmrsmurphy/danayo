## Notes
- [奨](../characters/奨.md) "prize, commend, promote" + [励](../characters/励.md) "strive, exhort, encourage" — to prize and exhort; to incentivise through commendation.

奨励 combines two distinct vectors of motivation into one compound: 奨 (the act of conferring recognition or reward) and 励 (the internal effort or the exhortation to exert effort). Together they describe not just giving a prize but the full dynamic of institutional encouragement — conferring recognition in order to motivate continued or greater effort.

This dual structure makes 奨励 the natural word for formal incentive programmes: 奨励金 (incentive grant, research award), 奨励策 (incentive policy), 奨励賞 (encouragement prize). The 奨励賞 is a notably East Asian institutional form: formally below first prize, it is awarded to works or individuals whose effort or promise warrants recognition even without winning. Rather than marking failure to win, it marks a deliberate act of encouragement — the institution expressing confidence in future work.

In contrast with the near-synonyms 奨学 (scholarship: support for learning) and 奨励 (incentive: recognition to drive performance), 奨励 clusters toward performance and production rather than study. 奨励対象 (target of incentivisation) and 奨励措置 (incentive measure) are common in policy and regulatory language across the Sinosphere.

Korean 장려 (奬勵, jangnyeo) follows the same usage. Mandarin 奖励 (jiǎnglì) uses the simplified form and appears in both administrative and everyday speech — 奖励机制 (incentive mechanism) is a standard economic and policy term.
