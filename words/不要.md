## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [要](../characters/要.md) "want, need, important" — literally "not want", descriptively ("unnecessary").

This compound is a genuine false friend across the family. Mandarin 不要 (bùyào) spans two senses — it is the ordinary way to issue a negative command ("不要动!," "don't move!") and can also describe something as not needed. Japanese 不要 (fuyō) and Korean 불요 kept only the descriptive half of that range: both mean strictly "unnecessary, not needed" (不要品/불요불급, "unneeded items"/"neither urgent nor necessary") and neither functions as a prohibitive command — Japanese uses な/でください forms and Korean uses -지 마 for "don't" instead, never 不要/불요. Vietnamese bất yếu is the Hán Việt reading and is attested with the prohibitive sense on paper, but living spoken Vietnamese reaches for native đừng or không được for "don't," not bất yếu.
