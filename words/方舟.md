## Notes
- [方](../characters/方.md) "square, rectangular" + [舟](../characters/舟.md) "boat, vessel" — a rectangular vessel; an ark.

方舟 in its most common modern usage refers to Noah's Ark (挪亞方舟, Nuòyǎ Fāngzhōu), the vessel described in Genesis 6–9. The compound captures the ark's defining characteristic as a box-shaped barge rather than a sleek-hulled ship: 方 emphasises its rectangular, chest-like form. This accords with the Hebrew תֵּבָה (tēvāh, "box, chest"), the word used in Genesis for both Noah's ark and the basket in which the infant Moses was floated on the Nile.

The Japanese はこぶね (箱舟, "box-boat") is structurally identical: 箱 (hako, "box, chest") + 舟 (fune, "boat"). Chinese and Japanese converged independently on the same image — the ark as a floating storage vessel rather than a navigational craft. This is also consistent with the biblical description, which gives the ark's proportions (300 × 50 × 30 cubits) as those of a rectangular barge optimised for buoyancy, not sailing.

方舟 is also used for the Ark of the Covenant (約櫃, Yuēguì) in some literary contexts, though the Covenant Ark is more precisely 约柜 in Chinese. In Dan'a'yo, 方舟 functions as the general term for any biblical "ark," with context disambiguating between the two.

The word also appears in secular scientific contexts: 方舟 is the name of several conservation and environmental programmes, echoing the biblical metaphor of preservation through catastrophe.
