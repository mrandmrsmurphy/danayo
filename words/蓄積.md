## Notes
- [蓄 (char)](../characters/蓄%20(char).md) "to store; to save up" + [積](../characters/積.md) "to pile up; to amass" — stand-in for [[積]], which cannot appear independently
- A near-synonym compound — "to store" plus "to pile up" — naming the gradual buildup of anything that grows through repeated small additions: wealth, knowledge, experience, sediment, fatigue. Mandarin xùjī, Cantonese cuk1 zik1, Japanese ちくせき (chikuseki), and Sino-Korean 축적 (chukjeok, source of the common verb 축적하다) all keep this core "accumulation" sense intact and central. Sino-Vietnamese súc tích is the outlier: the same two morphemes drifted from "amassed" to "compact, condensed" — describing speech or writing that packs a great deal into a small space, the linguistic residue of the same root image of things piling up densely together.
