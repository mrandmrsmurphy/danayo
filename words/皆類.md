## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [類](../characters/類.md) "kind; sort; category; type"

皆類 is the universal kind/category correlative — "every kind; every sort; all categories." The character 皆 marks collective universality across the full range of types: 皆類の動物が展示されている ("animals of every kind are on display"); 皆類の問題に対処できる ("capable of handling every type of problem"). This is the collective counterpart to 毎類 (each kind one by one) — 皆類 asserts that the predicate holds across all categories simultaneously as a complete set.

Mandarin 各類 (gèlèi, "all categories; every kind") or the more emphatic 各種各類 (gèzhǒng gèlèi, "all sorts and kinds; every conceivable type") are the natural equivalents. Japanese あらゆる種類 (arayuru shurui, "every kind/type; all manner of kinds") is the formal equivalent; 各種 (kakushu, "various kinds; all kinds") is the more compact everyday form. Korean 모든 종류 (modeun jongryu, "all kinds/types; every category") is the standard universal category expression. Vietnamese mọi loại (every type/kind) is the direct counterpart.

皆類 is the final entry in the Dan'a'yo correlative table. Together with 皆事, 皆物, 皆人, 皆名, 皆処, 皆時, 皆様, and 皆多, it completes the universal 皆 column — a full set of collective universals across every semantic dimension of the grid: matter, object, person, honorific person, place, time, manner, quantity, and kind. The 7×9 Dan'a'yo correlative table is fully perfected.
