## Notes
- [龍 (char)](../characters/龍%20(char).md) "dragon" + [眼](../characters/眼.md) "eye; eyeball"

龍眼 is "longan" (Dimocarpus longan) — a tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia and southern China. 龍 (dragon) + 眼 (eye; eyeball). The name "dragon's eye" refers to the appearance of the peeled fruit: the translucent white flesh surrounding the dark seed resembles a stylised eye, with the black seed as the pupil and the white flesh as the sclera. Longan is closely related to lychee (荔枝) and rambutan, all members of the soapberry family (Sapindaceae); the three fruits share a similar structure of thin skin, juicy flesh, and a single smooth seed.

Mandarin 龍眼 (lóngyǎn) is the standard botanical name; the dried fruit is also called 桂圓 (guìyuán, "osmanthus circle") — a staple of traditional Chinese medicine and a common dried snack. Cantonese 龍眼 (lung4 ngaan5) is the standard name; the English word "longan" is borrowed directly from Cantonese lung4 ngaan5. Japanese りゅうがん (ryūgan). Korean 룡안 (龍眼). Vietnamese long nhãn (Sino-Vietnamese: long=龍, nhãn=眼) is the standard name — Vietnam is a major commercial producer, and long nhãn appears on menus and in markets throughout the country. The alias 龙眼 is the PRC-simplified-character form (龙=simplified 龍; 眼 is unchanged).
