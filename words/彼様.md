## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

彼様 is the distal manner correlative — "in that other way; in that fashion (remote or unfamiliar)." Like 彼処 (that place yonder) and 彼時 (that time back then), 彼様 refers to a manner removed from the immediate discourse context — a method the speaker gestures toward as distant, observed from afar, or belonging to another group or context. In narrative, 彼様 can describe the customs, techniques, or styles of another culture or era: 彼様に生きた人々 ("people who lived in that fashion").

Japanese あのように (ano yō ni, "in that way — distal") carries the characteristic distal nuance: あのようにやれば良かった ("it would have been better to do it that way — looking back"); ああやって (ā yatte, "doing it like that — distal/admiring") is the informal equivalent, often carrying a tone of wonder or nostalgia. Korean 저렇게 (jeoreoke, "like that — distal") is the third member of the 이렇게/그렇게/저렇게 set and implies the manner is not immediately at hand. Mandarin 那樣 (nàyàng) covers both medial and distal, merging what Dan'a'yo and Korean/Japanese distinguish.

彼様 is the rarest of the three deictic manner entries in everyday use — most manner deixis in conversation is proximal (this way) or medial (that way). But it earns its systematic place for narrative and descriptive use: depicting customs, methods, or styles observed with social or temporal distance. The full three-way deictic manner paradigm (此様/其様/彼様 = this way/that way/that other way) gives Dan'a'yo a richer manner-deictic inventory than Mandarin or Vietnamese, matched only by Japanese and Korean.
