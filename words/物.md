## Notes
- [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; result"

物 is "thing; result" — in Dan'a'yo, 物 refers to concrete objects, outcomes, and material things as opposed to processes or affairs (which are 事). As a correlative row heading, 物 generates 此物 (this thing), 其物 (that thing), 何物 (what thing; what is it?), 某物 (something; a certain thing). The canonical Dan'a'yo contrast: 食事 (the act of eating — event) vs. 食物 (food — the thing eaten, the concrete result). In classical Chinese, 物 is a sweeping word for "things in the world; all phenomena; substance; entity." 物 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[物]].

Mandarin 物 (wù): 物品 (wùpǐn, goods; item), 物質 (wùzhí, matter; substance), 物理 (wùlǐ, physics — lit. the principle of things), 萬物 (wànwù, all things in the world), 生物 (shēngwù, living things; organism). Cantonese 物 (mat6): 物件 (mat6 gin2, thing; item). Japanese 物 (もの, mono) in its native reading is one of the most essential Japanese content words: 食べ物 (たべもの, food), 飲み物 (のみもの, drink), 着物 (きもの, garment/kimono), 物語 (ものがたり, tale — lit. "thing-telling"). Sino-Japanese もつ/ぶつ (motsu/butsu): 物資 (ぶっし, materials; supplies), 物証 (ぶっしょう, material evidence). Korean 물 (物) in Sino-Korean: 물품 (物品, goods), 동물 (動物, animal), 식물 (植物, plant). Vietnamese vật (物): vật chất (matter; substance), động vật (animal), thực vật (plant).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 묻 differs from Korean 물. 物 reads 묻 (mud/ㄇㄨㄊ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 微母 (ɱ) + 物韻 (ɨut) gives Dan'a'yo a closed syllable with final -t (romanized d), producing 묻 — while Sino-Korean gives 물 (mul), where the MC final -t in this rhyme class has shifted to -l. This -t → -l shift for MC entering-tone finals is a regular Sino-Korean correspondence, also seen in 抜 (bạt/받 vs 발) and 刷 (솯 vs 쇄).
