## Notes
- [遜](../characters/遜.md) "yield, be inferior" + [色](../characters/色.md) "appearance, quality"

遜色 literally combines "yielding" with "appearance" — a quality that visibly falls short, or "loses face," when set beside something else. The word is overwhelmingly used in negative or interrogative constructions: Mandarin 毫不遜色 ("not inferior in the slightest"), Japanese 遜色がない (sonshoku ga nai, "nothing to be ashamed of, holds its own"). It rarely stands alone as a flat description of inferiority; instead it frames a comparison, asserting (or denying) that one thing measures up to another.

遜 itself is a graphemic and phonetic relative of 孫 (grandchild) — both share the 系 "thread/lineage" component and the same Dan'a'yo syllable ㄙㄛㄋ — extended via 辶 (movement) to mean "to withdraw, defer, yield precedence," the deferential posture appropriate to a junior in a lineage. This sense of self-effacement survives most directly in 謙遜 (humility, modesty), already cross-linked from [[遜]]'s Words section. 遜色 applies that same "falling back, yielding ground" metaphor to comparative quality: to be 遜色 is to yield the field to something better.
