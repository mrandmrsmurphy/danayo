## Notes
### Form
霓金 is [霓](characters/霓.md) “secondary rainbow; iridescent arc” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md)) “metal,” following the rule for transition elements.

### Motivation
The international name _iridium_ derives from Greek Ἶρις (_Iris_), the rainbow goddess, referring to the many-colored salts of the element. 霓 denotes the **fainter, outer (secondary) rainbow** in Classical Chinese, distinguished from 虹 (primary rainbow). This makes it a precise semantic fit for the idea of iridescence.

### Why 霓 (not 虹)
- 虹 is already in use e.g., 虹素 for neon), so reusing it would violate the one-character uniqueness constraint.
- 霓 preserves the **rainbow domain** while remaining lexically distinct.
- It is classical, attested, and semantically narrower than generic color terms.

### Type of formation
A **semantic calque with differentiation**: we retain the original “rainbow” concept but select a less common member of that semantic field to avoid collision.

### Fit within the system
1. Meaning-based reconstruction of the Greek root.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a d-block element.
3. Provides a unique, pronounceable symbol (霓).
4. Maintains internal coherence with other color/spectrum-derived names (青, 紫, 茜, 虹).

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 铱 (phonetic)
- Japanese: イリジウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 이리듐 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: iridi (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 霓金 restores semantic transparency.

### Clarity and issues
- 霓 is less common than 虹 and may require learning, but it is precise within the classical lexicon.
- The distinction “secondary rainbow” is arguably more specific than required, but it does not distort the core meaning.

### Conclusion
霓金 is a strong solution under constraint: it preserves the rainbow-based etymology of _iridium_ while avoiding collision with existing names. The choice of 霓 is both semantically appropriate and systemically efficient.
