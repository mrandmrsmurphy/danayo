## Notes
- [偕](../characters/偕.md) "accompany" + [同](../characters/同.md) "same, together" — stand-in for [[偕]], which cannot appear independently

偕同 means "together with" or "in the company of" — used adverbially to indicate joint action or accompaniment. The compound reinforces togetherness through near-synonymy: 偕 (to accompany, to go along with) and 同 (same, together, jointly) both contribute the sense of shared participation. In Classical Chinese usage 偕 already implied mutual accompaniment, and 同 strengthens that reading into an explicit adverb of comitance.

The compound appears in formal and literary Chinese, Korean, and Japanese in phrases like 偕同前往 (going together), and has an archaic, elevated register compared to everyday equivalents such as 一起 (Chinese) or 一緒に (Japanese). In Korean 해동 is rare in speech but appears in classical or bureaucratic writing.
