## Notes
- [宏](../characters/宏.md) "vast, grand" + [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big" — this word is the stand-in for [宏](../characters/宏.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

宏大 describes something grand or magnificent by sheer scale — a building, an ambition, a vision — reinforcing 宏's own sense of vastness with 大's plain "bigness" rather than adding a distinct second idea. The pairing is unsurprising: 宏's own `korean_native` gloss (클, "big") is identical to 大's own, the same character-level tell already seen elsewhere in this vault for near-synonym compounds.

**Japanese こうだい (kōdai) is a real but comparatively rare spelling** — Wiktionary records 宏大 as an alternative orthography of the far more common 広大 (also こうだい), which uses 広 (the shinjitai of 廣, a different but semantically overlapping character for "broad, wide") rather than 宏. The reading itself is unaffected either way, since 宏's own on'yomi (コウ) and 大's (ダイ) concatenate to the same こうだい regardless of which "broad/vast" character is chosen.

Mandarin (hóngdà), Cantonese (wang4 daai6), Korean (굉대, goengdae), and Vietnamese (hoành đại) all preserve the compound transparently, with no fused or irregular readings.
