## Notes
- [空 (char)](../characters/空%20(char).md) "empty" + [港](../characters/港.md) "harbor" — literally "sky-harbor." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (空's own points to bare `空`, 港's own to `港湾`); 空港 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/空 (char).md` and `characters/港.md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **Content removed**: `aliases` previously listed `飛機場`/`機場` — these are unrelated Chinese synonyms for "airport," written with entirely different characters (機 "machine" + 場 "field," not 空 "sky" + 港 "harbor"), not alternate spellings of 空港 itself. Removed both; this vault's 空港 corresponds most directly to the Japanese term くうこう (kūkō) and Korean 공항, while Mandarin's own everyday word for "airport" is actually 机场/飛機場 (jīchǎng), a separate compound not yet given its own page here.
- Filled blank `cantonese` (`hung1 gong2`, matching `characters/港.md`'s own stored `gong2`). Vietnamese sân bay is a native construction rather than a Sino-Vietnamese calque, but is genuinely the correct, standard everyday term for "airport."
