## Notes
- [専](../characters/専.md) "special; dedicated" + [門](../characters/門.md) "gate; field" — a dedicated gate/field of one's own, i.e. one's own specialized domain.

Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all treat this compound as a plain noun for "specialty, area of expertise": 専門 anchors a large Japanese word family (専門家 "expert," 専門学校 "vocational school," 専門知識 "specialized knowledge"), 전문 behaves identically in Korean (전문가, 전문분야), and Vietnamese *chuyên môn* is likewise a noun naming one's field of knowledge or skill.

Mandarin has drifted away from this noun sense: 专门 today functions mainly as an *adverb* meaning "specifically, deliberately, for this purpose alone" (我专门来看你的, "I came specifically to see you"), not as the word for one's specialty or major — that role belongs to the separate word 专业 (zhuānyè), which covers both "major" (as a noun) and "professional" (as an adjective). So a Mandarin speaker reaching for "my specialty/major" would reach for 专业, not 专门, even though every other language in the family kept 専門/전문/chuyên môn as the ordinary noun for exactly that.
