## Notes
- [饗](../characters/饗.md) "banquet, offer a feast" + [宴](../characters/宴.md) "banquet, revel"
- This word is the stand-in for [[饗]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A reinforcing compound: both components mean "feast" or "banquet," together denoting a grand formal occasion. In Japanese, きょうえん (kyōen) is used in elevated or literary contexts — a lavish state dinner, a scholarly symposium, or a religious offering feast. Chinese 饗宴 (xiǎngyàn) appears in formal writing for ceremonial banquets; the more everyday term is 宴会 (yànhuì). Korean 향연 (饗宴) carries both the literal meaning (grand feast) and a figurative one: a 음악의 향연 "feast of music" — an aesthetic celebration.
