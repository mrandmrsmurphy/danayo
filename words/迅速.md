## Notes
- [迅](../characters/迅.md) "swift" + [速](../characters/速%20(char).md) "fast, quick" — stand-in for [[迅]], which cannot appear independently
- 迅速 doubles down on speed by yoking together two characters that already mean almost the same thing — 迅, the rarer and more literary of the pair, evokes a sudden burst (the radical 辵, "to walk/move," paired with a phonetic that itself suggested swiftness in Old Chinese), while 速 is the everyday word for "fast" found everywhere from highways (高速) to fast food (速食). Pressed together, the compound reads as urgency rather than mere velocity — not "quick" in the sense of nimble fingers, but "prompt" in the sense of a response that brooks no delay: 迅速な対応 (a swift response), 迅速に行動する (to act without hesitation). Mandarin xùnsù, Cantonese seon3 cuk1, Sino-Japanese じんそく (jinsoku), Sino-Korean 신속 (sinsok), and Sino-Vietnamese tấn tốc all carry this same charged sense of urgency, sitting at the formal end of a crowded family of "speed" compounds (急速, 加速, 火速, 快速) that fan out across these languages from a shared Sinitic root.
