## Notes
- [勾](../characters/勾.md) "seduce, entice" + [引 (char)](../characters/引%20(char).md) "pull, tug" — stand-in for [[勾]], which cannot appear independently

勾引 means to lure, seduce, or entice someone — drawing them in through beguiling means. The two components are near-synonyms in the sense of drawing: 勾 (gōu) implies a hook-like pulling, catching hold of someone's attention or desire, while 引 (yǐn) means to pull, guide, or lead along. Together the compound describes the act of drawing someone toward something, typically with a negative or illicit connotation: seducing a person into wrongdoing, luring a victim into a trap, or tempting someone away from loyalty.

In literary Chinese 勾引 appears in contexts of romantic seduction and criminal enticement alike. In modern Chinese gōuyǐn retains this dual register — it can describe both sexual seduction and the luring of a person into a scheme. In Japanese こういん has a more specific legal meaning: 勾引 in Japanese law refers to compelling the attendance of a witness or defendant by force (forcible summons), a quite different sense from the Chinese. Korean 구인 similarly carries the legal meaning of "compelled appearance" or, in common usage, "wanted person" (수배, 구인), which diverges from the Chinese seduction sense.
