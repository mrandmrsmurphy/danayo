## Notes
- [冬 (char)](../characters/冬%20(char).md) "winter" + [月 (char)](../characters/月%20(char).md) "month" — the winter month; the month that contains the heart of winter.

冬月 is the eleventh month of the traditional Chinese lunisolar calendar — the month that contains the winter solstice (冬至, dōngzhì). In the Earthly Branch system it corresponds to 子月 (the Rat month), the first branch in the daily cycle, which carries a distinctive cosmological significance: 子 is the hour of midnight, the turning point when the new day is born in darkness. Cosmologically, 冬至 is the exact parallel: the darkest day, the year's midnight, when 一陽來復 ("the one yang returns") — hidden yang energy begins its slow ascent back through the earth, even as the cold deepens.

冬月 is also called [[葭月]] ("reed month"): the rush or reed (葭, jiā) is said to begin stirring under the ice in the eleventh month, sending the first tentative shoots upward beneath the frozen surface. The reed is thus a symbol of hidden vitality at the darkest time — life that has not stopped even when all visible signs have ceased. The same image appears in classical divination: the hexagram 復 (Return, ䷗) in the I Ching corresponds to 冬至, depicting the return of the single yang line at the base of five yin lines.

The winter solstice festival (冬至節) was historically one of the most important celebrations in China and remains significant in Taiwan, Korea (동지, 冬至), and Japan (冬至, tōji), marked by specific foods — red bean porridge (팥죽, patjuk) in Korea, tangerine-bean soup (冬至の柚子風呂) in Japan — and by the sense of having passed through the year's dark midpoint.
