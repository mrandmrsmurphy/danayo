## Notes
- [宇](../characters/宇.md) "universe, space" + [宙 (char)](../characters/宙%20(char).md) "eternity" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" — one who travels through or comes from outer space; both astronaut and extraterrestrial life

The character 宇宙 (universe) is built on an architectural metaphor: 宇 originally meant the eaves of a building, 宙 the roof-beams — together they denote the totality of space and time, the structure that contains everything. Adding 人 ("person") produces a word equally at home meaning "cosmonaut" (a person who enters that structure) and "alien" (a person from beyond it).

All the major East Asian languages use the same compound: Mandarin 宇宙人 (yǔzhòurén), Japanese 宇宙人 (うちゅうじん), Korean 우주인, Vietnamese vũ trụ nhân. The widespread agreement reflects the shared Sinitic scientific vocabulary that spread through the region in the twentieth century alongside the space age.
