## Notes
- [杯 (char)](../characters/杯%20(char).md) "cup; cupful"

杯 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for cups, glasses, and their liquid contents — 量詞, measuring any liquid served in a cup-shaped vessel: 一杯水 (one cup of water), 三杯茶 (three cups of tea), 兩杯酒 (two glasses of wine). The character is a 形声 compound: semantic 木 (wood, from when cups were carved from wood) + phonetic 不 (OC *pɯ), giving the original sense of "a wooden cup or vessel." The classifier function extends naturally from the physical container to its measured content — 杯 is one of the container-to-measure-word transitions stable and semantically transparent across all five CJKV languages. 杯 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[杯]].

The CJKV classifier landscape for cups and liquid measures shows strong convergence. Mandarin 杯 (bēi) is the standard classifier for drinks and cups: 一杯水 (one glass of water), 喝了三杯 (drank three cups — noun dropped); 茶杯 (chábēi, teacup), 酒杯 (jiǔbēi, wine glass). Cantonese 杯 (bui1): 一杯水 (one glass of water), 飲三杯 (drink three cups); 盅 (zung1) is used for smaller ceremonial cups of tea or spirits. Japanese 杯 (はい/HAI) counts cups, glasses, and bowlfuls: 一杯のコーヒー (one cup of coffee); 一杯 (ippai) idiomatically means "full; a lot of" — from the sense of "one full cup." Korean 잔 (盞, jan) is the standard native classifier: 물 한 잔 (one glass of water), 커피 두 잔 (two cups of coffee); the Sino-Korean 배 (杯) appears in formal or literary contexts. Vietnamese chén (cup; bowl) and ly (glass) dominate colloquially: một ly nước (one glass of water), một chén cơm (one bowl of rice); Sino-Vietnamese bôi (杯) is primarily literary.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 뵈 does not match Korean 배. 杯 reads 뵈 (boi/ㄅㄛㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 幫母 (p) + 灰韻 (uʌi) gives a bilabial stop onset with the -oi diphthong. Dan'a'yo preserves the MC -uʌi diphthong as -oi (뵈/boi), while Sino-Korean 배 (bae) collapses the labial medial -u- into the following vowel and fronts it, producing -ae. The MC 灰韻 gives 뵈 in Dan'a'yo and 배 in Korean — the same divergence seen in [[枚]] (뵈-type) and its Korean 매, both descending from MC 明母 + 灰韻.
