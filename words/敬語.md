## Notes
- [敬](../characters/敬.md) "respect, honor" + [語](../characters/語.md) "language, speech" — speech that expresses respect through its grammatical form, rather than through word choice alone.

The grammar book is explicit that Dan'a'yo does not preserve the elaborate politeness systems (敬語体系) of Japanese or Korean — its single suffix 公, which adds a sense of austerity and politeness to a noun phrase, is a deliberately simplified gesture toward the same idea rather than a true grammaticalized honorific system. 敬 also appears, productively, in 敬意 ("respect," not yet created) and could in principle combine with [[敬語]]'s structural sibling [[代名詞]] in a discussion of honorific pronouns, a category Dan'a'yo notably lacks. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
