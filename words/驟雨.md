## Notes
- [驟](../characters/驟.md) "sudden; to gallop" + [雨 (char)](../characters/雨%20(char).md) "rain" — stand-in for [[驟]], which cannot appear independently.

驟雨 (zhòuyǔ) is a heavy rain shower that arrives suddenly and passes quickly — a squall or cloudburst, often accompanied by thunder, lightning, or strong winds, and especially common in summer. The Sino-Japanese しゅうう and Sino-Korean 취우 readings carry the same sense.

[[words/俄雨]] lists 驟雨 as a synonym, distinguishing the two by emphasis: 驟雨 highlights the *intensity* of the rain (a sudden downpour or squall), while 俄雨 highlights its *brevity* (a shower that comes and goes). 小雨, by contrast, describes light rain or drizzle, independent of duration.
