## Notes
- [上 (char)](../characters/上%20(char).md) "above" + [知 (char)](../characters/知%20(char).md) "know" — here 知 carries its less common reading and sense "wisdom," identical to [[智]] and written interchangeably as 上智; in this word 知 is read zhì in Mandarin rather than its ordinary zhī, an exceptional reading the Dan'a'yo pronunciation syangjui does not reproduce, following the usual convention of mechanical concatenation from each character's primary bound-form value.

上知 names the Confucian category of people born with innate, unteachable wisdom — the upper extreme in a famous pairing from the *Analects* (論語·陽貨): "唯上知與下愚不移" ("only the wisest and the most foolish do not change"). Confucius's broader point in this passage is that most people's character is shaped by habit and circumstance — "性相近也，習相遠也" ("by nature people are alike; it is practice that sets them apart") — but he carves out these two extremes, [[上知]] and [[下愚]], as fixed poles that instruction and environment cannot move. The pairing became a fixture of later Confucian discussion of human nature and education, including debate over whether Confucius meant the categories literally (a few people are simply unteachable) or, as the Joseon scholar Jeong Yak-yong later argued, as descriptions of degree of attainment rather than fixed essence.
