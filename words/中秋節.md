## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle" + [秋 (char)](../characters/秋%20(char).md) "autumn" + [節 (char)](../characters/節%20(char).md) "festival, node" — the Mid-Autumn Festival, held on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month, the midpoint of [[仲秋]].

**中秋節** is one of the most important traditional festivals across the Sinosphere, celebrated on the night of the full moon nearest the autumn equinox. Its central observances are moon-viewing (賞月) and the eating of mooncakes (月餅) — round pastries, often filled with lotus-seed paste or red bean and a salted egg yolk, whose shape echoes the full moon and symbolizes family unity and completeness.

The festival's mythological core is the story of **嫦娥** (Cháng'é), the woman who ascended to the moon after drinking an elixir of immortality, and her husband **后羿** (Hòu Yì), the archer who shot down nine of the ten suns that once scorched the earth. Variants of this story, along with the moon-rabbit (玉兔) pounding the elixir, are told across China, Vietnam (Tết Trung Thu, where children's lantern processions are especially prominent), and the Korean and Japanese traditions (where the holiday is less central but moon-viewing — 観月, 月見 — survives as a seasonal custom). In Korea the cognate holiday is **추석** (Chuseok, 秋夕), which falls on the same date but is structured primarily around ancestral rites (차례) and the harvest rather than the mooncake-and-moon-myth complex found further south.

中秋節 falls within [[仲秋]] (the second month of autumn) and is closely associated with [[桂月]] (the "osmanthus month," another name for the eighth lunar month) and the autumn equinox [[秋分]].
