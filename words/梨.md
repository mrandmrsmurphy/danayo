## Notes
- [梨 (char)](../characters/梨%20(char).md) "pear; pear tree"

梨 is the word for pear — the fruit of trees in the genus Pyrus, widely cultivated across East Asia. The Asian pear (also called nashi pear or Chinese pear, Pyrus pyrifolia) is distinct from the European pear in shape and texture: typically rounder, crisper, and more fragrant. 梨 is among the most culturally significant fruits in the Sinosphere, appearing in poetry, idiom, and ritual — pear blossom (梨花, líhuā) is a classical symbol of pure white beauty, and pear wood (梨木) is prized for carving and printing blocks.

Mandarin 梨 (lí) is the standard word for pear; 梨子 (lízi) adds the diminutive 子. Cantonese 梨 (lei4). Japanese 梨 (なし, nashi) names the Japanese Asian pear (Pyrus pyrifolia), the crisp round variety sold internationally as "nashi pear." Korean has 이 (i) as the Sino-Korean reading and 배 (bae) as the native Korean word — 배 refers specifically to the Korean pear variety (Pyrus pyrifolia var. culta). Vietnamese lê is the standard word for pear from the Sino-Vietnamese reading.

梨 has a notable phonological taboo in Mandarin: 梨 (lí) is homophonic with 離 (lí, to separate, to part ways). This makes giving pears as a gift inauspicious in traditional Chinese culture — 分梨 (fēn lí, to share a pear) sounds exactly like 分離 (fēn lí, to separate). It is one of the most well-known fruit-name homophones in Chinese gift-giving etiquette, alongside the prohibition on clocks (鐘 zhōng ≈ 終 zhōng, death). The Dan'a'yo reading 릐/liǝ/ㄌㄧㄜ does not create the same homophonic conflict with the Dan'a'yo word for 離.
