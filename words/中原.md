## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle; center" + [原](../characters/原.md) "plain; flatland"

中原 is "the Central Plains" — the heartland of Chinese civilisation, centred on the Yellow River basin in modern Henan province and the surrounding areas of Shanxi, Hebei, and Shandong. 中 (center, middle) + 原 (plain, flat land). The name encodes China's traditional self-conception: the Yellow River plain as the geographic and cultural center of the world, with civilisation radiating outward. Dynasties that controlled 中原 were considered the legitimate successors of Chinese civilisation; loss of 中原 to steppe peoples was the defining political catastrophe of Chinese history.

The concept of 中原 appears throughout classical Chinese historical and political writing — 問鼎中原 (to inquire about the tripods of the Central Plains, i.e., to contest supremacy over China) became a standard idiom for imperial ambition. The plains were the stage for nearly every major dynastic transition and civil war, from the Shang and Zhou foundations through the Three Kingdoms, the fall of the Han, and the Song-Jin and Song-Yuan confrontations. Japanese なかはら (nakahara) is a common Japanese surname and place name reusing the same characters with native Japanese readings (naka = middle, hara = plain/field) — unrelated in meaning to the Chinese geographic concept.

The alias 中州 (Zhōngzhōu, "Central Province/Region") is a classical alternative name for the same region — 州 (province, administrative region) in place of 原 (plain). 中州 appears in poetry and historical prose as a near-synonym for 中原 and, by extension, for China itself. kwin: true reflects that both Dan'a'yo and Korean render 中原 identically as 중원/中原, both inheriting their readings from Middle Chinese through closely parallel phonological paths (中 *tuŋ → jung/중; 原 ŋ+ʉɐn → 'wen/원).
