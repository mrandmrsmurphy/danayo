## Notes
- [病](../characters/病.md) "sickness; illness" + [症](../characters/症.md) "disease condition; symptom" — a dvandva-adjacent compound for an illness and its presenting condition; stand-in for [[症]], which cannot appear independently

**病症** (*bìngzhèng*) is the everyday compound for a disease or ailment — the illness (病) together with its symptomatic manifestation (症). Where 病 alone names the fact of being sick, and 症 alone names the symptomatic pattern of a condition, 病症 brings them together into the standard colloquial word for "illness, condition, what's wrong with someone."

The character 症 is highly productive as a medical suffix, forming the names of conditions and syndromes: 炎症 (inflammation), 癌症 (cancer), 自閉症 (autism spectrum disorder), 後遺症 (sequela, lingering aftereffect of a disease). In each case, 症 marks the pattern of presentation — what the condition *looks like* from the outside — as opposed to 病 (the underlying illness) or 疾 (acute disease). The compound 症状 (*zhèngzhuàng*, *shōjō* in Japanese) means "symptoms" — the observable signs of disease — and is the standard clinical term for what a patient presents with.

病症 as a compound is more colloquial and general than the clinical vocabulary. In Chinese, 病症 appears in everyday speech — 你有什麼病症? ("What's wrong with you? / What is your condition?") — as well as in traditional medicine contexts, where the identification and classification of 病症 is the foundational act of diagnosis before treatment (辨證論治, differentiating patterns and administering treatment). Chinese traditional medicine (中醫) analyses disease not primarily by biomedical category but by 症候群 (syndrome cluster) — the constellation of symptoms that together define a 病症 in its particular manifestation.

Japanese **びょうしょう** (*byōshō*) is used in clinical and general contexts for the condition or state of a disease. Korean **병증** (*byeongjeung*) is the standard medical term for a disease condition or syndrome pattern, used across both biomedical and traditional Korean medicine (한의학) contexts. Vietnamese **bệnh chứng** (*bệnh* from 病, *chứng* from 症) is the Sino-Vietnamese compound used in medical writing; everyday Vietnamese uses *bệnh* alone or *bệnh tật* for illness.
