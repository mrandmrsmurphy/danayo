## Notes
- [燃](../characters/燃.md) "burn" + [焼](../characters/焼.md) "burn" — stand-in for both [[燃]] and [[焼]], neither of which can appear independently

**Real typo corrected**: `english: burst` — should be `burn`, matching the actual meaning of 燃焼 ("combustion, burning"), not "burst." The same typo was also propagated onto `characters/燃.md`'s own Notes bullet, fixed there too.

**`pos` corrected `性詞`→`事詞`**, matching `characters/焼.md`'s own stored category — the same fix class as [[呼吸]]/[[戦闘]]/[[回転]]/[[落下]] earlier this sweep.

Mandarin ránshāo, Cantonese jin4 siu1, Japanese ねんしょう (nenshō), and Korean 연소 (yeonso) are all the standard word for "combustion" in both literal (完全燃焼, "complete combustion") and figurative senses (burning energy/fat, burning with emotion). Vietnamese nhiên thiêu is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search) carrying the identical literal/figurative double sense — đốt cháy mỡ thừa, "burn excess fat"; bùng cháy ngọn lửa giận dữ, "burn with a fire of anger."
