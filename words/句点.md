## Notes
- [句 (char)](../characters/句%20(char).md) "phrase, sentence, stanza, paragraph, clause, pericope" + [点 (char)](../characters/点%20(char).md) "point" — the punctuation mark at the end of a sentence; specifically the Western-style solid dot ".", as opposed to [[句号]], the small hollow circle "。" traditionally used across the Sinosphere.

句点/구점/くてん all name the same Western-style period, but Korean shows the richest history here: 구점 itself is a real, older grammatical term (from the classical 句讀 system, contrasting 句點, the end-of-sentence mark, with 讀點/두점, a mid-sentence pause mark), while the actual modern standard term for "." in today's Korean writing is 마침표 ("closing mark"). Korean also once had a horizontal/vertical split of its own — 온점 for horizontal writing versus 고리점 (also cross-referenced from [[句号]]) for vertical writing — before a 2015 orthography reform folded both into plain 마침표. Vietnamese dấu chấm ("dot mark") and Japanese くてん both name the Western period directly, without the same historical vertical/horizontal split.
