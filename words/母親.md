## Notes
- [母](../characters/母.md) "mother" + [親](../characters/親.md) "parent" — stand-in for [[母]], which cannot appear independently

母親 is "mother" — reinforcing 母 with the general noun 親 "parent." 母 cannot appear as an independent vault entry on its own, so 母親 is what legitimizes it.

Mandarin 母親 (mǔqīn) is a genuine everyday word, though somewhat more formal/written than the affectionate 媽媽 (māma). Cantonese 母親 (mou5can1) patterns the same way. Japanese 母親 (ははおや, hahaoya) is a real, common, and neutral everyday word — distinct from the personal address terms お母さん (okāsan) or the more casual ママ (mama), it's the word used to talk *about* someone's mother in the third person, e.g. 彼の母親 ("his mother"). Korean 모친 (mochin) is a real, formal/written register word — appearing in biographies, official documents, and news writing — while everyday spoken Korean uses 어머니 (eomeoni) or 엄마 (eomma) instead. Vietnamese mẫu thân is likewise a real, formal/literary word historically associated with aristocratic and classical usage (a reversed-order doublet, thân mẫu, also exists with the same meaning — a word-order variation echoing the same phenomenon seen on [[介紹]]/紹介 earlier in this sweep) — everyday Vietnamese instead says mẹ or má.
