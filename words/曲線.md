## Notes
- [曲](../characters/曲.md) "bent; curved" + [線](../characters/線.md) "line" — a curved line, as opposed to [[直線]] (straight line) and [[折線]] (broken line)

**曲線** (*qūxiàn*) is the standard mathematical and everyday term for a curve: a continuous one-dimensional geometric object that is not necessarily straight. The compound is directly compositional — 曲 (bent, curved) qualifying 線 (line) — and forms a natural pair with [[直線]] (*zhíxiàn*, straight line). The two together cover the basic taxonomy of lines in plane geometry.

In formal mathematics, the definition of 曲線 is broader than colloquial usage: a curve is any continuous map from an interval of the real line into a space, which includes straight lines as a degenerate case. In practice, 曲線 is reserved for the non-straight case. Important classes include **閉曲線** (*bì qūxiàn*, closed curve — one whose endpoints coincide, as in a circle or ellipse), **開曲線** (open curve), and **参数曲線** (parametric curve, defined by a parameter rather than an explicit equation). The **圓錐曲線** (conic sections — see [[圓錐]]) are the historically foundational family: circle, ellipse, parabola, and hyperbola, all arising as planar sections of a cone.

In design, engineering, and digital graphics, 曲線 encompasses the Bézier curves that underlie all vector illustration and font design: the smooth, controlled curves generated by a small number of control points, first formalised by Pierre Bézier at Renault and Paul de Casteljau at Citroën in the 1950s and 1960s AD, and now the standard model in every vector graphics application.

The contrast with [[弯曲]] is structural: 弯曲 is a 性詞 describing the property of being curved; 曲線 is the 名詞 naming the thing itself. One says 道路弯曲 ("the road is curved") but 道路的曲線 ("the curve of the road").

Japanese **きょくせん** (*kyokusen*) and Korean **곡선** (*gokson*) are both standard mathematical terms in their respective educational systems, closely parallel in usage to the Dan'a'yo 曲線. Vietnamese **đường cong** ("curved road/line," a native compound: đường "road; line" + cong "bent") is the everyday and mathematical standard; the Sino-Vietnamese *khúc tuyến* exists but is not in active use.
