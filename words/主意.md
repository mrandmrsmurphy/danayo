## Notes
- [主](../characters/主.md) "lord, master" + [意](../characters/意.md) "meaning, significance"

主意 is "main decision, big idea" — literally "master idea," the idea one settles on or takes charge of; the common set phrase 拿主意 (Mandarin, "to make up one's mind") captures the sense well. It is a genuine Dan'a'yo-internal homophone of [[注意]] "pay attention to; be careful of," since [[主]] and [[注]] share the exact same Dan'a'yo syllable (ㄐㄨ, both descending from the same 主/注 phonetic series — 注 is even listed as a Derived Character on 主's own page) and both compounds end in 意's own syllable (ㄜ). 主意 and 注意 are a matched pair worth reading together: same components in structure (headword + 意 "meaning"), same resulting sound, entirely different meanings.

Mandarin shows a real cross-strait pronunciation split: mainland usage generally reads 主意 with a neutral tone on 意 (zhǔyi, "idea, plan"), while Taiwan Guoyu regularly reads it zhúyì. Cantonese 主意 (zyu2 ji3). Japanese 主意 (しゅい, shui) leans toward "gist, main point, guiding purpose" — a slightly more formal/abstract register than the everyday Mandarin "figure out what to do" sense. Korean 주의 has the same homophone collision as the Dan'a'yo pair: 主意 ("one's own idea/opinion") and 注意 ("attention, caution") are both read 주의, since 主/注 share the identical Sino-Korean reading 주 and 意 is always 의 — in practice, 注意 (the "pay attention" sense) is by far the more common word, so context does essentially all of the disambiguating work. Vietnamese chủ ý ("intention, purpose, deliberateness," often used adverbially like "on purpose") preserves the "one's own guiding idea" sense fairly closely.
