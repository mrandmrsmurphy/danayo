## Notes
- [灰](../characters/灰.md) "ashes" + [塵](../characters/塵.md) "dust; dirt" — stand-in for [[塵]], which cannot appear independently

Mandarin huīchén, Cantonese fui1 can4, and Japanese かいじん (kaijin) are all real words for "ash and dust" together, sometimes figuratively "the remains/ruins of something destroyed" (化為灰塵, "reduced to ashes"). **Korean 회진 is a genuine, documented three-way homophone**: alongside this compound's own 灰塵 sense ("ash and dust," also figuratively "utter destruction, annihilation"), the identical spelling covers 回診 ("a doctor's rounds," visiting patients room to room) and 回進 ("to turn and advance") — three unrelated words sharing one pronunciation, confirmed via search. Left `vietnamese` unfilled rather than guess: no attested compound for 灰塵 was found (the character-level fields for both 灰 and 塵 are themselves noisy lists with no clear single reading to combine), and the everyday Vietnamese word for dust is simply the native bụi.
