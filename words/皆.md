## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every"

皆 is "all; every" — the universal collective quantifier in Dan'a'yo's correlative paradigm, generating 皆事 (all matters; everything), 皆人 (everyone; all people), 皆処 (everywhere), 皆時 (always; every time). 皆 marks total universal coverage of a set as a whole, contrasting with 毎 (distributive "every, each one individually"). Classical Chinese 皆 means "all; everyone; every" and is one of the most common universal quantifiers in the literary canon: 皆大歡喜 (all greatly rejoiced; a happy ending for all). 皆 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[皆]].

Mandarin 皆 (jiē) is literary: 皆大歡喜 (jiē dà huān xǐ, all greatly rejoicing — a standard phrase for a happy resolution), 面面皆是 (everywhere one looks, there it is). Modern spoken Mandarin uses 都 (dōu) for "all; both; every." Cantonese 皆 (gaai1): literary; colloquial Cantonese uses 都 (dou1) for "all." Japanese 皆 (みな/みんな, mina/minna) is the everyday word for "everyone; everything; all": 皆さん (みなさん, everyone — honorific), 皆で (みんなで, all together). Korean 개 (皆) in Sino-Korean; the common native forms are 모두 (modu, all) and 다 (da, all; completely). Vietnamese giai (皆): Sino-Vietnamese literary; modern uses tất cả (all, every).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 겨 differs from Korean 개. 皆 reads 겨 (gye/ㄍ⼶) in Dan'a'yo — MC 見母 (k) + 皆開韻 (ɣɛi) gives Dan'a'yo a front vowel -ye (⼶) — while Sino-Korean gives 개 (gae), using the -ae vowel. The Dan'a'yo front vowel and the Sino-Korean -ae differ in quality: Dan'a'yo preserves the y-coloring of the MC rhyme's palatal environment.
