## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; object; material result"

皆物 is the universal correlative in the object column: it refers collectively to all things, the totality of objects and materials in a given domain. The character 皆 (Dan'a'yo *gye*, ㄍ⼶) is the classical Chinese collective universal — "all of them, in every case" — functioning as an adverbial quantifier in classical syntax (皆死 "all died"; 皆知 "all knew"). In Dan'a'yo it is standardised as the universal column header across the correlative table, contrasting with the distributive 毎 (each, one by one).

The most familiar classical Chinese expression for "all things" is 万物 (wànwù / Japanese ばんぶつ, "the ten-thousand things") — a cosmological term covering the totality of creation, used extensively in Daoist and Confucian philosophy (万物生于有，有生于无 "the ten-thousand things arise from being; being arises from non-being," Laozi 40). Dan'a'yo 皆物 is the correlative-table form of this concept, choosing 皆 for compositional consistency across the grid rather than the more idiomatic 万 (which carries cosmological/literary connotations). Japanese 全て / すべて (all things) and Korean 모든 것 (modeun geot, all things) are the natural spoken equivalents.

As in all other rows, 皆物 (collective universal) contrasts with 毎物 (distributive): 皆物を愛でる ("appreciate all things, everything") treats the set holistically, while 毎物を愛でる ("appreciate each thing in turn") distributes the appreciation across individual items. The collective reading of 皆物 makes it the natural Dan'a'yo translation for philosophical references to "all that exists" or "everything in the world," while 毎物 is more suited to practical enumeration contexts.
