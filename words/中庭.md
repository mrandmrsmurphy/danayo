## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle; inner" + [庭](../characters/庭.md) "courtyard; garden"

中庭 is "courtyard; inner garden; atrium" — the open space enclosed within a building or compound. 中 (middle, inner — the enclosed center) + 庭 (courtyard, garden, the space of an official or domestic compound). In East Asian architecture, the 中庭 is both functional (providing light, ventilation, drainage, and a gathering space) and symbolic: a protected inner world at the center of the household or institution, shielded from the street by the surrounding walls and buildings. Traditional urban houses (such as Japanese machiya or Chinese siheyuan courtyard compounds) were organised around their 中庭.

Mandarin 中庭 (zhōngtíng) is the classical and literary term for an inner courtyard or central hall. Cantonese 中庭 (zung1 ting4). Japanese 中庭 (なかにわ, nakaniwa) is the standard word for an enclosed garden or courtyard within a building — particularly the light-well garden found in traditional merchant houses (町家, machiya) and adapted in modern architectural design. Korean 중정 (中庭, jungjong) covers the same range: 중정 공간 (courtyard space), 건물 중정 (building courtyard). Vietnamese trung đình (Sino-Vietnamese: 中=trung, 庭=đình) is the formal equivalent.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 諺文 중덩 does not match Korean 중정. 庭 reads 덩 (deng/ㄉㄝㄫ) in Dan'a'yo but 정 (jeong) in Sino-Korean. MC initial d (定 initial) + final eŋ (青 rhyme) gives Dan'a'yo ㄉㄝㄫ (*deng/덩), while Sino-Korean gives 정 — both the vowel (에/에 → ㅓ in Korean) and the initial (d-/d- → j- in Korean via affrication) differ. This d-/j- split from MC 定 initial is a regular phonological divergence between Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean.
