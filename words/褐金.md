## Notes
**Form.** 褐金 is [褐 (char)](characters/褐%20(char).md) “brown; tawny” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymological situation.** _Holmium_ is named from Latin _Holmia_ “Stockholm.” That toponym is not reducible to a useful single graph under your constraints, so a direct place-based abbreviation is weak here.

**Motivation.** You shift to a **property-based descriptor** grounded in early characterization:

- Per Teodor Cleve’s work on the erbia fractions associated holmium with **brownish coloration** in certain preparations.
- 褐 is the classical, specific term for brown, more precise than generic 色 terms.

Thus the name encodes a **historically attested color association** rather than the opaque place-name.

**Type of formation.**  
A **property-based designation** replacing an unusable toponym, consistent with your fallback strategy (cf. 硬金, 磁金, 吸金, 翠金).

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, no phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately.
3. Provides a distinct, high-frequency symbol (褐).
4. Integrates with your spectrum/color field (青, 紫, 茜, 翠), expanding it without collision.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 钬 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ホルミウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 홀뮴 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: holmi (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 褐金 restores semantic content.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 褐 is less commonly used than basic color terms but is precise.
- The brown association is not as iconic as, say, terbium’s green, but it is historically grounded and sufficient for a fixed mapping.

**Conclusion.**  
褐金 is a reasonable and consistent solution: it replaces an opaque place-name with a concrete, historically motivated property while maintaining distinctiveness within your system.
