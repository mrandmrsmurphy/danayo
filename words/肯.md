## Notes
肯 is a modal-like verb: "to be willing (to do something)," almost always taking a following verb as its complement rather than standing alone (肯做, "willing to do"; 不肯, "unwilling to," its far more common negated form). Chinese, Japanese, and Korean all keep this same "willing" sense — Japanese 肯じない (gaenjinai) survives specifically in the negative, an old written form for "not consenting," while Korean 긍 rarely appears bare outside compounds like 수긍 (首肯, "to nod in agreement, to assent").
