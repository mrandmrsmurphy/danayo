## Notes
- [刷 (char)](../characters/刷%20(char).md) "to brush; to scrub; to print" + [子](../characters/子.md) "nominalising suffix"

刷子 is "brush" — 刷 (to brush; to scrub; to print) + 子 (nominalising suffix). The 子 suffix converts the verbal root 刷 into the noun for the tool itself: "the thing one brushes with." 刷 (MC 生母 ʃ + 黠合韻 ɣuat) depicts the act of sweeping or scraping clean with a rapid motion; the same root covers printing by brushing ink onto a block and pressing. The compound 刷子 applies broadly to any handheld brush — paintbrush, scrubbing brush, cleaning brush — making it the most general colloquial word for "brush" in Chinese.

Mandarin 刷子 (shuāzi) is the standard colloquial term for a brush of any type: 牙刷 (yáshuā, toothbrush — 刷 used as a standalone suffix), 油漆刷子 (paint brush), 馬桶刷子 (toilet brush). Cantonese 刷子 (caat3 zi2). Japanese 刷子 (はけ, hake) is the standard word for a brush used in painting, lacquering, paste application, or bookbinding — the kanji 刷子 are the character representation of the native Japanese word はけ. In Japanese, 刷 (さつ, satsu) appears in compounds: 印刷 (いんさつ, insatsu, printing), 刷新 (さっしん, sasshin, renewal/reform). Korean 쇄자 (刷子, swaeja): 刷 most commonly appears in Korean as 인쇄 (印刷, printing). Vietnamese loát tử (Sino-Vietnamese: 刷=loát, 子=tử).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 솯즈 differs from Korean 쇄자 in both syllables. 刷 reads 솯 (swad/ㄙ⺢ㄊ) in Dan'a'yo but 쇄 (swae) in Sino-Korean — MC 生母 (ʃ) + 黠合韻 (ɣuat) gives Dan'a'yo a closed syllable with the final -t preserved (as ㄊ, Romanised d), while Sino-Korean has an open syllable in which the MC final stop is lost and the vowel shifted. 子 reads 즈 (jǝ/ㄐㄜ) in Dan'a'yo but 자 (ja) in Sino-Korean — the same MC 之韻 (ɨ) → Dan'a'yo schwa vs Sino-Korean -a split as in 陽子, 様子, 養子, and 国子.
