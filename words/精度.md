## Notes
- [精](../characters/精.md) "refined, pure, essence; precise" + [度](../characters/度.md) "degree, measure, extent" — degree of refinement; how precisely something is made or measured.

精度 names a technical property that must be carefully distinguished from its near-neighbour 準確度 (accuracy). Precision and accuracy are logically independent: a clock that runs consistently 3 minutes fast is precise (it deviates the same amount every time, reproducibly) but inaccurate (it does not show the true time). A marksman who places every shot in the same wrong corner of the target is precise but inaccurate; one who scatters shots around the centre is accurate on average but imprecise. 精度 measures the former — the reproducibility, consistency, and fineness of resolution of a measurement or process.

The character 精 carries the sense of purification or refining: 精製 (refinement, as of oil or metal), 精密 (fine and detailed, precise). In 精度, this etymology is active — precision is a kind of refinement, the removal of slop or uncertainty from a measurement, just as refining removes impurities from a material.

In manufacturing contexts, 精度 appears in compounds such as 製造精度 (manufacturing precision), 加工精度 (machining precision), and 寸法精度 (dimensional precision) — the tightness of tolerances in industrial production. In optics and instrumentation, 精度 describes how finely a measurement can be resolved. 高精度 (high precision) and 低精度 (low precision) are standard qualifiers across scientific and engineering writing in Japanese, Chinese, and Korean.
