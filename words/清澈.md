## Notes
- [清](../characters/清.md) "clear; pure; clean" + [澈](../characters/澈.md) "limpid; transparent (of water)" — stand-in for [[澈]], which cannot appear independently
- A near-synonym compound describing water, sky, or sound so clear that one can see straight through it — 清 supplying the broad sense of purity and cleanliness, 澈 narrowing it to the specific visual transparency of clear water (its radical 氵 marks it as a water-word from the start). Mandarin qīngchè and Cantonese cing1 cit3 use it everywhere from rivers to eyes to voices; the Sino-Korean reading 청철 (cheongcheol) survives mainly in literary and name-用 contexts (e.g. the old musical idiom 청철장려, "clear and stirring," describing the bright 우조 mode), while Japanese has no native loanword for the compound and instead renders the same idea with phrases like 澄み切っている — 澈 itself appearing far more often barred to compounds like this one than standing as an everyday word.
