## Notes
洒 is the simplified/alternate form of 灑 (also its own alias here), "to sprinkle, to spray" — most familiar in 灑脫/洒脱 (sǎtuō, "free and easy, unrestrained in manner") and 灑水/洒水 (to sprinkle water). The character is genuinely ambiguous on its own: beyond "sprinkle," it also carries an archaic sense "to wash" (a variant of 洗) and an entirely unrelated classical sense "solemn, dignified" — the three senses take three different Korean readings (세, 쇄, and 선 respectively), and only 쇄 belongs to the "sprinkle" sense used here.
