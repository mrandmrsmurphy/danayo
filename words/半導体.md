## Notes
- [半](../characters/半.md) "half" + [導](../characters/導.md) "guide, lead" + [体](../characters/体.md) "group, body, form" — literally "half-conducting body/substance"; a material whose electrical conductivity sits between a conductor and an insulator.

Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean all build this word the same three-morpheme way, with 体/체 functioning as an abstract noun-forming suffix ("-thing, -substance") rather than its literal "body" sense. Vietnamese restructures it: chất bán dẫn keeps 半 (bán, "half") and 導 (dẫn, "to conduct"), but drops 体/thể entirely, replacing it with the native noun chất ("substance, material") — the same underlying logic, "half-conducting substance," built with a native suffix instead of a calqued Sino one.
