## Notes
- [怨](../characters/怨.md) "to resent; to harbour a grievance" + [望](../characters/望.md) "to gaze toward; to long for"

怨望 combines two characters that together capture the emotional posture of the aggrieved: 怨 — nursing a grievance, typically against someone with power over you — and 望 — gazing longingly toward what is distant or withheld. The compound describes resentful longing: not the hot anger of 怒 but the cold, sustained brooding of someone who feels wronged and cannot stop thinking about it. 怨 is particularly associated with the grievances of subordinates toward superiors, or of those who are wronged but cannot openly retaliate; 望 adds the dimension of unfulfilled expectation or longing for redress that never comes.

The compound appears in classical Chinese texts in contexts of political remonstrance and romantic longing alike. The Shijing (Book of Odes) uses 怨 in both domains, and Qu Yuan's Lisao is saturated with the怨望 posture — grievance toward a ruler who has rejected faithful counsel. In modern Mandarin 怨望 (yuànwàng) is somewhat literary; the more everyday term is 怨恨 (yuànhèn, resentment-hatred) or simply 埋怨 (máiyuàn, to grumble, to blame). In Japanese, 怨望 (enbō) exists but is rare; 恨む (uramu) and 恨み (urami) cover the same emotional ground in native vocabulary. In Korean, 원망 (wonmang) is fully current and common — one of the more alive CJKV compounds in the Korean vernacular.

The 注音 correction here: 望's Dan'a'yo reading ends in ㄫ (velar nasal /ŋ/), giving ㄛㄋㄇㄚㄫ for the compound, not ㄛㄋㄇㄚㄋ. The kwin=false marking reflects the divergence between Dan'a'yo 온망 (following sinographic readings) and Korean 원망, where 怨 corresponds to 원 rather than 온.
