## Notes
- [十 (char)](../characters/十%20(char).md) "ten"

十 is the numeral "ten" in Dan'a'yo — the base unit of the decimal counting system above single digits: 十一 (eleven), 二十 (twenty), 十分 (very; ten parts — adverbial), 三十 (thirty). As one of the foundational numerals, 十 combines freely with single-digit numerals ([[一]] through [[九]]) and higher powers ([[百]], [[千]], [[万]]) to form all Dan'a'yo decimal numbers. The character is a 指事 — originally a single vertical stroke for one or a pictogram for a needle (OC *kjum), borrowed phonetically for 十 (OC *ɡjub "ten"); a horizontal stroke was added later, forming the cross shape of the modern character. 十 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[十]].

Mandarin 十 (shí, ten): 十一 (shíyī, eleven), 二十 (èrshí, twenty), 十分 (shífēn, very; ten-parts-worth), 十字架 (shízìjià, cross — lit. "ten-character frame"). Cantonese 十 (sap6, ten): 十一 (sap6 jat1, eleven), 二十 (ji6 sap6, twenty). Japanese 十 (じゅう/JUU or とお/tō): 十一 (jūichi, eleven), 二十 (nijū, twenty), とお (tō, ten — native reading); 十字 (jūji, cross; plus sign). Korean 십 (十): 십일 (十一, eleven), 이십 (二十, twenty), 열 (yeol, ten — native Korean numeral, used in pure Korean counting). Vietnamese thập (十 — Sino-Vietnamese): mười (ten — native, used in counting), mười một (eleven), hai mươi (twenty).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 십 exactly matches Korean 십. 十 reads 십 (sib/ㄙㄧㄆ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 禪母 (d͡ʑ, voiced palatal affricate) + 緝A三韻 (iɪp, entering tone with -p coda). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean map MC 禪母 to plain s- (ㄙ/ㅅ) and MC 緝韻 entering tone to -ib/-ip (ㄆ/ᆸ — the bilabial stop coda preserved in both). The entering-tone -p coda is retained in both Dan'a'yo (ㄆ = ᆸ in Hangul) and Sino-Korean (십 = s-i-p), producing identical 십 forms. Compare [[不]] (볻 = b-o-t) where Dan'a'yo also retains the entering-tone coda but Korean drops it — 十 is a case where both systems converge on full coda preservation.
