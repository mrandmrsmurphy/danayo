## Notes
- [率](../characters/率.md) "follow, lead; frank, straightforward" + [性](../characters/性.md) "nature, inborn character, essence" — to follow one's nature; to act in accordance with one's heaven-given character.

率性 is the second of the three foundational concepts in the opening of the Doctrine of the Mean (《中庸》), the canonical text on Confucian metaphysics and moral cultivation:

> 天命之謂性，率性之謂道，修道之謂教。
> "What Heaven commands is called human nature (性); following that nature is called the Way (道); cultivating the Way is called teaching (教)."

These three sentences are the most compressed summary of the Confucian metaphysical programme: 性 is received from Heaven as a pure endowment; 率性 — simply following it without deflection — constitutes the Way itself; 修道 (cultivating the Way) is what education is for. The radical implication is that the Way is not imposed from outside but is already latent in human nature: one does not find the Way by seeking it elsewhere, but by following what Heaven has already placed within.

率 in 率性 carries the sense of "straightforwardly following" rather than "leading" — following one's nature honestly, without distortion by selfish desire or social pressure. The contrast is with 戕性 (wounding or distorting one's nature) and with the Daoist concept of 自然 (self-so-ness, spontaneous naturalness), which reaches a similar conclusion by a different route: Daoism counsels non-interference with natural processes; Confucianism counsels active but nature-aligned cultivation.

In Neo-Confucianism (宋明理學), 率性 became the site of debate between 天命之性 (the nature as received from Heaven, purely good in principle) and 氣質之性 (the nature as temperamentally conditioned by material force) — the question of whether following one's nature straightforwardly is always sufficient, or whether 氣質 must first be corrected.
