## Notes
- [簡](../characters/簡.md) "concise; simple" + [易](../characters/易.md) "easy; uncomplicated"

簡易 is "simple; easy" — 簡 (concise, stripped of the superfluous, unpretentious in form) + 易 (easy, smooth to accomplish, accessible). The compound doubles down on simplicity from two angles: 簡 emphasises reduction (brevity, cutting away complexity), while 易 emphasises accessibility (ease of use or understanding). Together they form an intensifying near-synonym pair — something 簡易 is simultaneously uncomplex in structure and easy in execution. The compound also carries a "makeshift; basic" sense: simplified for practical deployment, as opposed to elaborate or refined.

Mandarin 簡易 (jiǎnyì) covers both "simple" and "makeshift/basic": 簡易讀本 (simplified/easy reader), 簡易法庭 (magistrate's court; court without full procedure), 簡易建築 (makeshift/temporary structure), 簡易操作 (simple operation). Cantonese 簡易 (gaan2 ji6) follows the same range. Japanese 簡易 (かんい, kan'i) is productive in formal compounds: 簡易保険 (simplified insurance), 簡易裁判所 (summary court — Japan's lowest court tier), 簡易包装 (simple packaging), 簡易宿所 (simple lodgings; hostel). Korean 간이 (簡易, gani) appears in 간이역 (unmanned/simple train station), 간이 화장실 (portable toilet), 간이식당 (simple eatery). Vietnamese giản dị is a common adjective for "simple; plain; unpretentious" — particularly used to describe a person's manner, lifestyle, or character.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 간역 differs from Korean 간이 in the second syllable. 易 reads 역 (yeg/⼶ㄎ) in Dan'a'yo, following the primary Middle Chinese reading (以 initial + 昔開 final). Sino-Korean 易 has two readings: 역 (used in 역경 = 易經, Book of Changes; 무역 = 貿易, trade) and 이 (used in 용이 = 容易, easy; 간이 = 簡易, simple). Dan'a'yo standardises each character to a single reading derived from its primary Middle Chinese path — 역 throughout for 易 — whereas Sino-Korean preserves a secondary reading (이) in certain fixed compounds.
