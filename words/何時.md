## Notes
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what; which; interrogative" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

何時 is the interrogative temporal correlative — "when; at what time?" — and, like 何処, it shares its written form with a common Japanese word: 何時 in Japanese orthography is いつ (itsu, "when"), an ordinary everyday interrogative adverb. The Dan'a'yo reading *hasi* differs, but the grapheme is identical. The alias 幾時 (jǐshí in Mandarin, gei2 si4 in Cantonese) represents an alternative classical Chinese and Cantonese interrogative for "when" — 幾 (how many) + 時 (time) → "what time, when?" Cantonese 幾時 (gei2 si4) remains the everyday colloquial "when" in Cantonese speech.

Mandarin 何時 (héshí) is a formal/classical "when" (何時回來 "when will you return?"); spoken Mandarin uses 什麼時候 (shénme shíhou, "what time/when?"). Japanese いつ (itsu) is the standard everyday interrogative for time, written either as いつ (hiragana) or 何時 (kanji). Korean 언제 (eonje, "when?") is the everyday form. The original field "몇 시" was a mistranslation — 몇 시 asks "what o'clock?" (a specific clock reading), not "when?" in the broader sense that 何時 covers; 언제 is the correct equivalent.

何時 was also the only 何X correlative to carry a circular alias — 何時 listed as an alias for itself, reflecting the fact that this written form appears in multiple languages with different readings. Removing the circular alias clarifies that 幾時 (classical/Cantonese "when") is the meaningful alternative form. The lack of a neologism tag reflects that 何時/いつ is in active use as a Japanese orthographic compound — like 何処, it is one of the least artificial entries in the interrogative column.
