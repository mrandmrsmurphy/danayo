## Notes
- [下 (char)](../characters/下%20(char).md) "below" + [愚 (char)](../characters/愚%20(char).md) "foolish" — "those at the bottom in foolishness," the lower extreme paired with [[上知]] (those wise from birth) in the *Analects* (論語·陽貨): "唯上知與下愚不移" ("only the wisest and the most foolish do not change"). See [[上知]] for the fuller context of the passage and its later interpretive history.

The pairing reduces Confucius's view of human malleability to its two limiting cases: ordinary people are shaped by habit and circumstance, but 上知 and 下愚 name the small minority at either pole whose disposition no amount of teaching or environment can alter. 下愚 in particular drew centuries of commentarial anxiety, since taken literally it implies a class of people permanently beyond the reach of moral education — in some tension with the more optimistic Confucian premise that virtue is, in principle, attainable through learning and self-cultivation. Note that the Dan'a'yo 注音 ㄏㄚ·ㄨ requires a disambiguating mark at the syllable boundary: without it, the concatenation of 下's ㄏㄚ and 愚's ㄨ would be indistinguishable from [[好]]'s own single-syllable ㄏㄚㄨ.
