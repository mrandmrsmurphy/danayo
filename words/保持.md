## Notes
- [保](../characters/保.md) "protect, safeguard" + [持 (char)](../characters/持%20(char).md) "hold, keep" — to keep something as it is, retaining rather than defending from harm. This word is the viable form of [保](../characters/保.md): 保 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 保持 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 保's sense the retentive "maintain, uphold" one — as in 現状保持 ("maintain the status quo") or 保持記録 ("a held record") — in contrast to [[保護]] (the defensive "shield from harm" sense that [[護]] stands in for instead via [[守護]]).

Mandarin bǎochí and Cantonese bou2ci4 both keep this "maintain, retain" register, distinct from the "protect from threat" sense of 保護 — one keeps a status or condition unchanged, the other defends against something that would change or harm it. Japanese ほじ follows the same reading pattern mechanically. Korean is a special case worth flagging directly: the mechanical Sino-Korean reading 보지 is a homophone of a vulgar slang term, which likely explains why 보지(保持) sees little use as spoken vocabulary in Korean despite being unremarkable in written/Sino-Korean register — 유지 (維持) is the word Korean actually reaches for in speech to mean "maintain."
