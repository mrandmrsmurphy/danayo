## Notes
- [礼 (char)](../characters/礼%20(char).md) "manners" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing, result" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (礼's own points to bare `礼`, 物's own to bare `物`); 礼物 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/礼 (char).md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/物 (char).md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- `korean` corrected to `례물` — North Korean 문화어 (as this vault's `korean` field always uses) does not apply the 두음법칙 initial-sound rule that shifts word-initial ㄹ to ㅇ in the South, so 礼 stays 례 rather than shifting to 예 (matching `characters/礼 (char).md`'s own stored `례`; South Korean 예물 is the everyday form, notably associated with wedding/engagement gifts).
- A clean, directly parallel compound in meaning across the sphere — Mandarin lǐwù, Cantonese lai5 mat6, Japanese れいもつ, Korean 례물, and Vietnamese lễ vật all converge on "a formal gift, present."
