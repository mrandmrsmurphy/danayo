## Notes
業/업/nghiệp all carry a genuine double life across the family: an ordinary, secular sense ("occupation, business, undertaking" — 卒業/졸업/tốt nghiệp, "graduation") sitting alongside the Buddhist technical sense, "karma" (Sanskrit karma, the law that intentional action brings its own consequence). Japanese is the one language here that marks this split with two separate readings of the same character: ぎょう for the everyday "occupation/business" sense (授業, 卒業, 業界), and ごう specifically for the Buddhist "karma" sense — even 自業自得, "you reap what you sow," is read jigōjitoku using ごう, not ぎょう, despite using the same character. Mandarin, Cantonese, Korean, and Vietnamese don't split the reading this way; context alone distinguishes "profession" from "karma."
