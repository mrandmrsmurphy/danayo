## Notes
- [憬](../characters/憬.md) "awaken" + [悟](../characters/悟.md) "realize, awaken to understanding" — a synonym-doubling of two "awaken" roots. This word is the viable form of [憬](../characters/憬.md): 憬 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 憬悟 is its `stand_in` compound — an undistorted match, since 憬's own gloss already is simply "awaken," with nothing left over.

憬悟 (jǐngwù) is a real but literary classical compound for "to awaken to understanding, come to one's senses" — the same semantic territory as 悟's own stand-in [[覚悟]] ("resolve," lit. "awakened readiness"), just without 覚悟's narrowing toward firm resolve specifically. Worth flagging: Japanese けいご (keigo) is an exact homophone of the extremely common 敬語 ("honorific language") — 憬悟 itself is rare/obscure in Japanese, so in practice the sound けいご almost always means 敬語, not this word.
