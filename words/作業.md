## Notes
- [作 (char)](../characters/作%20(char).md) "make; do" + [業 (char)](../characters/業%20(char).md) "work; occupation" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

Mandarin zuòyè, Cantonese zok3 jip6, Japanese さぎょう (sagyō), and Korean 작업 (jageop) are all the standard everyday word for "work, task, operation" (作業員/작업원, "a worker"; 作業服, "work clothes"). In Japanese specifically, 宿題 rather than 作業 is the everyday word for "homework," though 作業 does cover schoolwork/task-based assignments more broadly. Vietnamese tác nghiệp is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search), but carries a narrower professional/technical-operations register (used in journalism, healthcare, security work) than the more general Vietnamese word for "work," công tác.
