## Notes
- [学](../characters/学.md) "study, learning" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank"

学位 is the standard modern term for an academic degree — bachelor's, master's, or doctorate — across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and (as 学位/học vị) Vietnamese. 学 (a shinjitai/simplified form, with traditional 學 listed as its alias) is a 指事 character built around 子 ("child"), depicting a child receiving instruction; it is one of the most productive characters in the entire vocabulary, anchoring an enormous family of academic-discipline compounds already catalogued in [[学]]'s Words section (科学, 文学, 哲学, 心理学, and dozens more).

位 here carries its abstract sense of "rank, status" — a 学位 is literally one's "rank in learning," formalizing scholarly attainment into a graded social position, much as [[爵位]] formalizes noble rank. The three standard degrees — 学士 (bachelor's), 碩士/修士 (master's), 博士 (doctorate) — are themselves built on 士 ("scholar-official"), a separate but conceptually adjacent vocabulary of academic rank.
