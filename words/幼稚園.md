## Notes
- [幼](../characters/幼.md) "infant" + [稚](../characters/稚.md) "young; infantile" + [園](../characters/園.md) "garden; park"

幼稚園 is a Japanese Meiji-era coinage, a calque (loan translation) of the German word Kindergarten — Friedrich Fröbel's 1840 term for his new model of early childhood education. The Meiji Ministry of Education formalized the term when Japan's first officially recognized kindergarten opened in 1876. The rendering is slightly loose: Kindergarten's Kinder ("children") is mapped to 幼稚 (yōchi, "childish; immature") rather than a direct "child" character, and Garten ("garden") maps cleanly to 園.

As a Meiji-era coinage, 幼稚園 spread across the Sinosphere through Japan's cultural and colonial influence. Mainland China adopted 幼稚园 (yòuzhìyuán) but later replaced it with 幼儿园 (yòuéryuán, "infant garden") as the PRC standard — the aliases 幼兒園 and 幼儿园 reflect these variants. Taiwan and Hong Kong retain 幼稚園. Korean 유치원 (yuchiwon) is a direct phonological adaptation of the Japanese pronunciation, typical of the large body of Meiji Japanese coinages that entered Korean during the colonial period. Vietnamese does not use the Sino-Japanese compound; the standard terms are mẫu giáo ("mother education") and nhà trẻ ("children's house").

The Dan'a'yo form retains the Japanese/traditional spelling. kwin is false because the constituent characters' Dan'a'yo bound-form readings (윳듸온) diverge from their Sino-Korean readings (유치원).
