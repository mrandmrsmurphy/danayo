## Notes
- [膨](../characters/膨.md) "swell, bloat, inflate" + [脹](../characters/脹.md) "swell" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (膨's is `膨大`, 脹's is `腫脹`)

Mandarin péngzhàng, Cantonese paang4 zoeng3, Japanese ぼうちょう (bōchō), and Korean 팽창 (paengchang) all cover the neutral, general sense of physical or figurative "swelling, expansion" (人口膨脹/인구팽창, "population growth/expansion"). **Vietnamese has picked up a much stronger political connotation**: bành trướng (verified via search as a real, extremely common Sino-Vietnamese compound) specifically connotes aggressive territorial or political expansionism — chủ nghĩa bành trướng, "expansionism" as an ideology/policy of forceful, appropriative growth — rather than neutral physical swelling, for which Vietnamese instead uses phồng/nở/giãn nở.
