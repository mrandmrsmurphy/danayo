## Notes
- [選](../characters/選.md) "to choose; to select" + [抜](../characters/抜.md) "to pull out; to extract"

選抜 is "to select as the best; selection" — 選 (to choose; to select) + 抜 (to pull out; to extract). The compound names the act of drawing the outstanding from a larger group: not mere selection, but the extraction of the best from the mass — 抜 (pull out, extract) adds the sense of actively reaching in and drawing out what is superior. 選抜 is used for competitive or meritocratic selection: choosing team members, national representatives, scholarship recipients, or candidates singled out for promotion. 選抜 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for [[抜]], the bound character for "to pull out; to extract."

Mandarin 選拔 (xuǎnbá) is the standard term for competitive selection and meritocratic advancement: 選拔人才 (to select talented people), 選拔賽 (a qualifying competition; a selection trial). Cantonese 選拔 (syun2 bat6). Japanese 選抜 (せんばつ, senbatsu) is the standard word: 選抜試験 (selection examination), 選抜チーム (selected team). The term is most famous in baseball: 選抜高校野球大会 (the National High School Baseball Invitational Tournament at Kōshien, universally called センバツ, Senbatsu) is one of Japan's two premier high school baseball tournaments, held each spring. Korean 선발 (選抜, seonbal): 선발 투수 (starting pitcher — where baseball terminology preserves the same Japanese-origin usage), 선발 대표팀 (selected national team). Vietnamese tuyển bạt (Sino-Vietnamese: 選=tuyển, 抜/拔=bạt).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 숸받 differs from Korean 선발 in both syllables. 選 reads 숸 (swen/ㄙ⼔ㄋ) in Dan'a'yo but 선 (seon) in Sino-Korean — MC 心母 (s) + 仙A三合韻 (iuᴇn) gives Dan'a'yo a compound vowel with -w- medial preserved (숸, swen), while Sino-Korean drops the medial to give the plain 선 (seon). 抜 reads 받 (bad/ㄅㄚㄊ) in Dan'a'yo but 발 (bal) in Sino-Korean — MC 並母 (b) + 鎋開韻 (ɣɛt) gives Dan'a'yo a final -t preserved as ㄊ (Romanised d), while Sino-Korean shifts the MC -t final to -l, yielding 발 (bal). This -t → -l shift for MC entering-tone finals is a regular Sino-Korean correspondence in this rhyme class.
