## Notes
- [芦](../characters/芦.md) "reed" + [櫂](../characters/櫂.md) "oar" — stand-in for [[櫂]], which cannot appear independently.

芦 here stands in for its alias 櫓 ("a scull; a long oar worked from the stern of a boat"), not for its primary sense of "reed." 櫓櫂 (lǔzhào) is the everyday compound for "oars" in general — the equipment used to propel and steer a small boat — covering both the scull (櫓) worked at the stern and the handheld paddle or oar (櫂) used at the sides.

Because 芦 is the vault's canonical bearer of Dan'a'yo phonology for this character family (鹵 ro / 로 / ㄌㄛ), the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 here are mechanically concatenated from 芦's and 櫂's own fixed Dan'a'yo values (로톳 / lotou / ㄌㄛㄊㄛㄨ). The mandarin, cantonese, japanese, korean, and vietnamese fields instead reflect the real-world readings of 櫓櫂 as actually pronounced — using 櫓's own attested reading (lǔ / lou5 / ろ / 노 / lỗ) rather than 芦's (lú / lou4 / RO / 로 / lư). This is the same divergence pattern seen in 波蘭, 六楽, and 魏峨: the vault character supplies the phonology, the alias supplies the meaning and the real-world reading.
