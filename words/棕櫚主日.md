## Notes
- [[棕櫚]] "palm tree" + [[主日]] "Lord's Day" — each half is itself a word in the vault.
- Individual characters: [棕](../characters/棕.md) + [櫚](../characters/櫚.md) + [主](../characters/主.md) + [日 (char)](../characters/日%20(char).md)
- The alias 棕枝主日 uses 棕枝 ("palm branch"), which is itself an alias of 棕櫚 in the vault. The Mandarin and Cantonese readings reflect the established Chinese form 棕枝主日 (*zōngzhī zhǔrì*) rather than the literal 棕櫚主日 reading (*zōnglǚ zhǔrì*).

Palm Sunday is the Sunday before Easter, commemorating Jesus's entry into Jerusalem when crowds spread palm branches on the road before him (John 12:13). The event is recorded in all four Gospels, each noting the branches slightly differently — palms (φοῖνιξ) in John, cut branches (κλάδοι) in Matthew, leafy branches (στοιβάδες) in Mark, and just clothing in Luke — giving rise to minor variation in the holiday's name across traditions.

The Chinese term 棕枝主日 / 棕櫚主日 ("palm branch / palm tree Lord's Day") is the standard across Chinese-speaking Christian communities. Korean 종려주일 (棕欄主日) uses 棕欄 for the palm and 주일 for Sunday. Japanese uses しゅろのにちようび (棕梠の日曜日, "palm tree Sunday") in most liturgical contexts.
