## Notes
- [之 (char)](../characters/之%20(char).md) "relational particle" + [間](../characters/間.md) "between, among" — stand-in for [[間]], which cannot appear independently

之間 is the locative-relational particle marking "between, among" — attaching after one or more nouns (often coordinated with 和/與, "and") to mark the space, interval, or relationship between them: 中日之間 (between China and Japan), 朋友之間 (among friends). Like [[由]] and [[比]], this belongs to the vault's set of case-particle words built on classical Chinese function characters; the frontmatter `japanese`/`korean`/`vietnamese` fields here record each language's own reading of the *characters themselves* (a formal cross-reference, following the same convention as those other particle pages), not the everyday functional word — those real equivalents are given below instead. 間 cannot stand alone as a vault entry (`boundedness: 100`), so 之間 is what legitimizes it.

In the comparison chart, 之間 parallels Mandarin 之間/之间 (zhījiān, "between"), Cantonese 之間 (zi1gaan1), Japanese 〜の間 (~no aida) or bare あいだ (aida), Korean 사이 (sai, native — 之間's own literal Sino-Korean reading 지간 is rare and mostly literary/classical), and Vietnamese giữa ("between, among," a native word, not a Sino-Vietnamese calque of 之間). Mandarin 之間 leans formal/written — everyday speech often prefers plain 中間 (zhōngjiān) or a bare 和…之間 construction; 之 itself is a classical possessive/relational particle that survives productively only in fixed compounds like this one in modern Mandarin.

kwin is false: 之's own reading (티, ti) doesn't match its Sino-Korean reading (지, ji — different initial consonant class), while 間's own reading (간, gan) does match Korean 간 exactly. Since the compound's overall `kwin` reflects whether *every* constituent character individually matches Korean, one mismatch (之) is enough to make the whole compound `false`, even though 間 alone would be `true`.
