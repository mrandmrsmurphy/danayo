## Notes
- [者 (char)](../characters/者%20(char).md) "-er; -ist; one who"

者 is the agentive suffix in Dan'a'yo — "-er; -ist; one who" — suffixing to a verb or noun to derive the agent or practitioner of an activity: 教師者 (teacher — one who teaches), 学者 (scholar — one who studies), 敗者 (loser — one who lost), 記者 (journalist — one who records). In the topic-retaining particle system, 者 marks the active agent, identifying who performs the action or holds the role, complementing [[被]] (the passive patient marker). 者 is one of the most productive word-forming suffixes in the sinographic lexicon, present in hundreds of classical and modern compounds across all five CJKV languages. 者 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[者]].

Mandarin 者 (zhě): the literary agentive suffix — 学者 (xuézhě, scholar), 记者 (jìzhě, journalist), 作者 (zuòzhě, author), 读者 (dúzhě, reader), 患者 (huànzhě, patient — one who suffers), 强者 (qiángzhě, the strong one; the powerful). In modern Mandarin, 者 is productive only in formal/literary compounds; colloquial -的 (de + nominal) performs the agentive function in speech: 唱歌的 (the one who sings). Cantonese 者 (ze2): 學者 (hohk6 ze2, scholar), 記者 (gei3 ze2, journalist) in Sino-Cantonese formal compounds. Japanese 者 (もの/mono or しゃ/SHA): 学者 (gakusha, scholar), 記者 (kisha, journalist), 読者 (dokusha, reader); the native reading もの (mono) has a broader "person; being; thing" sense: あの者 (that person). Korean 자 (者, ja): 학자 (學者, scholar), 기자 (記者, journalist), 독자 (讀者, reader), 환자 (患者, patient) — directly parallel to Mandarin forms. Vietnamese giả (者): học giả (學者, scholar), ký giả (記者, journalist), tác giả (作者, author).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 차 does not match Korean 자. 者 reads 차 (ca/ㄑㄚ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 麻三開韻 (ia) gives an aspirated palatal affricate with the plain -a vowel. Dan'a'yo maps MC 章母 to ㄑ (aspirated ch-, ㅊ in Hangul), while Sino-Korean 자 (ja) maps the same MC initial to ㅈ (unaspirated j-). The vowel is identical (-a in both), but the initial differs: Dan'a'yo 차 (aspirated ㅊ) vs Korean 자 (unaspirated ㅈ). The systematic aspiration of MC 章母 in Dan'a'yo vs unaspirated Korean ㅈ is a recurring pattern — also seen in [[只]] (ㄑㄝ/처 vs 지) and [[且]] (ㄑㄛ/초 vs 차), where Dan'a'yo aspirates but Korean does not.
