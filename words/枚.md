## Notes
- [枚 (char)](../characters/枚%20(char).md) "flat sheets; thin stalks"

枚 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for flat, thin, or sheet-like objects — 量詞, counting sheets of paper, flat medals, tiles, stamps, leaves, and similar items: 一枚紙 (one sheet of paper), 三枚切手 (three stamps), 數枚板 (several planks). The character is a 會意 compound of 木 (wood) and 攵 (action), with original sense "a wooden twig; a slender stalk." In Classical Chinese, 枚 could count almost any individual item (不可枚舉, "cannot be enumerated one by one"), but has specialized in modern East Asian classifier systems to flat, thin, or coin-like objects. 枚 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[枚]].

The CJKV classifier landscape for flat and thin objects shows significant divergence. Mandarin uses 张 (zhāng) as the standard classifier for flat objects: 一张纸 (one sheet of paper), 一张票 (one ticket), 一张桌子 (one table); 枚 appears mainly in elevated register for small round objects like coins and medals. Cantonese 張 (zoeng1): 一張紙 (one sheet of paper), 一張飛 (one ticket — colloquial). Japanese 枚 (まい/MAI) is highly productive: it counts flat objects of all kinds — 一枚の紙 (one sheet of paper), 三枚の切手 (three stamps), 五枚のコイン (five coins), 二枚の板 (two boards) — and is among the more regular Japanese counters. Korean 장 (張, jang) parallels Mandarin 张: 종이 한 장 (one sheet of paper), 사진 두 장 (two photographs). Vietnamese tờ: một tờ giấy (one sheet of paper), một tờ báo (one newspaper); Sino-Vietnamese mai (枚) appears in formal compounds.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 뫼 does not match Korean 매. 枚 reads 뫼 (moi/ㄇㄛㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 明母 (m) + 灰韻 (uʌi) gives a bilabial nasal onset with the -oi diphthong. Dan'a'yo preserves the MC -uʌi as -oi (뫼/moi), while Sino-Korean 매 (mae) collapses the labial medial with the following vowel, producing plain -ae — the same 灰韻 divergence seen in [[杯]] (뵈/boi vs 배/bae). Note that 枚 (ㄇㄛㄧ/moi/뫼) is a Dan'a'yo homophone of [[毎]] (ㄇㄛㄧ/moi/뫼, "each; every") — both derive from MC 明母 + 灰韻, sharing identical Dan'a'yo phonology while remaining semantically distinct.
