## Notes
- [上 (char)](../characters/上%20(char).md) "up, above" + [面](../characters/面.md) "face, surface" — the upward-facing surface of something; by extension, a thing's outward appearance.

上面/상면/thượng diện all agree on being the more formal, specific term for "the upper surface" as opposed to the plain locational word for "above" — Mandarin/Cantonese shàngmiàn/soeng6 min6 sit at ordinary register and are used constantly in everyday speech for "on top of X," but Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all reach for a plainer alternative first: Japanese 上 (ue) for casual "on top," reserving 上面 (jōmen) for technical or formal contexts describing an actual surface or plane; Korean prefers native 위 for everyday "on top," with 상면 read as more bookish, closer to describing a literal upper surface (e.g. of an object or terrain); Vietnamese likewise defaults to native mặt trên or phía trên in speech, with thượng diện as the classical Sino-Vietnamese equivalent.
