## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

其様 is the medial manner correlative — "that way; in that manner; as you/we were just discussing." Like all 其-column entries, it picks up a referent from the shared discourse context or the addressee's frame: 其様 refers to the method just demonstrated, the approach already established in conversation, or a manner both speaker and listener understand to be "that way." In conversational use: 其様にすれば良い ("if you do it that way, it'll be fine"); 其様に考えたことはなかった ("I'd never thought of it that way").

Japanese そのように (sono yō ni, "in that way — medial") or そうやって (sō yatte, "doing it that way") are the precise medial manner forms. Korean 그렇게 (geureoke, "like that — medial") is the everyday form, the middle member of the 이렇게/그렇게/저렇게 (this way/that way/that way-distal) set. Mandarin 那樣 (nàyàng, "like that, in that way") covers both medial and distal manner in Chinese, since Mandarin has a binary rather than three-way deictic system. Vietnamese như thế (like that) or như thế đó (in that way there — medial).

The 様 row is unique in the correlative table in that all seven entries are adverbial rather than nominal: where 事/物/人/名 correlatives substitute for noun phrases, and 処/時 correlatives substitute for place/time adverbials, 様 correlatives substitute for manner adverbials — answering "how?" rather than "what?", "who?", "where?", or "when?" In Japanese grammar these are pro-adverbs (そう, こう, ああ for manner); in Dan'a'yo they fill the same slot systematically across all seven columns.
