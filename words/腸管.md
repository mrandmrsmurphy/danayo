## Notes
- [腸](../characters/腸.md) "intestine, sausage" + [管](../characters/管.md) "pipe, tube" — stand-in for [[腸]], which cannot appear independently

**A third instance this sweep of the same striking pattern found on [[羽翼]]/右翼 and [[指甲]]/지갑**: 腸's Sino-Japanese/Sino-Korean reading (ちょう/장) coincides exactly with 長 ("long, chief"), so 腸管 is a genuine, live homophone of the unrelated, very common word 長官 — Japanese ちょうかん (chōkan, "government minister/director-general") and Korean 장관 (jang-gwan, the same "minister" sense, or also "spectacle, grand sight" via a different unrelated 壯觀). Verified both 腸管 and 長官 are real, independently attested words in both languages (Japanese via search; the vault's own stored `korean` field had been wrongly replaced with the native word `창자`, "intestines," rather than this compound's own genuine Sino-Korean reading — corrected).

Vietnamese uses the honest compositional `tràng quản`; "tràng" alone is a thoroughly productive Sino-Vietnamese medical morpheme for "intestine" (trực tràng "rectum," đại tràng "colon," tá tràng "duodenum"), though the specific two-syllable compound isn't independently confirmed the way the Japanese/Korean readings are — the everyday native word for intestines generally is `ruột`.

**Aliases corrected**: kept `肠管` (genuine simplified form). Removed `腸子`/`肠子` — not orthographic variants of 腸管 at all, but a distinct, more colloquial compound (腸 + the noun-forming suffix 子, as in 種子/卵子 earlier this sweep) with no file of its own in this vault yet; flagged as a future word-creation candidate rather than folded in as if it were the same word, same treatment as [[長牙]]'s 尖牙 finding.
