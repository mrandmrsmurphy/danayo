## Notes
- [挫](../characters/挫.md) "failure, setback, frustration" + [折](../characters/折.md) "fold, break"
- This word is the stand-in for [[挫]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A compound meaning a serious check or reversal — the experience of being bent back on one's course. In Japanese, ざせつ (zasetsu) is the standard word for frustration, defeat, or loss of momentum; 挫折感 (zasetsu-kan) is the sense of having failed or been thwarted. Chinese 挫折 (cuòzhé) similarly means "setback" or "frustration," and is frequently used with negative states: 遭受挫折 "to suffer a setback." Korean 좌절 (挫折) carries the same meaning with a particular emotional weight — a common word in discussions of failure, despair, and resilience. The etymological image is of being bent and broken: 挫 "to crush, collapse inward" + 折 "to snap, fold over."
