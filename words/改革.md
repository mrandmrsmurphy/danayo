## Notes
- [改 (char)](../characters/改%20(char).md) "to change, redo" + [革](../characters/革.md) "leather; to remove, to reform"

改革 is the standard word for "reform" — a deliberate, structural change to a system, institution, or policy, as in 経済改革 ("economic reform") or 土地改革 ("land reform"). 改, a character built from 己 ("self") plus 攵 ("to strike, to act upon"), means "to change, to correct, to redo," and already anchors [[改善]] ("improvement"), [[改変]] ("alteration"), and [[改竄]] ("to falsify, tamper with" — all cross-linked from [[改 (char)]]'s own Notes).

革's core meaning is "animal hide, leather" — a 象形 pictograph of a stretched hide, head at top and tail/legs at bottom (noted in [[革]]'s own Notes) — but it carries a second, equally important sense of "to remove, to strip away, to renew," most famously in [[革命]] ("revolution," literally "to strip away the [Heavenly] Mandate," cross-linked from [[革]]'s Words section). 改革 and 革命 form a classic pair in East Asian political vocabulary: 改革 is gradual, working-within-the-system change, while 革命 is the wholesale overthrow that 改革 is so often invoked to forestall.
