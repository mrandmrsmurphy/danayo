## Notes
- [全 (char)](../characters/全%20(char).md) "whole; all; entire"

全 is the totalizing quantifier in Dan'a'yo — "all; whole; entire; complete" — prefixing to a noun to indicate totality or completeness: 全人 (everyone; all the people), 全体 (the whole body; entirety), 全世界 (the whole world). In the Dan'a'yo quantifier system, 全 marks maximal quantity — the entirety of whatever is modified — contrasting with [[少]] (few; scarce). The character is a 会意 of 入 (to enter; jade-related) and 王 (jade ruler), etymologized as "a whole, unbroken piece of jade; pure jade," extending to "complete; whole; entire; perfect." 全 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[全]].

Mandarin 全 (quán, all; whole; complete): 全部 (quánbù, all; entirety), 全国 (quánguó, the whole country), 完全 (wánquán, completely; perfectly), 全面 (quánmiàn, comprehensive; all-sided). Cantonese 全 (cyun4): 全部 (cyun4 bou6, all; entirety), 全面 (cyun4 min6, comprehensive). Japanese 全 (ZEN/ぜん): 全部 (zenbu, all; entirely), 完全 (kanzen, perfect; complete), 全体 (zentai, the whole; entirety); native すべて (subete, all; everything), まったく (mattaku, completely; entirely). Korean 전 (全): 전부 (全部, all; entirety), 전체 (全體, the whole), 완전 (完全, perfect; complete). Vietnamese toàn (全): toàn bộ (全部, all; entirely), hoàn toàn (完全, completely; thoroughly).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 줜 does not match Korean 전. 全 reads 줜 (jwen/ㄐ⼔ㄋ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 從母 (d͡z, voiced alveolar affricate) + 仙A三合韻 (iuᴇn). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean map MC 從母 to a voiced palatal/alveolar j- (ㄐ/ㅈ). The MC final iuᴇn: Dan'a'yo renders it as -wen (줜 = j-w-en, with the -w- round medial preserved as ㅘ in Hangul), while Korean gives -jeon (전 = j-eon, folding the medial differently without the distinct -w-). Dan'a'yo 줜 retains the MC round medial; Korean 전 drops it. The same medial-preservation pattern appears in [[将]] (좡 vs 장) where Dan'a'yo keeps the MC -u- but Korean drops it.
