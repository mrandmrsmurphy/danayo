## Notes
- [天](../characters/天%20(char).md) "heaven; sky" + [竺](../characters/竺.md) "bamboo; India" — stand-in for [[竺]], which cannot appear independently
- 天竺 — literally "heavenly bamboo," though the second character here carries no botanical meaning at all — is one of the oldest Sinospheric names for India, first attested in the Book of Later Han as a phonetic transcription of a word ultimately tracing back to Sanskrit síndhu "river, the Indus." Through the long history of Buddhist translation it became *the* name for India as the homeland of the Buddha and the source of the sutras: Xuanzang's famous seventh-century pilgrimage was a journey "to 天竺 in search of scriptures," a frame later immortalized in the novel Journey to the West. The name and its reading travelled with Buddhism into Japanese as てんじく (Tenjiku) — still recognizable today in place-names, shop-names, and the phrase 天竺もめん "Indian cotton, calico" — and into Sino-Korean 천축 (cheonchuk) and Sino-Vietnamese Thiên Trúc, both likewise preserved mainly in historical and Buddhist contexts rather than everyday geography, where the modern transliterations (印度, Ấn Độ, etc.) have long since taken over.
