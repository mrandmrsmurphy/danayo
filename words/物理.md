## Notes
- [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing, result" + [理](../characters/理.md) "-ology, reason, logic" — literally "the logic/principle of things." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (物's own points to bare `物`, 理's own to `理由`); 物理 is an independent compound. `kwin: true` per the AND-rule: both constituent characters are individually `kwin: true`.
- **Content corrected**: `mandarin` had a tone typo (`wùlí`→`wùlǐ`), checked against `characters/理.md`'s own stored `lǐ`. `english` previously read only "nature" — the modern, primary, everyday sense across the whole sphere (Mandarin wùlǐ, Cantonese mat6 lei5, Japanese ぶつり, Korean 물리, Vietnamese vật lý) is "physics," the school subject and science (物理课 "physics class"); the more abstract "nature/principle of things" survives as an older, more literary/classical sense. Rewrote to lead with "physics."
