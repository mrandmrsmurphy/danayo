## Notes
- [会 (char)](../characters/会%20(char).md) "meet, gather" + [意](../characters/意.md) "meaning, idea" — one of the [[六書]] (liùshū), the traditional six-category classification of Chinese characters.

**会意** (*huìyì*, lit. "joining meanings") characters combine two or more semantic elements, each contributing part of its own meaning, to express a new idea that none of the components conveys alone. The classic example is 信 ("trust, sincerity"): 人 ("person") + 言 ("speech") — a person's words held to be true. Another is 休 ("rest"): 人 ("person") + 木 ("tree") — a person leaning against a tree. Unlike 形声 characters, no component in a 会意 character is purely phonetic; every element is meant to be "read" for sense.

会意 is traditionally placed third among the six categories, after 象形 (pictograms) and 指事 (simple indicatives), and is understood as a step toward greater abstraction: where 象形 depicts a single concrete thing, 会意 synthesizes a new concept out of two or more existing ones, foreshadowing the combinatorial logic that would later dominate 形声 character formation.

In Japanese, 会意文字 (*kaii moji*) is taught as one of the four "structural" categories of 六書. Note that 会意 (*huìyì*/かいい, "compound ideograph") is a homophone in Korean with 회의 meaning "meeting, conference" (會議) — the two are unrelated etymologically but happen to share the same Sino-Korean reading. Korean and Vietnamese linguistic pedagogy preserve the 六書 framework, including this term, from the same Han-dynasty *Shuowen Jiezi* tradition.
