## Notes
- [恩](../characters/恩.md) "kindness; mercy" + [施](../characters/施.md) "to bestow; to apply; to carry out" — the active, verbal dimension of grace; the act of conferring blessing or favor upon another

恩施 is the only verb among the Dan'a'yo 恩- words. Where [[恩典]] is the doctrinal principle, [[恩寵]] is the relational quality, and [[恩意]] is the inner disposition, 恩施 is the outward act: the concrete event of grace being given. 施 is used in classical Chinese for the application or implementation of something — an official bestowing rewards, a physician applying treatment.

In theological use, 恩施 frames grace as dynamic event rather than static attribute: God actively confers blessing, the act itself is the grace. This active dimension is important in traditions emphasizing grace as prevenient or operative, not merely a quality God possesses but one God exercises.
