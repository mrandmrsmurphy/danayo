## Notes
- [指](../characters/指.md) "finger; toe" + [甲 (char)](../characters/甲%20(char).md) "armor" — the hard covering at a fingertip, "nail," by extension from 甲's own "armor, hard shell" sense.

**Mandarin has three genuine, independently attested readings, not a typo-dump**: zhǐjia (the standard neutral-tone colloquial form), zhǐjiǎ (the fuller formal/citation-tone form), and zhījia (a documented regional colloquial variant with tone drift on the first syllable) — verified via search rather than collapsing to one.

**A striking, likely-unintentional Korean homophone**: the honest compositional reading of this compound is 지갑 (jigap) — but 지갑 is also, completely independently, the extremely common everyday Korean word for "wallet" (from unrelated hanja, 紙匣, "paper case"). Real Korean does not use 指甲 as a word at all — the standard, exclusively-used term for fingernail is the native 손톱 — so this near-total collision is mostly theoretical, but worth flagging prominently given how common 지갑 "wallet" is. Japanese likewise doesn't really use 指甲 as an independent compound (the standard word is 爪, tsume); the frontmatter しこう (shikō) is an honest on'yomi-compositional reading only, not a claim of real usage — interestingly, `characters/甲 (char).md`'s own `japanese_native` list separately includes つめ (tsume) as one of 甲's own alternate kun'yomi (alongside よろい "armor" and きのえ "first heavenly stem"), which is what the word's old, wrong field had actually been quoting. Vietnamese chỉ giáp is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (found in historical/Qing-dynasty nail-care contexts, e.g. hộ giáp "nail guard"), though the everyday word is the native móng tay.

**Homophone**: [[自家]] "one's own family/household" shares this word's exact reading (ㄐㄧㄜㄍㄚㄆ/jiǝgab/즤갑) — the page already flagged this informally; converted to the standard `[!warning]` callout format in the correct position (after the meta-bind-embed), and fixed the reciprocal callout on 自家 the same way.
