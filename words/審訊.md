## Notes
- [審](../characters/審.md) "to examine; to judge; to deliberate" + [訊](../characters/訊.md) "to question; to interrogate; to summon"

審訊 is "inquest; judicial hearing; interrogation" — formal legal questioning of a witness, suspect, or party before an authority. 審 (to examine carefully, to judge, to deliberate — the judge's process of examining a case) + 訊 (to question, to interrogate, to summon for testimony). Both characters carry senses of authoritative examination: 審 from a position of judgment, 訊 through directed verbal inquiry. The compound covers the formal, procedural questioning that occurs within official legal proceedings.

Mandarin 審訊 (shěnxùn) is the formal legal term for interrogation and judicial questioning: 審訊嫌疑人 (to interrogate a suspect), 接受審訊 (to submit to questioning), 審訊記錄 (interrogation record). Cantonese 審訊 (sam2 seon3) is likewise used in Hong Kong legal contexts. 審訊 is not a standard compound in Japanese — the semantic territory is covered by 審問 (しんもん, shinmon, inquest/interrogation) or 尋問 (じんもん, jinmon, questioning/cross-examination); the japanese field gives 審問 as the closest Japanese equivalent. Korean 심문 (審問, simun) is the standard legal equivalent for official interrogation. Vietnamese thẩm vấn (thẩm = 審, vấn = to ask) is the standard Sino-Vietnamese legal term.

The two component characters appear independently across legal and administrative vocabulary: 審 in 審判 (trial/judgment), 審査 (review/audit), 審議 (deliberation); 訊 in 訊問 (interrogation), 訊息 (information/message). The Japanese absence of 審訊 as a standard compound reflects Meiji-era legal vocabulary choices — Japanese legal language drew from classical Chinese filtered through Edo-period and Meiji translation projects, resulting in different compound selections for overlapping concepts. kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 심신 ≠ Korean 심문.
