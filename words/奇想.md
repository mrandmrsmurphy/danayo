## Notes
- [奇](../characters/奇.md) "strange; odd" + [想](../characters/想.md) "thought; concept"

An 奇想 is a thought that lands sideways — an idea whose strangeness is part of its character, whether delightfully inventive or simply eccentric. The compound captures the flash of inspiration that is too odd to be mundane and too specific to be a mere daydream.

In Chinese the word frequently appears in the set phrase 突發奇想 (*tūfā qíxiǎng*, "a sudden wild idea"), describing the moment an outlandish thought arrives unbidden. In Japanese きそう carries a similar flavour, found in artistic and literary contexts where a work is praised or described as stemming from an unusual, creative leap of imagination — a fantastic conceit rather than a logical conclusion.
