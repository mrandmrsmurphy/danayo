## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human being"

其人 is the medial correlative in the person column — "that person (in your sphere, or known to us both)" — and also serves as Dan'a'yo's gender-neutral third-person singular pronoun: "he, she, they (singular)." The aliases 他 (char), 她, and 它 make this dual function explicit: in Chinese, 他 (tā, he), 她 (tā, she), and 它 (tā, it) are the gender-distinguished third-person pronouns, all pronounced identically; Dan'a'yo collapses this into the single form 其人, making no grammatical gender distinction in third-person person reference.

In Mandarin, 其人 (qírén) appears in classical and formal contexts meaning "that person, the person in question" (明其人之意 "clarify the intention of that person"; 不知其人 "not knowing that person"). Japanese その人 (sono hito) is the medial person demonstrative — "that person (near you or known between us)." Korean 그 사람 (geu saram) is its exact equivalent. Vietnamese nó is the informal third-person pronoun (he/she/it) for people in casual speech; the full Vietnamese pronoun system distinguishes by age and social rank (anh/chị/em etc.), a complexity that Dan'a'yo's unified 其人 does not replicate.

The classical Chinese 其 already carried third-person possessive meaning ("its, his, her"), making 其人 a natural candidate for the third-person person pronoun role. By standardising 其人 as a gender-neutral singular third person, Dan'a'yo aligns with English singular "they" and avoids the gender asymmetry embedded in Chinese 他/她 or the Japanese 彼/彼女 (which have additionally narrowed to mean "boyfriend/girlfriend" in colloquial use). Within the correlative table, 其人 is the only entry that carries this double function: deictic pronoun *and* personal pronoun.
