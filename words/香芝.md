## Notes
- [香](../characters/香.md) "fragrant, incense" + [芝](../characters/芝.md) "grass"

香芝 is the name of Kashiba City (香芝市, かしばし), a city in Nara Prefecture in the Yamato basin of central Japan. The Japanese reading かしば (Kashiba) is a kun'yomi place-name reading that does not follow the standard on'yomi of the characters (which would yield こうし, kōshi). This kind of special place-name reading (*jukujikun*, 熟字訓) is common in Japanese geography, where ancient place names are given historical or local pronunciations that predate the sinitic readings.

Kashiba lies in the historically significant Yamato region, adjacent to several ancient kofun burial mounds and near the old Takeuchi road (竹内街道), one of Japan's oldest roads. The character 香芝 was added to the Jōyō kanji list specifically because of its use in this prefecture name — a fact noted on the 香 character page.
