## Notes
- [殉](../characters/殉.md) "be martyred" + [難](../characters/難.md) "difficulty, disaster" — stand-in for both [[殉]] and [[難]], neither of which can appear independently
- 殉難 names the act, and the person, of dying for a cause — not death that simply happens to someone, but death accepted or sought out in the face of calamity (難) as the price of loyalty (殉, whose graphemic root 旬 originally marked a span of time, extended by the 歹 "death" radical into the specific sense of dying *alongside* something — a lord, a cause, a country). Where 殉教 narrows the sacrifice to religious martyrdom and 殉国 to dying for one's nation, 殉難 keeps the field wide: a magistrate who refuses to flee an invading army, sailors who go down with their ship, victims memorialized on a 殉難碑 (memorial to the fallen). Mandarin xùnnàn, Cantonese seon1 naan6, Sino-Japanese じゅんなん (junnan), Sino-Korean 순난 (sunnan), and Sino-Vietnamese tuẫn nạn all preserve this same solemn, commemorative register — a word that belongs to monuments and elegies rather than everyday speech.
