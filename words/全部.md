## Notes
- [全 (char)](../characters/全%20(char).md) "whole" + [部 (char)](../characters/部%20(char).md) "part" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

Mandarin quánbù, Cantonese cyun4 bou6, Japanese ぜんぶ (zenbu), Korean 전부 (jeonbu), and Vietnamese toàn bộ are all the standard, everyday word for "all, the entirety, everything" across the sphere — a close near-synonym of [[全体]], with 部 ("part") here framing the whole as the sum of all its parts, rather than 体's "body." Korean's field was narrowed from a comma-dump that also included the native words 모두/죄다 ("all, everyone"), the same pattern found on [[全体]] one iteration ago.
