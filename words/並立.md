## Notes
- [並 (char)](../characters/並%20(char).md) "side by side, simultaneously" + [立 (char)](../characters/立%20(char).md) "stand, establish" — to stand erect together, neither one subordinate to the other.

並 is the vocabulary's basic marker of simultaneity and equal footing — it also appears bound into [[並行]]-type vocabulary not yet created — while 立 contributes the "stand up, be established" sense found in 独立 and 国立 (neither yet created, but both predictable from the same root). 並立 specifically describes two or more things — theories, political parties, languages — that exist and hold their ground at the same time without one displacing the other; it is the natural word, for instance, for the way Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all 並立 as living daughters of a shared Sinitic vocabulary, which is itself close to the founding premise of Dan'a'yo. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
