## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human being"

皆人 is the universal correlative in the person column — "everyone; all people, collectively." The character 皆 (Dan'a'yo *gye*, ㄍ⼶) is the collective universal quantifier, treating the full set of people as a whole rather than distributing across them individually (that is 毎人's role). The Chinese equivalent is 大家 (dàjiā, lit. "great family → everyone"), listed as an alias; in Cantonese, daai6 gaa1 (大家) is similarly the standard colloquial word for "everyone." Dan'a'yo prefers 皆人 as the systematically compositional form within the correlative table, while 大家 is the more idiomatic Chinese alias.

Japanese 皆さん (mina-san) is one of the most common words in Japanese for "everyone" and is the standard form in public address, broadcast openings, and polite collective reference (皆さん、こんにちは "Hello, everyone"; 皆さんのおかげで "thanks to everyone"). The character 皆 (mina/kai) is the same used in Dan'a'yo. Korean 모두 (modu) is the colloquial "all, everyone"; 모두들 adds a collective-plural nuance; 여러분 (yeoreobun) is the polite public-address form. Vietnamese mọi người (all people, everyone) is the standard equivalent.

皆人 (collective) contrasts with 毎人 (distributive) exactly as 皆事 contrasts with 毎事: 皆人 addresses or refers to all people as a group, while 毎人 distributes a predicate across individuals one by one. 皆人が知っている ("everyone knows," understood as a collective fact) vs 毎人が確認する ("each person checks, one at a time"). In practice, 皆人 appears most naturally in public address, universal statements, and philosophical generalisations, while 毎人 appears in enumeration, allocation, and distribution contexts.
