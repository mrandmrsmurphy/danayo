## Notes
- [汚 (char)](../characters/汚%20(char).md) "dirty" + [垢](../characters/垢.md) "dirt, filth" — stand-in for [[垢]], which cannot appear independently.

汚 here stands in for its alias 污 ("dirty, filthy"), which shares its primary sense exactly — 汚/汙/污 are all variant forms of the same character with the same reading, so there is no phonetic divergence to reconcile in this case. 污垢/汚垢 (wūgòu) is the everyday word for the grime and filth that accumulates on a surface — skin, clothing, dishes, or any object — through use and exposure, distinct from more abstract or moral senses of "dirty" (corruption, disgrace) that 汚 alone can also carry.

Because 汚 and 垢 are true variants in this sense, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 (오곳 / 'ogou / ㄛㄍㄛㄨ) — mechanically concatenated from 汚's and 垢's own fixed Dan'a'yo values — directly reflect 污垢's real-world readings, with no adjustment needed beyond the normal cross-linguistic divergence already present in 垢's own readings.
