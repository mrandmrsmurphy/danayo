## Notes
- [暗 (char)](../characters/暗%20(char).md) "dark; hidden; concealed" + [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "within; midst of"

暗中 is "in the dark; in secret; covertly" — 暗 (dark, dim, hidden, concealed) + 中 (within, in the midst of). The compound literally means "in the midst of darkness" or "within the hidden." 暗 contributes concealment and obscurity; 中 contributes immersion within a state or location. In Dan'a'yo, 暗中 is a state-noun ("the dark; the hidden space") that requires the postposition 於 (at/in) to form a complete locative phrase — 於暗中 = "in the dark; in secret." This parallels the classical Chinese use of 中 as a location suffix: 水中 (in the water), 途中 (on the way), 空中 (in the air).

Mandarin 暗中 (ànzhōng) is used adverbially: 暗中操縱 (to manipulate from behind the scenes), 暗中觀察 (covert observation), 暗中幫助 (to help secretly). Cantonese 暗中 (ngam3 zung1) similarly. Japanese 暗中 (あんちゅう, anchū) appears in the idiom 暗中模索 (ançhū mosaku, "groping in the dark; fumbling for an answer; trial and error in the absence of information"). Korean 암중 (暗中, amjung) is used in formal and literary contexts. Vietnamese ám trung is the Sino-Vietnamese equivalent.

kwin: true for 暗中 — Dan'a'yo 암중 exactly matches Korean 암중. Both 暗 (암/'am) and 中 (중/jung) follow closely parallel MC-derived paths in Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, converging precisely. The body of the original file noted that 暗中 "requires 於 afterwards" — this is the key grammatical distinction between 暗中 (the state/location noun) and a bare adverb like 暗暗 (secretly, without any postposition needed).
