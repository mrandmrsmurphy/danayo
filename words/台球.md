## Notes
- [台 (char)](../characters/台%20(char).md) "platform, stand" + [球 (char)](../characters/球%20(char).md) "ball" — a ball played on a stand/table; billiards.

台球 is specifically Mainland Mandarin usage for billiards/pool. This single sport has three different Chinese names depending on region, none of them agreeing: Mainland says 台球, Taiwan says 撞球 (already its own entry, [[撞球]]), and Hong Kong Cantonese says 桌球 — which in Taiwan instead means *table tennis* ([[桌球]] here is built on the Taiwanese sense). So the same two characters, 桌球, name completely different sports depending on which side of the strait you're standing on, while 台球 avoids that particular collision only by being unambiguous to begin with — Mainland speakers don't use it for anything else.

Korean and Japanese sidestep the regional split entirely: both have a single settled term regardless of which Chinese region's spelling is in view — Korean 당구 (danggu) and Japanese ビリヤード (a direct English loanword, "billiard"), matching [[撞球]]'s own fields exactly. Incidentally, if 台球 were read as a straight Sino-Korean compound rather than translated, it would come out 대구 — identical to Daegu, South Korea's fourth-largest city — a coincidence, not a real usage.
