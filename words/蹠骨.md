## Notes
- [蹠](../characters/蹠.md) "sole of the foot; to tread" + [骨 (char)](../characters/骨%20(char).md) "bone" — stand-in for [[蹠]], which cannot appear independently
- A transparent anatomical compound — "sole-bone" — naming the five long bones of the midfoot that bridge the ankle and the toes, more commonly called 中足骨 ("mid-foot bones") in everyday Japanese medical usage, with 蹠骨 (しょこつ, shokotsu) standing as its more literal, classically-rooted synonym. Mandarin zhígǔ, Cantonese zek3 gwat1, and Sino-Korean 척골 (cheokgol) preserve the same literal "tread-bone" image — 蹠 itself, the character for the sole of the foot, survives almost nowhere outside this kind of technical compound, anchored here the way many bound anatomical terms are anchored by their Latin or Greek counterparts in English.
