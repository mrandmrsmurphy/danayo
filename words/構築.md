## Notes
- [構](../characters/構.md) "build, construct" + [築](../characters/築.md) "fabricate, build" — stand-in for [[構]], which cannot appear independently

構築 means to construct or build something, especially a physical structure, a system, or a strategic position. Both characters carry the sense of building: 構 (kòu / こう) emphasises arrangement and framing — putting parts together into a coherent whole — while 築 (zhù / ちく) emphasises the physical act of laying down materials and piling them up. Together they form an emphatic compound used for substantial or systematic construction.

In Japanese こうちく is widely used in both literal contexts (構築物, constructed structures; 構築工事, construction work) and figurative ones (信頼関係を構築する, "to build a relationship of trust"; システムを構築する, "to build a system"). In Chinese 构筑 similarly spans physical construction and the building of abstract structures like alliances or arguments. Vietnamese cấu trúc, from the same Sino-Vietnamese roots, has shifted to mean "structure" as a noun rather than the act of building, but the morphological connection is clear.
