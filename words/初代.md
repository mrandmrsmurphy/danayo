## Notes
- [初](../characters/初.md) "beginning; initial" + [代](../characters/代.md) "generation; replace"

**初代** (*shōdai*, "first generation") identifies the founding or inaugural holder of a role — the first president, the first dynasty, the first model of a product. It is most naturally at home in Japanese and Korean (초대), where it functions as an attributive noun: 初代 総統 (founding president), 初代 首領 (first supreme leader). In Mandarin, 初代 circulates mainly in tech and entertainment contexts influenced by Japanese; classical Mandarin usage prefers 首任 ("first appointee") or 第一代 ("first generation") for the same concept.

The word is less about chronological sequence than about origination — 初代 implies not merely being first but being the one from whom a line of succession descends.
