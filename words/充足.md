## Notes
- [充](../characters/充.md) "to fill" + [足 (char)](../characters/足%20(char).md) "foot; to be sufficient"

充足 means "adequate, sufficient, fully met" — used of supplies, conditions, or requirements that have been filled (充) to the point of sufficiency (足). 充, a character built from 亠 over 允, means "to fill, to fill out, to charge" — already prolific in [[充分]] ("ample, plenty"), [[補充]] ("to replenish, supplement"), and the next two words in this batch, [[充電]] and [[充当]], all cross-linked from [[充]]'s own Words section.

足's primary meaning is "foot" (as in [[足球]], "football," cross-linked from [[足 (char)]]'s Notes), but as with English "to foot the bill" or "sufficient" (from Latin *sub + facere*, "to make up to"), the character independently developed the abstract sense "enough, sufficient" — likely via the idea of having "enough to stand on." 充足 combines two independent words for "fullness/sufficiency" into a single emphatic compound, and is one of relatively few words in this batch where the Korean reading 충족 happens to coincide exactly with the compositional reading derived from its parts (충 + 족), reflected in this word's `kwin: true`.
