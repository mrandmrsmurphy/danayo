## Notes
- [間](../characters/間.md) "interval; space between" + [欠 (char)](../characters/欠%20(char).md) as 代用字 for 歇 "to cease; to stop"
- 欠 carries 歇 as an alias, authorising the Dan'a'yo spelling. This word is the viable form of [歇](../characters/歇.md): the character's meaning (to cease; to stop midway) is identical, but 歇 alone is too bounded to stand as a Dan'a'yo word. 間欠 is its `stand_in` compound.

間欠 describes something that stops and starts — that proceeds in pulses rather than continuously. The gap (間) between cessations (欠/歇) is the defining feature.

Notably, 間欠 (かんけつ) is not a Dan'a'yo invention: it is the established Japanese scientific and medical form, used in preference to the Chinese 間歇 across technical registers. 間欠泉 (かんけつせん, "intermittent spring") is the Japanese word for a geyser; 間欠熱 (かんけつねつ) is intermittent fever; 間欠性 (かんけつせい) is intermittency as a property. Chinese uses 間歇 (jiànxiē) throughout these same contexts — the alias 間歇 and its simplified form 间歇 capture the Chinese spellings. Dan'a'yo follows the Japanese orthography, where 欠 (KETSU) replaced 歇 in the standard scientific vocabulary.
