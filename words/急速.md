## Notes
- [急 (char)](../characters/急%20(char).md) "urgent, hasty" + [速 (char)](../characters/速%20(char).md) "speed, fast" — moving or progressing at great speed, with 急's sense of urgency carried along rather than plain neutral quickness.

急速 stays consistent in both meaning and register across the whole family, with no real semantic drift the way many other compounds in this vault show. It's common in technical and journalistic writing throughout: Japanese uses it constantly in product and news contexts (急速充電 "rapid charging," 急速冷凍 "flash freezing"), and Mandarin, Cantonese, Korean, and Vietnamese (cấp tốc, as in khóa học cấp tốc "crash course") all apply it the same way, to any process moving faster than the ordinary pace of things.

Within Mandarin specifically, 急速 is distinguished from the more neutral 快速 (kuàisù, plain "fast") by keeping a trace of 急's original urgency — 急速下降 "plummeting" implies alarm at the rate of descent in a way 快速下降 would not, since 急速 describes not just speed but a hurried, pressured quality behind it.
