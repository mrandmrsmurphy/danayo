## Notes
- [最 (char)](../characters/最%20(char).md) "most" + [近 (char)](../characters/近%20(char).md) "near" — literally "most near [in time]." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 最近 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/最 (char).md` and `characters/近 (char).md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- A clean, directly parallel compound across the whole sphere — Mandarin zuìjìn, Cantonese zeoi3 gan6, Japanese さいきん, and Korean 최근 all preserve the Sino compound directly. Vietnamese gần đây ("nearby, close by [in time]") is a native construction rather than a Sino-Vietnamese calque, but is genuinely the correct, standard everyday term for "recently" — not a fabricated compositional guess.
