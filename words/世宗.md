## Notes
- [世](../characters/世.md) "world, generation" + [宗](../characters/宗.md) "ancestor, founder" — a posthumous temple name (廟號) granted to a monarch in the Sinitic tradition, most famously Korea's 世宗[[大王]] (King Sejong the Great, r. 1418–1450), the fourth king of Joseon and creator of the Korean alphabet. The same temple name was also borne by several Chinese emperors, including the Han Wudi (Emperor Wu of Han), the Ming Jiajing Emperor, and the Qing Yongzheng Emperor — temple names are a fixed stock of titles reused across dynasties, not unique to a single monarch.
