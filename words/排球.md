## Notes
- [排 (char)](../characters/排%20(char).md) "row, rank, line" + [球 (char)](../characters/球%20(char).md) "sphere, ball" — literally "row-ball," describing the sport's rows of players hitting the ball back and forth over a net. Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/排 (char).md` and `characters/球 (char).md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- Mandarin páiqiú, Cantonese paai4 kau4, Japanese はいきゅう, and Korean 배구 all directly preserve the Sino compound. Vietnamese, however, does not use a Sino-Vietnamese calque at all — the modern standard term is the fully native coinage bóng chuyền (literally "passing/relay ball"), verified via search as the only real Vietnamese word for the sport; no attested Sino-Vietnamese alternative (e.g. "bài cầu") is in actual use. Left as a native coinage rather than fabricating a compositional reading that isn't attested.
