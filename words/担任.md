## Notes
- [担](../characters/担.md) "shoulder; bear" + [任](../characters/任.md) "responsibility; duty"

The compound is a transparent pairing: to 担任 a role is literally to shoulder a duty and take it on as one's own responsibility. The word is used formally and in writing across the entire CJKV sphere for assuming an office, appointment, or role — a teacher taking a class, an official taking a post, a director taking a production.

In Japanese, たんにん is a regular reading (TAN + NIN, both on-yomi) with no special phonology. The word has acquired a specific second meaning within the Japanese school system: 担任 refers to a homeroom teacher, or to the act of being assigned homeroom duties for a class. A teacher says 担任する to describe taking on a class at the start of the school year. This educational sense does not carry over into Chinese or Korean usage, where the word retains only its general sense of "to assume a post."

In Korean the word is 담임 (担任), used primarily in the same school-system context as Japanese: the 담임선생님 ("homeroom teacher") is a central figure in Korean school culture. The Vietnamese form đảm nhiệm preserves the Sino-Vietnamese phonology of both characters.
