## Notes
- [宿](../characters/宿.md) "lodge; long-standing; overnight" + [題](../characters/題.md) "topic; question; problem"

The compound encodes its meaning in the characters: 宿 carries the sense of something carried forward, held overnight, left from one session to the next — and 題 is the question or problem to be resolved. Homework is literally the unresolved topic you take with you when you leave.

宿題 is a Japanese coinage that has spread outward. It is the standard everyday word in Japanese (しゅくだい) and Korean 숙제 uses it with identical meaning and identical frequency. In Chinese, the word exists and is understood — especially in Taiwan and among Chinese speakers familiar with Japanese — but Mandarin-speaking mainland China more typically uses 作業 (*zuòyè*) for the same concept. The Wiktionary notes 宿題 as an orthographic borrowing from Japanese into Chinese.
