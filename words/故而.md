## Notes
- [故](../characters/故.md) "reason; cause; therefore" + [而 (char)](../characters/而%20(char).md) "and; linking particle"

故而 is the consequential linker in Dan'a'yo — "therefore; as a result; and so" — connecting a clause that follows as the logical consequence of what preceded: A，故而B ("A; therefore B"). 故 contributes the causal ground ("for this reason; on that account") and 而 contributes the linking function that connects the consequence to the premise. The compound parallels [[所以]] in meaning but leans more literary: 故而 draws on the classical use of 故 alone as a logical connective (吾故知之, "I therefore know it"), while 所以 is more widespread in modern Mandarin prose. In Dan'a'yo 故而 is the standard form for expressing logical consequence between clauses.

故 (ㄍㄛ/go/고 — Mandarin gù, Cantonese gu3, Korean 고, Japanese KO/こ): "reason; cause; old; happening; therefore" — MC 見母 (k) + 模韻 (uo); a 形声 compound of 攴 (strike; action) + 古 (old; phonetic), originally "happening; incident; cause," extended to "therefore; on account of that." In Classical Chinese 故 alone regularly introduces consequence: 故曰 ("therefore it is said"), 故知 ("hence one knows"). 而 (ㄋㄧ/ni/니 — see [[而]] for full analysis): the clausal linking particle, here functioning as the subordinating link between the causal premise (故) and the following clause. Together 故而 = "for this reason, and so → therefore."

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 고니 does not match Korean 고이. The first syllable converges: 故 gives 고=고 (MC 見母+模韻, identical in both). The second syllable diverges: 而 gives Dan'a'yo 니 (ni, MC 日母+之韻 → ㄋ nasal preserved) vs Korean 이 (i, null onset — 日母 dropped). This is the same initial-retention split as [[而]] itself (Dan'a'yo 니 vs Korean 이), [[若]] (Dan'a'yo 냐 vs Korean 야), and [[汝]] (Dan'a'yo 뇌 vs Korean 여): Dan'a'yo consistently retains MC 日母 as ㄋ, Korean consistently drops it.
