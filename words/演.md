## Notes
- [演 (char)](../characters/演%20(char).md) "unfold; enact"

演's core sense is "to unfold" — spreading something out over time or space until its full form becomes visible — and Dan'a'yo compounds fan out from that one idea across several independent domains rather than converging on a single narrow meaning: unfolding an idea (演說 "speech," 演示 "demonstrate," 演繹 "deduce"), unfolding a performance ([[演奏]] "play music," [[演出]] "stage, choreograph," [[出演]] "appear in a show," [[公演]] "public performance," [[講演]] "lecture"), unfolding an action (演習 "drill," 演算 "calculate"), unfolding through time (演化 "evolve," 演變 "transform"), and the role sense 表演 "act out" / 演員 "performer." Because none of these subordinates to another, 演 earns Dan'a'yo standalone status rather than being tied to any one compound as its `stand_in`.

Mandarin yǎn keeps this full range productive in modern speech, from 演讲 (speech) to 演变 (evolve) to 表演 (perform). Cantonese jin2 largely mirrors Mandarin's usage. Japanese えん (on'yomi only — no native kun'yomi) survives almost exclusively bound inside Sino-Japanese compounds like 演奏 (えんそう) and 演出 (えんしゅつ); it never stands alone as a native word the way it does as a Dan'a'yo root. Korean 연 (Sino-Korean) shows the same pattern — 연주 (play music), 연출 (direct/stage), 강연 (lecture) — always bound, never standing alone in ordinary speech. Vietnamese diễn has the richest independent life of the daughters: it functions as a genuinely free verb ("to perform"), and anchors everyday compounds like diễn viên ("actor"), biểu diễn ("to perform"), and diễn ra ("to take place, unfold" — a native extension building directly on the "unfold" core).
