## Notes
- [官](../characters/官.md) "official" + [僚](../characters/僚.md) "colleague, companion" — an official; collectively, the bureaucracy.

Japanese draws a clean line that Vietnamese has erased: 官僚 (kanryō) itself is neutral — just "bureaucrat, civil servant" — with the critical, red-tape connotation confined entirely to the derived term 官僚主義 (kanryō-shugi, "bureaucratism"). Vietnamese quan liêu never kept that separation; the pejorative sense has bled straight into the base word, which now functions as an everyday adjective for rigid, inefficient, buck-passing bureaucratic behavior ("bệnh quan liêu," "bureaucratic disease," a phrase used constantly in Vietnamese political and administrative criticism) rather than a neutral job description. Mandarin guānliáo sits closer to Vietnamese than Japanese here — it can name officials neutrally, but carries enough latent negative charge that "apparatchik" is a fair English gloss alongside plain "bureaucrat."
