## Notes
- [法 (char)](../characters/法%20(char).md) "law; rule; dharma; France (abbrev.)" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation; country"

法国 is the Dan'a'yo name for France, following the Chinese convention in which 法 serves as the standard abbreviation for 法蘭西 (the phonosemantic transcription of "France") and 国 means "country; nation." The pattern is shared across CJKV country names: [[英国]] (Britain), [[徳国]] (Germany), [[美国]] (USA). The aliases 仏蘭西 / 佛蘭西 / 佛兰西 are older phonetic transcriptions of "France" using different characters; 法國 is the traditional-form spelling. Japanese uses フランス (a phonetic borrowing via Portuguese) rather than 法国 in everyday writing; Korean similarly uses 프랑스 rather than the Sino-Korean reading 법국.

法 (ㄈㄚㄆ/fab/빱): "law; rule; dharma; France (abbreviation)" — see [[法語]] for full analysis. 国 (ㄍㄛㄎ/gog/곡 — Mandarin guó, Cantonese gwok3, Korean 국, Japanese KOKU/こく, Vietnamese quốc): "nation; country; state" — MC 見母 (k) + 德合韻 (wək, entering tone); originally 或 (a walled territory with a spear guard) with 囗 (enclosure) added for disambiguation. The entering-tone -k coda is preserved in Dan'a'yo ㄎ, Korean ㄱ, Cantonese -k, and Vietnamese -c; Mandarin drops it.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 빱곡 does not match Korean 법국. 法 gives 빱 vs 법 (initial and vowel differ); 国 gives 곡 vs 국 (vowel -o vs -u).
