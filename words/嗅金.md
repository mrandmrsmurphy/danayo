## Notes
### Form
嗅金 is [嗅](characters/嗅.md) “to smell; to sniff” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following the rule for transition elements.

### Motivation
The international name _osmium_ derives from Greek ὀσμή (_osmē_) “smell, odor,” referring to the sharp, characteristic odor of osmium tetroxide. The semantic core is olfaction. 嗅 captures the **act of smelling**, which is the closest available single-character expression of that root.

### Why 嗅 (not 臭)
- 臭 “stink; odor” is already assigned (臭素 for bromine).
- 嗅 shifts from the **quality (odor)** to the **perceptual act (to smell)**, preserving the etymological domain while avoiding collision.
- This maintains a clear internal distinction:
    - 臭 → inherent smell (bromine)
    - 嗅 → the act/perception of smell (osmium)

### Type of formation
A **semantic calque with functional shift**: the same conceptual field (olfaction) is retained, but mapped to a different lexical node to preserve uniqueness.

### Fit within the system
1. Meaning-based reconstruction of the Greek root.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a d-block element.
3. Provides a unique, high-frequency graph for symbol use.
4. Maintains coherence with other sensory-based names.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 锇 (phonetic)
- Japanese: オスミウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 오스뮴 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: osmi (phonetic)

Modern usage is phonetic; 嗅金 restores semantic transparency.

### Clarity and issues
- 嗅 emphasizes the action rather than the property; this is a minor shift but still anchored in the correct semantic domain.
- The association remains intuitive in a chemical context.

### Conclusion
嗅金 is a well-constructed solution under lexical constraints. It preserves the olfactory etymology of _osmium_ while avoiding duplication with 臭素, maintaining both semantic fidelity and internal consistency.
