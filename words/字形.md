## Notes
- [字 (char)](../characters/字%20(char).md) "character, written symbol" + [形](../characters/形.md) "shape, form" — the visual shape of a written character, as distinct from its sound or meaning.

In the traditional Chinese analysis of a character into three layers — 字形 (shape), 字音 (sound), and 字義 (meaning) — 字形 is the layer concerned purely with how a character looks: its strokes, components, and overall silhouette, independent of how it is pronounced or what it means. This is the relevant sense when discussing, say, why two characters that look almost identical (字形相近) are easily confused in handwriting, or how a character's shape has drifted across centuries of script reform. 形 itself recurs throughout the vocabulary of appearance and shape — compare [[形声]] ("phono-semantic character," one of the traditional six categories of character formation) — while 字 anchors a huge family of words about writing, from 文字 ("script") to 字典 ("dictionary") to 字庫 (the modern "font library" or "character repository," also created in this batch). Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
