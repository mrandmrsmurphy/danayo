## Notes
- [在 (char)](../characters/在%20(char).md) "exist; to be present"

在 is the existence copula in Dan'a'yo — "to exist; to be present" — asserting that the subject exists or is located: 此在 (this exists; this is here), 人在 (the person is present), 在此 (it is here). Among the six Dan'a'yo copulas, 在 occupies the existential/locative function for things that are present at a location; its antonym is [[莫]] (to not exist; to be absent). The character is a 形声 compound — phonetic 才 under semantic 土 (ground) — literally "to be established on the ground; to exist in a place." In classical Chinese, 在 + location creates locative constructions: 在京 (to be in the capital), 在世 (to be alive — lit. "to be in the world"), 在位 (to be on the throne). 在 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[在]].

Mandarin 在 (zài): the standard locative copula — 我在北京 (I am in Beijing), 书在桌上 (the book is on the table); also a progressive aspect marker: 我在看书 (I am reading a book). Cantonese 喺 (hai2): the spoken Cantonese locative copula — 我喺香港 (I am in Hong Kong), 書喺枱上 (the book is on the table); 在 (zoi6) appears in formal compounds and literary Cantonese. Japanese に (ni) with ある/いる marks location — 東京にいる (to be in Tokyo), 本が机にある (the book is on the desk); Sino-Japanese 在 (ざい/ZAI) in compounds: 在籍 (zaiseki, registered; enrolled), 現在 (genzai, the present). Korean 있다 (itda): covers both possession and location — 나는 서울에 있다 (I am in Seoul); Sino-Korean 재 (在) in compounds: 현재 (現在, the present), 재학 (在學, currently enrolled). Vietnamese ở: tôi ở Hà Nội (I am in Hanoi); Sino-Vietnamese tại (在): tại chỗ (in place; on the spot), hiện tại (現在, the present).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 재 exactly matches Korean 재. 在 reads 재 (jai/ㄐㄚㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 從母 (d͡z) + 咍韻 (ʌi) gives a voiced sibilant affricate with the -ai diphthong. Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map MC 從母 to ㄐ/ㅈ (j-) and MC 咍韻 to the -ai/-ae vowel (ㅐ in Hangul), producing identical 재 forms. Note that 在 (ㄐㄚㄧ/jai/재) is a Dan'a'yo homophone of [[哉]] (ㄐㄚㄧ/jai/재, "O — vocative particle") — both derive from MC 精/從母 + 咍韻, sharing identical Dan'a'yo phonology while one is a copula and the other a case particle.
