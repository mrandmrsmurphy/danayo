## Notes
- [展](../characters/展.md) "expand, unfold" + [翅](../characters/翅.md) "wing"

展翅 describes the action of spreading wings wide — the moment before takeoff, or the full extension of wings in flight or display. In Chinese (zhǎnchì), the compound is both literal (a bird or insect spreading its wings) and figurative (embarking on an ambitious new venture, as in 展翅高飛, "to spread wings and soar high"). The figurative sense is common in motivational and literary writing: a young person leaving home, a business expanding, a talent finally given room to grow.

In Japanese (てんし, tenshi), the compound is a homophone of 天使 (てんし, "angel") — a coincidence that is occasionally exploited in literary wordplay, given that angels and birds share the attribute of wings. In Korean (전시), 展翅 is a homophone of 展示 (전시, "exhibition, display") — a different compound using the same 展 character, also found in [[展示]] on the 展 character page.
