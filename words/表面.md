## Notes
- [表](../characters/表.md) "show, indicate" + [面](../characters/面.md) "face, surface" — the outer, visible face of something; extended to "surface" generally; stand-in for [[面]], which cannot appear independently.

Mandarin/Cantonese biǎomiàn/biu2min6 and Korean 표면 all keep this as the ordinary word for "surface," used just as readily in casual speech as in physics or geography. Korean also has two native alternatives in everyday use — 겉 and 거죽 — both meaning "the outside/outer layer," with 거죽 leaning toward a rougher or more concrete texture (an animal hide, tree bark) than the more abstract 표면. Vietnamese breaks from the Sino root entirely in ordinary usage: bề mặt (native "side" + "face") is the standard word for "surface" in every register, technical included, while biểu diện — the literal Sino-Vietnamese reading of 表面 — is essentially a dictionary-only form, not used in real speech or writing.
