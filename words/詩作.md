## Notes
- [詩](../characters/詩.md) "poem, ode" + [作 (char)](../characters/作%20(char).md) "make, compose, work" — a composed poem; the act of making poetry.

詩作 functions as both noun (a composed poem; a body of poetic work) and verb (to compose poetry). As a noun it emphasises the poem as a made thing — a work of craft — rather than simply a text or a song. The compound appears in literary criticism, biography, and cataloguing contexts: 詩作を残す ("to leave behind poems"), 詩作活動 ("poetic activity, the ongoing practice of composing poems").

The Chinese tradition treats 詩 as one of the highest cultural attainments. Confucius on the Classic of Poetry (詩經):

> 詩三百，一言以蔽之，曰：「思無邪。」(《論語·為政》2.2)
> "The three hundred poems summed up in one phrase: 'No depraved thoughts.'"

In Korean (시작), 詩作 is an exact homophone of 始作 (시작, "beginning, start") — a resonance that poets and critics in Korean occasionally exploit. The pun is culturally available: every poem is a beginning (始作) as much as it is a making (詩作).

In Japanese (しさく, shisaku), the word appears chiefly in literary and academic writing. The broader term 詩 alone covers both poetry as a category and individual poems; 詩作 specifically foregrounds the compositional act, the craft dimension of the poetic work.

Vietnamese thi tác (Hán-Việt reading of 詩作) appears in literary criticism and classical study, though native Vietnamese literary vocabulary (bài thơ for "poem," làm thơ for "composing poetry") is more common in everyday usage.
