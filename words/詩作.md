## Notes
- [詩](../characters/詩.md) "poem" + [作 (char)](../characters/作%20(char).md) "make, compose"

詩作 functions as both noun (a composed poem; a work of poetry) and verb (to compose poetry). As a noun it emphasizes the poem as a made thing — a work of craft — rather than simply a text or a song. The compound is used in literary criticism, biography, and cataloguing contexts: 詩作を残す ("to leave behind poems"), 詩作活動 ("poetic activity, the practice of writing poems").

In Korean (시작), the word is an exact homophone of 始作 (시작, "beginning, start") — a near-constant pun potential in Korean poetry criticism. In Japanese (しさく, shisaku), the word appears chiefly in literary and academic writing.
