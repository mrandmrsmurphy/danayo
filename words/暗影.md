## Notes
- [暗 (char)](../characters/暗%20(char).md) "dark; gloomy" + [影](../characters/影.md) "shadow; reflection"

**1. Shadow; gloom.** The primary literary sense: the shadow cast by an object, or more broadly the pervading darkness — physical or atmospheric — that falls over a scene or a state of mind. 暗影 carries a slightly heavier weight than plain 影 alone; the 暗 ("dark; hidden") deepens the shadow from a neutral optical phenomenon into something ominous or oppressive. A cloud casting an 暗影 over a landscape, or the 暗影 of grief settling over a household.

**2. Umbra (astronomy).** In Chinese astronomical and meteorological usage, 暗影 names the *umbra* — the darkest central cone of shadow during a solar or lunar eclipse, where the light source is completely occluded. The related term 暗影部 (*ànyǐngbù*) designates the umbra of a sunspot, the dark central region surrounded by the lighter penumbra. This technical astronomical sense is standard in Chinese-language science writing.

**Aliases.** The three alias forms follow directly from the alias chains of the component characters: 暗 absorbs 闇 (its classical/Japanese alternate), and 影 absorbs 翳 (its historical form, now displaced by 影 as a modern substitute):

| Form | Components |
|---|---|
| 暗翳 | 暗 + 翳 (翳 is alias of 影) |
| 闇影 | 闇 (alias of 暗) + 影 |
| 闇翳 | 闇 + 翳 (both alias forms) |

Japanese uses 暗翳 (*an'ei*) as an alternate spelling alongside 暗影; 闇 forms are older or more literary.

**Vietnamese note.** The Sino-Vietnamese reading *ám ảnh* shares the phonology of 暗影 but has undergone semantic drift: in modern Vietnamese, *ám ảnh* means "to haunt; to obsess" — a dark shadow that follows and persists rather than a shadow that falls in one place. The ghost of the original meaning is audible in the new one.
