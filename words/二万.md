## Notes
- [二 (char)](../characters/二%20(char).md) "two" + [万](../characters/万.md) "ten-thousand"

The numeral 20,000. East Asian numeral systems use 万 (myriad, 10,000) as a fundamental grouping unit — a unit with no direct English equivalent. Where English groups by thousands (thousand, million = thousand thousands, billion = thousand millions), Chinese, Japanese, and Korean group by ten-thousands: 万, 億 (= 万万 = 100,000,000), 兆 (= 万億). This makes 二万 the natural expression for 20,000 rather than "twenty thousand."

Note that because 万 is considered "overfull" as a bare syllable in Dan'a'yo (ㄇㄛㄋ is already a count word implying plurality), one normally says [[一万]] (one-myriad, 10,000) rather than bare 万 — just as one says "one hundred" in English. 二万 (two-myriad, 20,000) follows the same logic. Compare [[七万]], [[二百]], [[二千]].
