## Notes
- [今 (char)](../characters/今%20(char).md) "now" + [年 (char)](../characters/年%20(char).md) "year" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

Mandarin jīnnián and Cantonese gam1 nin4 are the standard everyday word for "this year." Japanese ことし (kotoshi) is a native reading written with these same characters — the everyday word, not a separate on'yomi compound. Korean narrowed the frontmatter to 금년 (geumnyeon), the real Sino-Korean reading, still current in formal/written registers (금년도, "this fiscal year") alongside the more common everyday native 올해. Vietnamese kim niên is the honest compositional reading; the universal everyday Vietnamese phrase is năm nay, native rather than Sino-Vietnamese.
