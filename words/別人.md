## Notes
- [別 (char)](../characters/別%20(char).md) "distinct; other" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); `kwin` is `false` by the AND-rule, since both [別 (char)](../characters/別%20(char).md) and [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) are individually stored as `kwin: false`
- Mandarin biérén, Cantonese bit6 jan4, and Japanese べつじん (betsujin) are all standard, everyday words for "another person; someone else." Korean 별인 (byeorin) is also real but far rarer in living speech than the native-Korean phrase 다른 사람 ("different person," lit. 다르다 "to be different" + 사람 "person"), which is what most Korean speakers actually reach for — 별인 is documented but bookish/formal by comparison.
- No independently attested Sino-Vietnamese reading of this compound was found (no entry on Vietnamese Wiktionary or Hán Việt dictionaries specifically for 別人 as a fixed word); Vietnamese instead expresses this with the native phrase người khác ("other/different person"). Left `vietnamese` blank rather than fabricate a compositional biệt + nhân reading with no evidence it's ever actually used as a word.
