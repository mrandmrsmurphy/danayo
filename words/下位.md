## Notes
- [下 (char)](../characters/下%20(char).md) "down, below" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank"

下位 names a low or subordinate position in any ranked system — a low rank in a hierarchy, a low place in a list of results, or a subordinate role relative to a superior. 下 is one of the most basic characters in the language, a 指事 (ideogram) consisting simply of a short stroke beneath a long one, marking the abstract notion of "below" — already richly cross-linked in [[下 (char)]]'s Words section to compounds like [[低下]] ("to lower, to droop") and [[下降]] ("to descend").

下位 forms a direct antonym pair with [[上位]] (the next word in this batch) — together 上位 and 下位 frame the entire vocabulary of ranking and hierarchy in Dan'a'yo, parallel to how 上下 itself (cross-linked elsewhere) frames the basic spatial axis of "up and down." The pairing is productive across registers: athletic rankings, corporate hierarchies, search-result ordering, and social status all use 上位/下位 in essentially the same structural sense.
