## Notes
- [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name"

名 is "name" — in Dan'a'yo, 名 serves both as a noun for a name and as a formal classifier for counting people with deference (where 人 is neutral and 名 is honorific): 五名 (five persons, polite), 何名様 (how many guests, very honorific). As a correlative row heading, 名 generates 此名 (this name; what this is called), 其名 (that name; its name), 何名 (what name; what is it called — note: 誰 is normal for persons, 何名 is very polite, 何人 is rude). The classical Chinese 名 encompasses "name; reputation; fame" as well as "to name; to designate." 名 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[名]].

Mandarin 名 (míng): 名字 (míngzì, name — of a person), 名片 (míngpiàn, business card), 名稱 (míngchēng, appellation; designation), 有名 (yǒumíng, famous), 名人 (míngrén, celebrity; notable person), 名譽 (míngyù, honor; reputation). Cantonese 名 (ming4): 名字 (ming4 zi6), 出名 (ceot1 ming4, famous). Japanese 名 (な, na / めい, mei / みょう, myō): the native な (na) appears in 名前 (なまえ, name); the Sino-Japanese めい (mei) in 名前 (also めいまえ, but なまえ is standard), 名人 (めいじん, master; expert — the title of the highest rank in Go, Shogi, and other traditional games), 名誉 (めいよ, honor). The go-on reading みょう (myō) appears in older compounds. Korean 명 (名) in Sino-Korean: 명함 (名銜, business card), 명성 (名聲, fame; reputation); native 이름 (ireuleum, name). Vietnamese danh (名): danh từ (名詞, noun — lit. name-word), danh tiếng (fame; renown), danh sách (名冊, list).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 밍 differs from Korean 명. 名 reads 밍 (ming/ㄇㄧㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 明母 (m) + 清開韻 (iᴇŋ) gives an -ing final and m- initial in both systems, but the vowel differs: Dan'a'yo gives a plain -i- vowel (밍, ming) while Sino-Korean palatalises to -yeo- (명, myeong). The -ng final is identical, making only the medial vowel quality the point of divergence.
