## Notes
- [空 (char)](../characters/空%20(char).md) "empty, sky" + [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "in, within"

空中 denotes the space above the ground — the air, the sky, or the condition of being airborne. The compound is common in all five CJKV languages and productive in both literal and figurative uses. Literally, it describes location (空中障害物, aerial obstacle; 空中戦, air battle; 空中分解, midair breakup). Figuratively, it generates the compound 空中楼閣, "castle in the air" — a structure (楼閣) that exists only in midair, i.e. a fantasy or an impractical plan with no grounding in reality.

In Japanese (くうちゅう, kūchū), the word is everyday vocabulary for "the air" and "aerial," appearing in aviation, military, and science contexts. In Korean (공중), it similarly covers both the physical space of the sky and figurative constructions of unreality.
