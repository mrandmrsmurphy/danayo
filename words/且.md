## Notes
- [且 (char)](../characters/且%20(char).md) "also; too; additionally"

且 is the additive topic-retaining particle in Dan'a'yo — "also; too; additionally" — suffixing to a noun phrase to assert that the predicate applies to that noun in addition to others already established: 我且 (I also; me too), 此且 (this as well; and also this). Among the Dan'a'yo topic-retaining particles (関詞), 且 broadens scope by inclusion, asserting that the marked noun shares the predicate alongside whatever else was already mentioned — contrasting with [[只]] (exclusively), which narrows scope. The character is a 象形 of a stone altar, the original form of 祖 (ancestor), phonetically borrowed for this grammatical function. 且 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[且]].

The comparison chart: 且 parallels Mandarin 也 (yě, also; too), Cantonese 都/亦 (dou1/jik6), Japanese も (mo, also; too), Korean 도 (do, also; too), Vietnamese cũng (also; too). Mandarin 也 (yě): 我也去 (I also go; me too), 这也行 (this will also do); 还 (hái) covers "also" in the sense of "in addition; moreover," and 又 (yòu) covers repeated occurrence. Cantonese 都 (dou1): 我都係 (me too), 佢都去 (he/she also goes) — 都 is the most productive additive particle in colloquial Cantonese. Japanese も (mo): 私も (me too), これもいい (this is also good), 何も (nothing at all — in negative context). Korean 도 (do): 나도 (me too), 이것도 (this too), 아무도 (anyone / nobody — in negative context). Vietnamese cũng: Tôi cũng đi (I also go), Cũng được (that'll also work).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 초 does not match Korean 차. 且 reads 초 (co/ㄑㄛ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 清母 (t͡sʰ) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ) gives an aspirated palatal affricate with the -o vowel. Dan'a'yo maps MC 魚韻 (ɨʌ) to plain -o (ㄛ/초), while Sino-Korean 차 (cha) maps the same MC final to -a, a different vowel grade. Both systems preserve the MC aspiration of 清母 (Dan'a'yo ㄑ, Korean ㅊ), but the vowel diverges: Dan'a'yo -o vs Korean -a. Note that 且 (ㄑㄛ/co/초) is a Dan'a'yo homophone of [[処]] (ㄑㄛ/co/초, "place") — both derive from MC 清/初母 + 魚韻, sharing identical Dan'a'yo phonology while one is a topic-retaining particle and the other a correlative pronoun.
