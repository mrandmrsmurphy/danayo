## Notes
- [魚 (char)](../characters/魚%20(char).md) "fish" + [鰭](../characters/鰭.md) "fish fin" — stand-in for [[鰭]], which cannot appear independently

魚鰭 (yúqí / ぎょき / 어기) is the compound for fish fin — the membranous appendages that fish use for locomotion, steering, and balance. 鰭 is the specific character for fin, formed with the fish radical (魚) and the phonetic component 耆. The compound 魚鰭 pairs the broader classifier 魚 (fish) with the specific 鰭 (fin) to make the character usable as a standalone entry in the lexicon.

Fish fins are classified in East Asian zoological and culinary contexts by position: 背鰭 (dorsal fin), 胸鰭 (pectoral fin), 腹鰭 (pelvic fin), 尾鰭 (caudal/tail fin), and 臀鰭 (anal fin). In culinary usage, the prized ingredient in shark fin soup (魚翅 in Chinese, though 鰭 is the anatomically precise term) is specifically the shark's fin; the compound 魚鰭 covers fin in the general zoological sense.
