## Notes
- [完](../characters/完.md) "finish, whole" + [了 (char)](../characters/了%20(char).md) "complete, finish" — two near-synonymous "finish" roots stacked together for emphasis: thoroughly done.

Beyond its everyday sense ("it's finished"), 完了 is the standard grammatical term, across Japanese and Korean alike, for the perfective aspect — the verbal category marking an action as completed rather than ongoing. The grammar book uses exactly this technical sense for Dan'a'yo's own suffix –已, glossed as "完了相 (perfective)" within its broader [[時相]] system, and explicitly notes that this suffix may co-occur with the durative –中 (in the fixed order 中已, "ongoing, then completed") and with the prospective [[将然]] suffix –将, which always sits closest to the verb. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
