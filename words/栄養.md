## Notes
- [栄](../characters/栄.md) "flourish; glory" + [養](../characters/養.md) "nourish; foster"

**栄養** is the East Asian compound for nutrition or nourishment — the process by which living organisms take in and assimilate substances necessary for growth, energy, and health. The compound literally means "flourishing nourishment": 栄 (榮, *róng*) "to flourish; to thrive" + 養 (*yǎng*) "to nourish; to raise." See [[栄養素]] for the individual nutrients that provide it.

A note on character variants: Dan'a'yo uses 栄 (the Japanese shinjitai of 榮, *róng*, "flourish/glory"), giving a Mandarin reading of *róngyǎng*. The standard Chinese compound for nutrition is **营养** (yíngyǎng), which uses the distinct character 营/營 (*yíng*, "manage; operate") — different in both form and Mandarin pronunciation, though both 栄 and 营 are read as エイ (*ei*) in Japanese, making 栄養 and 营養 homophonous in Japanese. The aliases 榮養 and 荣养 reflect the Chinese orthographic forms of the characters actually used in this word.
