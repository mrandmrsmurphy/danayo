## Notes
- [終](../characters/終.md) "end; conclude" + [止](../characters/止.md) "stop; halt" + [格 (char)](../characters/格%20(char).md) "case; standard"

終止格 is the terminative case in Dan'a'yo, marked by the particle [[至]] (ㄐㄧㄜ/jiǝ/즤). The terminative marks the endpoint or limit of motion or extent — where something reaches or stops: "to; until; up to; as far as." It attaches to the endpoint noun: 行至城門 ("walk to the city gate"), 至今 ("up to now; until now"), 由春至夏 ("from spring to summer"). The terminative contrasts with [[奪格]] (ablative, [[由]] — source/starting point) and [[向格]] (allative, [[向]] — direction of motion without necessarily reaching). Where 向 encodes trajectory, 終止格 encodes the terminus: the point at which motion or extent ends.

終 (ㄐㄨㄫ/jung/중 — Mandarin zhōng, Cantonese zung1, Korean 종, Japanese SHŪ/しゅう): "end; finish; conclude; from beginning to end" — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 冬韻 (uŋ); the character originally depicted a ball of silk thread wound to its end, generalized to "conclude; finally; terminate." 止 (ㄐㄧ/ji/지 — Mandarin zhǐ, Cantonese zi2, Korean 지, Japanese SHI/し): "stop; halt; cease; up to" — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 之韻 (ɨ); the character depicts a foot in its stopped position; a pictograph of halted motion. Together, 終止 means "terminate; bring to a stop" — the semantic compound for "terminative." 格 (ㄍㄚㄎ/gag/각 — Mandarin gé, Cantonese gaak3, Korean 격, Japanese KAKU/かく): "case; standard; grid; framework" — MC 見母 (k) + 陌二韻 (ɑk); originally "wooden framework; standard; to arrive at," the grammatical use of 格 to mean "case" is a calque of Japanese grammatical kaku (格), which itself calques Latin casus.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 중지각 does not match Korean 종지격. The middle syllable converges: 止 gives 지=지 (identical — MC 章母+之韻 yields the same reading in both). The outer syllables diverge on different principles. 終 gives Dan'a'yo 중 (jung, MC 冬韻 uŋ → -u vowel/ㅜ) vs Korean 종 (jong, -o vowel/ㅗ) — MC 冬韻 retains -u in Dan'a'yo (matching Cantonese zung1) but shifts to -o in Korean. 格 gives Dan'a'yo 각 (gak, MC 陌二韻 ɑk → -a vowel) vs Korean 격 (gyeok, -yeo vowel) — MC 陌二韻 (2nd-division entering tone) yields -a in Dan'a'yo but -yeo in Korean, a consistent 2nd-division divergence also seen in [[比較格]] (比: 븨 vs 비).
