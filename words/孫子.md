## Notes
- [孫](../characters/孫.md) "grandchild" + [子](../characters/子.md) "son; master"
- This word is the viable form of [孫](../characters/孫.md): the character's meaning (grandchild; descendant) is identical, but 孫 alone is too bounded to stand as a Dan'a'yo word. 孫子 is its `stand_in` compound.

**孫子** (*Sūnzǐ*, "Master Sun") refers both to Sun Wu (孫武, c. 544–496 BC), a military general from the state of Wu during the Spring and Autumn period, and to the strategic treatise attributed to him. The name follows the classical convention of appending 子 ("master") to a thinker's surname as an honorific — the same suffix that marks Confucius (孔子 *Kǒngzǐ*), Mencius (孟子 *Mèngzǐ*), and Laozi (老子 *Lǎozǐ*).

The *孫子兵法* (Art of War) is among the oldest surviving military treatises, organized in thirteen chapters covering terrain, deception, intelligence, and the psychology of command. Its influence stretches far beyond military affairs: it has been central to East Asian statecraft, martial arts traditions, and competitive theory in business and diplomacy alike.

For the text, see [[translation/孫子兵法]].
