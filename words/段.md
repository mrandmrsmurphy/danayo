## Notes
- [段 (char)](../characters/段%20(char).md) "segments; sections; passages"

段 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for segments, passages, episodes, and delimited stretches — 量詞, counting sections of road, paragraphs of text, periods of time, and scenes of narrative: 一段路 (one stretch of road), 三段文章 (three paragraphs), 一段時間 (a period of time), 兩段記憶 (two memories, treating each as a discrete episode). The original sense of 段 is "a step cut into rock; a tier," from the pictographic representation of a hammer (殳) working a cliffside to produce discrete ledges. This sense of deliberate division — cutting a continuous thing into bounded portions — underlies the classifier function: 段 always implies that the measured item has been sectioned or can be perceived as a bounded unit within a larger sequence. 段 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[段]].

The CJKV classifier landscape for segments and passages shows moderate divergence. Mandarin 段 (duàn) is broadly productive: 一段话 (one passage of speech), 一段路 (one stretch of road), 一段时间 (one period of time), 一段历史 (one period of history), 一段视频 (one video clip). Cantonese 段 (dyun6) closely parallels Mandarin: 一段路 (one stretch of road), 一段时間 (one period of time). Japanese does not use 段 (だん/DAN) as a productive classifier — DAN appears in fixed compounds meaning "step; grade; rank" (段階 dangkai, stage; level) and 段落 (danraku, paragraph; passage); the classifier for text sections is 節 (せつ/SETSU) or for lines 行 (ぎょう/GYŌ). Korean 단 (段) appears in Sino-Korean compounds (단계 段階, stage; 단락 段落, paragraph) but not as a standalone active classifier. Vietnamese đoạn (段) is fully productive as both noun and classifier: một đoạn đường (one stretch of road), một đoạn văn (one paragraph), một đoạn phim (one film clip) — one of the more active SV classifier survivals.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 돤 does not match Korean 단. 段 reads 돤 (dwan/ㄉ⺢ㄋ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 定母 (d) + 桓韻 (uɑn) gives a voiced stop onset with the -wan compound vowel and -n final. Dan'a'yo preserves the MC -u- medial, producing the -wan diphthong in 돤; Sino-Korean 단 (dan) drops the -u- medial entirely, a regular historical process that simplified labially-conditioned medials in Korean. The MC 桓韻 (uɑn) produced ⺢ㄋ (-wan/돤) in Dan'a'yo and plain -an (단) in Korean — an illustration of Dan'a'yo's systematic preservation of MC medial vowels that Korean has simplified.
