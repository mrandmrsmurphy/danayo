## Notes
- [奥 (char)](../characters/奥%20(char).md) "deep" + [門](../characters/門.md) "gate" — Dan'a'yo spells this with 奥 rather than the real-world 澳 ("bay, inlet") because 澳 doesn't exist as its own character in this vault, only as a listed alias of 奥 (same Middle Chinese origin, same modern pronunciation); 澳門, the real standard form, is kept here as an `aliases` entry.

Japanese and Korean both bypass the Sino reading of this name entirely in ordinary use, reaching instead for the same Western loanword: マカオ/마카오 (Makao), ultimately from Portuguese "Macau" filtered through English "Macao" spelling — neither language uses アオメン/오문, the reading the characters would mechanically produce. Mandarin and Cantonese keep the native 澳門/奥門 (Àomén/ou3 mun4) as the only name there is, and Vietnamese Áo Môn, the Sino-Vietnamese transliteration, also stays in active everyday use — unlike Japanese and Korean, Vietnamese never adopted a competing Western loanword for this one. The place-name's own etymology is itself a case of mishearing: 媽閣 (the A-Ma Temple, a Cantonese landmark) was misheard by Portuguese sailors asking for the area's name, giving "Macau" — a name with no real connection to the "bay gate" sense the Chinese characters 澳門 suggest at face value.
