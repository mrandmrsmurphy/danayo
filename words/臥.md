## Notes
- [臥 (char)](../characters/臥%20(char).md) "lie down; crouch" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin wò, Cantonese ngo6, Korean 와 (wa), and Vietnamese ngoạ all use this character productively and independently or in common compounds (臥薪嘗胆/와신상담, "sleep on firewood and taste gall," a shared Sinosphere-wide chengyu for enduring hardship toward revenge; 臥龍, "a hidden talent lying dormant"). Japanese has a real on'yomi (ガ, ga) but never uses 臥 as a standalone word — it appears only bound in compounds (横臥, おうが, "lying on one's side"; 病臥, びょうが, "confined to bed by illness"), with ordinary Japanese instead using native 横になる ("to lie down") for the everyday action.
