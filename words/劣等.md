## Notes
- [劣](../characters/劣.md) "inferior, bad" + [等 (char)](../characters/等%20(char).md) "grade, rank" — of inferior grade; below standard; the lower end of a scale

劣等 is the antonym of 優等 and occupies the bottom of the graded series: 劣等 → 中等 → 優等. In plain usage it describes quality, performance, or product that falls below an acceptable standard.

The compound's most culturally significant derivative is 劣等感 (れっとうかん / 열등감 / liètǒng gǎn) — inferiority complex, a concept introduced into East Asian psychology through the work of Alfred Adler (アルフレッド・アドラー). Adler's theory that much of human behavior is driven by compensation for perceived inferiority found enormous resonance in Japan and Korea, and 劣等感 became a common term in everyday speech for the feeling that one is fundamentally less capable, attractive, or worthy than others. The compound shows how 劣等 shifted from a neutral grade designation into a term for a painful psychological condition.
