## Notes
- [運](../characters/運.md) "move; transport" + [動 (char)](../characters/動%20(char).md) "move" + [家](../characters/家.md) "house; family" — together forming [[運動]] "exercise; movement"
- 家 functions here as a suffix meaning "practitioner of X" or "person dedicated to X", but this is **not a productive suffix in Dan'a'yo**. 運動家 is a lexicalized compound; the suffix does not apply freely to other words and must be noted each time it appears.

**運動家** covers two distinct senses that share the same form across East Asian languages. In its athletic sense it means a sportsperson or athlete — someone who practises 運動 as physical exercise or competition. In its political sense it means an activist or campaigner — someone who participates in a 運動 understood as a social or political movement. The Japanese form うんどうか leans toward the activist reading in modern usage, while the Mandarin 运动家 more often implies the athletic one; context disambiguates.
