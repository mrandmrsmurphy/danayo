## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

其時 is the medial temporal correlative — "then; at that time." Like all 其-column entries, it picks out a referent in the shared discourse context or in the addressee's frame: 其時 is the time just established by the narrative, the moment both speaker and listener understand to be "that time." In a story, 其時 anchors a key moment: 其時、彼人が到着した ("At that time, that person arrived"). In conversation, 其時 can refer to a time the addressee raised or a time known to both participants.

Japanese その時 (sono toki, "at that time — medial") is the most precise cross-linguistic parallel, using the adnominal その (medial demonstrative) + 時 (time). Korean 그때 (geuttae, "that time") is the medial temporal demonstrative, contrasting with 이때 (ittae, this time) and 저때 (jeottae, that time — distal). Mandarin 其時 (qíshí) appears in classical and formal prose meaning "at that time, then." Vietnamese lúc đó (at that time) is the medial temporal equivalent.

其時 and 其処 together form the medial spatio-temporal pair: 其処 is "there (medial)" and 其時 is "then (medial)." In narrative prose, anchoring an event with both 其処 and 其時 ("at that place at that time") places it precisely in the shared narrative frame — a compact expression of the spatio-temporal context that would require longer circumlocutions in most source languages. This spatio-temporal anchoring function is one of the practical advantages of Dan'a'yo's systematic correlative grid.
