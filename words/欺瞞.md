## Notes
- [欺](../characters/欺.md) "deceive, trick" + [𥈞](../characters/𥈞.md) "conceal, dupe" — stand-in for both [[欺]] and [[𥈞]], neither of which can appear independently.

This single word resolves two stand-in requirements at once. 欺's `stand_in` field calls for 欺瞞 (using the common alias form 瞞), while 𥈞's `stand_in` field calls for 欺𥈞 (using 𥈞 itself). But 𥈞 and its aliases 瞞/瞒 already share one identical reading (𥈞 carries `kwin: true`), so 欺瞞 and 欺𥈞 are simply two written forms of the exact same word — creating one file with both as aliases legitimizes both characters simultaneously.

欺瞞 (qīmán) means "deception, fraud, deceit" — the act of misleading someone through lies or trickery, often with an implication of betrayal of trust. It is a strong, fairly formal word for dishonesty, distinct from milder words for "joking" or "pretending." The Japanese ぎまん is a common, everyday word for fraud or deception in formal and legal contexts.

Because 欺 and 𥈞 are the vault's own characters and 𥈞's readings already align exactly with its alias 瞞, the 羅馬字, 諺文, 注音, mandarin, cantonese, japanese, korean, and vietnamese fields are all a straightforward concatenation of 欺's and 𥈞's own values — no divergence adjustment is needed.

This word resolves the existing [[欺𥈞]] reference in [[欺]]'s own Notes.
