## Notes
- [系](../characters/系.md) "connect; link; system" + [詞](../characters/詞.md) "word; term"

系詞 is the part-of-speech class of copulas in Dan'a'yo — the closed set of six linking verbs that connect a topic to its comment without expressing action or predication. The six members are [[是]] (to equal; identity), [[非]] (to not equal; negated identity), [[有]] (to have; possession), [[無]] (to not have; negated possession), [[在]] (to exist; existence), [[莫]] (to not exist; negated existence). Copulas differ from eventives ([[事詞]], which require an object) and statives ([[性詞]], which modify adnominally): 系詞 assert a relationship between the topic and the comment — identity, possession, or existence — without the topic performing any action. The set is closed; no new 系詞 can be derived. The term carries many aliases, listed above, reflecting different grammatical traditions' names for the copula across CJKV linguistics.

系 (ㄏㄝㄧ/hei/헤 — Mandarin xì, Cantonese hai6, Korean 계, Japanese KEI/けい, Vietnamese hệ): "connect; relate; link; system; series" — MC 匣母 (ɣ) + 齊韻 (ei); the character depicts silk threads being joined together, giving the core sense of "connection; system; relation." In grammatical metalanguage 系 designates the linking or connecting function — the copula connects subject and predicate without asserting change or agency. 詞 (ㄙㄚ/sa/사): "word; term" — see [[擬詞]] and [[感詞]] for full discussion. The compound 系詞 is the grammatical term for "copula; linking verb; copulative verb." The rich alias list reflects genuine terminological disagreement across CJKV grammatical traditions: 繫辭 (xìcí) appears in classical Chinese philosophical grammar (the 繫辭傳 of the Book of Changes); 連詞/連辞 uses 連 "connect" instead of 系; 指定詞 (zhǐdìngcí, "specifying word") follows some Chinese grammarians who emphasize the copula's identificational function; Japanese 繋辞 (keiji) is used in philosophical translation; 連係動詞 is the Korean grammatical tradition's term.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 헤사 does not match Korean 계사. The second syllable converges: 詞 gives 사=사 (identical, as in [[擬詞]], [[感詞]], [[系詞]]). The first syllable diverges on two independent dimensions. First, the initial: MC 匣母 (ɣ, voiced velar fricative) → Dan'a'yo ㄏ (h-, consistently devoiced to a voiceless fricative, as in [[或]] hog/혹) vs Korean ㄱ (g-, a velar stop — MC 匣母 merges toward 見母 k before 4th-division front vowels in Sino-Korean). Second, the vowel: MC 齊韻 (ei, 4th division) → Dan'a'yo plain -ei (ㄝㄧ, no palatal medial added) vs Korean adds the 4th-division -y- medial, giving -yei → 계 (gye). The same 4th-division -y- split appears in [[零]] (Dan'a'yo 렁 plain vs Korean 령 -y-): Dan'a'yo consistently suppresses the 4th-division palatal medial that Korean retains.
