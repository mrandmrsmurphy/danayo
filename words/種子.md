## Notes
- [種](../characters/種.md) "kind; type; seed; species" + [子](../characters/子.md) "son; child" — 種's own gloss already includes "seed," with 子 here in its productive noun-forming role (as in 電子, 原子, 帽子) rather than its literal "child" sense, giving 種子 as "the seed-thing."

種子 (しゅし, shushi) is the formal/scientific register word for "seed" in Japanese, alongside the everyday native 種 (たね) alone. Korean 종자 (jongja) is likewise the formal/technical term — used in agriculture and biology (종자은행, "seed bank") — next to the more everyday native 씨 or 씨앗. Mandarin zhǒngzi is, by contrast, the single ordinary everyday word for "seed," with no separate native alternative displacing it. Vietnamese chủng tử is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound, but its usage has narrowed almost entirely to a Buddhist-philosophical register — the technical term for a karmic "seed" (Sanskrit bīja) that gives rise to future phenomena of mind and matter — while the ordinary Vietnamese word for a literal plant seed is the native compound hạt giống.
