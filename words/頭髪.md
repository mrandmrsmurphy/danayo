## Notes
- [頭 (char)](../characters/頭%20(char).md) "head" + [髪](../characters/髪.md) "hair" — stand-in for [[髪]], which cannot appear independently

**Not a comma-dump error, a genuine regional split**: `mandarin` carries two real standard readings — Mainland Putonghua tóufà (fourth tone, matching `characters/髪.md`'s own stored `fà`) and Taiwan Guoyu tóufǎ (third tone) — verified via Wiktionary, which documents 髮/发's citation tone itself splitting along exactly this Mainland/Taiwan line.

Japanese とうはつ (tōhatsu) and Korean 두발 (dubal) are both the standard formal/institutional word for "hair" (dress codes, grooming regulations), alongside more everyday native alternatives (Japanese 髪の毛, kami no ke; Korean 머리카락, meorikarak). Vietnamese keeps the field value tóc, the ordinary everyday native word for hair — the fuller native compound đầu tóc ("head-hair") is more common than the literal Sino-Vietnamese calque đầu phát, which is attested but rare next to it.
