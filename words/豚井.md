## Notes
- [豚 (char)](../characters/豚%20(char).md) "pig" + [井](../characters/井.md) "bowl (via 丼)"

Butadon (ぶたどん) is a rice bowl topped with simmered pork. Although pork rice bowls exist across East Asia, the specific dish called butadon is most closely associated with Obihiro (帯広) in Hokkaido, where it was developed at the Pancake (ぱんちょう) restaurant in 1933 as a way to use the abundant locally raised pigs. Obihiro-style butadon uses thick-cut pork loin or belly grilled over charcoal and glazed with a sweet tare sauce, and the city continues to promote it as a regional speciality.

The Japanese reading ぶたどん uses native vocabulary throughout: 豚 takes its kun-yomi ぶた ("pig"), and 丼/井 is read as どん, the contracted form of どんぶり. This is not the regular concatenation of Dan'a'yo component readings, which would give *tun + jing*.
