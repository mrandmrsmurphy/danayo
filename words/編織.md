## Notes
- [編](../characters/編.md) "compile; weave" + [織](../characters/織.md) "weave" — stand-in for [[織]], which cannot appear independently
- 編織 doubles up two near-synonymous "weaving" roots into a single compound that, across the Sinosphere, names the broad craft of producing fabric or cordage by interlacing fibers — whether by knitting, weaving on a loom, plaiting, or braiding. Mandarin biānzhī and Cantonese pin1 zik1 use it as the everyday umbrella term for "to knit/weave," and Sino-Korean 편직 (pyeonjik) survives as a textile-industry term distinguishing knitted goods (편직물) from woven ones (직물). Japanese never fully naturalized the compound — native speakers reach instead for the indigenous pairing 編み物・織物 — so へんしょく (henshoku) remains a marginal, largely textbook reading; it is also a perfect homophone of 変色 "discoloration," which likely discouraged its everyday use. In Dan'a'yo, 編織 performs a quieter but essential service: as the only word built from 織 "to weave," it is the sole vehicle that lets this otherwise-bound character take its place in the language.
