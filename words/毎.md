## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each"

毎 is "every; each" — a distributive quantifier in Dan'a'yo's correlative paradigm, generating 毎事 (every matter), 毎人 (everyone, each person), 毎時 (every time; always), 毎処 (everywhere), 毎週 (every week). As a modifier 毎 distributes universally over its head noun with a one-by-one sense — each instance is covered individually. This contrasts with 皆 (all, the whole set), which covers the group collectively. The shinjitai 毎 (traditional 每) derives from 母 (mother) with a small stroke addition; in classical Chinese 每 means "every; each; always; on each occasion." 毎 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[毎]].

Mandarin 每 (měi) is the everyday word for "every; each; always": 每天 (every day), 每年 (every year), 每次 (every time), 每个人 (everyone). 每 is among the most frequent Mandarin characters in daily use. Cantonese 每 (mui5): 每日 (mui5 jat6, every day), 每年 (mui5 nin4, every year). Japanese 毎 (まい, mai) in compounds: 毎日 (まいにち, every day), 毎年 (まいとし/まいねん, every year), 毎朝 (まいあさ, every morning); the character rarely stands alone. Korean 매 (毎) in Sino-Korean: 매일 (every day), 매년 (every year), 매주 (every week). Vietnamese mỗi (每): mỗi ngày (every day), mỗi năm (every year); the Sino-Vietnamese compound vowel -ôi is distinct from Mandarin's plain -ei.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 뫼 differs from Korean 매. 毎 reads 뫼 (moi/ㄇㄛㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 明母 (m) + 灰韻 (uʌi) gives Dan'a'yo a diphthong -oi (ㄛㄧ), written 뫼 in Hangul — while Sino-Korean collapses the diphthong to 매 (mae), the plain -ae vowel. The Dan'a'yo form preserves more of the MC compound-vowel structure.
