## Notes
**Form.** 始金 is [始](characters/始.md) “beginning; first; to initiate” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md)) “metal,” matching the treatment of the f-block as 金-elements.

**Etymological background.** The international name _actinium_ comes from Greek ἀκτίς (_aktis_) “ray,” referring to radioactivity. A direct calque would point toward “ray” (光線, 射, 放), but those semantic slots are already occupied (放素 for radium, 射素 for radon). So a strict etymological rendering is no longer optimal.

**Motivation.** 始 does not follow the Greek root. Instead, it encodes a **system-internal structural fact**:

- actinium is the **first element of the actinide series**
- the series itself is conceptually a “beginning block” beneath the main table

So the name is a **positional/structural designation**, not a historical one.

**Type of formation.**  
A **functional reassignment under constraint**:

- original meaning (ray) is blocked by prior allocations
- new meaning chosen from **periodic structure** rather than physical property

This is analogous to the earlier move with elements whose etymology is unusable or already claimed.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Avoids collision with established radiation-based names (放, 射).
2. Provides a clear, high-frequency, unique symbol (始).
3. Reinforces visual/structural grouping in the table (始金 at the head of the actinides).
4. Maintains internal coherence: when etymology fails, use a salient feature.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 锕 (phonetic)
- Japanese: アクチニウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 악티늄 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: actini (phonetic)

All modern forms are phonetic; 始金 is a fully semantic reinterpretation.

**Clarity and issues.**

- The name does not reflect the original “ray” meaning at all.
- However, the system already distributes that semantic field across radium and radon, which are historically closer to the core phenomenon.
- The positional meaning is arguably more useful pedagogically.

**Conclusion.**  
Given the constraints and prior assignments, **始金 is a strong and coherent choice**. It sacrifices etymological fidelity but gains structural clarity and avoids semantic duplication, which is the more important constraint in the system.
