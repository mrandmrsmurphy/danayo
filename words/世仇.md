## Notes
- [世](../characters/世.md) "world, generation" + [仇](../characters/仇.md) "enemy, foe"

世仇 denotes enmity that spans generations — a feud inherited through bloodlines or institutional history rather than arising from personal experience. The compound is neutral in register but carries a gravity that distinguishes it from ordinary antagonism (敵對); it implies that the hostility is structural and deep-rooted, unlikely to be resolved by personal reconciliation alone.

The term appears prominently in historical and literary contexts across the Sinosphere — rival clans, neighboring states with centuries of conflict, and religious schisms all attract the label. In modern usage, 世仇 extends readily to geopolitical discourse (e.g., long-standing national rivalries) and sports rivalry when applied hyperbolically. In Japanese, the compound (せきゅう) is rare in spoken language but appears in classical literature and historical writing. The character 仇 (Dan'a'yo 羅馬字: gyu) is highly bounded; its stand-in word is [[仇敵]].
