## Notes
- [麟](../characters/麟.md) "qilin (mythical creature)" + [経 (char)](../characters/経%20(char).md) "classic; scripture" (alias: 經) — "the Unicorn Classic"

麟経 (*Línjīng*, "the Unicorn Classic") is one of several reverential alternative names for the [[春秋]] (*Spring and Autumn Annals*), alongside [[麟史]] ("the Unicorn History"). The epithet 麟 ("qilin," a mythical chimeric creature — see [[麒麟]]) alludes to the famous final entry of the *Spring and Autumn Annals*, which records the capture of a *qilin* in 481 BC — an omen traditionally read as portending the death of a sage, and linked in commentarial tradition to the death of Confucius not long after. The appearance of the *qilin*, an auspicious creature said to appear only in times of virtuous rule or at the passing of a sage, made it a fitting figure under which to honour the text as a whole.

The naming pattern — referring to a classic text by an evocative image drawn from its content rather than its plain title — is common in the reception history of the Confucian classics; compare how the *Spring and Autumn Annals* is also called [[麟史]] on the same basis. 麟経 and 麟史 are essentially synonyms, used in literary and scholarly contexts as elegant variation for [[春秋]] when referring specifically to the canonical chronicle rather than to the historical period it names ([[春秋時代]]).

The Korean form 인경 illustrates 두음법칙 (*dueum beopchik*, the "initial sound rule"): the character 麟 is read 린 in medial position (as in [[麒麟]], *girin*) but as 인 word-initially, since native Korean phonotactics disfavour word-initial ㄹ before front vowels in Sino-Korean vocabulary.
