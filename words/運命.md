## Notes
- [運](../characters/運.md) "movement; fortune" + [命](../characters/命.md) "life; fate; decree"

運命 is "fate; destiny; doom" — 運 (movement; the course of fortune) + 命 (life; the decreed allotment). The compound literalises fate as "the movement of what is decreed": 運 (the turning course of things; fortune's flow) combined with 命 (the heaven-issued decree that determines one's life and portion). Together they name the inescapable trajectory of a life as determined by forces beyond individual will. 運命 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for [[命]], the bound character for "life; fate; decree." The reversed compound [[命運]] (myeng'un, 命=fate + 運=movement) names the same concept with the same characters in opposite order — in Chinese both orderings are used, while Japanese strongly prefers 運命 (うんめい).

Mandarin 運命 (yùnmìng) is used but 命運 (mìngyùn) is the more common Mandarin form — Mandarin prefers the 命-first ordering. Cantonese 運命 (wan6 ming6). Japanese 運命 (うんめい, unmei) is the overwhelmingly dominant Japanese form: 運命の人 (the person destined for you), 運命論 (fatalism, determinism), 運命的 (fateful). Most famously, Beethoven's Symphony No. 5 is universally known in Japan as 運命 (Unmei, Fate) — the name attached to the opening four-note motif by Johann Friedrich Rochlitz, adopted into Japanese musical culture as the standard title. Korean 운명 (運命, unmyeong): 운명론 (fatalism), 운명적 만남 (a fateful encounter), 운명을 받아들이다 (to accept one's fate). Vietnamese vận mệnh (Sino-Vietnamese: 運=vận, 命=mệnh): vận mệnh quốc gia (the fate of the nation), mang vận mệnh (to carry a destiny).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 운명 exactly matches Korean 운명. 運 reads 운 in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean — MC 云母 (ø, null initial) + 文韻 (ɨun) gives a vowel-initial un-syllable in both systems. 命 reads 명 in both — MC 明母 (m) + 庚三開韻 (ɣiæŋ) gives myeng/명 in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean. The exact identity of 운명 in both systems makes this word fully transparent to a Korean reader — one of the cleaner kwin: true cases where no phonological divergence occurs in either syllable.
