## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle; China" + [華](../characters/華.md) "splendid; Chinese (ethnic/cultural)" + [民](../characters/民.md) "people; citizens" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation; state"

中華民国 is "the Republic of China" — the formal name of the state established in 1912 following the fall of the Qing Dynasty, which has governed Taiwan since 1949. 中華 (Zhōnghuá, "the Chinese nation in its cultural and ethnic dimension" — cf. [[中国]], which is the geopolitical China) + 民国 (mínguó, "republic; people's state" = 民=people/citizens + 国=state/nation). The name distinguishes from the People's Republic of China (中華人民共和国, founded 1949) in political system and constitutional continuity; the ROC claims legal continuity with the 1912 Republic.

Mandarin 中華民國 (zhōnghuá mínguó; traditional 中華民國) is the formal official name; colloquially, 台灣 (Taiwan) is universal in informal speech. Cantonese 中華民國 (zung1 waa4 man4 gwok3). Japanese 中華民国 (ちゅうかみんこく, chūka minkoku). Korean 중화민국 (中華民國, junghwaminguk). Vietnamese Trung Hoa Dân Quốc (Trung Hoa = 中華; Dân Quốc = 民國) is the full formal name.

The ethnic and cultural term 中華 (Zhōnghuá) encompasses all people of Chinese heritage regardless of citizenship — broader than 中国 (PRC), which is primarily a geopolitical term.
