## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [定](../characters/定.md) "fixed; decided; settled" — the state of not being determined or resolved; contrast [[一定]] (fixed, certain) and [[確定]] (finalized)

**不定** (*bù dìng*) is the negation of 定 — the state of not being fixed, settled, or determined. Its core meaning shades across three domains: the grammatical, the mathematical, and the general sense of variability and irregularity.

In **grammar**, 不定 is the standard Sinographic term for *indefinite* — the counterpart to 定 (definite) in the analysis of articles, pronouns, and verb forms. **不定冠詞** (*bùdìng guàncí*) is the indefinite article (English *a/an*; French *un/une*); **不定代詞** (*bùdìng dàicí*) is the indefinite pronoun (*someone, anyone, something*); **不定詞** (*bùdìngcí*, Japanese *futei-shi*) is the infinitive in Latin-derived grammar — the verb form not fixed to a person, number, or tense. This grammatical cluster makes 不定 one of the more frequently encountered terms in the formal analysis of non-Sinographic languages through a Sinographic lens.

In **mathematics**, 不定 covers two related ideas. **不定積分** (*bùdìng jīfēn*, indefinite integral) is the antiderivative — the integral without specified limits of integration, yielding a family of functions differing by a constant *C*, as opposed to the 定積分 (definite integral) which produces a specific number. **不定方程式** (*bùdìng fāngchéngshì*, indeterminate equation) is an equation with more unknowns than constraints, producing infinitely many solutions — Diophantine equations are the classical case. The term also appears in **不定形** (*bùdìng xíng*, indeterminate form) for expressions like 0/0 or ∞/∞ that require limiting analysis (L'Hôpital's rule and its kin) rather than direct evaluation.

In **general usage**, 不定 describes anything that lacks a fixed pattern, schedule, or outcome. **不定期** (*bùdìngqī*) means aperiodic, irregular — published or occurring without a fixed schedule. In colloquial Chinese, 不定 softens into an epistemic hedge: 不定能来 means "may not come / not certain to come" — closer to "perhaps not" than to a formal claim of indeterminacy. This conversational use is largely absent from Japanese and Korean, where the word stays in its formal registers.

Japanese **ふてい** (*futei*) is the dominant modern reading and is used across the grammatical and mathematical senses above; ふじょう (*fujō*, using the JOU reading of 定) appears in some compounds and classical texts. Korean **부정** (*bujeong*) is standard in both grammatical terminology and everyday use for "indefinite, uncertain." Vietnamese **bất định** is the formal Sino-Vietnamese compound used in grammatical and mathematical writing; in everyday Vietnamese, *không chắc* ("not certain") or *chưa xác định* ("not yet determined") are more natural.
