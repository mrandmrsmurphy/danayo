## Notes
- [愛 (char)](../characters/愛%20(char).md) "love; beloved" + [媛](../characters/媛.md) "beautiful woman; princess"
- The Japanese reading えひめ is special: 愛 takes the archaic reading え (not the standard あい), and 媛 takes its native reading ひめ ("princess; noblewoman"). The regular on-yomi would be あいえん, which is never used for this name.

Ehime (愛媛) is a prefecture on the northwest coast of Shikoku, Japan. The name derives from an Old Japanese poetic epithet for the ancient province of Iyo (伊予国), one of four that make up Shikoku. The characters 愛媛 were applied to represent the existing name えひめ ("beloved princess"), not coined from the characters' own meanings — the choice was poetic rather than descriptive.

The prefecture is best known for its mandarin oranges (愛媛みかん), which are among the most prized in Japan, and for Matsuyama, the prefectural capital and birthplace of the haiku poet Masaoka Shiki (正岡子規). Dogo Onsen (道後温泉) in Matsuyama is one of Japan's oldest hot springs and is thought to have inspired the bathhouse in Miyazaki Hayao's *Spirited Away*.

The simplified Chinese alias is 爱媛, using the simplified form of 愛 (爱).
