## Notes
- [妓](../characters/妓.md) "courtesan, female entertainer" + [女 (char)](../characters/女%20(char).md) "woman"

妓女 is the direct, unvarnished word for "prostitute." 妓, a character built from the 女 radical plus phonetic 支, originally denoted a female entertainer skilled in music and dance — a 芸妓 ("geisha," cross-linked from [[妓]]'s own Words section) — whose social and professional proximity to commercial sex work led the character's meaning to broaden over centuries from "skilled female performer" toward "sex worker" specifically, with 妓女 marking the unambiguous end of that semantic spectrum.

妓女 and the next word in this batch, [[舞妓]], thus represent the two poles of meaning that 妓 has carried throughout its history: 妓女 the blunt, modern "prostitute," and 舞妓 the elevated, art-focused "dancing girl/apprentice geisha" that preserves 妓's older, performance-centered sense. The semantic drift visible in 妓 — from "skilled performer" to "sex worker" — recurs across many languages' vocabulary for entertainers in contexts where female performance and commercial sex were historically entangled (cf. English "actress" once carrying similar connotations).
