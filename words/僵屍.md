## Notes
- [僵](../characters/僵.md) "stuff corpse, stiffen" + [死](../characters/死.md) "die" — stand-in for [[僵]], which cannot appear independently.

死 here stands in for its alias 屍 ("corpse, dead body"), not for its primary sense of "to die." 僵屍 (jiāngshī) literally means "a stiffened corpse" — a body that has gone rigid after death — but the compound is best known across the Sinosphere as the term for the reanimated corpse of East Asian folklore (the "jiangshi" or "Chinese zombie"): a stiff-limbed, hopping undead creature that drains the life force of the living. The Korean 강시 in particular is closely associated with this folkloric figure, popularized through Hong Kong cinema.

Because 僵 and 死 are the vault's canonical bearers of Dan'a'yo phonology, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 here are mechanically concatenated from their own fixed Dan'a'yo values (걍싀 / gyangsiǝ / ㄍ⼘ㄫㄙㄧㄜ). The mandarin, cantonese, korean, and vietnamese fields reflect the real-world readings of 僵屍, using 屍's own attested reading (shī / si1 / 시 / thi) rather than 死's (sǐ / sei2 / 사 / tử). The Sino-Japanese reading happens to coincide regardless — both 死 and 屍 are read シ — so きょうし requires no adjustment. This is the same divergence pattern seen in 波蘭, 六楽, and 魏峨.
