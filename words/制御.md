## Notes
- [制](../characters/制.md) "restrict; control" + [御](../characters/御.md) "govern; drive; defend"

The compound combines two characters with overlapping senses of governing and containing: 制 carries the image of cutting and shaping (knife applied to unfinished wood), extended to the idea of regulating and restricting; 御 carries the image of driving or managing — originally guiding horses, extended to governing, defending, and controlling. Together they express the active exertion of authority over something to keep it within bounds.

The Dan'a'yo form 制御 is identical to the simplified Chinese form and the Japanese shinjitai form; in both cases 御 has replaced the traditional 禦 (*yù*, "to resist; to ward off"), which 御 carries as an alias. The alternate traditional form 制馭 uses 馭 ("to drive horses; to control") as the second character, emphasizing the reining-in metaphor. Japanese also attests 制禦 as a classical spelling alongside the modern 制御.

Japanese せいぎょ is the regular on-yomi concatenation (SEI + GYO). Korean 제어 (制御) is the standard term for "control" across engineering, systems theory, and everyday usage — 제어 시스템 ("control system") is ubiquitous in technical Korean. Vietnamese *chế ngự* preserves the Sino-Vietnamese phonology and means "to master; to dominate; to bring under control."
