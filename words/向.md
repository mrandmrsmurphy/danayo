## Notes
- [向 (char)](../characters/向%20(char).md) "toward; -ward"

向 is the allative case particle — 向格, marking the direction of motion or orientation: "toward; in the direction of." It attaches to a goal or direction noun to indicate that something is aimed at or moving toward it: 向城走 (walk toward the city), 向西行 (travel westward), 向師問 (inquire toward the teacher). Unlike 于 (dative, specifically an animate recipient) and 至 (terminative, the endpoint actually reached), 向 marks the direction of orientation without necessarily implying arrival — it can express intent or facing without completion. In the comparison chart, the 向格 row is left blank for CJKV equivalents, reflecting that the allative is not typically a distinct grammatical case in these languages, its meaning instead expressed through directional complements or prepositions. 向 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[向]].

Mandarin 向 (xiàng) itself functions as an allative preposition meaning "toward; facing": 向北走 (walk northward), 向他請教 (consult him; seek guidance from him), 向前看 (look ahead). This overlaps closely with 往 (wǎng, toward; go to) and 朝 (cháo, facing; toward). Cantonese 向 (hoeng3): 向前行 (go forward; walk toward). Japanese: the allative is not a distinct particle — に (ni) marks goals of motion (北に向かう, head toward the north; 学校に行く, go to school) and directional complements handle the rest. Korean 향해(서) (hyanghae-seo, toward; in the direction of): 서울을 향해 달리다 (run toward Seoul), 미래를 향해 나아가다 (move forward toward the future). Vietnamese hướng (toward; in the direction of): đi hướng bắc (go northward), hướng tới tương lai (move toward the future). Sino-Vietnamese hướng mirrors the character 向 and its meaning directly.

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 향 exactly matches Korean 향. 向 reads 향 (hyang/ㄏ⼘ㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 曉母 (x) + 陽開韻 (ɨɐŋ) gives a fricative onset with the -yang vowel and -ng final in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, producing identical 향 forms.
