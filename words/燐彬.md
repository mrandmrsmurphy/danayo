## Notes
- [燐](../characters/燐.md) as 代用字 for 璘/瞵 "luster of jade" + [彬](../characters/彬.md) as 代用字 for 霦/㻞/斌/豳
- 燐 carries 璘 as an alias; 彬 carries 霦 as an alias. Together they authorize the Dan'a'yo spelling 燐彬 in place of the primary classical form 璘霦.

燐彬 is a classical literary adjective describing the shimmering, iridescent quality of fine jade — the way light moves across a polished surface and breaks into different colours, or simply gleams with an inner radiance. It belongs to a register of Chinese prose and poetry in which the beauty of precious stones is evoked through the precise naming of their visual qualities: not just "beautiful" but the particular visual behaviour of jade under light.

The compound survives in six classical graphic variants, all *línbīn* in Mandarin:

| First character | Second character | Form |
|---|---|---|
| 璘 (luster of jade) | 霦 (rain + 彬; hapax) | **璘霦** (canonical) |
| 璘 | 彬 (cultivated; refined) | 璘彬 |
| 璘 | 㻞 (jade variant) | 璘㻞 |
| 瞵 (gaze; glare) | 㻞 | 瞵㻞 |
| 璘 | 斌 (cultured; refined) | 璘斌 |
| 璘 | 豳 (archaic place character) | 璘豳 |

The proliferation of variants reflects the fate of a rare literary word: scribes and woodblock carvers working from memory or damaged originals substituted visually or phonetically similar characters, and multiple forms entered the written record without any single one achieving definitive currency. The second character 霦 is particularly obscure — it is defined in historical dictionaries as appearing *only* in 璘霦, with no independent usage; it is a character that exists solely to spell this compound.
