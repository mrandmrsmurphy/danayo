## Notes
- [分 (char)](../characters/分%20(char).md) "divide; part" + [之 (char)](../characters/之%20(char).md) "'s; which (classical possessive/linking particle)" — an independent compound, a fixed grammatical construction rather than an ordinary lexical word; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

**A genuinely Sino-specific grammatical construction, left honestly incomplete rather than fabricated**: 分之 is the fixed fraction-forming particle used denominator-first — 三分之一 (sān fēnzhī yī), literally "of three parts, one," i.e. "one third." Mandarin fēnzhī, Cantonese fan1 zi1, Japanese 分の (bun no, e.g. 三分の一), and Korean 분의 (bunui, e.g. 삼분의 일) all use this same construction. Vietnamese, however, expresses fractions with an entirely different word order and vocabulary — một phần ba ("one part three," numerator-first, using the native word phần rather than a 分之-style particle) — so `vietnamese` was left blank rather than force a fabricated compositional reading for a construction Vietnamese simply doesn't share.
