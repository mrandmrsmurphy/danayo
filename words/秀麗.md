## Notes
- [秀 (char)](../characters/秀%20(char).md) "outstanding, excellent" + [麗](../characters/麗.md) "lovely, beautiful" — a graceful, elegant beauty, most often applied to scenery. This word is the viable form of [麗](../characters/麗.md): 麗 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 秀麗 is its `stand_in` compound, keeping 麗's sense the "lovely, graceful" one — matching its own `korean_native` gloss 고울 ("to be beautiful, lovely") — in contrast to [[華美]] (the "radiant, resplendent" sense that [[華]] stands in for instead), splitting apart the two flavors the collapsed compound [[華麗]] used to blur together.

秀麗 is the word reached for across all three East Asian daughters specifically for graceful natural or physical beauty, distinct from 華美's showier ornateness. Japanese しゅうれい (shūrei) is the standard descriptor for a beautiful mountain or landscape (秀麗な富士山, "the graceful Mt. Fuji") and, applied to a person, a refined, elegant appearance. Korean 수려 (suryeo) — almost always as 수려하다 (suryeohada) — follows the identical pattern, describing striking natural scenery or a graceful face. Mandarin xiùlì and Cantonese sau3lai6 keep the same register.
