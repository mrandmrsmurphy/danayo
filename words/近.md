## Notes
- [近 (char)](../characters/近%20(char).md) "near" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin jìn, Cantonese gan6, and Vietnamese cận (cận thị, "nearsighted"; phụ cận, "vicinity") all use this character productively and independently for "near." Japanese uses the native kun'yomi adjective 近い (ちかい) for everyday "near," reserving on'yomi きん for compounds (近代, きんだい, "modern era"). `kwin: false` reflects a genuine, deliberate divergence: Dan'a'yo 긴 (gin) and Korean 근 (geun) trace to the same source but diverge in vowel — Vietnamese also shows the same split pattern, with the everyday native word for "near" being gần rather than the Sino-Vietnamese cận.
