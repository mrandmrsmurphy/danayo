## Notes
- [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "thing, action" + [情](../characters/情.md) "feelings, emotion"

事情 is "affair, matter, situation" — the circumstances or particulars surrounding some state of affairs, distinct from a single concrete "thing" ([[事物]]) by combining 事 "matter" with 情 "feeling, condition," giving a sense closer to "the way things stand" or "what's going on" than to any one discrete object or event.

Mandarin 事情 (shìqíng, "matter, affair, business"). Cantonese 事情 (si6cing4). Japanese 事情 (じじょう, jijō) leans toward "circumstances, reasons" in a slightly formal register — 事情がある ("there are circumstances/reasons [I can't say more]") is a common polite deflection. Korean 사정 (sajeong) carries the same "circumstances, one's own situation" sense and is very frequent in everyday speech — 사정이 있다 ("to have one's [personal] reasons/circumstances") is a standard idiom, paralleling the Japanese usage closely. Vietnamese sự tình ("the situation, the ins and outs of a matter") is used similarly, often in the context of explaining what actually happened in some affair.
