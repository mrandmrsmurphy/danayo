## Notes
- [標](../characters/標.md) "sign, mark, label" + [識](../characters/識.md) "recognize, know; mark, record"

This word is the stand-in for [標](../characters/標.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 標識 carries the same sense of a visible sign or distinguishing mark.

標識 denotes a sign, mark, or symbol used to identify, indicate, or distinguish — a road sign, a brand mark, a biological marker, a distinguishing feature. In Chinese, 標識 is a variant of the more common 標誌 (biāozhì), where the second character 誌 (to record, to mark) is substituted by 識 used in its alternate reading *zhì*. Both forms mean the same thing and are used interchangeably in formal writing, though 標誌 is more common in mainland China and 標識 appears more in traditional and Japanese-influenced contexts.

In Japanese (ひょうしき, hyōshiki), 標識 is the standard word for a posted sign — road signs (道路標識, dōro hyōshiki), warning markers, and identification marks of all kinds. The compound is common in daily life and technical writing. In Korean (표식), the word denotes a mark or symbol used for identification, appearing in military, scientific, and administrative contexts.
