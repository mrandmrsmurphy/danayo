## Notes
- [償](../characters/償.md) "repay; recompense" + [還](../characters/還.md) "return; repatriate" — the ordinary legal or financial act of returning what is owed; settling a debt or compensating for a loss

償還 is mundane repayment: loan repayment (貸款償還), reparations, or any context where something is simply returned or compensated. It carries no theological weight. Contrast [[救贖]] (redemption/atonement), which shares the surface metaphor of payment but operates in a completely different register: 救贖 involves divine grace and liberation from sin; 償還 involves ledgers and obligations.

Dan'a'yo uses 償還 for financial and legal restitution only. Using it to translate the Christian concept of redemption would strip the word of its theological depth and reduce the Cross to a transaction — the very conflation the lexipedia explicitly marks as an error (救贖 ≠ 償還).
