## Notes
- [侠](../characters/侠.md) "knight-errant, chivalrous hero" + [客](../characters/客.md) "guest, traveller, wanderer"
- This word is the stand-in for [[侠]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- The figure of the wandering chivalrous swordsman — a stock character across East Asian literary traditions. 侠 (hiệp in Vietnamese) encodes the ethic of heroic loyalty and righteous violence; 客 frames the hero as a roaming independent, beholden to no lord. In Chinese wuxia (武侠) fiction, 侠客 is the standard term for such a figure — from the Tang dynasty tales of 俠客行 (Xíkè xíng) to the novels of Jin Yong. In Japanese, きょうかく (kyōkaku) appears in historical dramas and refers to a swordsman or champion of the weak outside formal social structures. Korean 협객 similarly appears in period dramas for heroic wandering figures.
