## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big" + [韓](../characters/韓.md) "Han (Korean)" + [[民国]] ([民](../characters/民.md) "citizen" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation")

**大韓民国** ("Great Han Republic") is the official name of the Republic of Korea, commonly known as South Korea. The 大韓 component derives from the name of the Korean Empire (大韓帝国, 1897–1910), which itself revived the ancient 三韓 ("Three Hans") — the confederacies of the Korean peninsula in the early centuries AD. [[民国]] ("people's republic; republic") is the same compound that appears in the name of the Republic of China ([[中華民国]]), reflecting the parallel republican movements of the early twentieth century across East Asia.

The Republic of Korea was established in 1948 following the division of the peninsula after the Second World War. Its northern counterpart, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, uses the ethnonym 朝鮮 rather than 韓 in its official name.
