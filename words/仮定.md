## Notes
- [仮](../characters/仮.md) "provisional, borrowed" + [定](../characters/定.md) "to fix, to decide" — to assume; to suppose; a hypothesis; a provisional stipulation

仮 contributes the sense of the provisional and the borrowed — something treated as real for the moment without being confirmed as real. 定 fixes it in place. Together they name the cognitive and rhetorical operation of treating an unconfirmed proposition as settled in order to reason from it: if we assume X, then Y follows.

In logical and mathematical contexts 仮定 is the standard term for a hypothesis or premise — the conditions stipulated at the outset of a proof or argument. In everyday speech it functions as the verb "to suppose" and as a noun for the suppositions one is working with: 仮定の話 (a hypothetical discussion), 仮定すれば (assuming that). In grammar, 仮定条件 (hypothetical conditional) names the if-clause of a conditional construction — the assumed, not-yet-realized situation that triggers the consequence.

仮定 is distinct from 前提 (premise — what is taken as given and not argued for) and 仮説 (hypothesis in the scientific sense — a testable proposition, not merely an assumption for the sake of argument). The alias 假定 is the full traditional-character form; 仮 is the Japanese simplified form of 假.
