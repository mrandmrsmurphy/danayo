## Notes
- [制](../characters/制.md) "regulate, control" + [限](../characters/限.md) "limit, boundary" — a fixed bound placed on some quantity or action.

This entry uses the Japanese/Korean character order (制限) rather than the order Mandarin generally prefers for the same concept, 限制 (xiànzhì, "limit-regulate"); both orders are transparent compounds of the same two roots, and Korean's 제한 — extremely common in everyday vocabulary like 제한속도 ("speed limit") — independently fixes 制限 as the standard order in that language, which is why it was chosen here. Compare [[制約]] (already attested), which describes a constraining circumstance or condition rather than a hard numeric or legal boundary; 制限 is the more concrete sibling, the word for an actual cap, ceiling, or restriction with a specific extent. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
