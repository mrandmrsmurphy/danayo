## Notes
- [国](../characters/国.md) "nation" + [主](../characters/主.md) "lord, master" — the sovereign or ruling lord of a state. In Japan's Edo-period feudal system, 国主 (kokushu) named a specific rank of daimyo whose domain was large enough to constitute an entire historical province, distinguishing them from lesser lords; more generally across the Sinosphere the term simply names whoever holds supreme authority over a country, used historically of figures such as the Japanese emperor in his role as titular national sovereign even where actual governance lay with a shogun.
