## Notes
- [五 (char)](../characters/五%20(char).md) "five" + [色](../characters/色.md) "color"

The five primary colors of traditional East Asian cosmology, each corresponding to one of the Five Elements ([[五行]]) and one of the Five Directions ([[五方]]):

| Color | Element | Direction | Season | Associated animal |
|-------|---------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| 青 (blue-green) | 木 Wood | East | Spring | Azure Dragon 青龍 |
| 赤 (red) | 火 Fire | South | Summer | Vermilion Bird 朱雀 |
| 黄 (yellow) | 土 Earth | Center | Midsummer | Yellow Dragon 黄龍 |
| 白 (white) | 金 Metal | West | Autumn | White Tiger 白虎 |
| 黒 (black) | 水 Water | North | Winter | Black Tortoise 玄武 |

This color system was codified during the Han dynasty and remained the foundation of imperial ceremonial color for two millennia. Yellow (the center, Earth) was the exclusive color of the Emperor; only the Son of Heaven could wear yellow robes or live in yellow-roofed buildings. Red was the color of celebration and good fortune. White was the color of mourning in Chinese culture — the inverse of the Western convention. In Buddhist iconography, the five colors are associated with the five Dhyāni Buddhas.

The modern Chinese expression 五顔六色 (wǔyán liùsè, "five faces, six colors") means "colorful; multicolored," preserving 五色 as a shorthand for richness and variety of hue. Compare [[七色]] (the seven spectral colors of the rainbow).
