## Notes
- [九 (char)](../characters/九%20(char).md) "nine" + [族](../characters/族.md) "tribe; clan"

The nine degrees of familial kinship, counting four generations up and four generations down from the individual:

1. Great-great-grandparents (高祖)
2. Great-grandparents (曾祖)
3. Grandparents (祖)
4. Parents (父)
5. **Self** (己)
6. Children (子)
7. Grandchildren (孫)
8. Great-grandchildren (曾孫)
9. Great-great-grandchildren (玄孫)

The concept originates in the *Book of Documents* (尚書) and carried profound legal weight in imperial China through the punishment of **"extermination of the nine generations"** (誅九族 / 族誅). Under this doctrine — one of the gravest penalties in Chinese imperial law — if a person committed a capital crime such as high treason or regicide, every member of all nine generations of their family could be executed. This collective punishment was intended to eliminate any possibility of future revenge and to make every living relative a guardian of the offender's loyalty.
