## Notes
- [募](../characters/募.md) "recruit, gather by appeal" + [集](../characters/集.md) "collect, assemble" — to call for and gather applicants or participants; stand-in for [[募]], which cannot appear independently

**募集** (*mùjí*) is the standard compound for any public call to gather people or resources: recruiting employees, soliciting donations, calling for submissions, enrolling volunteers. 募 carries the sense of drawing in by appeal — summoning people through announcement — while 集 adds the assembling of what has been gathered. The compound covers the full arc of solicitation: the public call, the response, and the resultant gathering.

The word is ubiquitous in contemporary East Asian administrative, commercial, and institutional language. **社員募集** (*shain boshū*, employee recruitment) appears on job postings; **寄付金募集** (*kifukin boshū*, donation drive) on fundraising campaigns; **参加者募集** (*sankasha boshū*, seeking participants) on event announcements. The compound functions as both verb (募集する, to recruit) and noun (募集をかける, to put out a call for). In Chinese, 募集 is the formal register counterpart of the more colloquial 招募 (*zhāomù*) and 征募 (*zhēngmù*); in official and legal contexts — government bond issuance (公債募集), share offering (股票募集) — 募集 is the standard term.

The character 募 combines 莫 (phonetic) and 力 (force, effort) — effort applied to draw people toward a cause — though the phonetic component is primary. Its classical use was military: **募兵** (*mùbīng*, recruiting soldiers) is one of the earliest documented uses, contrasted with the conscript army (征兵制) — a 募兵制 (volunteer recruitment system) draws soldiers by incentive rather than compulsion. The distinction between volunteer and conscript armies, articulated through 募 vs. 征, runs through Chinese military history from the Han dynasty's experimentation with professional armies through the 宋-dynasty shift to contracted soldier groups and into modern debates about conscription.

Korean **모집** (*mojip*) is one of the most common Sino-Korean words in everyday administrative life: 직원 모집 (job recruitment), 회원 모집 (membership drive), 원고 모집 (call for submissions). That the Dan'a'yo 諺文 concatenation (모 + 집) is identical to the Korean reading makes this one of the more phonologically stable compounds in the vocabulary. Japanese **ぼしゅう** (*boshū*) is equally ubiquitous and essentially universal in Japanese institutional language. Vietnamese **mộ tập** (*mộ* from 募, *tập* from 集) is the Sino-Vietnamese compound; in practice, modern Vietnamese uses **tuyển dụng** (recruitment/hiring), **kêu gọi** (to call for), or **chiêu mộ** in contexts where 募集 appears in Chinese or Japanese.
