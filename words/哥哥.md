## Notes
- [哥](../characters/哥.md) "dear older brother" × 2 (reduplication)

哥哥 is the colloquial northern Mandarin term for an elder brother — informal, intimate, and warmer in register than the classical 兄 (xiōng). The character 哥 itself is not from the classical sinographic stratum: it does not appear in the Shuowen Jiezi and is generally considered a later borrowing or phonosemantic coinage of northern Chinese vernacular origin, possibly from Altaic contact languages that entered during the Tang period. Its graphemic form (two 可 stacked) reflects a phonetic reduplication rather than semantic composition. The reduplication in 哥哥 is a standard Mandarin kinship suffix pattern — 爸爸 (bàba, father), 媽媽 (māma, mother), 姐姐 (jiějie, elder sister) — that creates an intimate, childlike register.

Across the CJKV sphere, the colloquial 哥哥 form belongs to Mandarin and Cantonese; the other languages use native kinship terms for this relationship. Japanese employs お兄さん (onii-san) or 兄 (ani), Korean uses 형 (hyeong, male speaker) or 오빠 (oppa, female speaker), and Vietnamese uses anh (older brother or male peer). The sinographic form 兄 (xiōng in Mandarin) is the formal and literary term used in all five CJKV literary traditions, while 哥 marks the informal layer that Dan'a'yo has chosen to incorporate — likely reflecting its importance in modern spoken Mandarin, where it also functions as a general term of address for any older male (大哥, older brother figure; 哥們, buddies).

The Cantonese reading go1-4 go1 reflects tone sandhi: the first syllable is pronounced in the low-falling tone 4 in running speech even though the character's citation tone is tone 1, a common pattern with reduplicated kinship terms in Cantonese.
