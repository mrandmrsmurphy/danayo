## Notes
- [要](../characters/要.md) "important, need" + [請 (char)](../characters/請%20(char).md) "ask, invite, request, please"

要請 is the formal counterpart to [[要求]] (要 "need" + 求 "seek"). Both compounds put 要 "need, want" first, but the second character sets the register:

- 求 "to seek, pursue" gives 要求 the sense of a **demand or claim** — often a personal or unilateral assertion of what one is owed.
- 請 "to politely ask, invite" (cf. 申請 "apply", 招請 "invite formally") gives 要請 the sense of a **formal appeal or official request** — typically directed upward or outward, e.g. a request from a government, organization, or authority for action or cooperation.

So while both translate loosely as "request/demand," 要求 leans toward *insisting*, and 要請 leans toward *formally asking*.
