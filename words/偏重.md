## Notes
- [偏 (char)](../characters/偏%20(char).md) "lean, slant, partial" + [重 (char)](../characters/重%20(char).md) "heavy, weighty" — to place disproportionate weight on one side of something, at the expense of balance.

Where [[均衡]] names the balanced state, 偏重 names its failure: a one-sided emphasis, as when a curriculum is criticized for 偏重 on rote memorization at the expense of critical thinking, or an economy for 偏重 on a single export. 偏 supplies the "lopsided" half of a small family of bias-words built the same way, including the not-yet-created 偏見 ("prejudice," literally "a slanted view"); 重 is one of the vocabulary's most prolific roots, anchoring 重要 ("important"), 重量 ("weight"), and the technical 重複 ("duplication," literally "stacked-and-repeated"). Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
