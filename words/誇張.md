## Notes
- [誇 (char)](../characters/誇%20(char).md) "boast, brag" + [張](../characters/張.md) "stretch, extend" — to stretch a boast beyond its true size; to exaggerate.

誇張 stays remarkably consistent across the whole family, in both everyday use ("don't exaggerate," "an overstated claim") and as the standard technical term for the rhetorical device of hyperbole in literary and rhetorical analysis — 誇張法/과장법/khoa trương pháp all name the same figure of speech, the deliberate overstatement used for emphasis or vivid effect (in Japanese specifically 誇張法 rather than the borrowed Western term "hyperbole"). Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all keep the same literal "stretch a boast" image intact, with no semantic drift the way many compounds in this vault show.
