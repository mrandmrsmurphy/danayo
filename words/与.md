## Notes
- [与 (char)](../characters/与%20(char).md) "and; with; nominal conjunction"

与 is the nominal conjunction in Dan'a'yo — "and; with; together with" — connecting two nouns or noun phrases within a single argument: 父与母 (father and mother), 山与水 (mountain and river), 与君 (together with you; along with you). Among the Dan'a'yo linkers, 与 is the nominal connector — linking noun-level constituents — contrasting with [[而]] (clausal "and"), which connects whole predicates or clauses. The character is a 会意 of raised hands (from 臼 and 廾) supporting or passing an object, depicting "giving; offering; together with." 与 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[与]].

Mandarin 与 (yǔ, and; with — literary), 和 (hé, and — conversational: 山和水 "mountain and river"), 跟 (gēn, and; with — colloquial, follows subject NP), 以及 (yǐjí, as well as; and — formal enumeration). Cantonese 同 (tung4, and; with — colloquial: 你同我 "you and I"), 與 (jyu5, and; with — formal/written). Japanese と (to, and — exhaustive noun conjunction: 山と川 "mountain and river"), や (ya, and — non-exhaustive list: 山や川 "mountains, rivers, etc."), に (ni, with — in some constructions). Korean 과/와 (gwa/wa, and; with — nominal: 산과 강 "mountain and river"), 하고 (hago, and; with — colloquial: 너하고 나 "you and I"), 이랑/랑 (irang/rang, and; with — informal). Vietnamese và (and; with — both clausal and nominal), cùng (together with; also), với (with; along with).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 요 does not match Korean 여. 与 reads 요 ('yo/⼄) in Dan'a'yo — MC 以母 (j, palatal glide) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ). The MC palatal glide j (以母): Dan'a'yo gives initial /y/ (⼄ in Dan'a'yo Bopomofo), while Korean 여 incorporates the /y/ as part of the vowel onset. The MC final 魚韻 (ɨʌ): Dan'a'yo gives -o (요, y-o), while Korean gives -yeo (여, y-eo). Both preserve the MC palatal glide, but the vowel differs: Dan'a'yo -o (요) vs Korean -yeo (여). The 魚韻 → -o correspondence in Dan'a'yo was also seen in [[且]] (co/초 vs 차) and [[魚]] where Dan'a'yo consistently maps 魚韻 to the -o vowel.
