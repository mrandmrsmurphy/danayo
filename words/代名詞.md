## Notes
- [代](../characters/代.md) "substitute, stand in for" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name" + [詞](../characters/詞.md) "word, part of speech" — a word that stands in for a name: a pronoun.

The grammar book notes that Dan'a'yo has no separate class of personal pronouns (人称代名詞) in the usual sense, though two words take the plural suffix 等 in a special way reminiscent of one. 代 supplies the "substitute" sense found throughout the vocabulary in [[代用]] and [[代用字]] (already attested); 詞 is the general word-class suffix seen in [[形容詞]] ("adjective") and the grammar's own part-of-speech terms. The Vietnamese đại danh từ preserves the full three-morpheme structure even though colloquial modern Vietnamese often shortens it to đại từ. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
