## Notes
- [聖](../characters/聖.md) "holy; sacred" + [餐 (char)](../characters/餐%20(char).md) "meal; to eat" — the sacred meal; the liturgical rite in which bread and wine are shared in remembrance of Christ's death and in anticipation of the Kingdom

聖餐 is the standard Chinese Protestant term for the Lord's Supper or Eucharist. The compound literally means "holy meal," corresponding to the New Testament institution at the Last Supper (Matthew 26:26–29). In Reformed and evangelical usage, 聖餐 is the preferred term; Catholic usage in Chinese more often employs 聖體聖血 or simply 感恩祭 (Mass/Eucharist).

The Korean 성찬 (聖餐) is the dominant Protestant term in that tradition as well; the Catholic equivalent is typically 성체성사 (聖體聖事). In Dan'a'yo, 聖餐 is placed under [[聖礼]] (sacraments) in the [[基督敎]] lexipedia, alongside [[洗礼]] (baptism), as one of the two principal sacraments recognized across most of the Protestant tradition.
