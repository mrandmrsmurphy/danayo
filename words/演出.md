## Notes
- [演 (char)](../characters/演%20(char).md) "perform" + [出 (char)](../characters/出%20(char).md) "exit" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 演出 is an independent compound. `kwin: true` per the AND-rule: both constituent characters are individually `kwin: true`.
- **Content removed**: `english` previously read "choreograph, stage" — "choreograph" specifically means designing dance movements, which is not this word's actual sense; removed.
- A subtle divergence worth noting: Mandarin/Cantonese 演出/jin2 ceot1 centers on the performers' own act of performing, or the performance/show itself (一场演出 "a performance"). Japanese えんしゅつ leans more toward the director's/producer's creative act of staging and directing a production (演出家, "director/producer" of a play or show) — closer to "direction" than "performing" per se. Vietnamese diễn xuất specifically means "acting, acting performance" (as in film/theater reviews, diễn xuất tốt "good acting"), closest to the Mandarin sense.
