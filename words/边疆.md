## Notes
- [辺](../characters/辺.md) "edge, border, side" + [疆](../characters/疆.md) "boundary, territory" — this word is the stand-in for [疆](../characters/疆.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

边疆 names the frontier region itself — the outlying territory near a country's border, as opposed to 邊界/辺界 ("the border line" as a boundary) or 邊境/辺境 ("the border area" more generally). The word is written here with 辺, this vault's chosen form for the character that also appears as traditional 邊 and simplified 边 — three orthographic forms of one word, already unified under [[辺]]'s own `aliases:` field, matching this vault's convention that the parent entry stands for all its alias forms.

**Japanese preserves 辺疆 (henkyō) as the historically original form of a still-current word** — 辺境, the everyday modern spelling, substitutes 境 ("boundary") for 疆 for legibility (疆 is not a Jōyō character), the same kind of phonetic/semantic kanji substitution seen elsewhere in this vault (e.g. 罔/両 standing in for 魍/魎). [[辺境]] is already a Dan'a'yo word in its own right; 边疆 and 辺境 are near-synonyms sharing the same first syllable, differing mainly in register — 边疆 leans toward the classical/literary and toward Chinese/Korean usage specifically, while 辺境 is the default in Japanese.

Korean 변강 (byeon-gang) and Vietnamese biên cương both concatenate their characters' own independent readings transparently. **Flagging, not fixing**: [[辺]]'s own stored `cantonese` field reads `dou6`, which does not correspond to any attested reading of 邊/边/辺 — the real, Wiktionary-confirmed Cantonese for this word is `bin1 goeng1`, used here for 边疆's own frontmatter; `辺.md`'s stored value looks like a plain data error, worth a dedicated look outside the scope of this word-creation task.
