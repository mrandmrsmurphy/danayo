## Notes
- [相](../characters/相.md) "mutual, each other" + [当 (char)](../characters/当%20(char).md) "correspond to, hit the mark" — to correspond closely enough to count as equivalent; by extension, "considerable, fairly large."

The word does double duty in its source languages, much as it would here: as a verb-like predicate (X 相当 Y, "X corresponds to/is equivalent to Y" — the grammar book uses exactly this sense to compare 等 to the English et al. or etc., 英語「et al」「etc」に相当） and as a degree-adverb meaning "quite, fairly, considerably" (相当な努力, "considerable effort"). The dual function is shared across Mandarin, Japanese, and Korean alike, suggesting the two senses are old and tightly bound rather than a later Japanese innovation. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
