## Notes
- [日 (char)](../characters/日%20(char).md) "day, sun" + [語](../characters/語.md) "language" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (日's own points to bare `日`, 語's own to `言語`); 日語 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/日 (char).md` and `characters/語.md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- A two-character clipping of [[日本語]] ("Japanese language," lit. "Japan-language"), common in Chinese (rìyǔ) and Korean (일어) as the everyday word for the language. `japanese` was corrected from `にほんご` to `にちご` (verified via search) — にほんご is specifically the reading of the fuller three-character 日本語, not of 日語 itself; 日語 read as にちご exists in Japanese only as a rare, dialectal/academic synonym, not the everyday term (Japanese speakers overwhelmingly say 日本語).
