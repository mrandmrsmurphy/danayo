## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle, midst" + [止](../characters/止.md) "stop" — literally "to stop in the middle": breaking off an ongoing action or process before it reaches its natural end. This word is the viable form of [止](../characters/止.md): 止 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 中止 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 止's sense the ending of an activity, in contrast to [[停留]] (the staying-in-place sense that 停 stands in for) — splitting apart what the collapsed compound [[停止]] had blurred into one generic "stop."

Mandarin zhōngzhǐ and Japanese chūshi both describe calling something off partway through — 中止比賽/試合を中止する (calling off a match), 中止妊娠 (to terminate a pregnancy, in formal/legal register). Korean 중지 covers the identical ground and is just as common — 공사 중지 (a construction halt), 사용 중지 (suspension of use). Cantonese zung1zi2 follows Mandarin closely. Unlike [[停留]], where the subject is still going somewhere and merely pauses, 中止 marks the activity itself ending before completion — there's no "continuing on" implied afterward.
