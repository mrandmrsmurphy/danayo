## Notes
- [喜 (char)](../characters/喜%20(char).md) "glad; like; enjoy" + [悦](../characters/悦.md) "pleased; ecstatic" — stand-in for [[悦]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo

喜悅 is a coordinate compound — two near-synonyms joined to intensify the same emotional direction — meaning "joyous; ecstatically happy." 喜 contributes the sense of outward, active gladness (to rejoice; to enjoy; to celebrate), while 悦 contributes inner contentment and gratification (to be pleased; to delight in). Together they form the Dan'a'yo word for a deep, complete joy encompassing both its outward and inward dimensions. The compound appears widely in Mandarin literary prose and Classical Chinese, and Japanese きえつ (kietsu) is the Sino-Japanese reading, though the everyday Japanese equivalents are 喜び (yorokobi, joy) and 嬉しい (ureshii, glad). The alias 喜悦 is the simplified-form spelling (悦 for 悅).

喜 (ㄏㄧ/hi/히 — Mandarin xǐ, Cantonese hei2, Korean 희, Japanese KI/き, Vietnamese hỷ): "glad; pleased; enjoy; happy event" — MC 曉母 (x) + 之韻 (ɨ); a 会意 of 壴 (drum) + 口 (mouth), depicting the cry of joy at drumbeat, giving "to rejoice; to be glad; happy event." 悦 (⼶ㄊ/'yed/엳 — Mandarin yuè, Cantonese jyut6, Korean 열, Japanese ETSU/えつ): "pleased; gratified; ecstatic" — MC 以母 (j) + 薛A三合韻 (iuᴇt, entering tone); a 形声 of semantic 心 (heart) + phonetic 兌 (OC *lʷads), "to be inwardly gratified; delighted." The traditional form is 悅.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 히엳 does not match Korean 희열. 喜 gives 히 vs 희 (之韻 vowel quality differs); 悦 gives 엳 vs 열 (entering-tone -t coda preserved in Dan'a'yo, dropped in Korean).
