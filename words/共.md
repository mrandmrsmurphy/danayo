## Notes
- [共 (char)](../characters/共%20(char).md) "with; together"

共 is the comitative case particle — 共格, marking accompaniment: "with; together with." It attaches to a companion noun to indicate that the action is performed jointly: 共父走 (run together with father), 共朋友出 (go out with friends), 共衆人議 (deliberate together with the assembled people). In the comparison chart, 共 parallels Mandarin 跟/和 (gēn/hé, with; together with), Cantonese 同 (tung4), Japanese と (to, with; and), Korean 와/과 (wa/gwa, with; and), Vietnamese với (with; together). The comitative differs from the instrumental 以: 以杖行 is "walk by means of a cane" (the cane as tool), while 共師行 is "walk together with the teacher" (the teacher as companion). 共 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[共]].

Mandarin 跟/和 (gēn/hé): 我跟他走 (I'm going with him), 你和我 (you and I), 和朋友吃飯 (eat with friends). In classical Mandarin, 與 (yǔ) was used for "with"; modern usage prefers 跟 (gen, lit. "to follow; heel") for accompaniment and 和 (hé, lit. "harmony") for coordination. Cantonese 同 (tung4): 我同你去 (I'll go with you), 同朋友食飯 (eat with friends). Japanese と (to): 友達と話す (talk with a friend), 家族と旅行する (travel with family), 一緒に (together). Korean 와/과 (wa/gwa): 친구와 함께 가다 (go together with a friend), 부모님과 식사하다 (have a meal with one's parents). Vietnamese với (with; together): đi với bạn (go with a friend), cùng với nhau (together with each other).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 굥 differs from Korean 공. 共 reads 굥 (gyong/ㄍ⼄ㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 群母 (g) + 鍾韻 (ɨoŋ) gives Dan'a'yo a compound vowel -yong (ㄍ⼄ㄫ), preserving the -y- medial of 鍾韻 — while Sino-Korean gives 공 (gong), using the plain -ong vowel without the medial. Dan'a'yo systematically preserves the y-medial of 鍾韻 compounds where Sino-Korean drops it.
