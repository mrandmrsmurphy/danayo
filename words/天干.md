## Notes
- [天 (char)](../characters/天%20(char).md) "sky, heaven" + [干](../characters/干.md) "dry; stem" — the ten cyclic signs (甲乙丙丁戊己庚辛壬癸) associated with the five elements in their yin and yang aspects

The Ten Heavenly Stems (天干) are the upper half of the traditional sexagenary cycle. Each of the ten stems is associated with one of the five elements (木火土金水) in either a yang (甲丙戊庚壬) or yin (乙丁己辛癸) aspect. Paired with the Twelve Earthly Branches ([[地支]), they produce the sixty-year cycle used across East Asia for calendrical reckoning, historical dating, and the naming of years. 

The stem 干 (gān) in this usage is distinct from its common meaning "dry" — here it evokes the trunk or backbone of a system, the primary ordering principle from which secondary elements branch. Compare 地支 (earthly branches), where 支 similarly means "branch, offshoot."

The stems appear throughout the Astronomy lexipedia's 六十干支 table pairing them with the animals of the zodiac.
