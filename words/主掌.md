## Notes
- [主](../characters/主.md) "lord, master" + [掌](../characters/掌.md) "palm; to hold, to administer" — literally "to hold as master," i.e. to be primarily in charge of, to administer, to oversee.

主掌 (zhǔzhǎng) pairs 主 — the "master, lord" of [[主日]], [[主教]], and [[主義]] — with 掌, whose core sense of "palm of the hand" extends metaphorically to "to hold, to wield, to administer" (cf. [[掌管]], [[分掌]]). Together the compound names the holder of primary administrative responsibility for some office or duty, functioning as both a verb ("to be in charge of") and, by extension, a noun ("the person in charge").

The word appears in **《周禮·地官司徒·保氏》**, which opens its description of the 保氏 official: "保氏主掌諫王之惡" — "the Protector is primarily in charge of remonstrating against the faults of the king." Here 主掌 establishes 保氏's foremost duty, which is then specified by [[諌議]].

Note the homophone collision in Korean: 주장 is also the standard Sino-Korean reading of 主張 ("assertion, claim, advocacy") — an entirely different compound built on 主 + 張 ("to stretch, to assert") rather than 主 + 掌 ("to administer"). The two compounds are unrelated in meaning but identical in Sino-Korean pronunciation, a useful trap for readers moving between administrative ("in charge of") and rhetorical ("to claim") registers. The Cantonese, Japanese, and Vietnamese readings (zyu2 zoeng2, しゅしょう, chủ chưởng) do not share this collision, since 掌 and 張 diverge in those traditions.
