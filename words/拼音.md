## Notes
- [拼](../characters/拼.md) "to spell out; to combine phonetically" + [音](../characters/音.md) "sound; pronunciation; tone"

拼音 is Dan'a'yo's word for phonetic spelling and, more specifically, the proper name for Mandarin Pinyin — the romanization system developed and officially adopted by the People's Republic of China in 1958 as the standard phonetic transcription of Mandarin Chinese. The compound is borrowed directly from the Mandarin 拼音 (pīnyīn): 拼 (to spell out syllable by syllable, to combine) + 音 (sound, phoneme, reading) = "to spell out sounds." In Dan'a'yo, the word can refer both to the Mandarin Pinyin system specifically (a proper noun) and to phonetic orthography more generally.

Cross-linguistic transmission of the compound is unusually consistent: Korean 병음 (byeong-eum) is a Sino-Korean phonetic approximation used when discussing Pinyin as a named system; Japanese ぴんいん (pin'in) is a katakana borrowing of the Mandarin pronunciation, used for the same purpose; Vietnamese bính âm is the Sino-Vietnamese reading of 拼音, preserving the same two characters. All five language forms ultimately trace back to the same compound, making 拼音 one of the most pan-CJKV technical vocabulary items in the phonology domain.

In Dan'a'yo, 拼音 sits alongside [[注音]] (ㄐㄨ·ㄨㄇ, *Zhuyin* / Bopomofo) as the two major named phonetic systems in the CJKV sphere. Dan'a'yo uses 注音 as its own phonetic notation system — so in the context of this vault, 拼音 refers to Mandarin Pinyin as a named external romanization system, while 注音 is the notation used for Dan'a'yo readings themselves. Entries show 注音 (Dan'a'yo Bopomofo) and 羅馬字 (Dan'a'yo romanization); 拼音 values in character and word frontmatter record the Mandarin Pinyin pronunciation of the underlying Chinese characters.
