## Notes
- [堅](../characters/堅.md) as 代用字 for 鰹 "skipjack tuna" + [魚 (char)](../characters/魚%20(char).md) "fish"
- 堅 carries 鰹 (and its simplified form 鲣) as aliases, sanctioning this substitution. The aliases 鰹魚 and 鲣魚 preserve the standard and simplified Chinese spellings.

The skipjack tuna (*Katsuwonus pelamis*) is one of the most commercially significant fish in the world and the anchor of Japanese dashi stock. The character 鰹 is itself a phono-semantic compound — fish radical 魚 plus phonetic 堅 — so the Dan'a'yo substitution neatly reverses the construction back to the phonetic element alone.

In East Asian culinary tradition the fish is best known as the source of 鰹節 (*katsuobushi*), the dried, fermented, and smoked tuna flakes that form the backbone of Japanese dashi. The name 鰹/堅魚 ("firm fish") reflects the rock-hard texture of the fully dried block; the word *katsuo* in Japanese is itself derived from *katauo* ("hard fish"). In Chinese the compound 鰹魚 (*jiānyú*) is used as the standard name for the species.

In Japanese the compound is rarely written as 鰹魚; the fish is known simply as かつお (鰹). The Dan'a'yo reading けんぎょ (on-yomi KEN + GYO) applies to the compound as written.
