## Notes
**Form.** 韮金 is [韮](characters/韮.md) “leek; chive” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following the rule for metals.

**Etymology.** _Praseodymium_ comes from Greek πράσιος (_prasios_) “leek-green” + δίδυμος (_didymos_) “twin.” The name refers to the green coloration of its salts and its historical pairing with neodymium (the “twin” element).

**Motivation.** 韮 directly encodes the **leek** component of _prasios_. This is more precise than a generic color term (緑, 青), preserving the original imagery rather than collapsing it into “green.”

We intentionally **discard the “twin” component** (δίδυμος) because:

- it is shared with neodymium and not distinctive on its own
- it does not map cleanly to a single high-frequency graph
- the color term is the more salient differentiator

**Type of formation.**  
A **partial semantic calque**:

- retains the specific vegetal/color image (leek)
- omits the relational suffix (“twin”) for economy and distinctiveness

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, not phonetic.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a metal (lanthanide).
3. Provides a unique, concrete symbol (韮).
4. Maintains internal consistency with other plant/color-derived names (穀金, 薔薇金, 霓金).

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 镨 (phonetic)
- Japanese: プラセオジム (phonetic)
- Korean: 프라세오디뮴 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: praseodi (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 韮金 restores the original semantic image.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 韮 is specific and somewhat culinary/agricultural, but this matches the Greek precisely.
- The absence of the “twin” component may obscure the historical pairing, but that relation is already implicit in the periodic table.

**Conclusion.**  
韮金 is a precise and efficient semantic rendering of _praseodymium_, preserving the distinctive “leek-green” imagery while simplifying the compound to fit your system.
