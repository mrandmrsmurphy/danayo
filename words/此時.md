## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

此時 is the proximal temporal correlative — "now; at this time; the present moment." Where 此処 is the proximal place (here) and 此物 is the proximal object (this thing), 此時 is the proximal time: the moment of speaking, the current juncture in a narrative, or the contextually relevant present. The character 時 (Dan'a'yo *si*, ㄙㄧ) covers a range from instantaneous moment to extended season or era — the same breadth as English "time" — and combined with 此 it designates whatever time is most immediately in focus.

Cross-linguistic equivalents are well established: Mandarin 此時 (cǐshí) appears in formal and classical prose as "at this time, at this juncture" (此時此刻 "at this very moment"); colloquial Mandarin uses 現在 (xiànzài). Japanese 今 (ima, "now") is the ordinary everyday form; この時 (kono toki, "at this time") is the adverbial phrase equivalent when the time needs to be explicitly marked. Korean 지금 (jigeum, "now") is the standard word. Vietnamese bây giờ (now) or lúc này (at this time).

In narrative prose, 此時 can shift with the narrative present rather than the speech present — like the English "now" in past-tense storytelling ("Now the hero entered the room"), 此時 refers to the narrative moment, not necessarily the speaker's actual now. This deictic flexibility makes 此時 more versatile than a simple "current time" reading suggests. The 処/時 parallelism in the correlative table is productive: 此処 (here) and 此時 (now) together locate an event in the speaker's immediate spatio-temporal vicinity.
