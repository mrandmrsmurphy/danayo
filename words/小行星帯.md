## Notes
- [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small" + [行 (char)](../characters/行%20(char).md) "travel, go" + [星 (char)](../characters/星%20(char).md) "star" + [帯 (char)](../characters/帯%20(char).md) "belt, zone" — the region between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter containing most of the solar system's asteroids

The asteroid belt (小行星帯) encircles the sun between Mars and Jupiter, containing millions of rocky bodies — the 小行星 (小行星, asteroids) — that never coalesced into a planet. The individual bodies are called [[小行星]]. The discovery of the dwarf planet [[䦧神星]] (Eris) beyond Neptune in 2005 prompted the reclassification of Pluto as a [[矮行星]], demonstrating that belt-like zones of debris exist at multiple distances in the solar system.
