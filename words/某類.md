## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [類](../characters/類.md) "kind; sort; category; type"

某類 is the indefinite kind/category correlative — "some kind; a certain type; an unspecified sort." Like all 某-column entries, the category identity is deliberately withheld: 某類の問題が発生した ("a problem of a certain kind arose"); 某類に分類されている ("it is classified under a certain category"). This covers both genuine epistemic uncertainty (the speaker doesn't know the category) and deliberate vagueness (the type is known but not disclosed).

Mandarin 某類 (mǒu lèi, "a certain kind/category") is one of the rarer cases where a Dan'a'yo compound matches an attested Mandarin form directly — 某 + 類 are both well-established classical Chinese morphemes and compose naturally. Japanese ある種 (aru shu, "a certain kind/type; some sort of") is the standard indefinite category word: ある種の問題 ("a problem of a certain kind"); ある種の人 ("a certain type of person"). Korean 어떤 종류 (eotteon jongryu, "a certain kind/type; some sort") is the standard form.

某類 completes the 某 column for the category domain, adding an indefinite kind-pronoun alongside 某人 (someone), 某事 (something), 某物 (some object), 某処 (somewhere), 某時 (sometime), 某様 (somehow), and 某多 (some amount). Together the 某 column provides Dan'a'yo with a complete paradigm of indefinite pronouns across every semantic dimension in the correlative table — a systematic coverage that no single CJKV language matches in full.
