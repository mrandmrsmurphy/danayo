## Notes
- [瑞](../characters/瑞.md) "auspicious; lucky token" + [兆](../characters/兆.md) "omen; sign; portent" — stand-in for [[瑞]], which cannot appear independently
- 瑞兆 names the good kind of sign — not the ominous shiver of 前兆 (an omen that something is *about* to happen, often unwelcome) but a portent specifically marked as fortunate, auspicious, blessed. 瑞 by itself carries an old aristocratic shimmer: it named the jade tallies and tokens (圭瑞) that rulers exchanged as proof of legitimacy and good faith, and by extension came to mean anything auspicious, sacred, or favorably marked by heaven — a snowfall in the right season, a rare animal's appearance, a comet read as blessing rather than warning. United with 兆, "sign, omen," it becomes the literary, slightly formal word for a lucky portent: Mandarin ruìzhào and Cantonese seoi6 siu6, Sino-Japanese ずいちょう (zuichō), Sino-Korean 서조 (seojo, glossed plainly in Korean dictionaries as 상서로운 조짐, "an auspicious sign," and near-synonymous with the more common 길조), and Sino-Vietnamese thụy triệu all keep this same hopeful charge — a word reserved for the omens worth celebrating rather than fearing.
