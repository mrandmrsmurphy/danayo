## Notes
- [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name" + [字 (char)](../characters/字%20(char).md) "glyph" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (名's is bare 名, 字's is bare 字). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule, since both are individually `kwin: false`.
- **Gloss clarified**: `english` previously read "name characters," a garbled gloss — corrected to "name (personal name)," matching Mandarin's actual everyday meaning (你叫什么名字? "What's your name?"). Korean 명자 is a real, if less common than native 이름, Sino-Korean word for the same sense (confirmed on Korean Wiktionary).
- **Genuine cross-linguistic sense-narrowing, not an error**: Japanese 名字/苗字 (みょうじ) specifically means "surname; family name," distinct from 名前 ("given/full name") — narrower than Mandarin's general "name" (verified via search). Left `vietnamese` blank rather than force an unconfirmed compositional "danh tự," which lacked a clear dictionary attestation as a genuine standalone word, unlike e.g. [[友好]]'s hữu hảo.
