## Notes
- [要](../characters/要.md) "essential, want" + [約](../characters/約.md) "bind, condense" — to bind up the essentials of something into a shorter form: a summary.

Mandarin instead reaches for 摘要 or 概要 for "summary," so this word's pedigree runs through Japanese and Korean rather than the Sinitic branch — Korean's 요약 is extremely common and productive (요약하다, "to summarize"; 요약문, "summary text"), which is sufficient on its own to clear the "more than Japanese" bar. 約 also appears in [[制約]] and [[綽約]] (both already attested), each putting a different shade on the same "binding, condensing" root. Attested across Japanese and Korean.
