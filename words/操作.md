## Notes
- [操](../characters/操.md) "operate; manipulate; control" + [作](../characters/作%20(char).md) "do; make; act" — stand-in for [[操]], which cannot appear independently
- 操作 is the everyday Sinospheric word for the hands-on business of running something — working the controls of a machine, carrying out a procedure, executing a series of steps to make a system do what you want. It is a thoroughly modern, technical-register word: Mandarin cāozuò, Cantonese cou1 zok3, Sino-Japanese そうさ (sōsa), Sino-Korean 조작 (jojak), and Sino-Vietnamese thao tác all gravitate toward instruction manuals, control panels, and software interfaces — 操作法 "operating instructions," 操作系統/操作 시스템 "operating system." Korean speech holds a small trap here: 조작 is a perfect homophone of 造作 "fabrication, contrivance" — the word for rigging an election, doctoring evidence, or faking a result — so that "조작" alone, heard without its hanja, can mean either the blameless act of operating a washing machine or a scandal serious enough to end a career, with only context (and, in writing, the character itself) to tell the listener which is meant.
