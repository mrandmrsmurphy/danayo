## Notes
- [鶏 (char)](../characters/鶏%20(char).md) "chicken; rooster" + [肉 (char)](../characters/肉%20(char).md) "meat"

The compound has two Japanese readings worth noting. The on-yomi けいにく (KEI + NIKU) is the formal, written form found in menus and food labelling. The everyday spoken form is とりにく — a hybrid reading where 鶏 takes its native reading とり ("bird; chicken") while 肉 keeps its on-yomi にく. とりにく is far more common in conversation; けいにく reads as slightly stiff or clinical.

In Korean, the Sino-Korean 계육 (鶏肉) exists but is rarely used in ordinary speech, where the native compound 닭고기 (dak-gogi, "chicken meat") is standard. The same pattern holds in Vietnamese, where thịt gà (native) overwhelmingly dominates over any Sino-Vietnamese form. Chinese 鸡肉/雞肉 is the universal standard across all registers.

The three aliases reflect the character's traditional forms: 雞肉 is the standard traditional Chinese spelling (雞 being the traditional form), 鷄肉 the kyūjitai Japanese and alternate traditional Chinese form, and 鸡肉 the simplified Chinese spelling.
