## Notes
- [台 (char)](../characters/台%20(char).md) "platform; pedestal; machines"

台 in Dan'a'yo carries the core meaning of 臺, the traditional form it simplifies: a raised surface serving as a foundation, stage, or display base. The simplified character 台 has absorbed two other traditionally distinct graphs — 颱 (typhoon, now 台風 in modern Chinese) and the archaic first-person pronoun 台 (yí, used in literary texts predating 我) — making it one of the more semantically overloaded simplifications in the sinographic system. The Dan'a'yo word focuses on the concrete architectural sense: a platform or pedestal.

The word's extensions diverge notably across the CJKV sphere. In Mandarin, 台 (tái) functions as a measure word for machines and large appliances — 一台电视 (yī tái diànshì, "one television set"), 一台电脑 (yī tái diànnǎo, "one computer") — a classifier extension that has no parallel in the other languages. In Japanese, 台 (DAI) functions as a counter for machines, vehicles, and geographical elevations; 台所 (daidokoro, "kitchen") preserves an older sense of "work platform." In Vietnamese, đài generalises to towers and broadcasting stations: đài truyền hình (television station) and đài phát thanh (radio station) are the most common modern collocations. Korean 대 (台) appears primarily in Sino-Korean scholarly or technical compounds.

The Dan'a'yo word 台 thus occupies the structural sense common to all branches — a platform, base, or elevated surface — leaving the machine-classifier and broadcasting-station extensions to be handled by context or more specific compounds within the language.
