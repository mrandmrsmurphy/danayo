## Notes
- [諌](../characters/諌.md) "to admonish, to remonstrate" + [議](../characters/議.md) "to discuss, to comment" — to remonstrate with and advise a superior, especially a ruler.

諌議 (jiànyì) pairs 諌 (aliased to the more common 諫/谏) — "to admonish, to speak frankly against wrongdoing" — with 議, "to discuss, to deliberate" (cf. [[議員]], [[議定]]). As a verb, it means to candidly point out a ruler's faults and urge correction; as a noun, it is best known as the shortened form of **諫議大夫** (jiàn yì dà fū), the title of an imperial remonstrance official found in Chinese bureaucracies from the Qin/Han dynasties onward, whose formal duty was to criticize the emperor's conduct and policy without fear of reprisal.

The verbal sense is the one used in **《周禮·地官司徒·保氏》**: "保氏主掌諫王之惡，而養育國子以道" — "the Protector is primarily in charge ([[主掌]]) of remonstrating against the faults of the king, and of nurturing the sons of the state in the Way." Although the 《周禮》 text itself reads 諫王之惡 (with 王 "king" as the object), the compound 諫議/諌議 generalizes this specific act of royal admonishment into a single term, later crystallizing into the official title.

The Sino-Japanese かんぎ, Sino-Korean 간의, and Sino-Vietnamese gián nghị readings are straightforward compositional renderings. Simplified 谏议 and traditional 諫議 are recorded as aliases alongside the Dan'a'yo form 諌議, which uses [[諌]] (Dan'a'yo's form of 諫).
