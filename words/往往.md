## Notes
- [往 (char)](../characters/往%20(char).md) "to go towards; to proceed" + [往 (char)](../characters/往%20(char).md) "to go towards; to proceed" — reduplication

往往 is "often; frequently; as is often the case" — a reduplicated compound: 往 (to go towards) doubled. Reduplication in classical Chinese adverbs typically shifts meaning from directional/momentary to habitual/recurrent: "going and going (again and again)" → "happening repeatedly" → "often." In classical Chinese texts, 往往 also carried the sense of "scattered here and there" (dispersed, not in one place), which has since narrowed to the temporal habitual sense in modern CJKV.

Mandarin 往往 (wǎngwǎng) is a common adverb meaning "often; usually; as a rule; as often happens." Cantonese 往往 (wong5 wong5) is formal/literary, appearing in written registers. Japanese 往々 (おうおう, ōō) is a formal/literary adverb — 往々にして (ōō ni shite, "often; it is frequently the case that"); more literary than the everyday よく (yoku, often) or しばしば (shibashiba, frequently). Korean 왕왕 (往往, wangwang) = "from time to time; occasionally; often" — a Sino-Korean adverb that remains in active use. Korean also uses 이따금 (ittaegeum, from time to time) and 가끔 (gakkeum, sometimes/occasionally) as near-equivalents in native vocabulary.

往往 is one of the relatively rare Dan'a'yo words where kwin is true: the Dan'a'yo Hangul transcription 왕왕 exactly matches the Korean word 왕왕 (往往). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean converge on 왕왕 through independent inheritance from Middle Chinese — the phonological shape of 往 (MC *wɨɐŋ or similar) has remained close enough in both systems to produce identical Hangul. This is not a borrowing of one from the other but a convergence of parallel inheritance.
