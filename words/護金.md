## Notes
### Form
護金 is [護](characters/護.md) “to protect; to guard; to safeguard” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following the rule for transition elements.

### Etymological chain
The international name _palladium_ comes from the asteroid _Pallas_, which in turn is named for the Greek figure Pallas Athena. The deeper root is **Παλλάδιον (_Palladion_)**, the sacred protective image of Athena believed to safeguard a city. Thus the semantic core is **protection, guarding, tutelary power**.

### Motivation
護 directly captures this underlying idea of protection. Rather than attempting a phonetic rendering of “Pallas” or “Palladium,” which would violate our principles and be semantically empty, 護金 reconstructs the **original mythological meaning** embedded in the name.

### Type of formation
A **semantic back-derivation** through the naming chain:
- Palladium (element)  
    → Palladion (protective image)  
    → “that which protects”  
    → 護

This is analogous to other cases where the immediate etymon is opaque but a deeper layer yields a usable meaning.

### Fit within the system
1. Meaning-based, avoiding phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a d-block element.
3. Provides a clear, high-frequency graph for symbol use.
4. Avoids collision with other semantic domains already assigned.

### Comparative CJKV forms

- Modern Chinese: 钯 (phonetic)
- Japanese: パラジウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 팔라듐 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: palađi (phonetic)

Modern usage is entirely phonetic; 護金 restores a meaning-driven interpretation.

### Clarity and potential issues
- 護 is semantically abstract and not tied to any physical property of the element.
- However, this reflects the reality of the original naming, which is mythological rather than descriptive.
- Within Dan'a'yo, uniqueness of assignment ensures clarity despite abstraction.

## Conclusion
護金 is a well-justified solution to a difficult etymology. By tracing back to the _Palladion_ rather than the opaque modern form, it preserves meaning and fits cleanly within the semantic naming framework.
