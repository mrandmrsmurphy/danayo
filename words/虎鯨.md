## Notes
- [虎 (char)](../characters/虎%20(char).md) "tiger" + [鯨](../characters/鯨.md) "whale" — the orca or killer whale (*Orcinus orca*), named for its ferocity. The standard Mandarin/Cantonese term, also the standard Traditional/Simplified Chinese Wiktionary headword (synonyms: 殺人鯨, 逆戟鯨). Not the native Japanese term — Japanese instead uses シャチ, written with the dedicated character 鯱 (which doubly denotes the mythical shachihoko roof ornament); 虎鯨 is recognizable in Japanese as the Chinese word, but isn't the conventional kanji form, so 日本語 is left blank rather than guessing a reading. Korean likewise uses a fully native compound, 범고래 (범 "tiger" + 고래 "whale," both native Korean, not Sino-Korean), so 한국어 is left blank too.
