## Notes
- [提](../characters/提.md) "to raise, present" + [出 (char)](../characters/出%20(char).md) "to exit, go out"

提出 means "to submit, to bring forward, to put forward" — submitting a document, an application, a proposal, or a resignation to an authority. 提 contributes its core sense of "to raise up, to bring to someone's attention" (as in [[提供]], the previous word in this batch, and [[提案]] "proposal," cross-linked from [[提]]'s Words section); 出, a 会意 character combining 止 ("foot") with 凵 ("cave, pit") — the image of a foot stepping out of a cave — contributes the directional sense of "out, forth," seen throughout [[出 (char)]]'s large Words family ([[出版]] "to publish," [[出席]] "to attend," [[抽出]] "to extract").

提出 literally "raises [something] out [into view]" — the bureaucratic and academic act of formally producing a document so that it leaves the submitter's hands and enters official consideration. Its Vietnamese form đề xuất (also meaning "to propose, to suggest" in everyday speech) shows the same compound surviving in a slightly broadened sense outside the strictly bureaucratic "submit a form" usage dominant in Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
