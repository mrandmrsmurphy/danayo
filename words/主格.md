## Notes
- [主](../characters/主.md) "lord; master; principal" + [格 (char)](../characters/格%20(char).md) "case; status"

主格 is the nominative case in Dan'a'yo — the case marking the grammatical subject of a predicate. In case-grammar terms, the nominative is the "naming" or "governing" case: the entity performing the action (in active constructions) or being described (in stative and copular constructions). The term takes its name from 主 — "lord; master; principal" — framing the nominative subject as the governing or dominant argument in the clause, the one from which the predicate takes its inflection and around which the sentence is organized. 主格 is one of the most cross-linguistically stable grammatical terms in the CJKV tradition: Japanese しゅかく (shukaku), Korean 주격 (主格), and Mandarin 主格 (zhǔgé) all use the same characters with nearly identical meanings. Vietnamese chủ cách is the direct Sino-Vietnamese reflex: chủ (主, principal) + cách (格, case).

主 (ㄐㄨ/ju/주 — Mandarin zhǔ, Cantonese zyu2, Korean 주, Japanese SHU/しゅ, Vietnamese chủ/chúa): "lord; master; host; principal; main" — MC 章母 (t͡ɕ) + 虞韻 (ɨo); a 象形 character, originally depicting a flame or lamp with a central wick — the central, governing point — giving the extended senses of "lord; master; the principal one." In grammatical metalanguage 主 designates the primary or governing element: 主格 (nominative — the governing case), 主語 (subject — the governing sentence element), 主題 (topic — the governing discourse element). 格 (ㄍㄚㄎ/gag/각 — Mandarin gé, Cantonese gaak3, Korean 격, Japanese KAKU/かく, Vietnamese cách): "case; status; standard; grid" — MC 見母 (k) + 陌二韻 (ɑk, 2nd division); see [[与格]] for full analysis. 格 is the fixed suffix for all case names in Dan'a'yo.
