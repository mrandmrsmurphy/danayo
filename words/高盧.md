## Notes
- [高 (char)](../characters/高%20(char).md) "high" + [盧](../characters/盧.md) "hut; cottage" — phonetic transcription of Latin *Gallia* / Celtic *Gaul*; 盧 is a 名専字 used primarily in transliterations and proper names

**高盧** (*Gāolú*) is the classical Sinographic transcription of *Gallia* — the territory occupied by Celtic-speaking Gauls in antiquity, roughly corresponding to modern France, Belgium, Luxembourg, most of Switzerland, and portions of the Netherlands, Germany west of the Rhine, and northern Italy. The name is phonetic: Gāolú approximates the sound of "Gaul" or "Gal-" in Latin *Gallia*, with no semantic content intended in the component characters.

The region entered recorded history most prominently through Julius Caesar's *Gallic Wars* (58–50 BC), in which Rome conquered all of *Gallia Transalpina* (高盧 in the trans-Alpine sense). Caesar famously opened the *Commentarii* with the observation that Gaul was divided into three parts — a tripartite structure of Belgae, Aquitani, and Celts proper that shaped both Roman administrative practice and later European geography.

The modern successor state occupying most of the territory is [[法国]] (France). The Japanese and Korean readings (こうろ, 고로) are Sino-readings of the characters; modern Japanese and Korean usage generally prefers the phonetic forms ガリア and 갈리아 respectively.
