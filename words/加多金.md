## Notes
**Form.** 加多金 is [[加多]] + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” with a two-character base, consistent with your allowance for non-binary formations when needed.

**Etymological situation.** _Gadolinium_ derives from the mineral **gadolinite**, named after the Finnish chemist **Johan Gadolin**. This is an eponym with no recoverable semantic content in the source language.

**Motivation.** You construct a **hybrid formation**:

- **Sound:** 加多 approximates _gado-_ (Ga-do), preserving recognizability.
- **Meaning:** [加](characters/加.md) “to add” + [多 (char)](characters/多%20(char).md) “many” suggests **increase, abundance, multiplicity**.

So the name carries both:

1. a phonetic echo of the original
2. a meaningful internal reading (“adding more; increasing quantity”)

**Type of formation.**  
A **phonosemantic compound**:

- partial transliteration (加多 ≈ gado)
- paired with a meaningful internal interpretation
- - 金 for classification

This mirrors traditional 形聲 strategies, adapted to your constraints.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Respects the no-pure-phonetic rule while still retaining sound linkage.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a lanthanide.
3. Provides a two-character base where a single graph is not viable.
4. Maintains pronounceability and memorability.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 钆 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ガドリニウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 가돌리늄 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: gadolini (phonetic)

Modern forms are purely phonetic; 加多金 introduces semantic structure.

**Clarity and issues.**

- The meaning “add many” is not chemically relevant, but it is not misleading.
- The phonetic approximation is approximate but recognizable.
- As with other hybrids, transparency depends on familiarity with the system.

**Conclusion.**  
加多金 is a well-executed phonosemantic solution to an otherwise opaque eponym. It balances sound retention with internal meaning, fitting your system while remaining interpretable.
