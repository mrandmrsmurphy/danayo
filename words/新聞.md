## Notes
- [新 (char)](../characters/新%20(char).md) "new" + [聞 (char)](../characters/聞%20(char).md) "hear" — literally "newly heard [things]." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters). `kwin: true` per the AND-rule: both constituent characters are individually `kwin: true`.
- **A genuine and well-known cross-linguistic sense divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: in Mandarin and Cantonese, 新闻/xīnwén refers to "news" — reports of current events, the informational content (not the physical object) — with "newspaper" instead expressed by 报纸/bou3 zi2. Japanese しんぶん and Korean 신문 (verified via search), however, specifically mean "newspaper," the physical periodical publication — Japanese instead uses the loanword ニュース (nyūsu) for "news" in the informational sense. A learner should not assume 新聞 means "newspaper" in Mandarin, nor "news" in Japanese/Korean.
