## Notes
- [鴻](../characters/鴻.md) "swan goose; grand, vast" + [鵠](../characters/鵠.md) "swan; target"
- This word is the stand-in for [[鵠]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A classical Chinese compound for "swan" — the great migratory waterfowl — and by extension a symbol of lofty ambition. The Shiji (史記) records the saying: 燕雀安知鴻鵠之志哉 "How can a swallow or sparrow know the aspirations of a swan?" — 鴻鵠之志 (hónghú zhī zhì) became a fixed phrase for the grand designs of an exceptional person. Note that 鴻 (ㄏㄛㄫ) is a 名専字 in Dan'a'yo; 鵠 (ㄏㄛㄎ) is the bounded character for which this word serves as a stand-in.
