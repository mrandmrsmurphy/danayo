## Notes
- [臨](../characters/臨.md) "draw near; approach" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; when"

**臨時** ("facing the time") describes something that exists or is done for the immediate occasion only — not permanent, not planned in advance. The literal sense of the compound is apt: 臨 "to face; to be present at" + 時 "the time" — something arranged for right now, to meet this moment. It applies to provisional governments, temporary staff, emergency measures, and stopgap arrangements of all kinds.

The word is widely used across the CJKV sphere with consistent meaning: Mandarin 临时, Japanese 臨時 (りんじ), Korean 임시, and Vietnamese lâm thời all carry the same sense of the provisional and the ad hoc. Note that in Korean the initial 림 shifts to 임 by the initial-sound rule (두음법칙).
