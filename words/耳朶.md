## Notes
- [耳 (char)](../characters/耳%20(char).md) "ear" + [朶](../characters/朶.md) "flower blossom"

朶 originally depicted a tree branch with drooping, pendulous flower clusters — something soft and rounded hanging from a central structure. Applied to the ear, 耳朶 thus parses as "the blossoming, pendant part of the ear," a poetic coinage that captures the lobe's shape and softness. 

The anatomical term 耳垂 (ěrchuí, "drooping ear") names the same structure from a physiological angle; 耳朶 names it from an aesthetic one. In modern spoken Mandarin, 耳朵 (the simplified-character variant) is the ordinary colloquial word for the ear as a whole organ, not just the lobe. The literary or traditional form 耳朶 retains more of the original figurative precision. Japanese maintains the same distinction: じだ (耳朶) refers to the ear broadly, while みみたぶ (耳たぶ) names the lobe specifically. Korean 귓불 is a native Korean word for the earlobe, not the Sino-Korean reading of 耳朶 (which would be 이타).
