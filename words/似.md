## Notes
- [似 (char)](../characters/似%20(char).md) "like; as; resembling"

似 is the similative case particle — 類似格, marking likeness or analogy: "like; as; resembling." It attaches to the standard of comparison to form similes: 行似風 (move like the wind), 白似雪 (white like snow), 静似水 (still as water). In the comparison chart, 似 parallels Mandarin 像/如 (xiàng/rú, like; as; resemble), Cantonese 似 (ci5, the same character — colloquially "to look like; to resemble"), Japanese ように/みたいに (yō ni/mitai ni, like; as if), Korean 처럼 (cheoreom, like; as; resembling), Vietnamese như (like; as; as if). Unlike 比 (comparative, establishing relative degree), 似 establishes qualitative likeness — not "more than" but "in the manner of." 似 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[似]].

Mandarin 像/如 (xiàng/rú): 像山一樣高 (as tall as a mountain), 如夢似幻 (like a dream; dreamlike — a fixed phrase using both 如 and 似), 相似 (xiāngsì, similar; resembling). Cantonese 似 (ci5) is colloquially active: 你似你爸爸 (you look like your father), 似乎 (it seems; apparently — a widely used expression). This makes 似 one of the rare characters that is its own Cantonese colloquial word. Japanese ように (yō ni) derived from 様 (manner) + に: 海のように広い (as vast as the ocean), 夢のように美しい (beautiful as a dream); みたいに (mitai ni, informal): 彼みたいに上手 (as good as him). Korean 처럼 (cheoreom): 물처럼 흐르다 (flow like water), 새처럼 날다 (fly like a bird), 꿈처럼 아름답다 (beautiful as a dream). Vietnamese như (like; as): như nước (like water), như mơ (like a dream), như thường (as usual).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 사 exactly matches Korean 사. 似 reads 사 (sa/ㄙㄚ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 邪母 (z) + 之韻 (ɨ) gives a sibilant onset with an -a vowel in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, producing identical 사 forms. The MC voiced sibilant 邪母 (z) and the 之韻 vowel yield the same result in both systems — one of the cleaner kwin: true cases.
