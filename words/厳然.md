## Notes
- [厳 (char)](../characters/厳%20(char).md) "strict; stern" + [然 (char)](../characters/然%20(char).md) "so; thus"
- 厳 carries 儼 as one of its aliases; the alias 儼然 preserves the traditional Chinese and Korean spelling, and 俨然 is its simplified Chinese form.

The compound follows the productive Sinographic pattern of pairing a quality character with 然 ("in the manner of; having the character of") to form an adjectival or adverbial modifier. Compare [[毅然]] ("resolutely"), [[瞭然]] ("clearly"), [[厳粛]] ("solemnly"): 然 is the operator that converts the root quality into a stance or bearing one can adopt.

儼 (Dan'a'yo: 厳) originally depicted a figure of commanding, upright presence — someone whose bearing alone signals authority or gravity. The compound 儼然/厳然 thus describes the quality of presenting that impression: an assembly conducted with solemn formality, a person whose demeanour carries unmistakable dignity, or a state of affairs that is clearly and undeniably what it appears to be.

In Korean 엄연 (嚴然/儼然) is widely used in the fixed phrase 엄연한 사실 ("an undeniable fact") — the solemn gravity of the compound lending weight to factual assertion. In Japanese げんぜん carries the same sense of imposing, unmistakable solemnity and is found in formal writing and literary prose.
