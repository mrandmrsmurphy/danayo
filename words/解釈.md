## Notes
- [解 (char)](../characters/解%20(char).md) "untie, explain" + [釈](../characters/釈.md) "explain, release"

Both halves of 解釈 independently mean something close to "to loosen, explain, resolve" — 解 is a 会意 compound of 角 (horn), 刀 (knife), and 牛 (ox), depicting a knife separating a horn from an ox: to take apart, to disassemble, and by extension to explain by breaking something down into its parts. 釈 (a simplified form of 釋, with phonetic 睪) carries the closely related sense of "to release, set free, let go" — as in 釈放 (release from custody) — extended to "to clear up, resolve" an ambiguity.

Together, 解釈 is the standard word for "interpretation" in the broadest sense: legal interpretation of a statute, literary or scriptural exegesis, or simply explaining what something means. It pairs with [[釈]]'s other cross-linked compound [[希釈]] ("dilution") — a reminder that 釈's core sense of "loosening, releasing, thinning out" extends both to physical mixtures and to the unpacking of meaning.

釋, the traditional unsimplified form of 釈, is also the character used to transliterate Śākya in 釈迦 (Shakyamuni, the historical Buddha) — a reminder that 釈/釋's "release" sense underlies Buddhist liberation (解脱, moksha) as well as the everyday sense of explanation used here.
