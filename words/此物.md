## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; object; material result"

此物 is the proximal correlative in the object column: it picks out a specific thing near the speaker. The character 物 (Dan'a'yo *mud*, ㄇㄨㄊ) has a broad semantic range covering physical objects, materials, and their results — the grapheme combines 牛 (ox/livestock, semantic) with 勿 (phonetic, OC \*mɯd), originally denoting the mottled colouring of cattle, generalised to "thing" or "substance." Together, 此物 means "this thing right here" — the object immediately in the speaker's spatial or attentional vicinity.

The nearest cross-linguistic equivalents are: Mandarin 此物 (cǐwù), surviving in formal and classical registers meaning "this object" (此物何物？ "What is this thing?"); colloquial Mandarin uses 这个 (zhège, lit. "this [classifier]"), which is listed as an alias. Japanese これ (kore) is the standard proximal object pronoun; Korean 이것 (igeot) is its direct Korean counterpart. Vietnamese cái này or điều này covers the same ground, with cái marking concrete objects and điều abstract matters — Dan'a'yo 物 spans both.

The alias 這個 reflects the most common way "this thing" is expressed in modern Mandarin: 這 (proximal demonstrative) + 個 (general-purpose object classifier). Dan'a'yo replaces this analytic demonstrative + classifier construction with the compact classical compound 此物, prioritising cross-CJKV recognisability and morphological regularity over colloquial naturalness in any individual language.
