## Notes
- [圓 (char)](../characters/圓%20(char).md) "circle, round" + [形 (char)](../characters/形%20(char).md) "shape, form, type" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

**Real content bug corrected**: `english` included "wound," which has no plausible connection to 圓形 ("circular shape") — removed, replaced with "round shape."

Mandarin yuánxíng, Cantonese jyun4 jing4, Japanese えんけい (enkei), and Korean 원형 (wonhyeong) are all the standard, everyday geometric/descriptive term for "circular shape" (円形/圆形 — the vault's own alias 円形 is the Japanese shinjitai form of 圓, matching the character's own aliasing). Vietnamese viên hình is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search), though more formal/technical in register — the everyday native phrase is hình tròn.
