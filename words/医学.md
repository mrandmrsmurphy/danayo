## Notes
- [医](../characters/医.md) "doctor, medicine" + [学](../characters/学.md) "study, learning" — medicine as an academic and scientific discipline.

**医学** (*yīxué*) denotes medicine in its institutional and academic sense: the systematic study of the human body, disease, diagnosis, and treatment, as taught in medical schools (医学校/医科大学) and organized into specialties (内科学, 外科学, 産婦人科学, etc.). It is distinguished from 医療 (*iryō*, "medical care, treatment as practiced") and from traditional practice-traditions such as [[漢方]] — though in modern usage 漢方医学 ("kampo medicine [as a field of study]") shows the two terms can combine.

Across the Sinosphere the term and its reading are remarkably stable: Mandarin 医学/醫學 (*yīxué*), Cantonese *ji1 hok6*, Japanese 医学 (*igaku*), Korean 의학 (*uihak*), and Vietnamese *y học* all derive transparently from the same two morphemes and carry essentially the same modern, science-of-medicine sense — a reflection of the 19th–20th century wave of shared neologisms (新名詞) coined to translate Western academic disciplines into Literary Sinitic compounds and then borrowed across the region.

This word appears in [[七情]] (Seven Emotions) as part of the label "漢方医学" for the third of three traditional enumerations of the seven basic human emotions, alongside the Confucian and Buddhist schemes.
