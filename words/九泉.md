## Notes
- [九 (char)](../characters/九%20(char).md) "nine" + [泉](../characters/泉.md) "fountain; wellspring; spring"

A classical Chinese literary expression for the underworld — the realm of the dead. The "Nine Springs" (九泉) are imagined as deep subterranean waters where the souls of the deceased are believed to dwell eternally. In Chinese cosmology, nine (九) represents the ultimate extreme or greatest depth, and 泉 (spring, wellspring) evokes inexhaustible waters far beneath the earth. Together they describe the deepest imaginable place below the ground.

The term appears throughout classical Chinese poetry as a dignified and poetic way to speak of death and the afterlife, forming a cosmic pair with [[九天]] (the Ninth Heaven, the highest of heavens): the dead descend to 九泉 as the living aspire toward 九天 above. To say one will "meet again in 九泉" is to say one will meet again in death.
