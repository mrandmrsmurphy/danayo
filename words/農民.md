## Notes
- [農](../characters/農.md) "farming" + [民](../characters/民.md) "citizen" — a person engaged in farming.

Japanese carries a real register split absent from the others: 農民 (nōmin) survives mostly as historical/class terminology — the "farmer" class in the old 士農工商 (samurai-farmer-artisan-merchant) social order — and reads as somewhat dated or politically loaded in casual modern speech. The everyday word for an actual working farmer or farming household today is 農家 (nōka), not 農民. Mandarin/Cantonese 農民/nung4 man4, Korean 농민, and Vietnamese nông dân all stay in ordinary, unmarked use as the standard word for "farmer," with no equivalent split.
