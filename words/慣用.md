## Notes
- [慣](../characters/慣.md) "habitual, accustomed" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use" — usage that has become fixed by habit or convention, rather than by formal rule.

慣用 describes a word, spelling, or procedure that is "correct" only because everyone already uses it that way — a 慣用読み is a character reading that deviates from the dictionary-standard one but is accepted because it has become the de facto norm, and 慣用句 (created alongside this entry) names the idiomatic phrases that result from the same process applied to whole expressions. Readers should be aware that Korean 관용 is a true homograph: the very same hangul also spells 寬容 ("tolerance, leniency" — compare the already-present [[寬大]], a near-synonym of that other sense), a word built on entirely different characters and with no semantic relation to this one. Mandarin and Cantonese keep the two senses apart in writing (惯用 vs. 宽容) even though Korean does not, which is why context is essential when encountering bare 관용 in a Korean text. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
