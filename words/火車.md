## Notes
- [火 (char)](../characters/火%20(char).md) "fire" + [車](../characters/車%20(char).md) "cart, car" — literally "fire cart." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 火車 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/火 (char).md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/車 (char).md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **This word is dedicated to the Korean historical-weapon sense, not Mandarin's everyday "train" — a deliberate Dan'a'yo choice, following the same pattern as [[汽水]]/[[汽車]]**: this compound's designated meaning here is 화차 (hwacha), the famous Joseon-dynasty multiple rocket-launcher siege weapon (whose name literally means "fire cart"); modern Korean 화차 can also refer to a railway freight car. Japanese 火車 (kasha), read here, does not mean this at all — in Japanese folklore, kasha is a corpse-stealing yōkai (妖怪) that carries away the bodies of the wickedly deceased, an image rooted in Buddhist hell iconography of a flaming cart bearing sinners to the underworld; documented as an interesting cultural aside, not this word's designated Dan'a'yo sense. Mandarin/Cantonese 火车/fo2 ce1, by sharp contrast, is the everyday, extremely common word for "train" — deliberately routed instead to [[列車]] here, to avoid collision with the Korean/Japanese senses.
- Note the resulting tension in `vietnamese: hỏa xa` — historically/formally, hỏa xa also means "train" (đường hỏa xa, "railway"), tracking with the Mandarin sense rather than this word's Korean-weapon/Japanese-yōkai designation. Left as-is (a real, attested word for the character combination) rather than removed, but flagged as not fully aligned with this word's chosen sense.
