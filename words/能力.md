## Notes
- [能](../characters/能.md) "can, be able to" + [力 (char)](../characters/力%20(char).md) "power, strength" — the power to do something: ability, capability.

The grammar book pairs 能力 with [[許可]] under a single heading (能力・許可) describing the verbal prefix 可–, which marks both ability/capacity (能力・可能) and, in its preposition form 可以, permission — two senses that many languages bundle under a single modal element, as English "can" itself does for both "I can swim" and "you can go." 能 and 力 are both highly productive roots independently (能動, 力学, neither yet created, but both transparent). The Vietnamese năng lực is itself extremely common, used everywhere from job postings to constitutional law. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
