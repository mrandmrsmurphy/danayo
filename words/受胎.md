## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "to receive" + [胎](../characters/胎.md) "fetus, womb"

受胎 means "conception" — the moment a womb (胎) receives (受) new life. 胎, a character built on the phonetic 台 (also seen in [[胎児]] "fetus" and [[胎盤]] "placenta," both cross-linked from [[胎]]'s own Notes), denotes the fetus or the womb that carries it, and anchors the basic vocabulary of pregnancy and prenatal development across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.

受胎 is the fourth of five 受-compounds in this batch, and like [[受難]] illustrates how 受's neutral "to receive, to undergo" takes on highly specific meaning depending on its object: 受賞 receives an honor, 受難 receives suffering, 受理 receives a document, and 受胎 receives new life. In Christian usage (again paralleling [[受難]]'s Passion sense), 受胎告知 ("the Annunciation," literally "the announcement of conception") is the standard East Asian term for the angel Gabriel's announcement to Mary — a striking case of two words from this single batch, 受難 and 受胎, both having been pressed into service for central events of the Christian narrative during the era of Bible translation into Chinese, Japanese, and Korean.
