## Notes
- [呼 (char)](../characters/呼%20(char).md) "call; shout; exhale" + [吸 (char)](../characters/吸%20(char).md) "suck; inhale"

呼吸 pairs the two halves of a breath cycle — 呼 (exhale) and 吸 (inhale) — into the single verb "to breathe" (compare the reversed-order convention already seen this sweep on [[介紹]]/紹介 and [[草花]]/花草, though here the order is fixed and iconic: breathing out named before breathing in). **`pos` corrected `性詞`→`事詞`**: matches `characters/呼 (char).md`'s own stored `pos: 事詞` for the same verb-like ("event") category, rather than the quality/adjective-like `性詞` a word for "breathe" doesn't fit.

Mandarin hūxī, Cantonese fu1 kap1, Japanese こきゅう (kokyū), Korean 호흡 (hoheup), and Vietnamese hô hấp are all the standard, everyday word for "to breathe / respiration" across the sphere — unlike several recent iterations this sweep, no native alternative displaces the Sino-Xenic compound here in any of the five languages; this is simply the ordinary word. No `stand_in` relationship applies — both `characters/呼 (char).md` and `characters/吸 (char).md` already have their own bare-character `stand_in` (each is its own legitimizer), so 呼吸 is an independent compound, not a legitimizer for either.
