## Notes
- [華](../characters/華.md) "gorgeous, splendid" + [美 (char)](../characters/美%20(char).md) "beautiful" — radiant splendor, beauty that shines. This word is the viable form of [華](../characters/華.md): 華 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 華美 is its `stand_in` compound, keeping 華's sense the "radiant, resplendent" one — matching its own `korean_native` gloss 빛날 ("to shine, be radiant") — in contrast to [[秀麗]] (the "lovely, graceful" sense that [[麗]] stands in for instead), splitting apart the two flavors the collapsed compound [[華麗]] used to blur together.

Worth flagging: Japanese かび (kabi) is an exact homophone of カビ (kabi, "mold, mildew"), an extremely common everyday word — context always disambiguates, but it's a striking coincidence for a word meaning "gorgeous." Mandarin huáměi and Cantonese waa4mei5 both describe something showy and splendid — clothing, decoration, prose — leaning toward ornateness rather than plain loveliness, distinguishing it from 秀麗's more restrained, graceful register.
