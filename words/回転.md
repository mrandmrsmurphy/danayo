## Notes
- [回](../characters/回.md) "turns" + [転](../characters/転.md) "change; turn"

No `stand_in` relationship applies here: 回's own `stand_in` is bare `回` itself, and 転's own is `転化` — 回転 is an independent compound, not a legitimizer for either. **`pos` corrected `性詞`→`事詞`**, matching both constituent characters' own `事詞` category, the same fix class as [[呼吸]] and [[戦闘]] earlier this sweep.

Mandarin huízhuǎn, Cantonese wui4 zyun2, Japanese かいてん (kaiten), and Korean 회전 (hoejeon) are all the standard everyday word for "rotation, spin, turn" across the sphere (回転木馬/회전목마, "carousel," literally "rotating wooden horse"). Vietnamese hồi chuyển is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search), used especially in technical/gyroscopic contexts (con quay hồi chuyển, "gyroscope"), alongside the more general everyday native verbs xoay/quay for "to turn, spin."
