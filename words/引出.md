## Notes
- [引 (char)](../characters/引%20(char).md) "pull, draw" + [出 (char)](../characters/出%20(char).md) "exit, out" — to draw something out, to lead something forth.

引出 is a transparent verb-object compound built on the same root as [[牽引]], pairing 引 ("to draw, to tug") with the directional complement 出 ("out"). In Mandarin (yǐnchū) it functions broadly: physically drawing an object out of a container, or abstractly "deriving" a conclusion, "extracting" information, or "evoking" a reaction. Cantonese (jan5 ceot1) and the Dan'a'yo reading follow the same compositional pattern.

In Japanese, 引出 is more commonly encountered as 引き出し (hikidashi, "drawer") in its native kun-yomi nominal form, or as the verb 引き出す (hikidasu, "to pull out, to withdraw [money]"). Dan'a'yo instead adopts the Sino-Japanese on-yomi reading いんしゅつ (IN + SHUTSU), which surfaces in compounds such as 引出物 and in the Biblical chengyu [[引出奴家]], where it renders the Hebrew idiom "to lead out of the house of bondage." This on-yomi form preserves the verbal sense of guided extrication rather than the everyday "drawer" sense.

Korean 인출 (incul) is in fact the standard term for a bank "withdrawal," a natural narrowing of the "draw out" sense into the financial domain — a useful point of semantic drift to note alongside the more abstract Mandarin and Biblical usages. Vietnamese dẫn xuất (Hán-Việt) likewise retains the "derive, export" sense, used for example in chemistry for "derivatives."

For a usage in context, see [[引出奴家]], where 引出 ("led out") governs 奴家 ("house of slavery") to render the Exodus formula.
