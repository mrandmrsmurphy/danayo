## Notes
- [祭](../characters/祭.md) "sacrifice; offering; ritual" + [典](../characters/典.md) "canon; ceremony; statute" — stand-in for [[祭]], which cannot appear independently
- Originally denoting the formal rites by which a community honored its ancestors, deities, or the dead — 祭 the offering itself, 典 the codified form it must take — the compound has widened in modern usage from "sacrificial ceremony" to any grand, ceremonious public gathering: a cultural, artistic, or sporting festival. This semantic drift toward "festival" is most visible in Japanese さいてん (saiten), which today evokes school and town festivals as readily as religious observance, while Mandarin jìdiǎn and Sino-Korean 제전 (jejeon) keep a stronger connection to formal commemorative rites and large-scale public celebrations alike.
