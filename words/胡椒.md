## Notes
- [胡](../characters/胡.md) "foreign; reckless" + [椒](../characters/椒.md) "pepper" — stand-in for [[椒]], which cannot appear independently
- Literally "foreign pepper," the compound preserves the memory of black pepper's arrival in East Asia as an exotic import — Tang-dynasty records trace it to the Magadha region of India, and the 胡- prefix marks it (alongside words like 胡瓜 "cucumber" and 胡桃 "walnut") as part of a whole class of vocabulary for foreign goods that entered the Sinosphere along trade routes. The compound and its sense are shared widely: Mandarin hújiāo, Cantonese wu4 ziu1, Japanese こしょう (koshō), and Sino-Korean 호초 (hocho) — though colloquial Korean instead favors the native word 후추 (huchu) for everyday use.
