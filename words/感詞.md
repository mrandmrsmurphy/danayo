## Notes
- [感](../characters/感.md) "feel; sense; be moved" + [詞](../characters/詞.md) "word; term"

感詞 is Dan'a'yo's term for *interjection* — words that express emotion, reaction, or discourse attitude rather than propositional content. The category divides into two subtypes. [[自立]] (free-standing) 感詞 can either close an utterance as a sentence-final particle or stand alone as a complete utterance in their own right: a single 感詞 such as 啊 or 哎 can constitute a full response. [[依存]] (dependent) 感詞 must attach sentence-finally and cannot stand alone; they are bound SFPs (sentence-final particles, [[語気助詞]]) that colour the illocutionary force of the clause they close — conveying assertion, question, surprise, softening, or emphasis. Both subtypes are classed under [[修飾語]] in the Dan'a'yo grammar, modifying the comment of the utterance rather than its topic. 感詞 are the full-length form [[感嘆詞]] abbreviated to a two-character term.

感 (ㄍㄚㄇ/gam/감 — Mandarin gǎn, Cantonese gam2, Korean 감, Japanese KAN/かん, Vietnamese cảm): "feel; sense; be moved; affect" — MC 見母 (k) + 覃韻 (ʌm); a 形声 compound of semantic 心 (heart/mind) + phonetic 咸 (OC *ɡroːm), depicting being struck in the heart. In grammatical metalanguage 感 carries the nuance of emotional response or affective reaction — the defining quality of interjections as a word class. 詞 (ㄙㄚ/sa/사 — Mandarin cí, Cantonese ci4, Korean 사, Japanese SHI/し, Vietnamese từ): "word; term; lyric" — MC 從母 (z) + 之韻 (ɨ); see [[擬詞]] for discussion of 詞 vs 語 as competing CJKV suffixes for word-class terms. The standard CJKV term for interjection is [[感嘆詞]] (感嘆詞 — adding 嘆 "sigh; exclaim"), used in Japanese (感嘆詞 kantanshi, also 間投詞 kantōshi), Korean (감탄사 感嘆詞), and Mandarin (感叹词 gǎntàncí). Vietnamese shortens it further to thán từ (嘆詞), dropping 感 instead of 嘆. Dan'a'yo's 感詞 takes the opposite shortening strategy — keeping 感 and dropping 嘆 — producing a two-character form parallel to other Dan'a'yo POS terms (擬詞, 代詞, 数詞, etc.).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 감사 matches Korean 감사. Both 感 (감=감) and 詞 (사=사) converge in full. MC 見母+覃韻 → identical initial and vowel in Dan'a'yo and Korean; MC 從母+之韻 → identical /sa/ in both. The match is complete despite 感詞 being a Dan'a'yo coinage with no established Korean usage — the Sino-Korean readings of the individual characters happen to align perfectly. Note however that Korean 감사 is a heavily loaded homophone: 感謝 (감사, gratitude; thank you) and 監査 (감사, audit; inspection) are both high-frequency words. A Korean speaker hearing 감사 would not reach for "interjection" as a reading — context or the written form 感詞 is needed to disambiguate. This is one of several cases where Dan'a'yo achieves kwin: true with a reading that would create collision in actual Korean usage.
