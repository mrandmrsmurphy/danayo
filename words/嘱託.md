## Notes
- [嘱](../characters/嘱.md) "order, instruct" + [託](../characters/託.md) "entrust, rely on, commission"
- This word is the stand-in for [[嘱]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- To formally commission or entrust a task to someone — a handover of responsibility. In Japanese, しょくたく (shokutaku) is an employment and administrative term for a special-contract worker or commissioned person outside the regular staff — 嘱託社員 "contract/commissioned employee." The word combines the sense of 嘱 "to charge someone with a duty" and 託 "to entrust." In Chinese, 嘱托 (zhǔtuō) means "to ask someone to do something on your behalf; to entrust a matter to," appearing in contexts of last wishes (临终嘱托 "deathbed request") and delegated tasks. Korean 촉탁 (囑託) is used in bureaucratic and legal contexts for a commissioned or outsourced function.
