## Notes
- [拙](../characters/拙.md) "awkward, clumsy" + [劣](../characters/劣.md) "inferior, bad"
- This word is the stand-in for [[拙]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A reinforcing compound meaning clumsily inferior — the double failure of being both incompetent and poor in quality. In Japanese, せつれつ (setsuretsu) is commonly used to describe poor craftsmanship, clumsy prose, or shoddy work: 拙劣な文章 "crude writing." Chinese 拙劣 (zhuōliè) carries the same meaning, often applied to imitation, execution, or performance that falls below standard: 拙劣的模仿 "a poor imitation." Korean 졸렬 (拙劣) is a standard term for clumsy, bungling, or shoddy — frequently used in critical contexts: 졸렬한 수법 "a clumsy method." Both 拙 and 劣 belong to the vocabulary of self-deprecation in formal registers — 拙著 (setsucho in Japanese) "my humble work," 劣 underlying 劣等生 "poor student."
