## Notes
- [穀](../characters/穀.md) "cereal, grain" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing" — stand-in for [[穀]], which cannot appear independently

穀物 (こくもつ / 곡물 / gǔwù) is the standard East Asian compound for grain crops — the cereals and staple foods harvested from cultivated fields. 穀 (gǔ) refers specifically to husked grain or cereal, while 物 (wù) is the general classifier for "thing(s)", producing a compound meaning "grain-things" or simply "grains". Together they cover rice, wheat, millet, barley, sorghum, and other field crops that form the basis of the food supply.

The concept overlaps with the classical 五穀 (Five Grains — the canonical list of staple crops, which varies by tradition but typically includes rice, millet, wheat, soybean, and hemp or sorghum). 穀物 is the more general modern term, used in agricultural science, economics, and everyday speech across the Sinosphere. In Japanese こくもつ appears in compounds like 穀物相場 (grain market), 穀物倉庫 (granary), and is the standard word on food labels and in agricultural statistics. Korean 곡물 and Chinese 谷物 / 穀物 are similarly standard.
