## Notes
- [誹](../characters/誹.md) "to slander; to defame" + [謗](../characters/謗.md) "to slander; to speak ill of"

誹謗 is a semantic doublet: both 誹 and 謗 mean "to slander, to speak ill of someone behind their back or without justification." Both carry the semantic radical 言 (speech, word), encoding the speech act as the mechanism of harm. 誹 (phonetic 非, "not, wrong") etymologically suggests speech that is "not right" or "in the wrong"; 謗 (phonetic 旁, "beside, adjacent") evokes speech that goes around someone rather than to their face. Together they form the classical compound for defamatory speech: false or malicious statements that damage someone's reputation.

In modern legal contexts 誹謗 is a formal term for defamation. Japanese 誹謗罪 (hibō-zai) and 名誉毀損 (meiyokison, damage to honour) cover overlapping territory in criminal and civil law; 誹謗中傷 (hibō-chūshō, a four-character compound combining this word with 中傷, "slander/backstabbing") has become the dominant term in discussions of online harassment and cyberbullying in Japan. In Korean, 비방 (bibang) is the everyday term for badmouthing and defamation, while 명예훼손 (meonyehweson, damage to honour/reputation) is the legal term. In Chinese, 誹謗 (fěibàng) appears in both civil law (民事誹謗) and discussions of political criticism.

The 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 corrections here: 誹's Dan'a'yo readings are fi / 삐 / ㄈㄧ (not pi / 피 / ㄆㄧ). The initial consonant of 誹 in Dan'a'yo is f-, from the MC initial 非 (labio-dental); the previous entry had confused it with 謗's initial ㄆ (bilabial), giving double-ㄆ throughout. The correct 注音 is ㄈㄧㄆㄚㄫ, not ㄆㄧㄆㄚㄫ.
