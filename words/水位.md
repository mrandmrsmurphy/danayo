## Notes
- [水 (char)](../characters/水%20(char).md) "water" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, level"

水位 is the literal "position of the water" — the level or height of water in a river, lake, reservoir, or tide gauge, as measured against a fixed reference point. 水, a 象形 pictograph of flowing water (the same shape that compresses to the 氵 radical beside other characters), is one of the oldest and most productive characters in the script, already anchoring an enormous family of compounds in [[水 (char)]]'s own Words section — [[湖水]] ("lake"), [[膠水]] ("glue"), [[潜水艦]] ("submarine"), and dozens more.

水位 belongs to a small set of "physical-quantity + 位" compounds that round out this batch's exploration of 位 beyond rank and throne: where [[即位]]/[[在位]]/[[王位]] use 位 for an *office* and [[坐位]] for a *seat*, 水位 (and the [[方位]]/[[地位]]/[[単位]]/[[部位]] that complete the 15-word 位 family) use it for a *measured point along a continuum* — exactly the sense flagged in [[位]]'s own etymology note (人 "person" standing at 立 "a fixed spot"). Flood warnings, dam management, and tide tables all hinge on tracking changes in 水位 over time.
