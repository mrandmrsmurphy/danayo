## Notes
- [橋 (char)](../characters/橋%20(char).md) "bridge" — stand-in word for the character.

橋 ("bridge") is one of the oldest and most culturally loaded engineering concepts in the Sinosphere. The Chinese tradition distinguishes several canonical bridge forms: beam bridges (梁橋), arch bridges (拱橋), suspension bridges (吊橋), and pontoon bridges (浮橋), all attested in historical records and architectural encyclopaedias since the Han dynasty.

In Japanese, 橋 is read as はし (hashi) in native Japanese — a word that extends metaphorically to any connection or interface between two realms (橋渡し, hashiwatashi, "bridge-crossing," meaning mediation or introduction). The on-yomi キョウ (kyō) is used in Sino-Japanese compounds: 鉄橋 (tekkyō, iron bridge), 歩道橋 (hodōkyō, pedestrian bridge).

In Korean, 교 (橋, gyo) functions primarily in compounds — 다리 is the native Korean word for bridge. The compound 교량 (橋梁) is the formal term.

The Hán-Việt reading kiều preserves the Middle Chinese pronunciation most closely of all the languages; the native Vietnamese cầu (a separate word) is the everyday term. The Sino-Vietnamese kiều appears in names and classical contexts — including the surname 橋 carried by several legendary figures in Vietnamese literature.
