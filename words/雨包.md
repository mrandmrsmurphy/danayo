## Notes
- [雨 (char)](../characters/雨%20(char).md) "rain" + [包](../characters/包.md) as 代用字 for 雹 "hail"
- 包 carries 雹 as one of its aliases, making it the sanctioned stand-in wherever 雹 is needed. The alias 雨雹 preserves the original Korean spelling.

The word comes from Korean 우박 (雨雹), the standard Korean term for hail. The compound is transparent: 雨 ("rain") + 雹 ("hailstone"), rain that falls as ice. In Korean meteorology and everyday speech, 우박 refers specifically to the larger pellets of ice that fall during severe convective storms — distinct from 싸락눈 (graupel, small soft ice pellets) — so "large hail" as a gloss captures the typical Korean usage accurately.

In Mandarin, hail is usually expressed as 冰雹 (*bīngbáo*, "ice hail") or simply 雹 (*báo*); the compound 雨雹 is not standard Mandarin but the characters read naturally as *yǔbáo*. Japanese uses 雹 alone (ひょう, *hyō*) for hail, with 雨雹 not being a native Japanese compound. The Dan'a'yo form 雨包 is thus primarily a phonographic rendering of the Korean word, written with the vault's character set.
