## Notes
- [非 (char)](../characters/非%20(char).md) "ain't; is not"

非 is the inequality/negation copula in Dan'a'yo — the negative equative "to not be; to not equal" — denying that the subject and predicate refer to the same referent: 此非書 (this is not a book), 我非教師 (I am not a teacher). Among the six Dan'a'yo copulas, 非 is the direct antonym of [[是]] (to be; to equal). Etymologically, 非 (OC *pɯj) is understood as a fusion of the negation particle 不 (OC *pɯ, "not") and the copula 惟 (OC *ɢʷi, "to be"), attested in oracle bone inscriptions; the character itself is a 象形 of two wings facing opposite directions, depicting symmetric opposition. 非 has productive use in compounds denoting negation or illegality: 非法 (illegal), 非常 (extraordinary — lit. "not-normal"), 是非 (right and wrong). 非 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[非]].

Mandarin: the negative equative copula is 不是 (bú shì, "not is") — 我不是学生 (I am not a student); 非 appears in formal or literary contexts: 是非 (right and wrong), 非法 (illegal), 非常 (extraordinary). Cantonese 唔係 (m4 hai6, "not is"): 我唔係學生 (I am not a student); 非 in set compounds. Japanese ではない (de wa nai) or じゃない (ja nai, colloquial): 学生ではない (not a student); Sino-Japanese 非 in compounds — 非常 (hijō, extraordinary), 是非 (zehi, right and wrong; by all means). Korean 아니다 (anida): 나는 학생이 아니다 (I am not a student); Sino-Korean 비 (非) in compounds — 비상 (非常, extraordinary), 시비 (是非, right and wrong). Vietnamese không phải (là): Tôi không phải là học sinh (I am not a student); Sino-Vietnamese phi (非) in literary compounds: phi nghĩa (非義, unjust), phi thường (非常, extraordinary).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 삐 does not match Korean 비. 非 reads 삐 (fi/ㄈㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 非母 (f) + 微合韻 (ʉi) gives a labiodental fricative with the plain -i vowel. Dan'a'yo renders the MC 非母 (f) in Hangul as ㅃ (tense bilabial) — a marked initial used to distinguish the labiodental fricative ㄈ from plain bilabial stops — while Sino-Korean 비 (bi) maps the same MC initial to plain ㅂ (b). The vowel is identical (-i in both), but the initial differs: Dan'a'yo 삐 (tense bilabial ㅃ) vs Korean 비 (plain bilabial ㅂ). The use of ㅃ is the systematic Dan'a'yo Hangul transcription of the ㄈ initial across all f-initial syllables.
