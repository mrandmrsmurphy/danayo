## Notes
- [氷](../characters/氷.md) "ice" (alias: 冰) + [月 (char)](../characters/月%20(char).md) "month; moon" — "ice month"

氷月 (*Bīngyuè*, "ice month") is one of the many poetic alternative names for the twelfth lunar month, alongside [[臘月]] — see that entry for the principal name and its associated festival ([[臘八]]). Where 臘月 names the month after its ritual function (the year-end 臘 sacrifice), 氷月 names it after its seasonal character: deep winter, when rivers and ponds freeze over. This pattern — a month named for a ritual/calendrical role alongside a second name describing its natural-seasonal character — recurs throughout the traditional poetic calendar; compare [[良月]] and [[露月]] for the tenth month.

The character is written 氷 in the vault's standard form (an alternative graphic form of 冰, "ice"); the traditional/variant form 冰月 is retained as an alias. As with other poetic month-names, 氷月/冰月 is essentially a literary and calendrical curiosity in the modern languages — Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all use the numbered month (十二月/十二월/December, etc.) in ordinary speech, and 氷月 survives mainly in classical poetry, traditional calendars, and discussions of the lunar calendar's historical nomenclature.
