## Notes
- [海](../characters/海.md) "sea" + [洋](../characters/洋.md) "ocean; sea" — stand-in for [[海]], which cannot appear independently, smaller in scope than the larger [[大洋]] ("ocean," lit. "great 洋")

Mandarin hǎiyáng, Cantonese hoi2 joeng4, Japanese かいよう (kaiyō), and Vietnamese hải dương (also, notably, the name of a real Vietnamese coastal province, Hải Dương) are all the standard formal/scientific word for "ocean, marine" (海洋学/hải dương học, "oceanography"), each alongside a more everyday native word for the sea in casual speech (Japanese 海, うみ; Korean's own everyday word is native 바다, which the frontmatter previously and wrongly held in place of this compound's own Sino-Korean reading — corrected to 해양). `kwin: true` (previously unset) computed per the AND-rule — both `characters/海.md` and `characters/洋.md` are individually `true`.
