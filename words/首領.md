## Notes
- [首](../characters/首.md) "head; prime" + [領](../characters/領.md) "lead; territory"

**首領** (*shǒulǐng*, "head-leader") denotes a paramount leader — the supreme authority of a state, movement, or organisation. The compound covers a range from warlord chieftain to head of government, with the specific register depending on context: in Mandarin and Cantonese it can describe a gang boss or a revolutionary commander equally; in Vietnamese thủ lĩnh similarly spans military and political leadership.

Its most historically charged use is in Korean: **수령** (首領) was the ideological title of Kim Il-sung (金日成, 1912–1994) as founder and 初代 head of the 朝鮮民主主義人民共和国, rendered in North Korean political discourse as 위대한 수령 ("Great Leader"). The title places him above ordinary governmental roles such as 総統 or [[国務領]], marking a form of personal sovereignty derived from revolutionary legitimacy rather than constitutional office.
