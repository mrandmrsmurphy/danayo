## Notes
- [方](../characters/方.md) "direction, side, square; method" + [面](../characters/面.md) "face, surface, side; aspect" — the face of a direction; a sector or facet of something.

方面 is one of the most productive spatial-to-abstract compounds in the Sinosphere. Its concrete meaning is a quadrant or sector — the face of a given direction — which has extended to mean any domain, aspect, or field of concern. The compound's versatility comes from 面 doing double work: as a physical surface (the face of a side) and as an abstract facet (the face of an issue).

Spatial uses: この方面 (in this direction/area), 北方面軍 (Northern Army Group — the Japanese military sense, a large-scale territorial command). Abstract uses: 教育方面 (in the field of education), 各方面 (in all areas/respects), 安全方面 (in terms of safety). The shift from concrete quadrant to abstract domain is fully grammaticalised — no metaphorical effort is felt by a modern speaker.

The compound contrasts with its near-neighbour [[方向]] (direction, orientation): 方向 points to a vector without width, while 方面 designates a sector with depth and breadth — the difference between "heading north" (方向) and "developments in the northern theatre" (方面).

Vietnamese phương diện (方面) is directly cognate via Hán-Việt: phương (方, direction, method) + diện (面, face, surface). It is used in the same abstract-domain sense: về phương diện kinh tế ("in terms of economics"). The Hán-Việt compound is fully alive in formal Vietnamese, making this one of the more consistent pan-Sinospheric abstract vocabulary items.
