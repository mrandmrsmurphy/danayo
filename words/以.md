## Notes
- [以 (char)](../characters/以%20(char).md) "by; using; instrumental"

以 is the instrumental case particle — 具格, marking the means, tool, or method by which an action is performed: "by; using; through; with." It attaches to an instrument, material, or method: 以火燒 (burn by means of fire), 以筆書 (write with a brush), 以力服人 (subdue people by force), 以此為證 (use this as evidence). In the comparison chart, 以 parallels Mandarin 用 (yòng, to use; with), Cantonese 用 (jung6), Japanese で (de, by means of; with), Korean 로/으로 (-ro/-euro, by means of; with), Vietnamese bằng (by; using; with). The instrumental 以 is distinct from the comitative 共: 以杖行 is "walk by means of a cane" (using it as a tool), where 共 would imply walking together as companions. 以 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[以]].

Mandarin 以 (yǐ): the classical instrumental preposition, now largely replaced by 用 (yòng) in spoken Mandarin, but preserved in literary grammar and set compounds: 以...為 (to take...as; to regard...as), 以上 (above; more than — lit. from this upward), 以下 (below; less than), 以前 (before; formerly), 以後 (after; thereafter), 以便 (so as to; in order to), 可以 (may; can; permissible). Cantonese 用 (jung6): 用筆寫 (write with a pen), 用刀切 (cut with a knife). Japanese で (de) is the productive instrumental: バスで行く (go by bus), はさみで切る (cut with scissors), 日本語で話す (speak in Japanese). Korean 로/으로 (ro/euro): 버스로 가다 (go by bus), 가위로 자르다 (cut with scissors), 영어로 쓰다 (write in English). Vietnamese bằng (by means of; using): đi bằng xe buýt (go by bus), viết bằng bút (write with a pen).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 이 exactly matches Korean 이. 以 reads 이 ('i/ㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 以母 (j) + 之韻 (ɨ) gives a vowel-initial syllable with -i in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, producing identical 이 forms. The MC 以母 (j) null-initial before the 之韻 vowel yields the same plain vowel-initial syllable in both systems.
