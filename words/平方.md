## Notes
- [平](../characters/平.md) "even, flat" + [方](../characters/方.md) "direction, square" — a number multiplied by itself; also a prefix for area units (a square with that length on each side).

平方/へいほう/bình phương both serve double duty as "to square (a number)" and the "square-" prefix for area units (平方米/平方メートル/mét bình phương, "square meter") — a real, consistent match, with Japanese even treating 平方 and 二乗/自乗 as fully interchangeable synonyms for squaring. Korean has drifted away from this pattern on both fronts: modern Korean math uses 제곱 as the standard term for "squared" in general (x의 제곱, not x의 평방), and Korea's metric-terminology reform explicitly favored 제곱미터 over the older 평방미터 as the official area unit — 평방 now reads as dated in both contexts, surviving mainly in older documents and set expressions.
