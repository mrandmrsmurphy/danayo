## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each; distributive" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

毎様 is the distributive manner correlative — "in each way; for each method, one at a time." As a 副用名詞 (adverbial noun functioning as a manner adverb), it distributes a predicate across each separate manner or method individually: 毎様に試してみる ("try it in each way, one at a time"); 毎様に説明する ("explain it by each approach, separately"). The distributive 毎 marks individuated application across manner variants, which is distinct from the collective 皆様 ("in every way, in all respects simultaneously").

Mandarin 各種各樣 (gèzhǒng gèyàng, "various kinds and manners; in every conceivable way") captures a collective-distributive blend; the more precisely distributive form is 以各種方式 (yǐ gè zhǒng fāngshì, "by each/various method"). Japanese 各様に (kakuyō ni, "each in its own way, variously") is the most direct equivalent; さまざまな方法で (in various methods) covers the plurality-of-manners sense. Korean 각각의 방식으로 (gak-gak-ui bangsik-euro, "in each one's own way") is the explicit distributive form.

毎様 is the most abstract entry in the 様 row — distributing across "ways" demands more conceptual effort than distributing across persons (毎人), places (毎処), or times (毎時). It finds its clearest use in instructional and comparative contexts: listing multiple methods and applying the same predicate to each in turn. The 毎様/皆様 distinction — "each way (one by one, individually)" vs "in every way (comprehensively, all at once)" — mirrors the distributive/collective split running through all 毎/皆 column pairs in the correlative table.
