## Notes
- [縁](../characters/縁.md) "fate, chance" + [故](../characters/故.md) "happening" — stand-in for [[故]], which cannot appear independently.

縁 here stands in for its traditional variant 緣 — the two are simply traditional/Japanese-shinjitai forms of the same character, with identical readings in every language, so unlike most stand-in relationships in this family there is no phonetic divergence to reconcile. 緣故/縁故 (yuángù) means "the reason; the cause; the why of something" — the underlying circumstance that explains why something happened. It is a common, slightly formal way of asking after or stating a cause, distinct from the more abstract "fate, predestined connection" sense that 縁/緣 carries on its own (as in 因緣).

Because 縁 and 緣 are true variants, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 (연고 / 'yenggo / ⼶ㄋㄍㄛ) — mechanically concatenated from 縁's and 故's own fixed Dan'a'yo values — already match the real-world Sino-Korean reading exactly, hence `kwin: true`. The mandarin, cantonese, japanese, and vietnamese fields are likewise the direct, undivergent readings of 緣故/縁故.

This word resolves the existing [[縁故]] reference in [[縁]]'s own Notes.
