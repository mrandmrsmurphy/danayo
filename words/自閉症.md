## Notes
- [自](../characters/自.md) "self" + [閉](../characters/閉.md) "close, shut" + [症](../characters/症.md) "symptom, condition" — self-closing condition.

自閉症 is the shared East Asian medical translation of autism, coined in the 20th century to render the clinical concept introduced by Leo Kanner (1943) and Hans Asperger (1944). The core image — 自閉, "self-closing" — is a calque of the Greek αὐτισμός (autismos), coined by Eugen Bleuler (1911) from αὐτός (autos, "self") to describe a withdrawal into an interior world. 自 (self) + 閉 (shut) replicates the etymology exactly: a door closed from the inside.

The translation is notably pan-Sinospheric: Japanese 自閉症 (jiheishō), Korean 자폐증 (japyejeung), and Chinese 自闭症 (zìbìzhèng) all use the same three-character compound, making this one of the few modern medical terms where the entire East Asian vocabulary aligns. This convergence reflects the shared influence of Japanese medical translation in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, when Japan created the primary vocabulary for Western science and medicine that was then adopted into Chinese and Korean.

The neurodiversity movement has critiqued the framing of 自閉 — a "closed self" — as pathologising a perceptual difference as an abnormal withdrawal. Updated clinical framing in Japan now uses 自閉スペクトラム症 (autism spectrum disorder) and 発達障害 (developmental difference), though 自閉症 remains in common usage. Korean uses 자폐스펙트럼장애 in formal medical contexts. Vietnamese uses tự kỷ (from 自己, "self-self") or chứng tự kỷ — a parallel calque that follows the same logic without adopting the 閉 "shut" metaphor.
