## Notes
- [代](../characters/代.md) "substitute; stand in for; represent" + [詞](../characters/詞.md) "word; term"

代詞 is the POS class of pronouns in Dan'a'yo — words that stand in for nouns or noun phrases without themselves being nouns. The class divides into two subtypes: personal pronouns (人称代詞: [[我]], [[君]], [[我等]], [[君等]], and the archaic [[俺]], [[朕]], [[汝]], [[厥]]) and correlative pronouns (対応代詞: the systematic table of demonstrative–quantifier combinations across nine semantic domains, listed in the correlative chart of [[文法 - 97品詞]]). 代詞 is Dan'a'yo's shortened form of 代名詞 (the standard CJKV term for pronoun in Japanese だいめいし, Korean 대명사, and Mandarin 代名詞 dàimíngcí), dropping 名 ("name") to match the two-character POS naming convention of other Dan'a'yo terms: [[系詞]], [[連接詞]], [[数詞]], [[擬詞]], etc.

代 (ㄉㄚㄧ/dai/대 — Mandarin dài, Cantonese doi6, Korean 대, Japanese DAI/だい, Vietnamese đại): "substitute; replace; stand in for; generation; era" — MC 定母 (d) + 咍韻 (ʌi); a 形声 of semantic 亻 (person) + phonetic 弋, meaning "to stand in for; to replace; to represent." In grammatical metalanguage 代 is the "substitute; stand-in" element — a 代詞 is literally a "substitution-word," a word that takes the place of a noun. 詞 (ㄙㄚ/sa/사): "word; term" — MC 從母 (z) + 之韻 (ɨ); see [[系詞]], [[擬詞]], [[感詞]] for full analysis. Vietnamese đại từ preserves the compound structure intact: đại (代) + từ (詞).
