## Notes
- [康](../characters/康.md) "at ease, peaceful" + [寧](../characters/寧.md) "peaceful, calm" — a synonym-doubling meaning "peace and well-being." This word is the viable form of [康](../characters/康.md): 康 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 康寧 is its `stand_in` compound, keeping 康's sense the "at ease, comfortable, well" one — distinct from [[健全]] (the "sturdy, sound" sense that [[健]] stands in for instead) — splitting apart the two flavors the collapsed compound [[健康]] used to blur into one generic "healthy."

Korean 강녕 (kangnyeong) is the more historically prominent of the daughters here — it survives in classical/formal usage asking after someone's well-being (especially royalty: "성체 강녕하시옵니까," "is Your Majesty in good health"), a register distinct from the everyday 건강 (健康, "healthy" in the ordinary physical sense). Japanese こうねい (kōnei) and Mandarin kāngníng stay more literary/archaic, rarely appearing in colloquial speech, which reinforces that 康's real precise sense is "well-being, ease" rather than "robust physical health" — that's 健's job.
