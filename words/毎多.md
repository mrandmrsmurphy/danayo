## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each; distributive" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

毎多 is the distributive quantity correlative — "each one's respective amount; so much each; a certain share per unit." Like all 毎-column entries, the distributive 毎 applies the quantity across each individual item one by one rather than to the collective total: where 皆多 refers to the whole quantity together, 毎多 refers to each unit's individual portion. In practice: 毎多を配る ("distribute each one's respective share"); 毎多の食料を割り当てた ("allocated a respective amount of food to each").

There is no compact single word in any CJKV language for the distributive-quantity concept — "each one's respective amount" requires a phrase in all source languages. Mandarin 各自多少 (gèzì duōshǎo, "each one's respective how-much") or 按人頭 (àn réntóu, "per head/person") capture the per-unit distribution sense. Japanese それぞれの量 (sorezore no ryō, "each one's respective amount") or 一人一人に (hitori hitori ni, "to each one individually"). Korean 각자 얼마씩 (gakja eolmasik, "each for themselves, how much apiece") or 각각 (gakgak, "each/respectively"). Vietnamese mỗi người một lượng (each person one amount).

毎多 is the most abstract entry in the 多 row — distributing across "amounts" is more conceptually demanding than distributing across persons (毎人) or times (毎時). It finds its most natural use in allocation and distribution contexts: dividing resources among parties where each receives a contextually determined share. The 毎多/皆多 distinction — "each one's respective amount" vs "the total/whole amount" — runs parallel to the distributive/collective split across all 毎/皆 column pairs.
