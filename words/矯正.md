## Notes
- [矯](../characters/矯.md) "rectify" + [正 (char)](../characters/正%20(char).md) "correct" — stand-in for [[矯]], which cannot appear independently

矯正 means to correct or rectify something that has gone wrong or deviated from the proper standard — straightening a bent object, reforming a bad habit, correcting a child's posture, or rehabilitating an offender. The two components are near-synonyms: 矯 (to bend back into shape, to straighten by force) and 正 (correct, upright), together forming an emphatic compound for active correction rather than mere description.

In Japanese きょうせい is widely used in medical and educational contexts: 矯正歯科 (orthodontics), 姿勢矯正 (posture correction), and 矯正施設 (correctional facilities, prisons). The Japanese Ministry of Justice uses 矯正局 as the name of its corrections bureau. In Chinese 矫正 (jiǎozhèng) similarly spans dental, behavioural, and criminal-justice senses. In Korean 교정 covers the same range, including newspaper proofreading (교정 보다, "to proofread").
