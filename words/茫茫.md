## Notes
- [茫](../characters/茫.md) "vast, boundless" + [茫](../characters/茫.md) "vast, boundless" — reduplicated for vividness; an ideophone (擬詞) rather than a plain stative, since the doubling itself is what carries the intensified, atmospheric sense, the same pattern as [[懍懍]] and [[業業]].

Reduplicated 茫茫 covers both a purely visual sense — a landscape so vast it blurs into haze, as in 茫茫大海 "the boundless ocean" or 茫茫沙漠 "the endless desert" — and a cognitive one, a mind or memory gone hazy and indistinct (茫茫たる記憶 in Japanese, "a hazy, indistinct memory"). The two senses share one image: something too vast to bring into focus, whether that vastness is physical space or one's own recollection. The classical citation is Nguyễn Du's *Truyện Kiều*, "江水茫茫江岸平" (giang thủy mang mang giang ngạn bình, "the river water stretches vast, its banks level with the flood") — the source of the direct Sino-Vietnamese reading *mang mang* used here; modern colloquial Vietnamese has since drifted this into *mênh mang*, blending in a native element, so the two forms coexist with the older one preserved mainly in poetry.
