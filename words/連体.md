## Notes
- [連 (char)](../characters/連%20(char).md) "mutual" + [体](../characters/体.md) "body; form"
- The grammatical senses originate in Japanese linguistic tradition (first attested 1834); the Chinese senses are independent and bodily in meaning.

1. (Linguistics) **adnominal; attributive** — the modification of a noun by a preceding element; the domain of adnominal words and forms
2. (Linguistics) **連体形** — the attributive inflectional form of adjectives and verbs, used when the modified noun follows directly
3. (Linguistics) **連体詞** — the class of adnominal words (modifiers that precede nouns but cannot serve as predicates)
4. (Chinese) **conjoined; joined in bodies** — physically connected, as in conjoined twins (連体嬰兒)
5. (Chinese) **one-piece** — forming a single continuous garment or structure (連体泳衣, one-piece swimsuit)
