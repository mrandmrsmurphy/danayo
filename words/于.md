## Notes
- [于 (char)](../characters/于%20(char).md) "to; dative"

于 is the dative case particle — 与格, marking the animate goal of a verb: "to; for; toward (a person or living being)." It attaches to a recipient noun to indicate the beneficiary or indirect object: 帰于家 (return home to family), 告于師 (report to the teacher), 給于人 (give to a person). In the comparison chart, 于 parallels Mandarin 给 (gěi, to; for), Cantonese 畀 (bei2), Japanese に (ni, indirect object marker), Korean 에게/한테 (to/for a person), Vietnamese cho (to; for). Dan'a'yo restricts 于 to animate goals; inanimate locations use 於 (locative) instead. The classical Chinese 于 and 於 were historically interchangeable (both appear as aliases of each other), but Dan'a'yo assigns them distinct functions within the case system. 于 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[于]].

Mandarin: the dative function of 于/於 in classical Chinese is now covered by 给 (gěi, to; for; towards) in modern spoken Mandarin, while 于 survives in literary phrases: 生于 (born of; born to), 归于 (return to; attributed to), 于是 (thereupon; and so — here 于 functions as a conjunction). Cantonese 畀 (bei2) is the primary dative marker: 畀我 (give to me), 話畀佢聽 (tell him). Japanese に (ni) marks both dative and locative: 彼に話す (speak to him), 彼女に送る (send to her), 先生に聞く (ask the teacher). Korean 에게 (ege, to/for a person — formal), 한테 (hante, to — informal): 친구에게 주다 (give to a friend), 선생님께 드리다 (give to the teacher — honorific 께). Vietnamese cho (to; for): đưa cho anh (give to you), nói cho biết (tell so they know).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 우 exactly matches Korean 우. 于 reads 우 ('u/ㄨ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 云母 (ø, null initial) + 虞韻 (ɨo) gives a vowel-initial syllable with rounded -u in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, producing identical 우 forms. The null initial and the rounded back vowel of 虞韻 align cleanly across both systems.
