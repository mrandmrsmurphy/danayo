## Notes
- [繋](../characters/繋.md) "tie, bind, connect" + [辞](../characters/辞.md) "word, expression" — the word that "ties" a subject to its predicate: a copula.

Japanese 繋辞 (keiji) and Korean 계사 (whose hanja is 繫辭, written here with 辞, the alias under which the vault records 辭/繫) name the same grammatical category in the same two characters, even though modern Mandarin linguistics instead favors 系词 (xìcí, built on the unrelated-but-similar-looking root 系 "lineage, system" rather than 繋 "to tie"). The grammar book uses 繋辞 for the small closed class of Dan'a'yo words that can also function as verbal prefixes; it notes specifically that copulas are the only verbs that supplete for [[否定]] (negation), rather than taking the ordinary negating prefix 不–. Attested across Japanese and Korean.
