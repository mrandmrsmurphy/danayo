## Notes
- [紅 (char)](../characters/紅%20(char).md) "red; crimson" + [玉 (char)](../characters/玉%20(char).md) "jade; precious stone"

紅玉 is "ruby" — 紅 (red, crimson) + 玉 (jade; precious stone; gem). The compound applies the standard Sinitic gem-naming pattern: colour + 玉. Just as 碧玉 (bìyù) is green jade and 白玉 (báiyù) white jade, 紅玉 is the "red gem" — extended to ruby (corundum, Al₂O₃) by colour analogy. The shift from 玉=jade specifically to 玉=precious stone generally is well-established in CJKV mineralogical vocabulary; 玉 serves as the generic term for any prized gemstone, not only nephrite or jadeite.

Mandarin 紅玉 (hóngyù) is the literary and classical term for ruby; the everyday term is 紅寶石 (hóng bǎoshí, "red precious stone"). Cantonese 紅玉 (hung4 juk6). Japanese 紅玉 (こうぎょく, kōgyoku) is the formal/classical term for ruby — but also the name for the Jonathan apple variety (紅玉りんご), a deep-red cultivar introduced to Japan from the United States in the Meiji era and named for its ruby-red colour. Korean 홍옥 (紅玉, hongok) is used for both ruby and the Jonathan apple (홍옥 사과) — the same dual usage as Japanese. Vietnamese hồng ngọc (Sino-Vietnamese: 紅=hồng, 玉=ngọc) is the standard word for ruby: đá hồng ngọc (ruby stone).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 홍욕 differs from Korean 홍옥 in the second syllable. 玉 reads 욕 ('yog/⼄ㄎ) in Dan'a'yo but 옥 (ok) in Sino-Korean. Dan'a'yo preserves a y-initial glide — MC 疑 initial (ŋ) + ɨok final gives Dan'a'yo ⼄ㄎ, yielding 욕 with y-onset, while Sino-Korean drops the glide to give vowel-initial 옥. This is the same correspondence seen in 語 (Dan'a'yo 요/'yo vs Sino-Korean 어): MC 疑 initial consistently gives Dan'a'yo a y-glide while Sino-Korean gives a plain vowel-initial. The ruby/Jonathan-apple double meaning in Japanese and Korean 紅玉 is a notable semantic parallel — the same colour metaphor (ruby-red) applied independently to stone and fruit.
