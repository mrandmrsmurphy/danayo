## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

皆事 is the universal correlative in the action column: it refers to the totality of all acts, events, or matters — "everything, all of it." The character 皆 (Dan'a'yo *gye*, ㄍ⼶) is the classical Chinese collective universal quantifier meaning "all, every, in every case." In Middle Chinese, 皆 functioned as an adverbial universal (all of them do X), contrasting with 各 (each one separately) and 凡 (in general, as a rule). In Dan'a'yo, 皆 is standardised as the collective universal in the correlative table, contrasting with the distributive 毎 (each, one by one).

In classical Chinese and Sino-Japanese, 万事 (wànshì / ばんじ, ten-thousand matters) is the more conventional universal expression for "all matters, everything": 万事休矣 ("all is lost, every matter is finished"), 万事如意 ("may all matters go as you wish," a common New Year's greeting). 皆事 is Dan'a'yo's regularised correlative form, choosing 皆 for systematic consistency across the table rather than 万 (ten-thousand — more idiomatic but less compositional and less transparent to non-Chinese-background learners). Japanese 皆 (みな, mina) survives in everyday use for people (皆さん, everyone) but is not a productive prefix for compound nouns in modern Japanese; Chinese 皆 is similarly more adverbial than nominal in classical usage.

皆事 must be distinguished from the distributive 毎事: 皆事 says "all matters taken as a whole, collectively" whereas 毎事 says "every matter one by one, in each instance." This is the Dan'a'yo equivalent of the English distinction between "all things" (collective) and "each thing" (distributive). The same contrast runs through the entire correlative table — 皆人/毎人, 皆物/毎物, 皆処/毎処, 皆時/毎時 — and is imported directly from the classical Chinese 皆/每 distinction, which Dan'a'yo formalises as two dedicated columns in the correlative grid.
