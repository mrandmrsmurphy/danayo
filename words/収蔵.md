## Notes
- [収](../characters/収.md) "gather, collect" + [蔵](../characters/蔵.md) "hide, store"

This word is the stand-in for [蔵](../characters/蔵.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 収蔵 carries the same sense of safekeeping and storage.

収蔵 denotes the act of taking something in and keeping it safely — collecting and storing objects, particularly artworks, cultural artifacts, manuscripts, and specimens. Where [[貯蔵]] emphasizes the storage of goods or supplies, 収蔵 carries the sense of curation and preservation: a museum 収蔵 its collection, a library 収蔵 its holdings. In Chinese (收藏, shōucáng), the compound is the standard word for collecting — collecting art, antiques, stamps, or any valuables — and 收藏家 (collector) and 收藏品 (collected items) are common collocations. In Japanese (しゅうぞう, shūzō), 収蔵 appears in museum and archival contexts: 収蔵庫 (storage vault, storeroom), 収蔵品 (collection items). In Korean (수장), 수장고 (収蔵庫, storage vault) is the standard term for a museum's conservation storage area.
