## Notes
- [全 (char)](../characters/全%20(char).md) "whole" + [体](../characters/体.md) "group, body, form" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (全's is bare `全`, 体's is `体系`)

Mandarin quántǐ, Cantonese cyun4 tai2, Japanese ぜんたい (zentai), Korean 전체 (jeonche), and Vietnamese toàn thể (verified via search) are all the standard, everyday word for "the whole, the entirety" across the sphere (全体的に/전체적으로, "on the whole, overall"). Korean's field was narrowed from a comma-dump that also included the native words 모두/죄다 ("all, everyone"), which are real but unrelated to this compound's own Sino-Korean reading.
