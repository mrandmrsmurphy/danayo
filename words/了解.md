## Notes
- [了 (char)](../characters/了%20(char).md) "done, finished" + [解 (char)](../characters/解%20(char).md) "explain"

了解 is "to understand, comprehend" — pairing 了 in its older sense of "to bring to completion/resolution" with 解 "to explain, resolve, untangle," so that together the compound is closer to "have fully worked something out" than to bare passive recognition. The `瞭解` alias substitutes 了's own alternate form 瞭 (listed on `characters/了 (char).md`'s own `aliases` field), a traditional/formal variant spelling still common in Taiwan usage.

Mandarin 了解 (liǎojiě, "understand," also "to find out, look into" — 了解一下 "look into something"). Cantonese 了解 (liu5gaai2). Japanese 了解 (りょうかい, ryōkai) is distinctively used as a stock reply meaning "understood!, roger!" — the standard acknowledgment in military/aviation-style radio communication and increasingly in casual chat, a narrower pragmatic function than the Chinese verb's general "to understand" sense. Korean 요해 (yohae) exists but is comparatively rare in everyday speech, where 이해 (理解, ihae) is the far more common word for "understanding" — 요해 survives mainly in more formal or literary registers. Vietnamese liễu giải ("to understand, comprehend") is attested in the same order as the Chinese compound, though the reverse-ordered doublet giải liễu is, if anything, more frequently encountered, especially in Buddhist-influenced texts.
