## Notes
- [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time" + [相](../characters/相.md) "aspect, appearance" — the grammatical category covering both tense (location in time) and aspect (the internal shape of an event — ongoing, completed, about to happen).

This is the heading the grammar book itself uses for the whole system of verbal prefixes and suffixes marking [[完了]] (perfective), [[将然]] (prospective), and ongoing action — 時相接辞, "tense-aspect affixes." Korean linguistics uses 시상 (時相) in exactly this technical sense, often in the compound 시상 어미 ("tense-aspect ending"), making this one of the rarer cases where a grammatical term's pedigree runs more solidly through Korean academic terminology than through everyday Japanese or Mandarin vocabulary (Japanese more often uses the English loanword アスペクト for "aspect" specifically). Attested across Mandarin, Japanese, and Korean.
