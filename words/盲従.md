## Notes
- [盲](../characters/盲.md) "blind" + [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, obey"

盲従 is blind obedience — following orders, authority, or a leader without thought, judgment, or critical evaluation. The metaphor of blindness (盲) applied to following (従) captures the absence of independent discernment: one follows, but cannot see where one is going or why. The compound is used in political criticism, educational writing, and psychological analysis to name the failure mode of unconditional compliance.

In Japanese (もうじゅう, mōjū), 盲従 appears alongside 盲信 (blind faith) and 盲目的 (blindly, without judgment) as part of a cluster of compounds criticizing uncritical acceptance. In Chinese (盲从, mángcóng), the simplified form is common in political commentary: 盲目从众 (to follow the crowd blindly) and 不要盲从 ("don't follow blindly") are typical collocations. In Korean (맹종), the word is used in both religious and political contexts to describe followers who submit without reflection.
