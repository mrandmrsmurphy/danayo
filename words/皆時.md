## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

皆時 is the universal temporal correlative — "always; at all times, collectively." The character 皆 marks collective universality: 皆時 asserts that the predicate holds across all of time without temporal limitation. This contrasts with the distributive 毎時 (each time, occasion by occasion) and the indefinite 某時 (sometime, an unspecified occasion). 皆時 expresses a timeless or habitual truth — a property that holds universally: 皆時そうだ ("it is always so"); 皆時準備が必要だ ("one must always be prepared").

Mandarin 時時 (shíshí, "always; at all times; constantly" — a reduplication of 時) is the classical and pan-CJKV equivalent, found in idioms like 時時刻刻 ("every moment, at all times"). Alternatively, 总是 (zǒngshì, always) or 常常 (chángcháng, often/always) cover the universal temporal sense in modern Mandarin. Japanese いつも (itsumo, "always") is the standard everyday word; 常に (tsuneni, "always, habitually — more formal") is the literary equivalent. Korean 항상 (hangsang, "always") is the standard; 언제나 (eonjena, "always, anytime") emphasises universality across all possible occasions.

皆時 is the temporal counterpart to 皆処 (everywhere): together they form the spatio-temporal universals of the Dan'a'yo correlative system. The 皆時/毎時 distinction — "always" (collective: a universal truth) vs "each time" (distributive: a per-occasion instruction) — is practically significant: 皆時正直だ ("he is always honest" — a character trait) vs 毎時確認する ("check each time" — a procedure). The same collective/distributive contrast runs through 皆処/毎処, 皆人/毎人, and all other 皆/毎 column pairs, making the distinction a systematic feature of the Dan'a'yo correlative grammar.
