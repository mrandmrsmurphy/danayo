## Notes
- [暴 (char)](../characters/暴%20(char).md) "violent; sudden; brutal; to expose" + [風 (char)](../characters/風%20(char).md) "wind"

暴風 names the violent windstorm — wind made dangerous by the quality of 暴: sudden, extreme, explosive. 暴 (graphemically sun + water + rice or grain, suggesting things being exposed or thrown about under harsh weather) covers both the sense of exposure-to-violence and the sense of being suddenly, dramatically excessive. Combined with 風, it specifies wind that has reached the threshold of violence. The compound extends into 暴風雨 (violent storm with rain), 暴風雪 (blizzard, literally "violent storm snow"), and 暴風域 (storm zone).

The word exists in two inversions across the CJKV sphere: 暴風 (bàofēng, Dan'a'yo canonical form) and 風暴 (fēngbào, aliased form). Modern standard Mandarin strongly prefers 暴風 for the meteorological phenomenon and 風暴 for a more general storm or, figuratively, a political or social storm (政治風暴, a political storm). In Japanese, 暴風 (bōfū) appears in meteorological forecasts — 暴風警報 (gale warning) — and combines further with 雨 and 雪. In Korean, 폭풍 (pokpung, 暴風 with a different vowel development) is the standard term; the character 暴 maps to 폭 in Korean Sino-Korean phonology. Vietnamese bão uses a native term rather than a sinographic compound for tropical storms.

The mandarin and cantonese fields correct the original entry (which had 風暴's readings, fēngbào / fung1 bou6): the canonical Dan'a'yo form 暴風 reads bàofēng and bou6 fung1 in the order of the written characters.
