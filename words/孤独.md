## Notes
- [孤](../characters/孤.md) "lone, lonely" + [独](../characters/独.md) "alone, independent" — stand-in for [[孤]], which cannot appear independently

孤独 is a near-synonymous compound in which both elements mean "alone": 孤 conveys the sense of being without family or support — an orphan, a lone soldier, a solitary peak — while 独 emphasises acting or existing apart from others, without company or attachment. Together the compound names a state of profound aloneness, whether circumstantial (cut off from others) or existential (felt inwardly regardless of company).

In Japanese こどく (孤独) is one of the more emotionally weighted words in the language, frequently appearing in literature, song lyrics, and philosophical writing about modern urban alienation. The distinction between 孤独 (deep solitude, loneliness) and 独り (being physically alone) is well observed in Japanese usage. In Chinese 孤独 (gūdú) similarly carries a literary and melancholy register, contrasted with the plainer 寂寞 (jìmò) for everyday loneliness. Korean 고독 retains the same elevated, literary tone. Vietnamese cô độc is the Sino-Vietnamese equivalent used in formal and literary contexts.
