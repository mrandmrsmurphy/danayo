## Notes
- [孩](../characters/孩.md) "baby; child" + [子](../characters/子.md) "child; son" — `characters/孩.md`'s own `stand_in` points to this word (孩子), meaning 孩 is a bound morpheme that never appears independently outside this compound; 子's own points elsewhere (児子). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/孩.md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/子.md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **Gloss broadened**: `english` previously read only "baby; infant" — corrected to "child; kid(s)," matching the actual HSK-1 sense, which covers children of any age from infancy through the teens (and can also mean "my child/children," even referring to an adult child).
- **Comma-dump corrected to the genuine compositional reading**: `korean` previously held `어린이, 아이`, two unrelated native Korean words for "child" — neither is a reading of 孩子 itself. Replaced with 해자, the real, if narrow and uncommon, Sino-Korean compositional reading (verified via search): specifically "a child of two to three years old," narrower than Mandarin's general "child" sense. 해자 is also, coincidentally, an exact homophone of the entirely unrelated and far more common word 해자 ("moat," as around a castle) — a genuine homophone-across-different-hanja case, not an error.
- Filled `vietnamese` with hài tử, a real Sino-Vietnamese word with a dedicated dictionary entry (verified via search); the everyday native equivalent is trẻ con.
