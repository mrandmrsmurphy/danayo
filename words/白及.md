## Notes
- [白 (char)](../characters/白%20(char).md) "white" + [及 (char)](../characters/及%20(char).md) "reach out to"

**Bletilla striata** — the Chinese ground orchid, also called the hardy orchid or bai ji. A terrestrial orchid native to China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan, growing at forest margins, mountain meadows, and rocky slopes. It produces slender purple-pink flowers on upright stems in spring, and underground pseudobulbs (swollen stem-bases) that serve as the plant's storage organs and its primary medicinal part.

In **traditional Chinese medicine** (中醫), 白及 is classified as an astringent herb (收澀藥) that arrests bleeding (止血) and promotes tissue regeneration (生肌). Its principal applications in classical pharmacopeia:

- **Hemoptysis** (咳血) — coughing up blood, associated in TCM with lung heat and damaged lung vessels
- **Gastric and peptic ulcer bleeding** — taken internally as powder in water or rice congee
- **External wounds** — applied as poultice to stop bleeding and accelerate healing

The mechanism is partly mechanical: the pseudobulbs contain a sticky polysaccharide gel (bletilla glucomannan) that forms a physical adhesive film over wounded tissue, slowing blood flow and creating a scaffold for clotting. Modern pharmacological research has confirmed significant hemostatic activity. Pharmaceutical researchers are investigating bletilla extract as a component of surgical hemostatic materials and as a drug-delivery matrix, exploiting its biocompatibility and adhesive properties.

Note on characters: 白及 (báijí, using 及) and **白芨** (báijī, using 芨 — a grass-radical character specifically created for this plant) are near-homophones in Mandarin referring to the same species. The 及 spelling is more common in older medicinal texts; 芨 is the more botanically specific character. Both are in active use.
