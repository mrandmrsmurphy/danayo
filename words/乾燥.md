## Notes
- [乾 (char)](../characters/乾%20(char).md) "dry" + [燥](../characters/燥.md) "parched, dry, arid" — stand-in for [[燥]], which cannot appear independently; 乾's unified sense (pure yang, absence of moisture) applied to its "dry" application; written 干燥 in Simplified Chinese, the simplification this vault's `干` character represents, but 乾 is the real character here, not 干 ("shield")

Mandarin gānzào, Cantonese gon1 cou3, Japanese かんそう (kansō), and Korean 건조 (geonjo) are all the standard, everyday word for "dry, arid" (乾燥機/건조기, "clothes dryer"). Vietnamese has two attested Sino-Vietnamese readings (verified via search): can táo, using 乾's "gān"-parallel modern reading, and the more classical kiền táo, tied to 乾's older "qián" (Trigram/Heaven) reading — kept can táo as the field value since it matches the everyday adjective sense directly.
