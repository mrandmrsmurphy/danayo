## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that, those" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" + [等 (char)](../characters/等%20(char).md) "etc., rank"

其人等 is "they, them" in the formal/classical register — extending the X-等 collective-plural pattern already seen in [[人等]] ("persons") and [[我等]] ("we") with the demonstrative 其 ("that, those") standing in for third person, literally "those persons." The more common classical form of this pronoun across the literature is the shorter two-character 其等 (without 人); 其人等 is this vault's own elaborated parallel to 人等/我等, spelling the "person" element out explicitly rather than leaving it implicit.

Mandarin 其等/其人等 (qíděng) survives only in classical/legal register, alongside its close cousin 彼等 (bǐděng, "they," with a stronger connotation of an external or opposing party) — modern everyday Mandarin uses 他們 exclusively. Cantonese 其等 (kei4dang2) patterns the same way in writing; note that the spoken colloquial Cantonese "they/them," 佢哋 (keoi5dei6), is built from an entirely different character (佢) and is not a reading of 其 at all — a mix-up that shows up even in some online Cantonese dictionaries, worth flagging since it's the same character-conflation trap this word's own frontmatter fell into before this fix. Neither Japanese nor Korean nor Vietnamese appear to use this specific three-character formation as a real standalone word: Japanese "they" is 彼等/彼ら (karera, built from 彼, not 其), Korean "they" is the native 그들 (geudeul, not Sino-Korean), and Vietnamese "they" is the native họ. The frontmatter readings above (きじんとう, 기인등, kỳ nhân đẳng) are compositional on'yomi/Sino-Korean/Sino-Vietnamese readings of the three characters given as a formal cross-reference, not claims of natural everyday usage — the same honesty standard applied to [[予習]] and [[不用]] earlier in this sweep.

kwin is false: none of the three constituent characters individually match their own Sino-Korean readings against Dan'a'yo (其 그≠기, 人 닌≠인, 等 둥≠등 — all three mismatch), so the compound's `kwin` comes out false.
