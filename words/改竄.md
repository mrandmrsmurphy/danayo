## Notes
- [改](../characters/改%20(char).md) "alter; redo; renew" + [竄](../characters/竄.md) "revise; tamper; hide" — stand-in for [[竄]], which cannot appear independently
- 改竄 names the dishonest cousin of ordinary revision: not correcting an error in good faith but secretly doctoring a text, record, or document to make it say something other than what it originally said. The compound carries a built-in note of suspicion — 竄's core sense is "to hide, to conceal, to flee into hiding," so 改竄 is etymologically "altering-by-stealth," distinct from neutral terms like 改正 "correction" or 修正 "amendment." This shading survives intact everywhere the word travels: Sino-Korean 개찬 (gaechan, glossed in Korean dictionaries as deliberately altering a text's wording "to change its meaning") and Japanese かいざん (kaizan) both function as the standard accusation leveled at falsified financial records, tampered evidence, or doctored historical documents — a word reached for in scandal headlines rather than everyday correction.
