## Notes
- [二 (char)](../characters/二%20(char).md) "two" + [重 (char)](../characters/重%20(char).md) "heavy; layer"

Double; consisting of two layers, folds, or repetitions. The character 重 carries the sense of layering and stacking (its base meaning is "heavy," with heaviness conceptualized as multiple things piled on top of each other), making 二重 the natural compound for "two-fold" or "two-layered."

The word is productive in both Chinese and Japanese across many domains:

- **Music:** 二重奏 (duet for instruments), 二重唱 (vocal duet)
- **Optics/Vision:** 二重像 (double image, diplopia)
- **Law/Society:** 二重国籍 (dual citizenship), 二重課税 (double taxation)
- **Anatomy (Japanese only):** ふたえ (futae) — the kun'yomi reading of 二重 — specifically refers to **double eyelids** (二重まぶた), a culturally significant anatomical feature in Japan and Korea. "一重 vs. 二重" (monolid vs. double eyelid) is a common distinction in East Asian beauty discourse.

The Japanese has two readings: にじゅう (nijū, Sino-Japanese, used in most compounds) and ふたえ (futae, native Japanese, used specifically for eyelids and cloth layers).
