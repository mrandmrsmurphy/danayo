## Notes
- [参](../characters/参.md) "participate, join" + [入 (char)](../characters/入%20(char).md) "enter" — to enter and participate; to join a field or market

参入 combines the social act of joining (参) with the spatial act of entering (入): it is not merely stepping into a space but becoming a participant in it. The compound is used most heavily in business and economic contexts — 市場参入 (market entry), 新規参入者 (new market entrant), 参入障壁 (barriers to entry) — where it names the act of a new player entering an established competitive field.

The compound is also used in institutional and political contexts: a country's 参入 into an international agreement, or a new firm's 参入 into a regulated industry. In all uses the emphasis falls on the transition from outside to inside — becoming part of something rather than simply arriving at it. This distinguishes 参入 from 入場 (physical entry into a venue) and 加入 (joining a group or organization as a member).
