## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each; distributive" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name; honorific person classifier"

毎名 is the distributive honorific correlative — "each distinguished person, per honoured person" — the polite counterpart to 毎人. As a 副用名詞 (adverbial noun), it distributes a predicate across each individual member of a respected group, functioning as a frame-setting expression without a case particle: 毎名、御出席ください ("each distinguished person, please attend"). The combination of distributive 毎 and honorific 名 produces a form suited to formal address where individual attention must be distributed across each respected attendee.

Chinese 每位 (měi wèi) is the exact counterpart and is standard in formal written Chinese: 每位嘉賓 ("each distinguished guest"), 每位與會者 ("each esteemed participant"), 每位代表 ("each honoured delegate"). The structure of Mandarin 每位 mirrors Dan'a'yo 毎名 exactly: distributive morpheme + honorific person classifier. Korean 각 분 (gak bun, "each honoured person") uses 각 (各, each/individual) + 분 (polite person suffix). Japanese lacks a direct single-word equivalent; formal public address uses 皆様お一人おひとり ("each and every one of you, distinguished guests") or context-specific honorific constructions.

毎名 occupies the individualised-distributive slot in the 名 row, contrasting with the collective-universal 皆名: 毎名に資料をお渡しする ("pass materials to each distinguished person, one by one") vs 皆名に御礼を申し上げる ("express gratitude to all distinguished persons collectively"). This distributive/collective distinction (毎/皆) runs identically through the neutral 人 row (毎人/皆人) and the honorific 名 row (毎名/皆名), making the register shift from neutral to polite a simple column substitution rather than a separately learned grammatical category.
