## Notes
- [勉 (char)](../characters/勉%20(char).md) "to exert, to urge" + [励](../characters/励.md) "to encourage"

勉励 means "to exhort, to encourage someone to greater effort" — combining two near-synonymous verbs of encouragement into a single emphatic compound. 勉, built from 免 ("to escape, to avoid") plus 力 ("strength, effort"), means "to exert oneself, to make an effort, to urge" — already seen in [[勤勉]] ("hardworking, diligent," cross-linked from [[勉 (char)]]'s Words section).

励, an alias of 勵 (whose phonetic component 厉 gives the modern simplified shape), means simply "to encourage" — its only listed compound, [[激励]] (the character's own stand_in word, "to encourage, to cheer on"), shares this sense directly. 勉励 thus doubles up two verbs of encouragement, much as English sometimes pairs near-synonyms for emphasis ("urge and exhort"); in practice the compound appears most often in formal or written contexts — a teacher's parting words to students, or a leader's exhortation to followers — where its slightly elevated, literary register suits ceremonial encouragement better than the more colloquial [[勤勉]].
