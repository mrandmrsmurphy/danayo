## Notes
**Form.** 雷金 is [雷 (char)](characters/雷%20(char).md) “thunder” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for metals.

**Etymology.** _Thorium_ is named after **Thor**, the Norse god of thunder. The semantic core is therefore “thunder,” not a physical property of the element.

**Motivation.** 雷 is the direct and standard Sinitic equivalent for “thunder.” This is a **clean mythological calque**:

- Thor → thunder deity
- thunder → 雷
- - 金 → element

No indirection or reinterpretation is needed.

**Type of formation.**  
A **direct mythological semantic mapping**, analogous to your stronger cases (e.g., 穀金 for cerium, 護金 for palladium, but with less abstraction).

**Fit within the system.**

1. Fully meaning-based, no phonetic compromise.
2. Uses 金 appropriately for a metal (actinide).
3. Provides a simple, high-frequency, highly recognizable symbol (雷).
4. Preserves the original naming logic exactly.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 钍 (phonetic)
- Japanese: トリウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 토륨 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: thori (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 雷金 restores semantic transparency.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 雷 is unambiguous and vivid.
- Unlike many other elements, the mapping is immediately recoverable even without explanation.

**Conclusion.**  
雷金 is an ideal case in your system: a direct, precise, and transparent semantic rendering of _thorium_ with no compromises required.
