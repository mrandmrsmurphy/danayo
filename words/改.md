## Notes
- [改 (char)](../characters/改%20(char).md) "to change; to redo; to renew"

改 is one of the core Chinese verbs of change and correction. Its graphemic logic is directional: 攵 ("to tap/strike") acts on 己 as the phonetic, together suggesting physical intervention to redirect or correct. In both classical and modern usage, 改 denotes changing something that was wrong or suboptimal — from a prior state toward a corrected one. This distinguishes it from the broader 変 (change as transformation, including neutral or inevitable change) and the neutral 換 (exchange, swap): 改 always implies a prior deficiency being addressed.

In classical Chinese, 改 frequently takes a direct object specifying what is being corrected: 改道 (change course), 改名 (change name), 改元 (change the era name). The phrase 改過自新 — "correct one's faults and renew oneself" — captures the moral dimension that runs through classical uses of 改: self-correction as a virtue. This extends to the political: 改元, the imperial act of renaming a regnal era, was a formal reset, declaring that the old era's failures no longer applied.

In Japanese, 改 (KAI) is extremely productive in compounds — [[改革]] (reform), [[改善]] (improvement), [[改竄]] (falsification) — and also generates the native verb あらためる (aratameru, "to revise; to correct; to renew") and its intransitive あらたまる. The seasonal sense of あらたまった年 ("a year that has renewed itself") extends 改 into cyclical fresh starts, a nuance absent from the more deliberately corrective Mandarin usage.
