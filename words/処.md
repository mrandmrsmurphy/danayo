## Notes
- [処 (char)](../characters/処%20(char).md) "place"

処 is "place" — in Dan'a'yo, 処 functions as a noun for "place; location" and as a correlative row heading generating 此処 (here, lit. this place), 其処 (there, lit. that place), 彼処 (yonder, lit. that far place), 何処 (where?), 某処 (somewhere; a certain place), 皆処 (everywhere). 処 is the shinjitai for 處 (traditional), 处 (simplified), all sharing the meaning "place; to reside; to handle." As a correlative, 処 covers the full spatial deictic series that Japanese encodes as ここ (here), そこ (there), あそこ (yonder), どこ (where). 処 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[処]].

Mandarin 處 (chǔ/chù): 到處 (dàochù, everywhere), 何處 (héchù, where), 住處 (zhùchù, dwelling; residence), 處理 (chǔlǐ, to deal with; to process), 到處都是 (everywhere you look). Cantonese 處 (cyu2): 到處 (dou3 cyu2, everywhere). Japanese 処/處 (ところ, tokoro): the everyday native word for "place; location." The grammatical ところ is indispensable in Japanese: 食べるところ (just about to eat; in the act of eating), ところが (however; but then). The character alone appears in 処分 (しょぶん, punishment; disposal) and 処理 (しょり, handling; processing). Korean 처 (處) in Sino-Korean: 처리 (處理, handling; processing), 처소 (處所, place; location); native 곳 (got, place). Vietnamese xứ (處): xứ sở (land; region; one's native place); modern deictic forms are đây (here), đó (there), đó/kia (yonder), đâu (where).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 초 differs from Korean 처. 処 reads 초 (co/ㄑㄛ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 昌母 (t͡ɕʰ) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ) gives Dan'a'yo ㄑ (ch-) with a plain -o vowel — while Sino-Korean gives 처 (cheo), using the -eo vowel. Note that 処 (ㄑㄛ/co/초) is a Dan'a'yo homophone of [[且]] (ㄑㄛ/co/초, "also; additionally").
