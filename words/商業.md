## Notes
- [商](../characters/商.md) "merchant; to trade; to negotiate; commerce" + [業 (char)](../characters/業%20(char).md) "occupation; industry; enterprise; work"

商業 is "commerce; trade; the commercial sector" — 商 (merchant, buying and selling, to negotiate) + 業 (occupation, industry, the domain of organised activity). The compound designates commercial activity as an institutional domain: buying, selling, and exchange as a sector of the economy, distinct from agriculture (農業), manufacturing (工業), or the service sector. 商業 covers both the abstract concept (commerce as an institution) and the concrete field (the business world, commercial affairs, the retail environment).

Mandarin 商業 (shāngyè) is the standard term for commerce: 商業銀行 (commercial bank), 商業街 (shopping street / commercial district), 商業廣告 (commercial advertisement), 商業模式 (business model). Japanese 商業 (しょうぎょう, shōgyō) is the formal term — 商業高校 (commercial high school), 商業施設 (commercial facility), 商業主義 (commercialism). Korean 상업 (sangeop) is the standard: 상업 지구 (commercial district), 상업적 (commercial, adj.). Vietnamese thương nghiệp is the Sino-Vietnamese compound, used in formal and economic contexts.

The 注音 ㄙ⼘ㄫㄝㄆ reflects the Dan'a'yo readings of the two characters: 商 = ㄙ⼘ㄫ (*syang*) and 業 = ㄝㄆ (*eb*). The 業 reading ㄝㄆ diverges markedly from the Mandarin yè, Japanese gyō/gyou, and Korean eop — each reflecting different phonological reductions of the Middle Chinese syllable. The ROmaji syang'eb uses an apostrophe before 'eb because 業's syllable begins with a vowel (ㄝ), preventing the final consonant of *syang* from being misread as the onset of *eb*.
