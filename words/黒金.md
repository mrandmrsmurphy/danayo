## Notes
- [黒 (char)](../characters/黒%20(char).md) "black" + [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) "metal" — *Hassium* is named after Hesse (German Hessen), the German state where it was discovered at the GSI Helmholtz Centre in Darmstadt. The standard Mandarin place-name transliteration for Hesse, 黑森州, happens to begin with the character for "black," so 黒 here doubles as both a toponymic abbreviation and (coincidentally) the ordinary word for "black" — a half-calque, half-transliteration parallel to 海金 (Neptunium, from 海 "sea" via 海王星) and 冥金 (Plutonium, from 冥 "underworld" via 冥王星). Japanese and Korean instead borrow the IUPAC name as a loanword (ハッシウム, 하슘).
