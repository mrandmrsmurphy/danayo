## Notes
- [収](../characters/収.md) "gather together; collect" + [拾](../characters/拾.md) "pick up"

収拾 is "to tidy up; to sort out; to bring under control; to pick up" — 収 (to gather together; to collect) + 拾 (to pick up). The compound covers both physical and situational domains. In Chinese, 收拾 ranges from physically tidying a room, clearing a table, and packing luggage, to the colloquial sense of dealing with or punishing someone (我來收拾你, I'll deal with you). In Japanese, 収拾 is used almost exclusively for situational control — restoring order to chaos or managing a crisis. 収拾 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for [[拾]], the bound character for "pick up," legitimising it as a free word.

Mandarin 收拾 (shōushí) is colloquial and versatile: 收拾房間 (to tidy the room), 收拾行李 (to pack one's bags), 把他收拾了 (to deal with him — a colloquial threat). Cantonese 收拾 (sau1 sap6) carries the same range. Japanese 収拾 (しゅうしゅう, shūshū) is a formal noun used primarily for restoring order: 事態を収拾する (to bring the situation under control), 収拾がつかない (beyond control; unmanageable — a fixed phrase). Korean 수습 (收拾, susŭp) is the standard term: 사태 수습 (bringing the situation under control), 수습 대책 (countermeasures for managing the situation), 수습 기간 (a probationary/trainee period — an extended sense). Vietnamese thu thập (Sino-Vietnamese: 収=thu, 拾=thập) means "to collect; to gather" — used for gathering information, data, or materials (thu thập thông tin, to collect information).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 숫습 differs from Korean 수습 in the first syllable. 収 reads 숫 (suo/ㄙㄨㄛ) in Dan'a'yo but 수 (su) in Sino-Korean — MC 書母 (ɕ) + 尤韻 (ɨu) gives Dan'a'yo a compound vowel sequence (-uo), preserved in both the Romaji suo and the Hangeul 숫, while Sino-Korean simplifies to the plain vowel 수 (su), retaining only the u of the MC diphthong. The second syllable is identical: 拾 reads 습 (sǝb/ㄙㄜㄆ) in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean.
