## Notes
- [言 (char)](../characters/言%20(char).md) "say" + [及 (char)](../characters/及%20(char).md) "reach out to"

To mention; to refer to; to let one's speech arrive at a topic. The compound literally means "speech reaches" — to 言及 something is to allow one's words to touch upon it, often deliberately and with implication. The image embedded in the word is of speech as a reaching action: words extending until they arrive at the subject.

言及 occupies the formal and literary register in both Chinese and Japanese. Everyday equivalents (言う, 触れる in Japanese; 提到, 说到, 谈及 in Chinese) are more natural in spoken discourse. 言及 appears in academic writing, editorials, legal documents, official statements, and careful prose — contexts where precision about exactly what was said versus implied matters.

The **absence** of 言及 is often as significant as its presence. In political and legal analysis, 言及しなかった (genkyū shinakatta, "did not mention; conspicuously omitted") signals deliberate avoidance. If a politician's statement 言及しなかった nuclear policy, that omission is interpreted; if a ruling 言及しなかった damages, that silence has legal force. 言及 thus frames not only what is said but what one is responsible for having said — and by contrast, what one deliberately chose not to say.

In Korean, **언급** (言及, eon'geup) is a high-frequency formal word in exactly this pattern: 언급하다 ("to mention; to refer to"), 언급되다 ("to be mentioned"), 언급이 없다 ("there is no mention of"). Its everyday Korean counterpart would be 말하다 or 이야기하다, but official discourse defaults to 언급.
