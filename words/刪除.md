## Notes
- [刪](../characters/刪.md) "delete, strike out" + [除](../characters/除.md) "remove, exclude"

刪 is an etymologically vivid character: composed of 冊 (bound bamboo strips, the pre-paper writing medium) and 刂 (knife), it depicts the act of cutting bamboo to erase a character — the original deletion operation. 除 carries a broader sense of exclusion or removal. Together 刪除 means to delete or expunge, and the compound has become the standard digital-age term for deletion in Chinese computing (on menus, in interfaces, and in everyday speech).

In Korean, 산제 (刪除) is the direct Sino-Korean reading of the compound. However, modern Korean strongly prefers 삭제 (削除, sakje) for "delete" — especially in computing contexts — where 削 "to shave, pare" plays the role 刪 does in Chinese. The two compounds are near-synonyms; 刪除 is more literary and 削除 more colloquial in Korean.

In Japanese, 刪 (さん, san) is a relatively uncommon character even in classical usage — 削除 (さくじょ, sakujo) dominates. さんじょ as the reading of 刪除 is derivable but rarely encountered in Japanese texts.
