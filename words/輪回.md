## Notes
- [輪 (char)](../characters/輪%20(char).md) "wheel; ring" + [回 (char)](../characters/回%20(char).md) "turn; revolve"

**輪回** is the East Asian rendering of Sanskrit *saṃsāra* (संसार, "wandering; flowing through") — the endless cycle of birth, death, and rebirth that all sentient beings undergo as long as they remain bound by [[無明]] and craving. The compound captures the metaphor directly: 輪 "wheel" + 回 "revolve" — existence as a wheel that keeps turning, carrying beings from one life to the next with no inherent stopping point.

In Buddhist cosmology, the cycle encompasses all six realms of existence (六道), from the hells at the bottom to the god-realms at the top, with humans occupying a middle position considered particularly conducive to practice and liberation. The goal of Buddhist practice is to escape the wheel entirely — to achieve nirvāṇa (涅槃) and cease turning.

The term is consistent across the CJKV sphere: りんね, 윤회, luân hồi all carry the same doctrinal weight, reflecting the unified transmission of Buddhist teaching through Chinese scriptural translation. The Japanese spelling 輪廻 uses the graphic variant 廻 for 回, emphasising the sense of circular motion; the Chinese forms 輪迴 and 轮回 use the standard 迴/回.
