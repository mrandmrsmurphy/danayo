## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

此様 is the proximal manner correlative — "this way; like this; in this fashion." Where 此処 (here) and 此時 (now) locate an event spatially and temporally, 此様 characterises the manner of an action relative to the speech situation: "in the way I am currently demonstrating, in the manner at hand." The character 様 (Dan'a'yo *yang*, ⼘ㄫ) covers manner, way, style, and appearance — a broad semantic range shared by Mandarin 樣, Japanese 様, and Korean 양 — and combined with proximal 此, it means "like this, in this very way."

Cross-linguistic parallels are close: Mandarin 這樣 (zhèyàng, "like this, this way") is extremely common in everyday speech; the 這/那 + 樣 pattern mirrors Dan'a'yo's 此/其/彼 + 様 in structure if not in exact deictic granularity. Cantonese 噉樣 (gam2 joeng6, "like this/that way") or the shortened 噉 (gam2) is the colloquial form. Japanese このように (kono yō ni, "in this way") is the adverbial phrase equivalent; informal speech uses こうやって (kō yatte, "doing it this way"). Korean 이렇게 (ireoke, "like this, in this way") is the proximal manner adverb of the 이렇게/그렇게/저렇게 set.

The 様 column is the manner row of the correlative grid — the row that answers "how?" rather than "where?", "when?", or "who?" All seven 様-column entries are 副用名詞 (adverbial nouns functioning as manner adverbs), unlike the nominal and pronominal entries of earlier rows. In natural languages, manner deixis is often grammatically compressed (Mandarin has 這樣/那樣 without a distal distinction; Vietnamese như thế này/như thế đó collapses to two), so Dan'a'yo's three-way deictic manner paradigm (此様/其様/彼様) is more systematic than any individual source language.
