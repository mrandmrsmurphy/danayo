## Notes
- [截](../characters/截.md) "cut off, stop, obstruct" + [取](../characters/取.md) "take"
- This word is the stand-in for [[截]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- To cut off a portion and take it — to extract by severing, or to intercept in transit. In Chinese, 截取 (jiéqǔ) is used in computing for "screenshot" or "clip" (截取画面 "to capture a screen"), in logistics for "intercept a shipment," and in textual contexts for "excerpt." In Japanese, せっしゅ (sesshu) appears in similar technical senses, as well as in the concept of 截取地域 (sesshu chiiki, "interception zone"). Korean 절취 (截取) is the standard term for clipping, extracting, or tearing off — appearing in contexts like 쿠폰을 절취하시오 "please tear off the coupon."
