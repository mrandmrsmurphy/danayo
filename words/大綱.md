## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "large; main; primary" + [綱](../characters/綱.md) "main rope; guiding cord; general framework"

大綱 is "outline; general framework; main points" — 大 (large, main, primary) + 綱 (the main rope or cord of a net, metaphorically extended to "guiding principle; main heading; general framework"). 綱 literally names the principal rope from which netting hangs — the line that holds everything else together. Metaphorically extended in classical Chinese to mean the top-level structure organising everything beneath it: 三綱 (the Three Cardinal Guides in Confucian ethics), 綱目 (headings and subheadings in a text). 大綱 combines these: the primary framework, the top-level outline.

Mandarin 大綱 (dàgāng) is the standard term for an outline or syllabus: 課程大綱 (course syllabus/curriculum outline), 政策大綱 (policy framework). Cantonese 大綱 (daai6 gong1) in formal and educational contexts. Japanese 大綱 (たいこう, taikō) means general framework or broad outline: 大綱的な方針 (policy in broad outline). Korean 대강 (daeggang) covers both noun (an outline) and adverb (roughly; generally; in broad strokes): 대강 알다 (to know roughly), 대강을 잡다 (to grasp the outline). Vietnamese đại cương is the standard term for outline or course syllabus.

大綱 is a homophone of [[大江]] — "great river" — sharing the same 注音 ㄉㄚㄧㄍㄚㄫ, 諺文 대강, and ROmaji *daigang*. In Korean, 대강 (大綱, rough outline) and 대강 (大江, great river) are distinguished in writing by their characters but pronounced identically. This homophony is a natural consequence of the limited syllable inventory shared across CJKV languages — Sinographic writing disambiguates by character, context disambiguates in speech. kwin: true because Dan'a'yo 대강 exactly matches Korean 대강.
