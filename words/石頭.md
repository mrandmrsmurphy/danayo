## Notes
- [石 (char)](../characters/石%20(char).md) "rock" + [頭 (char)](../characters/頭%20(char).md) "head" — literally "stone-head"; an independent compound (both constituents already stand alone as their own `stand_in`)

**Real content bug corrected**: the stored `english` gloss was "stubborn person," which mismatched the actual primary meaning of this Swadesh-list entry (#156, "stone"). Mandarin shítou and Cantonese sek6 tau4 are simply the ordinary, everyday word for "a stone, a rock" — no figurative connotation required. Vietnamese đá likewise names plain "stone" (a native word, not Sino-Vietnamese).

**A genuine, striking semantic divergence, not an error**: Japanese いしあたま (ishiatama) and Korean 석두 (seokdu) — built from the identical characters — do *not* mean "stone" at all in those languages; both are real, idiomatic words for a stubborn or thick-headed person (verified via search: 석두 is glossed directly as "a contemptuous term for an extremely stupid or inflexibly stubborn person," parallel to the fully native Korean synonym 돌대가리, "stone-head"). Japanese's and Korean's own literal words for "stone" are instead 石 (いし) and 돌 respectively. So the same two characters, in the same order, denote the concrete object in Chinese and Vietnamese but a figurative insult in Japanese and Korean — kept both fields as their real, attested values rather than forcing false symmetry.
