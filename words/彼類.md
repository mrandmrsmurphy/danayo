## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [類](../characters/類.md) "kind; sort; category; type"

彼類 is the distal kind/category correlative — "that other kind; that sort (remote or unfamiliar)." Like 彼処 and 彼時, 彼類 refers to a category that is socially, culturally, or contextually remote — a type associated with another sphere, era, or group. Where 其類 is the kind already in conversation, 彼類 is the kind observed from outside or looked back upon with distance: 彼類の習慣は理解しにくい ("customs of that other kind are hard to understand"); 彼類の思想はもう廃れた ("ideas of that sort have already died out").

Japanese ああいう (ā iu, "that sort — distal; often with a note of wonder, distance, or retrospective assessment") or あのような (ano yō na, "like that — distal"). Korean 저런 종류 (jeoreon jongryu, "that kind — distal") or 저런 alone — the distal member of the 이런/그런/저런 deictic kind-triad, implying a category not in the speaker's or addressee's immediate sphere. Vietnamese loại kia (that kind over there — distal) uses the distal demonstrative kia.

Korean is the only CJKV language that provides a clean three-way deictic kind-demonstrative system (이런/그런/저런 = this kind/that kind/that other kind) matching Dan'a'yo's 此類/其類/彼類 precisely. Japanese has a similar three-way system with この/その/あの + ような/いう. Mandarin collapses all three into the 這種/那種 binary. This makes Korean and Japanese the most direct cross-linguistic parallels to the 類 row's full three-way deictic contrast.
