## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "obey, follow" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "thing, action, affair"

従事 means to engage in or be occupied with a particular work, calling, or field of activity — to follow the path of a given profession or duty. The compound is formal and applies to sustained engagement rather than a single act: 農業に従事する ("to be engaged in farming"), 教育に従事する ("to work in education"). The 従 (follow, be in accordance with) positions the subject as someone who has committed to a course of activity and proceeds along it consistently.

In Chinese (从事, cóngshì), the simplified form 从事 is the standard written form and appears widely in official, legal, and formal registers. The compound is productive in modern Chinese: 从事人员 ("engaged personnel"), 从事活动 ("to conduct activities"). In Japanese (じゅうじ, jūji), 従事する is a formal suru verb used in official writing, news reporting, and workplace contexts. In Korean (종사), the Sino-Korean reading is used in formal speech; 종사하다 is the verb form.
