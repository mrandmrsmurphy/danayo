## Notes
- [連 (char)](../characters/連%20(char).md) "link, connect" + [帯 (char)](../characters/帯%20(char).md) "belt, zone, carry" — joined together as if by a single belt: solidarity, or a shared, joint bond of responsibility.

The word carries two related senses across its source languages: a social/political one (連帯 as fellow-feeling and mutual support, as in labor or protest movements) and a legal one (連帯責任, "joint liability," where multiple parties are bound together as if by one obligation). The grammar book uses 連帯 in a third, specifically grammatical sense, glossing it as the effect of the suffix 等 when it marks a [[連帯]][[対象]] — extending a noun phrase to include others associated with the named referent, rather than (as a [[単純]] [[複数]] marker would) simply pluralizing it. The Vietnamese reflex liên đới is itself most often encountered in precisely the legal "joint liability" sense. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
