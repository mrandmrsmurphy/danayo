## Notes
- [優](../characters/優.md) "excellent, superior" + [等 (char)](../characters/等%20(char).md) "grade, rank" — of excellent grade; honors-level

優等 names the top band of a graded system. 優等生 (honor student, student of excellent standing) is the most common compound form in Japanese and Korean; 優等賞 (excellence award) and 優等卒業 (graduation with honors) follow the same pattern. The word functions as a straightforward positive evaluation: to achieve 優等 is to be recognized as above the middle range and approaching the best.

優 itself carries a double meaning — excellent and gentle/gracious (the actor's 優 in 俳優 is related) — but in 優等 only the evaluative sense is active. The antonym is 劣等, and the pair 優等/劣等 frames performance, ability, or quality as a spectrum with clear upper and lower ends.
