## Notes
- [懶](../characters/懶.md) "lazy" + [惰](../characters/惰.md) "lazy" — synonym compound, both roots independently meaning "lazy."

A consistent colloquial-vs-formal split runs across three of the four languages. In Mandarin, 懶惰 (lǎnduò) is the ordinary, everyday word for "lazy," while its near-synonym [[怠惰]] (dàiduò) is the more literary, written-register form — both real and common, but 懶惰 is what shows up in casual conversation. Korean shows the same pattern with different roots entirely: 게으르다 (a native adjective) is the everyday word for "lazy," while Sino-Korean 나타 (matching 懶惰's own reading) is markedly more formal/literary. Vietnamese lười biếng is likewise the standard, everyday term, with the more literal Sino-Vietnamese compound lãn đoạ existing but rarely used in speech. Japanese らんだ (the correct reading — らいだ is a documented historical misreading) doesn't show as sharp a register split from its own near-synonym 怠惰/たいだ; dictionaries generally treat the two as interchangeable.
