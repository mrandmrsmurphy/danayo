## Notes
- [甘 (char)](../characters/甘%20(char).md) "sweet" + [蕉](../characters/蕉.md) "banana; plantain" — "the sweet banana/plantain"

甘蕉 (*gānjiāo*) is a classical and literary term for the banana or plantain, distinguished from [[芭蕉]] and [[香蕉]] primarily by register and connotation rather than by sharp botanical boundary — see [[芭蕉]] for the three-way comparison. Where 香蕉 (*xiāngjiāo*, "fragrant banana") is the plain modern term for the common edible banana, and 芭蕉 names the fibrous, more ornamental Musa basjoo associated with Bashō, 甘蕉 — "sweet banana/plantain" — is an older, more literary designation found in classical texts and botanical nomenclature, emphasising the fruit's sweetness as its defining quality.

The character 蕉 itself denotes the banana/plantain plant generally and appears as the head of all three compounds; the qualifying first element (香 "fragrant," 芭 a transliteration element associated with Bashō's poetic name, or 甘 "sweet") shifts the register and the specific referent. 甘蕉 in particular is the term most likely to be encountered in pre-modern agricultural and medical texts (where banana/plantain parts were used in traditional pharmacology) and in poetry, where its archaic flavour suits a more elevated register than the everyday 香蕉.

Japanese かんしょう (*kanshō*) is recorded as an obsolete literary alternative for バナナ (*banana*, the now-standard loanword), paralleling the situation of 甘蕉 in Mandarin: a once-current term displaced by either a native/loanword (バナナ) or a more transparent modern compound (香蕉) in everyday usage, surviving mainly in literary and historical contexts.
