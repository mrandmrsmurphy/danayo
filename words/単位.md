## Notes
- [単](../characters/単.md) "single, simple" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, place"

単位 is the everyday word for "unit" in the broadest sense — a unit of measurement (meters, kilograms, yen), a unit in an organization, or, in Japanese and Korean academic usage, a unit of academic credit earned toward a degree (the very credits that accumulate toward a [[学位]], earlier in this batch). 単 is the shinjitai simplification of 單/单 — a 象形 character whose traditional form depicts a forked hunting tool — that in modern usage means "single, simple, mere," already seen in [[単独]] ("alone, solo") and [[単刀直入]] ("to go straight to the point," both cross-linked from [[単]]'s Chengyu section).

単位 literally combines "single [thing]" with 位 ("place, slot") — a single, standardized slot into which quantities are divided for counting and comparison. The word sits naturally alongside [[単身]] ("loneliness, singleness") in 単's compound family: both extend 単's core sense of "one, alone" into more abstract domains, one into solitude and the other into measurement.
