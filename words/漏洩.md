## Notes
- [漏 (char)](../characters/漏%20(char).md) "leak, drip" + [洩](../characters/洩.md) "vent, leak out"

漏洩 pairs two characters that both independently mean "to leak," giving the compound a doubled, intensified sense — most often used not for liquids but for information: 情報漏洩 (information leak), 秘密の漏洩 (disclosure of a secret), 機密漏洩 (leak of classified material). 漏 itself is built around 屚 (a roof, 尸, with rain, 雨, falling through it — a literal leaking house) plus the water radical, and survives independently in [[漏]]'s cross-linked compound [[滲漏]] ("seepage, leakage").

洩 is one of three graphic variants for this word — 洩, 泄, and 渫 are all listed as aliases of the same character in [[洩]]'s frontmatter, reflecting how often the water radical's exact placement and the phonetic component varied across regional and historical orthographies for words meaning "to discharge, let out, vent." 漏泄, built on the alternate form 泄, is recorded here as a word-level alias — the same character-variation pattern documented for [[陶汰]] and [[肥脊]] elsewhere in this batch, but in this case both forms remain common enough in different regions/registers that neither displaces the other as "canonical."

The Cantonese reading lau6 sit3 (using the 泄/sit3 reading rather than 洩's own jai6) and the secondary Japanese reading ろうせつ (rōei is more common) both reflect this same variant-character ambiguity at the level of the compound word.
