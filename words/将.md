## Notes
- [将 (char)](../characters/将%20(char).md) "will; shall; prospective"

将 is the prospective aspect marker in Dan'a'yo — "will; shall; about to" — prefixing to a verb to mark a future or imminent action: 将去 (will go; about to go), 将成 (will become; shall achieve). Among the Dan'a'yo aspect markers, 将 marks the prospective — the action is anticipated and has not yet occurred — complementing [[已]] (realized/perfective) and [[未]] (not yet; unrealized negative). In classical Chinese, 将 (OC *tsaŋ) served as a prospective marker (将来 "future; about to come"; 将然 "prospective aspect; about to happen") as well as a noun meaning "military commander" — both functions survive in Dan'a'yo compounds. 将 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[将]].

The comparison chart: Mandarin 将/将要 (jiāng/jiāngyào, will; about to), 要 (yào, will; going to — volitional future). Cantonese 會/將會 (wui6/zoeng1 wui6, will; shall). Japanese 〜ようとする (yō to suru, be about to do; intend to), 〜だろう (darō, will probably — inferential future). Korean 〜을/ㄹ 것이다 (-eul/l geot-ida, will; future tense), 〜겠- (-gess-, prospective/future modal: 가겠다 will go; 먹겠다 will eat). Vietnamese sẽ (will; shall — future preverbal marker: sẽ đi will go; sẽ ăn will eat), sắp (about to; imminently).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 좡 does not match Korean 장. 将 reads 좡 (jwang/ㄐ⺢ㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 精母 (t͡s, alveolar affricate) + 陽開韻 (ɨɐŋ). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean map MC 精母 to a sibilant-palatal initial (Dan'a'yo ㄐ j-; Korean ㅈ j-). The MC final ɨɐŋ: Dan'a'yo renders it as -uang/⺢ㄫ (좡, preserving the -u- medial as ㅘ in Hangul), while Korean gives plain -ang (장, medial dropped). Dan'a'yo 좡 (j-wa-ng) retains the MC round medial; Korean 장 (j-a-ng) loses it. The same medial-preservation pattern appears in [[全]] (줜/jwen vs 전/jeon) where Dan'a'yo keeps the MC -w- but Korean drops it.
