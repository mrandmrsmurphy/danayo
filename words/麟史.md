## Notes
- [麟](../characters/麟.md) "qilin (mythical creature)" + [史](../characters/史.md) "history; chronicle" — "the Unicorn History"

麟史 (*Línshǐ*, "the Unicorn History") is, like [[麟経]], a reverential alternative name for the [[春秋]] (*Spring and Autumn Annals*), drawing on the same allusion to the *qilin* recorded in the chronicle's final entry — see [[麟経]] for the shared background. Where 麟経 frames the text as a 経 ("classic, scripture," placing it among the canonical works of the Confucian tradition), 麟史 frames it as a 史 ("history, chronicle"), emphasising its character as a historical record of the state of Lu rather than its status as scripture. The two names are functionally interchangeable in literary usage.

The Korean form 인사 shows the same 두음법칙 (*dueum beopchik*) shift as [[麟経]]: 麟 is read 린 in medial position but 인 word-initially. Note that 인사 is also the standard Korean word for "greeting" (人事), an unrelated homophone — context disambiguates the two in practice, and 麟史 in this sense is confined to specialist discussion of the Confucian classics.
