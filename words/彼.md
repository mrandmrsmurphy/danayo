## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; that (far)"

彼 is "yon" — the distal demonstrative in Dan'a'yo's three-way system: 此 (near) vs. 其 (middle) vs. 彼 (far). 彼 marks entities remote from both speaker and addressee, corresponding to Japanese あの/あれ (ano/are, that over there) and Korean 저 (jeo-, that yonder). In classical Chinese, 彼 is a demonstrative and third-person pronoun meaning "that; he/she/it (over there)": 彼此 (this and that; one another; each other). Dan'a'yo preserves this distal value as a productive modifier across the correlative chart: 彼事 (that far matter), 彼処 (that place; yonder). 彼 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[彼]].

Mandarin 彼 (bǐ) is literary: 彼此 (each other; one another), 彼岸 (the far bank; the other shore; in Buddhism, the realm of nirvana beyond suffering). Colloquial Mandarin uses 那 (nà) for the distal demonstrative. Cantonese 彼 (bei2): literary; colloquial uses 嗰 (go2, "that over there"). Japanese 彼 (かれ, kare) has shifted in modern usage to mean "he; him; boyfriend"; 彼女 (かのじょ, kanojo) "she; girlfriend"; the original distal demonstrative sense survives in literary contexts and in 彼岸 (ひがん, the opposite shore; the equinoctial week). Korean 피 (彼) in Sino-Korean; native 저/그 for distal demonstratives. Vietnamese bỉ (彼): Sino-Vietnamese literary usage.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 브 differs from Korean 피. 彼 reads 브 (bǝ/ㄅㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 幫母 (p) + 支B三開韻 (ɣiᴇ) gives Dan'a'yo an unaspirated labial with the schwa vowel (-ǝ, ㄜ) — while Sino-Korean gives 피 (pi), using the aspirated labial ㅍ (ph-) and the -i vowel. Both the consonant aspiration and the vowel quality diverge between the two systems.
