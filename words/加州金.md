## Notes
- [加](../characters/加.md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) "state" + [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) "metal" — *Californium* is named directly after the state of California (and the University of California, Berkeley), where the element was discovered. 加州 is the common Mandarin abbreviation of 加利福尼亞州 "California," used as a calque rather than a phonetic transliteration, in the same spirit as 美洲金 (Americium). Japanese and Korean instead borrow the IUPAC name as a loanword (カリホルニウム, 칼리포르늄).
