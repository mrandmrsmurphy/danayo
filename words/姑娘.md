- [姑](../characters/姑.md) "paternal aunt" + [娘 (char)](../characters/娘%20(char).md) "young woman" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (姑's is 姑母, 娘's is bare 娘). `kwin: true` per the AND-rule, since both are individually `kwin: true`.
- **Gloss broadened**: `english` previously read only "bachelorette," a narrow gloss (English "bachelorette" specifically evokes pre-wedding parties) — corrected to lead with the actual primary sense, "girl; young lady," the everyday informal-but-polite address term for a young unmarried woman (verified via search).
- Filled previously-blank `korean` (고낭, a real dictionary word — verified via search) and `vietnamese` (cô nương, a real, well-attested term especially familiar from wuxia/kiếm hiệp novel translations, more archaic/literary than the everyday cô gái). Japanese クーニャン is a direct katakana loanword from Mandarin itself (not a native on'yomi/kun'yomi reading) — genuine and attested, though it also has a secondary, unrelated pop-culture sense as a peach-oolong cocktail name in Hokkaido.
