## Notes
- [縫](../characters/縫.md) "sew; mend" + [製](../characters/製.md) "manufacture; produce" — stand-in for [[縫]], which cannot appear independently

Japanese ほうせい (hōsei) and Korean 봉제 (bongje) are both the standard trade/industry term for "sewing, garment manufacture" (縫製工場/봉제공장, "sewing factory"). Mandarin féngzhì is likewise a real, if more technical/industrial, term next to the everyday verb 縫/缝 alone. Vietnamese phùng chế is an honest compositional reading — the related compound phùng công (縫工, "seamstress/tailor") is independently attested, but phùng chế itself wasn't found confirmed in the same way — while the everyday and industry-standard Vietnamese vocabulary uses native may ("to sew") and may mặc ("garment industry").
