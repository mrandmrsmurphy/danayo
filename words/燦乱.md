## Notes
- [燦](../characters/燦.md) "bright; resplendent" + [乱](../characters/乱.md) "in profusion; overflowing" (here standing in for 爛, "brilliant; dazzling," a meaning preserved in this word though 乱's everyday sense is "chaotic, disordered") — stand-in for [[燦]], which cannot appear independently
- 燦乱 doubles up on brilliance, pairing two characters that both gravitate toward "shining splendor" to produce the standard Sinospheric word for a dazzling, resplendent radiance — sunlight glittering on water, a night sky strewn with stars, a smile lighting up a face. Its form preserves a small fossil of script history: the second element is properly 爛 "brilliant, glowing-hot" (related to 爛漫 "glorious, in full bloom"), a character whose meaning has nothing to do with 乱 "disorder, chaos" despite the graphic resemblance and shared Middle Chinese rhyme that eventually collapsed the two into homophones — and in simplified writing, into the same glyph. The compound's reading and meaning ride essentially unchanged across the whole region: Mandarin cànlàn, Cantonese caan3 laan6, Sino-Korean 찬란 (challan), Sino-Vietnamese xán lạn, and Japanese さんらん (sanran), each retaining the older "brilliant" sense that the bare glyph 乱/爛 has since lost in ordinary speech.
