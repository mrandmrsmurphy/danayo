## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "-ing; progressive; in the midst of"

中 is the progressive aspect marker in Dan'a'yo — "-ing; in the process of; currently doing" — postfixing to a verb to mark an action currently underway: 食中 (eating; in the process of eating), 行中 (going; en route; currently walking), 学中 (studying; in the process of studying). Among the Dan'a'yo aspect markers, 中 marks the imperfective present — an action in progress — complementing [[已]] (completed) and [[将]] (prospective). The character originally depicted a flagpole planted at the center of a field (or a drum atop a pole), giving the primary meaning "center; middle; midst" — the progressive function extends naturally from "in the middle of" an action. 中 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[中]].

The comparison chart: Mandarin 在/正在 (zài/zhèngzài, currently doing; in the process of — pre-verbal progressive marker: 正在吃 is eating; 在写 is writing). Cantonese 緊 (gan2, progressive suffix on verb: 食緊 eating; 做緊 doing), 喺...度 (hai2...dou6, is at...doing — locative progressive). Japanese 〜ている (te iru, progressive or resultant state: 食べている is eating; 行っている has gone and is there), 〜つつある (tsutsu aru, in the process of — literary progressive). Korean 〜고 있다 (-go ita, progressive: 먹고 있다 is eating; 가고 있다 is going). Vietnamese đang (pre-verbal progressive marker: đang ăn eating; đang đi going; đang học studying).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 중 exactly matches Korean 중. 中 reads 중 (jung/ㄐㄨㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 知母 (ʈ, retroflex dental stop) + 東三韻 (ɨuŋ). Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map the retroflex initial ʈ to a palatal/alveolar j- (Dan'a'yo ㄐ, Korean ㅈ) and MC 東三韻 to plain -ung (중 in both). The 東三韻 is among the most consistently preserved MC finals in both systems — a simple -ung coda — producing identical 중 forms. A phonologically parallel kwin: true case is [[公]] (gong/공), another simple nasal-final rhyme mapping identically across both systems.
