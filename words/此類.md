## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [類](../characters/類.md) "kind; sort; category; type"

此類 is the proximal kind/category correlative — "this kind; this sort; this type of thing." Where 此物 points to a specific object and 此事 points to a specific matter, 此類 points to the entire category or kind that the referent belongs to: "the type we're dealing with right now." In practical use: 此類の問題は多い ("there are many problems of this kind"); 此類の食べ物が好きだ ("I like food of this type").

Mandarin 這種 (zhèzhǒng, "this kind/sort") is the standard everyday form; 這類 (zhèlèi, "this category") is slightly more formal and closely mirrors the Dan'a'yo composition 此 + 類. Cantonese 呢種 (ni1 zung2, "this kind") is the colloquial equivalent. Japanese こういう (kō iu, "this sort of; of this kind") or このような (kono yō na, "like this; of this nature") are the standard adnominal forms. Korean 이런 종류 (ireon jongryu, "this kind/sort") or 이런 alone as an adnominal ("this kind of") — the proximal member of the 이런/그런/저런 deictic triad.

The 類 row is the kind/category row of the correlative grid — the row that answers "what sort of thing?" across all seven columns. 類 (ㄌㄨㄧ, *lui*) does not follow the `(char)` file naming convention; its character entry lives at `characters/類.md`. Unlike the adverbial 様 and 多 rows, 類-row words are 代詞 — they substitute for category-denoting noun phrases, paralleling the 事/物/人/名 rows rather than the adverbial rows.
