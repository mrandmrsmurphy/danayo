## Notes
- [無 (char)](../characters/無%20(char).md) "lack; without" + [形 (char)](../characters/形%20(char).md) "form; shape; manifestation"

**無形** (*wúxíng*) denotes the absence of form — that which has no shape, outline, or perceptible physical manifestation. The compound operates across three distinct registers: Daoist metaphysics, Buddhist phenomenology, and ordinary modern usage, with a consistent core meaning but importantly different emphases in each.

In **Daoist thought**, 無形 is among the defining attributes of the Dao itself. Chapter 41 of the *Laozi* contains the celebrated line 大象無形 (*dà xiàng wú xíng*, "the great image has no form"), paired with 大音希聲 ("the great sound is barely audible"). The Dao, as the source and ground of all formed things, cannot itself be enclosed in any particular form; it is the formless from which all forms arise. This is not mere invisibility but an ontological priority — the Dao precedes and exceeds whatever shape or outline might be assigned to it. Compare [[無名]] ("without name"), the companion negation that also appears in the *Laozi*'s opening.

In **Buddhist phenomenology**, 無形 indexes the distinction between material and non-material dharmas — phenomena with and without physical form. This shades into the better-known compound [[無色]] (*arūpa* in Sanskrit/Pali), which specifically negates 色 (*rūpa*, material form/colour), as in the 無色界 (arūpa-dhātu, the formless realm, highest of the three planes of existence). 無形 is broader: it encompasses any phenomenon — including mental events, sound, and intention — that lacks spatial outline. In Chan (禅) literature, the phrases 無形無相 ("formless and without characteristics") and 無形之法 ("dharmas without shape") describe the Buddha-nature as it transcends all fixable attributes.

In **ordinary modern usage**, 無形 functions as an attributive or adverbial: 無形資産 (*wúxíng zīchǎn*, "intangible assets" — intellectual property, goodwill, brand value); 無形中 ("imperceptibly; without one's realizing"); 無形的壓力 ("invisible pressure"). The invisible hand of the market is a 無形之手. In this register 無形 means simply *not having a physical, graspable form* — an intangible that nonetheless has real effects.

Japanese 無形 (*mukei*) is the standard term in 無形文化財 (intangible cultural heritage, the UNESCO category encompassing performing arts, oral traditions, and crafts), and 無形資産 mirrors the Chinese accounting usage. Korean 무형 (*muhyeong*) follows the same pattern — 무형문화재 is the standard Korean-language term for UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage. Vietnamese *vô hình* is the Sino-Vietnamese form and is widely used in everyday speech with the plain sense of "invisible; unseen."
