## Notes
- [早 (char)](../characters/早%20(char).md) "early" + [飯](../characters/飯.md) "cooked rice; food" — literally "early meal." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (早's own points to bare `早`, 飯's own to `米飯`); 早飯 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/早 (char).md` and `characters/飯.md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **A genuine sense divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: Mandarin zǎofàn and Cantonese zou2 faan6 are everyday, standard words for "breakfast." Korean 조반 is likewise a real word for "breakfast," though more formal/literary than the everyday native 아침밥. Japanese はやめし (verified via search), however, does *not* mean "breakfast" at all — it means either "a fast eater" or, less commonly, "an early meal" eaten ahead of the usual time; the standard Japanese word for "breakfast" is 朝ご飯/朝食 (asagohan/chōshoku). Vietnamese does not use a Sino-Vietnamese calque — the real, standard term is the native bữa sáng/ăn sáng; no attested reading of 早飯 is in actual use, so `vietnamese` is left blank rather than fabricated.
