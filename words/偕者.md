## Notes
- [偕](../characters/偕.md) "accompany, be with" + [者 (char)](../characters/者%20(char).md) "one who; -er" — "the one who is with (you)"; the Dan'a'yo semantic translation of the divine name יַהְוֶה (YHWH).

偕者 is the Dan'a'yo rendering of the personal, covenantal name of the God of Israel — the name conventionally represented as LORD in English Bibles and 耶和華 in Chinese phonetic transliteration. Dan'a'yo does not transliterate where a semantic rendering is possible; 偕者 grounds the name in the theology of divine presence rather than in sound.

The name is revealed in Exodus 3 in two stages. First God promises Moses: «אֶהְיֶה עִמָּךְ» — "I will **be with** you" (3:12). Then, when Moses asks for the name, God says: «אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה» — "I AM WHO I AM" (3:14), and then gives the name יהוה as the form by which he is to be remembered forever (3:15). 偕者 — "the one who accompanies" — reads the name in light of that prior promise: YHWH is the God whose name is inseparable from his commitment to be present with his people. 偕 (accompany, be together with) + 者 (nominalizer: the one who) yields a name that is simultaneously a promise and an identity.

Dan'a'yo distinguishes three divine names where most translations use only two:
- [[上帝]] — renders אֱלֹהִים (Elohim): the transcendent, universal God; Creator
- [[天主]] — renders אֲדֹנָי (Adonai): sovereign Lord; the title used when YHWH is read aloud in Jewish practice
- 偕者 — renders יהוה (YHWH): the personal, covenantal name; the God who accompanies

**Theophoric names:** in compound proper names, 偕 appears as the theophoric suffix — the bound "-yah/-iah" element — paralleling Hebrew theophoric patterns where the divine name comes last:
- [[救偕]] — Jesus (יֵשׁוּעַ, Yeshua): "YHWH saves"
- [[助偕]] — Eleazar (אֶלְעָזָר): "God has helped"
- [[誰偕]] — Micah (מִיכָה): "Who is like YHWH?"

Note: the Japanese on-yomi reading かいしゃ is homophonous with 会社 (company). This is a coincidence of phonology and carries no semantic interference in Dan'a'yo context.
