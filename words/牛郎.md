## Notes
- [牛 (char)](../characters/牛%20(char).md) "ox; cow" + [郎](../characters/郎.md) "young man" — "the Cowherd Boy"

牛郎 (*Niúláng*) is the Cowherd Boy of the romance with [[織女]] (the Weaver Girl), one of the most widely told love stories in the Sinosphere. In the standard form of the legend, 牛郎 is a poor orphan herder who lives with an old ox; the ox, in some versions revealed to be a transformed celestial being, helps him meet and marry 織女, a weaver-goddess who has descended to earth. When the Queen Mother of the West (西王母) discovers that a mortal has married a goddess, she forcibly separates the couple, placing them on opposite banks of the Milky Way (天河). They are permitted to reunite only once a year, on the seventh night of the seventh lunar month — the festival of [[七夕]] — when a flock of magpies forms a bridge (鵲橋) across the river so the lovers can cross.

Astronomically, 牛郎 gives his name to [[牛郎星]] (Altair), the star representing him in the night sky, with the two smaller stars flanking it traditionally read as the couple's two children, carried on his shoulder pole as he crosses the river to meet 織女 (Vega) on the opposite side of the Milky Way.

The tale exists in cognate forms throughout East and Southeast Asia: Japan's *Tanabata* (七夕) celebrates the same reunion under the names *Hikoboshi* (彦星, the Cowherd) and *Orihime* (織姫, the Weaver Princess); Korea's *Chilseok* (칠석) uses the directly cognate names 견우 (*Gyeonu*, from 牽牛, "ox-leader" — an older form of the cowherd's name) and 직녀 (*Jiknyeo*, [[織女]]); and Vietnam's *Thất Tịch* preserves Ngưu Lang and Chức Nữ by their Sino-Vietnamese names.
