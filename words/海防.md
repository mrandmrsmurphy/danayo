## Notes
- [海](../characters/海.md) "sea" + [防](../characters/防.md) "defend, prevent"

海防 refers to the defense of a coastline — the military, naval, and fortification systems by which a maritime nation protects its shores from attack or infiltration. The compound is used in historical writing (Ming and Qing coastal defense against wokou pirates and Western navies), in modern military doctrine, and in place names.

Most notably, 海防 is the Chinese name of Haiphong (Vietnamese: Hải Phòng), Vietnam's third-largest city and principal northern port. The city's name records the same compound in its Hán-Việt form: hải (海, sea) + phòng (防, defense) — named for its strategic role guarding the approaches to Hanoi via the Red River Delta. In Japanese (かいぼう, kaibō), the term appears in Meiji-era military history when coastal defense became a central concern during Japan's opening to the West.

In Korean, 해방 (海防) is a near-homophone of 해방 (解放, liberation/emancipation), the word most strongly associated in Korean cultural memory with Korea's liberation from Japanese colonial rule in 1945. Context always disambiguates, but the overlap gives 海防 an unusual resonance in Korean.
