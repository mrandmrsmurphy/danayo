## Notes
- [鄭](../characters/鄭.md) "Zheng (surname; place name)" + [国](../characters/国.md) "state; country" — stand-in for [[鄭]], which cannot appear independently
- 鄭国 names one of the lesser vassal states of the Zhou dynasty (806–375 BC), centered near present-day Zhengzhou and Xinzheng in Henan, ruled by a branch of the royal Ji (姬) clan until its conquest by 韓. Though militarily minor, it left an outsized mark on the literary record — the 詩経 preserves an entire section of "Airs of Zheng" (鄭風), and the surname 鄭 it bequeathed remains one of the most common in the Sinosphere today, carried by well over twelve million people. The Japanese reading ていこく (teikoku) is a curious homophone of 帝国 "empire," a coincidence worth noting for any reader encountering the name in isolation; Korean more often calls the state by its native name 정나라 ("Jeong country"), reserving the Sino-Korean compound 정국 (jeongguk) for more formal or historiographical contexts.
