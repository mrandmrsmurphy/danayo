## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

某事 is the indefinite correlative in the action column: it refers to an action or matter deliberately left unspecified — the speaker either does not know what it is, or knows but declines to say. The character 某 (mǒu in Mandarin, Dan'a'yo *mou*) is the classical Chinese indefinite determiner, functioning like English "a certain" or "some unspecified": 某日 (a certain day), 某地 (some location), 某人 (a certain person). Unlike the interrogative 何 (which asks for identification) and the universal 皆 (which asserts totality), 某 leaves the referent deliberately vague — its referent exists but is being withheld or anonymised.

In Mandarin, 某事 (mǒushì) is a productive formal expression: 某事未竟 "some matter is unfinished"; 因某事缺席 "absent due to a certain matter." The indefinite 某 is pan-CJKV: Japanese 某 (ぼう) appears in formal and archaic contexts (某国 "a certain country"; 某氏 "a certain person" — used in journalism to anonymise), though less colloquial than in Chinese. Korean 某 (모) appears only in very formal Sino-Korean writing; everyday Korean uses 어떤 (a certain, some) or 무슨 (some kind of). Vietnamese *một việc nào đó* (some matter or other) is the analytic equivalent.

The indefinite 某 contrasts with both the interrogative 何 and the distal demonstrative 彼 in ways that matter for pragmatics. 何事 implies a genuine question about what the matter is; 彼事 implies the matter is known but distant in deictic space; 某事 implies deliberate vagueness — the referent may be known to the speaker but is withheld or unimportant to specify. This last function makes 某事 particularly useful in formal, legal, and diplomatic discourse: 某事已得解決 ("a certain matter has been resolved") closes the topic without identifying what was resolved, a rhetorical move unavailable in languages lacking a single-word indefinite pronoun for events.
