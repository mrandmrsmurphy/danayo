## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name; honorific person classifier"

其名 is the medial honorific correlative — "he/she (polite), that person (polite)" — serving as the deferential third-person singular person pronoun. It is the respectful counterpart to 其人, which carries ordinary (neutral-register) third-person reference. Where 其人 is the gender-neutral "he/she" for any third person, 其名 marks the speaker's deference toward the referent: 其名 is used when the person being discussed is of higher social status, is being spoken of formally, or when the speaker wishes to signal respect even in their absence.

Korean 그분 (geu-bun) is the exact parallel: 그 (medial demonstrative) + 분 (polite person suffix) = "he/she (polite)." Japanese その方 (sono kata) uses the medial adnominal その with the polite person nominaliser 方. Mandarin does not maintain the three-way deictic (proximal/medial/distal) distinction in the polite column — both 其名 and 彼名 map to 那位 (nà wèi, "that [位]"), since 那 covers both medial and distal in modern Mandarin. There is therefore no distinct Mandarin equivalent for 其名 as opposed to 彼名.

其名 differs from 其人 not only in register but in discourse function: 其人 can serve as a broad third-person narrative pronoun (like English "they," singular), while 其名 implies ongoing deference to a specific elevated individual. In polite speech about a respected third party — a senior colleague, a client, a distinguished guest — 其名 is the appropriate form, signalling to the listener that the speaker regards the absent referent with respect. This parallels how Korean speakers select 그분 over 그 사람 when the subject is someone deserving of honour.
