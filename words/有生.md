## Notes
- [有 (char)](../characters/有%20(char).md) "have, exist" + [生](../characters/生.md) "life, born" — possessing life: animate, as opposed to inanimate.

The grammar book uses 有生 to qualify the dative case [[与格]], marking that the suffix 于 specifically targets animate goals (a person or animal one gives something to), distinct from a plain destination. Mandarin linguistics uses 有生 (and its negation 无生) productively when discussing animacy as a grammatical feature, e.g. 有生名词 ("animate noun") — though Korean and Japanese linguistics more often frame the same distinction as 有情/無情 (情, "sentience," rather than 生, "life"), so this entry's pedigree leans on the Sinitic branch.
