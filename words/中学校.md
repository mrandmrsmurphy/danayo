## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle; intermediate" + [学](../characters/学.md) "study; learning" + [校](../characters/校.md) "school"

中学校 is "middle school; junior high school" — 中 (middle, intermediate) + 学 (study, learning) + 校 (school, educational institution). The compound is part of a three-tier naming system for schools: 小学校 (elementary school, 小=small) — 中学校 (middle school, 中=middle) — 高等学校/高校 (high school, 高=high). The system was formalised in Japan during the Meiji modernisation of education and spread throughout the CJKV educational vocabulary.

Japanese 中学校 (ちゅうがっこう, chūgakkō) is the standard term for junior high school (grades 7–9, ages roughly 12–15), formalised under the Fundamental Law of Education. Mandarin 中學校 (zhōngxuéxiào) or more commonly 中學 (zhōngxué). Cantonese 中學 (zung1 hok6). Korean 중학교 (中學校, junghakgyo) is the standard term. Vietnamese trường trung học (trường = school; trung học = secondary study) is the standard term.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 중학햣 diverges from Korean 중학교 in the third syllable — 校 reads 햣 (hyau/ㄏ⼘ㄨ) in Dan'a'yo but 교 (gyo) in Sino-Korean. The 校 divergence: MC initial ɣ (匣 initial) gives Dan'a'yo ㄏ (h-), while Sino-Korean gives ㄱ (g-). This h-/g- split from MC ɣ is a regular sound correspondence between Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean seen across many characters (cf. 学 hag/학, 好 ho/호). The alias 中學校 is the traditional-character form (學 is the traditional version of 学, 學校 vs 学校).
