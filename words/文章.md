## Notes
- [文](../characters/文.md) "culture" + [章 (char)](../characters/章%20(char).md) "chapter" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (文's own points to `文化`, 章's own to bare `章`); 文章 is an independent compound. `kwin: true` per the AND-rule: both constituent characters are individually `kwin: true`.
- **A genuine and important cross-linguistic sense divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: in Mandarin and Cantonese, 文章/man4 zoeng1 means "essay, article, piece of writing" — never a grammatical "sentence." Korean 문장 (verified via search), however, is specifically and primarily the grammar term for "sentence" (문장 성분 "sentence elements," 주어 문장 "sentence subject") — a genuinely different, narrower unit than "essay." Japanese ぶんしょう sits in between: its primary sense is "writing; composition; essay" (matching Mandarin), with "sentence" only a secondary sense. Vietnamese văn chương leans toward "literature; literary writing" in general, closer to the Mandarin/Japanese sense. A learner should not assume 文章 means "sentence" outside Korean, nor assume it always means "essay" in Korean.
