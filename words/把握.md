## Notes
- [把](../characters/把.md) "grip, handle" + [握](../characters/握.md) "grasp, hold" — two near-synonymous grasping-verbs compounded together for emphasis; stand-in for [[握]], which cannot appear independently.

把握 covers both the literal sense (to grasp something firmly) and, more commonly in modern usage, the figurative one: to grasp a concept, to have a firm handle on a situation, or — as a noun — the confidence that comes from that grip (有把握, "to be confident, to have it in hand"). This figurative extension is consistent across Chinese, Japanese (把握する, "to comprehend, get a grip on"), and Korean (파악하다, identical usage), all built on the same double-grasp logic.

Vietnamese breaks from the direct Sino-Vietnamese reading entirely. The character-by-character Hán-Việt would be something like *bả ác*, but that form is not actually used — everyday Vietnamese instead reaches for *nắm bắt*, a fully native compound (nắm "to grip," bắt "to catch") that independently recreates the same double-grasp logic as 把握, rather than borrowing the Chinese compound as a loanword the way Japanese and Korean did.
