## Notes
- [脅](../characters/脅.md) "threaten" + [威](../characters/威.md) "power, might, prestige"
- This word is the stand-in for [[脅]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A compound denoting threat or menace — the power to intimidate or coerce. In Japanese, きょうい (kyōi) is the standard term, used in geopolitical ("military threat"), environmental ("threat to the ecosystem"), and everyday ("menacing presence") contexts. In Chinese, 脅威 is less common; the more typical forms are 威脅 (wēixié "threaten; menace") or 威脅 reversed, and 脅迫 (xiépò "coercion, duress"). Korean 협위 is rare; the standard Korean word for threat is 위협 (威脅, wihyeop) — the same characters in reversed order, which is a common Sino-Korean / Sino-Japanese reversal pair.
