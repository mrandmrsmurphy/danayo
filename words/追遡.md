## Notes
- [追 (char)](../characters/追%20(char).md) "pursue, follow" + [遡](../characters/遡.md) "go upstream, trace back"

The metaphor is spatial: 遡 means to travel upstream against a river's current — the original sense of going against flow, backward in time or causation. 追 adds active pursuit to this movement, giving 追遡 the sense of deliberately tracing something back to its source. The compound is used for historical investigation, etymological tracing, and (in legal contexts) retroactive application of rules.

Dan'a'yo builds this word on the canonical character 遡, with the common Chinese form 溯 listed as an alias. The two characters differ only in their semantic component: 遡 uses 辶 (辵, movement/walking), while 溯 uses 氵 (water), both combined with the phonetic 朔 (OC *sŋraːɡ). Dan'a'yo regularizes to the movement-radical form (遡) as the canonical entry — the movement semantic is arguably the more transparent fit for "going against the current" as a directional act.

In Cantonese, zeoi1 sou3 is the attested reading. Japanese uses ついそ (tsuiso) as the Sino-Japanese compound reading, but the concept of retroactivity is more often expressed as 遡及 (sokyū, 遡及効力). Korean 추소 (追遡) is the hanja reading; in legal and administrative Korean, 소급 (遡及) dominates for retroactive application.
