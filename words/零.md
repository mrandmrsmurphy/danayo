## Notes
- [零 (char)](../characters/零%20(char).md) "zero; nil; remainder"

零 is the numeral "zero; nil; nought" in Dan'a'yo — the placeholder for an absent place value and the stand-alone numeral for zero: 零 (zero), 二百零三 (203 — lit. "two hundred zero three," where 零 fills the tens place). In the Dan'a'yo numeral system, 零 fills the intermediate placeholder role when a decimal position is skipped, as well as standing alone for absolute zero. The character is a 形声 compound: semantic 雨 (rain) + phonetic 令 (OC *liːŋs) — originally depicting fine scattered rain (drizzle), extended to "small remainder; odds and ends; zero" by the metaphor of stray isolated drops. 零 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[零]].

Mandarin 零 (líng, zero; nought): 零 alone for "zero," 二百零三 (203 — 零 marks the missing tens place), 零钱 (língqián, change; small coins — lit. "zero money"). Cantonese 零 (ling4, zero): 一百零三 (jat1 baak3 ling4 saam1, 103). Japanese 零 (れい/REI, zero): 零点 (reiten, zero points), 零度 (reido, zero degrees); also ゼロ (zero — from English), 〇 (maru, nought/zero in phone numbers and sequences). Korean 령/영 (零): 영 (yeong, zero — standard mathematical reading), 제로 (zero — from English), 영세 (零細, small-scale; minute). Vietnamese: không (zero — native), lẻ (odd; leftover — used for the placeholder reading: hai trăm lẻ ba = 203); linh/lênh (零 — Sino-Vietnamese placeholder reading in some dialects).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 렁 does not match Korean 령. 零 reads 렁 (leng/ㄌㄝㄫ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 來母 (l, lateral) + 青開韻 (eŋ). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean give lateral initial l- (ㄌ/ㄹ). The MC 青開韻 (eŋ) is a 4th-division (細音) rhyme: Dan'a'yo gives plain -eng (ㄝㄫ/렁 = r-eo-ng, without palatalization), while Korean 령 gives -yeong (r-yeo-ng), adding a -y- palatal onset before the vowel. The 4th-division palatalization that triggers -y- in Korean is suppressed in Dan'a'yo — Dan'a'yo 렁 (r-eo-ng, ㄌㄝㄫ) vs Korean 령 (r-yeo-ng) differ in this -y- medial: Dan'a'yo drops it, Korean retains it.
