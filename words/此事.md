## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

此事 is the proximal correlative in the action column: it picks out a specific act, event, or matter near the speaker or already prominent in shared discourse context. The character 此 (Dan'a'yo *cǝ*, ㄑㄜ) is the classical Chinese proximal demonstrative, preserved in formal and literary registers across CJKV; it contrasts with 其 (medial, near the addressee) and 彼 (distal, away from both). 事 (act, matter, affair) nomininalises events from everyday business to formal affairs of state. Together, 此事 means "this thing we are currently dealing with" — the matter at hand.

The nearest cross-linguistic equivalents are: Mandarin 此事 (cǐshì), which survives in formal writing and official discourse (此事已結案, "this matter is closed"); Japanese このこと (kono koto), using the native adnominal この rather than the Sino-Japanese 此; Korean 이 일 (i il), with the native demonstrative 이 + noun 일 (matter, work); Vietnamese *việc này* (this affair). The pattern across East Asian languages is that the literary sinographic demonstratives (此, 其, 彼) have been displaced in spoken registers by native equivalents, while Dan'a'yo systematically restores the classical sinographic forms as compact, cross-linguistically neutral correlatives.

Dan'a'yo's correlative table is a 7×9 grid — seven determiners (此 proximal, 其 medial, 彼 distal, 何 interrogative, 毎 distributive, 某 indefinite, 皆 universal) × nine semantic rows (事, 物, 人, 名, 処, 時, 様, 多, 種類) — modelled on the Esperanto correlative system but realised in sinographic vocabulary. Because the system is fully compositional, 此事 can be parsed and produced by anyone who knows its two component values: 此 = proximal and 事 = action/matter. This regularity is a deliberate auxiliary-language design feature, allowing the entire 63-form table to be derived from fourteen atomic values.
