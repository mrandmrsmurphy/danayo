## Notes
- [浴](../characters/浴.md) "bathe" + [衣](../characters/衣.md) "clothes" — yukata, a light unlined cotton kimono originally worn as a bathrobe. Mandarin (yùyī) and Cantonese (juk6 ji1) both read these characters in their own pronunciation to refer to the garment, the same naturalization pattern as 寿司/抹茶. Korean and Vietnamese instead phonetically transliterate the Japanese word itself (유카타, "áo yukata") rather than reading the hanja, so those fields are left blank.
