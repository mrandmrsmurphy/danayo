## Notes
- [禁](../characters/禁.md) "prohibit, forbid" + [令 (char)](../characters/令%20(char).md) "order, command, edict" — a commanded prohibition; a formal order forbidding something.

令 in classical Chinese governance was one of the highest-ranking categories of administrative document, below the imperial 詔 (decree) but above the 格 (regulation) and 式 (format). A 禁令 thus carries the full force of state command: not a guideline or a custom but a binding order with legal consequences for violation. The compound appears across the entire spectrum of state prohibition: 禁酒令 (alcohol prohibition edict), 禁書令 (book-burning order), 禁飛令 (no-fly zone order), 禁止令 (restraining order in legal contexts).

The Legalist tradition (法家) made 禁令 central to its political theory: Shang Yang's reforms of Qin (4th century BC), codified in the 商君書, treated clear and rigorously enforced prohibitions as the foundation of effective governance. The Qin state's later conquest of the Warring States was partly attributed to this clarity of command — subjects knew exactly what was forbidden and what the consequences were.

In modern usage 禁令 appears in administrative law, public health contexts (禁令措施, prohibition measures), and international relations (制裁禁令, sanctions and bans). The pattern 解除禁令 ("lift a ban") is particularly frequent in news contexts.
