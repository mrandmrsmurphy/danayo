## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [丹](../characters/丹.md) "cinnabar" — phonetic rendering of *Bhuṭān*; the characters carry no semantic content

Unlike [[獅子国]] or [[泥婆羅]], the name 不丹 has no classical pedigree in Chinese sources — Bhutan was simply too remote from the pilgrimage routes and tributary networks to have acquired a settled Sinospheric rendering before the modern era. 不丹 is the established modern Chinese transcription across all Chinese-using communities. The source name *Bhuṭān* is itself disputed: likely from Sanskrit *Bhoṭa-anta* ("border of Bod/Tibet"), acknowledging the kingdom's Tibetan cultural and geographic roots, though *Bhu-utthan* ("high land") is also proposed.

The Dzongkha name for the country is Druk Yul (འབྲུག་ཡུལ།), "Land of the Thunder Dragon" — the Thunder Dragon (Druk) being both the national emblem and the source of the term *Drukpa*, the dominant school of Tibetan Buddhism practiced in Bhutan. This identity is entirely absent from 不丹, which is why the Bhutanese government itself has periodically promoted romanizations based on *Druk Yul* in international contexts, without success.

Bhutan maintained deliberate isolation from outside contact through much of its history, a policy that has only gradually relaxed since the 1960s. It joined the United Nations in 1971 — the same year [[孟加拉]] came into existence — and opened to tourism only in 1974. Its place in the [[南亜]] lexicon is consequently thin.
