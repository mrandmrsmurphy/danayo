## Notes
- [所 (char)](../characters/所%20(char).md) "place; that which; nominalizing particle"

所 is a grammatical particle and nominalizer — it precedes a verb to form a noun phrase denoting the object or receiver of that action: 所 + 有 = 所有 (that which is had = possessions); 所 + 做 = 所做 (that which is done); 所 + 愛 = 所愛 (the loved one, literally "that which is loved"). The English gloss "-ee" captures this passive-nominalization function: just as English "employ → employee" forms the receiver-of-action noun, 所 + V forms the receiver-of-action phrase in classical CJKV grammar. In Dan'a'yo, 所 functions as an 助詞 (grammatical particle) for constructing passive-nominal expressions.

Mandarin 所 (suǒ) is a classical Chinese nominalizer, most productive in formal written compounds: 所有 (suǒyǒu, all that is had; possessions), 所謂 (suǒwèi, so-called, literally "that which is called"), 所見所聞 (that which is seen and heard). In modern spoken Mandarin, 所 appears mainly in these frozen compounds rather than as a productive particle. Cantonese 所 (so2) functions similarly in formal/literary registers. Japanese 所 (ところ, tokoro) as a grammatical element forms nominalized clauses: V-dictionary-form + ところ ("at the point of V-ing; the fact that one V-s"). Korean 소 (所) appears in Sino-Korean compounds but is not a productive particle in modern Korean.

所 in Dan'a'yo inherits the classical Chinese particle function rather than the Japanese ところ (place) noun sense — it is a function word (助詞) rather than a content noun. The word entry exists alongside the character entry [所 (char)](/characters/所%20(char).md) because the particle function warrants independent documentation as a grammatical word. The Sino-Vietnamese readings sỡ/sở/sớ reflect tone and dialect variation in the Vietnamese adaptation of the Middle Chinese syllable (MC *ʂɨʌX or similar); all three are Sino-Vietnamese readings of the same character in different registers or dialect layers.
