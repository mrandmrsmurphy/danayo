## Notes
- [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "-ly; in the manner of; appearance" + [子](../characters/子.md) "child; nominalising suffix"

様子 is "manner; appearances; state of things; circumstances" — 様 (-ly; in the manner of; appearance) + 子 (nominalising dummy suffix). 様 alone functions as an adverbializer and appearance-marker in East Asian grammar (-like; in the manner of: ～のような in Japanese), while 子 here is a nominalising suffix that turns the adverbial into a noun: not an action or quality but a perceivable state. The alias 樣子 is the traditional/full-form character spelling (様 is the shinjitai of 樣). The compound is used primarily in Chinese and Japanese; Korean and Vietnamese express the same concept with different vocabulary. Dan'a'yo 様子 is a homophone of [[陽子]] (proton) and [[養子]] (adopted child) — all three read 'yangjǝ (⼘ㄫㄐㄜ).

Mandarin 樣子/样子 (yàngzi) is colloquial for "appearance; manner; shape": 他的樣子很奇怪 (the way he looks is very strange), 就是這樣子 (it's just like this). Cantonese 樣子 (joeng6 zi2): 有樣子 (presentable; well-mannered), 冇樣子 (mou5 joeng6 zi2, having no decorum). Japanese 様子 (ようす, yōsu) is the standard word: 様子を見る (to see how things stand), 様子がおかしい (something seems off), 様子を伺う (to read the room). Korean 양자 (様子) is the Sino-Korean reading of the characters, but not the everyday word for this concept — Korean uses 모양 (模樣, appearance) or 상태 (狀態, state/condition). Vietnamese dạng tử (Sino-Vietnamese: 様=dạng, 子=tử); Vietnamese uses dáng vẻ or trạng thái for "manner/state."

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 양즈 differs from Korean 양자 in the second syllable. 子 reads 즈 (jǝ/ㄐㄜ) in Dan'a'yo but 자 (ja) in Sino-Korean — the same MC 之韻 (ɨ) → Dan'a'yo schwa (-ǝ) vs Sino-Korean -a correspondence as in [[陽子]] and [[養子]]. The first syllable 양 is identical in both systems: MC 疑母 (ŋ) + 陽韻 (ɨɐŋ) gives yang in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, the nasal initial quality of MC 疑 dropping away to yield a vowel-initial syllable in both daughter forms.
