## Notes
- [縦 (char)](../characters/縦%20(char).md) "vertical, longitudinal" + [縞](../characters/縞.md) "stripe, pattern" — stand-in for [[縞]], which cannot appear independently

縦縞 (たてじま in Japanese) refers to vertical stripes running parallel from top to bottom, as opposed to 横縞 (よこじま, horizontal stripes). The compound is primarily a Japanese textile and design term, describing patterns found in kimono fabrics, prison uniforms in period fiction, and business suits (where vertical pinstripes have a slimming effect). 縞 alone means a stripe or checkered pattern and is used for silks with any regular woven pattern; the prefix 縦 (vertical, longitudinal) specifies direction.

In the Dan'a'yo context 縞 is a bounded character and 縦縞 serves as its legitimising compound. In modern Chinese the concept is usually expressed as 竖条纹 or 纵条, making 縦縞 effectively a Japanism when used in Chinese-language contexts.
