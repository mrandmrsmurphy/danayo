## Notes
- [初](../characters/初.md) "beginning, first" + [等 (char)](../characters/等%20(char).md) "grade, rank" — elementary; of the first and most basic level

初等 names the entry level of any ranked system. In educational contexts, 初等教育 (primary/elementary education) designates the foundational stage of formal schooling — the level at which literacy and numeracy are first taught. 初等数学 (elementary mathematics), 初等物理学 (introductory physics), and 初等文法 (basic grammar) all use 初等 to signal that the content is foundational rather than advanced, accessible rather than specialized.

The character 初 (刀 knife + 衤 cloth — the first cut into cloth, hence "beginning") contributes the sense that 初等 names not merely the low end of a scale but the necessary precondition for everything that follows: without the first cut, there is no garment.
