## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

此多 is the proximal quantity correlative — "this much; this many; this great an amount." Where 此処 (here) and 此時 (now) deictic-anchor an event spatially and temporally, 此多 deictic-anchors a quantity to the immediate speech context: the amount being shown, felt, or just experienced. It answers the quantity-deictic question — "how much?" — with a proximal pointer: 此多でいい ("this much will do"); 此多の人が来た ("this many people came").

Cross-linguistic parallels are close to the surface: Mandarin 這麼多 (zhème duō, "this much/many") is the everyday form and is kept as an alias for cross-referencing; Cantonese 咁多 (gam3 do1, "this/that much") is the colloquial equivalent. Japanese これほど (korehodo, "to this extent; this much" — often implying surprise at the degree) or これだけ (koredake, "only this much; this amount and no more") cover the proximal quantity. Korean 이만큼 (imankkeum, "this much; to this extent") is the standard proximal quantity adverb.

The 多 row is the quantity/degree row of the correlative grid, answering the "how much/many?" question across all seven deictic-quantifier columns. All 多-row entries function as 副用名詞 (adverbial nouns), modifying the degree or extent of predicates rather than substituting for noun phrases. Note: the Dan'a'yo 注音 for 多 is ㄉㄜ (*dǝ*), not ㄉㄚ — several earlier drafts of 多-row files carried a systematic ㄉㄚ error that has been corrected.
