## Notes
- [尊](../characters/尊.md) as 代用字 for 鱒 "trout" + [魚 (char)](../characters/魚%20(char).md) "fish"
- 尊 carries 鱒 (traditional) and 鳟 (simplified) as aliases, and 魚 carries 鱼 as an alias — together authorizing the aliases 鱒魚 and 鳟鱼. The 尊 character file itself uses the term 借代字 for this relationship.

Trout (*Salmo*, *Oncorhynchus*, and related genera) are salmonid fish native to cold freshwater streams across the Northern Hemisphere. The character 鱒 is itself a phono-semantic compound — fish radical 魚 plus phonetic 尊 — so the Dan'a'yo substitution cleanly reverses to the phonetic alone, exactly as happened with 堅魚 (鰹).

In Japanese, trout is simply ます (*masu*, kun-yomi of 鱒), and the compound 鱒魚 is not standard usage — the fish is named by the single character alone. The on-yomi そんぎょ (SON + GYO) applies to the Dan'a'yo compound as written. Specific varieties include 虹鱒 (*nijimasu*, rainbow trout) and 鮭鱒 (*keison*, salmons and trouts collectively).
