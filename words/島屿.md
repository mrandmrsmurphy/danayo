## Notes
- [島 (char)](../characters/島%20(char).md) "island" + [屿](../characters/屿.md) "islet, small island" — islands and islets collectively; an archipelago

島屿 pairs two near-synonyms for island, differing in scale: 島 refers to an island of any size, 屿 specifically to a smaller islet or rock. Together they form a collective noun covering the full spectrum — the compound is used in geographic and political writing wherever the reference is to a group of islands without specifying size. It appears in expressions like 島屿国家 (island nation), 南海島嶼 (South China Sea islands), and 太平洋島屿 (Pacific islands), as well as in the formal names of island chains.

The distinction between 島 and 屿 mirrors similar paired compounds in Classical Chinese that coordinate two words of related but slightly different meaning to create a more general or emphatic term — a pattern sometimes called 連綿詞 or synonymic compounding. Neither character is redundant; together they assert that the referent includes islands of every category. Compare [[南北]] (north and south → the full north-south axis).
