## Notes
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what; which; interrogative" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

何様 is the interrogative manner correlative — "how; in what way; by what method?" As a 副用名詞, it modifies a predicate by questioning its manner: 何様にすれば良いのか ("how should one go about it?"); 何様に解釈するか ("how should this be interpreted?"). The alias 怎么样 (zěnmeyàng, simplified) is the ubiquitous Mandarin "how/what's it like?" — perhaps the most frequently used manner interrogative in any CJKV language, ranging from method ("how do you do it?") to state inquiry ("how are you doing?") to suggestion ("how about it?").

Mandarin 怎麼樣 (zěnmeyàng) and 怎麼 (zěnme, "how; in what way") are the primary equivalents. Cantonese 點樣 (dim2 joeng6) or the shortened 點 (dim2, "how; in what way") is the colloquial form. Japanese どのように (dono yō ni, "in what way") for formal manner; どうやって (dō yatte, "how, by what means") for informal spoken use; どう (dō, "how") is the short adverbial form. Korean 어떻게 (eotteoke, "how, in what way") is the all-purpose manner interrogative.

Like 何処 and 何時, 何様 requires no neologism tag — "how" words are lexically well-established across all source languages, and the composition 何 + 様 is fully transparent. The Dan'a'yo form 何様 aligns with Mandarin 怎麼樣 structurally (interrogative + manner/way) even if the morphological components differ. The alias 怎么样 is the simplified-character form of 怎麼樣, retained as a cross-reference for Mandarin readers who might not recognise the Dan'a'yo form immediately.
