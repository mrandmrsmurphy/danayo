## Notes
- [潜](../characters/潜.md) "latent; hidden" + [伏 (char)](../characters/伏%20(char).md) "crouch; prostrate"

The compound layers two images of concealment: 潜 carries the sense of being submerged or invisible, as in something lurking below the surface, while 伏 pictures a figure crouching low against the ground, staying out of sight. Together they describe the act of going to ground — hiding, lying low, staying out of view on purpose.

The word spans three main semantic domains across the CJKV sphere. In espionage and security contexts it refers to undercover or sleeper operation: a 潜伏特工 (undercover agent) is one who has embedded themselves in a target environment and waits. In medicine and epidemiology, 潜伏期 (incubation period) describes the interval between infection and the appearance of symptoms — the disease lurking invisibly in the body. In general literary usage it simply means to hide or to lie in wait, as a predator might before striking.

In Japanese the reading is せんぷく rather than the expected せんふく: after the nasal ん, the ふ (FUKU) onset shifts to ぷ (PUKU), a regular phonological assimilation that affects bilabials following moraic nasals. The alternate on-yomi BUKU listed in the 伏 character record reflects the same underlying shift. Korean 잠복 (潛伏) is used in the same espionage and medical senses, and the phrase 잠복근무 ("stakeout duty") is a standard term in police and investigative contexts.
