## Notes
- [非 (char)](../characters/非%20(char).md) "ain't" (here a pure phonetic loan for "Fei-," not its "not, un-" sense) + [洲 (char)](../characters/洲%20(char).md) "islet, continent" — a phono-semantic transliteration of "Africa," the same pattern used for [[南非]] "South Africa."

Japanese and Korean both dropped this Sino name entirely in ordinary use, replacing it with a fuller phonetic transliteration of "Africa" itself: アフリカ/아프리카 (Afurika/Apeurika). Japan's own history with 非洲 is documented — the single-character abbreviation 非 for Africa saw brief use around 1868, but was already fading by the late 19th century in favor of longer ateji transliterations like 阿弗利加, and today only the katakana survives. Mandarin and Cantonese keep 非洲/fei1 zau1 as the only real name for the continent. Vietnamese châu Phi keeps the Sino-Vietnamese roots but in reversed word order from the Chinese original (非洲 = "Phi" + "châu") — Vietnamese noun-modifier order puts the head noun châu ("continent") first, giving châu Phi rather than the literal calque "Phi châu."
