## Notes
- [参](../characters/参.md) "participate, attend" + [謀 (char)](../characters/謀%20(char).md) "plan, scheme, strategize" — staff officer; one who participates in strategic planning; a strategist or advisor

参謀 names someone who participates in the planning rather than commanding in the field — the adviser who sits with the decision-maker and works through strategy. In military organization, the 参謀 (staff officer) is the backbone of modern command structures: 参謀本部 (General Staff Headquarters) is the supreme planning body of an army, and 参謀総長 (Chief of the General Staff) its head. The Prussian Generalstab model, which Japan adopted during Meiji modernization, institutionalized the 参謀 as a professional class of military planners trained at dedicated staff colleges.

In everyday usage, 参謀 has extended to any behind-the-scenes strategic advisor: the campaign manager who is the politician's 参謀, the consultant who serves as a company's 参謀. Korean 참모 follows the same pattern — 참모총장 (Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff) is the formal military title, and 참모 alone can describe any trusted strategic counselor. Vietnamese tham mưu is particularly established in institutional language: Bộ Tổng Tham mưu (General Staff Directorate) is the Vietnamese military's central planning command.
