## Notes
- [連 (char)](../characters/連%20(char).md) "connect" + [濁](../characters/濁.md) "voiced, turbid" — rendaku, the Japanese phonological process by which a compound's second-element initial consonant becomes voiced (e.g. 鼻 hana + 毛 ke → 鼻毛 hanage). A Japanese-rooted linguistic concept, but the characters are read natively in Mandarin (liánzhuó), Cantonese (lin4 zuk6), and Korean (연탁) — the same naturalization pattern as 猿楽.
