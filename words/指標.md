## Notes
- [指](../characters/指.md) "finger; to point" + [標](../characters/標.md) "mark, signpost" — indicator; index; benchmark; metric

The compound's internal logic is gestural: 指 is the pointing finger, 標 is the mark being pointed at. Together they name the thing that directs attention toward a measurement or condition — an indicator in the sense of something that stands in for, or makes visible, something else. A thermometer reading is a 指標 of temperature; a GDP figure is a 指標 of economic activity; a falling birth rate is a 指標 of demographic change.

In modern usage across all five languages — Mandarin zhǐbiāo, Cantonese zi2 biu1, Japanese しひょう (shihyō), Korean 지표, Vietnamese chỉ tiêu — the word covers both the abstract sense of an indicator and the concrete sense of a numerical metric or key performance indicator (KPI). Japanese and Korean usage is particularly dense in policy, medicine, and economics, where 指標 is the standard term for any quantified measure used to track progress or evaluate a system.

指標 is distinct from 目標 (target, goal — what one aims to reach) and from 標準 (standard, norm — a fixed reference point). A 指標 points; it need not prescribe.
