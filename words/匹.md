## Notes
- [匹 (char)](../characters/匹%20(char).md) "animals (classifier)"

匹 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for animals — 量詞, counting quadrupeds, fish, and most creatures of the natural world: 一匹馬 (one horse), 三匹魚 (three fish), 數匹犬 (several dogs). The original sense of 匹 was "a bolt of cloth; a matched pair," but its classifier-for-animals meaning developed from its use counting horses — the most economically important animal of classical times, often traded in bolts of cloth as a unit of exchange. Dan'a'yo 匹 preserves this inherited classifier function, covering the same semantic range as the Japanese animal counter 匹 (ひき/hiki) while extending to fish and most other creatures beyond the specifically equine meaning of Mandarin 匹.

The CJKV classifier landscape for animals is notably non-uniform. Mandarin 匹 (pǐ) applies specifically to horses and mules; 條 (tiáo) counts fish and elongated animals; 頭 (tóu) counts large livestock; modern colloquial speech extends 只 (zhī) to almost any animal. Cantonese 條 (tiu4) covers fish and snakes; 隻 (zek3) covers birds and smaller animals. Japanese 匹 (ひき/hiki, written 匹 or 疋) counts small animals broadly — 一匹の犬 (one dog), 二匹の猫 (two cats), 三匹の魚 (three fish) — while 頭 (とう/tō) counts large animals and 羽 (わ/wa) counts birds. Korean uses 마리 (mari) as the universal animal classifier — a native Korean form, not Sino-Korean, covering all animals: 개 한 마리 (one dog), 물고기 세 마리 (three fish). Vietnamese uses con (originally "child; young creature") as the universal animal classifier: một con chó (one dog), ba con cá (three fish).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 핃 does not match Korean 필. 匹 reads 핃 (pid/ㄆㄧㄊ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 滂母 (pʰ) + 質三韻 (iɪt) gives an aspirated bilabial stop with an -it final. Dan'a'yo preserves the MC -t coda in the syllable ending, giving 핃 with a final stop; Sino-Korean 필 (pil) converts the same MC -t coda to -l through a regular historical sound change in Korean that rhotic-ized dental stops in closed syllables. The MC 質三韻 produced ㄧㄊ (-it/핃) in Dan'a'yo but -il (필) in Korean — identical vowel, different coda.
