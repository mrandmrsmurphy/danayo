## Notes
- [恩](../characters/恩.md) "kindness; mercy" + [寵](../characters/寵.md) "to pet; to favor specially; beloved" — the personal, elective dimension of God's beneficence; the sense that one has been singled out for God's warm regard

Among the Dan'a'yo 恩- words for grace and related concepts, 恩寵 occupies the register of personal, relational favoritism. 寵 carries the overtone of something precious and cherished — a child doted upon, a pet held close. 恩寵 is thus grace experienced as intimacy and special election, not merely as an abstract gift or principle.

In the Korean Protestant tradition, 은총 (恩寵) and 은혜 (恩惠) are both widely used for grace; 은총 leans toward the sense of "being in favor with" (as in 은총을 받다, "to receive favor"). Compare [[恩典]], which is the more formal, doctrinal term for grace as a theological category, and [[恩意]], which emphasizes God's affectionate intention.
