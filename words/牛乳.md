## Notes
- [牛 (char)](../characters/牛%20(char).md) "cow" + [乳](../characters/乳.md) "milk" — a plainly compositional word, "cow-milk," found across the whole CJKV sphere; stand-in for [[乳]], which cannot appear independently.

Chinese and Japanese both write "milk" with the same two characters but split usage differently. Chinese distinguishes 乳 (the mammary gland/breast itself, more anatomical) from 奶 (the liquid it produces), so everyday spoken Mandarin overwhelmingly prefers 牛奶 (niúnǎi, recorded here as an alias) for the drink, reserving 牛乳 for more formal, technical, or product-label contexts. Japanese never made that same 乳/奶 split — 乳 alone already covers both the gland and its secretion — so ぎゅうにゅう (牛乳) is simply the plain, default word for milk, with no everyday alternative the way Chinese has 牛奶. Korean 우유 is the direct Sino-Korean reading of the same two characters and functions as the ordinary word, same as Japanese. Vietnamese breaks from the pattern entirely: the Sino-Vietnamese reading *ngưu nhũ* is essentially unused, and everyday Vietnamese reaches for the native word *sữa* instead, the same substitution pattern seen in [[導演]] (監督/감독 standing in for the Chinese-only term).
