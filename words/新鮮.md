## Notes
- [新 (char)](../characters/新%20(char).md) "new" + [鮮](../characters/鮮.md) "fresh"

The compound 新鮮 describes freshness — of produce, fish, air, or experience — and is one of the most common everyday adjectives across Chinese, Japanese, and Korean. Its origin lies in 鮮, which itself is a compound meaning "delicious" or "vivid" (魚 "fish" + 羊 "sheep"), later narrowing to the freshness of food; 新 reinforces this with the sense of newness, making explicit what 鮮 implied: something recently obtained or still at peak quality.

In Classical Chinese, 鮮 could appear alone to mean "rare, few" (a distinct sense still active in 鮮有, 鮮明), but 新鮮 as a two-character compound stabilized the "fresh" meaning across the CJKV sphere. Chinese and Korean use 新鮮 / 신선 (sinseon) identically for fresh food and novel ideas. Japanese 新鮮 (shinsen) follows the same range.

The simplified Chinese variant 新鲜 reflects the simplification of 鮮 → 鲜 (fish radical simplified, sheep component intact).
