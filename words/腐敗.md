## Notes
- [腐](../characters/腐.md) "rotten; corrupt" + [敗](../characters/敗.md) "be defeated" — stand-in for [[腐]], which cannot appear independently

**`pos` corrected `名詞`→`事詞`**, matching the verb-like ("event") reading of "to rot, to decay/spoil" rather than a bare noun.

Mandarin fǔbài, Cantonese fu6 baai6, Japanese ふはい (fuhai), and Korean 부패 (bupae) all cover both the literal sense (food/organic matter rotting) and the figurative one (moral or institutional corruption, 腐敗政治/부패정치, "corrupt politics") evenly, in ordinary everyday register. Vietnamese hủ bại is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search), but has leaned more heavily toward the figurative, formal/literary sense — moral decline, corrupt customs (phong tục hủ bại) — reserved for journalism and literature rather than everyday speech about spoiled food.
