## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; object; material result"

彼物 is the distal correlative in the object column: it refers to a thing away from both speaker and addressee — "that thing over there," "that object (of theirs)." The character 彼 (bǐ in Mandarin, Dan'a'yo *bǝ*) was the classical Chinese third-person distal pronoun and demonstrative, now largely replaced in colloquial registers but preserved in Dan'a'yo as the mandatory distal member of the deictic triad. 彼物 accordingly means "that thing yonder" — physically or conceptually distant from the current conversational space.

In Mandarin, 彼物 (bǐwù) appears in formal and classical texts meaning "that object" (彼物非此物 "that thing is not this thing"); contemporary Mandarin uses 那个 (nàge) for both medial and distal. Japanese あのもの (ano mono) is the natural spoken equivalent — あの (adnominal distal demonstrative) + もの (thing) — and can carry a nuance of emotional remoteness or something out of reach; Korean 저것 (jeogeot) is the standard distal object pronoun.

The three-way proximal/medial/distal system across the 物 row (此物/其物/彼物) mirrors the demonstrative systems of Japanese (これ/それ/あれ) and Korean (이것/그것/저것), both of which maintain the full three-term distinction. Mandarin's collapse to a two-term system (这/那) means that 彼物 has no direct spoken Mandarin counterpart — 那个 (nàge) serves for both 其物 and 彼物 in Mandarin. This is one of several places where Dan'a'yo's classical-based grammar aligns more closely with Japanese and Korean than with modern Mandarin.
