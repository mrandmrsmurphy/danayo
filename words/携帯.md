## Notes
- [携](../characters/携.md) "carry, lead by hand" + [帯](../characters/帯%20(char).md) "belt, carry"
- This word is the stand-in for [[携]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- Originally meaning "to carry with one" (携帯する). In modern Japanese, 携帯 (けいたい) alone refers to a mobile phone, an abbreviation of 携帯電話 (けいたいでんわ "portable telephone"). Chinese 携带 (xiédài) means "to carry along or bring with" — applied to objects rather than people. Korean 휴대 (攜帶) similarly means "portable" or "hand-held," as in 휴대폰 "mobile phone" and 휴대용 "portable/for carrying." The shift from "carry" to "mobile device" is a specifically Japanese semantic development that spread into Korean borrowing.
