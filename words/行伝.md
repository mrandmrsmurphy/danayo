## Notes
- [行 (char)](../characters/行%20(char).md) "deeds, conduct" + [伝](../characters/伝.md) "to transmit; a record, biography" — "a record of deeds," best known as the second element of **使徒行傳/[[使徒]]行伝** (Acts of the Apostles), the New Testament book recounting the deeds of the early Church following Jesus's ascension.

Here 傳/伝 carries its "biography, chronicle" sense (as in 傳記, *zhuànjì*, "biography") rather than its more common "transmit, propagate" sense — both senses derive from the same root meaning "to pass something on," whether a message (transmission) or a life's deeds (a written record passed down to posterity). 行傳 as "chronicle of acts/conduct" thus parallels titles like 水滸傳 (*Water Margin*, lit. "chronicle of the water's edge") and 三國志/三国伝 forms found throughout Sinitic narrative tradition.

In Japanese Bible translations, 使徒行伝 (*Shito Gyōden*) was the standard Protestant title for Acts for over a century; the title has more recently been rendered 使徒の働き in some modern translations, while Japanese Catholic Bibles use 使徒言行録. Korean retains **사도행전** (使徒行傳) as the standard title across denominations. The split between [[宗徒]] (Catholic) and [[使徒]] (Protestant/Orthodox) for "apostle" does not carry through as cleanly to 行傳/行伝 itself, which remains widely recognized as "Acts" regardless of which term for "apostle" precedes it.
