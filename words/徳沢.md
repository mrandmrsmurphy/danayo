## Notes
- [徳 (char)](../characters/徳%20(char).md) "virtue" + [沢](../characters/沢.md) "stream, marsh; beneficence"

徳沢 means "grace, beneficence, kindness extended [to others]" — in classical usage, the virtue (徳) of a ruler or benefactor flowing outward like water (沢) to nourish those beneath them. The word is built here on the canonical pair 徳 (itself an alias of 德) and 沢 (an alias of 澤/泽), with the more familiar real-world spellings 德澤 and 徳澤 recorded as word-level aliases — following the same alias-parent pattern as [[坐位]] and [[処女]] earlier in this project.

沢's literal meaning is "marsh, swamp, wetland" (cf. [[薮沢]], "marsh," cross-linked from [[沢]]'s Words section) — but in compounds it extends metaphorically to "moisture, beneficence, favor that spreads like water across land," exactly the image 徳沢 relies on. The Japanese reflex of this word, 御蔭 / お陰 (okage) aside, survives chiefly in the Korean compound 덕택 (deoktaek), which has drifted from "the grace [of a benefactor]" to the everyday adverbial phrase "thanks to ~" (~의 덕택에) — a shift from a noun naming a virtue to a function word expressing causation, paralleling how English "thanks to" similarly grammaticalized from an expression of gratitude.
