## Notes
- [皮](../characters/皮.md) "hide" + [膚](../characters/膚.md) "skin" — stand-in for [[膚]], which cannot appear independently

Both halves already mean roughly "skin/hide" on their own, so the compound is a near-synonym doubling rather than one component modifying the other — a common formation strategy for giving a bounded morpheme (here 膚) a viable independent word.

Mandarin pífū, Cantonese pei4 fu1, and Japanese ひふ (hifu) are all the ordinary, everyday clinical/general term for "skin" in their respective languages. Korean is a genuine three-way split worth noting: 피부 (pibu, this compound's own Sino-Korean reading) is the standard everyday word, while the character's own field also carries the fully native 살갗 (salgat) as a more literary/poetic alternative — narrowed the frontmatter to 피부 alone, since that's what actually corresponds to this specific compound, and moved 살갗 here as commentary rather than leaving it crammed into the same field. Vietnamese bì phu is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound, used specifically in dermatological/clinical contexts, but the everyday Vietnamese word for skin is the native da (as in bệnh da liễu, "dermatology," which prefers da over bì phu even in its own medical register).
