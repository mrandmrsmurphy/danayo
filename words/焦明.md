## Notes
- [焦 (char)](../characters/焦%20(char).md) as 代用字 for 鷦 + [明 (char)](../characters/明%20(char).md) "bright; luminous"
- 焦明 is already an attested classical Chinese alternate form of 鷦明 — the substitution of 焦 for 鷦 is not a Dan'a'yo invention but an ancient variant. The third alias 鷦䳟 arises because 明 carries 䳟 as an alias.

The 鷦明/焦明 is a supernatural bird of the south from classical Chinese mythology, appearing in the cosmological literature alongside the fenghuang ([[鳳凰]]). It is described as a radiant creature with extraordinary plumage, inhabiting the extreme south. Its name pairs 鷦/焦 (*jiāo*) — phonetically chosen, as this syllable recurs in the names of several mythological southern birds — with 明 ("bright; luminous"), evoking a bird of dazzling brilliance.

The substitution of 焦 ("scorched; intensely hot") for 鷦 ("wren") is purely phonetic: the two characters share the reading *jiāo* but have no semantic connection. This makes 焦明 an instructive example of the distinction between semantic and phonetic 代用字 — here 焦 lends only its sound, not its meaning, to the compound. The result has a fortuitous logic: a blazing, luminous bird of the far south, rendered with a character meaning "scorched," is not without poetic resonance.
