## Notes
- [湿 (char)](../characters/湿%20(char).md) "wet, damp" + [度](../characters/度.md) "degree, manner" — the degree of wetness in the air; humidity.

Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean all keep this as the fully Sino compound, unchanged in structure across the family (shīdù/sap1 dou6/しつど/습도). Vietnamese độ ẩm breaks the pattern in one specific way: only the second half, độ ("degree"), is the Sino-Vietnamese reading of 度; the first half swaps out thấp (the Sino-Vietnamese reading of 湿 itself, attested but not used here) for ẩm, a near-native word for "damp, humid" — a half-Sino, half-native hybrid rather than the fully Sino compound the other four languages keep intact.
