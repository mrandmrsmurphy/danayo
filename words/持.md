## Notes
- [持 (char)](../characters/持%20(char).md) "hold" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin chí, Cantonese ci4, and Korean 지 (ji) all use this character productively and independently or bound in extremely common compounds across the sphere (維持/유지, "maintain"; 堅持/견지, "persevere, hold firm"; 支持/지지, "support"). Japanese uses the native kun'yomi verb 持つ (もつ) for the everyday "to hold, to carry," rather than a bare on'yomi citation form. Vietnamese trì is the real, attested Sino-Vietnamese reading (duy trì, "to maintain"; kiên trì, "to persevere"; chủ trì, "to preside over") — `characters/持 (char).md`'s own stored `vietnamese` field ("rì, chày, chiì") looks like corpus noise rather than genuine attested readings; flagged, not corrected here, since fixing the character's own frontmatter is out of scope for a words-only iteration.
