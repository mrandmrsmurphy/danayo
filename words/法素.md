## Notes
### Form
法素 is [法 (char)](characters/法%20(char).md) + [素](characters/素.md) “element.” The suffix follows the main-group rule.

### Motivation
The international name _francium_ derives from _France_, named by Marguerite Perey (1939). In CJKV, “France” is [[法国]] (法 + 国). Selecting 法 as the elemental sign is thus a **reduction of a standard place-name transcription**, parallel to 波素 (from 波蘭 “Poland”).

### Type of formation
As with 波素, this is a **toponymic abbreviation**:

- source: 法国 (France)
- reduction: 法 (first graph retained)
- suffixation: 素 to mark elemental status

This matches established Sinitic practice where a single graph stands for a country or its language (e.g., 英 for England/English).

### Fit within the system

1. Avoids phonetic loans while preserving the international naming origin.
2. Maintains one-character uniqueness for symbol use.
3. Keeps internal consistency with other place-derived elements (波素, 日本素, etc.).

### Comparative CJKV forms

- Modern Chinese: 钫 (phonetic), 鍅 in Taiwan
- Japanese: フランシウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 프랑슘 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: franxi (modern) / kiểu Hán-Việt: pháp (for France)

Modern usage is phonetic; 法素 restores a graph-based system tied to established toponymy, and shares a radical with the Taiwanese form

### Clarity and ambiguity

- 法 ordinarily means “law; method,” but in compounds like 法国 it conventionally represents “France.”
- Within a chemical register, reserving 法 as an element symbol removes ambiguity.

### Conclusion
法素 is a consistent toponymic formation, parallel to 波素, and an appropriate semantic–conventional reconstruction of _francium_.
