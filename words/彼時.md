## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; moment; season; occasion"

彼時 is the distal temporal correlative — "that other time; back then; at that distant point in time." Where 其時 refers to a time recently established in the discourse or contextually shared, 彼時 refers to a more remote time — further in the past, less directly in conversational focus, or separated from the present by a significant gap in experience or perspective. In narrative, 彼時 looks back to a time that feels distant: historically remote, emotionally removed, or prior to a major change in circumstance.

Japanese あの時 (ano toki, "that time — distal") is the exact parallel: あの時は若かった ("back then, we were young"); あの時のことは忘れない ("I'll never forget what happened at that time"). The distal あの carries a nuance of looking back with distance, nostalgia, or solemnity that その時 does not. Korean 저때 (jeottae, "that time — distal") is the distal member of the 이때/그때/저때 temporal deictic set. Mandarin 彼時 (bǐshí) is found in classical and formal texts meaning "at that time" in the distancing, retrospective sense (彼時情勢不同 "the situation back then was different").

The temporal distance of 彼時 can encode emotional remoteness as well as chronological distance. In reflective or elegiac prose, 彼時 refers to an era separated from the present by loss, change, or perspective — the same mood that Japanese あの時 conveys when used in nostalgic or commemorative contexts. This "temporal-emotional" use of the distal-time form parallels how the distal-place form 彼処 can imply not just spatial but social or relational distance. The full three-way temporal deictic (此時/其時/彼時 = now/then/back then) gives Dan'a'yo precise temporal deixis matched only by Japanese and Korean among the CJKV languages.
