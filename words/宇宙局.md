## Notes
- [宇](../characters/宇.md) "universe, spatial extent" + [宙 (char)](../characters/宙%20(char).md) "cosmos, temporal extent" + [局 (char)](../characters/局%20(char).md) "bureau, agency" — space agency

The compound 宇宙 is itself remarkable: 宇 names the six spatial directions (上下四方, the four cardinal directions plus zenith and nadir), while 宙 names the extent of time from past through future. Together they formed the classical Chinese term for the totality of space and time — a near-equivalent to the modern concept of the universe as a space-time continuum, arrived at through cosmological reflection rather than physics. Adding 局 produces the institutional term for the government body responsible for activities in this totality.

As a generic compound, 宇宙局 names any space agency: NASA (米国航空宇宙局 in Japanese), ESA, ISRO, and others. The Japanese JAXA (宇宙航空研究開発機構) uses a different structure, but 宇宙局 remains the transparent generic. The Dan'a'yo form serves the same purpose: a cross-linguistically readable designation for any national or international body charged with space exploration and policy.
