## Notes
- [槐](../characters/槐.md) "pagoda tree" + [樹](../characters/樹.md) "tree, plant"
- This word is the stand-in for [[槐]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- The pagoda tree (Styphnolobium japonicum, formerly Sophora japonica), native to China and Korea and long cultivated for shade, timber, and traditional medicine. Its flowers (槐花, huáihuā) are used in Chinese cuisine and as a yellow dye. In Japan the tree is called えんじゅ (enju) by its native name, with the Sino-Japanese on-reading of the compound being かいじゅ; in Korea the native name is 회화나무, while the Sino-Korean reading 괴수 (槐樹) is homophonous with 怪獸 "monster." Historically, the Zhou court planted Three Ducal Trees (三槐, sān huái) — pagoda trees symbolizing the Three Excellencies — lending the tree an aristocratic symbolism in East Asian culture.
