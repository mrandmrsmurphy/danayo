## Notes
- [袋 (char)](../characters/袋%20(char).md) "sack, bag" + [熊 (char)](../characters/熊%20(char).md) "bear"

袋熊 "bag-bear" is the wombat — named for its pouch, like most marsupials in this naming family. Be careful not to confuse it with [[樹袋熊]] "tree bag-bear," the koala; the two animals are often mixed up in casual usage (both are stout, pouched, tree-or-burrow-dwelling Australian marsupials), and 袋熊 even appears as a loose alias for 樹袋熊 in some sources. Here the two are kept distinct: 袋熊 = wombat, 樹袋熊 = koala.
