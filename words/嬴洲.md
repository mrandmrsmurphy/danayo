## Notes
- [嬴](../characters/嬴.md) "Yingzhou" + [洲 (char)](../characters/洲%20(char).md) "islet, continent"

嬴洲 (more often written 瀛洲, with the water-radical variant 瀛 listed as an alias of 嬴) names one of the three legendary mountain-islands of the Bohai Sea — alongside 蓬萊 and 方丈 — said in Chinese mythology to be inhabited by immortals (仙人) and to hold the elixir of eternal life. 嬴 is itself a fairly rare character whose primary modern role, per its own frontmatter, *is* this place-name; its phonetic component 夃 (shared with [[盈]], "to be full") points to an Old Chinese root reconstructed as *leŋ.

洲, a 形声 character (semantic 水 "water" + phonetic 州), means "islet" or, in modern compounds, "continent" — seen in [[北美洲]] and [[南美洲]] ("North/South America," cross-linked from [[洲 (char)]]'s Words section) as well as [[星洲]] ("Singapore," literally "Star Islet"). 嬴洲's island, surrounded by impassable seas and accessible only to the immortals, became a recurring poetic image for Japan itself in later Chinese literature — an "isle of the immortals" lying to the east across the water, much as [[瀛]] (the variant form) is itself a component of place-names associated with Japan.
