## Notes
- [側](../characters/側.md) "side; flank" + [面](../characters/面.md) "face; surface; aspect" — stand-in for [[側]], which cannot appear independently
- A literal "side-face," 側面 names both the physical lateral surface of an object — the side of a building, a profile view of a face — and, by the same metaphorical extension that "side" undergoes in English, an aspect or facet of something abstract: a side of an argument, a side of someone's character. The compound and both senses travel together across the Sinosphere — Mandarin cèmiàn, Cantonese zak1 min6, Japanese そくめん (sokumen, which adds a further nuance of "indirectness," as in 側面から攻める "to attack from the side"), Sino-Korean 측면 (cheungmyeon), and Sino-Vietnamese trắc diện — making it one of the more reliably stable technical-register compounds for describing perspective itself.
