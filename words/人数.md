## Notes
- [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" + [数](../characters/数.md) "number; count"

**人数** (*rénshù*) is the count of persons in a given group, event, or category — a headcount, attendance figure, or total number of people. The compound is compositionally transparent and among the most workaday in the Sinographic lexicon. It appears across registers: administrative (定員人数, authorised headcount), statistical (死亡人数, death toll), logistical (参加人数, number of participants), and casual (人数分, enough for everyone).

The compound should be distinguished from related terms: [[人口]] (*rénkǒu*, "population") refers to the total population of a place as a demographic quantity, while 人数 is a situational count tied to a specific event or set. [[人員]] (*rényuán*, "personnel") carries the sense of an official roster of staff or members, implying affiliation. 人数 is simply how many people — the raw count, with no implications of residency or membership.

In Japanese the compound reads にんずう (*ninzū*), with the ず (*zu*) reflecting the voiced rendaku form of 数 (*sū*) when it follows a component in close compounds. Korean 인수 (*insu*) is the regular Sino-Korean reading. Vietnamese *nhân số* is the Sino-Vietnamese compound; vernacular usage often prefers *số người* (số + người, "number of people" in native Vietnamese order).
