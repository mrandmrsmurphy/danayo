## Notes
- [抽](../characters/抽.md) "pluck; draw out" + [出 (char)](../characters/出%20(char).md) "exit; come forth" — to draw something out from within; stand-in for [[抽]], which cannot appear independently

**抽出** (*chōuchū*) describes the act of pulling something out from a larger body — drawing a single element from a set, isolating a substance from a mixture, or retrieving specific data from a corpus. The compound is a verb-complement construction (抽 "draw/pluck" + 出 "outward"), making the directionality explicit in the same pattern as [[射出]] (emit outward) and [[滲出]] (seep out).

In **chemistry**, 抽出 (Japanese: 抽出 *chūshutsu*, Chinese: 抽取 *chōuqǔ* or 萃取 *cuìqǔ*) refers to extraction — the separation of a target substance from a mixture using differences in solubility, volatility, or affinity. **液液抽出** (liquid-liquid extraction) transfers a solute between two immiscible liquid phases; **溶媒抽出** (solvent extraction) uses an organic solvent to separate compounds from an aqueous solution. These techniques are foundational in pharmaceutical synthesis, metallurgy, food processing, and environmental chemistry.

In **statistics and research methodology**, 抽出 means sampling: 無作為抽出 (*mursakui chūshutsu*, random sampling) selects elements from a population without systematic bias. 抽出検査 (*chūshutsu kensa*, sampling inspection) tests a subset of a production batch to infer quality across the whole — the standard method in industrial quality control.

In **computing and data processing**, 抽出 is extraction in the ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) sense: pulling records from a database, retrieving relevant passages from a text corpus, or identifying features from a dataset. Natural language processing uses 情報抽出 (*jōhō chūshutsu*, information extraction) for the task of identifying named entities, relationships, and events from unstructured text.

Japanese **ちゅうしゅつ** (*chūshutsu*) is the dominant technical term across all these domains in scientific and engineering writing. Chinese more often uses 抽取 (*chōuqǔ*) for the general drawing-out sense and 萃取 (*cuìqǔ*) specifically for solvent extraction. Korean **추출** (*chuchul*) covers the full range — chemical extraction, statistical sampling, and data retrieval — and is the standard term across technical and scientific registers. Vietnamese **trừu xuất** is the Sino-Vietnamese compound; technical Vietnamese often uses the borrowed terms trích xuất or chiết xuất for chemical extraction contexts.
