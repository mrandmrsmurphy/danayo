## Notes
- [地 (char)](../characters/地%20(char).md) "land" + [境](../characters/境.md) "boundary; border"
- This word is the viable form of [境](../characters/境.md): the character's meaning (boundary; border; frontier) is identical, but 境 alone is too bounded to stand as a Dan'a'yo word. 地境 is its `stand_in` compound.

The compound reads plainly: the boundary of a territory, a piece of land's edge where one domain ends and another begins. It is the concrete, geographic expression of 境 — not an abstract realm or philosophical register, but a line on the ground.

The Korean reading 지경 (*jigyeong*) is particularly direct: 지경 is the Korean native concept-word for this same idea, and 地境 is simply its Hanja spelling. Both 境 and 界 list 지경 as their `korean_native` value, reflecting that the two characters are close synonyms in the abstract, but 地境 anchors 境 specifically to the territorial, earthly sense.

In Japanese, 地境 has two readings. The on-yomi ちきょう (CHI + KYOU) is the regular concatenation of the component characters' Sino-Japanese readings. The native compound じざかい uses the kun-yomi 地 (*ji*) and the native Japanese word さかい ("boundary; border"), with rendaku さかい → ざかい following the preceding element. Both readings are in use; ちきょう is the more formal register.
