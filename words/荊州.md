## Notes
- [荊](../characters/荊.md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Jing Province, the sixth of the [[九州]]

荊州 covered the south-central region — roughly modern Hubei and Hunan — the territory of the powerful ancient state of Chu (楚). Chu was the great southern rival of the Zhou cultural sphere, absorbing and transforming it over centuries; 荊州 was its heartland. In the Three Kingdoms period, 荊州 became the most strategically contested territory in China, fought over by Cao Cao, Liu Bei, and Sun Quan, and its control or loss determined the fate of each state. The phrase 荊州 still carries this resonance of strategic centrality and violent contest.
