## Notes
- [救](../characters/救.md) "rescue; save; relieve" + [贖](../characters/贖.md) "ransom; redeem" — the theological act by which God rescues humanity from sin and its consequences, particularly through Christ's atoning sacrifice

救贖 is the standard Christian theological term for redemption. The character 贖 specifically evokes the ancient practice of paying a ransom price to free a slave or captive: the redeemer literally "buys back" the enslaved person. In Christian theology, this metaphor frames Christ's death as the payment of a ransom that frees humanity from bondage to sin and death.

Dan'a'yo distinguishes 救贖 sharply from [[償還]], which means ordinary repayment of a financial debt or legal obligation. 償還 involves returning what is owed in a transactional sense; 救贖 involves grace, sacrifice, and liberation in a theological sense. The two are not interchangeable, even where the underlying metaphors of debt and payment overlap in ordinary speech.

The Vietnamese cứu chuộc (救贖) mirrors the compound exactly: cứu (救 = to save) + chuộc (贖 = to ransom).
