## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle" + [耳 (char)](../characters/耳%20(char).md) "ear"

中耳 is "the middle ear" — the anatomical region between the eardrum (tympanic membrane) and the inner ear (内耳). 中 (middle) + 耳 (ear). The middle ear encompasses the tympanic cavity, the three ossicles (malleus, incus, stapes — 槌骨, 砧骨, 鐙骨), and the Eustachian tube connecting to the nasopharynx. It serves to amplify and transmit sound vibrations from the eardrum to the fluid of the inner ear. The three-part anatomical division (外耳/中耳/内耳: outer ear / middle ear / inner ear) follows the classical East Asian directional-compound naming pattern for bodily structures.

Mandarin 中耳 (zhōng'ěr) is the standard anatomical term; the apostrophe in the Pinyin separates the two syllables (zhōng-ěr) to prevent misreading as zhōngěr. Cantonese 中耳 (zung1 ji5). Japanese 中耳 (ちゅうじ, chūji): 中耳炎 (otitis media = inflammation of the middle ear) is the most common clinical collocation. Korean 중이 (中耳, jungI): 중이염 (中耳炎, otitis media) is the standard clinical term. Vietnamese trung nhĩ is the Sino-Vietnamese anatomical term (中=trung, 耳=nhĩ).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 중니 differs from Korean 중이 in the second syllable. 耳 reads 니 (ni/ㄋㄧ) in Dan'a'yo but 이 (i) in Sino-Korean. MC initial ȵ (日 initial) gives Dan'a'yo ㄋ (n-), while Sino-Korean merges this initial into the following vowel to give vowel-initial 이. This n-/∅ split from MC 日 initial is the same correspondence seen in [[中国人]] (人: Dan'a'yo 닌 vs Sino-Korean 인). The clinical compound 中耳炎 (otitis media) is one of the most familiar uses of 中耳 across CJKV medical traditions.
