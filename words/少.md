## Notes
- [少 (char)](../characters/少%20(char).md) "few; a little; scarce"

少 is the scarcity quantifier in Dan'a'yo — "few; a little; scant; not many" — prefixing to a noun to indicate small quantity: 少人 (few people; not many people), 少時 (little time; briefly), 少量 (a small amount). In the Dan'a'yo quantifier system, 少 occupies the low end of quantity, contrasting with [[全]] (all; whole) and [[多]] (many; much). The character is a 象形 depicting scattered small grains — perhaps sand (compare 沙) — to indicate "a few; a little," and is phonetically related to 小 (OC *smewʔ, "small"), which depicts three scattered dots. 少 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[少]].

Mandarin 少 (shǎo, few; little; scarce): 少人 (few people), 不少 (not a few; quite a few), 多少 (how many; somewhat), 少量 (shǎoliàng, a small amount). Cantonese 少 (siu2): 少少 (siu2 siu2, a little; a bit), 多少 (dō siu2, how much; somewhat). Japanese 少ない (sukunai, few; scarce — native), 少し (sukoshi, a little — native); Sino-Japanese: 少量 (shōryō, small amount), 多少 (tashō, more or less). Korean 소 (少): 다소 (多少, more or less; somewhat), 소수 (少數, minority; small number), 희소 (稀少, rare; scarce). Vietnamese thiểu (少): thiểu số (少數, minority), ít (few; little — native), chút (a little — native).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 솟 does not match Korean 소. 少 reads 솟 (sou/ㄙㄛㄨ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 書母 (ɕ, palatal fricative) + 宵A韻 (iᴇu, triphthong). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean map MC 書母 to plain s- (ㄙ/ㅅ). The MC triphthong iᴇu: Dan'a'yo preserves the final -w glide as a coda, written ㄙㄛㄨ (-sou) and represented in Hangul as final ᆺ (솟 = s-o-s), while Korean 소 simplifies the triphthong to plain -o, dropping the terminal glide. The Dan'a'yo -ᆺ coda marking the MC -w glide is a systematic feature also seen in [[鳥]] (촛), [[草]] (찻), and [[某]] (못).
