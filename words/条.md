## Notes
- [条 (char)](../characters/条%20(char).md) "long thin objects"

条 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for long, thin, flexible objects — 量詞, counting ropes, rivers, roads, fish, snakes, legs, branches, rules, and news items: 一条路 (one road), 三条魚 (three fish), 兩条繩 (two ropes), 數条規則 (several rules). The character is a 會意 compound of 木 (wood) and 夊 (movement), with original sense "a thin branch or twig"; the classifier function developed from this characteristic shape — slender, linear, flexible. 条 has also extended productively to abstract "items" in news and legal contexts: 一条消息 (one piece of news), 一条法律 (one legal provision). 条 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[条]].

The CJKV classifier landscape for long thin objects shows strong convergence around 条/條. Mandarin 条 (tiáo) is among the most productive classifiers: 一条鱼 (one fish), 一条河 (one river), 一条路 (one road), 一条裤子 (one pair of trousers — the garment treated as a single elongated item), 一条新闻 (one news item), 一条命 (one life — "a thread of life"). Cantonese 條 (tiu4) parallels Mandarin closely: 一條魚 (one fish), 一條路 (one road), 一條褲 (one pair of trousers). Japanese does not have a direct equivalent: 本 (ほん/HON) counts long slender objects — 一本の鉛筆 (one pencil), 一本の川 (one river) — filling the role of 条 in Chinese. Korean 조 (條) appears in Sino-Korean legal and administrative compounds: 제3조 (Article 3), 조항 (條項, clause; provision); for elongated objects in colloquial speech, 줄 (line; rope) is used natively. Vietnamese điều (條) is used extensively in legal language: điều 3 (Article 3), điều kiện (條件, condition); the colloquial classifier for long objects is sợi (strand; thread: một sợi dây, one rope).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 조 exactly matches Korean 조. 条 reads 조 (jo/ㄐㄛ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 定母 (d) + 蕭韻 (eu) gives a voiced stop onset with the -o vowel. Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map the MC 蕭韻 -eu to plain -o and devoice the MC 定母 stop in syllable-initial position, producing identical 조 forms. The MC 蕭韻 is one of the cleaner convergence cases between the two systems — neither develops a medial or alters the nucleus, giving the same output on both sides.
