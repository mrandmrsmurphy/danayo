## Notes
- [独](../characters/独.md) "alone, independent" + [立 (char)](../characters/立%20(char).md) "stand"

独立 denotes the condition of standing on one's own — free from dependence on or control by another. The compound is both concrete (独立した家, a detached house; 独立した司法権, an independent judiciary) and abstract (independence as a political condition, self-sufficiency as a personal virtue). The image of 立 — standing upright — gives the compound a quality of dignity: to be independent is not merely to be separate but to be standing.

Politically, 独立 is the standard word for national independence across all CJKV languages, and its resonance differs significantly by country. In Vietnam, độc lập is inseparable from the 1945 Declaration of Independence (*Tuyên ngôn Độc lập*), written by Hồ Chí Minh and opening — deliberately — with a paraphrase of the American Declaration of Independence. The phrase độc lập — tự do — hạnh phúc ("independence, freedom, happiness") remains the official motto inscribed on all Vietnamese state documents. In Korea, 독립 is the defining term of the independence movement (독립운동) against Japanese colonial rule, centered on the March First Movement (삼일운동) of 1919. In Japan, 独立 carries the modern meaning of national and personal independence, and 独立記念日 (Independence Day) refers to foreign nations' independence holidays. In Chinese, 獨立 covers both the personal and political senses.
