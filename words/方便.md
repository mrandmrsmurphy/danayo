## Notes
- [方](../characters/方.md) "direction" + [便 (char)](../characters/便%20(char).md) "convenient" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (方's own points to `方向`, 便's own to bare `便`); 方便 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/方.md` and `characters/便 (char).md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **A striking four-way sense divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: Mandarin/Cantonese fāngbiàn/fong1 bin6 is the everyday adjective "convenient, suitable" (also a common euphemism for "to relieve oneself"). Korean 방편 and Japanese ほうべん (hōben), however, both function as a noun meaning "expedient means, a makeshift or provisional solution" — most notably the Buddhist technical term for upāya, a bodhisattva's skillful, situationally-adapted teaching method (方便品, the "Expedient Means" chapter of the Lotus Sutra). Vietnamese phương tiện has drifted furthest of all: it now means "means; tool; vehicle" in ordinary modern usage (phương tiện giao thông "means of transportation/vehicle," phương tiện thông tin đại chúng "mass media"), a semantic shift from "convenient method" to "the physical instrument itself." A learner should not assume "convenient" carries across all four languages.
