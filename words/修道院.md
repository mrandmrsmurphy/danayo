## Notes
- [修](../characters/修.md) "to cultivate; to practise; to repair" + [道 (char)](../characters/道%20(char).md) "Way; path; Tao" + [院](../characters/院.md) "courtyard institution; enclosed compound" — an enclosed religious community devoted to the cultivation of the Way; covers monasteries, abbeys, and convents regardless of gender

修道 as a compound means the practice of spiritual cultivation — the disciplined, enclosed life of contemplation and religious observance. 院 denotes an institutional compound (as in 寺院, a Buddhist temple complex). Together, 修道院 is the standard Sinosphere term for a Christian monastic institution, used across Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant reference works.

Dan'a'yo uses 修道院 to cover all three institution types listed in the [[基督敎]] lexipedia: abbey (a monastery headed by an abbot), convent (a house of nuns), and monastery (the general term for enclosed religious communities). The word carries no gender specification in Chinese usage, making it appropriate for this broad coverage.
