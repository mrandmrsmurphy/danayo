## Notes
- [東](../characters/東.md) "east" + [芝](../characters/芝.md) "grass (Shibaura)"

東芝 is the name of Toshiba (東芝株式会社), the Japanese multinational electronics and infrastructure corporation. The name is a contraction of **東京芝浦電気** (Tōkyō Shibaura Denki, "Tokyo Shibaura Electric"), the company's former full name, itself formed from the merger of Shibaura Engineering Works (芝浦製作所, founded 1875) and Tokyo Electric Company (東京電気, founded 1890) in 1939. 芝 here refers to Shibaura (芝浦), a waterfront district in Minato, Tokyo — 芝浦 means "Shiba inlet," with 芝 as a place name element (the Shiba area of old Edo). 東 abbreviates Tokyo.

In Korean, 동지 (東芝) is a near-homophone of 동지 (冬至, winter solstice) and 동지 (同志, comrade) — context always disambiguates. The company is known in Korean as 도시바 (Dosiba) by transliteration. The simplified Chinese form 东芝 is used in mainland China.
