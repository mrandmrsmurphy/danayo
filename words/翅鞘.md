## Notes
- [翅](../characters/翅.md) "wing, fin" + [鞘](../characters/鞘.md) "scabbard, sheath"

翅鞘 (elytron; pl. elytra) is the hardened forewing of beetles (Order Coleoptera) and related insects, which has been modified from a flight wing into a rigid protective covering for the hindwings and abdomen. The compound is directly descriptive: 翅 (wing) + 鞘 (scabbard, sheath) — a wing-sheath, or a wing that functions as a sheath. When a beetle is at rest, the two elytra meet precisely along the midline of the back; when in flight, they are raised and held clear while the membranous hindwings do the actual work of flying.

The elytron is one of the defining features of the beetle order, whose success — Coleoptera is the largest order of animals on Earth, with over 400,000 described species — is partly attributed to the protection elytra afford in tight spaces and against desiccation. In Japanese (ししょう, shishō), the term is used in entomological writing. In Korean (시초), the Sino-Korean reading is a homophone of 始初 (시초, "beginning, origin"), though context always distinguishes them.
