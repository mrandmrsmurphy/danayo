## Notes
- [権](../characters/権.md) "authority; expedient measure" + [謀 (char)](../characters/謀%20(char).md) "scheme; stratagem"

権謀 pairs 権 in its classical sense of "expedient adaptation" — from the image of a weighing scale (権 originally meant a counterweight) — with 謀 "scheme; stratagem." The compound captures strategic flexibility in the service of power: the art of reading a situation and adapting one's means accordingly, rather than following fixed principles. It sits in the tradition of Chinese realpolitik and is related to but distinct from 陰謀 (covert conspiracy) and 策略 (tactical planning); 権謀 specifically implies the cunning deployment of authority.

The compound most often appears in the four-character phrase 権謀術数 (quánmóu shùshù; けんぼうじゅっすう kenbo jutsusū; 권모술수 gwonmo sulsu) — "stratagems and calculations" — which is the pan-Sinospheric idiom for political machination and unscrupulous maneuvering. In all three languages the phrase carries a predominantly negative register, implying cunning that shades into manipulation. A politician described as 権謀術数に長けた ("skilled in 権謀術数") is being accused of Machiavellian scheming rather than praised for resourcefulness.

In classical Chinese political thought, 権 as "expedient measure" (権宜, quányí) was a recognized concept in Confucian ethics — the ability to depart from fixed rules when circumstances demand. 権謀 extends this into the domain of statecraft and intrigue, a combination that later Chinese commentators often associated with the Legalist and Strategist (縦横家) traditions rather than the Confucian mainstream.
