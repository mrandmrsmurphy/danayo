## Notes
- [知 (char)](../characters/知%20(char).md) "know"

知 is "to know" and, as a noun, "knowledge." The character combines [[矢]] "arrow" with 口 "mouth" — traditionally explained as speech (口) as swift and direct as an arrow (矢), i.e. knowing something well enough to state it plainly and quickly. It is highly productive in Dan'a'yo compounds spanning ordinary cognition ([[知識]] "knowledge", [[熟知]] "familiar with, well-versed") and formal notification ([[通知]] "notify", [[報知]] "report, inform"), plus the set phrase [[知音]] "bosom friend" (literally "one who knows your music/tone," from the legend of Yu Boya and Zhong Ziqi).

Mandarin 知 (zhī, "to know"). Cantonese 知 (zi1). Japanese has the on'yomi ち (chi), used in the abstract-noun compounds (知識 chishiki "knowledge", 通知 tsūchi "notification") — the reading recorded here — alongside the native verb 知る (しる, shiru, "to know"), which carries the same core meaning but as a full conjugating verb rather than a bound compound element. Korean 지 (ji) is the Sino-Korean reading, traditionally recited by its hun-eum mnemonic "알 지" (al ji — 알, "know," paired with the sound 지); only the sound half, 지, appears in actual Sino-Korean compounds like 지식 (知識, "knowledge"). Vietnamese tri is the Sino-Vietnamese reading (tri thức "knowledge," mirroring 知識).

知 has a well-known extended/differentiated character, [[智]] "wisdom" (zhì in Mandarin, a different tone; trí, not tri, in Vietnamese) — historically 知 with 日 added to specialize the meaning from "knowing" to "wisdom." Despite the close etymological relationship, 智 has its own full character entry in this vault rather than being listed as a mere orthographic variant of 知, since the two now carry distinct readings and meanings in every daughter language.

知 also has three coincidental Dan'a'yo homophones sharing its exact reading ㄐㄨㄧ/jui/쥐: [[池]] "pond, reservoir," [[酔]] "drunk," and [[馳]] "to run fast, drive quickly" — four otherwise-unrelated characters that happen to converge on the same Middle Chinese syllable.
