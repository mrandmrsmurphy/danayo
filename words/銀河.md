## Notes
- [銀 (char)](../characters/銀%20(char).md) "silver" + [河](../characters/河.md) "river"

銀河 is literally the "Silver River" — the arc of the Milky Way seen against the night sky, which Chinese astronomers described as a river of silver light. The image is one of the oldest in East Asian astronomy and poetry, attested in the *Classic of Poetry* (詩経) and in Han-dynasty astronomical texts.

The name is inseparable from the legend of the Weaver Girl and the Cowherd: [織女星](織女星.md) (Vega) and [[牛郎]] (Altair) are lovers separated by the Silver River, permitted to cross it on a bridge of magpies only once a year on [[七夕]] (the seventh night of the seventh month). 銀河 thus carries both an astronomical and a deeply romantic register in Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese literary tradition.

In modern usage the word has extended to mean "galaxy" in general (especially in Japanese, where 銀河 is the standard scientific term and the Milky Way is more specifically 天の川). Korean uses 은하 for galaxy and 은하수 (Silver River Water) specifically for the Milky Way. Vietnamese retains Ngân Hà as the literary name. See also [[織女星]].
