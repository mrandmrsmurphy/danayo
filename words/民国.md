## Notes
- [民](../characters/民.md) "citizen" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation"

**民国** (*mínguó*, "people's state") is the standard East Asian compound for a republic — a state governed in the name of the people rather than a monarch. It is the shared element in [[中華民国]] (Republic of China, established 1912) and [[大韓民国]] (Republic of Korea, established 1948), and as a standalone term most commonly refers to the Republic of China era and its continuing calendar system, still used in Taiwan.

The compound contrasts with 帝国 ("empire") and 王国 ("kingdom"), marking the shift from dynastic rule to republican government in early twentieth-century East Asia.
