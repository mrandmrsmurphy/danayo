## Notes
- [折](../characters/折.md) "to fold; to bend" + [線](../characters/線.md) "line" — a line made of straight segments joined at angles

**折線** (*zhéxiàn*, "broken line") is the mathematical term for a polyline (also called a polygonal chain): a connected series of straight line segments joined end to end at angles, as opposed to a single straight line ([[直線]]) or a smooth [[曲線]]. The compound is directly compositional — 折 ("to fold, to bend at an angle") qualifying 線 ("line") — and the three terms 直線, 曲線, and 折線 together cover the basic taxonomy of plane-geometric lines: straight, smoothly curved, and angularly segmented.

折線 appears throughout elementary and applied mathematics. In statistics and data visualisation, a **折線図/折線グラフ** (line chart / line graph) plots data points connected by straight segments — the most common chart type for showing change over time. In geometry, a 折線 may be **開折線** (open polyline, with distinct start and end points) or, when its endpoints coincide, form a **多角形** (polygon) — every simple polygon's boundary is a closed 折線. In computer graphics and geographic information systems, 折線 (often under the English loanword "polyline") is a fundamental primitive for representing roads, borders, and paths as ordered sequences of vertices.

Japanese **せっせん** (*setsusen*) and Korean **절선** (*jeolseon*) are the standard terms in their respective mathematics curricula, parallel in formation and usage to 曲線/きょくせん/곡선. Vietnamese *chiết tuyến* is the Sino-Vietnamese form; the more common Vietnamese mathematical term is the native compound **đường gấp khúc** ("folded-and-bent line"), paralleling the relationship between Sino-Vietnamese *khúc tuyến* and native *đường cong* noted at [[曲線]].
