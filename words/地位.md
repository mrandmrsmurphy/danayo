## Notes
- [地 (char)](../characters/地%20(char).md) "earth, ground" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank"

地位 names a person's (or a nation's, or an institution's) standing in society — social status, rank, or importance. 地 is a 形声 compound combining the semantic radical 土 ("earth") with the phonetic component 也; its core meaning of "ground, land, place" is already extensively developed in [[地 (char)]]'s own Words section, from [[土地]] ("land") and [[地球]] ("Earth, the planet") to the more abstract [[陣地]] ("military position") and [[地点]] ("point, location").

地位 extends that spatial vocabulary metaphorically: just as [[陣地]] is the literal "ground" a military unit holds, 地位 is the figurative "ground" a person stands on in a social hierarchy — "to have a high 地位" is to occupy elevated metaphorical terrain, exactly as [[上位]] and [[下位]] (earlier in this batch) frame hierarchy along a vertical axis. The parallel between literal terrain (地) and social terrain (地位) is a cross-linguistically common metaphor, reflected in English expressions like "high ground," "standing," and "rank."
