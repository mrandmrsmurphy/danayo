## Notes
- [胸](../characters/胸.md) "chest; breast; bosom" + [部](../characters/部.md) "part" — stand-in for [[胸]], which cannot appear independently

Mandarin xiōngbù, Cantonese hung1 bou6, Japanese きょうぶ (kyōbu), and Korean 흉부 (hyungbu) are all the standard formal/clinical register word for "chest, thorax" (胸部レントゲン, "chest X-ray"; 흉부외과, "thoracic surgery"), each alongside a more everyday native alternative for the body part in casual speech (Japanese 胸 alone, むね; Korean 가슴). Vietnamese keeps the honest compositional hung bộ in the frontmatter, but the everyday, essentially universal word is the native ngực; hung survives mainly bound in more literary/figurative compounds about one's inner feelings (胸襟 hung khâm, "breadth of mind"; 胸次 hung thứ) rather than as a productive anatomical term on its own.
