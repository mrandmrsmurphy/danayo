## Notes
- [史](../characters/史.md) "history; chronicle" + [学](../characters/学.md) "learning; study" + [者 (char)](../characters/者%20(char).md) "-er; person" — together forming [[史学]] "historiography"
- 者 functions here as a suffix meaning "person who practises X", parallel to its use in 学者 (scholar), 医者 (physician), and 文学者 (literary scholar). Note that like 家, 者 is **not a productive suffix in Dan'a'yo** — each 者-compound must be listed as its own word.

**史学者** is a scholar whose professional domain is [[史学]] — the critical study of the past. The term is the standard East Asian academic title for a historian at the research or university level, distinguishing a specialist in historical method and interpretation from a mere chronicler or enthusiast. It corresponds closely to Japanese しがくしゃ, Korean 사학자, and Vietnamese sử học giả, all of which carry the same academic register.
