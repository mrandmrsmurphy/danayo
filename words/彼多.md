## Notes
- [彼 (char)](../characters/彼%20(char).md) "yon; distal demonstrative; he/she (literary)" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

彼多 is the distal quantity correlative — "that much (distal); that great a quantity; as much as that over there." Like 彼処 (that place yonder) and 彼時 (that time back then), 彼多 refers to a quantity that is removed from the immediate discourse frame — a number or amount witnessed from afar, recalled from a distant context, or associated with another party's sphere. The distal quantity can carry an evaluative charge: 彼多もある ("there's that great a quantity — expressing wonder or distance").

Japanese あれほど (arehodo, "to that great extent — distal") is the precise parallel and frequently carries a tone of awe or retrospective assessment: あれほどの人数 ("a number of people that great"); あれほど言ったのに ("I told you that many times — distal/retrospective"). Korean 저만큼 (jeomankkeum, "that much — distal") is the distal member of the 이만큼/그만큼/저만큼 quantity adverb set. Mandarin 那麼多 (nàme duō) covers both medial and distal, merging the distinction.

The original 彼多 file carried the incorrect Korean form 저많은 — an attributive adjective form (그 + 많은 = "that many, adjective") rather than the adverbial quantity word 저만큼 ("that much, to that extent"). The correct Korean adverbial quantity pronoun is 저만큼, paralleling 이만큼 (此多) and 그만큼 (其多). The 이만큼/그만큼/저만큼 triad in Korean provides a clean three-way deictic quantity paradigm matching Dan'a'yo's 此多/其多/彼多.
