## Notes
- [打](../characters/打.md) "hit" + [撃](../characters/撃.md) "strike; hit; beat" — stand-in for [[打]], which cannot appear independently

**Small fix**: `mandarin: "dǎjī, dǎjí"` carried a second, apparently spurious tone variant (dǎjí) with no attestation found in dictionaries — removed, keeping only the standard `dǎjī`.

Mandarin dǎjī, Cantonese daa2 gik1, Japanese だげき (dageki), and Korean 타격 (tagyeok) are all the standard word for a physical "strike, blow, impact" — extending, in every one of these languages, to a figurative "blow" or shock (a psychological/economic "hit"). Vietnamese keeps the field value đánh, the versatile everyday native verb "to hit" in the most general sense (đánh nhau, "to fight"; đánh trống, "to beat a drum"); the real Sino-Vietnamese cognate đả kích is attested but has narrowed specifically to a verbal/rhetorical register — "to attack, to criticize sharply" (in debate, social commentary) — rather than this word's physical-impact sense, a semantic-narrowing parallel to [[恐怖]]'s khủng bố finding two iterations ago.
