## Notes
- [公 (char)](../characters/公%20(char).md) "public, impartial" + [平](../characters/平.md) "even, level"

公平 describes treatment or judgment that is even-handed and unbiased — literally, public (公) and level (平). The compound frames fairness as the absence of favoritism: what is 公 (open, held in common, not private or partial) and 平 (balanced, neither raised nor lowered for any party). The two characters reinforce each other: 公 from the angle of transparency and shared ownership, 平 from the angle of equivalence and equilibrium.

The word is active across all CJKV languages in legal, moral, and everyday discourse. In Chinese (gōngpíng), it is the standard term for fairness and is productive in 公平競爭 (fair competition), 公平交易 (fair trade), 公平正義 (fairness and justice). In Japanese (こうへい, kōhei), 公平 is the formal term for impartiality, often contrasted with 不公平 (unfair) and used in institutional contexts — judicial decisions, grading, resource distribution. In Korean (공평), the word appears in both formal and everyday speech; the related 평등 (平等, equality) and 형평 (衡平, equity/balance) occupy adjacent semantic space. In Vietnamese (công bình), the Hán-Việt form is used in formal writing; the everyday colloquial is often công bằng.
