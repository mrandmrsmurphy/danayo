## Notes
- [二 (char)](../characters/二%20(char).md) "two" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person"

A pair of people; a couple. The word carries both a neutral counting sense ("there are two people here") and an intimate one: "the two of us," "a couple" (romantic or otherwise).

The Japanese reading **ふたり** (futari) is remarkable in that it preserves an ancient native Japanese counting system that is otherwise almost entirely extinct. While Sino-Japanese numerals (一人=いちにん, 三人=さんにん) are used for most counts of people, the first two have fossilized native forms: ひとり (hitori, one person) and **ふたり** (futari, two people). The component ふた- derives from the Old Japanese word for "two" (cognate with Korean 둘 dul), and -り is an archaic counter for persons. Together ふたり is one of the most common words in the Japanese language, used in everything from everyday speech to classical poetry.

In Korean, 이인 (二人) is the Sino-Korean form, while the native Korean equivalent is 두 사람 (du saram).
