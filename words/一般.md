## Notes
- [一 (char)](../characters/一%20(char).md) "one" + [般 (char)](../characters/般%20(char).md) "category, kind" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

**Real typo corrected**: `mandarin: jībān` — 一 is never read jī; corrected to `yìbān`, the standard reading.

Mandarin yìbān, Cantonese jat1 bun1, Japanese いっぱん (ippan), and Korean 일반 (ilban) are all the standard, everyday word for "general, ordinary, common" across the sphere (一般人/일반인, "an ordinary person"; 一般的, "generally speaking"). Vietnamese nhất ban is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search) carrying the identical range of senses — ordinary, common, average, alike.
