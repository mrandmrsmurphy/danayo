## Notes
- [巡](../characters/巡.md) "to patrol; go on circuit" + [回](../characters/回%20(char).md) "to turn; rotate; time" — stand-in for [[巡]], which cannot appear independently
- 巡回 is the ordinary word for making one's rounds — a watchman walking his beat, a doctor or judge traveling a circuit of towns, a touring troupe carrying a play from city to city. Both halves of the compound already mean something close to "go around" on their own (巡 carries the specific sense of an inspection tour, the rounds a guard or official makes; 回 is the bare verb of turning or returning), so doubling them produces a word that is less about the destination than about the looping motion itself — the same ground covered again and again. Mandarin xúnhuí, Cantonese ceon4 wui4, Sino-Japanese じゅんかい (junkai), Sino-Korean 순회 (sunhoe), and Sino-Vietnamese tuần hồi all preserve this sense intact, and all five languages use the word equally naturally for police patrols, traveling exhibitions, medical outreach teams, and theatrical tours — a rare case where a Sino-Xenic compound's semantic range has barely drifted at all across the cultural sphere.
