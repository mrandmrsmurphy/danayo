## Notes
- [錐](../characters/錐.md) "awl" + [子](../characters/子.md) "nominalizing suffix" — stand-in for [[錐]], which cannot appear independently
- 錐子 is the everyday workman's word for the awl — the small sharp-pointed hand tool used to pierce starting-holes in leather, wood, or cloth before a needle, screw, or nail can follow. The bare character 錐 already carries the meaning on its own (and survives independently in compounds like 圓錐 "cone"), but in colloquial Mandarin and Cantonese speech the noun almost always appears with the nominalizing suffix 子 attached — zhuīzi, zeoi1 zi2 — exactly as countless other small household objects do (椅子, 帽子, 刷子). Japanese, lacking the productive 子-suffixing pattern, instead reaches for the native word きり for the tool itself, leaving すいし (suishi) as a more bookish, dictionary-attested alternative that mirrors the Chinese compound character-for-character; Sino-Korean 추자 (chuja) occupies a similarly marginal, literary register, with 송곳 remaining the everyday native-Korean word for "awl."
