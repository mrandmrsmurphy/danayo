## Notes
- [爵](../characters/爵.md) "peerage; ceremonial wine vessel" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, rank"

爵位 names a title of nobility — duke, marquis, count, viscount, baron, and their East Asian equivalents — and the rank it confers. 爵 is a 会意 character whose original meaning was a three-legged bronze vessel for warming and pouring ceremonial wine, used in Zhou-dynasty investiture rituals; the vessel itself became metonymic for the rank it was awarded alongside, much as English "to be knighted" derives a verb from the ceremonial sword-tap. 爵's only other cross-linked compound, [[男爵]] ("baron," from [[爵]]'s Words section), names one specific rank within the system that 爵位 names as a whole.

爵位 sits one tier below [[王位]] and [[帝位]] in the hierarchy this batch traces: 帝位 (emperor) > 王位 (king) > 爵位 (the various ranks of nobility serving under a king or emperor). The five-rank European-style peerage (公侯伯子男, duke/marquis/count/viscount/baron) used in modern Japanese and Korean translations of Western nobility is itself a revival of an ancient Zhou-dynasty ranking system, attesting to how deep this vocabulary's roots run.
