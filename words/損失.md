## Notes
- [損](../characters/損.md) "diminish, harm" + [失](../characters/失.md) "lose"

損失 is a near-synonym compound: 損, whose graphemic component 員 (originally a phonetic, now reanalyzed) sits beside the hand radical 扌, denotes damage or reduction — to make something less than it was — while 失, a pictograph of a hand letting something slip away, denotes the act of losing possession outright. Together they cover the full range of "loss," from gradual erosion of value to sudden, total deprivation.

The word functions as both verb ("to suffer a loss, to lose") and noun ("a loss, damages"), and is the standard term for financial and material loss in all of Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean — covering everything from a company's quarterly losses to casualties in war (損失人員). It contrasts with the related compound 喪失 (sōshitsu/상실), which leans more toward the loss of abstract qualities — confidence, rights, memory — than of concrete property.

Both constituent characters are independently productive in Dan'a'yo: 損 also appears in compounds like 破損 (damage, breakage), and 失 in 失業 (unemployment) and 失礼 (rudeness), already cross-linked from [[失]]'s own Words section.
