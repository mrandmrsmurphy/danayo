## Notes
- [工](../characters/工.md) "work" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (工's is 工作, 人's is bare 人). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule, since both are individually `kwin: false`.
- All cross-linguistic fields were already correct, standard readings: Mandarin gōngrén, Cantonese gung1 jan4, Japanese こうじん (also こうにん; confirmed real, meaning "craftsman; artisan," verified via search), Korean 공인 (confirmed real via search, meaning "craftsman; laborer"), and Vietnamese công nhân are all everyday/attested words for "worker; craftsman."
- **Genuine homophone-across-different-hanja, not an error**: Korean 공인 for 工人 is coincidentally identical in sound to the entirely unrelated and far more common compounds 공인(公認, "official recognition") and 공인(公人, "public figure") — verified via search that all three are distinct, real words. Context always disambiguates.
