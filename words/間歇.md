## Notes
- [間](../characters/間.md) "interval; space between" + [歇](../characters/歇.md) "to rest; to cease; to stop midway"
- 間歇 is the stand-in word for [歇](../characters/歇.md) ("to rest; to cease"), which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo. Together the compound names a gap (間) of rest or cessation (歇): something that pauses and resumes rather than running continuously.

Note that Japanese technical and scientific registers generally write this word as 間欠 (かんけつ) rather than 間歇 — a same-sound kanji substitution (同音の漢字による書きかえ) that replaced the rarer 歇 with the common-use 欠 in vocabulary like 間欠泉 ("geyser"), 間欠熱 ("intermittent fever"), and 間欠性 ("intermittency"). That substitution works in Japanese because 歇 and 欠 happen to share the on'yomi けつ there. Dan'a'yo does not follow it: 歇 ("rest, cease," ㄏㄝㄊ/hed) and 欠 ("yawn, lack," ㄎ⼘ㄇ/kyam) are assigned distinct sounds and distinct meanings under 一字一音 and 毎字明意, so collapsing them into one glyph would erase a real semantic and phonological distinction. 間歇 is written with 歇 directly.
