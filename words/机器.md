## Notes
- [机](../characters/机.md) "machine" + [器](../characters/器.md) "container, vessel" — literally "mechanism-vessel," a device that holds and performs a mechanical function. Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (机's own points to `机会`, 器's own to `容器`); 机器 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/机.md` and `characters/器.md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **Genuinely close but not identical to [[机械]]**: 机器/機器 jīqì is the broad, general word for "machine" or "device" of any kind (机器人 "robot," 洗衣机 "washing machine" uses the bound form 机), while 机械/機械 jīxiè leans toward "machinery" in the more mechanical, often industrial sense (moving parts, mechanisms). Korean 기기 (both characters happen to share the reading 기) is likewise the everyday word for "device, apparatus" (전기기기 "electrical apparatus"). Vietnamese cơ khí specifically means "mechanical engineering/machinery" (kỹ sư cơ khí "mechanical engineer") — closer to the 机械 sense than a general-purpose "machine" word.
