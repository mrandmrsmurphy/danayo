## Notes
- [方](../characters/方.md) "direction, square" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position"

方位 names a direction or bearing — a point of the compass, the orientation of a building, or the bearing from one location to another. 方, a 象形 character of disputed origin (traditionally read as two boats lashed side-by-side, though modern scholarship is less certain), is the basic word for "direction, side, square," anchoring [[五方]] ("the Five Cardinal Directions" — the four compass points plus the center) and the cardinal-direction compounds [[東方]], [[西方]], [[南方]], [[北方]] all cross-linked from [[方]]'s own Words section.

方位 sits at the geographic end of the spectrum this batch of 位-words traces: while [[即位]]/[[在位]]/[[王位]] use 位 for political office and [[地位]]/[[上位]]/[[下位]] use it for social rank, 方位 — alongside [[水位]] — uses 位 for orientation in physical space. Traditional East Asian geomancy (風水, fengshui) relies heavily on 方位, assigning each of the eight or twenty-four traditional directions cosmological significance for the placement of buildings, graves, and furniture.
