## Notes
- [午](../characters/午.md) "noon" + [飯](../characters/飯.md) "cooked rice; food" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (午's is 正午, 飯's is 米飯). `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/午.md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/飯.md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **Wrong-word conflation corrected**: `korean` previously held 점심, the modern everyday Korean word for "lunch" — but that word is actually 點心 (a completely different compound, lit. "dot the heart/mind," a snack-turned-lunch term), not a reading of 午飯 at all. The genuine Sino-Korean reading of 午飯 is 오반 (verified via search) — a real but now old-fashioned/historical term that referred to a more substantial midday meal, as distinct from the originally-lighter 점심. Corrected the field to 오반 and documented 점심's actual identity in prose.
- Japanese ごはん is genuinely correct for 午飯 (confirmed via multiple dictionaries — goo, Weblio, Kotobank all attest 午飯 read ごはん, meaning "lunch"), but it happens to be a homophone of the entirely unrelated everyday word 御飯 ("cooked rice; a meal," written with 御 not 午) — another instance of the recurring Sino-Japanese homophone-across-different-hanja pattern seen elsewhere in this sweep (cf. [[羽翼]]/右翼, [[思考]]'s 사고 cluster).
- Deduplicated a comma-dump `vietnamese` field (`bữa trưa, bữa ăn trưa`, two native synonyms rather than a genuine reading of the Sino-compound) down to bữa trưa, the shorter and more common of the two; bữa ăn trưa is an equally valid longer synonym.
