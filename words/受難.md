## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "to receive, undergo" + [難](../characters/難.md) "difficulty, disaster"

受難 means "to undergo hardship, to suffer disaster" — and, in specifically Christian usage (borrowed via Japanese and Chinese Bible translation), names "the Passion of Christ" (受難週, "Holy Week"; 受難曲, "a Passion," as in Bach's St. Matthew Passion). 難, a character of complex 漢-type graphemic origin, means "difficult, disaster, calamity" — already developed in [[殉難]] ("martyrdom"), [[苦難]] ("hardship, tribulation"), and [[艱難]] ("arduous trials," all cross-linked from [[難]]'s Words section).

受難 is the second of five 受-compounds in this batch, and pairs naturally with [[受賞]] (the previous word) as a kind of dark mirror: where 受賞 is "to receive something good" (an award), 受難 is "to receive something terrible" (suffering, disaster) — together illustrating how 受's neutral core sense of "to receive, to undergo" takes its emotional valence entirely from what follows it. The Christian theological sense of 受難 — Christ's suffering accepted willingly for the sake of others — adds yet another layer: not merely passive misfortune, but suffering *undergone* in a morally significant, even redemptive sense.
