## Notes
- [工](../characters/工.md) "work; craft; industry; artisan" + [廠](../characters/廠.md) "workshop; factory shed; large covered work space"

工廠 is "factory; manufacturing plant" — 工 (work, craft, production) + 廠 (a large covered building for industrial work, a workshop or factory shed). The 廠 character specifically denotes a physical industrial space rather than industry as an abstraction, giving 工廠 the concrete sense of a place where manufacturing happens. This is the standard Mandarin and Cantonese term for factory.

Mandarin 工廠 (gōngchǎng) is the mainland Chinese standard: 汽車工廠 (car factory), 電子工廠 (electronics plant). Cantonese 工廠 (gung1 cong2) is standard in Hong Kong — 工廠區 (industrial estate). Japanese uses 工場 (こうじょう, kōjō or こうば, kōba) rather than 工廠 — 廠 is not standard in Japanese vocabulary, so Japanese defaults to 場 (place/ground). Korean 공장 (gongjang) = 工場 (工 + 場) for the same reason — 廠 does not feature in Korean, and Korean inherited the Japanese/literary form 工場. Vietnamese công xưởng (công = 工, xưởng = 廠 in Sino-Vietnamese) is the standard factory term.

The divergence between 工廠 (Chinese/Dan'a'yo standard, with 廠 = factory shed) and 工場 (Japanese/Korean standard, with 場 = place/ground) reflects different historical channels for this vocabulary within the Sinosphere. Dan'a'yo follows the Mandarin/Cantonese convention with 工廠 as the primary entry. The Japanese 工場 was noted as a possible alternative (こうじょう, kōjō, the cognate form), but 工廠 is retained as the entry headword since it more precisely encodes the "factory building" sense and aligns with the Chinese mainstream.
