## Notes
- [或 (char)](../characters/或%20(char).md) "or; perhaps; some; a certain"

或 is a linker (連接詞) in Dan'a'yo meaning "or" — the disjunctive conjunction, contrasting with [[而]] and [[与]] which both express "and." As a linker, 或 connects clauses or noun phrases of equal status where one or the other (but not necessarily both) holds: A 或 B ("A or B"). In Classical Chinese, 或 also carries the sense "perhaps; maybe; someone; a certain X" — an indefinite pronoun use that survives in compounds like 或者 (huòzhě, or; perhaps) and 或然 (perhaps; possibly). Dan'a'yo uses 或 in its conjunctive role; the indefinite/modal senses are secondary. Vietnamese hoặc — the standard everyday word for "or" — is the direct Sino-Vietnamese reflex of 或, making this one of the rarer cases where the Sino-Vietnamese reading has entirely displaced any native equivalent in common usage.

或 (ㄏㄛㄎ/hog/혹 — Mandarin huò, Cantonese waak6, Korean 혹, Japanese WAKU/わく, Vietnamese hoặc): "or; perhaps; some" — MC 匣母 (ɣ) + 職韻 (wək), entering tone. The character 或 is the graphic origin of 國 (country; state) — 或 originally depicted a walled territory with a spear, and 國 added the outer 囗 (enclosure) for disambiguation; the "or; perhaps" meaning arose from phonetic borrowing. The MC 匣母 (ɣ, voiced velar fricative) devoices to ㄏ (h-) in Dan'a'yo, matching Korean ㅎ (h-). The entering-tone coda -k is preserved in Dan'a'yo as ㄎ and in Korean as ㄱ — Cantonese waak6 and Vietnamese hoặc also preserve the entering-tone checked syllable, while Mandarin huò and Japanese WAKU dissolve the coda (-k → null in Mandarin, → vowel extension in Japanese wa-ku).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 혹 matches Korean 혹 exactly. MC 匣母 (ɣ) → both give h- (Dan'a'yo ㄏ, Korean ㅎ). MC 職韻 (wək) → both give -ok with preserved entering-tone -k coda (Dan'a'yo ㄎ, Korean ㄱ). The entering-tone preservation — dropped in Mandarin and Japanese — is retained in both Dan'a'yo and Korean here, as also seen in [[不]] (볻 vs 부 — where Dan'a'yo preserves but Korean drops) and [[十]] (십=십 — where both preserve). 或 joins [[十]] as a case where the entering-tone coda lands identically in both, producing a full kwin match.
