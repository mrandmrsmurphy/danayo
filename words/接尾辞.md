## Notes
- [接 (char)](../characters/接%20(char).md) "attach, connect" + [尾 (char)](../characters/尾%20(char).md) "tail" + [辞](../characters/辞.md) "word, expression" — an element attached to the "tail" of a word: a suffix.

This three-character form was chosen over the bare two-character 接尾, which is not an independent word in any of the source languages — Japanese and Korean both require a final element naming the unit being attached (語/辞 in Japanese 接尾語/接尾辞, 辭 in Korean 접미사, whose hanja is 接尾辭 — written here with 辞, the alias under which the vault already records 辭). The grammar book's own three **制限接尾辞** ("restrictive suffixes") and its nominalizing **接尾辞** are both named with the full three-character form, matching this entry rather than the bare root. Note that Mandarin and Cantonese instead use an entirely different formation, 后缀/词尾 ("tail of the word"), so this word's pedigree runs through Japanese and Korean rather than the Sinitic branch — still satisfying the "more than Japanese" standard via Korean's well-established 접미사. Attested across Japanese and Korean.
