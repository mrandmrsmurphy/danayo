## Notes
- [如 (char)](../characters/如%20(char).md) "like, as" + [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what"

如何 is "how, in what manner" and, as a suggestion marker, "how about, what about" — literally "like what," a fusion of 如 "like, as" with the interrogative 何 "what." It remains genuinely current in modern Mandarin (unlike some of this sweep's other classical-register X-等 pronouns), sitting alongside the more colloquial 怎麼樣 (zěnmeyàng) as the more formal/written choice.

Mandarin 如何 (rúhé, "how" — 近況如何 "how are things lately," a common formal inquiry). Cantonese 如何 (jyu4ho4). Japanese 如何 (いかが, ikaga) is a real, everyday, and notably *polite* word for "how" — いかがですか is the standard formal way to offer something or ask after someone's preference or condition, more polite than plain どう (dō). Korean 여하 (yeoha) survives mainly as a bound root inside longer adverbs rather than standing fully alone — 여하튼 (yeohateun, "anyway, in any case"), 여하간 (yeohagan, same meaning), 여하한 (yeohahan, "whatever kind of"). Vietnamese như hà is attested with the same double sense as the Chinese original, "thế nào" (how) or, in some contexts, "cớ sao" (why) — though thế nào (native) is the far more common everyday phrasing.

kwin is false: neither 如 nor 何 individually match their own Sino-Korean readings against Dan'a'yo — 如's Dan'a'yo 뇨 differs from Korean 여, and although 何's own `kwin` is `true` in isolation (하=하), the compound as a whole comes out `false` since one mismatch (如) is enough under the AND-rule established earlier in this sweep.
