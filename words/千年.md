## Notes
- [千](../characters/千.md) "thousand" + [年 (char)](../characters/年%20(char).md) "year" — a span of one thousand years; the junction separator · is required between the two ㄋ's

**千年** (*qiānnián*) is the plain durational term for a period of one thousand years — a millennium as a length of time, not as a named historical unit. It answers the question "how long?" rather than "which one?". For the counted historical unit — the First Millennium, the Third Millennium — see [[千年紀]], which follows the same pattern as [[世紀]] (century) with the 紀 suffix marking a named periodisation unit.

千年 appears freely in historical, literary, and everyday contexts: 千年の歴史 "a history spanning a thousand years," 千年前 "a thousand years ago," 千年後 "a thousand years hence." It carries no inherent grandiosity — it is simply the arithmetical product of 千 and 年, as straightforward as [[百年]] (a hundred years) or [[万年]] (ten thousand years). In poetry and classical literature across the Sinosphere, 千年 is a conventional marker of deep time and enduring legacy, as in the stock phrase 千年不朽 "imperishable for a thousand years."

Japanese **千年** (*sennen*) is the ordinary compound used in the same range of contexts. Korean **천년** (*cheonnyeon*) is equally standard; the Korean literary and pop-cultural register has produced numerous 천년-compounds invoking historical depth. Vietnamese **thiên niên** is the Sino-Vietnamese form; Vietnamese more commonly uses the native **nghìn năm** or **ngàn năm** (literally "a thousand years") in everyday and poetic speech.
