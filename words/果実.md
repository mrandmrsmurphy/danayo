## Notes
- [果](../characters/果.md) "fruit; berry; candy; pastry" + [実](../characters/実.md) "true; accurate" — stand-in for [[果]], which cannot appear independently

果's own gloss ("fruit") already carries the word's core meaning; 実 (true, real — from the sense of "a solid, substantial thing," as in a seed or kernel filled out to maturity) reinforces it as "the true/real product of the tree," giving 果実 its slightly more formal, botanical-scientific register next to bare 果 or the everyday native forms.

Japanese かじつ (kajitsu) is the standard word for "fruit" in botanical, culinary, and formal registers (果実酒, "fruit liqueur"; 果実酒 fruit wine), alongside the native くだもの for everyday speech. Korean 과실 (gwasil) likewise names fruit formally, alongside the far more common native 과일 (gwail, itself a contraction of the same Sino-Korean root plus a native suffix). Vietnamese quả thực is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese reading of 果實 — but in modern Vietnamese its dominant use has drifted to an adverbial sense, "indeed, in truth" (quả thực là..., "it's truly the case that..."), built from the same literal components (果 "result, indeed" + 實 "real, true"); the literal "fruit" sense survives but is secondary and more literary, with trái cây as the ordinary everyday word for fruit.
