## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name; honorific person classifier"

某名 is the indefinite honorific correlative — "a certain distinguished person; someone (polite, unspecified)" — the respectful counterpart to 某人. It refers to a person of elevated status whose identity is deliberately withheld: known to the speaker but not to be named, or genuinely unspecified but presumed to be of standing. The addition of 名 (honorific) to 某 (indefinite) encodes deference even toward an anonymised referent — implying that the unnamed person is someone deserving respect.

Chinese 某位 (mǒu wèi) is the direct equivalent and common in formal and journalistic Chinese: 某位嘉賓表示… ("a certain distinguished guest indicated…"); 依據某位專家的意見 ("according to the opinion of a certain expert"). The 某位 construction allows anonymisation while preserving the social register of the referent. Korean 어떤 분 (eotteon bun, "a certain honoured person") or 모 분 (某 분, "a certain [respectful] person") serve the same function. Japanese 某方 (bō-kata) would be the formal equivalent but is not in active use; Japanese relies on contextual honorifics or circumlocution for this function.

某名 occupies a specific pragmatic niche: it anonymises a high-status referent while keeping the register of deference intact. This distinguishes it from 某人 (indefinite but neutral-register, for any unspecified person) and from 其名 (a known, discourse-salient polite referent). In diplomatic communiqués, official minutes, or formal testimony — where naming a specific respected individual would be inappropriate — 某名 provides a compact, formally neutral placeholder that signals both the withheld identity and the withheld person's elevated standing.
