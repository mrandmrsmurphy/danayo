## Notes
- [老 (char)](../characters/老%20(char).md) "old; prefix" + [鼠](../characters/鼠.md) "rat" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person"

老鼠人 (rat person) is a Chinese internet slang term describing a person in a state of chronic low energy and low motivation: sleeping through the day, awake at night, subsisting on takeout, scrolling without purpose, vaguely anxious about the future but unable to act on it. The name comes from the behavioral analogy to a rat — scurrying at the margins, avoiding light, hiding in small spaces, largely invisible to the productive world.

The genealogy of the term runs through two overlapping streams. The first is 鼠鼠文学 (rat literature), a self-deprecating meme that emerged on Chinese social media in early 2023. It derived from a phonetic substitution: 鼠 (shǔ, rat) replaces 叔 (shū, uncle) in the stock phrase 叔叔我呀 ("as for me, uncle…"), producing 鼠鼠我呀 — an absurdist, self-mocking declaration of smallness and failure. Unemployment, poverty, loneliness, and social irrelevance were catalogued in the rat-literature voice with a mix of humor and resignation. The second stream is a parallel Russian internet phenomenon — крысиные люди (rat people) — describing young people living in rented rooms, nocturnal and socially withdrawn, which entered Chinese online discourse through translation and spread on Weibo and Douyin.

By early 2025, 老鼠人 had consolidated into a recognized lifestyle category. Vlogs documenting daily rat-person existence — instant noodles at 3am, curtains drawn, a phone as the primary social contact — went viral. A variant phrase, 低能量老鼠人 (low-energy rat person), circulated particularly among Chinese students abroad, describing the isolation of winter months in northern cities: hiding from the cold, avoiding sunlight, making no plans.

老鼠人 sits on the same spectrum as [[臥平]] and [[放腐]] but occupies a distinct register. Where 躺平 is a political stance (a deliberate rejection of the 996 grind) and 摆烂 is an active nihilism (tanking deliberately), 老鼠人 describes something more diffuse and less chosen: a general state of being ground down, of days dissolving without intention. The rat person is not necessarily refusing the system — they may simply lack the energy to engage with it at all. The term is used both self-deprecatingly, by people who identify with the lifestyle, and descriptively, as a compassionate or rueful observation about a generation that has quietly withdrawn from public life.
