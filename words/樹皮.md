## Notes
- [樹](../characters/樹.md) "plant" + [皮](../characters/皮.md) "skin, hide, outer layer"

Both halves of this compound already carry the meaning transparently: 樹 for the tree, 皮 (whose own primary gloss is "hide, skin") for its outer covering — "tree-skin," bark.

Japanese じゅひ (juhi) and Korean 수피 (supi) are both real words for "bark," but sit in a more technical/botanical register than the everyday native alternatives (Japanese 木の皮, "tree's skin"; Korean 나무껍질, "tree's shell/peel," built from entirely native roots). Mandarin shùpí, by contrast, is the ordinary everyday word with no separate native form displacing it. Vietnamese vỏ cây (native, "tree shell/peel") is likewise the everyday word; the parallel Sino-Vietnamese compound thụ bì is real but rare, surfacing mainly in traditional-medicine pharmacopoeia entries naming a specific tree's bark as a medicinal ingredient (e.g. 海桐皮 hải đồng bì, 香加皮 hương gia bì) rather than as a general-purpose word for bark.
