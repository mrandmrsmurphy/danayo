## Notes
- [大](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big, great" + [河](../characters/河.md) "river"

大河 (dàhé) can denote a river generally, or specifically the Yellow River (黃河) in older/literary Chinese usage, the same metonymic narrowing seen in 河 itself historically naming the Yellow River before broadening to "river" in general.

In Japanese, たいが (taiga) has strong cultural resonance well beyond its plain dictionary meaning: NHK's long-running "Taiga drama" (大河ドラマ) franchise, historical epics broadcast annually since 1963, borrowed the word precisely for its connotation of a long, sweeping narrative — "a great river of a story." The reading たいが itself is a straightforward rendaku (連濁) voicing of 河's on'yomi か (ka) to が (ga) once compounded after 大, not an irregular or fused reading.

大河 is a true homophone of [[大廈]] ("mansion, large building") in Dan'a'yo — both read ㄉㄚㄧㄏㄚ/대하/daiha — despite the two source characters (河 "river" vs 廈 "building") being semantically and graphically unrelated; the collision is coincidental, arising only from 大 combining with two unrelated characters that happen to share the same Dan'a'yo syllable.
