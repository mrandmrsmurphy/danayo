## Notes
- [織](../characters/織.md) "to weave" + [女 (char)](../characters/女%20(char).md) "woman; girl" — "the Weaver Girl"

織女 (*Zhīnǚ*) is the Weaver Girl of the romance with [[牛郎]] (the Cowherd Boy) — see that entry for the legend itself. 織女 is a goddess associated with weaving and, by extension, with the textile arts generally; in some tellings she is a granddaughter or daughter of the Queen Mother of the West (西王母) or of the Jade Emperor, who descends to the mortal world and is later forcibly separated from her mortal husband and returned to the heavens, reunited with him only once a year at [[七夕]].

Astronomically, 織女 gives her name to **織女星** (*Zhīnǚ xīng*), the star Vega in the constellation Lyra — one of the brightest stars in the night sky and, together with [[牛郎星]] (Altair) and Deneb, one of the three vertices of the Summer Triangle. Vega's traditional Chinese astronomical name within the lunar lodge system identifies it with the asterism 織女 in the Chinese constellation 女宿/牛宿 region, a classification that, as with [[牛郎星]], became conflated with the popular Qixi legend.

The pairing 牛郎織女 (*Niúláng Zhīnǚ*) functions as a set phrase for the lovers as a couple, and by extension for any pair of lovers kept apart by circumstance, meeting only rarely. Japanese *Orihime* (織姫, "Weaver Princess") and Korean 직녀 (*Jiknyeo*) are the cognate figures in the Tanabata and Chilseok traditions respectively, both directly continuing the same character compound (織 + 女/姫) with the same underlying association of weaving, womanhood, and the star Vega.
