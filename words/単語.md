## Notes
- [単](../characters/単.md) "single, simple" + [語](../characters/語.md) "speech, word" — a single, discrete unit of speech: a word.

This is the ordinary Japanese term for "word" (単語), and Korean independently uses the cognate 단어 in exactly the same sense — though, notably, the real Korean reading of 単 in this compound (단) happens to differ from Dan'a'yo's own bound-form syllable for 語 here, so the two spellings don't fully coincide. Mandarin and Cantonese instead build their everyday word for "word" on 詞/词 rather than 語/语 (单词, dāncí), so 単語 as a compound is comparatively rare there, even though both characters individually are common Sinitic vocabulary. 語 itself is one of the vocabulary's most important roots, underlying 国語 ("national language," not yet created) and the language's own self-description; 単 supplies the "discrete, individual" sense found in 単純 ("simple," not yet created) and [[単位]] ("unit"). Attested across Japanese and Korean.
