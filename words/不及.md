## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [及 (char)](../characters/及%20(char).md) "reach out to"

To not reach; to fall short of; to be inferior to or unable to match. 不及 covers three overlapping senses:

1. **Temporal**: to fail to do something in time — 來不及 (lái bùjí, "there's no time left; to be too late") is one of the most common phrases in modern Chinese. Structurally it means "to come and not arrive in time," used whenever there is insufficient time to complete an action.

2. **Comparative**: A不及B = "A does not measure up to B; A is inferior to B." This is the classical comparison structure: 後生可畏，焉知來者之不如今也 (Analects) and similar formulations use 不及 to establish hierarchies of quality, virtue, or achievement.

3. **Physical**: to not actually reach a place, level, or target — a projectile that falls short, influence that does not extend far enough.

The word has fossilized into several important set expressions. **措手不及** (cuòshǒu bùjí, "to be caught off guard; unable to respond in time") is a four-character idiom describing sudden events that leave no time to prepare. **防不及防** (fángbùjífáng, "impossible to guard against; one cannot prevent what one cannot anticipate") describes threats that outpace any defense.

In Japanese, the reading **しかず** (shikazu) is an archaic form of 及ばず (oyobazu, "does not reach") — most famous in the idiom 百聞は一見に如かず (hyakubun wa ikken ni shikazu): "a hundred hearings do not equal one seeing" — the Japanese form of "one picture is worth a thousand words," literally meaning that direct observation surpasses any number of secondhand accounts.
