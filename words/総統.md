## Notes
- [総](../characters/総.md) "whole; total" + [統](../characters/統.md) "command; govern; unite"

**総統** (*zǒngtǒng*, "supreme governor") is the standard Dan'a'yo word for the president or head of state of a republic. The compound literally means "overall command" — 総 "total; comprehensive" + 統 "to govern; to unify; to command" — carrying a stronger sense of unified executive authority than its Japanese and Korean counterpart 大統領.

The term is most closely associated with the Republic of China (中華民国), where 總統 has been the title of the head of state since 1948 and remains so in Taiwan today. It is also the natural Vietnamese form: *tổng thống* is the standard term for a president across Vietnamese political discourse. This three-branch coverage (Mandarin, Cantonese, Vietnamese) makes it the most widely shared CJKV word for the office.

For the specifically Korean usage, see [[国務領]], the title of the head of the [[大韓民国]] Provisional Government (1925–1927).
