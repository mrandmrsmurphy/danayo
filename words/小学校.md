## Notes
- [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small" + [学](../characters/学.md) "learning; study" + [校](../characters/校.md) "school institution"

小学校 is "elementary school; primary school" — 小 (small) + 学 (learning) + 校 (school institution). The three-character compound follows the standard Japanese school-naming pattern: [[小学]] (elementary learning) + 校 (institutional suffix). The compound covers the first stage of formal compulsory education, typically ages 6–12. Mandarin does not use 小学校 — Mandarin simply says 小学 (xiǎoxué) alone for elementary school, without appending 校, so 小学校 should not be treated as a Mandarin compound despite the obvious overlap in characters. The aliases 初等學校 / 初等学校 (chūděng xuéxiào, "first-rank school") are the formal literary alternative for the same institution.

Japanese 小学校 (しょうがっこう, shōgakkō) is the legal and everyday term under Japan's School Education Act (学校教育法): six compulsory years covering ages 6–12. The system runs: 小学校 (6 years) → [[中学校]] (3 years) → 高校 (3 years) → [[大学]] (university). Korean 소학교 (小学校, sohakgyo) is the term used in North Korea; South Korea officially uses 초등학교 (初等學校, elementary school) since 1996, having retired 국민학교 (national citizen school) for its associations with Japanese colonial-era education. Vietnamese trường tiểu học (小=tiểu, 学校=trường học): the standard Vietnamese term for elementary school; the six-year tiểu học stage is the first level of Vietnam's compulsory education system.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 소학햣 differs from Korean 소학교 in the third syllable only. 小 reads 소 in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean — MC 心母 (s) + 宵A韻 (iᴇu) converges to so in both systems. 学 reads 학 in both — MC 曉母 (x) + 覺韻 (ɣʌk) gives hag/학 in both. 校 reads 햣 (hyau/ㄏ⼘ㄨ) in Dan'a'yo but 교 (gyo) in Sino-Korean — MC 匣母 (ɣ) + 肴韻 (ɣau) gives Dan'a'yo ㄏ (h-), while Sino-Korean gives ㄱ (g-/k-); this h-/g- split from MC 匣母 is a regular correspondence found across all 校-containing compounds in Dan'a'yo.
