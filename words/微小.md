## Notes
- [微](../characters/微.md) "tiny; minute; micro-; subtle" + [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small"

微小 is a dyad of smallness in which the two characters occupy slightly different registers of scale. 小 is the ordinary word for "small" — the opposite of 大, covering everything from children to minor inconveniences. 微 denotes something far smaller: the imperceptible, the barely detectable, the subtle difference that requires close attention to notice. 微 functions as the East Asian equivalent of the "micro-" prefix in scientific vocabulary: 微生物 (microorganism), 微波 (microwave), 微積分 (calculus, literally "tiny accumulation of parts"). Together 微小 describes what is both 微 (beyond normal perception) and 小 (objectively small in scale), creating a compound for the genuinely minute.

A significant homonym arises in Korean: 미소 (miso) is simultaneously the Sino-Korean reading of 微小 (tiny) and the word for "smile" (微笑). The two are written with different second characters — 微小 (small) vs 微笑 (smile/laugh) — but are phonologically identical in modern Korean, which creates a productive ambiguity in contexts where physical scale and warmth of expression come into unlikely contact. Japanese retains the distinction clearly: 微小 (bishō, tiny) and 微笑 (bishō or hohoemi, smile) are homophones in the Sino-Japanese readings but the native reading ほほえむ disambiguates the smile.

The kwin=false marking reflects the divergence between Dan'a'yo 뮈소 (mui + so) and Korean 미소 (miso) — where 微 maps to 미 in Korean but to 뮈 in Dan'a'yo, systematically differing in the vowel for this character class.
