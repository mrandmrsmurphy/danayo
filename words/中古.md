## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle, intermediate" + [古](../characters/古.md) "old, ancient" — middle-old; intermediate antiquity.

中古 resolves into three distinct senses depending on context — an unusually wide semantic range for a two-character compound:

**1. Secondhand / used goods** — the dominant modern sense in Japanese and Korean. 中古品 (used goods), 中古車 (used car), 中古本 (secondhand book). The logic is temporal: 中古 occupies the middle ground between 新品 (brand new) and 古物/古董 (genuine antiques). Japanese ちゅうこ (chūko) in this sense is ubiquitous in consumer culture, and the 中古市場 is a significant economic sector; Korean 중고 is identical in usage. The sense may have developed from the idea of something that has passed through its first period of life but has not yet reached true antiquity.

**2. The Middle Ages / medieval period** — in East Asian historical periodisation adapted from European scholarship, 中古 is one rendering of "medieval." The tripartite scheme 古代 (ancient) / 中古 or 中世 (medieval) / 近代 (modern) maps Western periodisation onto East Asian historiography. Japanese typically prefers 中世 (chūsei) for medieval in a general sense; 中古 in Japanese literary history specifically designates the Heian period (794–1185 CE).

**3. Middle antiquity in Chinese literary periodisation** — in classical Chinese literary history, 中古 designates roughly the Eastern Han through Tang period (1st–9th centuries CE), distinguished from 上古 (high antiquity, pre-Han) and 近古 (recent antiquity, Song onward).

These three senses are fully non-overlapping in practice; context determines which is intended without ambiguity.
