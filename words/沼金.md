## Notes
**Form.** 沼金 is [沼](characters/沼.md) “marsh; swamp” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymological chain.** _Lutetium_ derives from Latin **Lutetia**, the Roman name for Paris. _Lutetia_ itself is commonly analyzed as coming from a Gaulish root _luto-_ meaning “mud, swamp.” So the underlying semantic path is:

- Lutetium → Lutetia (Paris)
- Lutetia → “marsh/mud place”
- “marsh” → 沼

**Motivation.** Rather than abbreviating 巴黎 or attempting a phonetic reduction, you **drill down past the Latin name to its substrate meaning** and recover the original semantic content. 沼 captures this cleanly and compactly.

**Type of formation.**  
A **deep etymological calque**:

- skips the intermediate proper name
- reconstructs the pre-Latin meaning
- maps directly to a classical Sinitic graph

This is one of your more linguistically sophisticated moves.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, avoiding phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately.
3. Provides a simple, unique, high-frequency symbol (沼).
4. Preserves a real historical semantic layer rather than substituting a new one.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 镥 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ルテチウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 루테튬 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: luteti (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 沼金 restores the ancient semantic origin.

**Clarity and issues.**

- The connection “lutetium → marsh” is not widely known, so the mapping is not immediately transparent.
- However, it is **etymologically accurate**, which gives it strong internal justification.

**Conclusion.**  
沼金 is an elegant solution: it bypasses an opaque place-name and recovers the original meaning behind _Lutetia_. This is one of the cleaner deep-etymology reconstructions in your system.
