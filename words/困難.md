## Notes
- [困](../characters/困.md) "distressed, sleepy, fatigued" + [難](../characters/難.md) "difficult" — `characters/難.md`'s own `stand_in` points to this word (困難), meaning 難 is a bound morpheme that never appears independently outside this compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/困.md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/難.md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- **Verified correct, not a bug**: `korean: 곤난` matches the standing North-Korean-pronunciation rule for this vault. South Korean has shifted this word to 곤란 via a sound-softening process (활음조), but 곤난 remains the original reading and is still standard in North Korean 문화어 (verified via search) — the stored form is exactly right and was deliberately left unchanged.
- **Left `vietnamese` deliberately blank — a false-friend trap, not a gap**: the etymologically expected compositional reading khốn nạn is indeed the direct Sino-Vietnamese descendant of 困難, but it has undergone drastic semantic pejoration in modern Vietnamese to mean "wretched; damn; a contemptible person" (a strong insult), no longer "difficult" (verified via search). Filling this field with khốn nạn would actively mislead a learner into using a vulgar insult when they mean "difficult" — left blank rather than include a technically-compositional but practically-wrong and offensive reading.
