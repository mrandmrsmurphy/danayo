## Notes
- [若 (char)](../characters/若%20(char).md) "if, like" (here phonetically borrowed, not its "if" sense) + [干](../characters/干.md) "shield; a certain number" — an unspecified but limited quantity, "some, a certain amount of."

Mandarin ruògān, Japanese じゃっかん, and Vietnamese nhược can all sit at a similar register: formal or written, common in legal, administrative, and academic text ("若干问题," "certain issues") but a little stiff in casual speech, where looser native words (点儿, ちょっと, một vài) are more natural. Korean 약간 broke from that pattern entirely — it's become one of the most common degree-words in ordinary spoken Korean, used constantly and almost reflexively ("약간 피곤해," "I'm a little tired"), closer in register to filler words like 좀 than to its more formal Sino-Japanese and Sino-Vietnamese cognates.
