## Notes
- [露 (char)](../characters/露%20(char).md) "dew" + [月 (char)](../characters/月%20(char).md) "month; moon" — "dew month"

露月 (*Lùyuè*, "dew month") is one of several traditional alternative names for the tenth lunar month, alongside [[良月]] (the principal name used in this vault) and 亥月 (named for its position in the duodecimal 地支 cycle). Where 良月 names the month for the felicity of the season — harvest complete, granaries full, the world at ease — 露月 names it for a seasonal natural phenomenon: the heavy morning dew characteristic of early winter mornings, as nighttime temperatures begin to drop below the dew point while days remain comparatively mild. This naming pattern, in which a month receives both a "character" name and a "natural phenomenon" name, recurs across the poetic calendar; compare [[氷月]] alongside [[臘月]] for the twelfth month.

露月 survives almost entirely as a literary and calendrical curiosity in the modern languages; Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all use the numbered month (十月/시월/October, etc.) in ordinary speech. The Japanese reading つゆつき (*tsuyutsuki*, a native kun-yomi compound of つゆ "dew" and つき "month/moon") parallels the native-reading style of [[良月]]'s よいつき (*yoitsuki*), both favouring native Japanese vocabulary over Sino-Japanese ろげつ for these poetic month-names.
