## Notes
- [居](../characters/居.md) "reside" + [住](../characters/住.md) "reside, live, dwell" — a synonym-doubling that names the plain act of residing somewhere. This word is the viable form of [住](../characters/住.md): 住 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 居住 is its `stand_in` compound — an undistorted match, since 住's own gloss already is simply "reside, live, dwell" with no further nuance. Contrast [[居所]] (the noun "dwelling, whereabouts" that [[居]] stands in for instead): 居住 names the action, 居所 names the place.

Mandarin jūzhù and Cantonese geoi1zyu6 are both standard, everyday verbs for "to reside, dwell" — 居住証 (residence permit), 居住環境 (living environment). Japanese kyojū and Korean 거주 (geoju) follow the same pattern productively — 居住地 (place of residence), 居住者 (resident, occupant). All four describe the ongoing state of living somewhere, not a specific address.
