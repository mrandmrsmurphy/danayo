## Notes
- [小 (char)](../characters/小%20(char).md) "small" + [雨 (char)](../characters/雨%20(char).md) "rain" — light rain, drizzle.

**小雨** is the plain, transparent compound for rain that is light in volume rather than brief in duration — the natural counterpart to **大雨** ("heavy rain"). Mandarin *xiǎoyǔ* and Cantonese *siu2 jyu5* both function as ordinary descriptive compounds and as the technical terms used in weather forecasting for a defined intensity band (in Chinese meteorological classification, 小雨 denotes rainfall below a specified hourly threshold, distinguishing it from 中雨 and 大雨).

In Japanese, 小雨 is read with the special kun'yomi **こさめ** (*kosame*) in everyday usage — one of many weather terms with native readings distinct from the on'yomi concatenation **しょうう** (*shōu*), which survives as a more technical/written register. Korean **소우** (*sou*) and the compositional Vietnamese **tiểu vũ** are less commonly used in casual speech, where native or hybrid terms (Korean 가는비, Vietnamese mưa nhỏ/mưa phùn) are more typical — as is often the case, the Sino-Xenic compound persists mainly in literary, technical, or formal registers.

小雨 is one of two synonyms of [[俄雨]] mentioned in that entry — alongside [[驟雨]] — distinguished from 俄雨 ("a passing shower," emphasizing brevity) by emphasizing intensity (or lack thereof) rather than duration.
