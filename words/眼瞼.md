## Notes
- [眼](../characters/眼.md) "eyeball, pupil" + [瞼](../characters/瞼.md) "eyelid" — stand-in for [[瞼]], which cannot appear independently

眼瞼 (yǎnjiǎn / がんけん / 안검) is the standard Sino-Japanese medical and literary term for the eyelid — the fold of skin that covers and protects the eye. The compound pairs 眼 (the eye or eyeball) with 瞼 (the eyelid specifically), where 瞼 carries the more precise anatomical meaning and 眼 provides the broader context. In everyday speech Japanese uses まぶた, Chinese uses 眼皮 (yǎnpí), and Korean uses 눈꺼풀, but 眼瞼 remains the standard term in medical contexts across all three languages.

The eyelid as an anatomical structure is described in classical Chinese medical texts (眼科, ophthalmology), where 眼瞼 appears in discussions of conditions like ptosis (眼瞼下垂, drooping eyelid) and blepharitis (眼瞼炎). In Japanese ophthalmology 眼瞼 is the formal term used on medical charts and in clinical descriptions, while まぶた is reserved for everyday speech.
