## Notes
- [複](../characters/複.md) "compound, multiple" + [数](../characters/数.md) "number" — more than one in number; the grammatical category of plurality.

This is the term used in the grammar book's own discussion of how Dan'a'yo marks number: the suffix 等 is explicitly described as not a [[単純]] 複数 marker (a plain plural marker, "merely more than one") but as a [[連帯]] marker instead, expanding a noun phrase to include associated others. 複 itself is a highly productive root across the vocabulary, also seen in [[重複]] ("duplication," already attested); 数 anchors the basic vocabulary of counting and quantity. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
