## Notes
- [恩](../characters/恩.md) "kindness; mercy" + [意](../characters/意.md) "intention; mind; sentiment" — the affective, intentional dimension of God's goodwill; grace understood as the inner disposition of love that motivates God's acts

Among the Dan'a'yo 恩- words, 恩意 names the least visible dimension of grace: not the formal principle (like [[恩典]]), not the relational warmth (like [[恩寵]]), but the underlying intention and sentiment behind divine beneficence. 意 is the word for mental intention, the directedness of the will — and 恩意 frames God's grace as rooted in a loving disposition toward the creature.

In Chinese devotional writing, 恩意 is sometimes used to describe the quality of warmth and personal regard in God's dealing with humanity. It bridges [[恩典]] (grace as theological principle) and [[恩寵]] (grace as relational favor) by emphasizing the affective will behind both. Compare [[恩施]], which is the active, verbal expression of that goodwill — the bestowing of grace as an act.
