## Notes
- [魏](../characters/魏.md) "Wei (state, surname)" + [峨](../characters/峨.md) "lofty" — towering, majestic.

巍 (wēi), "towering, lofty, majestic," is an obscure character appearing 6 times in the 《論語》, most famously in **泰伯**'s praise of the legendary sage-kings: "巍巍乎！舜禹之有天下也而不與焉" ("How majestic! Such was the way Shun and Yu held the empire, yet remained unattached to it"). 巍 lies outside Dan'a'yo's closed character set, so the vault substitutes 魏峨 — pairing 魏, a graphically and phonetically related character (both built on the 委 component, sharing a Middle Chinese ŋ- initial), with 峨, whose own meaning "lofty" supplies the translation. This is a "Component + Translation" substitution per [[translation/論語 (book)]]'s Vocabulary list.

The mandarin/cantonese/japanese/korean/vietnamese readings of 魏峨 are drawn from the attested compound 巍峨 (wēi'é/wéi'é), "towering, majestic" — formed from 巍+峨 with essentially the same sense. Vietnamese "nguy nga" is a common Sino-Vietnamese word carrying this same meaning. Note that 諺文/羅馬字/注音 remain tied to 魏's own fixed Dan'a'yo identity (어/'e/ㄝ, the "Wei" reading), diverging in tone from 巍's wēi — paralleling the precedent set by [[六楽]]/[[楽]], where the Dan'a'yo identity of a character and the reading required by a particular compound diverge.
