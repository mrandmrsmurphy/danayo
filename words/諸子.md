## Notes
- [諸 (char)](../characters/諸%20(char).md) "various" + [子](../characters/子.md) "master" (using 子's honorific sense, as in 孔子 Confucius or 老子 Laozi, where it suffixes a thinker's surname) — "the various masters," the collective term for the independent philosophers and teachers of the late Zhou dynasty, especially the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods. Each individual 子 founded or represented a distinct school of thought; 諸子 names them as a class, without yet specifying how many schools they amounted to. The compound is most familiar as the first half of [[諸子百家]] (the Hundred Schools of Thought), pairing "the various masters" with "the hundred schools" they founded.
