## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; his/her/its"

其 is "that (middle distance)" — the medial demonstrative in Dan'a'yo's three-way system: 此 (near) vs. 其 (middle) vs. 彼 (far). In classical Chinese, 其 is primarily a third-person possessive and demonstrative ("his/her/its; that") and one of the most frequent characters in the literary canon, appearing throughout the Analects, Mencius, and Shijing. Dan'a'yo assigns it the specific function of middle-distance determiner, covering entities previously referenced or near the addressee — corresponding to Japanese その (that near you) and Korean 그 (that; the previously mentioned one). 其 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[其]].

Mandarin 其 (qí) functions primarily as a third-person pronoun and possessive in classical and literary usage: 其人 (that person; the person in question), 其次 (next; secondly), 尤其 (especially; above all), 其中 (among them; within), 其實 (actually; in fact). In modern Mandarin 其 rarely stands alone and appears mainly in set two-character compounds. Cantonese 其 (kei4): 其他 (kei4 taa1, other), 其實 (kei4 sat6, actually). Japanese: the native adnominal その (sono) covers the middle-distance sense; the Sino-Japanese on-reading ぎ (gi) appears in literary compounds. Korean 기 (其) in Sino-Korean. Vietnamese kỳ (其): the primary Sino-Vietnamese reading, used in literary compounds.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 그 differs from Korean 기. 其 reads 그 (gǝ/ㄍㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 群母 (g) + 之韻 (ɨ) gives Dan'a'yo the schwa vowel (-ǝ, ㄜ) — while Sino-Korean gives 기 (gi), retaining the closer -i vowel of the MC ɨ rhyme. Dan'a'yo preserves the schwa quality of 之韻 where Sino-Korean shifts to -i.
