## Notes
- [坐 (char)](../characters/坐%20(char).md) "to sit" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, place"

坐位 is the everyday word for "seat" — a place to sit, whether on a train, in a theater, or at a table. 坐, a 指事 character depicting two figures (人) seated on the ground (土), is the basic verb "to sit"; 坐位 literally combines this with 位 ("place, position") to mean "the place where one sits."

Dan'a'yo builds this word on the canonical character 坐, with the extremely common variant 座 — formed by adding the roof radical 广 over 坐, distinguishing the noun "seat" (座) from the verb "to sit" (坐) in modern usage — recorded as a word-level alias (座位), following the same alias-parent pattern established for [[追遡]] and [[禿就]] elsewhere in this vault. In practice 座位 is, if anything, the more common spelling in contemporary Chinese; 坐位 and 座位 are listed as homophonous variants of the same word. [[坐席]], cross-linked from [[坐 (char)]]'s own Notes, is a near-synonym built the same way from 坐 + 席 ("mat, seat").
