## Notes
### Form
不穏素 is [[不穏]] “unstable; uneasy” + [[素]] “element.” This departs from the usual binome but remains within allowances for fixed three-character names (cf. 苦土素).

### Motivation
The international name _astatine_ derives from Greek ἄστατος (_astatos_) “unstable.” The defining property is extreme radio-instability. 不穏 is a close semantic rendering: it conveys lack of stability and an inherent tendency toward change or decay.

### Choice of 不穏
- [不 (char)](characters/不%20(char).md) marks negation; [穏](characters/穏.md) denotes calmness, stability.
- The compound is idiomatic in Japanese-influenced Sinitic registers and communicates “not stable” without technical jargon.
- Compared with alternatives like [[不安]] (“uneasy”) or [[不定]] (“indeterminate”), 不穏 emphasizes dynamic instability rather than mere uncertainty.

### Fit within Dan'a'yo
1. Meaning-based reconstruction of the Greek etymology.
2. Uses 素 as required for a halogen (group 17).
3. Accepts a tri-character form where a concise, accurate binome is not available.

### Symbol selection
Since a single-character symbol is required, either component can serve, but

- 不: simple, high frequency, but semantically very broad (generic negation).
- 穏: more specific to “calm/stable,” hence better tied to the etymology, and less likely to collide with other elements.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 砹 (phonetic)
- Japanese: アスタチン (phonetic)
- Korean: 아스타틴 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: astatin (phonetic)

As with several late-discovered elements, modern forms are purely phonetic; 不穏素 restores semantic transparency.

### Clarity and issues
- The register of 不穏 is slightly literary but still interpretable.
- The three-character length is exceptional but justified by precision.
- Care is needed to reserve 穏 uniquely as an elemental symbol.

### Conclusion
不穏素 is a faithful semantic calque of _astatine_’s etymology, and selecting 穏 as the one-character symbol preserves both distinctiveness and conceptual accuracy.
