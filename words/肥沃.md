## Notes
- [肥](../characters/肥.md) "fertile, plump" + [沃](../characters/沃.md) "watered, irrigated" — stand-in for both [[肥]] and [[沃]], neither of which can appear independently.

肥沃/비옥/ひよく stay consistent as the ordinary word for fertile soil across Mandarin, Korean, and Japanese. Vietnamese is the outlier: there is no everyday use of the literal character-for-character compound here. Instead, Vietnamese reaches for either màu mỡ — a native expression (and by far the most common in speech) — or phì nhiêu, a *different* Sino-Vietnamese compound that keeps 肥/phì but swaps in 饒 ("abundant") for 沃, giving essentially the same meaning through a different pairing of roots. Neither option is a direct cognate of 肥沃 itself.
