## Notes
- [所 (char)](../characters/所%20(char).md) "-ee (nominalizer)" + [以 (char)](../characters/以%20(char).md) "by, using" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (所's own points to bare `所`, 以's own to bare `以`); 所以 is an independent compound, not a legitimizer for either. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/所 (char).md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/以 (char).md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- A genuine part-of-speech divergence across the sphere, not a bug: Mandarin/Cantonese suǒyǐ/so2 ji5 have grammaticalized into an everyday causal conjunction ("so, therefore," introducing a result clause), while Japanese ゆえん (yuen) and Korean 소이 (verified via search) stayed closer to the literal, compositional sense — a noun meaning "the reason (why); the cause," used in a comparatively literary/formal register (e.g. Japanese 然る所以 "the reason why it is so"; Korean is largely confined to classical/formal writing today). Vietnamese sở dĩ sits in between: still common in modern speech, but functioning as a fixed causal frame (sở dĩ ... là vì/do ... — "the reason ... is that ...") rather than a simple "so."
