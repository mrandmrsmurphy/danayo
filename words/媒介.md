## Notes
- [媒](../characters/媒.md) "go-between, matchmaker" + [介](../characters/介.md) "lie between, mediate" — something that stands between two parties or substances and carries an effect from one to the other.

媒 originally named the human matchmaker who arranges a marriage by standing between two families (媒人), and from that narrow social role broadened into the abstract sense of "any go-between." 介 contributes the same in-between geometry from a different angle — it is the character behind 介入 ("intervention," not yet created) and behind Dan'a'yo's own grammatical term for a particle that sits between two elements. Together, 媒介 is the general word for any carrier or vector of transmission: a disease's 媒介 (a mosquito), a culture's 媒介 (language itself), or, in the everyday sense, the mass 媒体 ("media," not yet created but built on the same 媒 root). The Vietnamese reflex môi giới has narrowed somewhat further to "broker, matchmaking" in modern usage, closer to 媒's original sense than to the abstracted Sino-Japanese one. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
