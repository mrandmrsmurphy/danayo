## Notes
- [危](../characters/危.md) "danger; precarious; imperilled" + [机](../characters/机.md) "crucial moment; pivot; opportunity (simplified from 機)"

危机 is "crisis; critical juncture" — a compound of 危 (danger, precariousness) and 機/机 (a pivotal or crucial moment; the point where things can go either way). The two characters capture the two-faced nature of crisis: 危 = the danger dimension, 機 = the pivotal/opportunity dimension. The oft-quoted "crisis = danger + opportunity" framing is genuinely encoded in this compound: 機 originally denoted a crossbow trigger or mechanism — metaphorically, the pivotal point where a slight force determines a major outcome. The simplified character 机 represents traditional 機 in this compound.

Mandarin 危機/危机 (wēijī) is the standard term for crisis — 金融危機 (financial crisis), 人道主義危機 (humanitarian crisis), 危機管理 (crisis management). Japanese 危機 (きき, kiki) is the standard Japanese word for crisis: 危機的状況 (critical situation), 危機感 (sense of crisis), 危機管理 (crisis management — a direct loan from or parallel to the Chinese). Korean 위기 (wigi) is the standard term: 위기 관리 (crisis management), 위기 상황 (crisis situation). Vietnamese nguy cơ (danger/risk/imminent crisis) is the closest Vietnamese equivalent, covering the danger-potential sense.

危机 uses the simplified character 机 for the traditional 機 (機 = mechanism, opportunity, crucial moment), following mainland Chinese orthographic convention. Dan'a'yo generally draws from the classical/traditional inventory, so this entry reflects the simplified-character influence in its Mandarin source. The Dan'a'yo readings are 危 = ⼔ㄧ (*wei*, vowel-initial — hence the leading apostrophe in ROmaji *'wei*) and 机 = ㄍㄧㄜ (*giǝ*), giving the compound 注音 ⼔ㄧㄍㄧㄜ and ROmaji *'weigiǝ*. The Hangul 웨긔 concatenates 웨 (wei/危) and 긔 (giǝ/机), differing from Sino-Korean 위기 (wigi) — the usual kwin: false pattern.
