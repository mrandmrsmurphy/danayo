## Notes
- [頚 (char)](../characters/頚%20(char).md) "neck" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin jǐng, Cantonese geng2, and Vietnamese cảnh (specifically the front of the neck, as opposed to 項/hạng for the nape) all use this character productively and independently for "neck." Japanese and Korean, by contrast, both prefer fully native words in everyday speech — Japanese くび (kubi) and Korean 목 (mok) — reserving the Sino-Xenic reading (けい, kei; 경, gyeong) for technical/medical compounds like 頚椎/경추 ("cervical vertebra").
