## Notes
- [書](../characters/書.md) "book; document; writing" + [本 (char)](../characters/本%20(char).md) "root; origin; basis"

書本 is a Dan'a'yo stand-in word for [[書]] — the character for "book; document; writing." 書 has high boundedness (90) and does not stand freely as a word in natural CJKV usage; 書本 legitimises it as a standalone nominal by appending [[本 (char)]] ("root; basis"), a pattern well-attested in Cantonese and informal Mandarin, where 書本 (shūběn / syu1 bun2) is the natural everyday word for "book." The compound reads literally as "the writing-root" or "the book itself," with 本 adding concreteness and noun-hood to the abstract character 書. In Dan'a'yo, 書本 is the free word for "book," while 書 alone remains a bound morpheme in compounds: 書院 (academy), 書法 (calligraphy), 書道 (the way of writing/calligraphy), 書簡 (letter).

Mandarin 書本 (shūběn) is the standard informal and spoken word for "book," widely used alongside the formal 書籍 (shūjí) and the classifier-noun construction 一本書 (yī běn shū, one book — where 本 hon serves as a counter for volumes). Cantonese 書本 (syu1 bun2) is the everyday Hong Kong term for "book," naturally extended to school textbooks (課本, fo3 bun2). Japanese does not use 書本 as a compound; the natural Japanese for "book" is 本 (ほん, hon), and the Sino-Japanese reading しょほん (shohon) is non-standard and not in use. Korean 서본 (書本, seobon) is the Sino-Korean reading of the compound but not the everyday Korean word for "book" — Korean uses 책 (冊, chaek). Vietnamese thư bản (Sino-Vietnamese: 書=thư, 本=bản); the everyday Vietnamese for "book" is sách.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 쇼본 differs from Korean 서본 in the first syllable. 書 reads 쇼 (syo/ㄙ⼄) in Dan'a'yo but 서 (seo) in Sino-Korean — MC 書母 (ɕ) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ) gives Dan'a'yo the -yo vowel, while Sino-Korean gives -eo. This vowel split from MC 魚韻 is a regular correspondence: Dan'a'yo retains the rounded quality of the MC vowel as -yo, while Sino-Korean shifts it to -eo. The second syllable is identical in both: 本 reads 본 (bon/ㄅㄛㄋ) in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean.
