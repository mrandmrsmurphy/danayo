## Notes
- [予](../characters/予.md) "beforehand" + [習](../characters/習.md) "practice"

予習 is "to prepare for lessons" — studying material in advance of a class, the mirror image of [[復習]] "to review." 予 here carries its "beforehand, in advance" sense, sharing that meaning (and, notably, its Cantonese and Korean readings — see below) with its own documented alternate forms 豫/預, both reflected in the page's `預習`/`豫習` aliases.

Mandarin 预习/預習 (yùxí, "to preview/prepare a lesson"). Cantonese 予習 (jyu6zaap6) — note that 予's *own* primary Cantonese reading is jyu4 (its "give, bestow" sense, as in 賜予), but in this "beforehand" sense the character is read jyu6, following its interchangeable partner 預 rather than its own base reading. Japanese 予習 (よしゅう, yoshū) is the term's home register — a fixture of Japanese schooling vocabulary, paired constantly with 復習 (fukushū, "review") as the two halves of independent study. Korean 예습 (yeseup) shows the same reading-substitution pattern as Cantonese: 予's own character-page reading is 여 (yeo), but 예습 uses 예, the reading proper to 預/豫 — the "beforehand" sense of 予 consistently borrows its cognates' pronunciation across both Cantonese and Korean, not its own primary one. No confidently-attested direct Vietnamese calque was found for this specific compound (unlike [[了解]]'s liễu giải); Vietnamese generally expresses the concept with a descriptive phrase (chuẩn bị bài, "prepare the lesson") rather than a fixed Sino-Vietnamese compound, so the `vietnamese` field is left blank rather than guessed.
