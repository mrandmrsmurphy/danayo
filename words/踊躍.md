## Notes
- [踊](../characters/踊.md) "jump" + [躍](../characters/躍.md) "leap"

踊躍 literally stacks two near-synonymous "jump/leap" roots, and the resulting compound reads figuratively rather than physically: it describes the eagerness of leaping rather than an actual jump. In Modern Written Mandarin yǒngyuè survives as a formal register word for enthusiastic participation — 踊躍發言 ("to speak up eagerly"), 踊躍參加/踊躍捐款 ("participate/donate enthusiastically") are standard in announcements and official writing, though colloquial speech reaches for 積極 or 熱烈 instead. The same compound also carries a distinct Buddhist devotional sense inherited from sutra translation: 踊躍歡喜 ("to leap for joy") is a stock phrase describing a listener's rapture on hearing the Buddha's teaching, and this reading is shared across the Chinese, Japanese, and Korean Buddhist canons rather than being a Mandarin-only coinage. Korean 용약 (踊躍) preserves this literary/devotional register directly, essentially unused in ordinary speech. Japanese ようやく is likewise a bookish, literary word — not to be confused with the unrelated native adverb ようやく ("finally, at last"), an accidental homophone with a completely different etymology.
