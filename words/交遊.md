>[!warning] Homophones
> [[交友]] "make friends"

## Notes
- [交 (char)](../characters/交%20(char).md) "mix, exchange" + [遊](../characters/遊.md) "roam, travel" — to mix and roam together with others; the broader, more literary member of the pair.

交遊 and 交友 are near-total synonyms in real-world Chinese, but with a real difference in scope that the vault's homophone collision papers over: 交友 describes forming close, individual friendships — the emotional bond itself, as in 朋友圏 "circle of close friends." 交遊 describes the broader activity of socializing and associating widely, often with many people at once; the set phrase 交遊廣闊 ("one's social connections are vast") uses 交遊 precisely because it names a *network*, not an individual bond, and would sound wrong with 交友 substituted in. Historically it also carries a faint connotation of impropriety when applied to mixed-sex association ("男女交遊"), a shade 交友 never picked up.

The homophony is not a coincidence of Dan'a'yo derivation — it is an inherited one. 友 and 遊 are true Middle Chinese homophones, both 以母尤韻 (yǐ-initial, yóu-rhyme), differing in their daughter languages only by tone: Mandarin yǒu/yóu, Cantonese jau5/jau4. Japanese collapsed the tone distinction entirely, so 交友 and 交遊 are *already* homophones there too — both こうゆう — meaning Dan'a'yo (which likewise drops MC tone) is simply reproducing a real, independently-attested Sino-Japanese collision rather than manufacturing a new one. Korean keeps them apart only because 유 (遊) and 우 (友) never merged in the first place (교유 vs 교우).
