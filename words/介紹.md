## Notes
- [介](../characters/介.md) "mediator" + [紹](../characters/紹.md) "introduce" — stand-in for [[紹]], which cannot appear independently

介紹 is "to introduce" — pairing 介 "go-between, mediator" with 紹 "to connect, introduce" (紹 alone means much the same thing, but is a bound character that never stands alone; 介紹 is precisely what legitimizes it in this vault, per its own `stand_in` field).

Mandarin 介绍/介紹 (jièshào). Cantonese 介紹 (gaai3siu6). Japanese and Korean both use the *reversed* compound rather than 介紹's own character order: Japanese 紹介 (しょうかい, shōkai) and Korean 紹介/소개 (sogae) — both put 紹 "introduce" first and 介 "mediator" second, opposite the Mandarin/Cantonese/Dan'a'yo word order, though built from the identical two characters. This reversal is common enough that the vault's own `紹介` alias records it directly. Vietnamese giới thiệu keeps the Chinese word order (giới = 介, thiệu = 紹) and is the standard everyday verb for "to introduce" in Vietnamese, just as common as its Mandarin counterpart.
