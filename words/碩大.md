## Notes
- [碩](../characters/碩.md) "big, great" + [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big"

碩大 is a near-synonym compound built from two characters that both mean "big" — 碩, with the stone radical 石 and phonetic 石 doubling as semantic reinforcement (a "big" stone being a natural metaphor for solidity and size), and 大, the basic pictograph of a person standing with arms outstretched, already cross-linked to a long list of "big" compounds in [[大 (char)]]'s Words section (壮大, 拡大, 巨大, and others).

While 大 alone is one of the most common characters in the language, 碩 is comparatively rare and bookish outside of a handful of fixed expressions — most prominently 碩士 (master's degree, literally "great scholar"), the academic title between 学士 (bachelor's) and 博士 (doctorate). 碩大 itself is a literary intensifier: where 大 simply means "big," 碩大 means conspicuously, impressively big — closer to "enormous" or "massive" than to ordinary "large." Both 諺文 (석대) and Korean (석대) coincide here, since 碩's Korean reading 석 happens to match its Dan'a'yo syllable exactly (kwin: true) — one of the more direct character-to-syllable correspondences in this batch.
