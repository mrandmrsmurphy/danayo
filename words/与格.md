## Notes
- [与 (char)](../characters/与%20(char).md) "give; and; with" + [格 (char)](../characters/格%20(char).md) "case; status"

与格 is the dative case in Dan'a'yo — the case marking the recipient or indirect object of an action. In Dan'a'yo's grammatical framework, case roles are expressed through postpositional particles attached to noun phrases; the dative particle marks the entity to whom or for whom something is given, done, or directed. The dative covers the canonical indirect object (giving, telling, showing something *to* someone), the benefactive (doing something *for* someone), and the experiencer in certain predicate types. The term takes its name directly from 与 — "give; hand over" — centering the transfer-to-recipient relationship that defines the dative role. The alias 與格 is the traditional-form spelling (與 for 与).

与 (⼄/yo/요 — Mandarin yǔ, Cantonese jyu5, Korean 여, Japanese YO/よ, Vietnamese dữ/với): "give; and; with; together with" — MC 以母 (j) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ); a 会意 character, with the traditional-form 與 depicting hands raised in offering, giving the core sense of "to give; to hand over," extended to the conjunction "and" and the preposition "with." In grammatical metalanguage 与 identifies the recipient role: 与格 is the "giving-case," the case of the entity receiving the transfer. 格 (ㄍㄚㄎ/gag/각 — Mandarin gé, Cantonese gaak3, Korean 격, Japanese KAKU/かく, Vietnamese cách): "case; status; standard; grid" — MC 見母 (k) + 陌二韻 (ɑk, 2nd division); a 形声 of semantic 木 (wood) + phonetic 各 (OC \*klaːɡ), originally "to reach; to arrive," extended to "standard; framework; grammatical case." In Dan'a'yo linguistic terminology 格 is the fixed suffix for all case names: [[処格]] (locative), [[具格]] (instrumental), [[共格]] (comitative), [[終止格]] (terminative), [[奪格]] (ablative), [[向格]] (allative), [[比較格]] (comparative), [[様態格]] (manner), and 与格 (dative).
