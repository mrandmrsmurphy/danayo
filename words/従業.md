## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, be engaged in" + [業 (char)](../characters/業%20(char).md) "profession, business, work"

従業 denotes the state of being employed or engaged in a trade or occupation. Where [[従事]] emphasizes the act of engaging in an activity (often applied to vocations, fields, or missions), 従業 emphasizes the ongoing employment relationship — being part of a business or enterprise. The compound is the base for the extremely common 従業員 (じゅうぎょういん, jūgyōin), "employee" — one of the most frequently encountered compound words in modern Japanese business and labor contexts.

In Chinese (从业, cóngyè), the simplified form is standard; 从业人员 ("workers, employed persons") and 从业资格 ("professional qualification") are typical collocations. In Korean (종업), the term appears in 종업원 (従業員, employee) and 종업식 (終業式, closing ceremony — a homophonous but unrelated word that uses 終 rather than 従). The 諺文 종업 and Korean 종업 are identical, giving this word a rare kwin: true for a 従-initial compound.
