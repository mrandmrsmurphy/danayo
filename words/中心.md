## Notes
- [中 (char)](../characters/中%20(char).md) "middle" + [心 (char)](../characters/心%20(char).md) "heart; essential point"

中心 is "center; core; crux" — 中 (middle) + 心 (heart). The compound means the central, most essential point of something — both literally (the geometric center) and figuratively (the core purpose, the essential element, the hub). 心 grounds the compound in the heart-as-center metaphor ubiquitous in Chinese thought: just as the heart sits at the center of the body and animates everything around it, 中心 is the animating center of any system, structure, or argument.

Mandarin 中心 (zhōngxīn) is the standard term for center and hub: 市中心 (city center), 中心思想 (central idea; thesis), 研究中心 (research center), 商業中心 (commercial center). Cantonese 中心 (zung1 sam1). Japanese 中心 (ちゅうしん, chūshin) covers the same range: 中心部 (central area), 中心地 (central location), 中心人物 (key figure; central person). Korean 중심 (中心, jungsim) is the standard: 중심지 (center; hub), 중심부 (central area), 중심축 (central axis). Vietnamese trung tâm (Sino-Vietnamese, 中=trung, 心=tâm) is the standard word for center and hub: trung tâm thương mại (commercial center), trung tâm thành phố (city center).

kwin: true for 中心 — Dan'a'yo 중심 exactly matches Korean 중심. Both derive from Middle Chinese: 中 (ʈ + ɨuŋ → jung/중) + 心 (s + iɪm → sim/심) following closely parallel phonological paths. This is one of the cleaner kwin: true cases in the compound vocabulary — both characters are among the most common in Chinese, with highly consistent CJKV readings, and the Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean phonological paths converge precisely.
