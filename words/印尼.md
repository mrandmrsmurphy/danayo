## Notes
- [印](../characters/印.md) "India (clipped)" + [尼](../characters/尼.md) "nun; clipped transliteration syllable" — the standard clipped form of [[印度尼西亜]] ("Indonesia"), formed the same way [[英国]] retains 英 from 英吉利.

**印尼** (*Yìnní*) is overwhelmingly the more common form in running Mandarin and Cantonese text — the full transliteration 印度尼西亞/印度尼西亚 is reserved for formal contexts (treaties, official country listings), much as "Indonesia" itself is rarely abbreviated in English but Chinese-language news, weather reports, and casual writing default to 印尼. Korean **인니** (*inni*) is the parallel clipped form, though Korean more often uses the loanword **인도네시아** directly, with 인니 appearing mainly in headlines and compounds where brevity matters (e.g. 인니어, "the Indonesian language").

Japanese has no equivalent kanji clipping in ordinary use — インドネシア (katakana) serves for both the full and short forms, and 印尼 would be recognizable mainly to readers familiar with Chinese place-name abbreviations (as part of the broader set of one-character country abbreviations like 米, 英, 仏, 独, 露 used in Japanese compounds such as 日印関係, "Japan-India relations" — note that 印 alone, without 尼, is the standard Japanese abbreviation for *India*, making 印尼 potentially ambiguous outside of context). For Dan'a'yo's purposes, 印尼 follows the same compositional reading pattern as its constituent characters, with いんに given as the regular on-reading.
