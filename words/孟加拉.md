## Notes
- [孟](../characters/孟.md) "first, great" + [加](../characters/加.md) "increase" + [拉 (char)](../characters/拉%20(char).md) "pull" — phonetic rendering of *Baṅgāla*

孟加拉 transcribes the name of the Bengal delta region, from *Baṅgāla* (the Vanga people + the suffix *-āla*). The form 孟加拉 is the standard Sinographic rendering used in Taiwan, Hong Kong, Macau, Singapore, and Malaysia for the modern state of Bangladesh. In the People's Republic, 孟加拉国 uses a suffix. The earlier Ming dynasty rendering was 榜葛剌, recorded in Ma Huan's 瀛涯勝覽 from Zheng He's voyages (1405–1433); 孟加拉 displaced it as the standard form.

Bengal was a major center of Buddhist civilization before the rise of Muslim sultanates in the 13th century, and Chinese contact with the region is attested from at least the Tang dynasty — Xuanzang's records mention several Bengal-region kingdoms by name. The colonial partitions of Bengal (1905, 1947) and the Bangladesh Liberation War of 1971 produced the modern nation from the eastern half of the former British province.

Compare [[獅子国]], another South Asian country whose Dan'a'yo name derives from classical Sinospheric usage rather than modern phonetic transcription.
