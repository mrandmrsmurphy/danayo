## Notes
- [劇 (char)](../characters/劇%20(char).md) "drama, play" + [詩](../characters/詩.md) "poem"

劇詩 denotes poetry written for performance or in a dramatic form — verse that is inherently theatrical in structure, voice, or staging. The compound covers dramatic monologue, verse drama, and the lyric portions of opera and theatrical tradition. In European literary terminology, it corresponds to *dramatic poetry* as a genre distinct from epic and lyric poetry — a classification tracing to Aristotle's *Poetics* that entered East Asian literary discourse through translation and cross-cultural exchange.

In Japanese (げきし, gekishi), 劇詩 is primarily a translation equivalent for the Western genre. In Chinese (jùshī), it is used in modern literary criticism and translation studies. The compound reflects the broad semantic range of 劇 — which covers drama, theatre, opera, and intense dramatic action — applied to the poetic tradition.
