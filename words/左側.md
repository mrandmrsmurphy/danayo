## Notes
- [左](../characters/左.md) "left" + [側](../characters/側.md) "side; aspect" — stand-in for [[左]], which cannot appear independently

**Real bug corrected**: `korean` was `우측, 왼쪽` — `우측` is the reading for the unrelated word [[右側]] ("right side"), almost certainly copy-pasted by mistake, and `왼쪽` is the fully native Korean word for "left side," not a Sino-Korean reading of 左側 at all. Corrected to `좌측` (jwacheuk), the real Sino-Korean reading, directly paralleling 右側's own 우측 fix.

Mandarin zuǒcè, Cantonese zo2 zak1, and Japanese ひだりがわ (hidarigawa, mirroring 右側's みぎがわ exactly) are all the standard word for "the left side." Vietnamese tả trắc is the honest compositional reading (左=tả, as in tả hữu "left and right"; 側=trắc, as in trắc diện "side face," 側面's own real reading) — the everyday native word for "left" is simply trái.
