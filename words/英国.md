## Notes
- [英](../characters/英.md) "hero" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation" — literally "hero-nation," a flattering rendering built on 英 (the same syllable that opens the older transliteration 英吉利) rather than a literal description of the country.

英国 is the everyday geopolitical term covering the whole United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland — not narrowly "England" — across Chinese, Japanese, and Korean alike; Japan's own Ministry of Foreign Affairs lists 英国 as the standard short name for the country on its official pages, exactly parallel to how Chinese news and government usage treats 英国 as synonymous with "the UK." The name is a historical accident: it derives by metonymy from 英吉利/英格蘭, the transliteration of "England," the same way English speakers themselves loosely say "England" to mean the whole of Britain.

The precision the name glosses over is preserved in two related words: [[英格蘭]] is reserved for England specifically, used whenever a speaker needs to distinguish it from Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland within the UK (the England football team is 英格蘭隊, never 英国隊, since the UK fields no unified team in most sports); [[連合王国]] is the fully formal name for "United Kingdom" itself, used mainly in official/legal contexts rather than everyday speech. In practice, as 英格蘭's own notes observe, most speakers reach for 英国 regardless of which of the three they actually mean.

The register this leaves 英国 in is not uniform across the Sinosphere. In Korean specifically, 영국 carries a faint awareness of its own imprecision — speakers know it literally privileges "England" over the UK's other constituent nations, and a careful speaker switches to 연합왕국 when the distinction actually matters, e.g. addressing someone Scottish or Welsh, so as not to imply their country is simply "England." Japanese shows no equivalent hesitation: the Ministry of Foreign Affairs uses 英国 as the unmarked official name with no register concern at all, suggesting the metonymy reads as fully naturalized there rather than as a diplomatically loaded shortcut.

Vietnamese Vương quốc Anh ("Kingdom of Anh," i.e. "Kingdom of England/Britain") preserves the same England-for-Britain metonymy independently, built on its own transliteration Anh (from 英) rather than borrowing the Sino-Vietnamese compound directly.
