## Notes
- [地 (char)](../characters/地%20(char).md) "earth, land" + [支](../characters/支.md) "branch" — the twelve cyclic signs (子丑寅卯辰巳午未申酉戌亥) used with the Ten Heavenly Stems to form the sexagenary cycle

The Twelve Earthly Branches (地支) form the lower half of the traditional sexagenary calendar cycle. Each branch is associated with one of the twelve zodiac animals (鼠牛虎兔龍蛇馬羊猿鶏狗猪) and with a two-hour division of the day, a compass direction, and a month. Paired with the Ten [[天干]] (Heavenly Stems), they generate a cycle of sixty unique combinations used across East Asia for calendrical reckoning, fortune-telling, and the traditional naming of years.

The 地支 are also the source of the twelve-hour divisions of the traditional day: 子 (11pm–1am), 丑 (1–3am), and so on around the clock. The Astronomy lexipedia's 六十干支 table shows all sixty stem-branch combinations, each numbered and paired with a zodiac animal.
