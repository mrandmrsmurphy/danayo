## Notes
- [分 (char)](../characters/分%20(char).md) "to divide" + [掌](../characters/掌.md) "to manage, hold"

分掌 means "to divide up [duties]" or, as a noun, "a division of responsibilities" — each person or department holding (掌) their own separate (分) portion of a larger task. 分 is a 会意 character combining 八 ("to separate, divide in two") with 刀 ("knife") — the image of cutting something into parts — and is one of the most productive characters in the vocabulary, already anchoring [[分娩]] ("give birth"), [[分泌]] ("secrete"), [[分析]] ("analyze"), and [[気分]] ("mood"), all cross-linked from [[分 (char)]]'s Words section.

分掌 forms a natural pair with [[掌管]] (earlier in this batch): where 掌管 names the act of *holding overall charge* of something, 分掌 names the *parceling out* of that charge among several hands — the organizational complement to centralized management. In Japanese corporate and bureaucratic usage, 分掌 (ぶんしょう) appears in compounds like 業務分掌 ("division of business duties"), formally documenting who is responsible for what within an organization.
