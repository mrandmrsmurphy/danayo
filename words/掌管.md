## Notes
- [掌](../characters/掌.md) "palm; to hold, manage" + [管](../characters/管.md) "tube; to administer"

掌管 means "to be in charge of, to manage" — to hold something in one's hands (掌, "palm") and direct it (管, in its extended sense of "to administer, to control"). 掌 is a 形声 character (phonetic 尚 over semantic 手, "hand") whose literal meaning is "palm, sole" — the part of the hand that holds and grips — already extended metaphorically to "to hold, to be in charge of" in both 掌管 and [[仙人掌]] (the next word in this batch, where 掌 instead contributes its literal "palm-shaped" sense to "cactus").

管's own core meaning is "pipe, tube" — a 形声 character built on the bamboo radical 竹 over phonetic 官, originally a bamboo pipe or flute, and by extension any tube-shaped channel through which something flows or is directed (cf. [[導管]], 管's stand_in word, "conduit"). The metaphorical leap from "tube that channels flow" to "to administer, to control" parallels English "channel" (as in "to channel resources") and "pipeline." 掌管 thus literally combines two independent metaphors for control — "grasping in the palm" and "directing through a channel" — into a single compound verb of administration.
