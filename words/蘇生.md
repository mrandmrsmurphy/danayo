## Notes
- [蘇](../characters/蘇.md) "revive, come back to life" + [生](../characters/生.md) "life"

A compound for revival, resurrection, or resuscitation — both medical (CPR, resuscitation of the unconscious) and figurative (the revival of a tradition, economy, or culture). 蘇 already carries the full sense of coming back to life; 生 reinforces and substantivizes it, making 蘇生 more concrete than 蘇 alone, which is too bounded to stand as an independent word.

In Japanese, 蘇生 (sosei) is the primary term for both medical resuscitation (心肺蘇生, shinhai sosei, cardiopulmonary resuscitation) and figurative revival, and is actively used in modern speech and writing. Chinese uses 复苏/復甦 (fùsū) more commonly for revival, where 甦 is a compound character explicitly encoding the meaning "revive" (更 "again" + 生 "life"). The alternative spellings 甦生 and — in simplified Chinese — 苏生 reflect 蘇's graphic variants; Wiktionary notes 甦生 as an attested alternative.

Korean 소생 (蘇生) carries both the medical sense and a literary/Buddhist sense of spiritual revival, and appears in 소생시키다 (to revive, to resuscitate). Vietnamese equivalents tend to use hồi sinh (回生, "return to life") rather than directly borrowing 蘇生.
