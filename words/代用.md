## Notes
- [代](../characters/代.md) "replace, generation" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use" — to use one thing in place of another it stands in for.

代 carries the core sense of "standing in for, taking the place of" found across a wide family of vocabulary — 代表 ("representative," already attested elsewhere in the vocabulary as existing) and 代理 ("proxy, agent," not yet created) both lean on the same root — while 用 is the basic verb-noun for "use, application" seen in [[作用]] ("function, effect") and 用法 ("usage," not yet created). 代用 narrows the combination to the concrete case of substitution: a 代用品 is a substitute product (sugar standing in for honey, a stunt double standing in for an actor), and the word carries a slight connotation of "making do" — adequate, but not the genuine article. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
