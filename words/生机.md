## Notes
- [生](../characters/生.md) "life" + [机](../characters/机.md) "mechanism, opportunity" (Dan'a'yo form of 機)

生機 denotes the animating force of living things — the quality of vitality, vigor, and generative energy that distinguishes the living from the inert. In Chinese, it appears in two distinct senses: the vitality of an organism or environment (生機勃勃, "bursting with vitality") and the slender chance of survival in a desperate situation (一線生機, "a thread of hope"). The tension between these two senses — abundant life on one hand, fragile life on the other — gives the word a particular richness.

In Japanese (せいき, seiki), the compound is literary and appears in nature writing and classical-style prose. The Dan'a'yo form uses 机 (the simplified and Japanese form of 機) rather than the traditional 機; the alias 生機 covers the traditional Chinese spelling.
