## Notes
- [百 (char)](../characters/百%20(char).md) "hundred" + [升 (char)](../characters/升%20(char).md) "liter" — a hundred liters, a numeral prefixed directly onto the base unit [[升]].

This is the one place the family splits along real-versus-loanword lines rather than the usual meaning-drift pattern: Chinese genuinely has a native compound for this unit — 百升 (bǎishēng) is attested alongside 公石 (gōngshí) and 百公升 (bǎigōngshēng) as real terms for "hectoliter" — while Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese all reach directly for the Western loanword instead (ヘクトリットル, 헥토리터, hectolit), since none of them built a native kanji/compound term for a unit this specialized and rarely used outside of Chinese-language contexts. The Dan'a'yo compound follows the Chinese path, deriving 百升 compositionally from its own numeral 百 and unit 升 rather than borrowing the Western loanword the other three languages settled on.
