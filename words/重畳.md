## Notes
- [重 (char)](../characters/重%20(char).md) "repeat, layer" + [畳 (char)](../characters/畳%20(char).md) "stack, fold" — to lay one thing on top of another, repeating it: layering, overlap, and — in linguistics — reduplication.

This entry's second character is written 畳, the Japanese form, but the vault treats it as one character with its Mandarin/Korean traditional cousins 疊/叠 listed as aliases — so this same word is, transparently, Mandarin 重叠/重疊 (chóngdié, the standard linguistics term for reduplication) and Korean 중첩 (重疊, "overlap, layering"), not a Japan-only coinage despite its surface spelling. The grammar book devotes a whole section to 重畳語法 ("reduplication grammar"): whether a [[単字]] reduplicates alone or only its main character reduplicates within a [[合成]]詞, why already-doubled words like 人人 cannot reduplicate again (requiring [[迂言]] instead), and how reduplicating a noun yields a diminutive while reduplicating an adjective yields an emotional superlative. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
