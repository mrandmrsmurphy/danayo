## Notes
- [分 (char)](../characters/分%20(char).md) "divide" + [析](../characters/析.md) "split, parse" — to analyze; analysis

Both components share the semantic core of breaking a whole into parts: 分 denotes division in general (the character shows a knife 刀 separating 八), while 析 specifies the splitting of wood along the grain (木 + 斤, axe on wood), extended to careful, methodical decomposition. Together they form a compound in which the act of analysis is understood as controlled separation — distinguishing constituents that were previously undifferentiated.

The compound is shared across all five languages of the Dan'a'yo sphere with near-identical semantics: Mandarin fēnxī, Cantonese fan1 sik1, Japanese ぶんせき (bunseki), Korean 분석 (bunsok), Vietnamese phân tích. In each language it covers both the action (to analyze) and the result (an analysis), functioning as a verbal noun — the same single form serves both roles without derivational marking.

分析哲学 (analytic philosophy) and 分析化学 (analytical chemistry) show the compound's reach: it became the standard Meiji-era translation for "analysis" in both the natural sciences and philosophy when the term arrived from European discourse, and it transferred unchanged into modern Chinese and Korean intellectual vocabulary through the shared written medium.
