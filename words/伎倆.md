## Notes
- [伎](../characters/伎.md) "talent, skill" + [両](../characters/両%20(char).md) "both" — stand-in for [[伎]], which cannot appear independently
- 伎倆 is a classical Chinese binome. The character 伎 (skill, performance, craft) is paired with 倆 (here an alias of [[両 (char)|両]]), which functions as a syllabic complement giving the compound its two-syllable form. In literary Chinese the word is neutral — "skill, talent, ability" — but in modern Mandarin it has shifted toward the negative register, typically meaning "underhanded trick, machination, ploy." The phrase 鬼蜮伎倆 ("the tricks of demons and water-sprites") captures the dominant modern sense: something clever but not quite honest.
- In Japanese, 伎倆 (ぎりょう, giryō) is an alternative spelling of 技量 (きりょう, kiryō), with no negative connotation — purely "ability, competence, skill" in formal or literary use.
- In Korean, 기량 (伎倆) likewise means "skill, talent, ability" without the negative overtone.
