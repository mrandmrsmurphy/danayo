## Notes
- [造](../characters/造.md) "manufacture, create" + [幣 (char)](../characters/幣%20(char).md) "currency, coin" + [局 (char)](../characters/局%20(char).md) "bureau" — mint; the government bureau that manufactures currency

造幣 is the act of manufacturing coins (and by extension paper money): 造 (to make, to manufacture) + 幣 (currency — originally silk bolts used as currency, later extended to metal and paper money). 局 designates it as a government bureau.

Japan's 造幣局 (Japan Mint), established in 1871 in Osaka during the Meiji modernization, is one of the oldest and most famous institutions bearing this name. It produces coins for circulation, commemorative coins, and medals; its Osaka headquarters is also celebrated for a cherry-blossom viewing path (桜の通り抜け) along the mint grounds, open to the public each spring. The compound passed into Korean as 조폐국 (or the parastatal 조폐공사, Korean Minting and Security Printing Corporation).
