## Notes
- [針](../characters/針.md) "needle" (here phonetically standing in for 箴, "to admonish," which doesn't exist as its own character in this vault) + [言 (char)](../characters/言%20(char).md) "speech, words" — a maxim of self-admonishment.

箴言/잠언/しんげん/châm ngôn all share the same real-world double life: an everyday word for "proverb, maxim, aphorism," and also the standard name for the biblical Book of Proverbs (旧約聖書の箴言/구약성경의 잠언, traditionally attributed to Solomon). Japanese draws out a genuine nuance the others leave implicit: 箴言 specifically denotes a maxim meant to admonish *oneself*, distinct from ことわざ (kotowaza), the ordinary word for a folk proverb aimed at general wisdom or advice to others — the distinction traces to 箴's own original sense, a needle used to "correct" or "prick" oneself into right conduct.
