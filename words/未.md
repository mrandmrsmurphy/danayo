## Notes
- [未 (char)](../characters/未%20(char).md) "not yet; haven't yet"

未 is the imperfective-negative aspect marker in Dan'a'yo — "not yet; haven't yet" — marking that an expected or anticipated action has not yet been realized: 未食 (hasn't eaten yet), 未去 (hasn't gone yet; not yet gone). It occupies the gap between [[已]] (done; realized) and [[将]] (will; prospective) — the action is anticipated but not completed. In classical Chinese, 未 (OC *mɨts) was the primary negation of completion: 未嘗 (has never), 未必 (not necessarily), 未知 (not yet known). Dan'a'yo retains this completion-negating function directly. 未 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[未]].

The comparison chart: Mandarin 没有 (méiyǒu, haven't; didn't — negates completed action), 还没 (hái méi, still haven't; not yet). Cantonese 未 (mei6, not yet) — notably, Cantonese uses 未 directly as the "not yet" marker: 食未? (have you eaten yet?), 未食 (haven't eaten yet). Japanese まだ (mada, not yet; still: まだ食べていない hasn't eaten yet), まだ〜ていない (not yet done). Korean 아직 (ajik, not yet; still: 아직 안 먹었다 still hasn't eaten), 못 (mot, cannot yet — inability). Vietnamese chưa (not yet; haven't yet — directly equivalent: chưa ăn hasn't eaten yet; chưa đi hasn't gone yet).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 뮈 does not match Korean 미. 未 reads 뮈 (mui/ㄇㄨㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 微母 (ɱ, labiodental nasal) + 微韻 (ʉi, diphthong). Both Dan'a'yo and Korean map the MC labiodental nasal ɱ to a plain bilabial nasal m- (ㄇ/ㅁ). The MC diphthong ʉi: Dan'a'yo preserves it in full as -ui (ㄨㄧ/뮈), while Sino-Korean 미 simplifies the diphthong to plain -i, dropping the -u element. Dan'a'yo's retention of the full MC diphthong ʉi contrasts with the Korean monophthongization — the same pattern by which Dan'a'yo preserves MC -w glides that Korean has dropped.
