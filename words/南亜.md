## Notes
- [南](../characters/南.md) "south" + [亜](../characters/亜.md) "Asia" — South Asia; the Indian subcontinent and surrounding nations

南亜 is one of the most transparent compounds in Dan'a'yo: 南 (south) + 亜 (the standard Sinospheric abbreviation for 亜細亜, Asia), yielding the standard Chinese term for the region. It covers the Indian subcontinent and its Himalayan and island periphery: historically, the core nations are India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, and Bhutan, with the Maldives and sometimes Afghanistan included.

The region's significance to the Sinosphere derives above all from Buddhism. The dharma traveled north and east from the Gangetic plain and from Gandharan Pakistan (犍陀羅), passing through Nepal on its way to Tibet, and by sea to Sri Lanka (獅子国) and thence to Southeast Asia. The great Chinese pilgrims — Faxian (法顯), Xuanzang (玄奘), Yijing (義淨) — all crossed into 南亜 in search of texts and teachers, and their accounts constitute some of the most detailed records of the subcontinent in the first millennium AD. This shared Buddhist inheritance is the primary thread linking the Sinosphere to South Asia.

In modern geopolitical usage, 南亜 corresponds to the SAARC bloc (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation), founded in 1985. Dan'a'yo words for member states: [[獅子国]] (Sri Lanka), [[孟加拉]] (Bangladesh), [[巴基斯坦]] (Pakistan), with India and the remaining members awaiting entries.
