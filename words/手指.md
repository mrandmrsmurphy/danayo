## Notes
- [手 (char)](../characters/手%20(char).md) "hand" + [指](../characters/指.md) "finger, toe" — stand-in for [[指]], which cannot appear independently.

Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean all keep 手指/수지/しゅし as the real, standard word for "finger." Vietnamese is the outlier: ngón tay, a fully native compound, is the everyday word in speech, while the Sino-Vietnamese reading thủ chỉ survives mainly as word trivia — a well-known riddle asks "bàn tay là thủ, ngón tay là chỉ, vậy cánh tay là gì?" ("the hand is thủ, the finger is chỉ, so what's the arm?"), treating the Sino-Vietnamese body-part vocabulary as a puzzle rather than everyday usage.
