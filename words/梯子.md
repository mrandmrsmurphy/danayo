## Notes
- [梯](../characters/梯.md) "ladder" + [子](../characters/子.md) "generic noun suffix" — this word is the stand-in for [梯](../characters/梯.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

梯子 is transparently 梯 "ladder" plus 子 in its most common modern role: a light, semantically-empty noun suffix (the same 子 seen throughout [[子]]'s own huge "thing" word-list — 椅子 "chair," 帽子 "hat," 扇子 "fan" — rather than its literal "child" sense). The word denotes the ordinary portable ladder, and by extension in colloquial Japanese, the practice of visiting several venues of the same kind in one outing (梯子する, "to bar-hop" or "pub crawl").

**Japanese reads this compound with a fixed native reading, はしご (hashigo), not the plain concatenation of on'yomi** (梯 TEI + 子 SHI would give ていし, which does survive as a secondary, more formal Sino-Japanese reading — but はしご is overwhelmingly dominant in ordinary use). This is the same "fused/dominant native reading" pattern already flagged for 呪文's じゅもん, though here the native reading itself is native Japanese vocabulary for the object, not a separate word entirely.

**Korean has no real Sino-Korean life for this compound at all** — everyday Korean uses the purely native word 사다리 (sadari) for "ladder," not a Hanja compound. 제자 (jeja) is used here only as the mechanical concatenation of 梯's 제 and 子's 자, per this vault's own derivation convention, not as an attested real word — and it happens to coincide exactly with the common Korean reading of an entirely unrelated word, 弟子 ("disciple, apprentice," also 제자), a pure homophone collision with no semantic connection. `vietnamese` is omitted for the same reason in reverse: everyday Vietnamese uses the native word *thang* for "ladder," with no attested Sino-Vietnamese compound reading for 梯子 itself.
