## Notes
- [遷](../characters/遷.md) "shift; move; change" + [移](../characters/移.md) "move; transfer; shift" — stand-in for both [[遷]] and [[移]], neither of which can appear independently
- 遷移 doubles down on a single idea — two near-synonyms for "moving" or "shifting" fused into one compound that names the act of relocation or transition itself, whether of a capital, a population, a species, or (in modern technical registers) a system's state. The reading is consistent and transparent across the Sinosphere: Mandarin qiānyí, Cantonese cin1 ji4, Sino-Korean 천이 (cheoni), and Sino-Japanese せんい (sen'i) all keep the compound's two halves intact and legible, while Sino-Vietnamese thiên di follows the same pattern. Both 遷 and 移 carry an old, slightly formal flavor — 遷 especially appears in classical contexts of dynasties moving their capitals or officials being reassigned to distant posts — so 遷移 retains a faint air of the deliberate, often consequential kind of movement: not a casual stroll but a meaningful relocation, a passage from one state or place to another that leaves something behind.
