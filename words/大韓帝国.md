## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "great" + [韓](../characters/韓.md) "Han (Korean)" + [[帝国]] "empire" ([帝](../characters/帝.md) "emperor" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation")

大韓帝国 is the Korean Empire (1897–1910), the name adopted by the Joseon dynasty when King Gojong proclaimed himself emperor, asserting Korea's formal equality with the Qing empire of China and the imperial states of Japan and Russia. The proclamation marked the end of the tributary relationship with Qing China (already effectively dissolved after the First Sino-Japanese War of 1895) and declared Korea a fully sovereign imperial state. The new name — 大韓 (Greater Han, i.e. greater Korea) + [[帝国]] (empire) — was a deliberate assertion of sovereign dignity using the same political vocabulary as the neighboring powers.

The empire lasted only thirteen years. Japan's victory in the Russo-Japanese War (1905) led to the Protectorate Treaty, which transferred control of Korean foreign affairs to Japan; the subsequent annexation treaty of 1910 ended the empire and incorporated Korea into the Japanese Empire. The name 대한제국 remains historically resonant: 대한 (大韓) survives in the official names of both present-day Korean states — 대한민국 (大韓民国, Republic of Korea) and 조선민주주의인민공화국 (North Korea, which uses the Joseon-era name instead).
