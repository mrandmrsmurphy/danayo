## Notes
- [莫 (char)](../characters/莫%20(char).md) "not exist; to be absent"

莫 is the non-existence copula in Dan'a'yo — "to not exist; to be absent" — denying that the subject exists or is present: 人莫 (the person does not exist; nobody is there), 莫此 (this does not exist here; it isn't here). Among the six Dan'a'yo copulas, 莫 is the direct antonym of [[在]] (to exist; to be present). While [[無]] negates possession (to not have), 莫 negates existence (to not be present anywhere) — a subtle but distinct opposition. The character's original meaning was "dusk; the sun sinking into the bushes" — a 象形 of 日 (sun) descending into 艸 (grass/bushes) — phonetically borrowed for the negative existential sense; the original visual meaning was recovered in 暮 (mù, dusk/evening). 莫 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[莫]].

Mandarin: no single lexeme covers 莫 as a negative existential copula; 不在 (bùzài, "not present") or 没有 (méiyǒu, "not exist") covers the meaning contextually. In classical Chinese, 莫 served as a negative universal ("no one; nothing; not any"): 莫若 (nothing is better than), 莫非 (could it be that? — lit. "not except"), 莫大 (the greatest; nothing bigger). Cantonese 唔在 (m4 zoi6, "not be-present"): 佢唔在 (he/she is not there); 冇 (mou5) for non-possession. Japanese いない (inai, animate absence) and ない (nai, inanimate absence): 誰もいない (there is nobody), 本がない (there is no book); 莫 (まく/MAKU) in archaic Sino-Japanese contexts. Korean 없다 (eopda): serves both non-possession (無) and non-existence (莫) — 아무도 없다 (there is nobody), 여기 없어요 (it isn't here). Vietnamese không có / không ở: Không có ai (there is nobody), Không ở đây (not here); Sino-Vietnamese mạc (莫) in set literary compounds.

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 막 exactly matches Korean 막. 莫 reads 막 (mag/ㄇㄚㄎ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 明母 (m) + 鈬開韻 (ɑk) gives a bilabial nasal onset with the -ak coda. Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map MC 明母 to m- and MC 鈬開韻 (ɑk) to plain -ak, producing identical 막 forms. The MC 鈬開韻 with its -k coda is one of the more straightforwardly preserved finals in both systems — no medial, no diphthong, a simple stop coda — making 莫 one of the phonologically stable kwin: true cases.
