## Notes
- [抜](../characters/抜.md) "select, pull out" + [擢](../characters/擢.md) "select, promote"
- This word is the stand-in for [[擢]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- To single out a person for promotion or special appointment — to pluck them from the crowd by recognizing their excellence. In Japanese, ばってき (batteki) is a formal word for the act of selecting and promoting a talented person: 若手を抜擢する "to select and promote a young talent." The compound reinforces with two near-synonyms — 抜 "pull out from a group" (cf. 選抜) and 擢 "raise up, promote" — both evoking the image of being drawn upward out of the ranks. In Chinese, 拔擢 (bázhuó) is literary/classical; the modern equivalent is 提拔 (tíbá). Korean 발탁 (拔擢) is the standard word for talent selection: 신인 발탁 "selection of a new talent."
