## Notes
- [黒 (char)](../characters/黒%20(char).md) "black" + [猩](../characters/猩.md) "ape" — chimpanzee (*Pan troglodytes*); shortened from 黒猩猩

黒猩 is the Dan'a'yo shortening of 黒猩猩 (黑猩猩, hēi xīng xīng), the full Mandarin term for chimpanzee, paralleling the abbreviation pattern used across 大猩 (gorilla) and 倭猩 (bonobo). The "black ape" name reflects the chimpanzee's dark coat and face.

*Pan troglodytes* is humanity's closest living relative, sharing approximately 98.7% of the human genome. Native to the forests and savannas of sub-Saharan Africa, chimpanzees live in fission-fusion communities of 15–150 individuals with complex dominance hierarchies, sophisticated tool use — stone hammers to crack nuts, stripped twigs to extract termites — and cultural transmission of behaviors between generations. Jane Goodall's research at Gombe Stream beginning in 1960 fundamentally changed the understanding of primate intelligence and the boundary between human and animal behavior. Chimpanzees are classified as endangered; habitat loss and bushmeat hunting have reduced wild populations to an estimated 170,000–300,000 individuals.
