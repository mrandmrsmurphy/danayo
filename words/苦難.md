## Notes
- [苦 (char)](../characters/苦%20(char).md) "bitter, suffering" + [難](../characters/難.md) "difficulty, disaster" — hardship; tribulation; severe suffering

Both components reinforce each other: 苦 names the subjective texture of suffering — the bitterness of pain or deprivation — while 難 names its objective dimension, the difficulty or calamity that occasions it. The compound therefore describes suffering that is not merely felt but inflicted by circumstance.

The word resonates deeply with Buddhist vocabulary. 苦 is the first of the Four Noble Truths (四聖諦) — dukkha, the pervasive unsatisfactoriness of conditioned existence. 苦難 extends this into the register of historical and narrative hardship: the 苦難 of a people under oppression, of refugees in flight, of the faithful under persecution. It appears with particular frequency in religious texts, historical chronicles, and the language of social movements across all five languages.

Korean 고난 is especially common in Christian contexts — the word translates "tribulation" and "passion" (as in the passion of Christ), and appears in hymns and sermons far more often than in secular speech. This is a case where the same Sinitic compound has been differentially weighted by each language's dominant religious tradition.
