## Notes
- [橙](../characters/橙.md) "orange (fruit)" + [色](../characters/色.md) "color"

橙 is the bitter orange (Citrus × aurantium), from which the color term derives. Orange as a named color arrived late in many languages — in English "orange" as a color word does not predate the fruit's introduction to Europe — and the same pattern holds in Chinese: the color 橙色 is named directly after the fruit. The Japanese on-yomi reading とうしょく is used in formal contexts; the native reading だいだいいろ (daidai-iro, named after the bitter orange) is more common in everyday Japanese speech.
