## Notes
- [業 (char)](../characters/業%20(char).md) "profession; business; karma" + [業 (char)](../characters/業%20(char).md) (ideophonic reduplication)

業業 carries two classical senses that pull in opposite directions:

**1. Fearful; apprehensive.** This is the primary Dan'a'yo meaning. The *Shūjīng* (書經, Book of Documents) uses 業業 to evoke a state of cautious, anxious vigilance — the feeling of someone who knows how much is at stake and dares not relax. It is best known in this sense through the four-character compound 兢兢業業 (*jīngjīng yèyè*), where it pairs with [[恐恐]] (aliased as 兢兢): "wary and fearful; diligent and careful." The two ideophones reinforce each other — 兢兢 (trembling caution) + 業業 (apprehensive vigilance) = the complete posture of someone carrying a heavy responsibility with full awareness of the stakes.

**2. Lofty; imposing; large.** The *Shījīng* (詩經, Classic of Songs) uses 業業 to describe great steeds — huge, magnificent, physically impressive. This is the visual and spatial sense: something that looms large, that commands attention by its sheer scale.

The two meanings are not entirely unrelated: the same quality of something weighty and consequential underlies both — one felt as fear from below, one seen as grandeur from outside.
