## Notes
- [折](../characters/折.md) "to bend; to break; to fold" + [畳 (char)](../characters/畳%20(char).md) "to pile up; to repeat; tatami mat" — stand-in for [[折]], which cannot appear independently
- A literal, compositional pairing — "to bend" plus "to layer" — that across the Sinosphere denotes the act of folding something flat into a smaller, layered shape: Mandarin zhédié, Cantonese zit3 dip6, Sino-Korean 절첩 (jeolcheop). Japanese keeps the on'yomi compound せつじょう as the more formal/technical equivalent, but in everyday speech prefers the native kun'yomi form 折り畳み (おりたたみ, oritatami) — the word behind compact, collapsible everyday objects like 折りたたみ傘 "folding umbrella" and 折りたたみ椅子 "folding chair." 畳 itself is the Japanese shinjitai simplification of 疊 (the form preserved in the word's traditional-script alias 折疊 / simplified 折叠), and not coincidentally also names the woven straw tatami mat — itself something laid down in overlapping layers.
