## Notes
- [英](../characters/英.md) "hero; flower" + [格 (char)](../characters/格%20(char).md) "case, status" + [蘭](../characters/蘭.md) "orchid" — a phonetic transliteration of "England", reading the three characters for their sounds rather than their meanings.

英格蘭 (*Yīnggélán*) is the standard Mandarin transliteration for "England" as a country/cultural-historical entity, distinct from [[英国]] "the United Kingdom" (lit. "hero/English country"), which is the everyday geopolitical term covering the whole of the UK. As [[英国]]'s notes observe, the England/UK distinction made by 英格蘭 vs. 英国 is technically correct but rarely observed in practice — most speakers simply say 英国 for both the country England and the sovereign state of the United Kingdom.

英格蘭 belongs to the same family of 英-initial transliterations as the older [[英吉利]] "England (dated)", both ultimately rendering the "Eng-" of "England" with 英 (*yīng*, lit. "hero, flower") before adding further syllables to approximate the rest of the English name — 格蘭 here corresponding to "-gland", and 吉利 in the older form corresponding to "-glia"/"-gland" via a different syllabification. Where 英吉利 has fallen out of common use except in fixed compounds, 英格蘭 remains the standard term whenever a Mandarin speaker specifically needs to distinguish England from Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland within the United Kingdom — for instance in sports contexts (the England national football team is 英格蘭隊, never 英国隊, since the UK does not field a unified team in most sports).

The Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese forms given here are likewise straightforward character-by-character renderings of the same transliteration, reflecting how this name for England spread across the Sinosphere as a unit alongside the broader vocabulary of Western geography introduced in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
