## Notes
- [予](../characters/予.md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Yu Province (豫州), the seventh of the [[九州]]; 予 is the Dan'a'yo canonical form, 豫 being its alias

豫州 was the central province — roughly modern Henan — occupying the middle of the central plain, the heartland of early Chinese civilization. The Yellow River meanders through it; it was the region of the ancient capital zones of the Shang and early Zhou. In the *Tribute of Yu*, 豫州 is listed with the most fertile soil and the highest-grade tribute, reflecting its political centrality. Henan province (河南) today covers much of the same territory and retains a special status in Chinese historical consciousness as the cradle of Chinese civilization.
