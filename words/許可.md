## Notes
- [許 (char)](../characters/許%20(char).md) "permit, allow" + [可](../characters/可.md) "can, may, permissible" — two near-synonymous "permit" roots stacked together: permission, authorization.

The grammar book pairs 許可 with [[能力]] as the two senses bundled into Dan'a'yo's modal prefix 可–: the preposition form 可以 ("the permission-word," 許可詞) specifically marks permission, distinct from the bare prefix's ability/capacity sense, and the two may co-occur with negation. 可 itself is one of the vocabulary's most productive modal roots — see [[可読]] and [[可愛]] (already attested) for two more of its many "can be [verbed]" derivatives. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
