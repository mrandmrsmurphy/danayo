## Etymology
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what, which" + [名 (char)](../characters/名%20(char).md) "name" — the most deferential rung of the who-ladder: [[誰]] is the neutral interrogative, [[何人]] is blunt to the point of rudeness, and 何名 is reserved for the most polite register. The Japanese gloss どなた happens to be the real, everyday honorific word for "who" in Japanese — a genuine coincidence of sense with this Dan'a'yo-internal coinage, not a borrowing.
