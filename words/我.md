## Notes
- [我 (char)](../characters/我%20(char).md) "I; me; first-person pronoun"

我 is the first-person pronoun in Dan'a'yo — "I; me" — one of the few true personal pronouns in the language: 我食 (I eat; I am eating), 我去 (I go; I will go). Dan'a'yo has a genuine plural for 我: [[我等]] ("we; us" — lit. "I-plural"), which is noteworthy since most Dan'a'yo nouns form plurals analytically rather than morphologically. Archaic alternatives include the humble [[俺]] and the royal [[朕]], neither in common use. 我 is the Swadesh 1 word — among the most basic and universally attested vocabulary — and one of the most used characters in the Classical Chinese corpus. 我 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[我]].

Mandarin 我 (wǒ, I; me), 我们 (wǒmen, we; us), 咱们 (zámen, we inclusive — speaker + listener), 本人 (běnrén, I; myself — formal written). Cantonese 我 (ngo5, I; me), 我哋 (ngo5 dei6, we; us). Japanese 私 (watashi, I — polite/neutral), 僕 (boku, I — masculine casual), 俺 (ore, I — masculine rough), 我 (ware, I — literary/classical: 我思う故に我あり "I think therefore I am"). Korean 나 (na, I — general), 저 (jeo, I — humble/polite toward seniors), 우리 (uri, we; our — also used possessively: 우리 나라 "our country; Korea"). Vietnamese tôi (I — neutral/formal), tao (I — intimate/rough), em/anh/chị (I — role-relative pronouns depending on social register vs. addressee).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 아 exactly matches Korean 아. 我 reads 아 ('a/ㄚ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 疑母 (ŋ, velar nasal) + 歌韻 (ɑ). The MC velar nasal initial ŋ drops to a null initial in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, leaving a pure vowel onset. MC 歌韻 (ɑ) gives Dan'a'yo ㄚ (-a/아) and Korean 아 (-a) — the same vowel in both, producing identical 아 forms. Note that when MC 歌韻 follows a consonantal initial (as in [[他]] ㄊㄜ/트 vs 타; [[可]] ㄎㄜ/크 vs 가), Dan'a'yo shifts the vowel to -ə while Korean retains -a. With null initial, as here, the -a is preserved in both systems.
