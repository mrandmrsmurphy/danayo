## Notes
- [大](../characters/大%20(char).md) "big, great" + [廈](../characters/廈.md) "mansion, building"
- This word is the stand-in for [[廈]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A grand or large building, typically a multi-story structure. In Chinese, 大廈 (dàshà) is the standard term for a "mansion" or "high-rise," appearing in the names of landmark buildings (帝國大廈, the Empire State Building). The character 廈 also appears in the name of Xiamen (廈門 Xiàmén), a coastal city in Fujian — known in Vietnamese as Hạ Môn. In Japanese, 大廈 (たいか taika) is an archaic or literary term for a great house; the modern equivalent is ビル (biru, from "building"). In Korean, 대하 appears in formal and literary contexts, with 건물 (建物) being the everyday term.
