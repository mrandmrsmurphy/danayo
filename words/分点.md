## Notes
- [分 (char)](../characters/分%20(char).md) "divide" + [点 (char)](../characters/点%20(char).md) "point" — the two moments each year when the sun crosses the celestial equator and day and night are of equal length

An equinox (分点) is literally the "division point" — the moment when the sun's path crosses the equator, dividing the year into its two great halves. There are two each year: the vernal equinox (春分点) in March and the autumnal equinox (秋分点) in September. Contrast with [[至点]] (solstice point), the turning point where the sun reaches its greatest distance from the equator.
