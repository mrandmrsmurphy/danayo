## Notes
- [是 (char)](../characters/是%20(char).md) "is; to equal"

是 is the identity/equality copula in Dan'a'yo — the foundational "to be; to equal" — linking a subject to its category or predicate noun: 此是書 (this is a book), 我是教師 (I am a teacher), 彼是誰 (who is that?). Among the six Dan'a'yo copulas, 是 occupies the prototypical equative function, asserting that the subject and predicate refer to the same referent or category; its antonym is [[非]] (to not be; to not equal). In classical Chinese, 是 was originally a demonstrative pronoun meaning "this; right; correct" — modern Mandarin preserves this in 是非 (right and wrong) and 是否 (whether or not) — before being reanalyzed as a copula in later classical and vernacular Chinese. 是 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[是]].

Mandarin 是 (shì): the universal equative copula — 我是学生 (I am a student), 这是书 (this is a book); 是...的 marks completive or contrastive clauses: 我是在北京出生的 (it was in Beijing that I was born). Cantonese 係 (hai6) is the spoken copula: 我係學生 (I am a student); 是 (si6) appears in formal Cantonese and set compounds. Japanese lacks a true equative copula: だ (da, informal) and です (desu, polite) are copular auxiliaries historically derived from にてある (nite aru, "by means of being"), and share properties with verbs and particles. Korean 이다 (-ida): the Korean copula is a suffix attaching to the predicate noun — 나는 학생이다 (I am a student). Vietnamese là: the equative copula — Tôi là học sinh (I am a student) — does not inflect and pairs with không phải for negation.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 서 does not match Korean 시. 是 reads 서 (se/ㄙㄝ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 禪母 (d͡ʑ) + 支三開韻 (iᴇ) gives a devoiced palatal sibilant with the -e vowel. Dan'a'yo maps MC 支三開韻 to the mid -e vowel (ㄝ/서), while Sino-Korean 시 (si) maps the same MC final to the high -i vowel, a different vowel grade. The MC 支三開韻 produced ㄝ (-e/서) in Dan'a'yo and -i (시) in Korean — a vowel-height divergence from the same MC source.
