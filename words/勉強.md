## Notes
- [勉 (char)](../characters/勉%20(char).md) "to exert, to force" + [強 (char)](../characters/強%20(char).md) "strong, by force"

勉強 is one of the clearest examples in this vault of a single written compound that has drifted to almost unrelated meanings in different languages. In Mandarin and Cantonese, 勉強 retains its original, transparent sense — "to force [oneself or someone]," "reluctant, strained, inadequate" (combining 勉's "to exert effort" with 強's "by force, forcibly," the same 強 seen in [[強化]] and [[強固]], cross-linked from [[強 (char)]]'s Notes). Vietnamese miễn cưỡng preserves this same "reluctant, forced" sense.

In Japanese, however, 勉強 (べんきょう, benkyō) underwent a striking semantic narrowing/shift to mean simply "to study, studying" — the everyday word a Japanese schoolchild uses for homework, with the older "force oneself" sense surviving only as a secondary meaning ("to give a discount," literally "to strain oneself [to lower the price]"). The likely bridge between the senses is the idea of "forcing oneself to do something difficult" — applied generally in Chinese (forcing oneself to do *anything* unwilling) but narrowed in Japanese specifically to the effortful, sometimes unwilling work of academic study. This batch's 14th word thus serves as a cautionary tale for any zonal vocabulary project: even a fully transparent two-character compound can carry sharply divergent meanings depending on which language's usage dominates.
