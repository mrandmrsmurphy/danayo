## Notes
- [洞](../characters/洞.md) "cave, ravine, vale" + [穴](../characters/穴.md) "cave, hole" — stand-in for [[穴]], which cannot appear independently ([[洞]]'s own primary stand-in is the separate, closely related word [[洞窟]]).

Korean draws a real, specific distinction between this word and its close sibling 洞窟/동굴: 동혈 denotes a smaller opening or hollow in a cliff or rock face, while 동굴 names a large, general cave or cavern system — 동굴 is by far the more commonly used everyday word for "cave," with 동혈 reserved for the narrower sense. Japanese どうけつ is the Sino-Japanese compound reading; the everyday native word for "cave" is instead ほらあな (horaana, from 洞/ほら + 穴/あな). Vietnamese hang động (native "hang" + Sino-Vietnamese "động") is the real standard word, not a literal calque of 洞穴's own characters — critically, a literal rendering using 穴's own reading, huyệt, would actually mislead: in both Korean and Vietnamese, the reflex of 穴 (혈/huyệt) has specialized toward acupuncture/pressure-point terminology (穴位/huyệt đạo) rather than staying a general word for "cave, hole."
