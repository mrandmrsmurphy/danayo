## Notes
- [元](../characters/元.md) "element; origin; first" + [凶 (char)](../characters/凶%20(char).md) "unlucky; sinister; evil"

The chief culprit; the ringleader; the original and primary source of wrongdoing. 元 (source, origin, first) + 凶 (sinister evil) = "the first evil; the original wrongdoer" — the one at the root of the problem, from whom all subsequent harm flows.

元凶 operates on two distinct levels, both common in modern usage:

**1. The criminal mastermind** — the primary human perpetrator, as opposed to accomplices, accessories, or those who merely carried out orders. In a conspiracy or criminal organization, the 元凶 is the one who conceived and directed the crime: 犯罪の元凶 ("the ringleader of the crime"). This usage implies moral primacy in guilt: however many participated, the 元凶 bears the heaviest responsibility.

**2. The root cause** — the underlying structural or systemic factor from which a problem originates: 汚染の元凶 ("the root cause of the pollution"), 混乱の元凶 ("the source of the chaos"), 格差の元凶 ("the primary driver of inequality"). This abstract usage is extremely common in editorial writing, political argument, and analytical prose — stronger than "contributing factor" and carrying a prosecutorial weight: to identify something as 元凶 is to declare it *primarily responsible*, not merely implicated.

The prefix 元 inverts its usual register here with striking effect. In other compounds, 元 signals prestige and primacy in a positive sense: [[元号]] ("era name"), 元旦 ("New Year's Day"), 元帥 ("field marshal"). In 元凶, the same prefix of primacy is applied to evil — the first among wrongdoers, the origin of calamity. This etymological irony gives the word a particular rhetorical force: to be named 元凶 is not merely to be accused, but to be declared the *source* from which harm radiated outward.
