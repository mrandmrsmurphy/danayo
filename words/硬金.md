## Notes
### Form
硬金 is [硬 (char)](../characters/硬%20(char).md) “hard” + [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following the rule for transition elements.

### Etymological problem
The international name _molybdenum_ comes from Greek μόλυβδος (_molybdos_) “lead.” This reflects historical confusion: molybdenite (MoS₂), galena (PbS), and graphite were once grouped together as “lead-like” minerals. The root is therefore **misleading for modern semantics**, and both “lead” (鉛) and “heavy” (重) are already occupied in your system.

### Motivation for 硬
Choosing 硬 shifts from inherited etymology to **salient material property**:

- Molybdenum is notable for **high hardness and strength at elevated temperatures**.
- It is widely used in **steel alloys** to increase hardness and durability.
- The “hard metal” idea is a standard practical association in metallurgy.

Thus 硬金 is a **functional-descriptive name**, consistent with the fallback rule when etymology fails or collides.

### Type of formation

- Not a calque of the Greek root (which is unusable here).
- A **property-based designation**, similar in spirit to other descriptive names in your system.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Avoids duplication with 鉛 and 重.
2. Uses a high-frequency, transparent character.
3. Provides a clear, distinct one-character symbol (硬).
4. Maintains 金 for a d-block element.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 钼 (phonetic)
- Japanese: モリブデン (phonetic)
- Korean: 몰리브덴 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: molypđen / molypden (phonetic)

As usual, modern forms are phonetic; 硬金 restores a meaning-based system, albeit not tied to the historical etymon.

**Clarity and potential issues.**

- 硬 is semantically broad (“hard”), and many metals are hard; the term is **not uniquely identifying** without prior assignment.
- However, uniqueness is enforced by your system, not by intrinsic semantics, so this is acceptable.

**Conclusion.**  
Given that the original etymology is both misleading and already lexically occupied, 硬金 is a reasonable and internally consistent solution. It trades historical fidelity for functional descriptiveness, which aligns with your stated fallback strategy.
