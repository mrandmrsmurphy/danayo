## Notes
- [服](../characters/服.md) "wear, submit to" + [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, obey"

服従 means submission or obedience — following and complying with authority, orders, or rules. The character 服 carries both the sense of wearing (clothing oneself in) and the sense of submitting (accepting a role or position), making it an apt pairing with 従 (to follow in accordance with). The compound is used as a noun (服従を要求する, "to demand obedience") and as a suru verb.

In Chinese (服从, fúcóng), the simplified form is standard in formal and military contexts: 服从命令 ("to obey orders"), 服从组织 ("to submit to the organization"). In Japanese (ふくじゅう, fukujū), 服従 is the standard word for obedience in hierarchical relationships, appearing in military, organizational, and religious writing: 絶対服従 ("absolute obedience"). In Korean (복종), it is the common formal term for obedience and appears in both secular and religious contexts (하나님께 복종, "submission to God").
