## Notes
- [詩](../characters/詩.md) "poem" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person"

詩人 is the standard word for *poet* across all five CJKV languages — one of the oldest and most stable literary terms in the Sinosphere. The compound is ancient; 詩人 appears in the *Mao Shi* preface (毛詩序) to the *Classic of Poetry* (詩經), establishing the poet as someone through whom the spirit of the age speaks. In classical Chinese literary theory, the poet does not merely compose verses but serves as a sensitive register of social and moral conditions — 詩言志 ("poetry articulates the will/aspiration") is the foundational principle.

In Japanese (しじん, shijin), the word is used for poets of all traditions — Chinese, Japanese, and Western. In Korean (시인), it is the everyday word for poet in modern usage. In Vietnamese (thi nhân), the Hán-Việt form is literary; modern Vietnamese more commonly uses nhà thơ. The word's longevity across languages reflects the prestige of poetry as the highest literary form throughout the East Asian cultural tradition.
