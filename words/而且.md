## Notes
- [而 (char)](../characters/而%20(char).md) "and; linking particle" + [且 (char)](../characters/且%20(char).md) "also; furthermore; in addition"

而且 is the additive-reinforcing linker in Dan'a'yo — "moreover; furthermore; and also" — a stronger form of [[而]] alone. Where 而 simply coordinates two clauses ("A and B"), 而且 signals that B adds something beyond what A already established: "A, and what is more, B." The compound combines 而 as the linking particle with 且 as the additive intensifier ("also; in addition; besides"). In modern Mandarin 而且 (érqiě) is one of the most common written conjunctions, used equally in formal prose and casual speech. It frequently pairs with [[不但]] in the correlative construction 不但A，而且B ("not only A, but moreover B") — the standard Chinese way of expressing additive emphasis.

而 (ㄋㄧ/ni/니 — see [[而]] for full analysis): clausal linking particle, MC 日母+之韻. 且 (ㄑㄛ/co/초 — Mandarin qiě, Cantonese ce2, Korean 차, Japanese SHA/しゃ, Vietnamese thả): "also; furthermore; in addition; for now" — MC 清母 (t͡sʰ) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ); the character 且 originally depicted a stacked offering vessel and was borrowed as a conjunction/particle. In Classical Chinese 且 alone means "moreover; at the same time; for now," and 且夫 is a classical paragraph-opener ("furthermore; moreover, as for"). The Dan'a'yo compound follows the Mandarin usage directly. Cantonese uses 而且 (ji4 ce2) in formal written registers. Japanese has no common Sino-Japanese compound equivalent; the natural equivalents are なおかつ (naokatsu, and furthermore) and しかも (shikamo, moreover). Korean and Vietnamese use the Sino-readings (이차, nhi thả) only in literary contexts.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 니초 does not match Korean 이차. Both syllables diverge. 而 gives Dan'a'yo 니 (ni, MC 日母+之韻 → ㄋ preserved) vs Korean 이 (i, null onset — the same 日母 split as [[而]], [[若]], [[故而]]). 且 gives Dan'a'yo 초 (co, MC 清母+魚韻 → -o vowel) vs Korean 차 (cha, -a vowel) — the same 魚韻 vowel divergence as [[且]] alone.
