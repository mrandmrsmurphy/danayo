## Notes
- [嬌](../characters/嬌.md) "lovable, tender" + [眉 (char)](../characters/眉%20(char).md) "eyebrows" — stand-in for [[嬌]], which cannot appear independently.

眉 here stands in for its alias 媚 ("to charm; to flatter; coquettish"), not for its primary sense of "eyebrows." 嬌媚 (jiāomèi) describes a deliberately charming, coquettish manner — the kind of studied prettiness or flirtatiousness, often in expression or bearing, that is meant to delight or win over an observer. It carries a slightly knowing or theatrical connotation, distinct from plain beauty.

Because 嬌 and 眉 are the vault's canonical bearers of Dan'a'yo phonology, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 here are mechanically concatenated from their own fixed Dan'a'yo values (걋믜 / gyaumiǝ / ㄍ⼘ㄨㄇㄧㄜ). The mandarin, cantonese, and vietnamese fields reflect the real-world readings of 嬌媚, using 媚's own attested reading (mèi / mei6 / mị) rather than 眉's (méi / mei4 / mi). The Sino-Korean reading happens to coincide regardless — both 眉 and 媚 are read 미 — so 교미 requires no adjustment. This is the same divergence pattern seen in 波蘭, 六楽, and 魏峨.
