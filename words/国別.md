## Notes
- [国](../characters/国.md) "country, nation" + [別 (char)](../characters/別%20(char).md) "distinguish, separate" — sorted or organized with the country as the dividing line: country-by-country, by nation.

別 is the vocabulary's general suffix of categorization, turning a noun into "sorted by X" — compare [[種類]] ("kind, category"), which groups by type rather than by a named dividing axis the way 別-compounds do. 国別 is the most common of this family in practice, used wherever data, rankings, or regulations are broken down nation by nation (国別統計, "country-by-country statistics"); the same pattern productively yields 性別 ("by sex/gender") and 地域別 ("by region"), neither yet created but both transparent given 別's role here. Korean prefers to insert a linking syllable (국가별, "by 국가/[[国家]]," rather than a bare 국별) and so does not independently attest this exact two-character form, but Mandarin's 国别 is in active, everyday use — it is in fact the standard Mandarin word for "nationality" on official forms, a sense distinct from but historically continuous with the "by-country" sense used here. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, and Japanese.
