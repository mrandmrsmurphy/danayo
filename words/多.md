## Notes
- [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many"

多 is "many" — a stative quantifier in Dan'a'yo's correlative paradigm, generating 此多 (this many; this much), 其多 (that many; that much), 何多 (how many?; how much?). As a stative 多 means "much; many; abundant" and can modify a noun or serve as a predicate: 人多 (many people; there are many people). The correlative row 多 captures the quantificational dimension — how much or how many of something — extending the system beyond spatial and categorial distinctions to degree. 多 contrasts with 少 (few; little). 多 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[多]].

Mandarin 多 (duō): 多少 (duōshǎo, how many; how much), 多么 (duōme, how; what a), 很多 (hěn duō, a lot; many), 多大 (duōdà, how big; how old), 多種多樣 (many types; great variety). 多 is among the most frequent Mandarin characters in daily speech. Cantonese 多 (do1): 多謝 (do1 ze6, thank you — lit. "many thanks"), 好多 (hou2 do1, many; a lot of). Japanese 多 (た, ta / おお, ō): 多い (おおい, many; numerous), 多分 (たぶん, probably — lit. "much portion"), 多様性 (たようせい, diversity), 多数 (たすう, majority; many in number). Korean 다 (多): 다양 (多樣, diverse), 다수 (多數, majority), 다행 (多幸, fortunate — lit. "much good fortune"). Vietnamese đa (多): đa dạng (diverse), đa số (majority — lit. "many-numbered").

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 드 differs from Korean 다. 多 reads 드 (dǝ/ㄉㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 端母 (t) + 歌韻 (ɑ) gives Dan'a'yo the schwa vowel (-ǝ, ㄜ) — while Sino-Korean gives 다 (da), using the plain -a vowel. This MC 歌韻 schwa correspondence is consistent across all Dan'a'yo words containing 多: 繁多 (pandǝ vs 번다), 多様 (dǝ'yang), etc. Dan'a'yo preserves the schwa where Sino-Korean gives -a.
