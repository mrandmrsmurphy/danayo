## Notes
- [目 (char)](../characters/目%20(char).md) "eye" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

目's own pictographic origin (a human eye, later rotated 90°) already carries the word's full meaning, so no legitimizing second syllable is needed the way most bounded characters get one (compare [[森林]], [[頭髪]] earlier in this sweep) — like [[天]], [[小]], and [[道]] in the original HSK batch, 目 stands as a complete word on its own.

Mandarin mù, Cantonese muk6, and Korean 목 (mok) all use 目 productively and independently in compounds (題目, 目的, 目標), though as a bare standalone word for "eye" itself Mandarin actually prefers 眼睛 in everyday speech, reserving unbound 目 for literary/classical registers (目不轉睛, 一目瞭然) and technical vocabulary (科目, 項目, "subject/category," extending from "eye" to "a distinguishing feature/item"). Vietnamese mục is the real Sino-Vietnamese reading, but likewise almost never means "eye" in isolation in modern Vietnamese — it survives bound in compounds with the extended "item, category" sense (mục lục, "table of contents"; mục tiêu, "objective/target"), while the everyday word for the body part is the native mắt.
