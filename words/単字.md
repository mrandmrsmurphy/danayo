## Notes
- [単](../characters/単.md) "single" + [字 (char)](../characters/字%20(char).md) "character, written symbol" — a word made of just one character, as opposed to a [[合成]]詞 (a compound built from several).

The grammar book uses 単字 in exactly this contrastive sense when describing [[重畳]] (reduplication): if a 単字 is itself an independent word, the whole character simply repeats, but if a character only makes sense as part of a multi-character compound, reduplication targets the compound's main character instead. The word is common and transparent in Mandarin and Cantonese discussions of how Chinese characters and words relate (单字成词, "single characters forming words," is a standard descriptive phrase). One caution worth flagging: real Sino-Korean readings of 単/字 would combine to 단자, which is already firmly claimed by the unrelated everyday word 端子 ("[electrical] terminal") — for this reason Korean tends to avoid the bare compound here, expressing the idea periphrastically instead, and the korean field is left blank above rather than asserting a confusable reading. Attested across Mandarin and Cantonese.
