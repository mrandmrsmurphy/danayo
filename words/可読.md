## Notes
- [可 (char)](../characters/可%20(char).md) "can, able to be" + [読](../characters/読.md) "read" — capable of being read; legible or comprehensible.

可 is one of the most productive modal prefixes in the modern vocabulary, turning a verb into "[verb]-able": 可愛 ("can be loved" → "cute"), 可能 ("can be" → "possible"), and here 可読 ("can be read"). It almost always shows up paired with its abstract-noun derivative 可読性 ("readability"), the term programmers and editors use for how easily a text or piece of code can be followed — but the bare adjective 可読 is the older and more general form. 読 itself is the core reading-verb across the vocabulary, shared with [[注音]] ("Zhuyin," literally "annotate the sound, i.e. mark the reading") and [[漢語]]'s sister term 漢字 ("Chinese characters," not yet in the vocabulary). Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
