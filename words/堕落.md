## Notes
- [堕](../characters/堕.md) "to fall; to degenerate" + [落](../characters/落.md) "to fall; to descend; to drop"

堕落 is a double-falling compound: both characters mean "to fall," but they arrive at that meaning through different graphemic paths. 堕 (phonetic 随/隋, semantic 土) carries the sense of collapsing downward under gravity or moral weight — it is the character for miscarriage (堕胎), for ruin, and for the fall of dynasties. 落 (phonetic 洛, semantic 艸) is the fall of leaves, the settling of a thing to its natural resting place. Together they amplify into the comprehensive sense of moral collapse: a person or institution that has sunk from a previously higher state into corruption, dissipation, or depravity.

In Buddhist cosmology, 堕落 specifically describes falling into a lower realm of rebirth — a hungry ghost, an animal, or a hell being — as a consequence of unwholesome actions. This soteriological sense predates the more secular moral usage and gives the compound its strong negative charge even in non-Buddhist contexts. In modern Chinese, Japanese, and Korean, 堕落 (duòluò / daraku / 타락) is the standard literary and journalistic term for moral degeneration: politicians "堕落", social institutions "堕落", individuals who were once principled are said to have "堕落した." The traditional orthography 墮落 uses the original form of 堕; the simplified Dan'a'yo form follows modern Chinese and Japanese practice.

The 羅馬字 and 注音 corrections here: 堕's Dan'a'yo readings are dwa / ㄉ⺢ (not da / ㄉㄚ), giving dwalag and ㄉ⺢ㄌㄚㄎ for the compound. The kwin=false marking reflects the divergence between Dan'a'yo 돠락 and Korean 타락 (tarak), where 堕 maps to 타 in Korean Sino-Korean phonology but to 돠 in Dan'a'yo.
