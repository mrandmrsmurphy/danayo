## Notes
### Form
射素 is [射](characters/射.md) “to emit; to shoot out; to radiate” + [素](characters/素.md) “element.” The suffix matches the rule for main-group elements.

### Motivation
The name _radon_ continues the Latin _radius_ “ray,” via _radium_ (“the ray-emitting substance”). Radon was originally termed “radium emanation,” emphasizing its emission from radium and its radioactive decay. 射 directly captures the core idea: the emission of rays.

### Type of formation
This is a **semantic calque of the underlying root** rather than of the specific form “radon.” Mapping _radius → emission of rays_ mirrors the later naming stage.

### Fit within Dan'a'yo
1. Meaning-based, avoiding phonetic borrowing.
2. Consistent with other radiation-derived names (e.g., 射 vs 放射 concepts).
3. Uses 素 appropriately for a noble gas (group 18).

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 氡 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ラドン (phonetic)
- Korean: 라돈 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: radon (phonetic)

As with many late elements, modern usage is phonetic; 射素 restores semantic transparency.

### Symbol choice
- 射 serves cleanly as the single-character symbol.
- It is common but not likely to collide with other element names if reserved.

### Conclusion
射素 is a compact and accurate semantic reconstruction of the “ray/emission” root behind _radon_. It fits the constraints and maintains internal coherence with other meaning-based names.
