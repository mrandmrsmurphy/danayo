## Notes
- [漢](../characters/漢.md) "Han, Chinese" + [方](../characters/方.md) "method, prescription" — the traditional medical systems of the Sinosphere, considered as a body of practice distinct from Western (洋方/西洋) medicine.

**漢方** (*kanpō*) is the standard Japanese term for the herbal-medicine tradition inherited from Chinese medicine and adapted over centuries within Japan — encompassing diagnostic methods, herbal formulae (漢方薬), and practices such as acupuncture (鍼) and moxibustion (灸). Though rooted in Chinese medical classics, 漢方 developed its own schools and pharmacopoeia distinct from contemporary Traditional Chinese Medicine (中医/中醫), and is integrated into Japan's modern healthcare system — many 漢方薬 formulae are covered by national health insurance and prescribed alongside conventional pharmaceuticals.

In Korean, **한방** (*hanbang*, 漢方) refers analogously to traditional Korean medicine (한의학, 韓醫學), administered by licensed practitioners (한의사) within a healthcare system that, as in Japan, runs in parallel with Western biomedicine. The Mandarin term 汉方 (*hànfāng*) is comparatively rare in mainland usage, where 中医/中藥 are the standard terms — 汉方 mostly appears as a loan referring specifically to the Japanese kampo tradition, or in product names.

This word appears in the [[七情]] (Seven Emotions) entry as the label for the third of three competing seven-fold emotional schemes — alongside Confucian and Buddhist enumerations — found in the [[医学]] tradition's accounts of human affect.
