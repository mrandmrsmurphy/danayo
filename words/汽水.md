## Notes
- [汽](../characters/汽.md) "vapor, steam" + [水 (char)](../characters/水%20(char).md) "water" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (汽's own points to `蒸汽`, 水's own to bare `水`); 汽水 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/汽.md` is `kwin: false`, `characters/水 (char).md` is `kwin: true` → compound `false`.
- **A deliberate Dan'a'yo sense choice, not a mistake**: this compound genuinely diverges across the sphere. Japanese 汽水 (kisui) is the real hydrology technical term for "brackish water" (a mix of fresh and salt water, as in an estuary — 汽水域 "brackish-water zone"), the sense adopted here. Mandarin 汽水/汽水 qìshuǐ, by contrast, has grammaticalized into the everyday colloquial word for "soda pop, soft drink" (the vapor/steam bubbles resembling carbonation) — a completely different, unrelated sense from "brackish water." To avoid conflating the two, this vault deliberately routes the "soft drink" sense to [[清涼飲料]] instead, keeping 汽水 dedicated to the technical hydrology sense.
