## Notes
- [独](../characters/独.md) "alone; solo" + [立 (char)](../characters/立%20(char).md) "stand"

**獨立** (*dúlì*, "standing alone") describes the state of being self-governing, self-sufficient, or free from external control. In its political sense — the dominant one across East Asian discourse — it refers to national independence: the condition of a state that is not subject to another power. In this sense it is the root of [[独立国]] (independent state) and appears in 独立宣言 (declaration of independence) and similar compounds.

Beyond politics, the word carries the broader sense of autonomy or self-reliance in any domain — a person, institution, or system acting on its own without dependence on others. The literal image, 独 "alone" + 立 "standing", captures the meaning directly: to stand by oneself.
