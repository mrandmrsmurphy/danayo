## Notes
- [柏 (char)](../characters/柏%20(char).md) "cypress; cedar; evergreen conifer"

柏 is the word for cypress and cedar — evergreen conifers prized across the Sinosphere for their wood, longevity, and ceremonial associations. The character has two standard Mandarin readings: bǎi (the common tree-name reading, as in 柏樹 = cypress tree) and bó (in compounds and proper nouns, most famously 柏林 = Berlin). In Dan'a'yo, the reading follows ㄅ⼘ㄎ (*byag*), aligned with the bǎi/tree-name pronunciation.

The CJKV associations of 柏 diverge across languages. In Chinese, 柏 (bǎi) = cypress (Cupressaceae family), strongly associated with graveyards, longevity, and durability — 松柏 (pines and cypresses) is a classical pair denoting evergreen endurance. In Japanese, 柏 (かしわ, kashiwa) has been applied to a different tree entirely: the Japanese oak (Quercus dentata), prized for its large leaves used in 柏餅 (kashiwa mochi, oak-leaf rice cakes eaten on Children's Day). The cypress sense in Japanese is usually expressed by 檜 (ひのき, hinoki) or 杉 (すぎ, sugi, cedar). Korean 백 (baek) follows the Chinese cypress sense. Vietnamese bách is the Sino-Vietnamese reading for cypress/cedar.

柏 appears in the Mandarin name for Berlin — 柏林 (Bólín), read with the bó pronunciation — a phonetic approximation of the German toponym that became standard across Chinese and Korean (베를린). This gives 柏 a double life: as a tree name (bǎi/cypress) in botanical and literary contexts and as a phonetic syllable (bó/Berlin) in geographic transliteration. The two readings have no etymological connection beyond sharing the character; the Dan'a'yo reading *byag* follows the tree-name reading.
