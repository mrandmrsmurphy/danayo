## Notes
- [連 (char)](../characters/連%20(char).md) "connected; mutual" + [合 (char)](../characters/合%20(char).md) "join; combine" + [王 (char)](../characters/王%20(char).md) "king" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation" — built from two already-real words, [[連合]] "union, alliance" + [[王国]] "kingdom": literally "the united kingdom."

連合王国 is the fully formal name for the state, used in official and legal contexts where [[英国]] — the everyday term, and the one nearly everyone reaches for regardless of register — would be imprecise or presumptuous; see [[英国]]'s own notes for when Korean speakers specifically prefer 연합왕국 over 영국. Japanese 連合王国 sees real use alongside 英国 in formal/diplomatic writing, distinct from the unrelated 連合国 ("the Allied Powers/United Nations," built on the same 連合 but a different second element). Vietnamese has no equivalent formal/informal split at all: everyday and official usage alike just say Anh or Vương quốc Anh for the UK, the same term used for [[英国]] — the compositional Liên hiệp Vương quốc given here is not independently attested, only a mechanical rendering of 連合+王国's own readings.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 련곱왕곡 diverges from Korean 연합왕국 in three of four syllables — 連 loses its initial ㄹ in modern Korean (연, the same 두음법칙 shift as in [[連合]] alone), 合 surfaces as 합 (h-) against Dan'a'yo's ㄍ- (g-), and 国 takes vowel ㅜ (국) where Dan'a'yo keeps ㅗ (곡); only 王 (왕) matches exactly.
