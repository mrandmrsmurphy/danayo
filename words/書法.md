## Notes

Coordinate terms:
- 書芸 - "the art of calligraphy".  Technically, the ancient art from the time of Zhou.  However, the term has come to mean Korean calligraphy today.  (Koreans maintain this is because their style _is_ the ancient style!)
- [[書契]] - the invention of writing; system of literacy
- [[書道]] - Japanese calligraphy

## Ancient
The ancient art was composed of six writing styles:
- **象形 (_Xiàngxíng_):** Pictographs (e.g., 月 for moon).
- **指事 (_Zhǐshì_):** Ideograms (e.g., 上 for up).
- **會意 (_Huìyì_):** Compound ideograms (e.g., 明—sun+moon—for "bright").
- **形聲 (_Xíngshēng_):** Phono-semantic compounds (radical + phonetic).
- **轉注 (_Zhuǎnzhù_):** Derivative cognates (characters with shared origins/meanings).
- **假借 (_Jiǎjiè_):** Phonetic loan characters.
