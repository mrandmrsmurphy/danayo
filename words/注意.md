## Notes
- [注](../characters/注.md) "pour" + [意](../characters/意.md) "meaning"

注意 is "to pay attention to, be careful of" — literally "to pour meaning/focus" onto something, the standard everyday verb for attention or caution across the Sinosphere (注意事項 "points to note," 注意! as a warning sign). It is a genuine Dan'a'yo-internal homophone of [[主意]] "main decision, big idea": [[注]] and [[主]] share the exact same Dan'a'yo syllable ㄐㄨ (注 is a direct phonetic derivative of 主, listed on 主's own page under Derived Characters), and both compounds end in 意's own syllable ㄜ — same structure, same sound, different meaning, a matched pair worth reading alongside [[主意]].

Mandarin 注意 (zhùyì). Cantonese 注意 (zyu3 ji3). Japanese 注意 (ちゅうい, chūi) is an everyday, high-frequency word — ご注意ください ("please be careful") is a fixture of Japanese public signage. Korean 주의 has a real homophone collision with [[主意]]: both "attention, caution" (注意) and "one's own idea/opinion" (主意) read identically as 주의, since 注/主 share the same Sino-Korean reading 주 and 意 is always 의 — 注意 is by far the more common of the two in practice. Vietnamese chú ý ("pay attention," chú ý đến "pay attention to") is the everyday equivalent, closely paralleling the Chinese/Japanese sense.
