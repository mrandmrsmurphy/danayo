## Notes
- [石 (char)](../characters/石%20(char).md) "stone; rock" + [油 (char)](../characters/油%20(char).md) "oil" — an exact semantic parallel to Latin *petroleum* (petra + oleum, "stone oil")

**石油** (*shíyóu*) is petroleum — the fossil hydrocarbon found in geological formations, the basis of crude oil and the industries derived from it. The name compounds 石 "stone" with 油 "oil" to identify the material by its origin: oil from the earth's rock. The formation is identical in logic to the Latin/Greek *petroleum* that gave the English term, arrived at independently across Eurasia.

The word appears with striking early precision in the **夢溪筆談** (*Měngxī Bǐtán*, "Dream Pool Essays", c. 1088 AD) of the Northern Song polymath **沈括** (*Shěn Kuò*, 1031–1095 AD). Shen Kuo records encountering a dark oily liquid seeping from the earth in the Fú-Yán region of Shaanxi: 鄜延境内有石油，舊說「高奴縣出脂水」，即此也 ("Within the territory of Fú-Yán there is stone-oil; old records describe 'the county of Gāonú producing grease-water' — this is that substance"). He burned it, found it produced an unusually dense black smoke, and collected the soot as ink — recording with characteristic curiosity both the material and its uses. His closing line is famous: **此物後必大行於世** ("this material will certainly become greatly used in the world") — written eight centuries before the petroleum age.

The industrial exploitation of petroleum began in earnest with Edwin Drake's well at Titusville, Pennsylvania, in 1859 AD, which initiated the modern oil industry. East Asian fields — Shaanxi and Xinjiang in China, Niigata in Japan, the Dutch East Indies — were developed from the late 19th century onward under colonial and national industrial programmes. By the 20th century 石油 had become the material foundation of industrial civilisation: transport fuel, heating, plastics, pharmaceuticals, synthetic fibres, and fertilisers all derive from it. The geopolitics of petroleum have shaped every major conflict and alliance since.

Japanese 石油 (*sekiyu*) is the standard term across all registers. Korean 석유 (*seogyou*) is the everyday word, appearing in compounds like 석유화학 (petrochemistry) and 석유파동 (oil shock). Vietnamese uses Sino-Vietnamese *thạch du* in formal writing; vernacular usage prefers *dầu mỏ* (oil-well/mine) for petroleum, with *dầu* (from 油, *du*) appearing throughout compounds such as *dầu ăn* (cooking oil) and *xăng dầu* (petrol/fuel).
