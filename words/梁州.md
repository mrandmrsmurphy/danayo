## Notes
- [梁 (char)](../characters/梁%20(char).md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Liang Province, the eighth of the [[九州]]

梁州 was the southwestern province, covering the rugged terrain of the Sichuan basin and surrounding mountains — roughly modern Sichuan and parts of Shaanxi south of the Qinling range. Its isolation, defined by the great mountain barriers of the Qinling and the gorges of the upper Yangtze, gave it a distinctive character in Chinese geography: fertile within, nearly impenetrable from without. This geography made the Sichuan basin a natural refuge for states and dynasties under pressure, and the kingdom of Shu (蜀) — Liu Bei's state in the Three Kingdoms period — found its base here.
