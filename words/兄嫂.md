## Notes
- [兄 (char)](../characters/兄%20(char).md) "older brother" + [嫂](../characters/嫂.md) "sister-in-law" — this word is the stand-in for [嫂](../characters/嫂.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

兄嫂 names one's older brother's wife — a specific, named position in the traditional East Asian kinship system, distinct from a younger brother's wife (弟嫁) or a sister's husband. The compound is mildly redundant by design: 嫂 alone already means exactly this relationship, and 兄 is prefixed to make the reference fully explicit and to parallel the naming pattern used for other in-laws.

**Japanese carries two distinct readings for this exact compound**: あによめ (aninoyome), a native compound built from 兄 (ani, "older brother") + 嫁 (yome, "bride, wife") — the ordinary, everyday reading — and けいそう (keisō), the learned Sino-Japanese reading built from the same on'yomi as 兄's [[況]] and other 兄-derived compounds (KEI) plus 嫂's own SOU. あによめ is overwhelmingly the common form; けいそう appears mainly in more formal or classical registers. Korean 형수 (hyeongsu) and Mandarin/Cantonese xiōngsǎo/hing1 sou2 concatenate transparently, with no such register split. Vietnamese huynh tẩu is attested but comparatively rare in everyday speech, where reduplicated tẩu tẩu is more commonly used to address a sister-in-law directly.
