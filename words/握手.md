## Notes
- [握](../characters/握.md) "grasp; hold" + [手 (char)](../characters/手%20(char).md) "hand" — literally "grasp hand." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (握's own points to `把握`, 手's own to bare `手`); 握手 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/握.md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/手 (char).md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- Mandarin wòshǒu, Cantonese ak1 sau2, Japanese あくしゅ, and Korean 악수 all directly preserve the Sino compound. Vietnamese, however, does not use it — the real, standard modern term for "to shake hands" is the native construction bắt tay (literally "catch/grasp hand"); no independently attested Sino-Vietnamese reading of 握手 is in actual use, so `vietnamese` is left blank rather than fabricated.
