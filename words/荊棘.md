## Notes
- [荊](../characters/荊.md) "thorn, bramble" + [棘](../characters/棘.md) "jujube, thorn bush" — stand-in for [[棘]], which cannot appear independently

荊棘 is a near-synonymous compound naming thorny shrubs and brambles in general: 荊 (jīng) refers to the chaste tree (Vitex negundo) and similar thorny shrubs associated with wildness and poverty, while 棘 (jí) refers to the jujube bush (Ziziphus jujuba) and other thorny woody plants. Together the compound evokes a tangle of thorns — undergrowth that blocks passage, tears clothing, and resists cultivation.

In classical Chinese literature 荊棘 carried strong connotations of desolation, neglect, and the aftermath of war or ruin. A land overgrown with 荊棘 was a land depopulated and abandoned: the image appears in poems of exile, lament for fallen dynasties, and descriptions of devastated cities. 荊棘途中 ("amid the thorns and brambles") became a metaphor for hardship and a difficult path through life. In Japanese けいきょく retains this literary sense, appearing in formal prose and classical references, while everyday thorns (いばら) uses the native reading of 荊. Korean 형극 similarly appears in literary and historical contexts.
