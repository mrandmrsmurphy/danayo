## Notes
- [痛](../characters/痛.md) "pain, torment" + [哭 (char)](../characters/哭%20(char).md) "cry, weep" — to wail, to cry bitterly.

慟 (tòng), "to wail with grief, to be overcome with sorrow," appears 4 times in the 《論語》, most poignantly in **先進**'s account of Confucius mourning his disciple 顏淵: "子哭之慟...有慟乎" ("the Master wailed for him in utter grief... 'Was I so overcome?'"). 慟 lies outside Dan'a'yo's closed character set, so the vault substitutes the attested compound 痛哭 (tòngkū) — "to wail bitterly, to cry one's heart out" — a "Translation" substitution per [[translation/論語 (book)]]'s Vocabulary list, joining 痛 ("pain, anguish") with 哭 ("to cry, to weep").

痛哭 is a widely attested word across all five languages: Sino-Japanese つうこく, Sino-Korean 통곡, and Sino-Vietnamese thống khốc all carry the same "to wail bitterly" sense.
