## Notes
- [共 (char)](../characters/共%20(char).md) "together; jointly" + [和](../characters/和.md) "harmony; peace" + [国](../characters/国.md) "nation" — [[共和]] is itself a word

**共和国** ("jointly-harmonious state") is the standard East Asian compound for a republic — a state whose government derives its authority from the people rather than from a hereditary monarch. The [[共和]] component has a specifically Chinese historical origin: it refers to the *Gonghe Regency* (共和行政, 841–828 BC), a famous interregnum in the Western Zhou dynasty during which ministers governed collectively after the king was deposed. When nineteenth-century translators needed a Chinese equivalent for the Western concept of *res publica*, 共和 was chosen for the parallel — rule held in common, without a single sovereign.

共和国 therefore carries this double weight: a modern political concept grafted onto one of the oldest recorded episodes of collective governance in Chinese history. It contrasts with [[民国]] ("people's state"), which emphasises the popular basis of authority, and with [[総統]] as the executive title within such a state.
