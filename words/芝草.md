## Notes
- [芝](../characters/芝.md) "sesame, grass" + [草 (char)](../characters/草%20(char).md) "grass"

芝草 combines two words for grass or ground-cover vegetation, with 芝 carrying the additional sense of the lingzhi mushroom (靈芝) and auspicious plants more broadly. In Chinese, 芝草 can function as a synonym for 靈芝 (língzhī) — the reishi fungus prized in traditional medicine — while also denoting grass and lawn plants in a more literal sense. The Korean reading 지초 is identical to the native Korean word for the plant *Lithospermum erythrorhizon* (지초, gromwell), whose root yields a red dye, though the characters are distinct.

In Japanese, the on'yomi reading しそう (shisō) is the literary compound form; the kun'yomi しばくさ (shibakusa) describes lawn grass or turf, and is the basis for the place name [[東芝]] (Toshiba, from the Shibaura district of Tokyo).
