## Notes
- [実](../characters/実.md) "substance; fruit; reality; true" + [用](../characters/用.md) "use; function; utility"

実用 pairs 実 (substance, fruit, what actually exists rather than what is merely named or claimed) with 用 (use, function, practical application) to describe the quality of being genuinely useful — of having a function in the real world rather than merely in theory or appearance. The compound frames practicality as a matter of grounded substance (実) meeting actual application (用): not the beautiful but non-functional, not the theoretically sound but inapplicable, but what actually works in practice.

The compound is highly productive as a prefix. 実用主義 (pragmatism, the philosophical school) uses it to describe the view that truth is what proves useful; 実用化 means "to bring into practical use" or "to commercialise" a technology or innovation; 実用的 is the adjectival form ("practical, utilitarian"); 実用新案 (utility model) is a category of intellectual property protection in Japan and Korea for practical innovations that don't rise to full invention status. In Chinese, 实用 (shíyòng) functions identically and is equally productive.

The kwin=false correction here: Dan'a'yo 싣용 (sid + 'yong, concatenated from the sinographic readings) differs from Korean 실용 (siryong) in the first character — 実 maps to 싣 (Dan'a'yo, with stop final) versus 실 (Korean, with lateral final), reflecting the different phonological histories of Middle Chinese *-t endings in the two systems. The previous kwin: true was incorrect.
