## Notes
- [九 (char)](../characters/九%20(char).md) "nine" + [経 (char)](../characters/経%20(char).md) "classic; channel; warp"

A collective term for nine foundational texts of the Confucian literary canon. The specific works included in the Nine Classics varied by era and tradition; one widely accepted grouping comprises:

1. 易経 (Book of Changes / I Ching)
2. 書経 (Book of Documents / Classic of History)
3. 詩経 (Book of Songs / Classic of Poetry)
4. 周礼 (Rites of Zhou)
5. 儀礼 (Book of Etiquette and Ceremonies)
6. 礼記 (Book of Rites)
7. 春秋 (Spring and Autumn Annals)
8. 孝経 (Classic of Filial Piety)
9. 論語 (Analects of Confucius)

The term was codified during the **Tang dynasty** when the *Kaicheng Stone Classics* (開成石経) were carved — a monumental project in which the authoritative text of each of the nine classics was engraved on stone tablets to settle textual disputes and preserve the canon. The concept of nine classics existed earlier, but the Tang codification gave it lasting institutional weight. The Nine Classics formed the core curriculum of the imperial examination system (科挙) and thus shaped the education of the entire governing class of China for over a millennium.
