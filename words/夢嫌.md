## Notes
- [夢 (char)](../characters/夢%20(char).md) "dream" + [嫌](../characters/嫌.md) as 代用字 for 魘 "nightmare incubus"
- 嫌 carries 魘 (traditional) and 魇 (simplified) as aliases; 夢 carries 梦 as an alias — together they authorize 夢嫌 as the Dan'a'yo form of both 夢魘 and 梦魇.

The four aliases represent two distinct structural approaches to "nightmare" in the Sinosphere. **夢魘 / 梦魇** place 夢 first and treat 魘/魇 as the operative character: 魘 (Dan'a'yo: 嫌) depicts the nightmare as an entity — a supernatural pressing-down on the sleeper, an incubus or malevolent spirit that sits on the chest and causes the suffocation and terror of the bad dream. The character 魘 is formed with the ghost radical 鬼, making the demonic nature explicit. **噩夢 / 惡夢** invert the order — 夢 is in second position — and take the simpler route of characterising the dream itself as frightening (噩, "terrifying") or evil (惡). These two structural families are synonymous in meaning but differ in where they locate the horror: in the spirit pressing down, or in the quality of the dream itself.

Japanese and Korean converge on the "evil dream" structure: Japanese 悪夢 (*akumu*) and Korean 악몽 (惡夢, *angmong*) both follow the 惡+夢 pattern. Vietnamese *ác mộng* (惡夢) does the same. The `mandarin` and `cantonese` readings given here reflect the 夢魘 form, which is the Dan'a'yo structure.
