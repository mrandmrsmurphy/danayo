## Notes
- [俄](../characters/俄.md) "suddenly" + [雨 (char)](../characters/雨%20(char).md) "rain"

俄雨 compounds 俄, meaning "suddenly" or "in a brief moment" (as in 俄顷, "in an instant"), with 雨, "rain." The result is a rain that arrives without warning and passes quickly — what English calls a shower or passing rain. The semantic logic is parallel to the Japanese reading: 俄 carries the kun'yomi にわか, meaning "sudden" or "impromptu," so にわか雨 (にわかあめ) is the direct Japanese equivalent using the same character with the same meaning.

Synonyms include [[驟雨]] and [[小雨]], though these carry different nuances: 驟雨 (sudden heavy rain, squall) emphasises intensity; 小雨 (light rain, drizzle) emphasises lightness. 俄雨 captures the temporal character — it is the rain that appears and disappears — rather than its weight or volume.
