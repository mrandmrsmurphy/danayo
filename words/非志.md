## Notes
- [非 (char)](../characters/非%20(char).md) "not" + [志](../characters/志.md) "will, intention" — Dan'a'yo neologism for Fiji; the characters are chosen purely for phonetic fit: 羅馬字 *fiji*

非志 is a Dan'a'yo coinage exploiting the phonological system: 非 yields romanisation *fi* and 志 yields *ji*, producing *fiji* exactly. The standard Chinese name 斐濟 (Fěijì) — included as an alias — uses different characters and a different phonological path; in Dan'a'yo, 斐 and 濟 do not give the same clean result. 非志 is accordingly a neologism: a form that does not appear in any Chinese-language tradition but is coined within Dan'a'yo's own system.

The name *Fiji* derives from the Fijian word *Viti* — the indigenous name for the main island — first recorded by European navigators in the eighteenth century as "Feegee" or "Fiji", an approximate rendering of how neighboring Tongans pronounced *Viti*. Fiji is an archipelago of some 330 islands in the southwestern Pacific, of which about 110 are inhabited. It became independent in 1970 and is a member of the Pacific Islands Forum.

Fiji sits outside the East Asian cultural sphere that [[南亜]] and the Sinospheric canon address, so no classical Chinese name exists. 非志 gives it a home in Dan'a'yo through form rather than history.
