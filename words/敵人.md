## Notes
- [敵](../characters/敵.md) "enemy, foe" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person" — the concrete, personal adversary: a hostile individual, as opposed to hostility or conflict in the abstract. This word is the viable form of [敵](../characters/敵.md): 敵 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 敵人 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 敵's sense the concrete adversary, in contrast to [[仇恨]] (the abstract grudge 仇 stands in for) — splitting apart what the collapsed compound [[仇敵]] had blurred into one generic "enemy."

Mandarin dírén and Cantonese dik6jan4 are both standard, everyday words for "the enemy" as a person or group — 敵人的陣地 (the enemy's position), 消滅敵人 (destroy the enemy). Japanese and Korean both tend to use the bare character instead of this compound: Japanese 敵 (teki) alone covers "the enemy" without needing 人 appended (敵国 "enemy nation," 敵対者 "adversary" cover the compound senses instead), and Korean similarly favors 적 (敵) alone, or 원수 (怨讐) for a specifically personal foe. So — the mirror image of [[仇恨]] — 敵人 as a fixed two-character unit is really a Mandarin/Cantonese habit; Japanese and Korean get the same meaning without needing to add "person" to the character at all.
