## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "receive" + [動 (char)](../characters/動%20(char).md) "move, act" — receiving an action rather than performing it: the passive voice.

Mandarin instead uses 被动 (bèidòng, "by-moved") for the same grammatical category, but Korean's 수동 — and 수동태 ("passive voice") specifically — is the standard linguistic term, matching Japanese 受動 character-for-character even though the two languages independently diverge from Mandarin's choice of first root (受 "receive" vs. 被 "by, undergo"). The grammar book uses 受動 for the verbal prefix 被–, which it notes may only attach to 評詞 (transitive, "eventive" verbs) — a book-internal grammatical category not yet formalized as its own vocabulary entry. Attested across Japanese and Korean.
