## Notes
- [喊](../characters/喊.md) "war cry, shout" + [声](../characters/声.md) "sound, voice"
- This word is the stand-in for [[喊]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- The sound of shouting — a raised voice in anger, encouragement, or battle. In Chinese, 喊声 (hǎnshēng) covers any loud cry, from a vendor's call to a battle shout; the more literary 呐喊 (nàhǎn) emphasizes the rallying or protest dimension. In Korean, 함성 (喊聲) is the standard word for "battle cry" or "roar of a crowd," common in sports broadcasts (관중의 함성 "the roar of the crowd") and historical narratives of war. Japanese かんせい (kansei) is largely confined to classical or military contexts.
