## Notes
- [灰 (char)](../characters/灰%20(char).md) "ash" + [色](../characters/色.md) "color"

灰 = ash; 灰色 is literally ash-colored. In Chinese and Korean usage, grey occupies a culturally modest register — undistinguished, neutral, belonging neither to the vibrant nor the severe. A related term is [[鼠色]] (ㄙ⼄ㄙㄧㄎ, "rat color"), which points to a similar grey but emphasizes a warmer, brownish-grey tone associated with fur rather than ash. In Japanese aesthetics, grey and near-grey tones (利休鼠, 納戸色) carry positive associations with restraint and wabi-sabi sensibility.
