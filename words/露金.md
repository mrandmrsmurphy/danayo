## Notes
### Form
露金 is [露 (char)](characters/露%20(char).md) + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal.” The suffix follows the rule for transition metals (d-block).

### Motivation
The international name _ruthenium_ derives from _Ruthenia_, a Latinized name for regions of Eastern Europe (associated with Rus’, broadly the East Slavic lands). In CJKV, Russia is conventionally rendered 露西亞 (historically also 露國, 露羅斯, etc.). Selecting 露 is therefore a **toponymic abbreviation** from an established transcription.

### Type of formation

- source: 露西亞 (Russia) / Ruthenia-equivalent sphere
- reduction: 露 (first graph retained)
- suffixation: 金 to mark metallic class

This parallels other place-derived elements.

### Fit within the system
1. Avoids phonetic multi-character borrowing while preserving the international naming origin.
2. Maintains one-character uniqueness for symbol use.
3. Uses 金 appropriately for a transition metal.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 钌 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ルテニウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 루테늄 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: rutheni (phonetic; Russia = Nga, but Sino-Vietnamese 露 exists in older strata)

As with many elements, modern forms are phonetic; 露金 restores a graph-based connection to the place-name origin.

### Clarity and ambiguity
- 露 commonly means “dew; to expose,” but in compounds like 露西亞 it conventionally stands for “Russia.”
- Within a chemical register, reserving 露 as an element symbol is sufficient to avoid confusion.

### Conclusion
露金 is a consistent toponymic abbreviation aligned with Dan'a'yo: it encodes the Ruthenia/Russia origin of _ruthenium_ while preserving structural regularity and single-character symbolism.
