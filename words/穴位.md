## Notes
- [穴](../characters/穴.md) "cave, hole" + [位](../characters/位.md) "position, location"

穴位 is the term from traditional East Asian medicine for an acupuncture point — one of the specific locations on the body where needles, pressure, or moxibustion are applied along the meridian system. 穴, a 象形 pictograph of a cave entrance or door-flap, denotes any small opening or cavity; combined with 位 ("location"), 穴位 literally means "cavity-location" — a small, specific point on the body's surface, conceived of as an opening into the deeper circulatory pathways (経絡/经络) beneath.

穴 already appears in [[墓穴]] ("grave, burial pit," cross-linked from [[穴]]'s Words section) — a reminder that the character's core sense is any small enclosed cavity, whether in the earth or the body. In Japanese, 経穴 (keiketsu) is the more formal term for an acupoint, with ツボ (tsubo) the everyday word; 穴位/けつい is closer to the Chinese-medicine register, used more in translation from Chinese sources than as native medical vocabulary.
