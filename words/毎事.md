## Notes
- [毎 (char)](../characters/毎%20(char).md) "every; each; distributive" + [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "act; matter; affair"

毎事 is the distributive correlative in the action column: it refers to each and every instance of an action or matter, distributing universally across occurrences. The character 毎 (Dan'a'yo *moy*, ㄇㄛㄧ — 每 in simplified Chinese) is the East Asian distributive morpheme with enormous productivity as a prefix in temporal and event nouns: 毎日 (every day), 毎週 (every week), 毎月 (every month), 毎年 (every year), 毎回 (every time), 毎度 (each occasion). 毎事 extends this pattern to the action/matter slot in the correlative table.

毎事 functions as a 副用名詞 (adverbial noun) rather than a plain 代詞 — it operates as a frame-setting expression that can appear without a case particle to set the distribution for the entire predicate, exactly as 毎日 and 毎年 do: 毎事、最善を尽くす ("in every matter, one does one's best"). In classical Chinese, 每事 (měi shì) similarly appears as an adverbial quantifier: 每事必謀 ("for every matter one must deliberate"). Japanese 毎事 (まいごと) is not current vocabulary, but the morphological pattern is transparent to any reader familiar with 毎-compounds.

The distributive column (毎) contrasts with the universal column (皆): 毎X distributes the predicate across each individual instance of X one by one, while 皆X refers collectively to all instances. 毎事 = "for each matter (one at a time)"; 皆事 = "all matters (collectively)." This distinction — distributive vs. collective universal — runs throughout the entire correlative table (毎人/皆人, 毎処/皆処, 毎時/皆時) and corresponds to the classical Chinese contrast between 每 (per-instance) and 皆 (collective totality), which Dan'a'yo imports intact as a systematic grammatical distinction.
