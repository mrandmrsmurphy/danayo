## Notes
- [充](../characters/充.md) "to fill" + [電](../characters/電.md) "electricity"

充電 is the modern, universal word for "to recharge" — filling (充) a battery or device with electricity (電). 電, a 形声 character (semantic 雨 "rain" + phonetic 申, the same 申 that depicts a lightning bolt) originally meant "lightning," and remains the basis of an enormous family of modern technological vocabulary catalogued in [[電]]'s own Words section: [[電話]] ("telephone"), [[電車]] ("tram, electric train"), [[電気]] ("electricity"), [[電視]] ("television"), and more — nearly all coined in the late 19th and early 20th centuries to translate Western technical terms.

充電 is a particularly clear case of a "new compound, old characters" word: neither 充 nor 電 individually meant anything related to batteries before the 20th century, yet the compound reads transparently across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese as "fill with electricity" — exactly the kind of compositional, calque-friendly vocabulary that made kanji/hanja/hán tự compounds so adaptable to modern technology, and a big part of why Dan'a'yo's project of a shared written vocabulary across these languages remains plausible even for thoroughly modern concepts.
