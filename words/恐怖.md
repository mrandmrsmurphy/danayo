## Notes
- [恐](../characters/恐.md) "fear, be afraid of" + [怖](../characters/怖.md) "dreadful" — stand-in for [[怖]], which cannot appear independently

Mandarin kǒngbù, Cantonese hung2 bou3, Japanese きょうふ (kyōfu), and Korean 공포 (gongpo) all cover the general concept of "fear, dread, horror" broadly (恐怖映画/공포영화, "horror movie"; 恐怖症, "-phobia"). **Vietnamese has narrowed the same compound to a specific, much heavier sense**: khủng bố (matching the compositional reading of 恐+怖 exactly) means specifically "to terrorize" or, as a noun, "terrorism" — chủ nghĩa khủng bố, "terrorism [as an ideology/tactic]," kẻ khủng bố, "a terrorist" — rather than the broader everyday "fear," for which Vietnamese uses the unrelated native word sợ hãi. A striking parallel to [[論理]]'s displacement finding earlier in the character sweep and [[一切]]'s Buddhist-to-ordinary-vocabulary drift in this one: the same compound can carry a far more general sense in some languages and a narrowly specialized one in another.
