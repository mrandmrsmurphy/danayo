## Notes
- [学](../characters/学.md) "study, learn" + [生](../characters/生.md) "life; living person; birth" — one who lives by studying; a living student.

学生 is the pan-Sinospheric word for student at all levels of formal education, and its compound carries a quiet Confucian resonance: 生 in this context means not just "person" but a living, active presence — one who is alive in and through their study. The complementary compound is [[先生]] (sensei / 선생 / 先生), where 先 ("before, first") marks the teacher as one who arrived at knowledge before the student. Teacher and student are defined by temporal relation to the same learning: one has been there first, the other is still on the way.

The Analects opens with 学 as the foundational activity:

> 子曰：「學而時習之，不亦說乎？」(《論語·學而》1.1)
> "The Master said: 'To learn and then practise it at the right time — is that not delightful?'"

This is the first sentence Confucius speaks in the collected text, and it frames the entire Confucian project: 学 is not passive reception but active practice (習, shí/xí, "to practise, rehearse"). A 学生 is someone engaged in that active process.

The East Asian academic culture shaped by this tradition produced the imperial examination system (科挙, kēju), which defined meritocratic ambition across China, Korea, and Vietnam for over a millennium and whose shadow still falls on modern university entrance examinations across the region. Vietnamese học sinh uses native vocabulary (học, "study" + sinh, "life/person") rather than the Hán-Việt reading, but is structurally identical in meaning.
