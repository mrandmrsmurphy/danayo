## Notes
- [結 (char)](../characters/結%20(char).md) "tie, join" + [束 (char)](../characters/束%20(char).md) "bundle" — literally "tie into a bundle." Neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters); 結束 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/結 (char).md` and `characters/束 (char).md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **A genuine and important cross-linguistic sense divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: Japanese けっそく and Korean 결속 both keep the literal, compositional sense "unity, solidarity, binding together" (団結 near-synonym) — the sense this vault's `english` field describes. Mandarin 结束/jiéshù, however, has grammaticalized into an entirely different, extremely common everyday word meaning "to end, finish, conclude" (会议结束了 "the meeting has ended") — nothing to do with binding or unity. Vietnamese kết thúc tracks the Mandarin sense ("to end"), creating the same kind of internal tension seen on [[火車]]'s own `vietnamese` field — left as-is (a real, attested word for these characters) rather than removed, but flagged as not aligned with the Japanese/Korean sense this word page otherwise documents.
