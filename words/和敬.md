## Notes
- [和](../characters/和.md) "peaceful, harmonious" + [敬](../characters/敬.md) "respect" — harmony and respect.

誾 (yín), describing speech that is "harmonious yet respectful, mild and deferential," appears 4 times in the 《論語》, most notably in **鄉黨**'s depiction of Confucius's demeanor at court: "與下大夫言，侃侃如也；與上大夫言，誾誾如也" ("speaking with lower officials, he was forthright and at ease; speaking with higher officials, he was mild and respectful"). 誾 lies outside Dan'a'yo's closed character set, so the vault substitutes 和敬 — joining 和 ("harmonious, peaceful") with 敬 ("respect, reverence") — a "Translation" substitution per [[translation/論語 (book)]]'s Vocabulary list, capturing both qualities described by 誾.

和敬 is itself an attested compound, most familiar from the Japanese tea ceremony's four foundational principles 和敬清寂 (わけいせいじゃく, "harmony, respect, purity, tranquility") attributed to Sen no Rikyū. The Sino-Japanese わけい, Sino-Korean 화경, and Sino-Vietnamese hoà kính readings follow directly from 和 and 敬's own readings. Korean 화경 happens to match 諺文 화경 exactly, character for character.
