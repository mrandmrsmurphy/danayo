## Notes
- [郭 (char)](../characters/郭%20(char).md) "outermost part, outer wall" (Dan'a'yo stand-in for 廓) + [清](../characters/清.md) "pure, clear"

In Dan'a'yo, 郭 serves as the stand-in for 廓 (outer expanse, open space), which shares its pronunciation; the alias 廓清 is the standard Chinese form.

郭清 / 廓清 carries two related but distinct senses. The classical and literary sense is "to purge or sweep clean" — to clear away corruption, disorder, or enemies with thoroughness, leaving nothing behind. In Chinese political and historical writing (kuòqīng), the word evokes decisive, large-scale elimination of a problem: 廓清積弊 (to sweep away accumulated abuses). The Japanese literary reading (かくせい, kakusei) preserves this sense.

The modern medical sense is surgical: 郭清 (郭清術, kakusei-jutsu in Japanese) denotes the systematic dissection and removal of tissue, typically lymph nodes, during cancer surgery. This usage is dominant in contemporary Japanese medical writing: リンパ節郭清 (lymph node dissection). The two senses share the same underlying image — methodical, complete clearance — applied first to political space, then to anatomical space.
