## Notes
- [憑 (char)](../characters/憑%20(char).md) "rely on, based on, evidence"

憑 carries three closely related senses that all share the underlying image of leaning upon something for support. As a verb: to rely on or depend upon (憑借, to rely on; 憑什麼, "on what basis? by what right?"). As a preposition: by means of, based on (憑証, by proof; 憑良心, by conscience). As a noun: evidence, proof, or a credential (証憑, documentary evidence; 憑証, voucher, receipt).

The 形声 etymology brings together 心 (heart/mind) and the phonetic 馮 (OC *bɯŋ) — the image of the mind leaning on something, finding its support. The same root gives 馮 its secondary meaning of riding or mounting (as on horseback), and the shared sense of "supported upon" runs through the character's range. In Vietnamese, bằng covers the "based on, by means of" sense and is one of the most common functional words in the language. In Japanese (ひょう, hyō), the character is hyōgai (outside the jōyō list) and appears chiefly in classical and literary writing.
