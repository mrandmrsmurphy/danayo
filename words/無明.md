## Notes
- [無 (char)](../characters/無%20(char).md) "without; lacking" + [明 (char)](../characters/明%20(char).md) "brightness; illumination"

**無明** is the East Asian rendering of Sanskrit *avidyā* (अविद्या, "non-knowing") — the fundamental ignorance that, in Buddhist teaching, underlies all suffering. It is not merely a lack of information but an active misapprehension: the delusion that the self is permanent, that phenomena are inherently real, and that things can provide lasting satisfaction. In the Twelve Links of Dependent Origination (十二因縁, *pratītyasamutpāda*), 無明 is the first link — the root condition from which craving, attachment, and the entire cycle of conditioned existence (輪廻, *saṃsāra*) arise.

The compound is consistent and stable across the CJKV sphere — むみょう, 무명, vô minh — a rare case of full terminological agreement across five languages, reflecting the shared transmission of the Dharma through Chinese Buddhist scriptural translation. In Mandarin, 无明 is a precise homophone of [[無名]] "namelessness; anonymity" (both *wúmíng*), though the two words are distinct in Dan'a'yo phonology (ㄇㄜㄇ⼶ㄫ vs ㄇㄜㄇㄧㄫ) and in meaning.
