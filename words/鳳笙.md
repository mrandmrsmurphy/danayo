## Notes
- [鳳](../characters/鳳.md) "phoenix" + [笙](../characters/笙.md) "sheng mouth organ"

The 笙 (sheng) is one of the oldest free-reed instruments in the world, a circular mouth organ of bamboo pipes with a gourd windchest, documented in Chinese texts as early as the Shijing (詩經). The association with the phoenix is ancient and poetic: according to several classical sources, the cry of the arriving phoenix resembled the timbre of the 笙, so a particularly fine or ritual sheng came to be called 鳳笙 — the "phoenix sheng," suggesting both divine origin and exceptional beauty of tone.

In Japanese court music (雅楽, gagaku), ほうしょう (hōshō) designates the 17-pipe mouth organ specifically used in the gagaku ensemble, distinguishing it from other configurations of the sheng family. The Japanese term thus shifts register: while Chinese 鳳笙 is primarily a poetic epithet, Japanese 鳳笙 is a technical instrument classification in living ritual usage.

The simplified Chinese form is 凤笙, corresponding to 鳳 → 凤. Related instruments include the Korean saenghwang (생황, 笙簧) and the various Southeast Asian free-reed instruments that descend from the same ancient gourd-pipe tradition.
