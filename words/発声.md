## Notes
- [発 (char)](../characters/発%20(char).md) "issue forth, discharge" + [声](../characters/声.md) "vocalize, emit sound" — literally "to issue forth a sound." `characters/声.md`'s own `stand_in` points here, meaning 声 does not stand independently outside this compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: both `characters/発 (char).md` and `characters/声.md` are individually `kwin: false`.
- **A genuine semantic-narrowing divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: Mandarin fāshēng, Cantonese faat3 sing1, Japanese はっせい (used constantly in music/singing pedagogy — 発声練習, "vocal warm-up") and Korean 발성 (발성법, "vocalization technique") all keep the broad, compositional sense "to vocalize; the act of producing vocal sound." Vietnamese phát thanh, however, has narrowed in modern usage specifically to "broadcast" (radio/audio broadcasting — đài phát thanh "radio station," phát thanh viên "radio announcer"), rather than the general "vocalization" sense found elsewhere in the sphere.
