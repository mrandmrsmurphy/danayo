## Notes
- [同](../characters/同.md) "same" + [等 (char)](../characters/等%20(char).md) "grade, rank" — equal in rank; equivalent in standing

同等 names equivalence of rank, qualification, or status — the condition of two things occupying the same position on a scale. It is not a general word for equality but a specific term for parity of level: a 同等 qualification is one recognized as carrying the same standing as another; 同等の扱い (同等の待遇) is treatment without discrimination in rank.

同等 is worth distinguishing from two near-synonyms: 平等 (píngděng / びょうどう) denotes equality of rights or treatment — the normative claim that people deserve equal consideration — while 対等 (duìděng / たいとう) denotes parity of power or position between parties in a relationship. 同等 is more neutral and comparative: it simply states that two things are at the same level on whatever scale is in view.
