## Notes
- [恐](../characters/恐.md) "fear; dread" + [恐](../characters/恐.md) "fear; dread"
- (ideophonic) to act or move in a state of fearfulness, apprehension, or careful dread; with trepidation

The reduplication intensifies and stylises the quality: not merely afraid, but moving through the world in a posture of constant fearful caution. The form 兢兢 is the classical alternate spelling of the same ideophone — 兢 carries the additional sense of "trembling with care" — and is the form used in the chengyu [[戦戦恐恐]].

**兢 itself was deliberately not created as a Dan'a'yo character.** It is bound entirely to two idioms (戰戰兢兢 and 兢兢業業), has no clean Middle Chinese phonology precedent anywhere else in the vault, and is Hyōgai in Japanese. Both idioms it would anchor already have attested real-world orthographic variants substituting a different, existing character for the same sound: 戰戰兢兢 → 戦戦恐恐 (confirmed interchangeable across Japanese dictionaries, hence 恐 here), and 兢兢業業 → 矜矜業業 (『三國志・王基傳』; 陳喬樅's 經説考 confirms 兢 and 矜 share the same sound). See [[feedback_character_inclusion_philosophy]] for the general principle.

恐々 is the Japanese rendering with the standard repetition mark.
