## Notes
**Form.** 副金 is [副 (char)](characters/副%20(char).md) “secondary; subsidiary; assistant” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for actinides.

**Etymology.** _Protactinium_ derives from Greek πρότος (_protos_) “first” + _actinium_. The meaning is **“before actinium”**, i.e., the element that precedes actinium in a decay chain (Pa → Ac).

**Motivation.** Rather than forcing a literal “before” (前) or “proto-” rendering, you choose 副 to encode a **relational hierarchy**:

- actinium = 始金 (the “beginning metal”)
- protactinium = 副金 (the “subordinate/precursor metal” relative to that beginning)

This captures the **dependent, prior-to status** without duplicating 始 or introducing awkward constructions.

**Type of formation.**  
A **relational semantic mapping within the system**:

- Greek relational prefix (proto-, “before”)
- interpreted through hierarchy (primary vs secondary)
- encoded as 副

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, no phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately.
3. Provides a simple, high-frequency symbol (副).
4. Integrates tightly with 始金, forming a conceptual pair:
    - 始金 (actinium) = “beginning”
    - 副金 (protactinium) = “subsidiary/preceding relative to that beginning”

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 镤 (phonetic)
- Japanese: プロトアクチニウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 프로트악티늄 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: protactini (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 副金 restores relational meaning.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 副 typically means “secondary” rather than strictly “prior,” so the temporal nuance is slightly shifted to hierarchical dependence.
- However, within your system, the pairing with 始 makes the relationship intelligible.

**Conclusion.**  
副金 is a compact and internally coherent solution. It does not mirror the Greek literally, but it captures the relational structure in a way that integrates cleanly with 始金 and avoids redundancy.
