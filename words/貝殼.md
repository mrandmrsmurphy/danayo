## Notes
- [貝](../characters/貝.md) "shellfish" + [殻](../characters/殻.md) "husk" — stand-in for [[殻]], which cannot appear independently.

Unlike most of the words in this batch, 貝殼 needs no alias substitution: both 貝 and 殻 are already the vault's own characters, and 殼/殻/壳 are already unified as aliases of one another. What it does carry is a *reading* divergence of the kind seen in 六楽/楽 — in most compounds 殼/殻 is read qiào (its primary Mandarin reading, as in 地殼 "the earth's crust"), but in 貝殼 specifically it takes the alternate reading ké, the everyday word for the hard covering of a mollusk, egg, nut, or seed.

貝殼 (bèiké) is the ordinary word for "seashell" — the hard calcified covering left behind by a mollusk such as a clam, snail, or oyster. The Japanese かいがら is a native (kun'yomi) compound rather than a Sino-Japanese on'yomi reading, reflecting how thoroughly domesticated this everyday object's name is in Japanese.

Because 貝 and 殻 are the vault's own characters with no alias involved, the 羅馬字, 諺文, and 注音 (배칵 / baikag / ㄅㄚㄧㄎㄚㄎ) are a straightforward concatenation of their own fixed Dan'a'yo values, and the mandarin field simply reflects 殻/殼's secondary "ké" reading in this specific compound.
