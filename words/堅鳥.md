## Notes
- [堅](../characters/堅.md) as 代用字 for 鰹 "skipjack tuna" + [鳥 (char)](../characters/鳥%20(char).md) "bird"
- 堅 carries 鰹 as an alias, sanctioning this substitution. The aliases 鰹鳥 and 鲣鸟 preserve the traditional and simplified Chinese spellings.

The booby (*Sula* spp., family Sulidae) is named the "bonito bird" because it congregates over schools of feeding skipjack tuna: the fish drive smaller prey to the surface, and the boobies plunge-dive into the same column of water. Fishermen across the Pacific have long used the sight of boobies to locate 堅魚/鰹魚 below.

In Japanese the compound has the special reading かつおどり — 堅/鰹 takes the native kun-yomi かつお rather than the on-yomi けん, and 鳥 undergoes rendaku (とり → どり) following the preceding element. This is not the regular on-yomi concatenation, which would give けんちょう. The brown booby (*Sula leucogaster*) is the species most specifically called かつおどり in Japanese; the Chinese term 鰹鳥/堅鳥 (*jiānniǎo*) covers the broader Sulidae family.

The simplified Chinese alias 鲣鸟 uses 鲣 (simplified 鲣/鰹) and 鸟 (simplified 鳥).
