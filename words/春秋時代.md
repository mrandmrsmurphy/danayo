## Notes
- [[春秋]] "the Spring and Autumn Annals; spring and autumn" + [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time" + [代](../characters/代.md) "era; generation" — "the era of the Spring and Autumn [Annals]"

春秋時代 (*Chūnqiū Shídài*, 770–476 BC) is the first of the two major sub-periods of the Eastern Zhou dynasty, named retrospectively after the [[春秋]] (*Spring and Autumn Annals*), the chronicle traditionally said to have been compiled or edited by Confucius covering the history of the state of Lu across roughly this span. The period is conventionally dated from the relocation of the Zhou capital eastward to Luoyang in 770 BC to the partition of the state of Jin in 453 or 403 BC (the exact end date varies by historian), after which the period is termed the [[戦国]][[時代]] ("Warring States period").

Politically, 春秋時代 is characterised by the gradual decline of central Zhou royal authority and the rise of regional states (諸侯, *zhūhóu*) competing for hegemony (霸, *bà*) while nominally still acknowledging the Zhou king. It was an era of intense diplomatic and military activity among dozens of states, several of which — Qi, Jin, Chu, Qin, and others — successively held the position of dominant power (霸主, *bàzhǔ*). It is also the formative period of much of classical Chinese philosophy: Confucius, Laozi, and Sun Tzu are all traditionally placed within or at the end of this era, and the *Zuo Zhuan* (左傳), *Guoyu* (國語), and other major historical and philosophical texts either describe or originate from this period.

The name itself illustrates a recurring pattern in Sinitic historiography, in which a historical period takes its name from the title of a chronicle covering it — 春秋, literally "springs and autumns" (i.e., years), being both the generic word for "annals" and the specific title of the chronicle of Lu, with 春秋時代 thus meaning both "the era of the [Lu state's] Annals" and, more loosely, "the springs-and-autumns era."
