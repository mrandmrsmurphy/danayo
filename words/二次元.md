## Notes
- [二 (char)](../characters/二%20(char).md) "two" + [次](../characters/次.md) "order" + [元](../characters/元.md) "element"

Literally "the second dimension" — a geometric term referring to two-dimensional space (a flat plane with x and y axes, but no z-axis depth). In mathematics and physics, 二次元 is the formal term for 2D space, contrasted with 一次元 (one-dimensional, a line), 三次元 (three-dimensional, ordinary physical space), and 四次元 (four-dimensional, including time).

However, 二次元 has acquired a second, culturally significant meaning in Japanese internet culture and subsequently across East Asia: **the fictional world of anime, manga, and video games**, as opposed to the real world (三次元, or simply 三次, "3D"). This usage emerged in the 1990s–2000s in Japanese otaku communities, where the distinction between 二次元 (flat/drawn characters and worlds) and 三次元 (real, three-dimensional reality) became a fundamental way of describing one's relationship to fiction. A 二次元キャラ is a fictional drawn character; 二次元が好き ("I love 2D") means a preference for anime/manga aesthetics and characters over real-world relationships.

This slang usage has since spread into Korean (이차원) and Chinese internet communities, where 二次元 (Chinese: 二次元文化, "2D culture") now describes the entire ecosystem of anime, manga, light novels, and related media imported from Japan or created in the Japanese style. The concept is central to modern East Asian youth culture and fandom.

The suffix 次元 (じげん, cìyuán) means "dimension" in the mathematical sense — 次 is "order/sequence" and 元 is "element/origin" — making 二次元 literally "the second-order element" of space.
