## Notes
- [回 (char)](../characters/回%20(char).md) "turns, return" + [収](../characters/収.md) "gather together" — this word is the stand-in for [収](../characters/収.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

回収 names the act of taking something back that had been given, sent, or dispersed out — collecting a defective product from customers, retrieving recyclable materials, calling in an outstanding debt — with 回 supplying the "back, return" direction and 収 the "gather in" action. It is the standard, everyday word for this across Mandarin (huíshōu, HSK 5), Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean, all four preserving a clean, transparent concatenation of the two characters' own readings.

**Vietnamese colloquially prefers the reversed order** *thu hồi* ("gather" + "return") to the direct compositional *hồi thu* used here — a common pattern where a Sino-Vietnamese compound's two morphemes settled into the opposite sequence from their Chinese source, the same phenomenon behind pairs like 命運/vận mệnh. Both orders are transparently built from the same two roots; only the sequencing differs.

Despite needing 回収 as its official legitimizing compound, 収 is far from a quiet, single-use character — it already carries real independent life in this vault via 収入 ("income"), 収穫 ("harvest"), 収蔵 ("to store, collection"), and 収拾 ("tidy, pick up"), all listed on the character's own page. 回収 simply happens to be the specific compound chosen to establish 収's Dan'a'yo viability, not a sign that 収 is otherwise unproductive.
