## Notes
- [薬 (char)](../characters/薬%20(char).md) "medicine; drug" + [剤](../characters/剤.md) "dose; preparation; agent" — stand-in for [[剤]], which cannot appear independently
- 薬剤 names medicine considered as a prepared, dosed substance — the compound's second element 剤 narrows the broad idea of "medicine" (薬) down to a specific measured preparation, the kind dispensed by a pharmacist rather than gathered as a raw herb. This technical, clinical register is shared widely: Mandarin yàojì, Cantonese joek6 zai1, Japanese やくざい (yakuzai, written 藥劑 in the older kyūjitai orthography preserved here as an alias), and Sino-Korean 약제 (yakje). The compound underlies a cluster of pharmacy-related vocabulary — 薬剤師 "pharmacist" foremost among them — that treats medicine not as a substance in the abstract but as something formulated, measured, and administered.
