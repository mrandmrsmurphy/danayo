## Notes
- [掌](../characters/掌.md) "palm; sole" + [握](../characters/握.md) "grasp; hold" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (掌's own points to `手掌`, 握's own to `把握`); 掌握 is an independent compound. `kwin: true` per the AND-rule: both constituent characters are individually `kwin: true`.
- A clean, directly parallel compound across the whole sphere — "to grasp/hold with the palm" extended figuratively to "to master, control, or have a firm command of" (a skill, situation, or power). Mandarin zhǎngwò, Cantonese zoeng2 ak1, Japanese しょうあく, Korean 장악, and Vietnamese chưởng ác (e.g. chưởng ác binh quyền "to hold military power") all converge on this same figurative sense, with no notable divergence between languages.
