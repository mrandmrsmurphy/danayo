## Notes
- [天 (char)](../characters/天%20(char).md) "sky, heaven"

天 is "sky, heaven" — one of the most productive single characters in the vault, heading dozens of compounds spanning religion ([[天主]] "God", [[堕天使]] "fallen angel", [[天子]] "Son of Heaven, the emperor"), astronomy ([[天文学]] "astronomy", [[天王星]] "Uranus", [[天狼星]] "Sirius"), everyday nature vocabulary ([[天気]] "weather", [[天鵝]] "swan"), and abstract senses extended from "heaven-given" ([[天真]] "innocent, naive", [[天才]] "genius", [[天生]] "natural, intrinsic"). The character is 象形 (pictographic): a person (大) with a stroke marking the top of the head — the space above a person, i.e. the sky.

Mandarin 天 (tiān). Cantonese 天 (tin1). Japanese splits between the on'yomi TEN, used in nearly all compounds (天気 tenki "weather", 天文 tenmon "astronomy"), and the archaic native reading あめ/あま (ame/ama), which survives mainly in set phrases like 天の川 (amanogawa, "the Milky Way," literally "river of heaven") — the Dan'a'yo word page records this native reading rather than the on'yomi. Korean shows the same split: Sino-Korean 천 appears in compounds (천지 "heaven and earth"), while the everyday standalone word for "sky" is the native 하늘 (haneul) — again the reading this word page stores. Vietnamese thiên is the Sino-Vietnamese reading used in compounds and set phrases, while everyday speech uses the native trời.

天 also supplies half of a Dan'a'yo periodic-table coinage: [[天金]] "uranium" is built from 天 in the sense of Uranus (the planet/god) plus [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) "metal" — deliberately mirroring the real-world etymology of the English word "uranium," itself named after the planet Uranus.

1. sky, heaven
2. component of [[天金]], the Dan'a'yo coinage for the element uranium (via Uranus)
