## Notes
- [天 (char)](../characters/天%20(char).md) "heaven, sky" + [鵝](../characters/鵝.md) "goose" (together [[天鵝]] "swan") + [絨](../characters/絨.md) "velvet, fine wool"

天鵝絨 is built on [[天鵝]] ("swan," literally "heavenly goose") plus 絨, the character for soft, napped fabric — fine woven or felted fiber, as in wool or down. The compound's literal image is "swan('s)-down fabric": velvet's defining quality, a dense, soft pile that traps light and feels like fine feathers, is named here by analogy to the down of a swan, much as English "velvet" descends from Latin *villus* ("shaggy hair, fleece").

絨 itself is too phonologically and semantically bound to stand alone as a Dan'a'yo word — its `stand_in` is 天鵝絨 itself, meaning the compound is not just *a* word containing 絨, but *the* word that licenses 絨 to exist as an entry at all. 鵝, by contrast, has its own independent stand-in word, [[鵝鳥]] ("domestic goose"), distinguishing the wild/poetic "heavenly goose" (swan, 天鵝) from the everyday domesticated bird.

In Japanese, 天鵞絨 has historically been read びろうど (*birōdo*) — ateji, a phonetic borrowing of Portuguese *veludo* ("velvet"), reflecting velvet's arrival in Japan via 16th-century Portuguese trade alongside other ateji loanwords like 煙草 (tabako, "tobacco"). The Sino-Japanese on'yomi reading てんがじゅう (tengajū) given here is the "literal," compositional reading rather than the historically dominant loanword pronunciation — a useful reminder that Dan'a'yo's character-by-character readings sometimes diverge from a word's most common real-world pronunciation. 鵞 (used in the historical spelling 天鵞絨) is an alias of 鵝, noted in [[鵝]]'s own entry.
