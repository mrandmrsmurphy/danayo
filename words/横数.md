## Notes
- [横](../characters/横.md) "horizontal; transverse; lateral" + [数](../characters/数.md) "number; count" — a number that lies on the perpendicular (imaginary) axis of the complex plane

The standard Sinospheric term for imaginary numbers is 虚數 (虚数 in Japanese, 허수 in Korean, số ảo in Vietnamese), where 虚 means "empty" or "unreal." The Dan'a'yo term 横数 takes a different approach: it names the imaginary unit by its geometric position. In the Argand diagram, the real numbers lie along the horizontal axis and imaginary numbers along the vertical — but Dan'a'yo inverts this framing, treating the real axis as the baseline and imaginary numbers as the "transverse" or "crossing" values, the numbers that lie across the main line of counting. The name echoes the meaning of 横 (across, lateral) in words like [[横断]] (to cross over).

The imaginary unit itself is √−1; the set of imaginary numbers is all real multiples of it. Together with the real numbers, they form the complex numbers.
