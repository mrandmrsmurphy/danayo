## Notes
Korean and Japanese both keep a native word as the everyday term for "rudder, helm" — Korean 키 (not the Sino-Korean 타 used here) and Japanese かじ (not on'yomi タ/ダ, mostly confined to compounds) — while Vietnamese lái has gone further than either: it is a fully naturalized, ancient loan from this very character (Old Chinese \*lˤajʔ), and has broadened well past boats into the ordinary verb for "to steer, to drive" in general (lái xe, "to drive a car"). The more literal Sino-Vietnamese reading đà survives only in specialized nautical compounds like đà công ("helmsman"), alongside a Cantonese-influenced variant, tài (from 舵's Cantonese reading to4), in tài công, another word for a boat pilot.
