## Notes
- [法 (char)](../characters/法%20(char).md) "law; rule; dharma; France (abbrev.)" + [語](../characters/語.md) "language; speech"

法語 is genuinely polysemous in Dan'a'yo, carrying two distinct meanings from the same characters read in different senses. As a language name, 法語 means "French" — 法 serves as the conventional abbreviation for [[法国]] (France), following the Chinese tradition, and 語 is the language-name suffix shared by [[英語]], [[日語]], [[露斯亜語]], and other language names in the Dan'a'yo lexicon. As a compound read on its literal merits, 法語 means "dharma speech; religious discourse; sermon" — 法 in the Buddhist sense (the dharma; the teaching of the law) and 語 as spoken words or discourse, giving "the words of the teaching; a religious discourse." The Japanese 法語 (ほうご) carries precisely this second meaning: the spoken or written teachings of a Buddhist or Zen master. Vietnamese pháp ngữ parallels both senses: "French language" in formal register and "dharma speech" in Buddhist contexts. Korean uses 프랑스어 for French and 법어 for the dharma-speech reading.

法 (ㄈㄚㄆ/fab/빱 — Mandarin fǎ, Cantonese faat3, Korean 법, Japanese HOU/ほう, Vietnamese pháp/phép): "law; rule; method; dharma; France (abbreviation)" — MC 非母 (f) + 乏韻 (iɐp, entering tone); a 会意 classically depicting 水 (water) + 廌 (a mythical law-discerning beast that butted the guilty), giving "law; standard; method." In Dan'a'yo, 法 anchors legal and doctrinal compounds (法律, 法則, 法輪) and also serves as the abbreviated name for 法国 (France). 語 (⼄/'yo/요 — Mandarin yǔ, Cantonese jyu5, Korean 어, Japanese GO/ご, Vietnamese ngữ): "language; speech; word" — MC 疑母 (ŋ) + 魚韻 (ɨʌ); see [[言語]] for full analysis.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 빱요 does not match Korean 법어. 法 gives 빱 vs 법 (entering-tone vowel and initial both differ); 語 gives 요 vs 어 (魚韻 -o vs -eo).
