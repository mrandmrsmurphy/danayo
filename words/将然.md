## Notes
- [将 (char)](../characters/将%20(char).md) "about to, going to" + [然](../characters/然.md) "so, thus, -ly" — being on the verge of happening; the prospective aspect.

This is a technical term drawn from the traditional Chinese grammatical tradition of describing aspect through paired opposites — 已然 ("already realized") against 将然 ("about to be realized") — a framework distinct from, though parallel to, the 已然形/未然形 distinction of classical Japanese conjugation. The grammar book borrows exactly this opposition for Dan'a'yo's own [[時相]] system: the suffix –将 marks 将然相 (prospective/future), paired with –已 for [[完了]]相 (perfective), and the ordering rule keeps –将 always closest to the verb stem, innermost of any aspectual suffix chain. Because it names a specific analytical category rather than an everyday word, attestation here rests on Mandarin grammatical scholarship rather than ordinary dictionary usage in Cantonese, Japanese, or Korean.
