## Notes
- [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human" + [称](../characters/称.md) "call; designate; appellation"

人称 is the grammatical term for *person* — the category distinguishing speaker (first person), addressee (second person), and non-participant (third person). In Dan'a'yo the [[人称代詞]] (personal pronoun) system is organized around this distinction: [[我]] and archaic [[俺]] for first person; [[君]], [[汝]], and archaic [[朕]] for second person; third-person reference is handled primarily through the correlative pronoun table ([[対応代詞]]). The term 人称 is consistent across the CJKV linguistic tradition — Mandarin rénchēng, Japanese にんしょう (ninshō), Korean 인칭 all use the same characters — making it one of the grammatical metalanguage terms with near-complete cross-linguistic agreement. Vietnamese linguistic terminology uses nhân xưng in formal contexts, though the native term ngôi (position; rank) is more common in everyday grammatical discussion. The alias 人稱 is the traditional-form spelling.

人 (ㄋㄧㄋ/nin/닌 — Mandarin rén, Cantonese jan4, Korean 인, Japanese NIN・JIN/にん・じん, Vietnamese nhân): "person; human being" — MC 日母 (ȵ) + 眞韻 (iɪn); a pictograph of a standing person in profile, one of the most fundamental sinographic characters and the semantic root of the grammatical term: 人称 is "person-appellation," the designation that locates a referent relative to the speech act. 称 (ㄑㄧㄫ/cing/칭 — Mandarin chēng, Cantonese cing1, Korean 칭, Japanese SHŌ/しょう, Vietnamese xưng): "call; designate; name; appellation; weigh" — MC 昌母 (t͡ɕʰ) + 蒸韻 (ɨŋ); a 形声 of semantic 禾 (grain; standing upright) + phonetic 爯, originally "to weigh on a balance," extended to "to designate; to call by a name; to refer to." In grammatical compounds 称 carries the sense of "how something is referred to": 人称 (how persons are referred to), 名称 (designation; name), 称号 (title; appellation).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 닌칭 does not match Korean 인칭. The second syllable converges: 称 gives 칭=칭 (MC 昌母+蒸韻 → both give aspirated initial ㄑ/ㅊ and -ing coda 칭). The first syllable diverges: 人 gives Dan'a'yo 닌 (nin, MC 日母+眞韻 → ㄋ nasal preserved, -in coda) vs Korean 인 (in, null onset — 日母 dropped). This is the same MC 日母 retention pattern seen throughout the Dan'a'yo pronoun system: [[而]] (닌칭 shares the 日母-initial 人), [[若]] (냐 vs 야), [[汝]] (뇌 vs 여), [[故而]] (고니 vs 고이), [[而且]] (니초 vs 이차). Dan'a'yo preserves MC ȵ as ㄋ consistently; Korean drops it consistently.
