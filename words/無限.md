## Notes
- [無 (char)](../characters/無%20(char).md) "lacking; without" + [限](../characters/限.md) "limit; boundary" — that which has no limit; the mathematical concept of infinity

無限 is shared across all branches of the Sinosphere in both ordinary speech and mathematics. In everyday use it means "boundless," "endless," or "unlimited" — 無限の可能性 (infinite possibilities), 無限責任 (unlimited liability). In mathematics it refers to the concept of infinity (∞), the symbol introduced by John Wallis in 1655. Compare [[無極]] (the Limitless, the cosmological concept in Daoist metaphysics: the state before 太極).

The symbol ∞ is sometimes read in Dan'a'yo as 無限大記号 (the "infinity sign"), or simply referred to by this word.
