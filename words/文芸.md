## Notes
- [文](../characters/文.md) "culture" + [芸](../characters/芸.md) "skill, art" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (文's own points to `文化`, 芸's own to `芸術`); 文芸 is an independent compound. `kwin: false` per the AND-rule: `characters/文.md` is `kwin: true`, `characters/芸.md` is `kwin: false` → compound `false`.
- **A genuine semantic-narrowing divergence, documented rather than forced into agreement**: Mandarin wényì, Cantonese man4 ngai6, Japanese ぶんげい, and Korean 문예 all preserve the broad literal sense of "literature and art" as a joint cultural category. Vietnamese văn nghệ (verified via search), however, has shifted in common usage to specifically mean amateur performance and entertainment — singing, dancing, and theatricals, as in a school or community "văn nghệ" show — rather than the abstract literature-and-art pairing (which Vietnamese instead expresses with the fuller phrase văn học nghệ thuật). A learner should not assume văn nghệ always carries the same abstract breadth as its Sino-Japanese/Korean/Mandarin counterparts.
