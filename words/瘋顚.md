## Notes
- [瘋](../characters/瘋.md) "crazy; insane" + [顚](../characters/顚.md) "summit; epilepsy" — stand-in for [[瘋]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.

This compound has drifted in three different directions across the family. Japanese ふうてん shows the most dramatic shift: originally "mental derangement" (used clinically enough that Meiji-era mental hospitals were called 瘋癲病院), it was repurposed in 1967 to name aimless, unemployed young drifters loitering around Shinjuku Station (フーテン族), and then cemented in its modern sense — "an easygoing vagabond, free of ties" — by the beloved film character 車寅次郎, "フーテンの寅さん," from the 男はつらいよ series; today it barely registers as "insane" at all. Korean 풍전, checked directly against Korean dictionaries, has drifted somewhere else entirely: it's now a specific traditional-medicine (한의학) term for epilepsy brought on by "wind pathogen" (풍사), not a general word for "crazy" the way the characters suggest. Vietnamese phong điên is attested — it appears in the Vietnamese translation of 紅樓夢 (Dream of the Red Chamber) as "khùng khùng điên điên" — but everyday spoken Vietnamese for "crazy" reaches for the native words điên or khùng alone, not the Sino-Vietnamese compound. Mandarin and Cantonese are the only members of the family that still use 瘋顚/瘋癲 as an ordinary, general word for "crazy, deranged."
