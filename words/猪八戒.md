## Notes
- [猪](../characters/猪.md) "boar, pig" + [八 (char)](../characters/八%20(char).md) "eight" + [戒](../characters/戒.md) "precept, admonish" — "Pig [of the] Eight Precepts."

猪八戒/저팔계/Trư Bát Giới/ちょはっかい is one of Tang Sanzang's disciples in 西遊記 (Journey to the West), alongside Sun Wukong and Sha Wujing. Unusually for a proper noun in this vault, there is no real divergence to report here — every language in the family simply reads the same three characters in its own native phonology, since the name travels as a shared literary reference across the whole Sinosphere rather than being independently translated. 八戒 ("Eight Precepts") is itself a Buddhist monastic nickname, referencing the eight precepts a novice is expected to keep — an irony the story leans on constantly, since Zhu Bajie is characterized by gluttony and lust, precisely the vices those precepts forbid. His formal dharma name, granted by Guanyin, is actually 猪悟能 ("Pig Awakened to Power"), already cross-referenced from [[猪]]'s own Words section — 八戒 is the more commonly used nickname.
