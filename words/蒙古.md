## Notes
- [蒙](../characters/蒙.md) "cover; Mongolia" + [古](../characters/古.md) "ancient, old"

蒙古 "Mongolia" is the standard CJKV transcription of the Mongol ethnonym (the name's own etymology is disputed, but a common folk gloss reads it as "brave" or "eternal"); the characters 蒙 "cover" and 古 "ancient" were chosen purely for their sound, not their meaning. 蒙古 is also the **stand-in** for [[蒙]], which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse — without 蒙古 fixing its reading, 蒙 would have no independent foothold in the lexicon. Don't confuse this with [[蒙朧]] "indistinct, hazy," a separate compound that happens to share the same first character but is unrelated in meaning.
