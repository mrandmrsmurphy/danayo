## Notes
- [九 (char)](../characters/九%20(char).md) "nine" + [数](../characters/数.md) "number, count" — the Nine Numbers, the sixth and final of the Six Arts (六芸) of Zhou-dynasty noble education.

九数 (jiǔshù) is the last item in the **《周禮·地官司徒·保氏》** curriculum, after [[五礼]], [[六楽]], [[五射]], [[五馭]], and [[六書]]. The Han-dynasty commentator Zheng Xuan (鄭玄), citing Zheng Zhong (鄭眾), enumerated the Nine Numbers as nine branches of practical mathematics:

1. **方田** (fāng tián) — area of fields (land measurement)
2. **粟米** (sù mǐ) — millet and rice (proportional exchange of grains)
3. **差分** (chā fēn) — graded distribution (proportional allotment by rank)
4. **少廣** (shǎo guǎng) — diminishing breadth (extracting square and cube roots)
5. **商功** (shāng gōng) — assessing labor (volumes of earthworks and construction)
6. **均輸** (jūn shū) — equitable transport (proportional taxation and shipping)
7. **方程** (fāng chéng) — rectangular arrays (simultaneous linear equations)
8. **贏不足** (yíng bù zú) — excess and deficit (the "false position" method)
9. **旁要** (páng yào) — surveying by auxiliary lines (right-triangle/gōugǔ problems)

These nine categories became the chapter titles of the **《九章算術》** ("Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art"), the foundational text of the Chinese mathematical tradition, compiled some centuries after the 《周禮》 but preserving this Zhou-era classification of practical arithmetic and geometry.

As the final of the Six Arts, 九数 sits alongside [[五礼]], [[六楽]], [[五射]], [[五馭]], and [[六書]] — together rounding out the curriculum from ritual and music, through martial and practical skills, to literacy and numeracy.
