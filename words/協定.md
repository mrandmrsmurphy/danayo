## Notes
- [協](../characters/協.md) "cooperate, work together" + [定](../characters/定.md) "to fix, to decide" — an agreement; a jointly fixed decision; a pact between parties

協 denotes the bringing together of separate forces toward a common outcome — the character shows three instances of 力 (force/effort) combining under 十 (a crossroads or joint). 協定 is a decision made by this joint process: not imposed by one party but arrived at together and then fixed in place by 定.

In international usage, 協定 occupies the middle register between informal 合意 (understanding, mutual agreement) and formal 条約 (treaty, binding under international law). The General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT, 関税と貿易に関する一般協定) and various bilateral trade and security 協定 illustrate this usage. In labor relations, 労働協定 (labor agreement) and 労使協定 (management-labor accord) are standard terms. In all these cases, the key feature is that the agreement is reached jointly (協) and formally fixed (定) — not merely understood but recorded and binding.

Vietnamese hiệp định is one of the best-known uses: the Paris Peace Accords (1973) are known in Vietnamese as Hiệp định Paris, and the Geneva Accords (1954) as Hiệp định Genève — two agreements that determined the course of twentieth-century Vietnamese history.
