## Notes
- [腐](../characters/腐.md) "rotten, corrupt" + [朽](../characters/朽.md) "decayed, rotten wood"
- This word is the stand-in for [[朽]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A compound reinforcing the meaning of organic or moral decay. In Chinese, 腐朽 describes both physical rotting (timber, organic matter) and institutional or moral corruption — 腐朽的制度 "a corrupt system." In Japanese, ふきゅう (fukyū) appears in literary and classical contexts; the chengyu [[朽木糞牆]] invokes 朽 to evoke a person or thing beyond redemption. The compound pair (腐 "putrefied flesh" + 朽 "rotten wood") draws on the contrast between animal and vegetable decay to convey thoroughgoing deterioration.
