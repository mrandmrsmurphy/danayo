## Notes
- [緑 (char)](../characters/緑%20(char).md) "green" — the bare character used directly as a word, its own `stand_in`.

Mandarin lǜ, Cantonese luk6, Korean 록 (rok, the vault's standing North Korean 문화어 form — South Korean 두음법칙 would give 녹), and Vietnamese lục all use this character productively and independently for "green" (緑色/록색, 緑地/록지). Japanese, by contrast, almost always uses the native みどり (midori) for the color itself, reserving the on'yomi りょく/ろく for compounds (緑化, りょっか, "afforestation, greening").

The "abbreviation for beryllium" fact belongs to the character itself — see `characters/緑 (char).md`'s own Notes bullet linking [[緑柱素]] — rather than being restated here.
