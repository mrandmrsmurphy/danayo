## Notes
- [直 (char)](../characters/直%20(char).md) "straight, erect" + [径](../characters/径.md) "diameter" — stand-in for [[径]], which cannot appear independently

直径 (ちょっけい in Japanese; zhíjìng in Mandarin; 직경 in Korean) is the standard East Asian mathematical and scientific term for diameter — the length of a straight line passing through the centre of a circle from one side to the other. The compound pairs 直 (straight, direct) with 径 (path, diameter), where 径 already had a sense of "direct route" or "short path" in classical Chinese before acquiring the geometric meaning through contact with Western mathematics during the late Ming and Qing dynasties.

The modern geometric sense was standardised through Jesuit missionary mathematics texts (particularly Matteo Ricci's collaborations with Xu Guangqi in the early seventeenth century), which translated Euclidean geometry into classical Chinese. 直径 became the Chinese rendering of *diameter* in this context. The term subsequently passed into Japanese and Korean mathematical vocabulary with the same meaning. It contrasts with 半径 (bànjìng / はんけい / 반경), meaning radius (half-diameter).
