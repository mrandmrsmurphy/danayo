## Notes
- [卵](../characters/卵.md) "egg; ovum" + [子](../characters/子.md) "son; child" — stand-in for [[卵]], which cannot appear independently

子 is here in its productive noun-forming role (as in 電子, 種子, 精子), not its literal "child" sense — 卵子 names specifically the female reproductive cell, distinct from the more general "egg" that bare 卵 alone covers (which can also mean a chicken egg, etc.).

Mandarin luǎnzǐ, Cantonese leon2 zi2, Japanese らんし (ranshi), and this word's own Korean reading are all the standard clinical/biological term for "ovum, egg cell" specifically. **Korean field corrected**: the stored value was `난자` (nanja, applying South Korean 두음법칙 word-initial ㄹ→ㄴ shift), but per the vault's standing North Korean/문화어 convention (matching `characters/卵.md`'s own `란`, not a South-Korean-shifted form) the correct value is `란자` (ranja). Vietnamese uses bare noãn (not a two-syllable "noãn tử" compound) as the precise biological term for the egg cell/ovule — attested in both botany (the plant ovule) and human reproductive biology — distinct from the everyday, generic word for "egg" (including a food egg), trứng.
