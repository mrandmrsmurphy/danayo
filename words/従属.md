## Notes
- [従 (char)](../characters/従%20(char).md) "follow, obey" + [属](../characters/属.md) "belong, be subordinate to"

従属 denotes the state or relationship of being subordinate — belonging to, being under the authority of, or being dependent on a higher power or principal entity. As a suru verb (従属する), it means to be subordinate or to fall under the jurisdiction of something. The compound appears in political writing (従属国, satellite state; 従属関係, relationship of subordination), grammar (従属節, subordinate clause), and philosophy.

In Korean (종속), the word is in active use in political and academic writing; 종속 이론 (dependency theory) is a translation of the Latin American *teoría de la dependencia*. Note that the Dan'a'yo Bopomofo form yields 諺文 종족, which is a homophone of 種族 (종족, ethnic group / species) — the Korean reading 종속 and the Dan'a'yo 諺文 종족 are distinct.
