## Notes
- [東](../characters/東.md) "east" + [伝](../characters/伝.md) "transmit" — "eastward transmission," used above all of 佛教東伝 (the eastward transmission of Buddhism), the centuries-long movement of Buddhist teaching, texts, and institutions from India through Central Asia into China, and from China onward into Korea, Japan, and Vietnam. The term frames a whole current of cultural and religious history as a single directional movement, paralleling how cartography and historiography elsewhere in this lexipedia speak of civilizational diffusion in directional terms.

The Korean reading 동전 is a true homograph of the unrelated, far more common word 동전 ("coin," 銅錢) — context disambiguates the two in practice, since they share no semantic field.
