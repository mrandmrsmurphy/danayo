## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [同](../characters/同.md) "same"

不同 is "different, dissimilar" as an adjective, and "to differ, disagree" verbally — a straightforward negation compound, "not (the) same." It appears constantly as a general-purpose modifier ("a different X," "X differs") and pairs naturally with [[同一]] and [[大同小異]] as points along the same same/different spectrum.

Mandarin 不同 (bùtóng, "different"). Cantonese 不同 (bat1tung4). Japanese 不同 (ふどう, fudō) is a somewhat more formal/written register than the everyday 違う (chigau) or 異なる (kotonaru), showing up in set phrases like 順不同 (junfudō, "in no particular order," a disclaimer on Japanese lists). Vietnamese bất đồng ("disagreement, discord") has narrowed to a more specifically interpersonal sense than the Chinese/Japanese general "different" — bất đồng ý kiến ("difference of opinion") and bất đồng quan điểm ("disagreement in viewpoint") are the words' natural home, closer to English "disagreement" than plain "different."

Korean 부동 (budong) has a striking homophone collision: 不同 ("different, dissimilar") and 不動 ("immovable, fixed" — as in 부동산 budongsan, "real estate," literally "immovable property") are entirely different hanja compounds that happen to share the identical Sino-Korean reading, since 同 and 動 both read 동. Context alone disambiguates them in speech.
