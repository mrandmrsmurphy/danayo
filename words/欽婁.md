## Notes
- [欽](../characters/欽.md) + [婁](../characters/婁.md) — a Dan'a'yo-original phonetic coinage for Wales, chosen to approximate the Welsh endonym **Cymru** (/ˈkəm.rɪ/) in Dan'a'yo's own romanization: 欽 = *kum* (≈ "Cym-"), 婁 = *lu* (≈ "-ru," with Dan'a'yo's l/r merger standing in for Welsh r). This is unlike most place-name words in the vault, which transliterate a real Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, or Korean rendering of the name — no such CJKV-attested term exists for "Cymru" specifically (the standard Sino name, 威爾士, instead transliterates English "Wales"). The mandarin/cantonese/japanese/korean/vietnamese fields above give only the mechanical reading of the characters 欽婁 in each language's own system; none of them reflect real-world usage of this term for Wales.
