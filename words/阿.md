## Notes
- [阿 (char)](../characters/阿%20(char).md) "ah; name prefix"

阿 in Dan'a'yo serves a dual function: as an interjection (感詞) it is the exclamation "ah!" — a pure vocalization of surprise, recognition, or mild emphasis — and in written Dan'a'yo it serves as the determiner (限定詞) for personal names, appearing as a prefix that marks the following word as a proper name or familiar term of address: 阿明 (familiar address for someone named 明). The character is a 形声 compound — semantic 阜 ("hill; mound") + phonetic 可 (OC *kʰaːlʔ) — but its primary Dan'a'yo function as a name-marker is phonological rather than semantic: 阿 (ㄚ/a/아) is chosen because its syllable is an open, sonorous, unstressed prefix that carries no semantic weight. 阿 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[阿]].

Mandarin 阿 (ā) is primarily a phonetic prefix in names and kinship terms: 阿姨 (āyí, maternal aunt), 阿弟 (ādì, younger brother), 阿里 (Ālǐ, Ali — transliteration). Cantonese 阿 (aa3) is among the most productive colloquial syllables in the language — virtually all familiar kinship and address terms begin with 阿: 阿爸 (aa3 baa1, dad), 阿媽 (aa3 maa4, mom), 阿哥 (aa3 go1, big brother). Japanese 阿 (ア/A) appears primarily in Buddhist terms — 阿弥陀 (Amida, from Sanskrit Amitābha), 阿修羅 (Ashura) — and historical phonetic notation. Korean 아 (a) independently functions as the vocative suffix for names ending in a vowel — 철수아 (Cheolsu-a!) — a native Korean grammatical particle, not a Sino-Korean form. Vietnamese a: used as a phonetic syllable in Sino-Vietnamese transliterations — 阿彌陀 = A Di Đà (Amitābha Buddha).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 아 exactly matches Korean 아. 阿 reads 아 (a/ㄚ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 影母 (ʔ) + 歌韻 (ɑ) gives a glottal-stop (effectively null) initial with an open -a vowel. Both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean map MC 影母 to a null initial and MC 歌韻 (ɑ) to the plain -a vowel, producing identical 아 forms. This is one of the most phonologically straightforward kwin: true cases — an open syllable with no consonant and the most basic vowel, with no opportunity for divergence.
