## Notes
- [論](../characters/論.md) "theory, thesis" + [理](../characters/理.md) "reason, principle" — the reasoned principle behind discourse; logic.

論理 is itself a 和製漢語 (a Japanese-coined Sino-compound): 19th-century Japanese scholars built it to translate the Western concept of "logic" as a formal discipline, and it was subsequently borrowed into Chinese via Liang Qichao (梁啓超), who used it in his writing on Mohist logic. It remains the standard, everyday word for "logic" in Japanese (論理的, "logical") and Korean (논리, 논리적) alike.

Chinese later abandoned its own import: in 1917 the scholar Zhang Shizhao introduced 邏輯/逻辑 (luóji), a phonetic transliteration of the English word "logic" itself, arguing that a translated compound like 論理 couldn't capture the term's full technical sense. 逻辑 went on to become the standard modern Chinese term for the discipline, while 论理 retreated to a more colloquial, verb-like register — "to reason with someone," "to argue on principle" (跟他论论理), or "reasonably speaking" (论理她早该退休了) — rather than naming the field itself. So the same compound Japan lent to China as a technical term was later displaced there: still the ordinary word for "logic" in the language that coined it, but no longer quite that in the language that borrowed it.
