## Notes
- [差](../characters/差.md) "discriminate, differ" + [異](../characters/異.md) "different, strange" — the two roots for "differ/different" doubled up into the abstract noun "difference."

差異/さい/차이 all sit at the same everyday register — the ordinary word reached for whenever "difference" or "discrepancy" needs a noun rather than a verb (差 alone tends to be bound, needing a suffix like 差別 or 差額 to stand on its own). Vietnamese sai dị is the outlier: it's a real, correctly-formed Sino-Vietnamese compound used in formal, academic, and technical writing (mathematics, statistics, scientific reports), but everyday spoken Vietnamese reaches for the native compound khác biệt (or sự khác biệt) instead — sai dị would sound stiff or bookish in casual conversation the way "discrepancy" sounds stiffer than "difference" in English.
