## Notes
- [長 (char)](../characters/長%20(char).md) "senior, eldest, chief; long" + [上 (char)](../characters/上%20(char).md) "above, superior" — those who are senior and above; one's elders and superiors.

長上 collapses two axes of Confucian seniority into a single compound: 長 (temporal seniority — one who has lived longer, arrived first, holds office longer) and 上 (hierarchical position — one who stands above in rank or station). In practice, these axes align: the elder is typically also the superior, and the superior is typically also older. 長上 names the set of persons toward whom deference, care, and appropriate conduct (禮, ritual propriety) are owed in the Confucian social order.

The Confucian five relationships (五倫) define the structured social world: ruler and subject (君臣), parent and child (父子), husband and wife (夫婦), elder and younger sibling (兄弟), friend and friend (朋友). 長上 covers the senior pole of the first four — the ruler, the parent, the husband (in classical hierarchy), the elder sibling — all of whom hold an obligation to guide and protect in return for the deference they receive. The fifth relationship, friendship (朋友), is the only horizontal one; it has no 長上 and no junior.

Korean 윗사람 (윗 = upper + 사람 = person) is the native Korean semantic equivalent: entirely native vocabulary achieving the same meaning as the Sino-Korean 장상 (長上). Its use here in the frontmatter reflects how Korean often prefers native vocabulary for core social concepts even when a Sino-Korean form exists.
