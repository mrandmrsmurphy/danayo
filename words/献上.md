## Notes
- [献](../characters/献.md) "offer, present" + [上](../characters/上%20(char).md) "up, above"
- This word is the stand-in for [[献]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- To present something upward — to a superior, ruler, or deity. In Japanese, けんじょう (kenjō) is a formal humble term for gifting to someone of higher status; 献上品 (kenjōhin) is a "tribute item" or offering gift. Chinese 献上 (xiànshàng) carries the same directional meaning of presenting upward, distinguishing this act from equal-to-equal giving. Korean 헌상 (獻上) appears in historical contexts for tributary gifts presented to the king or court; 현상 (懸賞 "prize money") is a homophone but unrelated. Vietnamese hiến thượng maintains the Sino-Vietnamese reading; the everyday term for an offering is cúng dường (from Sanskrit).
