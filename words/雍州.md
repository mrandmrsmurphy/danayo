## Notes
- [雍](../characters/雍.md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Yong Province, the ninth of the [[九州]]

雍州 was the northwestern province — roughly modern Shaanxi, Gansu, and parts of Ningxia — the Wei River valley and the loess plateau. It was the homeland of the state of Qin (秦), the state that ultimately conquered all other states to unify China in 221 BC. The Wei River valley around modern Xi'an (長安) became the imperial heartland of the Qin and Han dynasties; 雍州 thus transitions in Chinese history from a frontier zone at the edge of the civilized world to the seat of empire. The westernmost of the nine provinces, it also formed the gateway to Central Asia and the Silk Road.
