## Notes
- [興 (char)](../characters/興%20(char).md) "entertain; rise" + [旺](../characters/旺.md) "flourish; prosperous"
- This word is the viable form of [旺](../characters/旺.md): the character's meaning (flourishing; prosperous) is identical, but 旺 alone is too bounded to stand as a Dan'a'yo word. 興旺 is its `stand_in` compound.

The pairing is semantically reinforcing: 興 carries the sense of something rising, gathering momentum, coming into prominence, while 旺 adds the idea of full bloom and peak vitality. Together they describe an entity — a business, a city, a dynasty, a lineage — that is not merely doing well but actively surging.

興旺 is heavily at home in commercial and civic language. A shop might be said to be 生意興旺 ("business thriving"), a neighbourhood 人丁興旺 ("population flourishing"), a royal house 國運興旺 ("national fortunes prospering"). The compound appears frequently in Lunar New Year greetings and auspicious couplets across the Sinosphere, where invoking prosperity is itself a performative act.

The simplified Chinese form is 兴旺 (兴 being the simplified rendering of 興). In Japanese the word is less common in modern usage than its Chinese range, but it is found in formal or literary contexts with the on-yomi reading こうおう (KOU + OU).
