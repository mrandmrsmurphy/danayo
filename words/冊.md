## Notes
- [冊 (char)](../characters/冊%20(char).md) "bound volumes"

冊 is the Dan'a'yo classifier for bound volumes — 量詞, counting books, booklets, notebooks, and any bound collection of written material: 一冊書 (one book), 三冊日記 (three diaries), 數冊文典 (several grammar books). The character itself is a 象形 pictograph of bamboo slips (木牘) strung together with a cord — the vertical strokes of 冊 directly represent the individual slips, the horizontal stroke the binding cord. This was the original form of the Chinese book before paper, and the etymological link between the physical object and the classifier is preserved: 冊 counts the bound collection as a discrete, complete unit. 冊 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[冊]].

The CJKV classifiers for books show considerable variety. Mandarin 本 (běn, "root; origin; copy") is the most common classifier for books: 一本书 (one book), 一本杂志 (one magazine); 册 (cè) is used in more formal or literary contexts: 一册 (one volume). Cantonese 本 (bun2) parallels Mandarin for everyday books. Japanese 冊 (さつ/SATSU) is the standard written-language counter for books and bound volumes: 一冊の本 (one book), 三冊 (three volumes); 本 (ほん/HON) also counts long, slender objects and colloquially, books. Korean does not use a Sino-Korean 책 form as a classifier — instead 권 (卷, gwon, from 卷 "roll; volume") is the standard counter: 책 한 권 (one book). Vietnamese cuốn ("roll; volume") is the standard classifier for books: một cuốn sách (one book); Sino-Vietnamese sách (冊) functions more as a noun meaning "book" than as a classifier.

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 착 does not match Korean 책. 冊 reads 착 (cag/ㄑㄚㄎ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 初母 (t͡ʃʰ) + 麥開韻 (ɣɛk) gives a palatal affricate onset with the -ak coda. Dan'a'yo maps the MC 麥韻 to plain -ak (착), while Sino-Korean 책 (chaek) maps the same MC final to -aek, retaining a front-vowel grade. The MC 麥開韻 produced ㄚㄎ (-ak/착) in Dan'a'yo but -aek (책) in Korean — a regular vocalic divergence from the same MC source.
