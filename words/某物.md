## Notes
- [某 (char)](../characters/某%20(char).md) "some; a certain; indefinite" + [物 (char)](../characters/物%20(char).md) "thing; object; material result"

某物 is the indefinite correlative in the object column: it refers to a thing deliberately left unnamed — the speaker either does not know what it is, or knows but declines to specify. Where 何物 asks "what thing?" (interrogative, seeking identification), 某物 says "a certain thing" (indefinite, withholding identification). The character 某 is the classical Chinese indefinite determiner, productive across temporal (某日 "a certain day"), spatial (某処 "some place"), personal (某人 "a certain person"), and now object (某物 "a certain thing") domains.

In Mandarin, 某物 (mǒuwù) appears in formal and technical writing — legal documents, academic prose, operational instructions — where a referent must be acknowledged without being named: 因某物遮擋 ("blocked by a certain object"). Japanese 某物 (ぼうぶつ) is archaic; the more common formal equivalent is 某のもの (certain thing/property). Korean 어떤 것 (eotteon geot, "a certain thing") is the natural spoken equivalent; the Sino-Korean 某物 (모물) would be understood but is not used. Vietnamese *một vật nào đó* (some object or other) is the analytic equivalent.

Semantically, 某物 occupies the deliberate-vagueness niche alongside 某事 (some matter), 某人 (some person), and 某処 (some place): together they form the indefinite column of the correlative table, covering the full ontological range of unknowns-by-choice. The column is useful not only for genuine uncertainty but for rhetorical or legal anonymisation — referring to a thing whose identity is known to the speaker but strategically suppressed. This distinguishes 某物 from both 彼物 (a known distal referent) and 何物 (a genuinely unknown referent the speaker is asking about).
