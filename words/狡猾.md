## Notes
- [狡](../characters/狡.md) "cunning, sly" + [滑 (char)](../characters/滑%20(char).md) "slippery" — this word is the stand-in for [狡](../characters/狡.md), which cannot appear independently in Dan'a'yo.

狡猾 is a synonym-doubling compound: both halves independently mean "crafty, sly, cunning," reinforcing rather than combining two distinct senses — the same shape as many two-character adjective compounds in this register. Applied to people or animals (classically, especially the fox and the hare, both proverbial tricksters across the CJKV sphere), it carries a squarely negative charge: shrewdness turned toward deception rather than mere cleverness.

**A genuine graphemic curiosity sits underneath this word.** The second character's own written form here — 猾, "crafty" — has no separate character page in this vault; it is recorded as an alias of [滑](../characters/滑%20(char).md), "slippery," which does. This isn't a mistake to correct: 猾 and 滑 both derive from the same phonetic component 骨 and share the exact same Middle Chinese reading (户八切, 匣母鎋韻合口), making them true homophones with completely unrelated meanings — structurally the same relationship as this vault's own [[且]]/[[処]] pair, just resolved differently: 且/処 got two separate character entries sharing one reading, while 猾/滑 were folded into a single entry. Per this vault's own convention, when a character is registered as an alias, the parent form is the real entry and the alias itself isn't used independently — so this word's own `characters:` frontmatter lists [滑 (char)](../characters/滑%20(char).md), not a separate 猾 page, even though the word is conventionally written and read here as 狡猾, not 狡滑.

Mandarin (jiǎohuá), Cantonese (gaau2 waat6), Japanese (kōkatsu), Korean (gyohwal), and Vietnamese (giảo hoạt) all preserve the compound transparently and regularly, with every syllable a clean concatenation of the two characters' own independent readings — no fused or irregular reading, unlike some doubled-adjective compounds elsewhere in this vault.
