## Notes
- [亥](../characters/亥.md) "the twelfth Earthly Branch; the Pig" + [月 (char)](../characters/月%20(char).md) "month" — the month of the Pig; the tenth month.

亥月 is the tenth month of the traditional Chinese lunisolar calendar, named for 亥 (hài), the twelfth and final of the Twelve Earthly Branches (十二地支). The apparent mismatch — twelfth branch, tenth month — arises because the traditional year begins in 寅月 (the Tiger month, first month of spring), making 亥 the tenth month counted from that starting point.

亥 corresponds to the Pig (豬/猪) in the zodiac, and to the double-hour 21:00–23:00 in the daily cycle, and to the direction north-northwest. It is the closing branch of the cycle, completing the journey from 子 (Rat, the first branch) through to 亥 (Pig, the twelfth) — after which 子 begins again.

The pig's symbolic resonance suits a month in early winter (approximately November in the solar calendar): the harvest is secured and stored, the fields are at rest, and abundance is available. Pigs in Chinese agricultural culture were closely associated with household wealth — the character 家 (home, family) depicts a pig under a roof — and with contented ease. 亥月 is thus the month when the year's stored energy comes to rest, before the deep cold of 子月 and 冬月.
