## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [人 (char)](../characters/人%20(char).md) "person; human being"

此人 is the proximal correlative in the person column, picking out the person nearest the speaker — "this person here." In classical Chinese, 此人 appears naturally as a compact phrase for immediate reference to a person present in context (此人可用 "this person is useful"; 此人非善 "this person is not virtuous"). Dan'a'yo adopts it as the proximal human deictic, parallel to 此物 (this thing) and 此事 (this matter) in the other semantic rows.

The nearest cross-linguistic equivalents are: Mandarin 此人 (cǐrén), used in formal and literary prose (此人我識 "I know this person"); Japanese この人 (kono hito), the standard proximal person demonstrative in spoken Japanese (この人は誰ですか "Who is this person?"), with この方 (kono kata) as the polite register; Korean 이 사람 (i saram) as the neutral proximal person phrase. Unlike 其人, which also serves as a gender-neutral third-person pronoun, 此人 is primarily a deictic pointing to someone present or immediately in focus — not a substitute for a personal pronoun in narrative contexts.

The 人 row is notable for using 誰 rather than 何人 in the interrogative column (see [[誰]]). The grammar file notes that 何人 (*hanin*) exists but is rude; 何名 is very polite. This means the 人 row has a built-in register distinction absent from other rows: 誰 (neutral) / 何人 (rude) / 何名 (polite) for the "who?" function. 此人 itself is stylistically neutral — it does not carry the honorific weight of 此名 (this distinguished person) nor the informality of a bare demonstrative pronoun.
