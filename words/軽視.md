## Notes
- [軽 (char)](../characters/軽%20(char).md) "light, trivial" + [視 (char)](../characters/視%20(char).md) "regard, look upon" — to look down on; to regard as insignificant; to underestimate the worth or danger of something.

軽視 occupies a register between contemptuous dismissal (蔑視, 侮視) and deliberate inattention (無視). The compound implies an active misjudgment of worth — treating something as less important than it deserves — rather than malicious contempt. In legal, policy, and safety discourse, 軽視 frequently appears in warnings against undervaluing risks: 安全を軽視する (to take safety lightly), 人命を軽視する態度 (an attitude that regards human life as cheap). The gravity of the accusation scales with what is being undervalued — 軽視 of a minor preference is ordinary carelessness; 軽視 of human dignity or constitutional rights rises to a serious charge.

Korean 경시 carries identical weight and appears in the same collocations: 생명을 경시하는 풍조 (a social tendency to regard life lightly). Chinese 轻视 is the standard Mandarin equivalent. Vietnamese *khinh thị* follows the Sino-Vietnamese form, with *khinh* (軽) retaining its sense of contempt or dismissiveness.
