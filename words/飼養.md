## Notes
- [飼](../characters/飼.md) "raise, rear" + [養](../characters/養.md) "foster, raise" — stand-in for [[飼]], which cannot appear independently.

Vietnamese avoids the literal Sino-Vietnamese reading of this compound for a good reason: 飼 alone reads tự, but "tự dưỡng" is already a real, different Vietnamese word — historically 廝養 (a different pair of characters entirely, "servant"), and in modern usage a term for "self-care, self-cultivation." Rather than collide with that existing word, Vietnamese uses the hybrid nuôi dưỡng (native nuôi, "to raise/feed" + Sino dưỡng, "to nourish") for animal-rearing specifically, or the fully native chăn nuôi for animal husbandry as an activity/industry. Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean all keep the fully Sino compound intact with no such collision (sìyǎng/zi6 joeng5/しよう/사양).
