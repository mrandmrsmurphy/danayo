## Notes
- [右](../characters/右.md) "right" + [学](../characters/学.md) "learn" — the Right School, the higher of a pair of state schools recorded in Shang-dynasty education; see [[左学]] for the *Book of Rites* source and the pairing's overall structure. 右學 cared for and educated the state elders (國老) — the hereditary, ennobled class — and centered on instruction in ritual and music (禮樂), making it the functional predecessor of the later [[太学]] tier of education.

The school's name reflects Shang cosmological convention rather than simple geography: the Shang dynasty revered the right and treated west as the cardinal direction corresponding to "right," so the Right School was sited in the western suburb of the capital and was known interchangeably as 西學 ("Western School") and 瞽宗 ("ancestral hall of the blind [musicians]"), the latter name reflecting the historical association of blind musicians with the transmission of ritual music in early Chinese court culture. Its status as the senior of the two schools, reserved for the nobility, anticipates the social stratification of education that persisted through the [[小学]]/[[太学]] and even the Tang Six Schools system (see [[律学]]) many centuries later.
