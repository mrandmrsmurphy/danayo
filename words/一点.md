## Notes
- [一 (char)](../characters/一%20(char).md) "one" + [点 (char)](../characters/点%20(char).md) "point" — an independent compound; neither character's own `stand_in` points here (both are bare self-standing characters)

**Real content bug corrected**: the stored `english` gloss was "dot, speck, spot" — the literal, compositional reading — but as an HSK-1 word, 一点/一點's overwhelmingly primary sense (verified via search) is the quantifier "a little, a bit" (一点儿水, "a little water"; 便宜一点儿, "a little cheaper"), used constantly in everyday speech before nouns and after adjectives. The literal "a (single) point/dot" sense does exist (这一点, "this point [of an argument]") but is the secondary, more formal/analytical usage. Corrected the gloss to the primary quantifier sense.

Japanese いってん and Korean 일점 both exist as literal compositional readings ("one point"), but neither functions as the everyday "a little" quantifier the way Mandarin/Cantonese 一点/一点儿 does — Japanese uses native 少し (sukoshi) and Korean uses native 조금 (jogeum) for "a little" instead, reserving いってん/일점 for the literal "a point/dot" sense. Vietnamese nhất điểm is attested with the same "a little bit" calque sense (verified via search), though everyday Vietnamese strongly prefers the native một chút/một ít.
