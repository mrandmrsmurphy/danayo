## Notes
- [普](../characters/普.md) "universal; common; everywhere" + [及 (char)](../characters/及%20(char).md) "reach out to"

To spread widely; to reach everywhere; to become universal or standard. The compound means "reaching all places" — 普 (universal, all-encompassing, reaching every direction) + 及 (to reach, to arrive at). Where [[波及]] describes the involuntary spread of effects like ripples, 普及 describes the intentional or welcome spread of something beneficial: a technology, a practice, a body of knowledge, or a right — arriving until it is everywhere.

**普及率** (fukyūritsu in Japanese, pǔjílǜ in Chinese) — penetration rate; adoption rate — is standard vocabulary in market research and technology diffusion analysis. 스마트폰 普及率 (smartphone penetration rate), テレビの普及率 (television adoption rate), インターネット普及率 (internet penetration rate) all measure how thoroughly a technology has spread through a population. These statistics are central to development economics: countries measure 普及率 to track progress in education, healthcare access, financial inclusion, and connectivity.

In Chinese, 普及 has been central to modernization policy discourse for over a century. The phrase **普及教育** (universal education) and **普及医療** (universal healthcare) frame 普及 as a state obligation — the responsibility to ensure that beneficial goods reach everyone rather than only the privileged. Mao-era campaigns explicitly used 普及 for mass literacy, public health vaccination, and technological education as tools of social transformation.

**普及版** (fukyūban / pǔjíbǎn) — a "popular edition" of a book, cheaper and more widely distributed than a deluxe or collected edition. **普及型** — "standard type; mass-market model" — describes consumer products designed for broad adoption. In both cases 普及 marks a deliberate design choice to reach many rather than few.

The contrast with [[波及]] is illuminating and built into the words themselves: 波及 (wave-reaching) implies spread that propagates outward whether one wills it or not — the natural physics of consequences. 普及 (universal-reaching) implies spread aimed at comprehensiveness — deliberate, aspirational, and measured by how much of the population or territory has been reached.
