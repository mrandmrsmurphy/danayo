## Notes
- [樹](../characters/樹.md) "plant" + [袋 (char)](../characters/袋%20(char).md) "sack, bag" + [熊 (char)](../characters/熊%20(char).md) "bear"

樹袋熊 "tree bag-bear" is the koala — built on the same "bag-bear" pattern as [[袋熊]] "wombat," but with 樹 "tree" added to mark the arboreal animal as distinct from its burrowing cousin. The native Mandarin and Cantonese readings (shùdàixióng / syu6 doi6 hung4) follow this same literal "tree-bag-bear" construction, while Japanese and Korean instead use loanwords from English (コアラ, 코알라) rather than calquing the Chinese compound. The alias 子守熊 "child-minding bear" is a common Japanese-origin name referencing how koala mothers carry their joeys; 袋熊, listed here as a secondary alias, properly belongs to the wombat but is sometimes loosely applied to the koala as well — see [[袋熊]] for the disambiguation between the two.
