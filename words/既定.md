## Notes
- [既](../characters/既.md) "already, previously" + [定](../characters/定.md) "decided, fixed" — already decided; established before the present discussion; predetermined

既定 names what has already been settled and is therefore not available for renegotiation. 既定事実 (established fact — something that has already happened and must be accepted), 既定方針 (established policy — a course already fixed), 既定路線 (predetermined track — an outcome that was always going to happen) are the most common compounds. The word carries a note of finality: invoking 既定 is a way of closing off further debate by appealing to what was decided before the current conversation began.

In legal and administrative language, 既定 contrasts with 未定 (undecided, yet to be determined) and 暫定 (provisional, temporarily fixed). The space between these three terms maps the lifecycle of a decision: 未定 (not yet decided) → 暫定 (provisionally decided) → 既定 (definitively and previously decided).
