## Notes
- [[徘]] + [[徊]] — a classic 聯綿詞 (binome): neither character means anything on its own, and the two exist solely to spell this one word, "to pace back and forth, to wander without direction." Both halves share the same graphemic logic (semantic [[Radical 060|彳]] "step, walk" + a phonetic component — 非 for 徘, 回 for 徊) and the same fate: bound forever to each other, the same pattern seen in [[蜘蛛]] (spider) and [[逍遥]] (carefree wandering).

Attested across Mandarin 徘徊 (páihuái), Japanese 徘徊 (はいかい, haikai — notably the clinical term for dementia-related wandering), and Korean 배회 (baehoe). Vietnamese bồi hồi is the same etymological compound, though its meaning has drifted in the modern language from literal pacing to a restless, anxious, or nostalgic feeling — the semantic shift from physical wandering to an inner, emotional unsettledness.
