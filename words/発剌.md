## Notes
- [発 (char)](../characters/発%20(char).md) "to issue forth, to discharge" + [剌](../characters/剌.md) "lively"

発剌 means "lively, vivacious, full of energy" — most familiar from its Japanese reading はつらつ (hatsuratsu), as in 元気溌剌 ("brimming with energy"). The compound is built here on the canonical character 発, of which 溌 (the form that appears in the word's most common spelling, 溌剌) is one of an unusually large family of aliases — alongside 发, 發, 醗, 醱, 潑, 撥, and others — all converging on 発's core sense of "to issue forth, to send out, to discharge" (cf. [[発電]], "to generate power," and [[爆発]], "to explode," both cross-linked from [[発 (char)]]'s Words section). 剌, whose only other role in the vault is as the stand_in component of this very word, contributes a sense of vigorous, snapping motion — together painting an image of energy bursting forth.

発剌 is a useful test case for the alias-parent rule's limits: while the Japanese reading はつらつ and Korean reading 발랄 are both straightforwardly compositional (発 ハツ/발 + 剌 ラツ/랄), the Mandarin and Cantonese readings of the word's most common spelling, 潑剌 (pōlà, put3 laat6), diverge from 発's own frontmatter readings (fā, faat3) — in this specific compound, 潑 is read with its older "pō/put" pronunciation rather than the "fā/faat" pronunciation that 発 itself carries in modern standard usage. This is the same kind of divergence noted for [[勉強]] earlier in the vault's word-creation work: a single written form can carry different spoken readings depending on which compound it appears in, even when one character is nominally just an alias of another.

The word's three recorded aliases — 溌剌 (the most common modern Japanese spelling), 撥剌 (an older literary variant, found for instance in classical poetry describing fish leaping vigorously from water), and 潑剌 (the form whose Mandarin/Cantonese readings are given above) — all trace back to the same root image of something snapping or springing forth with sudden vigor, whether that something is a fish, a flame, or a person's spirits.
