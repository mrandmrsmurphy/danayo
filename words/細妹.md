## Notes
- [細 (char)](../characters/細%20(char).md) "fine, thin, slender" + [妹](../characters/妹.md) "younger sister" — stand-in for [[妹]], which cannot appear independently (its own separate stand-in, [[妹妹]], is a different, more standard word for the same core meaning).

細妹 is not really an ordinary standard-Mandarin word — it belongs to Cantonese, where sai3 mui6 (the second syllable's tone shifting from 6 to 2 in casual/affectionate address, a real, documented Cantonese tone-change pattern) is the everyday colloquial term for "younger sister," alongside the more formal 妹妹. Hakka broadens the same two characters further: 細妹（仔） in Hakka means "girl, young woman" generally, not restricted to "younger sister" specifically — a genuine semantic widening between two Sinitic varieties using the identical compound. Japanese いもうと, Korean 여동생, and Vietnamese em gái are all just the plain, native words for "younger sister" in their own languages, with no special connection to 細妹 as a lexical item.
