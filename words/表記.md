## Notes
- [表](../characters/表.md) "to express; to represent; to show outwardly" + [記](../characters/記.md) "to record; to write down; a written record"

表記 is "notation; orthography; written representation" — the act or system of representing something in written symbols. 表 (to express, to make visible, to represent outwardly) + 記 (to record in writing, to note down). The compound denotes both the process (representing sound or meaning in written form) and the resulting system (an orthographic or notational convention): 音の表記 ("notation of sounds"), 仮名表記 ("kana orthography"), 表記の統一 ("standardisation of notation").

Mandarin 表記 (biǎojì) appears in formal and technical writing for notation and representation. Japanese 表記 (ひょうき, hyōki) is the standard term for orthography and notation — particularly prominent in Japanese linguistics, publishing, and editorial work: 表記の揺れ (orthographic variation), 表記基準 (notation standards), 外来語の表記 (notation/orthography of loanwords). Korean 표기 (pyogi) is the standard linguistic term: 한글 표기 (Hangul orthography), 외래어 표기법 (rules for transcribing foreign words), 발음 표기 (phonetic notation). Vietnamese ký hiệu (symbol, notation) is the closest general equivalent.

表記 is directly relevant to the Dan'a'yo project: the vault uses multiple notation systems for each entry — 注音 (Bopomofo phonetic notation), 羅馬字 (Roman-letter transcription), and 諺文 (Hangul phonetic transcription). Each is a 表記 system: a standardised written representation of Dan'a'yo's phonological inventory. The simplified alias 表记 retains the simplified-character form of 記 for cross-referencing with mainland Chinese sources.
