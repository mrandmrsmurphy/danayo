## Notes
- [事 (char)](../characters/事%20(char).md) "thing; action"

事 is "thing; action" — in Dan'a'yo, 事 refers to matters, affairs, and happenings rather than concrete objects. As a correlative row heading, 事 generates 此事 (this matter), 其事 (that matter), 何事 (what matter; what happened?), 毎事 (every matter). The systematic contrast with 物 (thing; object) is foundational: 食事 (the act of eating, eating-as-event) vs. 食物 (food; that which is eaten, eating-as-object). 事 covers the processual, abstract, or situational — the happening rather than the thing produced. As a grammatical particle, 事 also marks the topic as an action in the topic-retaining relational system. 事 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[事]].

Mandarin 事 (shì): 事情 (shìqíng, matter; affair), 事件 (shìjiàn, incident; event), 什么事 (what's the matter?), 没事 (it's nothing; never mind), 国事 (affairs of state). Among the most frequent Mandarin characters. Cantonese 事 (si6): 咩事 (mē si6, what's the matter?), 好事 (hou2 si6, good deed). Japanese 事 (こと, koto) is essential for nominalization in Japanese grammar: 食べること (the act of eating; eating), 好きなこと (what one likes; things one is fond of). The Sino-Japanese reading じ (ji) appears in compounds: 事務 (じむ, office work), 事実 (じじつ, fact), 事情 (じじょう, circumstances). Korean 사 (事) in Sino-Korean: 사건 (事件, incident; case), 사무 (事務, office work). Vietnamese sự (事): sự việc (matter; affair), sự thật (truth; fact).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 지 differs from Korean 사. 事 reads 지 (ji/ㄐㄧ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 崇母 (d͡ʒ) + 之韻 (ɨ) gives Dan'a'yo the palatal affricate ㄐ (j-) with a schwa-like -i vowel — while Sino-Korean gives 사 (sa), where the MC voiced retroflex affricate devoices and the vowel shifts to -a. Both the consonant manner and the vowel quality diverge between the two systems.
