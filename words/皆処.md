## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [処 (char)](../characters/処%20(char).md) "place; location"

皆処 is the universal place correlative — "everywhere; at all locations." The character 皆 marks collective universality across the entire set of places, contrasting with the distributive 毎処 (at each place, one by one). 皆処 says "no matter where you look, at every location collectively" — a blanket spatial assertion rather than a location-by-location distribution. In the correlative table's spatial column, 皆処 is the broadest possible place reference: all places, every location.

Mandarin 到處 (dàochù, lit. "reaching everywhere") is the most common equivalent, with the directional 到 (reach, arrive at) adding a sense of pervasiveness or movement into all spaces: 到處都是人 ("everywhere there are people"); 找遍到處 ("searched everywhere"). Cantonese 到處 (dou3 cyu3) is similarly idiomatic. Japanese どこでも (dokodemo, "wherever; everywhere; anywhere") uses the place interrogative どこ + the universal particle でも: どこでもドア ("anywhere door," the Doraemon gadget) has made this compound culturally iconic. Korean 어디에나 (eodi-e-na, "anywhere; everywhere") uses the interrogative 어디 + universal -에나.

The 処 row contributes to a productive spatial-temporal parallelism in the Dan'a'yo correlative table: 処 (place) and 時 (time) are the two frame-setting rows that operate as adverbial nominals without case particles — 此処 and 此時 both set the spatial and temporal frame of a predicate. The 皆処/皆時 pair ("everywhere/always") and the 毎処/毎時 pair ("at each place/at each time") make the distributive and universal quantification over space and time neatly parallel and compositionally transparent.
