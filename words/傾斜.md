## Notes
- [傾](../characters/傾.md) "lean; incline" + [斜](../characters/斜.md) "slant; tilt" — a synonym compound for slope or inclination; stand-in for [[斜]], which cannot appear independently

**傾斜** (*qīngxié*) is the standard term for a slope, gradient, or angle of inclination — any surface or line that deviates from the horizontal or vertical. The compound joins two near-synonyms: 傾 (to lean toward something) and 斜 (to be aslant, off-axis), together naming the measurable fact of obliqueness.

In **mathematics and engineering**, 傾斜 refers to the gradient or grade of a surface: the rise over run, expressed as a ratio, percentage, or angle. 傾斜角 (*keishakaku*, angle of inclination) is the angle between a slope and the horizontal plane. 傾斜計 (*keishakei*) is an inclinometer — the instrument that measures this angle. In **geology**, 傾斜 (dip) describes the angle at which a geological stratum or fault plane is inclined from the horizontal, paired with 走向 (strike, the horizontal direction of the same plane) to fully characterise the orientation of a rock formation.

In **civil engineering and transportation**, 傾斜 governs road and rail design: a gradient of 傾斜 that is too steep reduces traction, increases braking distance, and challenges vehicle performance. The Shinkansen network's strict gradient limits — typically under 15‰ (1.5%) on main lines — required extensive tunnelling through Japan's mountainous terrain. 傾斜地 (*keishachi*, sloping land) is a category of terrain classification in Japanese land surveying.

The word is primarily physical and quantitative. For abstract inclination — a tendency toward something, a predisposition — the more common word is [[傾向]] (*qīngxiàng*, trend, tendency), which uses 傾 but replaces 斜 with 向 (direction). The two words share the 傾 component but diverge: 傾斜 is a concrete angle, 傾向 is an abstract drift.

Japanese **けいしゃ** (*keisha*) is the standard term across engineering, geography, and everyday usage. Korean **경사** (*gyeongsa*) functions similarly; it also appears in the fixed phrase 경사가 지다 ("to be on a slope, to slope"). Vietnamese **khuynh tà** is the Sino-Vietnamese compound used in technical and formal writing; everyday Vietnamese uses *độ dốc* ("degree of steepness") for gradient.
