## Notes
**渇金** is a binomial neologism for the chemical element **Tantalum** (Atomic Number: 73). It is a mythological and functional calque that translates the story of [Tantalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tantalus) into a CJKV conceptual framework.

### 1. The Mythological Calque: The "Thirsty" Metal

The name **[Tantalum](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tantalum#Etymology)** was chosen by its discoverer, [Anders Ekeberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anders_Gustaf_Ekeberg), because the oxide of the metal was "unable to absorb acid even when immersed in it."

- **The Allusion:** He compared this to the Greek myth of [Tantalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tantalus), who was cursed to stand in water that receded whenever he tried to drink.
- **The Translation:** By using **渇** (Mandarin: _kě_; Japanese: _katsu_; Korean: _gal_; Vietnamese: _khát_), we are referencing that "eternal thirst." **渇金** literally translates to "Thirsty Metal," perfectly mirroring the etymology.

### 2. Physical Property: The Refractory King
Tantalum is famous for being **chemically inert** and having an incredibly high [melting point](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tantalum#Physical_properties) ($3017°C$).

- **The Logic:** Like Tantalus, who could never "reach" the water or fruit, Tantalum "refuses" to react with most acids or melt under standard high-heat conditions. It is a metal that remains "unsatisfied" and unchanged by its environment.

### 3. Single-Character Symbol: 渇 (K)
As a symbol in formulas, **渇** (Thirst) provides a unique and visually complex marker:

- **The Symbol:** **渇**
- **The Formula:** Tantalum Pentoxide ($Ta_2O_5$) is rendered as **$渇_二養_五$** (Thirst (x2) + Nourishing (x5)). This beautifully describes a molecule that is "twice thirsty" and "five-times nourished" yet remains chemically stable.

### 4. Why it beats the phonetic **钽** (_tǎn_)
- **Official (**钽**):** A modern phonetic character created solely to sound like the first syllable of Tantalum. It has no meaning.
- **Neologism (**渇金**):** It tells the story of the element's discovery and its primary chemical trait (acid resistance) in a single word.
