## Notes
- [嬉](../characters/嬉.md) "enjoy; play; amuse oneself" + [遊](../characters/遊.md) "play; roam; travel"
- This word is the stand-in for [[嬉]], which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.
- A compound of near-synonyms evoking carefree, pleasurable wandering. In classical Chinese and literary Japanese, 嬉遊 (kiyū) describes the roaming play of leisure — not structured game but joyful wandering enjoyment. The character 嬉 in Japanese native vocabulary gives うれしい (ureshii, "happy, glad"), reflecting the emotional warmth underlying the play sense. 遊 adds movement — roaming and traveling — giving the compound the flavor of a pleasure excursion or festive outing. Korean 희유 and Vietnamese hi du are the Sino-Vietnamese readings; both languages more commonly express "play" with native vocabulary.
