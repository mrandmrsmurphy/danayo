## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [安](../characters/安.md) "peaceful; at ease" — the negation of 安; an inner state of restlessness, unease, or dread; contrast [[不穏]], which describes an external situation as ominous

**不安** (*bù'ān*) is the standard term for anxiety, unease, and psychological insecurity across the CJKV sphere. Where 安 (peaceful, at rest) implies that a person has found their proper place — sheltered, settled, undisturbed — 不安 is the failure of that settling: the mind that cannot rest, the person who cannot find their footing. The contrast with [[不穏]] is structural: 不穏 describes an external atmosphere as threatening or ominous — the situation is 不穏; 不安 describes an internal state — the person is 不安. The two often co-occur: an 不穏な情勢 ("ominous political situation") produces 不安 in those living through it.

In **Chinese**, 不安 (*bù'ān*) covers a range from mild discomfort to acute distress. The four-character compound **坐立不安** ("unable to sit or stand still") captures its most visceral sense: fidgeting, restless, agitated beyond the ability to settle in any position. 不安 also appears in formulaic social language: 讓您不安了 ("sorry to have caused you unease") functions as an apology for inconvenience or imposition. The clinical language of anxiety disorders uses the extended compounds 焦慮 (*jiāolǜ*) or 不安症 in preference to bare 不安, though the latter remains the common literary and everyday word.

In **Japanese**, 不安 (*fuan*) is among the most common and versatile words in the emotional vocabulary. It covers everything from the everyday anxious thought — 試験が不安だ ("I'm anxious about the exam") — to the existential condition of groundlessness. Japanese 20th-century intellectual culture, deeply engaged with German existentialism, used 不安 as the standard translation for Heidegger's *Angst* and Kierkegaard's *Angest* — the fundamental mood of a being thrown into a world without guaranteed meaning or stable ground. 不安障害 (*fuan shōgai*, anxiety disorder) is the clinical term in psychiatry. The compound 不安定 (*fuantei*, unstable, precarious) extends the meaning toward structural instability, making the 安/安定 pair a productive stem in both 不安 and 不安定.

In **Korean**, 불안 (*buran*) is equally standard across clinical, literary, and everyday registers: 불안감 (feeling of anxiety), 불안장애 (anxiety disorder), 불안하다 (to feel anxious, to be ill at ease). The word carries no particular political charge — that role is reserved for [[不穏|불온]] — and functions as the neutral psychological term for the state of inner unease.

In **Vietnamese**, **bất an** ("not at peace") is used for both personal unease and social insecurity or unrest. It appears in political and journalistic contexts — tình trạng bất an xã hội ("a state of social unrest") — as well as in the introspective register of literature. Its range thus overlaps somewhat with both 不安 (inner unease) and [[不穏]] (ominous external atmosphere), the distinction being carried more by context than by the word's form.
