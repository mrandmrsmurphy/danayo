## Notes

**火紅素** is a binomial neologism for the chemical element **Strontium** (Atomic Number: 38). It replaces the phonetic transcription **锶** (_sī_) with a descriptor that focuses on the element’s iconic [incandescent properties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strontium#History) and its historical association with pyrotechnics.

### 1. The Physical Property: The Crimson Flame
Strontium is most easily identified in a laboratory setting by its [flame test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flame_test), which produces a brilliant, saturated crimson red.

- **The Name:** By using **[[火紅]]** (_huǒhóng_), literally "Fire Red," you are describing the exact hue used in [emergency flares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strontium#Applications) and fireworks.
- **The Logic:** Unlike the biological/botanical red of **茜素** (Rubidium), **火紅素** identifies Strontium as the "Red of the Flame." It is a functional, visual descriptor that is immediately intuitive to anyone who has seen a firework display.

### 2. Etymological Contrast
The name **[Strontium](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/strontium)** is derived from the Scottish village of [Strontian](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/strontium#Etymology), where it was first discovered in a lead mine.

- **The Shift:** While the Western name is purely geographic, your neologism is **phenomenological**. It prioritizes what the element _does_ over where it was _found_. This aligns with Dan'a'yo's goal of high "guessability"—the name explains the element's most famous use.

### 3. Single-Character Symbol: 火 (F)
As a symbol for the periodic table, **[火 (char)](../characters/火%20(char).md)** (Fire) is one of the most recognizable characters in the entire Sinosphere.  With [紅 (char)](../characters/紅%20(char).md), it means "firework" but is alone in chemical formulas.
- **Visual Power:** It is simple, symmetrical, and powerful.    
- **Mnemonic:** It acts as a permanent reminder that this element is the "Fire" element of the alkaline earth metals.
