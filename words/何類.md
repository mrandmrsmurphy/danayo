## Notes
- [何 (char)](../characters/何%20(char).md) "what; which; interrogative" + [類](../characters/類.md) "kind; sort; category; type"

何類 is the interrogative kind/category correlative — "what kind; what sort; what type?" As a 代詞, it questions the category or class of a referent: 何類の食べ物が好きですか ("what kind of food do you like?"); 何類に属するか ("what category does it belong to?"). Like all 何-column entries, 何類 asks rather than asserts — it invites the addressee to identify the category, rather than pointing to one deictically.

Mandarin 哪種 (nǎzhǒng, "which kind/sort; what type") is the standard form for category interrogation; the more classical 何種 (hézhǒng) is compositionally closer to Dan'a'yo's 何類. Cantonese 邊種 (bin1 zung2, "which kind/sort") is the colloquial Cantonese category interrogative. Japanese どんな (donna, "what kind of; what sort of") is the adnominal interrogative for categories — どんな人 ("what kind of person?"), どんな問題 ("what sort of problem?"). Korean 무슨 (museun, "what kind of; which sort of") is the adnominal interrogative for kind.

The original file used mandarin: shénme (什麼, bare "what?") and cantonese: sam6 mo1 (甚麼, Cantonese "what?") — both too general, querying identity rather than category. The correct forms 哪種/邊種 specifically interrogate the kind or type, matching 何類's compositional meaning (何 "what" + 類 "kind"). The alias 什么 has been removed accordingly; the Dan'a'yo composition is transparent enough for CJKV readers without it.
