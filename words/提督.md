## Notes
- [提](../characters/提.md) "to raise, present" + [督](../characters/督.md) "to oversee, supervise"

提督 is the title of "admiral" — the officer who oversees (督) and directs (提) a fleet. 督, a 形声 character built on the phonetic 叔 over the semantic 目 ("eye"), means "to oversee, to supervise, to keep watch over," already familiar from [[督促]] ("to urge, press for action") and the loan-translation [[基督教]] ("Christianity," from "Christ" — both cross-linked from [[督]]'s Words section).

Like [[提供]] and [[提出]] earlier in this project, 提督 uses 提 in its extended sense of "to bring forward, to direct" rather than its literal "to raise with the hand." The title entered East Asian naval terminology during the 19th-century modernization of Qing, Meiji-era Japanese, and Joseon/Korean navies, all of which adopted Western-style fleet hierarchies — 提督 sits at the top of that hierarchy, commanding a 艦隊 (fleet) much as [[提督]]'s near-synonym 司令官 commands a more general military unit.
