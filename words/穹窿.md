## Notes
- [穹](../characters/穹.md) "high; sky" + [隆 (char)](../characters/隆%20(char).md) "prosperous; abundant", here in its 窿 variant's narrowed sense "an arched cavity, a vault"
- 穹窿 is the stand-in word for [穹](../characters/穹.md) ("high; sky"), which is too bounded to stand alone in Dan'a'yo.

穹窿 names a vaulted, arching shape — a dome, a curved ceiling, the bowl of the sky itself. The compound doubles down on a single image: 穹 already names the high vault of heaven, and 窿/隆 (an arch, a rise, a swelling prominence) reinforces it from the architectural side. The result describes both literal domed structures (穹窿 天井, vaulted ceilings; the fornix in neuroanatomy) and, by extension, the heavens conceived as a great dome arching overhead — a usage shared with its near-synonym 穹蒼. Korean preserves the same image directly: 궁륭 (gungnyung) names an arched ceiling or roof, "shaped like a drawn bow or a rainbow, high in the middle and curving down at the ends." Japanese きゅうりゅう (kyūryū) keeps the celestial sense alongside the architectural one — the vast sky, an arch-like shape, a domed ceiling — three readings of the same underlying curve.
