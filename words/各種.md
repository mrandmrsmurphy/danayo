## Notes
- [各 (char)](../characters/各%20(char).md) "each, individually" + [種](../characters/種.md) "kind, type, seed, species" — neither character's own `stand_in` points here (各's is bare 各, 種's is 種類). `kwin: true` per the AND-rule, since both are individually `kwin: true`.
- Mandarin gèzhǒng, Cantonese gok3 zung2, Japanese かくしゅ, and Korean 각종 are all standard, everyday readings.
- **Left `vietnamese` honestly blank, not fabricated**: no dictionary attestation was found for a fixed compound "các chủng" as a genuine standalone word (unlike e.g. [[友好]]'s hữu hảo, which had a dedicated Wiktionary entry). Vietnamese 各 (các) has instead been grammaticalized into an extremely common native-feeling plural marker/article (các bạn, các anh chị em) rather than retained as a lexical noun-compounding morpheme paired with chủng — so the two-character compound doesn't appear to survive intact as a word the way it does in Mandarin/Cantonese/Japanese/Korean.
