## Notes
- [墳](../characters/墳.md) "burial mound, grave" + [墓](../characters/墓.md) "grave, tomb"

This word is the stand-in for [墳](../characters/墳.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 墳墓 carries the same sense of a burial site.

Both constituent characters mean grave or tomb, and their combination is an emphatic synonym compound — a common pattern in literary Chinese where two near-synonyms are joined to create a more formal or elevated term. 墳 (fén) specifically denotes a burial mound — a grave marked by a raised earthen tumulus — while 墓 (mù) denotes a grave or tomb more generally. Together, 墳墓 refers to a burial place or burial site in a broad, formal sense.

The compound appears in literary and historical writing across all CJKV languages. In Japanese (ふんぼ, funbo), it is a formal archaeological and historical term; 先祖の墳墓 ("ancestral graves") and 墳墓の地 ("the land of one's ancestors' graves," i.e. one's homeland) are set phrases. In Korean (분묘), the word is used in legal, historical, and cemetery administration contexts. In Vietnamese (phần mộ), it is the standard Hán-Việt term for a grave or burial site, appearing in both religious observance and formal writing.
