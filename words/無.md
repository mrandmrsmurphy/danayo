## Notes
- [無 (char)](../characters/無%20(char).md) "lack; to not have"

無 is the absence copula in Dan'a'yo — "to lack; to not have" — denying that the subject possesses the predicate: 我無書 (I have no book), 此地無水 (this place lacks water; there is no water here). Among the six Dan'a'yo copulas, 無 is the direct antonym of [[有]] (to have; to possess). The character's original sense was a rain-prayer dancer holding ox-tail ornaments in both hands — a 象形 of the ritual gesture — but was phonetically borrowed to mean "not have; lack," displacing the visual meaning; the original sense was recovered in 舞 (dance). 無 has extensive use in Buddhist and philosophical terminology: 無我 (anattā, non-self), 無明 (avidyā, ignorance), 無常 (anicca, impermanence). 無 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[無]].

Mandarin 没有 (méiyǒu): the standard negative possessive/existential — 我没有书 (I don't have a book / there is no book); literary 无 (wú, simplified) in formal compounds: 无论 (regardless), 无法 (unable; without method), 无数 (countless), 一无所有 (having nothing at all). Cantonese 冇 (mou5): the uniquely Cantonese negative possessive copula, historically derived from 無 but now a distinct morpheme — 我冇書 (I don't have a book), 冇問題 (no problem). Japanese ない (nai, inanimate absence) and いない (inai, animate absence): 本がない (there is no book), 誰もいない (there is no one); Sino-Japanese 無 (む/MU) in compounds — 無限 (mugen, infinite), 無我 (muga, non-self), 無常 (mujō, impermanence). Korean 없다 (eopda): 책이 없다 (there is no book / I don't have a book); Sino-Korean 무 (無) in compounds — 무한 (無限, infinite), 무아 (無我, non-self). Vietnamese không có: Tôi không có sách (I don't have a book); Sino-Vietnamese vô (無) in literary compounds: vô hạn (無限, infinite), vô thường (無常, impermanence).

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 므 does not match Korean 무. 無 reads 므 (mǝ/ㄇㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 微母 (ɱ) + 虞韻 (ɨo) gives a labiodental nasal onset with the central -ǝ vowel. Dan'a'yo maps the MC 虞韻 (ɨo) to the mid-central ㄜ vowel (므/mǝ), while Sino-Korean 무 (mu) maps the same final to the high back -u vowel. The MC 虞韻 produced ㄜ (-ǝ/므) in Dan'a'yo and -u (무) in Korean — a vowel-height divergence parallel to [[個]] (ㄍㄜ/그 vs 개), where Dan'a'yo gives a central vowel and Korean a different grade from the same MC source.
