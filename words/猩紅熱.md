## Notes
- [猩](../characters/猩.md) "orangutan; vivid red" + [紅 (char)](../characters/紅%20(char).md) "crimson" + [熱 (char)](../characters/熱%20(char).md) "fever" — scarlet fever (*Streptococcus pyogenes* infection)

The name reaches back through the 猩猩 (orangutan), whose face was a classical reference point for an intense, vivid red — deeper than 紅 alone. 猩紅 as a color compound means the specific scarlet of that face; adding 熱 names the fever characterized by a rash of precisely that color. The compound appears in classical Chinese medical literature and was adopted into Japanese (猩紅熱, しょうこうねつ, shōkōnetsu) and Korean (성홍열) with identical meaning.

Scarlet fever is caused by group A *Streptococcus pyogenes* producing an erythrogenic toxin. The disease presents with sudden high fever, sore throat (strep throat), and within one to two days a characteristic sandpaper-textured rash spreading from the trunk — the scarlet that gives the disease its name. The tongue develops the "strawberry tongue" appearance. Before antibiotics, scarlet fever was a leading cause of childhood death in East Asia and Europe; it remains treatable with penicillin but has not been eradicated.
