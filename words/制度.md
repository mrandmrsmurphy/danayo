## Notes
- [制](../characters/制.md) "regulate" + [度](../characters/度.md) "standard"

制度 pairs 制 (to cut, shape, and regulate — originally the act of trimming timber to a standard) with 度 (a measured standard or degree), yielding a formal system: the regulated framework within which conduct is organised. The word covers everything from constitutional arrangements to school grading systems to the rules of a guild.

In classical Chinese political thought, establishing correct 制度 was a foundational act of governance. The *Rites of Zhou* (周礼) and related canonical texts are largely concerned with specifying institutional structures — the proper number of officials, their grades, the ceremonies due each rank. To 立制度 (establish a system) was to legitimate a dynasty; the collapse of 制度 signalled dynastic decline. This gives the word a weight in East Asian political discourse that the English "system" does not always carry.

Compare [[体制]], which refers specifically to a ruling or governing structure (a regime, a constitutional system), whereas 制度 is broader and more neutral — any institutional framework, including non-political ones.
