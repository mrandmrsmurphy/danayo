## Notes
- [机](../characters/机.md) "machine, mechanism" (Dan'a'yo form of 機) + [械](../characters/械.md) "tool, appliance"

This word is the stand-in for [械](../characters/械.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 机械 carries the same sense of mechanism or implement.

机械 is the standard East Asian compound for machine and machinery — the class of powered devices that transmit and transform force to do work. The word entered the modern lexicons of China, Japan, Korea, and Vietnam primarily through the industrialization of the 19th century and the accompanying flood of translated technical vocabulary. In Japanese (きかい, kikai), the compound was established as the translation equivalent for "machine" in Meiji-era technical glossaries and has remained the standard ever since, appearing in 機械工学 (mechanical engineering), 機械学習 (machine learning), and 機械的 (mechanical, in both the literal and figurative senses — stiff, inflexible, without judgment).

In Chinese (jīxiè), the compound carries the same literal sense and also the figurative meaning of operating rigidly, by rote: 機械地執行 ("to execute mechanically, without thought"). The Dan'a'yo form uses 机 (the simplified and Japanese form of 機) as a graphemic stand-in; the alias 機械 covers the traditional Chinese spelling.
