## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative; its/their (classical)" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

其多 is the medial quantity correlative — "that much; that many; as many as that." Like all 其-column entries, it picks up a quantity from the shared discourse context or the addressee's frame: 其多あれば十分だ ("if there's that much, it's enough"); 其多の人が参加した ("that many people participated"). The amount in question is understood from what was just mentioned or from what the addressee has indicated.

Mandarin 那麼多 (nàme duō, "that much/many") is the standard everyday form and is kept as an alias; Japanese それほど (sorehodo, "to that extent; that much" — often with an evaluative nuance: "that great a degree") or それだけ (soredake, "that much and no more; just that amount"). Korean 그만큼 (geumankkeum, "that much — medial") is the standard medial quantity adverb of the 이만큼/그만큼/저만큼 set. Vietnamese nhiều như vậy (as many as that; that much) is the natural equivalent.

A note on the ROmaji: 其多 is *gǝdǝ* — both syllables carry the ǝ vowel (ㄜ). The earlier erroneous ROmaji *gǝda* conflated 多's Dan'a'yo reading (ㄉㄜ, *dǝ*) with its Mandarin reading (多=duō) or Korean reading (다). The canonical reading established in the 多 (char) file is consistently ㄉㄜ throughout the 多 row.
