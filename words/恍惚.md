## Notes
- [恍](../characters/恍.md) "vague; absent-minded" + [惚](../characters/惚.md) "confused"
- 恍惚 is the stand-in word for both [恍](../characters/恍.md) and [惚](../characters/惚.md), neither of which is viable alone in Dan'a'yo: the compound is the only form in which either character normally appears, in Chinese as in Dan'a'yo.

恍惚 names a state of dreamlike unmooring — the mind adrift between waking and absence, too dazzled or too dimmed to fix on anything. It covers both the rapture of being swept away (a trance, an ecstatic blur) and the blankness of distraction or confusion, with the boundary between the two often left productively unclear. Korean 황홀 (hwanghol) leans toward the rapturous end — "ecstatic, enraptured" — while Japanese こうこつ (kōkotsu) keeps closer to the trance/absorption sense, as in 恍惚の境地 ("a state of rapture/absorption"). Both readings sit comfortably within the Chinese huǎnghū's wider span from "vague and indistinct" to "spellbound."
