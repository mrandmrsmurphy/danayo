## Notes
- [代](../characters/代.md) "replace, generation" + [替](../characters/替.md) "change, replace, substitute" — stand-in for [[替]], which cannot appear independently

代替 is "to replace, substitute (for)" — a synonym compound pairing two characters that already independently mean "replace," giving a general-purpose verb for standing in for or taking the place of something or someone. 替 cannot appear as an independent vault entry on its own (`boundedness: 90`), so 代替 is what legitimizes it.

Mandarin 代替 (dàitì). Cantonese 代替 (doi6tai3). Japanese 代替 (だいたい, daitai) is common in more technical/institutional registers — 代替品 (daitaihin, "substitute good/product"), 代替エネルギー (daitai enerugī, "alternative energy") — while everyday speech more often reaches for the native verb 代わる (kawaru) or 代わりに (kawari ni, "instead of"). Korean 대체 (daeche) is very productive and everyday — 대체 휴일 (substitute holiday), 대체로 (daecero, "generally, on the whole" — a common adverb built from the same root). Vietnamese đại thế carries the same "substitute, replace" sense, though thay thế (native thay + Sino thế) is the more frequent everyday phrasing for the plain verb "to replace."

kwin is true: both 代 and 替 individually match their own Sino-Korean readings exactly (대=대, 체=체), so the compound's `kwin` — true only when every constituent character matches — comes out true as well.
