## Notes
**土素** is a binomial neologism for the chemical element **Tellurium** (Atomic Number: 52). It is a direct semantic calque of the Latin root _[tellus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tellurium#Etymology)_, meaning "earth."

### 1. Etymological Mirroring: Earth and Moon

As noted in the [Wiktionary entry for Tellurium](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tellurium#Etymology), the element was named in 1798 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth.

- **The Inspiration:** He named it after the Roman earth goddess, **[Tellus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Tellus)**.
- **The Counterpart:** This was done to create a pair with **Selenium** (which he had previously helped identify), named after the Greek moon goddess, [Selene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selenium#History).
- **The Translation:** By using **土** (Mandarin: _tǔ_; Japanese: _to/do_; Korean: _to_; Vietnamese: _thổ_), we are capturing the "Earth" essence of the name. It transforms the abstract Latin _tellus_ into a universally understood CJKV concept.

### 2. Physical and Chemical Context
Tellurium is a [rare, brittle, silver-white metalloid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tellurium#Properties).

- **The Logic:** While it is a metalloid, it is often found in the earth's crust as [tellurides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telluride_\(chemistry\)) of gold and silver. Using **土素** ("Earth Element") reinforces its status as a fundamental building block of the planet's mineralogy.
