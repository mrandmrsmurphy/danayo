## Notes
- [白 (char)](../characters/白%20(char).md) "white" + [亜](../characters/亜.md) "chalk" (Dan'a'yo stand-in for 堊) + [紀](../characters/紀.md) "period, era"

白亜紀 is the Cretaceous Period (approximately 145–66 million years ago), the third and final period of the Mesozoic Era. The name derives from [[白亜]] (chalk), referring to the extensive chalk deposits — formed from the compressed remains of coccolithophores and other marine microorganisms — that characterize the geological record of this period in Europe and elsewhere. The English name "Cretaceous" comes from the same Latin root (*creta*, chalk).

The Cretaceous is defined by the dominance of non-avian dinosaurs, the proliferation of flowering plants (angiosperms), and the widespread shallow epicontinental seas that produced the chalk strata. It ends with the Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event (K-Pg boundary), when a mass extinction eliminated approximately 75% of species, including all non-avian dinosaurs. In Japanese paleontology and geology writing, 白亜紀 (はくあき, hakuaki) is the standard term, with the compound attested in Japanese geological literature from at least 1883. The first systematic use is credited to the translation work of the Meiji-era geological survey.
