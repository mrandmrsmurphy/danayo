## Notes
- [簡](../characters/簡.md) "simple, brief" + [略 (char)](../characters/略%20(char).md) "omit, abbreviate" — reduced to its essentials, with the inessential parts cut away.

簡 began life as a word for the bamboo or wooden strips (竹簡) used as writing material before paper, and from there came to describe anything pared down to a few essential slips of information — hence its modern sense of "simple, brief." 略, meanwhile, carries a core sense of "to omit, skip over, summarize," visible in [[侵略]] ("invade," literally "encroach and seize") as well as in common but as-yet-uncreated compounds like 省略 ("omission") and 戦略 ("strategy," a plan with the unnecessary stripped away). 簡略 sits between [[簡単]] ("simple, easy" — the more everyday word for a task or method) and outright omission: it specifically describes language, writing, or procedure trimmed down to a brief, economical form. Attested with this meaning across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese (giản lược).
