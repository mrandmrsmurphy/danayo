## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this"

此 is "this" — the near demonstrative in Dan'a'yo's three-way proximal system: 此 (near, proximate to speaker) vs. 其 (middle distance, previously referenced or near the addressee) vs. 彼 (far, distal from both). As a modifier, 此 precedes a noun to identify something in the speaker's immediate environment: 此人 (this person), 此事 (this matter), 此処 (here). The same three-way distinction reflects the classical Chinese literary demonstrative system 此/其/彼, preserved in Dan'a'yo as a productive grammatical paradigm across the entire correlative chart. 此 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[此]].

Mandarin 此 (cǐ) is a literary and formal demonstrative, contrasting with colloquial 这 (zhè): 此時 (at this time), 此處 (at this place), 因此 (therefore; lit. because of this). Cantonese 此 (ci2) is similarly literary; colloquial Cantonese uses 呢 (ni1) for "this." Japanese この (kono) is the adnominal demonstrative for "this"; the Sino-Japanese on-reading し (shi) appears in literary compounds. Korean 차 (此) in Sino-Korean; the native demonstrative is 이 (i-) as in 이것 (this thing), 이곳 (this place). Vietnamese thử (此): rare in modern usage; contemporary Vietnamese uses đây or này for "this."

kwin is false: Dan'a'yo 츠 differs from Korean 차. 此 reads 츠 (cǝ/ㄑㄜ) in Dan'a'yo — MC 清母 (t͡sʰ) + 支三開韻 (iᴇ) gives Dan'a'yo the schwa vowel (-ǝ, ㄜ) with an affricate initial — while Sino-Korean gives 차 (cha), using the plain -a vowel. Dan'a'yo preserves the MC schwa where Sino-Korean shifts to -a.
