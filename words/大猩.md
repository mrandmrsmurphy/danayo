## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "large, great" + [猩](../characters/猩.md) "ape" — gorilla (*Gorilla gorilla* / *Gorilla beringei*); shortened from 大猩猩

大猩 is the Dan'a'yo shortening of 大猩猩 (大猩猩, dà xīng xīng), the Mandarin term for gorilla — the great ape. Gorillas are the largest living primates: adult male western lowland gorillas average 170 kg; mountain gorilla silverbacks can exceed 200 kg.

The word gorilla entered English via the 1847 description by American naturalist Thomas Savage, who encountered western gorillas in Gabon and named them after the Greek-rendered Punic word (Γόριλλαι, *Gorillai*) recorded by the Carthaginian explorer Hanno of Carthage around 500 BC, who reported encountering hairy wild people on the West African coast. The Chinese 大猩猩 (great ape) is a calque by meaning rather than sound. Two species are recognized: *Gorilla gorilla* (western gorilla, two subspecies) and *Gorilla beringei* (eastern gorilla, including the mountain gorilla of the Virunga volcanoes). All gorilla populations are critically endangered or endangered; the mountain gorilla has fewer than 1,100 individuals remaining.
