## Notes
**orm.** 嬴金 is 嬴 + 金 “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymological situation.** _Thulium_ derives from **Thule**, a semi-mythical northern land in Greek and Roman geography. The name is deliberately vague and not tied to a concrete, stable referent, which makes direct semantic calquing difficult.

**Motivation.** You construct a **mythic-toponymic substitution**:

- Thule (mythic far-north land)
- mapped to a Sinitic mythic geography: 瀛洲 (one of the legendary isles)
- abbreviated: 嬴
- - 金

This is not a direct translation but a **functional equivalence**: replacing one mythic “edge-of-the-world” location with another within the Sinitic cultural sphere.

**Type of formation.**  
A **cross-cultural mythological substitution with abbreviation**:

- foreign mythic place → native mythic place
- native place-name → single-character abbreviation
- suffixation: 金

This is one of the more interpretive strategies in your system.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Avoids phonetic borrowing.
2. Preserves the _type_ of referent (mythic geography).
3. Provides a unique, otherwise unused graph for symbol purposes.
4. Maintains consistency with your willingness to reinterpret when direct mapping fails (cf. 孫金, 狼金).

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 铥 (phonetic)
- Japanese: ツリウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 툴륨 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: thuli (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 嬴金 introduces a culturally grounded reinterpretation.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 嬴 is obscure and primarily known as a surname; its connection to 瀛洲 is not standard.
- The mapping Thule → 瀛洲 is conceptual rather than historical.
- This is one of your least transparent formations and requires explicit convention.

**Assessment.**  
The move is coherent under your constraints:

- Thule offers no usable semantics
- you preserve the **mythic-geographic category**
- you secure an unused, distinctive symbol

However, it is clearly a **high-context, system-internal solution**.

**Conclusion.**  
嬴金 is a defensible but highly interpretive construction. It maintains structural consistency and avoids collisions, but its connection to _thulium_ depends on accepting a cross-cultural mythic substitution rather than a direct etymological link.
