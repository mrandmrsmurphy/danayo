## Notes
- [停](../characters/停.md) "halt, stop" + [留 (char)](../characters/留%20(char).md) "remain, stay" — stopping in the sense of staying put where you are, not the ending of an action. This word is the viable form of [停](../characters/停.md): 停 alone is too bound to stand as a Dan'a'yo word, and 停留 is its `stand_in` compound, deliberately chosen to keep 停's sense the physical/positional pause, in contrast to [[中止]] (the discontinuing-an-action sense that 止 stands in for) — splitting apart what the collapsed compound [[停止]] had blurred into one generic "stop."

Mandarin tíngliú and Japanese teiryū both live most visibly in 停留所/停留場 ("stop," as in bus stop or tram stop) — the place where a vehicle pauses briefly before continuing, never the place where a journey ends. Korean 정류 shows the identical pattern in 정류장 (bus stop), and is arguably the more essential half of the pair in Korean, since 정류장 is one of the most common compound nouns in everyday use. Cantonese ting4lau4 follows Mandarin's usage closely. The shared thread across all of these: 停留 names a pause within continuity — the bus, the traveler, the process is still going somewhere, just not yet.
