## Notes
- [抑](../characters/抑.md) "to press down; to restrain" + [制](../characters/制.md) "to regulate; to control; system" — stand-in for [[制]], which cannot appear independently
- A near-synonym compound — "to press down" plus "to bring under control" — denoting the act of holding something back before it can run its course: an emotion, an impulse, a disease, a chemical reaction, an enemy's advance. The pairing is a workhorse of formal and technical registers across the Sinosphere — Mandarin yìzhì, Cantonese jik1 zai3, Japanese よくせい (yokusei), Sino-Korean 억제 (eokje), and Sino-Vietnamese ức chế — appearing equally in psychology (impulse suppression), medicine (inhibiting a pathogen or response), and physics or chemistry (restraining a reaction), wherever a force needs to be checked from the outside rather than allowed to run free.
