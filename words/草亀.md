## Notes
- [草 (char)](../characters/草%20(char).md) "grass" + [亀 (char)](../characters/亀%20(char).md) "turtle"

The name 草亀 ("grass turtle") reflects the species' preferred habitat — the grassy margins of rivers, ponds, and rice paddies across temperate East Asia. The animal in question is *Mauremys reevesii*, the Chinese pond turtle (also called Reeves' turtle after the British naturalist John Reeves), one of the most familiar freshwater chelonians in the region. It ranges from central China through Japan, Korea, and Taiwan, and has historically been so abundant that it became the default mental image of "turtle" for much of the population — hence the folk gloss "ordinary turtle."

In Japanese the compound takes the special reading くさがめ: 草 contributes its native reading くさ rather than its on-yomi そう, and the following かめ ("turtle") undergoes rendaku — sequential voicing — to がめ. The species is also known colloquially as 臭亀 (read identically, くさがめ, "stinky turtle") for the musky odour it releases when threatened.

In Taiwanese Hokkien, 草龜 doubles as the name of a turtle-shaped glutinous-rice confection (草仔龜) prepared during Qingming and other festivals, linking the term to ritual food culture as well as the living animal.

The species is currently listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List, principally due to overcollection for the food and traditional-medicine trades across the region.
