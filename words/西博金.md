## Notes
- [西](../characters/西.md) + [博](../characters/博.md) + [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) "metal" — a transliteration of *Seaborg*, after chemist Glenn T. Seaborg, who co-discovered ten transuranium elements and for whom this one was named while he was still living (an exception IUPAC made to its usual rule against naming elements after living people). 西博 renders the first two syllables of 西博格, the standard Mandarin transliteration of "Seaborg," truncated rather than spelled out in full. Japanese and Korean instead borrow the IUPAC name as a loanword (シーボーギウム, 시보귬).
