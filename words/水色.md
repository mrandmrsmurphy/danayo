## Notes
- [水 (char)](../characters/水%20(char).md) "water" + [色](../characters/色.md) "color"

水色 is most established as a color term in Japanese (みずいろ, mizu-iro), where it denotes a pale, transparent sky blue — the color of clear water in a shallow basin or of a winter sky. It is one of the classic traditional Japanese colors (伝統色). In Mandarin, 水色 more often means "the color of water" in a generic sense rather than naming a specific hue, and sky blue is more commonly expressed as 天蓝色. In Korean, 수색 can also mean "search" (搜索), creating a homophonic ambiguity that limits the color term's currency.
