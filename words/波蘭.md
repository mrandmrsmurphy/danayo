## Notes
- [波](../characters/波.md) "wave" + [蘭](../characters/蘭.md) "orchid (for the sound 'lan')" — a phonetic transliteration of "Poland", reading the two characters for their sounds rather than their meanings.

波蘭 (Bōlán) belongs to the same family of 蘭-final country transliterations as [[英格蘭]] "England" and [[新西蘭]] "New Zealand", where 蘭 (lán) is repurposed purely for its sound to render the "-land"/"-lan" ending of a Western place name, with no connection to its literal meaning of "orchid." Here 波 ("wave, ripple") supplies the initial "Po-" syllable, again chosen for sound rather than sense.

The transliteration is shared across the Sinosphere: Cantonese bo1 laan4 and Vietnamese Ba Lan follow the same character-by-character rendering as Mandarin. Japanese, however, does not use the Sino-Japanese reading of 波蘭 in everyday usage — like many European country names, Poland is rendered in modern Japanese with the katakana borrowing ポーランド (Pōrando), taken directly from the English/Polish name rather than via the Chinese characters, even though 波蘭 does exist as a historical kanji transliteration (paralleling 英吉利 for England).

Korean 파란 (Paran) is worth noting alongside the Dan'a'yo 諺文 form 바란: the character 波 carries the standard Sino-Korean reading 파 (pa), which diverges from its Dan'a'yo 諺文 value 바 (ba) — the same kind of divergence seen on [[波]]'s own character page, where `korean: 파` differs from `諺文: 바`. This is why `kwin` is `false` for 波蘭: the word inherits the character-level mismatch.

The simplified form 波兰 (with 兰 for 蘭) is listed as an alias, following [[蘭]]'s own `aliases` field.
