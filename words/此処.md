## Notes
- [此 (char)](../characters/此%20(char).md) "this; proximal demonstrative" + [処 (char)](../characters/処%20(char).md) "place; location"

此処 is the proximal place correlative — "here; at this place." The character 処 (Dan'a'yo *co*, ㄑㄛ) covers place and location; its grapheme depicts a person resting in a shelter, suggesting a settled location or dwelling place. Combined with 此 (proximal), 此処 designates the location nearest the speaker: the spot where one currently is, the space being pointed to. The Chinese alias 這裡 (zhèlǐ, "this-interior → here") is the most common spoken equivalent; Dan'a'yo 此処 is the compositional sinographic form derived systematically from the correlative table.

Cross-linguistic equivalents are consistent across the CJKV sphere: Mandarin 這裡 (zhèlǐ) or 這兒 (zhèr, Beijing r-suffix variant); Cantonese 呢度 (ni1 dou6, colloquial) or 這裡 (ze2 leoi5, formal/literary); Japanese ここ (koko, the proximal place pronoun); Korean 여기 (yeogi, here); Vietnamese tại đây (at this place) or bare đây (here). All five CJKV languages maintain dedicated proximal place pronouns, making 此処 one of the most cross-linguistically well-supported correlatives in the table — each language has a natural counterpart for "here" without needing circumlocution.

此処 appears on the Swadesh list (entry 9), confirming its status as core basic vocabulary. As a place correlative, 此処 commonly appears with the locative particle 於 (於此処 = "at this place; here") or as a bare frame-setting adverbial (此処、止まれ = "here, stop"). The 処 row covers the spatial domain of the correlative table, running parallel to the temporal 時 row: 此処/此時, 其処/其時, 彼処/彼時 form neat spatial-temporal pairs across the deictic columns.
