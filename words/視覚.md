## Notes
- [視 (char)](../characters/視%20(char).md) "look, view" + [覚](../characters/覚.md) "perceive, become aware" — the sense of sight, or the faculty of seeing, as one of the five traditional senses (五感) alongside hearing, smell, taste, and touch.

視 supplies the "looking" half of many compounds describing attention and observation — already present in the vocabulary's earlier [[視認]]-adjacent work and in everyday words like 視野 ("field of view," not yet created) — while 覚 is the broader perception-verb, "to sense, notice, become aware of," seen in 感覚 ("the senses, sensation") and 自覚 ("self-awareness"), neither yet in the vocabulary but both built on the same root. 視覚 specifically isolates the visual channel: in contrast to 聴覚 ("hearing," literally "listening-perception," also uncreated), it is the sense most often cited in discussions of reading and writing systems, including Dan'a'yo's own use of furigana-style ruby annotation to make pronunciation visually explicit. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
