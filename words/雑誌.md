## Notes
- [雑 (char)](../characters/雑%20(char).md) "miscellaneous; mixed; various" + [誌](../characters/誌.md) "journal; written record; to record"

雑誌 is "magazine; periodical" — 雑 (miscellaneous, mixed, diverse) + 誌 (a written record, a journal — as in 日誌 = diary, 地誌 = gazetteer). The compound literally means "a record of miscellaneous things," capturing the nature of a magazine as a periodical gathering diverse articles, topics, genres, and voices in one publication. 雑誌 is primarily a Japanese coinage (wasei-kango, 和製漢語) that spread to Chinese and Korean via Japan's publishing influence in the modern era.

Japanese 雑誌 (ざっし, zasshi) is the standard everyday word for magazine — the likely source of the modern CJKV usage, coined in the Meiji era for Western-style periodicals as journalism and print media spread across the Sinosphere. Mandarin 雜誌 (zázhì) is the standard term for magazine. Cantonese 雜誌 (zaap6 zi3) is likewise standard. Korean 잡지 (japji) is the everyday word for magazine. Vietnamese tạp chí is the Sino-Vietnamese reading — 雑 → tạp (miscellaneous), 誌 → chí (record/journal) — and is the standard Vietnamese word for magazine or journal.

The alias 雜誌 is the traditional-character form (雜 is the traditional version of 雑, simplified in Japan and mainland China). The 誌 character in this compound means "journal; written record" rather than "to symbolize" — the record-keeping sense, as in 志 (ambition/record) and its cognate 誌 (to inscribe, to record). The kwin: true reflects that Dan'a'yo's Hangul transcription 잡지 exactly matches Korean 잡지, both deriving from the same Middle Chinese roots through closely parallel phonological paths.
