## Notes
- [外](../characters/外.md) "outside, foreign" + [語](../characters/語.md) "language, speech" — an outside language; a foreign tongue.

外語 is the standard pan-Sinospheric compound for a foreign language, though the expanded form 外国語 (wàiguóyǔ, "outside-country language") is often preferred when precision matters. In Japanese, 外語 alone is literary or formal; the common spoken form is 外国語 (がいこくご, gaikokugo). In Korean, 외어 (外語) is the classical reading, while 외국어 (外國語, oegugeoh) is the standard modern term.

The compound carries a quiet reflexive quality within the Dan'a'yo project: Dan'a'yo is itself a constructed 外語 for every speaker who learns it, by design. Unlike national languages — Mandarin, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese — which are native languages to millions and foreign languages only to outsiders, Dan'a'yo has no native community. Every user of Dan'a'yo is, by definition, approaching it as a 外語. The language was coined precisely to be a shared foreign language, a neutral third ground across the East Asian sphere.

Vietnamese ngoại ngữ (外語) uses the Hán-Việt readings intact: ngoại (外, "outside") + ngữ (語, "language"), making this one of the more consistent pan-Sinospheric terms. The Vietnamese form is fully compositional and transparent to any speaker familiar with Hán-Việt readings.
