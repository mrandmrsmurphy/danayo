## Notes
- [挟 (char)](../characters/挟%20(char).md) "clasp, hold between" + [攻 (char)](../characters/攻%20(char).md) "attack"

挟攻 denotes an attack launched simultaneously from two or more directions, so that the enemy is caught between converging forces — a pincer or flanking attack. The image in 挟 is of holding something pressed between two things (as between fingers or under an arm), applied here to an enemy force caught between two attacking arms. 

The compound is a Japanese military and strategic term (きょうこう, kyōkō), derived from the Chinese tactical concept 夾攻 (jiāgōng), which uses 夾 ("to sandwich") rather than 挟. The two characters are synonymous in this usage; 挟 is the standard Japanese substitute. The related compound [[挟撃]] (きょうげき, kyōgeki) specifies the strike itself; 挟攻 emphasizes the overall tactical approach. In Korean (협공), the word is used in both military history and sports commentary to describe coordinated two-pronged pressure.
