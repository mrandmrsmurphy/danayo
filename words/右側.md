## Notes
- [右](../characters/右.md) "right (direction)" + [側](../characters/側.md) "side; aspect" — stand-in for [[右]], which cannot appear independently

**Real bugs corrected**: `japanese` was `みぎりがわ`, an apparent typo — the real reading is `みぎがわ` (migigawa; also attested as the on'yomi うそく, usoku, in more formal register), confirmed via search. `korean` was `오른쪽`, the fully native Korean word for "right side," not a reading of 右側 at all — corrected to `우측` (ujeuk), the real Sino-Korean reading; both terms are genuinely interchangeable in everyday Korean (편의점은 우측/오른쪽에 있습니다, "the convenience store is on the right"), per search. Fixed a missing space in `cantonese` (jau6zak1→jau6 zak1).

Mandarin yòucè and Cantonese jau6 zak1 are the standard word for "the right side" (右側通行, "keep to the right/right-hand traffic"). Vietnamese hữu trắc is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound (verified via search); bare hữu alone is also commonly sufficient in Vietnamese for "right" in spatial, political, and geographic senses (hữu ngạn, "the right bank of a river").
