## Notes
- [希](../characters/希.md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Ji Province (冀州), the first and central of the [[九州]]; 希 is the Dan'a'yo canonical form, 冀 being its alias

冀州 was the central-northern province of the [[九州]] as described in the *Tribute of Yu* (禹貢) chapter of the *Book of Documents* (書經). It occupied the great northern plain — roughly modern Hebei, Shanxi, and the surrounding region — and was considered the province of the royal domain itself, the political heart of the Zhou world. The *Tribute of Yu* describes its soil as the whitest and its tribute grains as the finest. Its centrality in the geographic scheme reflects the Zhou political ideology of the royal domain as the pivot around which the four quarters turn.
