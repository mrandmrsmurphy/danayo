## Notes
- [鋭](../characters/鋭.md) "sharp; keen; acute" + [利](../characters/利.md) "profit" — stand-in for [[鋭]], which cannot appear independently; here 利 contributes its older "sharp, keen" sense (as in 利器, "a sharp weapon"; 鋒利, "sharp-edged"), not its more familiar modern "profit" sense

Mandarin ruìlì, Cantonese jeoi6 lei6, Japanese えいり (eiri), and Korean 예리 (yeri) are all the standard word for "sharp, keen" — both literally (a blade) and figuratively (a sharp mind or observation, 鋭利な観察/예리한 관찰). Vietnamese nhuệ lợi is a real, attested Sino-Vietnamese compound carrying the same double sense (verified via search).
