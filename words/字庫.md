## Notes
- [字 (char)](../characters/字%20(char).md) "character, written symbol" + [庫](../characters/庫.md) "storehouse, repository" — a repository of characters: a font, or the digital store of glyphs a font draws from.

庫 is the storehouse-root behind 倉庫 ("warehouse") and 金庫 ("safe, treasury" — both already attested elsewhere as existing in the vocabulary), generalizing easily to any organized collection held in reserve. 字庫 is specifically the modern, computing-era sense: the set of glyph shapes — a font, or the underlying character set a font is drawn from — that a system has on hand to render text, as distinct from [[字形]] (the shape of any one character within that set) or 字典 ("dictionary," not yet created, a repository of character meanings rather than shapes). The term is standard in Mandarin- and Cantonese-language computing and typesetting contexts (中文字库) but has no equally fixed equivalent in Japanese or Korean, where the loanword フォント/폰트 ("font") is generally used instead — making 字庫 one of the rarer cases in this batch where the pedigree runs through Mandarin/Cantonese rather than Japanese.
