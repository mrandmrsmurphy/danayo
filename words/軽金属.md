## Notes
- [軽 (char)](../characters/軽%20(char).md) "light" + [金 (char)](../characters/金%20(char).md) "metal" + [属](../characters/属.md) "category, kind" — light metals; in materials science and engineering, the metals of low density (roughly below 5 g/cm³), principally aluminum, magnesium, titanium, and beryllium.

The term contrasts directly with 重金属 (heavy metals). 軽金属 appears in formal metallurgical classification and industrial discourse: 軽金属工業 (light metals industry), 軽金属学会 (Japan Institute of Light Metals, founded 1950). The category is economically and strategically significant — aluminum and titanium are essential to aerospace, automotive, and defense applications, and control over 軽金属 supply chains has become a geopolitical lever: China's dominance in rare-earth and light-metal refining shapes global manufacturing policy.

Korean 경금속 and Chinese 轻金属 follow the same compound structure and domain. Vietnamese commonly uses the analytic phrase *kim loại nhẹ* (metal + light/not-heavy) rather than the Sino-Vietnamese *khinh kim thuộc*, reflecting the tendency of Vietnamese technical vocabulary to favor descriptive native phrasing over calqued Sino-Vietnamese forms.
