## Notes
- [受](../characters/受.md) "to receive" + [託](../characters/託.md) "to entrust, commission"

受託 means "to be entrusted with [a task, responsibility, or assets]; to accept a commission" — the recipient's side of an act of [[嘱託]] (cross-linked from [[託]]'s own Words section, "to commission, to entrust"). 託, a 象形-classified character (despite denoting an abstract relationship — note its graphemic_classification reflects its visual form rather than its meaning) built on the speech radical 言, means "to entrust, to commission, to rely on" — its aliases 讬/托 reflecting centuries of script simplification around the same root meaning.

受託 is the fifth and final 受-compound in this batch, completing the sequence [[受賞]] (receiving an honor), [[受難]] (receiving suffering), [[受理]] (receiving a document for processing), [[受胎]] (receiving new life), and now 受託 (receiving a charge or commission). In financial contexts, 受託 underlies words like 受託者 ("trustee") and 信託 ("trust, as in a financial trust") — wherever one party formally takes on responsibility for assets or duties handed over by another, 受託 names that party's side of the transaction.
