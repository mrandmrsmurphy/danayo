## Notes
- [参](../characters/参.md) "consult, attend" + [考](../characters/考.md) "examine, think over" — to consult for reference; reference material; for your information

参考 names the act of consulting something as a source of guidance without necessarily adopting its conclusions — to look at something and think it over. 参考書 (reference book), 参考文献 (bibliography, references section), and ご参考まで (for your reference — a common closing phrase when sharing information without obligation) show the range. It implies an open, exploratory engagement with a source rather than the binding citation of 引用 (quotation) or the specific directed comparison of 参照.

参考意見 (advisory opinion, opinion submitted for reference rather than as binding judgment) illustrates the word's epistemic character: what is shared 参考 is offered as potentially useful input, not as a conclusion. Vietnamese tham khảo has the same range and is widely used in academic and official writing. Korean 참고 is equally productive: 참고로 말씀드리면 (to mention this for your reference) is a standard hedging formula in formal speech.
