## Notes
**Form.** 桃金 is [桃](characters/桃.md) “peach; peach-pink” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymological situation.** _Erbium_ is named after **Ytterby**, like terbium and ytterbium. The place-name is not reducible to a usable single graph, so a direct toponymic strategy is weak.

**Motivation.** You instead use a **color-based property**:

- erbium(III) salts are characteristically **pink to rose-colored**
- 桃 denotes a **soft peach-pink**, more specific than generic 紅

This preserves the visual identity of the element in a way parallel to 翠金 (terbium).

**Type of formation.**  
A **property-based designation** replacing an opaque place-name, consistent with your fallback strategy.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, no phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately.
3. Provides a clear, distinctive symbol (桃).
4. Complements the Ytterby set semantically:
    - 翠金 (green)
    - 桃金 (pink)
    - 褐金 (brown)

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 铒 (phonetic)
- Japanese: エルビウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 어븀 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: erbi (phonetic)

Modern forms are phonetic; 桃金 restores semantic content.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 桃 is concrete and easily visualized.
- The color association is well-established in spectroscopy and materials contexts.
- It avoids overlap with other color terms already in use.

**Conclusion.**  
桃金 is a strong, clean solution: it replaces an unusable toponym with a precise and recognizable color descriptor, maintaining internal coherence with related elements.
