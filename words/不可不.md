## Notes
- [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" + [可 (char)](../characters/可%20(char).md) "able to" + [不 (char)](../characters/不%20(char).md) "not" — a double negative meaning "unable to not [do something]," i.e. obligatory: one must.

This is a genuine, well-attested Classical Chinese construction (不可不知, "one cannot not know, i.e. must know"; 不可不慎, "one must be careful"), not a Dan'a'yo-internal coinage. Korean preserves it as a single fossilized adverb, 불가불 ("inevitably, willy-nilly, necessarily"), and the same construction survives in Japanese kanbun-derived literary register as 不可不…ず. It completes the modal square alongside [[可]] ("able"), [[不可]] ("unable"), and [[可不]] ("able to not") — the strongest member of the square, expressing necessity rather than mere permission.
