## Notes
- [細 (char)](../characters/細%20(char).md) "fine, thin" + [菌](../characters/菌.md) "fungus, germ" — stand-in for [[菌]], which cannot appear independently

細菌 (xìjūn / さいきん / 세균) is the standard East Asian scientific term for bacteria, coined during the late nineteenth century when Western microbiology was being absorbed into Chinese and Japanese. The compound literally means "fine germ" or "minute fungus": 細 contributes the sense of extreme smallness and 菌 the older sense of fungus or mould, the closest pre-modern category available for microorganisms.

The term was standardised in Japanese scientific usage by the Meiji period and spread widely across Chinese and Korean medical and biological literature. In modern usage 細菌 is distinguished from 真菌 (fungi proper) and ウイルス / 病毒 (viruses), anchoring it precisely to prokaryotic microorganisms. Common collocations include 細菌感染 (bacterial infection), 細菌兵器 (biological weapons), and 腸内細菌 (intestinal bacteria / gut flora).
