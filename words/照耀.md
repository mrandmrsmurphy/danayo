## Notes
- [照 (char)](../characters/照%20(char).md) "shine, illuminate" + [耀](../characters/耀.md) "radiance, glory, dazzle"

This word is the stand-in for [耀](../characters/耀.md), which is too bounded to appear alone in Dan'a'yo discourse; 照耀 carries the same sense of brilliant, radiating light.

照耀 describes the action of light falling upon something with brilliance — the sun shining down on water, a lamp illuminating a room, a star casting its glow. Both constituent characters carry the sense of luminous intensity: 照 (zhào) emphasizes directed illumination (to shine upon, to light up a surface), while 耀 (yào) emphasizes the quality of the light itself — dazzling, glorious, radiant. Together the compound conveys light that is both active (it falls on things) and magnificent (it overwhelms with brightness).

The compound appears in classical poetry and prose describing natural light, imperial glory, and spiritual radiance. In modern Chinese and Japanese, it remains literary in register — everyday contexts prefer 照射 (to irradiate) or 照明 (lighting) for prosaic illumination, while 照耀 carries a more elevated, poetic connotation.
