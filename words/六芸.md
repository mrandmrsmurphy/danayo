## Definition

They originate in early Confucian tradition, described in texts such as the **Zhou ritual classics**, and became the model of elite education in East Asia.

### The Six Arts
1. **[[礼]] (Rei / Lǐ) — Rites**  
    Ritual propriety, ceremony, social order, moral formation.  
    Not mere etiquette, but the structure of civilization.
2. **[[音楽]] (Gaku / Yuè) — Music**  
    Moral and emotional cultivation through ordered sound.  
    Music was seen as harmonizing the soul with cosmic order.
3. **[[words/射術]] (Sha / Shè) — Archery**  
    Martial discipline combined with ritual precision.  
    Archery contests were moral exercises, not just military training.
4. **[[words/御術]] (Gyo / Yù) — Charioteering**  
	Mastery of coordinated action, composure, and physical control.
5. **[[words/書法]] (Sho / Shū) — Calligraphy / Writing**  
    Literacy, textual transmission, cultural continuity.
6. **[[数学]] (Sū / Shù) — Mathematics / Calculation**  
    Practical reckoning, calendrics, administration.

### Structural Insight
The Six Arts combine:
- Moral formation (礼, 楽)
- Martial discipline (射, 御)
- Intellectual skill (書, 数)
