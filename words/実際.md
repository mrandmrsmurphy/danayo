## Notes
- [実](../characters/実.md) "real, true, fruit" + [際 (char)](../characters/際%20(char).md) "border, occasion, the point where things meet" — the point where appearance gives way to what is genuinely the case; actuality.

実 is the basic root of factuality across the vocabulary, anchoring [[事実]] ("fact") and the not-yet-created 現実 ("reality") and 実現 ("to realize, bring about" — both confirmed elsewhere as existing or readily derivable); 際 contributes a more specific sense of "the edge or boundary at which something occurs," seen in 国際 ("international," already in the vocabulary, literally "at the border between nations"). 実際 combines them into the everyday adverbial and adjectival "actually, in practice" — the word reached for when contrasting theory with practice, or claim with fact, as in 理論上 versus 実際上 ("in theory" versus "in actual practice," neither yet created but both transparent extensions). The Vietnamese reflex thực tế is fully productive in modern Vietnamese in exactly this sense. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese.
