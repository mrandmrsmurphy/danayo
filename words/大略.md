## Notes
- [大 (char)](../characters/大%20(char).md) "great; large" + [略 (char)](../characters/略%20(char).md) "to abbreviate; outline; plan"

大略 (*dàlüè*) is, like its near-synonym [[大概]], an adverb of approximation: "roughly, approximately, in broad strokes." As a noun it denotes a rough outline or sketch of a plan — and, by extension in classical usage, a "grand strategy" or "great plan" (as in 雄才大略, "heroic talent and grand strategy," a stock description of an outstanding ruler or general).

The semantic core is 略, "to omit, abbreviate, summarize" — what remains after the details are stripped away is the 大略, the "big-picture outline." Compared to 大概, which carries an epistemic shading of "probably" alongside its sense of approximation, 大略 is more purely about scope and detail-level: it describes how finely or coarsely something is rendered, rather than how certain the speaker is.

In Japanese 大略 (たいりゃく, *tairyaku*) retains both senses — "roughly speaking" as an adverb, and "outline / summary" as a noun, often in formal or written registers (a 大略を述べる, "to give an outline"). Korean 대략 (*daeryak*) and Vietnamese đại lược pattern the same way, with 대략 being extremely common in everyday spoken Korean as a hedge equivalent to "approximately" or "roughly."
