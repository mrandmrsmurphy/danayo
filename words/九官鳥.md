## Notes
- [九 (char)](../characters/九%20(char).md) "nine" + [官](../characters/官.md) "official" + [鳥 (char)](../characters/鳥%20(char).md) "bird"

The **common hill myna** (*Gracula religiosa*), a starling native to the hill regions of South and Southeast Asia, from the Indian subcontinent through southern China, Southeast Asia, and Indonesia. It is celebrated across East Asia as one of the most accomplished talking birds in the world — capable of mimicking human speech, laughter, and environmental sounds with remarkable fidelity, and with better tonal accuracy than most parrots.

The origin of the name 九官鳥 ("nine-official bird") is debated. One explanation points to the bright yellow wattles on the bird's neck and face, which were likened to the insignia worn by imperial officials of rank nine (九品) — the lowest tier of the nine-rank official system in the Chinese bureaucracy. Another explanation is purely phonetic. The Japanese name きゅうかんちょう (kyūkanchō) is a direct borrowing of the Chinese.

The hill myna has been kept as a cage bird throughout East Asia for centuries, prized both for its vocal talents and its striking black plumage with white wing patches and vivid orange-yellow facial markings.
