## Notes
- [土 (char)](../characters/土%20(char).md) "earth" + [星 (char)](../characters/星%20(char).md) "star"

土星 is the "Earth Star" — Saturn named for its correspondence with 土 (earth/soil), the fourth of the Five Elements (五行: wood, fire, earth, metal, water). Ancient Chinese astronomers assigned each of the five visible planets to one element: [[木星]] (Jupiter, wood), [[火星]] (Mars, fire), 土星 (Saturn, earth), [[金星]] (Venus, metal), [[水星]] (Mercury, water). Saturn's slow, plodding orbit — roughly 29 years — made 土 (the element of solidity and weight) a fitting match.

The association saturates the calendar: Saturday is [[土曜日]], literally "earth-day." Vietnamese Thổ tinh is a direct calque of the Chinese. The naming convention predates Western contact and is entirely independent of the Latin *Saturnus*.
