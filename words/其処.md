## Notes
- [其 (char)](../characters/其%20(char).md) "that; medial demonstrative" + [処 (char)](../characters/処%20(char).md) "place; location"

其処 is the medial place correlative — "there (near you); at that place (in your sphere or our shared context)." Like all 其-column entries, 其処 picks out a referent in the addressee's space or in the common ground between speaker and listener — the place the other person occupies, the location both parties know about. It contrasts with 此処 (here, near me) and 彼処 (yonder, away from both).

Japanese そこ (soko) is the exact medial place pronoun — "there, near you" — and is one of the most semantically precise cross-linguistic equivalents across the whole correlative table; the Japanese ko/so/a place system (ここ/そこ/あそこ) maps directly onto Dan'a'yo 此処/其処/彼処. Korean 거기 (geogi, "there — medial") does the same. Mandarin 那裡 (nàlǐ) covers both 其処 and 彼処, as Mandarin collapsed the three-way deictic into a proximal/distal (这/那) system; Vietnamese đó (there, medial) is the appropriate single equivalent (Vietnamese distinguishes đây/đó/đằng kia for proximal/medial/distal).
