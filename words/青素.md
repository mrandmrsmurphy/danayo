## Notes
### Form.
青素 is a transparent semantic compound: [青 (char)](characters/青%20(char).md) “blue” + [素](characters/素.md) “element; basic substance.” Within the system, 素 marks main-group elements (groups 1–2, 13–18), so the morphology is regular.

### Motivation.
The name calques the international etymology of _cesium_, from Latin _caesius_ “sky blue.” The reference is to the intense blue lines observed in the emission spectrum at the element’s discovery (Kirchhoff and Bunsen, 1860). Thus 青 captures the salient historical feature without phonetic borrowing.

### Fit within Dan’a’yo conventions

1. It avoids phonetic loans and remains meaning-based.
2. It aligns with other color-derived names in the table (e.g., 茜素, 紫素, 虹素), preserving a recognizable semantic field.
3. The use of 素 is consistent with its placement in group 1.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 铯 (purely phonetic).
- Japanese: セシウム (phonetic katakana).
- Korean: 세슘 (phonetic hangul).
- Vietnamese: xêzi (phonetic).

These forms largely abandon the original semantic motivation. 青素 restores it by reinterpreting the name through its defining scientific observation rather than its later transliterated forms.
### Clarity and ambiguity.
青 “blue/green” can be broad in classical usage, but the association with spectral lines narrows the interpretation. Within a periodic context, ambiguity is minimal, especially given the parallel naming of other elements by color.

### Conclusion
青素 is a faithful semantic reconstruction of _cesium_’s original naming logic, well-formed within the internal grammar of the system and more etymologically transparent than existing CJKV forms.
