## Notes
- [時 (char)](../characters/時%20(char).md) "time; when"

時 is "time" — in Dan'a'yo, 時 functions as a noun for time and as a correlative row heading generating 此時 (now; at this time), 其時 (then; at that time), 彼時 (at that far time; in that distant age), 何時 (when?), 毎時 (every time; always), 某時 (at some time; once). As a temporal adverbial noun, 時 can combine directly with a preceding stative or eventive to form a time clause: 食時 (at mealtime; when eating). The character 時 combines 日 (sun; day) as semantic component with 寺 (OC \*ljɯs) as phonetic, making its etymology transparent as the movement of the sun — time. 時 also serves as the Dan'a'yo stand-in for the character [[時]].

Mandarin 時 (shí): 時間 (shíjiān, time period; time), 時候 (shíhòu, time; moment; when), 時代 (shídài, era; epoch), 有時 (yǒushí, sometimes), 同時 (tóngshí, at the same time), 臨時 (línshí, temporary; provisional). Cantonese 時 (si4): 時間 (si4 gaan3), 有時 (jau5 si4, sometimes). Japanese 時 (とき, toki) in its native reading is one of the most essential Japanese words: 時間 (じかん, jikan, time period; hour), 時代 (じだい, jidai, era — as in 江戸時代, the Edo period). The grammatical とき (toki) is indispensable as a nominalizer: 食べる時に (when eating; at the time of eating). Korean 시 (時) in Sino-Korean: 시간 (時間, time; hour), 시대 (時代, era; epoch), 당시 (當時, at that time), 일시 (一時, temporarily; at one time). Vietnamese: thì (時) is one of the most important Vietnamese grammatical words, functioning as a topic marker ("as for... then...") and as the Sino-Vietnamese reading of 時; thời (時) in compounds: thời đại (era), thời gian (time period).

kwin is true: Dan'a'yo 시 exactly matches Korean 시. Both read si/시 — MC 船母 (ʑ) + 之韻 (ɨ) gives a plain -i vowel with a sibilant onset in both Dan'a'yo and Sino-Korean, producing identical forms. 時 is one of the straightforward kwin: true cases with no phonological divergence in either the initial or the rhyme.
