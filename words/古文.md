## Notes
- [古](../characters/古.md) "ancient, old" + [文](../characters/文.md) "culture, writing" — literally "ancient writing."

古文 (gǔwén) is the traditional Chinese name for what is known in English as Classical Chinese: the written language of the Zhou, Spring and Autumn, Warring States, and Han periods (down to roughly the end of the Han Dynasty, AD 220), in which the great classics of Chinese philosophy, history, and poetry were composed. The term contrasts with [[文言]] ("literary writing"), which refers to the more conservative literary register used after the fall of the Han, modeled on but not identical to 古文.

The Japanese こぶん and Korean 고문 both preserve this same sense of "ancient text" or "the classical canon," and are used in education to refer to pre-modern literary writing — in Japanese, こぶん specifically denotes pre-modern Japanese texts written in classical grammar, by extension from the Chinese term. The Vietnamese cổ văn likewise refers to classical/ancient prose, particularly the Chinese classical tradition as studied in traditional Vietnamese education.

[[Lookup/CC/Classical Chinese]] uses 古文 as its informal name for this period's written language, alongside the more formal <ruby>[古典](/words/古典.md)<rt>ㄍㄛㄐㄝㄋ</rt></ruby><ruby>[漢語](/words/漢語.md)<rt>ㄏㄚㄋ·⼄</rt></ruby> ("Classical Chinese," literally "classical Han language").
