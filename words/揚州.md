## Notes
- [揚 (char)](../characters/揚%20(char).md) + [州 (char)](../characters/州%20(char).md) — Yang Province, the fifth of the [[九州]]

揚州 was the southeastern province, covering the region south of the Yangtze — roughly modern Jiangsu, Zhejiang, and southern Anhui. It was the most southerly and exotic of the nine in early texts, described as a land of rivers, lakes, metals, and strange products. During the Sui and Tang dynasties, the city of 揚州 (Yangzhou) on the Yangtze became one of the wealthiest and most cosmopolitan cities in the world, a hub of the Grand Canal trade. The Tang poet Du Mu's lament for its destruction — 十年一覺揚州夢 — made 揚州 a byword for vanished splendour.
