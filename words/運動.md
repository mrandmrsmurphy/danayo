## Notes
- [運](../characters/運.md) "move; transport" + [動 (char)](../characters/動%20(char).md) "move"

**運動** spans two clusters of meaning united by the idea of directed motion. In its physical sense it means exercise, sport, or athletic activity — the movement of the body. In its political or social sense it means a movement or campaign — coordinated collective action toward a goal. The two readings coexist in all five CJKV languages, though the balance differs: in Mandarin, 运动 is strongly associated with political campaigns (especially in the language of the Communist period), while in Japanese うんどう more readily suggests physical exercise or electioneering.

See also [[運動家]] for the person who practises either sense.
