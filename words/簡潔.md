## Notes
- [簡](../characters/簡.md) "simple, brief" + [潔](../characters/潔.md) "clean, pure" — brief and clean at once: concise, succinct, without clutter.

簡潔 describes language or writing specifically — a 簡潔 explanation says exactly what it needs to and no more — distinguishing it from [[簡略]] (brevity achieved by omitting detail) and [[簡単]] (ease, regardless of length). The grammar book uses it to describe a structural property of Dan'a'yo itself: its adnominal constructions are 簡潔 (concise) compared to Japanese or Korean, though also more rigid (硬直的) as a consequence — a trade-off the book draws explicit attention to. Attested across Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese, and Korean.
