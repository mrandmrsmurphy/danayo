## Notes
- [夜 (char)](../characters/夜%20(char).md) "night" + [半](../characters/半.md) "half" — half of the night; its midpoint.

夜半 is most famously preserved in 楓橋夜泊 ("Night Mooring at Maple Bridge"), a Tang dynasty poem by 張繼 (Zhang Ji, fl. 753), one of the most memorised poems in the Chinese tradition:

> 月落烏啼霜滿天，江楓漁火對愁眠。
> 姑蘇城外寒山寺，夜半鐘聲到客船。
> "The moon sets, crows cry, frost fills the sky; river maples, fishing fires, I lie awake in melancholy.
> Beyond Gusu's walls, at Hanshan Temple — the midnight bell tolls to this traveller's boat."

The 夜半鐘聲 — the midnight bell of Hanshan Temple reaching across the water — became one of the most resonant images in Chinese poetry: sound travelling through cold dark air as the marker of the night's halfway point, experienced by a lone insomniac. The poem made Hanshan Temple famous and the temple still rings a bell each New Year's Eve at midnight in its memory.

In the traditional Chinese division of the day into twelve periods (時辰), 夜半 corresponds to 子時 — 11 pm to 1 am — the hour of the Rat, the first of the Earthly Branches, when the new day is conceptually born in darkness. Modern Korean 자정 (子正, "midpoint of the Rat hour") makes this origin explicit; 야반 (夜半) is the classical Sino-Korean equivalent.
