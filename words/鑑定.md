## Notes
- [鑑](../characters/鑑.md) "mirror; to examine clearly" + [定](../characters/定.md) "to decide, to determine" — to appraise; to authenticate; an expert determination

鑑 is the large bronze mirror used in antiquity — its surface, polished to reflect clearly, was the emblem of objective scrutiny. 鑑定 is the act of holding something up to that mirror and rendering a verdict: the assessment of an expert who sees clearly and decides definitively. The compound covers valuation of antiques (骨董の鑑定), authentication of artworks, forensic analysis, and professional certification.

鑑定士 (appraiser, certified assessor) is the professional title; 不動産鑑定士 (real estate appraiser), 古物鑑定士 (antique authenticator). In legal and forensic contexts, 精神鑑定 (psychiatric evaluation) and DNA鑑定 (DNA analysis) show the compound's extension into evidentiary and clinical assessment — any domain where expert scrutiny produces a binding determination.

The compound is stable across all five languages: Mandarin jiàndìng, Cantonese gaam3 ding6, Japanese かんてい (kantei), Korean 감정 (gamjeong), Vietnamese giám định. The kwin coincidence (Dan'a'yo 감정 = Korean 감정) reflects genuine phonological convergence: both 鑑 and 定 have regular Sino-Korean readings that happen to align with the Dan'a'yo derivation.
