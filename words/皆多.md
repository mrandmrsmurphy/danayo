## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [多 (char)](../characters/多%20(char).md) "many; much; numerous"

皆多 is the universal quantity correlative — "the whole amount; all of it; the total quantity." The character 皆 marks collective universality: 皆多 asserts that the quantity in question is the complete total, leaving nothing out. Where 毎多 distributes an amount across each unit individually, 皆多 treats the full quantity as a unified whole: 皆多を使い切った ("used up the whole amount"); 皆多を一度に ("all at once, the entire quantity"). In practical use, 皆多 answers questions of completeness — is this all? Yes, 皆多.

Mandarin 全部 (quánbù, "all/everything; the whole amount") is the standard equivalent — 全部 functions both nominally (all of it as a noun phrase) and adverbially (all of it, entirely). Japanese 全部 (zenbu, pronounced differently but sharing the characters) is identical in form and similarly versatile: 全部食べた ("ate all of it"); 全部で三人 ("three in total"). Korean 전부 (jeonbu, "all; the whole; entirely") or 모두 (modu, "all; everyone/everything") cover the universal-quantity sense. Vietnamese tất cả (all, everything) or toàn bộ (the whole amount) are the natural equivalents.

皆多 is the quantity counterpart to 皆処 (everywhere), 皆時 (always), and 皆様 (in every way): together these four entries complete the universal row of the Dan'a'yo correlative table across the spatial, temporal, manner, and quantity dimensions. The 皆多/毎多 distinction — "the whole total" (collective) vs "each one's share" (distributive) — mirrors the same distributive/collective split running through all 皆/毎 column pairs.
