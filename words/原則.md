## Notes
- [原](../characters/原.md) "source, original" + [則](../characters/則.md) "rule" — principle; a fundamental guiding rule

原 carries the sense of a source or origin — the flat plain that extends from the foot of a cliff, and by extension whatever is primary or underived. 則 is a rule in the binding sense: the character shows a cauldron (鼎) and a knife (刀), with the associated meaning of a standard by which things are cut or measured. The compound thus means a rule that is foundational rather than derivative — not a specific regulation enacted for a situation, but an underlying commitment from which other judgements follow.

原則 sits in a cluster of related terms that are worth distinguishing: 原理 (yuánlǐ / げんり / 원리) names a principle in the theoretical or scientific sense — a law of how things work; 規則 (guīzé / きそく / 규칙) names a rule in the procedural sense — a stipulated regulation; 準則 (zhǔnzé / じゅんそく / 준칙) names a criterion or operational standard. 原則 occupies the normative register — it is what one appeals to when justifying a course of action as a matter of principle, and what one invokes when refusing to make an exception.

The compound is stable across all five languages: Mandarin yuánzé, Cantonese jyun4 zak1, Japanese げんそく (gensoku), Korean 원칙, Vietnamese nguyên tắc. The Korean 원칙 reflects a divergence in 則: Korean reads the character as 칙 (chik) in this compound rather than the expected 죽, an irregular reading preserved across centuries of borrowing.
