## Etymology
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all, every" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; honorific suffix" — completing the 様-row of the correlative pronoun grid alongside [[此様]], [[其様]], [[彼様]], [[何様]], [[毎様]], and [[某様]].

Unlike the rest of this grid, 皆様 is not purely a Dan'a'yo-internal coinage: the same two characters spell a genuine, extremely common Japanese word, 皆様 (minasama), the standard polite way to address "everyone" in announcements, customer service, and formal speech. The match in spelling and meaning here is a coincidence of independently regular composition (皆 "all" + the honorific suffix 様) rather than a borrowing, but it means this particular grid cell happens to double as a real attested word.
