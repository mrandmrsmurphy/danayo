## Notes
- [皆 (char)](../characters/皆%20(char).md) "all; every; universal" + [様 (char)](../characters/様%20(char).md) "manner; way; style; appearance"

皆様 is the universal manner correlative — "in every way; in all respects; comprehensively." The character 皆 marks collective universality: 皆様 asserts that the predicate holds across the full range of manners or aspects simultaneously: 皆様に優れている ("excellent in every way"); 皆様に同意する ("agree in every respect"). This is the collective counterpart to the distributive 毎様 (in each separate way, one by one): where 毎様 enumerates methods individually, 皆様 asserts comprehensiveness as a whole.

Important Japanese coincidence: 皆様 (minasama) in Japanese is the polite plural "everyone" — 皆 "all" + the honorific suffix 様 — the standard polite form of address in announcements, customer service, and formal speech. In Dan'a'yo, 皆様 (*gye'yang*) is the universal manner correlative "in every way," while the honorific-plural-person function belongs to 皆名. Japanese readers should note that these coincide in writing but diverge completely in Dan'a'yo function and reading. The overlap reflects that the same two characters compose independently in each system (皆 + 様 = "all" + "manner/honorific") rather than any borrowing — and it is the same structural coincidence noted in 皆名.

Mandarin 各方面 (gèfāngmiàn, "in every respect/aspect") is the most natural equivalent; the intensive 方方面面 (fāngfāng miànmiàn, "in every aspect — a reduplicative form") covers the emphatic sense. Japanese あらゆる点で (in every respect), すべての面で (in all aspects), どの点でも (in every single way). Korean 모든 면에서 (in every aspect/way), 어느 모로 보나 (whichever way you look at it). 皆様 is the manner-universal counterpart to 皆処 (everywhere) and 皆時 (always), completing the universal row of the Dan'a'yo correlative table.
