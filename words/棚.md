## Notes
Japanese has essentially abandoned the Sino-Japanese reading of this character in ordinary use: 棚 stands almost always as native たな (tana), not on'yomi ほう, and that holds even inside compounds — 本棚 (hondana, "bookshelf"), 食器棚 (shokkidana, "cupboard"), and even the technical term 大陸棚 (tairikudana, "continental shelf") all keep the native reading rather than switching to Sino-Japanese. Korean does the opposite in that exact same technical term: 대륙붕 ("continental shelf") keeps the Sino-Korean reading 붕, even though the everyday standalone word for an ordinary household shelf is 선반 (native), not bare 붕 — so Korean and Japanese land on opposite readings (Sino vs. native) for the same geological term while both abandon the Sino form for everyday furniture. Vietnamese shows a similar drift: kệ, the ordinary word for "shelf" (kệ sách, "bookshelf"), doesn't derive transparently from a Sino-Vietnamese reading of 棚 either.
