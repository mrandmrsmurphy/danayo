## Notes
- [放](../characters/放.md) "release, let go" + [腐](../characters/腐.md) "rotten, corrupt"

放腐 is the Dan'a'yo rendering of 摆烂 (bǎi làn), the Chinese internet slang that emerged as a darker successor to 躺平 / [[臥平]]. Where 臥平 describes a passive withdrawal — lying down and refusing to rise — 放腐 describes something more active and nihilistic: not merely declining to participate, but actively embracing and accelerating deterioration.

The term entered wide usage on Weibo in March 2022, generating hundreds of millions of reads. Its origin is the Chinese NBA fanbase's adaptation of the English basketball concept of "tanking" — a team deliberately losing games to secure a higher draft pick. This etymology is significant: tanking requires deliberate agency. 摆烂 is not mere resignation; it is a choice to make things worse, a kind of performative collapse.

The backdrop is the same economic pressure that produced 臥平, but sharpened. China's youth unemployment rate reached a record 21.3% in June 2023 following a prolonged economic slowdown. A generation that had been raised on the promise of relentless growth found that education, effort, and ambition were no longer reliably converting into outcomes. If 臥平 was the decision to stop running, 摆烂 is the decision to start walking backwards — a sarcastic, self-deprecating declaration that the game is already lost, so one might as well stop pretending.

The arc 臥平 → 放腐 mirrors a broader tonal shift in Chinese online discourse about youth and work: from the relatively dignified quietism of 2021 to the sardonic nihilism of 2022–23. Both terms were subject to censorship pressure, though 放腐's bleaker register made it harder for the state to propose an optimistic counter-narrative.

In Dan'a'yo, 放 (release, let go) captures the stance adequately despite the slight passive reading; 腐 (rot, decay, corrupt) carries the right sense of deliberate deterioration. The word is coined expressly to gloss 摆烂 and inherits its full social-political valence.
