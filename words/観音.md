## Notes
- [観](../characters/観.md) "observe" + [音](../characters/音.md) "sound"

観音 is short for 観世音(菩薩), the East Asian name for the bodhisattva **Avalokiteśvara**. The name is a translation rather than a transliteration: early translators (following Kumārajīva) read the Sanskrit name as *Avalokita-svara*, "the one who perceives/observes the sounds [of suffering beings]," rendered literally as 観 "observe" + 音 "sound." This contrasts with Xuánzàng's later, more literal rendering 觀自在 ("the lord of observation"), which never displaced 観音 in popular use. The bodhisattva is depicted as male in early Indian Buddhism but became widely worshipped as a female "goddess of mercy" (Guanyin/Gwaneum/Quan Âm) across East Asia.
