## Notes
- [二 (char)](../characters/二%20(char).md) "two" + [百 (char)](../characters/百%20(char).md) "hundred"

The numeral 200. In the East Asian counting system, 百 (hundred) operates as a standalone numeral unit — unlike in English where "hundred" always requires a multiplier ("one hundred"), in Chinese 百 alone means 100 without a leading 一. Thus 二百 unambiguously means 200, while 百 alone means 100.

The Dan'a'yo form follows the pattern: 二 (ㄋㄧㄜ) + 百 (ㄅㄚㄎ) = ㄋㄧㄜㄅㄚㄎ. The anti-counterfeiting form of 二 is 弍 (the character has alias 弍), and for 百, the accountancy form 佰 is used in formal documents to prevent fraudulent alteration of amounts. Compare: [[二十]], [[二千]], [[二万]].
