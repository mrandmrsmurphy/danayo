## Notes
- [秋 (char)](../characters/秋%20(char).md) "autumn" + [田](../characters/田.md) "field; rice paddy" — literally "autumn field(s)"

秋田 (*Akita*) is a prefecture of Japan in the Tōhoku region, on the Sea of Japan coast of northern Honshu, as well as the name of its capital city. The name is a native Japanese place name (やまとことば) written with the characters 秋 ("autumn") and 田 ("rice paddy"), evoking the image of fields ready for the autumn harvest — appropriate for a region long associated with rice cultivation, including the celebrated Akitakomachi (あきたこまち) rice variety.

In Mandarin and Cantonese, 秋田 is read with the regular Sino-Mandarin/Sino-Cantonese values of its two characters (an orthographic borrowing, as with most Japanese place names written in kanji shared with Chinese), giving Qiūtián / cau1 tin4. Korean and Vietnamese, by contrast, render the Japanese place name phonetically as 아키타 / Akita rather than through Sino-Korean or Sino-Vietnamese character readings, following the general modern convention of transliterating Japanese toponyms by sound rather than by character.

秋田 also occurs as a Japanese surname (Akita), and the prefecture name appears combined with [[県]] ("prefecture") as 秋田県 (Akita-ken) in formal contexts — see [[山本]] for an example of 秋田県 used to specify the home prefecture of a Japanese surname.
