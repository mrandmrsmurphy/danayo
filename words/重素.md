## Notes
### Form
重素 is a regular binome: [重 (char)](characters/重%20(char).md) “heavy” + [素](characters/素.md) “element; basic substance.” The suffix matches the rule for main-group elements.

### Motivation
The international name _barium_ derives from Greek _barys_ “heavy,” via _baryta_ (barite, $重硫養_四$). The defining historical observation was the unusually high density of its compounds. 重 directly encodes this etymology.

### Fit within the Dan'a'yo system

1. Meaning-based, not phonetic.
2. Parallel to other semantically reconstructed names (e.g., 青素 for cesium).
3. Uses a high-frequency, transparent graph; easy to remember.

### Comparative CJKV forms
- Modern Chinese: 钡 (phonetic).
- Japanese: バリウム (phonetic).
- Korean: 바륨 (phonetic).
- Vietnamese: bari (phonetic).

A traditional Chinese descriptive term **重晶石** (barite, “heavy spar”) shows the same semantic root, so 重 is historically grounded even within Sinitic chemistry.

**Clarity and ambiguity.** 重 is polysemous (“heavy; weighty; important; repeated”), but within a chemical register the reading “heavy” is strongly cued. The pairing with 素 further narrows interpretation.

**Conclusion.** 重素 is a direct semantic calque of _barium_’s original naming logic, consistent with your conventions and supported by earlier Sinitic terminology.
