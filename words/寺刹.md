## Notes
- [寺](../characters/寺.md) "Buddhist temple" + [刹](../characters/刹.md) "Buddhist sacred domain; moment"

The two characters describe a Buddhist establishment from different angles. 寺 is the native Sinitic word for a walled temple or monastery — originally meaning "government office" (from 士 + 寸, a scribal post) before being assigned to Buddhist institutions arriving from India in the Han dynasty. 刹 (alternatively written 剎) is a transcription of Sanskrit kṣetra ("field, domain"), used in Buddhist texts to mean either a sacred site or — via the Sanskrit kṣaṇa — an instant (刹那). Together 寺刹 pairs the architectural space (寺) with the sanctified domain concept (刹) to produce the formal term for a Buddhist temple complex.

In Korean, 사찰 (寺刹) is the standard formal word for Buddhist temple, used in official and scholarly contexts, alongside the native Korean 절 (jŏl) used in everyday speech. Korean Buddhism having received its tradition largely through Chinese, 寺刹 carries literary and institutional weight that the native word lacks.

A notable phonological quirk: the Sino-Vietnamese reading would yield tự sát, a perfect homophone of 自殺 (tự sát, "suicide"). This collision renders 寺刹 essentially unusable as a Vietnamese compound in spoken contexts; Vietnamese Buddhism uses 절 or chùa (native forms) instead.
