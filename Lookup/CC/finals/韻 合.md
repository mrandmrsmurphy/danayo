> **Final 合** genuinely two-way splits, matching its own documented ap/op ambiguity — and 合 itself lands in the minority group (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
合 [ʌp] splits along its own documented ambiguous D value (`ap/op`): 9 of 16 characters land on **ㄚㄆ** (ap), and 7 land on **ㄛㄆ** (op).

As on [[韻 帖|帖]], the character the final is named after doesn't land in the majority group: **合** itself lands on the minority **ㄛㄆ**. It's part of a near-homophone cluster with **蛤** and **盒** — all k/ɣ-initial, all Korean 합 — that scatters across both groups: 蛤 lands on the majority ㄚㄆ, while 合 and 盒 land together on ㄛㄆ.

Data-quality flag: 䏜's Cantonese field (*ho1*) and Korean field (급) look inconsistent with each other and with its own m-initial — an m-initial 合 word would be expected to read closer to *nap/map*-type in Cantonese and Korean alike.
