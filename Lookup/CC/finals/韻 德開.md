> **Final 德開** splits into two groups, with a notable near-homophone divergence (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
德開 [ək] splits 10–4: 10 characters land on **ㄨㄎ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`uk`); 4 land on plain **ㄜㄎ**.

The split doesn't reduce to crowding or initial class — **徳** and **得** are both t-initial, both Mandarin *dé*, both Cantonese *dak1* (differing only in Korean: 덕 vs 득), yet land in different groups (ㄨㄎ vs ㄜㄎ) with no other t-initial member on either side to force the divergence. A clean, small-scale instance of the "same initial, same reading, arbitrary outcome" pattern documented repeatedly in this sweep.
