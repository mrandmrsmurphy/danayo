> **Final 佳開** splits three ways: ㄚㄧ / ⼘ㄧ / ⺢ㄧ (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
佳開 [ɣɛ] fractures into three real groups: 9 characters land on **ㄚㄧ**, 7 on **⼘ㄧ**, and 5 on **⺢ㄧ**, plus one singleton (擺 → ⼶). This roughly matches the Vowels table's documented dual outcome (`ai/yai`) for the ㄚㄧ/⼘ㄧ portion.

Worth flagging clearly: the 5-member **⺢ㄧ** group is a genuine counter-example to the "合 (rounded) finals reliably carry a w-glide, 開 finals don't" rule confirmed 9/9 with zero exceptions earlier in this sweep (documented in [[skill_cc_phonology]]) — 佳開 is, as its name states, an 開 (unrounded) final, yet 拐, 矮, 柴, 厓, and 洒 all carry the ⺢ w-glide marker anyway. Their MC initials (g, ʔ, d͡ʒ, ŋ, ʃ) share no obvious common thread, so this isn't a phonotactic sub-rule — it looks like a genuine exception to what had otherwise been an exceptionless pattern. This final's own reference-table row does hint at it, listing a `yai` (glide-bearing) alternate alongside the plain `ai`.
