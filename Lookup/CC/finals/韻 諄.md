> **Final 諄** splits into ㄨㄋ vs ⼜ㄋ by initial type, with four characters scattering to four different alternate codas (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
諄 [iuɪn] splits fairly evenly by initial type: 15 characters (mostly real-consonant, non-sibilant initials) land on **ㄨㄋ**, while 13 (null-initial plus most sibilants) land on **⼜ㄋ**, matching the y-glide quality of the Vowels table's tentative winner (`un~`).

The standout finding is what happens to this final's sibilant-initial (s/z/ɕ/d͡ʑ/ʑ) cohort — 13 characters total, all with an original -n coda in every daughter language with no exceptions (循, 隼, 峻, 巡, 旬, 殉, 洵, 瞬, 筍, 舜, 迅, 順, 馴 all show -n in Mandarin, Cantonese, and Korean alike). 9 of them fit onto the already-crowded ⼜ㄋ. The remaining 4 don't just dodge to one alternate slot — they scatter to **four completely different codas**, none matching each other:

- **隼** → **⼜ㄫ** (ŋ-coda)
- **筍** → **⼜ㄊ** (t-coda, a stop — the coda's manner changes entirely, not just its place)
- **舜** → **⼜ㄇ** (m-coda)
- **馴** → **⼜ㄎ** (k-coda, also a stop)

None of these codas are supported by any daughter language — this is homophony avoidance at its most aggressive: with 13 characters competing for one crowded slot, the four overflow characters don't even converge on a shared escape route, they spread across four mutually distinct codas so as not to collide with each other either. This is the richest multi-escape case found in this sweep, surpassing 氏's single cascading double-escape.

A smaller, more ordinary exception: **錀** dodges a modest 2-member ㄌㄨㄋ (輪, 倫, both l-initial) via a plain vowel shift to **ㄛㄋ**.
