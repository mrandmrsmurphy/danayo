## CJKV Evolution
庚三開 [ɣiæŋ] lands 29 of 34 characters on **⼶ㄫ**, matching the y-glide quality of the Vowels table's documented dual/triple winner (`ing/eng/yeng`).

Two crowded sub-slots produce five escapees taking two different overflow routes. The b/p labial cluster on ⼶ㄫ already holds 7 characters (坪, 評, 平, 柄, 丙, 兵, 病); **苹** and **秉**, both also labial, dodge it — 苹 via **ㄝㄫ**, 秉 via **ㄧㄫ**. The k/g cluster is even larger (11 members: 京, 驚, 警, 竟, 境, 景, 競, 鯨, 敬, 荊, 鏡); **檠** dodges it, also landing on **ㄧㄫ**. Separately, the null-initial (ʔ) pair 影/映 already crowds ⼶ㄫ; **瑛** and **英**, both also null-initial, dodge together to **ㄝㄫ**.

Notably, labial initials combine freely with the y-glide (⼶) here — unlike the w-glide ban confirmed repeatedly elsewhere, the documented phonotactic rule only bars labials from the *w*-glide, and this final's own 7-member labial+⼶ group confirms that distinction directly.
