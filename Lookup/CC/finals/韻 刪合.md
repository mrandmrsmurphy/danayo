> **Final еҲӘеҗҲ** splits evenly: вәўг„Ү (m-coda) vs вәўг„Ӣ (n-coda) (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
еҲӘеҗҲ [ЙЈuan] splits perfectly evenly, 4 characters each way, between an **вәўг„Ү (m-coda)** group (жӮЈ, й–ў, ејҜ, ж№ҫ) and an **вәўг„Ӣ (n-coda) group** (з’°, ж…Ј, йӮ„, й ‘). Neither MC initial (both groups mix real consonants and null initials) nor tone (жӮЈ/waan6 and з’°/waan4 land on opposite sides despite sharing an identical Korean reading нҷҳ) explains the split.

Worth flagging: [[ж–Үжі• - 99йҹ»еӣі]]'s own reference table records this final's Dan'a'yo outcome as `wam` (m-coda) вҖ” but еҲӘ is historically a еұұж”қ rime, the -n-coda class (parallel to еҜ’/жЎ“/е…ғ/д»ҷ etc.), not a е’ёж”қ -m-coda rime like и«Ү/иҰғ/й№Ҫ. The m-coda documented in the table only matches half this corpus; the other half's n-coda is what MC rime-class history would actually predict. This may be worth a second look at the reference table itself, though that's outside the scope of this page.
