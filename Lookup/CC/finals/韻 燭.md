> **Final 燭** splits evenly into ㄛㄎ vs ⼄ㄎ, plus one likely mistagged outlier (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
燭 [ɨok] is the entering-tone division-3 counterpart of [[韻 鍾|鍾]], and shows the same kind of unconditioned per-character split expected there: 11 characters land on **ㄛㄎ** and 11 on **⼄ㄎ**, both groups freely mixing real consonant initials with no findable single conditioning rule — consistent with the division-3 "simplicity principle" pattern.

One character doesn't fit either group: **豕** lands on **ㄧㄎ**, but more importantly its daughter readings (Mandarin shǐ, Cantonese ci2, Korean 시) don't resemble an entering-tone -k rime at all — none of them show a stop coda or the expected -u-/-o- vowel quality of this final's other 22 members. This looks like a mistagged `middle_chinese_final` in the source corpus rather than a genuine linguistic exception; 豕 (boar) more plausibly belongs to a 支/脂-class open rime. Flagging for a possible corpus fix rather than treating it as an unconditioned poll outcome.
