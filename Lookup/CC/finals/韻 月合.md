> **Final 月合** fractures four ways, tracking initial type and voicing (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
月合 [ʉɐt] fractures into 4 groups, matching the Vowels table's own dual-uncertain winner (`wet/wat`): **ㄝㄊ** (4: 閥, 罰, 襪, 伐), **⼔ㄊ** (3: 越, 曰, 月, all null/ŋ-initial), **ㄚㄊ** (2: 髪, 発), and **⺢ㄊ** (1: 闕).

The cleanest pattern is a voicing-conditioned vowel split hiding inside the labiodental subset: 伐 (voiced v-initial) lands on ㄈㄝㄊ, while 髪 and 発 (voiceless f-initial) land on ㄈㄚㄊ — same resulting bopomofo consonant letter (ㄈ), different vowel, tracking voicing rather than any crowded-slot dodge. This is a genuine initial-conditioned rule, not homophony avoidance.
