> **Final 屋三** mostly evolved into ㄨㄎ (uk), with two real exceptions (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
屋三 [ɨuk] is the division-3 entering-tone reading of the 屋 rime group, and it's overwhelmingly regular: **ㄨㄎ (uk) for 41 of 45 characters**. Two characters, 育 and 郁, spell the same final as **⼜ㄎ** instead — but this isn't a real exception: both have a null/glide MC initial (以 j and 影 ʔ respectively), so the glide fuses into the single bopomofo vowel-letter ⼜ ("yu") the same way it does throughout [[聲 以|以]]'s and [[聲 影|影]]'s own pages, rather than being written as a separate consonant. The underlying final is still `uk`.

The two genuine exceptions:
- **尗 → ㄙ⼜ㄎ** inserts that same glide (⼜) after a *real* consonant initial, and it's unexplained. 尗 and 叔 share the exact same MC initial (書 ɕ) and the exact same final (ɨuk) — a minimal pair — yet 叔 reads plain ㄙㄨㄎ while 尗 reads ㄙ⼜ㄎ with the glide inserted. Nothing in either character's Mandarin (shū for both), Cantonese, or Korean readings explains the difference.
- **偪 → ㄆㄧㄆ** doesn't even keep the expected final at all. Its Dan'a'yo reading (`pib`) has nothing to do with `uk` — but it matches its own Korean reading exactly: 偪's Korean is 핍 (*pip*), and the Dan'a'yo final `-ib` (ㄧㄆ) is just that Korean final carried over directly, bypassing the regular MC-final derivation entirely. No other character in this final's roster shows anything like it — it appears to have been derived from Korean alone, de novo.
