> **Final 東三** mostly evolved into ㄨㄫ (ung), with two homophony-driven exceptions (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
東三 [ɨuŋ] is almost entirely uniform: **ㄨㄫ (ung) for 29 of 31 characters**. That count folds in 融, whose syllable is spelled ⼜ㄫ rather than plain ㄨㄫ — but per the same rule established on [[聲 以|以]]'s own page, 融 has a null/glide MC initial (以 j), so the glide belongs to the initial, not the final; its real final is the regular `ung`.

The two genuine exceptions — **瘋 → ㄈㄜㄫ** and **馮 → ㄧㄫ** — both look like deliberate homophony avoidance, not unexplained scatter. Both characters have a light-labial initial (非 f for 瘋, 奉 v for 馮) that would regularly combine with this final's `ㄨㄫ` to give `ㄈㄨㄫ` — but that exact syllable is already doubly occupied within this very final by 風 and 楓 (both attested as `ㄈㄨㄫ`). Rather than adding a third (瘋) or fourth (馮, whose own initial 奉 usually surfaces as `b` rather than `f` — see [[聲 奉|奉]] — making this a double deviation) homophone to an already-crowded slot, both were shifted to a different vowel instead. This is a concrete, corpus-verifiable instance of the general homophony-reduction principle behind Dan'a'yo's vowel choices.
