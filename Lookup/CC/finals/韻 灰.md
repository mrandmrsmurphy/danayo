## CJKV Evolution
灰 [uʌi] lands 39 of 43 characters on **ㄛㄧ**, loosely matching the Vowels table's tentative winner (`oi~`, marked uncertain).

This final's labial-initial (p/b) cohort shows a genuinely new kind of homophony avoidance not yet seen elsewhere in this sweep. Of 8 total p/b-initial characters, 4 (陪, 賠, 背, 悖) land on the expected **ㄅㄛㄧ**, but the other 4 (徘, 佩, 培, 輩) land on **ㄈㄛㄧ** instead — reusing the labiodental ㄈ letter normally reserved for 非/敷/奉-type MC initials, even though all four are genuine bilabial p/b-initial characters with no labiodental quality in any daughter reading (徘: pái/pui4/배; 佩: pèi/pui3/패; 培: péi/pui4/배; 輩: bèi/bui3/배). Rather than dodging a crowded slot by changing the *vowel* — the strategy used everywhere else in this sweep — these four dodge by changing the *initial letter itself*, borrowing an entirely different consonant class's spelling to keep from colliding with the main labial cluster.

Two smaller pairs round out the exceptions: **呆** and **苺** (both m-initial) dodge a 6-member ㄇㄛㄧ (毎, 梅, 煤, 媒, 妹, 枚) by shifting to **ㄚㄧ**. **腿** and **退** (both tʰ-initial) land on **ㄧㄜ** with no crowded slot to explain it — an unconditioned pair, since no other t/tʰ-initial character occupies this final's main group at all.
