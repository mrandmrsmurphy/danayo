> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Final 之** evolved into ㄧ, with a striking three-way split among its own sibilant-initial members (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
之 [ɨ] is the largest final documented on this project so far (91 characters) and converges well: 67 land on **ㄧ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`i`).

The standout finding is inside the sibilant-initial (s/z-type) subset itself. All characters whose MC initial maps to bopomofo ㄙ split three ways with no findable conditioning factor: 9 stay on the main **ㄧ** vowel (詩, 侍, 時, 試, 始, 伺, 市, 史, 使), 8 land on **ㄚ** (祠, 飼, 似, 思, 寺, 詞, 司, 糸), and 4 land on **ㄜ** (柿, 祀, 嗣, 巳) — three genuinely different outcomes for what is otherwise the exact same initial class, with real consonant type, voicing, and MC rime all identical across the three groups. This is the clearest large-scale illustration yet of the vault's "simplicity principle" (no findable rule, arbitrary per-character pick) operating within a single initial class rather than across different ones.

Two further singleton exceptions round out the page. **俟** would regularly land on ㄙㄧ — the single most crowded slot on the entire page, already shared by all 9 of the ㄙㄧ characters listed above — and instead escapes to a wholly unique **ㄐㄧㄜ**. **士** is likewise unique, landing on **ㄙㄚㄧ**; interestingly, its MC initial (d͡ʒ) is shared only by 事, which nonetheless lands in the main ㄧ group, so the two haven't converged despite matching initials.
