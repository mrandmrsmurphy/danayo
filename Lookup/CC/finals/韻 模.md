> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Final 模** evolved into ㄛ, with several homophony-driven overflow groups (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
模 [uo] is a division-1 final and converges as expected: 90 of 101 characters land on **ㄛ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`o`).

The exceptions trace to two extraordinarily crowded slots. **ㄏㄛ** alone holds 13 characters in the main group (冴, 戸, 琥, 狐, 乎, 虎, 弧, 壷, 呼, 扈, 雇, 護, 互) — the single most crowded syllable found anywhere in this sweep so far. Under that pressure, 5 more ɣ-initial characters (胡, 湖, 瑚, 糊, 醐) overflow to **ㄛㄨ** rather than join it. **ㄌㄛ** is nearly as crowded (8 members: 路, 炉, 盧, 鈩, 賂, 虜, 露, 芦), and 魯 — sharing that same l-initial — dodges by switching coda entirely to **ㄛㄇ** (m-coda) rather than picking a new vowel, echoing the coda-shift dodge already seen on 冬 (彤) and 刪合.

**溥** is the most striking case: its first-choice overflow slot ㄆㄛㄨ is *already* taken by 舗 (also pʰ-initial), while the regular ㄆㄛ holds 3 more pʰ-initial characters (怖, 普, 浦). With both the regular slot and the obvious escape route occupied, 溥 needs a genuine second-level dodge and lands on **ㄨㄛ** — the same kind of cascading double-avoidance already documented on 氏 ([[韻 脂A三開|脂A三開]]).

租, 組, and 祖 (all t͡s-initial) land together on **ㄜ**, likely for the same reason: their regular ㄐㄛ slot already holds 粗 and 祚 (d͡z-initial, same bopomofo letter).
