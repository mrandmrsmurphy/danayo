> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Final 泰合** splits cleanly two ways: ⼔ and ⺢ㄧ (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
泰合 [uɑi] is the rounded (合口) reading of the 泰 rime, and it splits evenly: **⼔ for 會/最 (2 characters)** and **⺢ㄧ for 外/桧/歪/絵 (4 characters)**. The split isn't driven by MC initial — both groups mix real consonants and null initials (會 has 曉 x, 最 has 精 t͡s; 外/歪 are null-initial 疑 ŋ, while 桧/絵 have real consonants 見 k and 匣 ɣ) — nor does it track cleanly through any single daughter language: 會 and 絵 even share the identical Korean reading (회) despite landing in different Dan'a'yo groups. This looks like a genuine two-way split inherited from the final itself, not something conditioned by what precedes it.
