> **Final 魚** splits into ⼄ vs ㄛ by initial type, with recurring intra-class fracturing (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
魚 [ɨʌ] is a division-3 final and fractures heavily, as expected: 44 of 75 characters land on **⼄**, 21 on **ㄛ**, and the rest scatter further. The ⼄/ㄛ split loosely tracks initial type — null/yod, velar, lateral, and most sibilant initials land on ⼄, while retroflex and palatal-affricate initials dominate ㄛ — matching the dual outcome (`o/yo`) the Vowels table already documents for this final. But the split isn't clean by initial class, which is where the real finding is.

This final independently confirms the "same initial class splits arbitrarily" phenomenon first spotted at scale on [[韻 之|之]] — and it recurs *twice* here, for two different initial classes:
- The retroflex sibilant **ʃ** (書母/生母-type) splits: 梳 lands on ㄙㄛ (the main ㄛ group), while 所, 蔬, 疎, and 疋 — sharing the identical MC initial — land on **ㄙㄜ** instead. No conditioning factor distinguishes them.
- The palatal nasal **ȵ** splits *three* ways: 如 lands on the main ⼄ group (ㄋ⼄), 女 lands on ㄋㄜ, and 汝 lands on a wholly unique **ㄛㄧ** (ㄋㄛㄧ) — three different vowel outcomes for one MC initial on one final.

The remaining singletons (箸 → ⺢, 俎 → ㄛㄨ, 除 → ㄝㄧ) each dodge a slot already shared by 1–2 other same-initial characters (猪/貯 on ㄐㄛ for 箸's would-be neighbors; 阻/詛 for 俎; 儲/佇 for 除) — ordinary homophony overflow, consistent with everything else found this sweep.
