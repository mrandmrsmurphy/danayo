> **Final 凡** is an extreme case of true-homophone scatter — all 10 characters read essentially alike, yet land in five different groups (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
凡 [ɨɐm] is one of the most striking homophony-avoidance cases in this sweep. All 10 characters share the same phonetic root (凡) and read almost identically across every language — Mandarin *fán*/*fàn*, Cantonese *faan3/4/6*, Korean 범, without exception — yet Dan'a'yo scatters them across **five** separate outcome groups: 5 on **ㄚㄇ**, 2 on **ㄛㄇ**, and one each on **ㄨㄆ**, **ㄧㄇ**, and **ㄚㄋ**.

Two of the escapes go beyond vowel-shift into coda-class changes, extending the coda-shift homophony-avoidance mechanism catalogued elsewhere in this sweep: **柉** swaps its nasal coda (-m) for a stop coda (-p), landing on ㄨㄆ; **釩** swaps -m for -n, landing on ㄚㄋ. Between the sheer number of true homophones (10) and the number of distinct escape routes (5), this final is the densest concentration of the "true homophones, arbitrary outcomes" pattern found so far, surpassing even [[韻 麻二合|麻二合]]'s earlier-documented cluster.
