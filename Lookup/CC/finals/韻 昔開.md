> **Final 昔開** evolved into ㄝㄎ, with a crowding-driven y-initial escape and two double-shift escapees (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
昔開 [iᴇk] lands 23 of 31 characters on **ㄝㄎ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`ek`). A further 5 y-initial characters (䋇, 駅, 易, 液, 訳) land on **⼶ㄎ** — the vault's standard y-initial spelling, matching the convention confirmed on [[韻 庚三開|庚三開]] and [[韻 清開|清開]], not an exception.

**亦** (also y-initial) is a near-homophone of 訳/駅 (all Mandarin *yì*, Cantonese *jik6*/*yik6*, Korean 역) and dodges the already-crowded 5-member ⼶ㄎ slot, landing alone on **ㄜㄎ**.

Two further escapees land together on **ㄧㄎ** by different routes. **刺** (t͡sʰ) shares its bopomofo letter ㄑ with a crowded 3-member slot (尺, 赤, 斥, all t͡ɕʰ) and dodges via vowel-shift. **辟** — MC initial b, which by the vault's usual mapping should spell ㄅ like p-initial 璧 — instead takes the aspirated letter ㄆ (as if pʰ, like 癖/僻) and *then* shifts vowel, a double-shift escape reminiscent of the initial-letter-borrowing mechanism documented on [[韻 灰|灰]].
