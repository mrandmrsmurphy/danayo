> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Final 登開** genuinely three-way splits, matching its own ambiguous documented D value

## CJKV Evolution
登開 [əŋ] is one of the few finals whose Vowels-table D value is itself listed as ambiguous (`ǝ/o/ung`), and the corpus bears that out directly: 8 of 15 characters land on **ㄜㄫ** (the ǝ reading), 6 land on **ㄨㄫ** (the ung reading), and 1 (硼) lands on **ㄛㄫ** (the o reading).

The split doesn't track initial cleanly — d-initial 謄/騰 land on ㄜㄫ while d-initial 鄧 lands on ㄨㄫ — so this reads as a genuine three-way historical split rather than a single dominant outcome with motivated exceptions, unlike most other finals in this sweep.
