> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Final 齊開** evolved into ㄝㄧ, with two small homophony-driven overflow groups (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
齊開 [ei] is a division-4 final and converges cleanly: 58 of 63 characters land on **ㄝㄧ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`ei`).

The dental-stop initials (d/t/tʰ) form an unusually large combined cohort here — 6 members on ㄉㄝㄧ (第, 蹄, 抵, 邸, 低, 底) plus 8 on ㄊㄝㄧ (締, 帝, 体, 題, 諦, 剃, 醍, 逓), 14 characters total sharing essentially one syllable family. Under that pressure, 4 more dental-initial characters (啼, 氐, 弟, 梯) overflow to plain **ㄝ**, dropping the -ㄧ entirely.

**唳** dodges a similarly crowded ㄌㄝㄧ (7 members: 犂, 隷, 黎, 礼, 莉, 醴, 離) — but rather than picking a new vowel, it reuses the **⼶** spelling normally reserved for null/yod-initial characters, even though 唳's own initial (l) is a real consonant. This is the same kind of borrowed-spelling escape already seen on 誰 ([[韻 脂A合|脂A合]]).
