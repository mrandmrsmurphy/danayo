> **Final 葉三** genuinely scatters, matching its own three-way documented ambiguity, plus a variant-character split (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
葉三 [iᴇp]'s Vowels-table D value is itself listed as three-way ambiguous (`op/up/ep`), and the corpus mostly bears that out: 4 of 7 characters land on **ㄛㄆ** (op), 1 (捷) on **ㄝㄆ** (ep), and 1 (枼) on **ㄨㄆ** (up) — plus 葉 itself lands separately on a fourth group, **⼄ㄆ**.

**葉** and **枼** are, historically, the same character — 枼 is the ancient form from which 葉 derives — and share identical readings (both j-initial, Mandarin *yè*, Cantonese *jip6*, Korean 엽), yet land on two different groups (⼄ㄆ vs ㄨㄆ). The same "identical character, arbitrary outcome" pattern documented on [[韻 乏|乏]]'s 法/灋 pair.
