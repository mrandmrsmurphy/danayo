> **Final 微合** mostly evolved into ㄨㄧ, with a striking split among its own labiodental-initial members (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
微合 [ʉi] lands 26 of 36 characters on **ㄨㄧ**, matching the `ui` half of the Vowels table's documented dual winner (`oi/ui`) for this final.

The standout finding is what happens to the final's 9 labiodental-initial (f/fʰ) characters — an unusually large single-initial cohort for one final. Rather than converging on one outcome, they scatter across **four** different vowel qualities: 斐, 扉, and 緋 keep the main ㄨㄧ outcome (ㄨ as vowel nucleus, ㄧ as off-glide coda — not an on-glide, so this doesn't touch the "ㄈ takes no on-glide" phonotactic rule at all); 菲, 誹, 妃, and 非 land on plain **ㄧ** with no ㄨ nucleus; 飛 lands on its own **ㄝㄧ**; and 費 lands on **ㄚㄧ**. With 9 characters needing distinct syllables from a single initial class, spreading them across four vowel outcomes looks like homophony avoidance operating at the scale of an entire initial cohort, not just one exception dodging one collision — the same phenomenon already confirmed at the sub-initial-class level on [[韻 之|之]] and [[韻 魚|魚]].

Also worth noting: **彙**, already perfected on its own [[彙 (char)|character page]] as the character that *kept* a real historical consonant others in its 云-initial family lost, lands on the singleton outcome **ㄨ** here — its own dedicated finding, cross-referenced rather than re-derived.
