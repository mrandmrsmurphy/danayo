> **Final 支三開** mostly evolved into ㄝ, with homophony-driven overflow groups ㄧ, ㄜ, and one cascading double-escape (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
支三開 [iᴇ] lands 25 of 38 characters on **ㄝ**, matching the Vowels table's own documented winner (`e`) for this final exactly. The remaining 13 characters split into three overflow groups, and nearly all of them trace directly back to a crowded same-initial slot within the ㄝ group:

- **ㄧ (10 members: 婢, 匙, 斯, 移, 脾, 罹, 翅, 祇, 痺, 蜘)**: the ㄝ group's ㄙㄝ slot alone already holds 8 characters (是, 堤, 弛, 提, 施, 徙, 璽, 賜) — by far the most crowded slot on this page — which explains why 匙, 斯, and 翅 (all sibilant-initial) overflow to ㄙㄧ. Similarly ㄐㄝ (6 members: 漬, 紙, 枝, 支, 肢, 紫) pushes 蜘 out to ㄐㄧ, and ㄍㄝ (3 members: 岐, 伎, 示) pushes 祇 out to ㄍㄧ. 婢, 脾, and 痺 all share phonetic component [[卑]] itself (which sits on ㄅㄝ) — using the regular ㄅㄝ output would make all three literal homophones of their own phonetic ancestor. 移 (null/yod initial) is the one member with no crowding to dodge — likely just the plain phonotactic default for yod-initial characters on this final, not an avoidance case.
- **ㄜ (2 members: 璃, 此)**: same story — 麗 already holds ㄌㄝ alone, and 只/雌 already hold ㄑㄝ, so 璃 and 此 each shift to a second escape vowel instead of ㄧ.
- **ㄧㄜ (1 member: 氏)**: the most striking case. 氏 shares its MC initial (ʑ) with 堤/提, both already on the maximally-crowded ㄙㄝ. Its first-choice overflow, ㄙㄧ, is *also* already occupied (by 匙/斯/翅) — so 氏 needs a second escape and lands on the wholly distinct ㄙㄧㄜ, a genuine cascading double-avoidance unique on this project so far.
