## CJKV Evolution
刪開 [ɣan] converges mostly: 17 of 19 characters land on **ㄚㄋ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`an`).

Both escapees dodge crowded slots. Null-initial (ʔ/ŋ) characters spell without a leading letter here, and that slot already holds three members (贋, 雁, 顔, all ŋ); **晏** (ʔ) — which even shares Mandarin *yàn* with 贋/雁 — dodges a fourth collision, landing on **⼘ㄋ**. Separately, the k-initial slot ㄍㄚㄋ already holds two members (姦, 菅); **諌** (k) dodges it, landing alongside 晏 on **⼘ㄋ**.

Data-quality flag: 諌's Mandarin (*dǒng*) and Cantonese (*dung2*) fields don't match its own Korean (간), Japanese (KAN), or Vietnamese (*gián*) — all of which point to the expected "admonish" reading (roughly *jiàn*/*gaan3*), not "dǒng." The mandarin/cantonese fields look miscopied, plausibly from 董.
