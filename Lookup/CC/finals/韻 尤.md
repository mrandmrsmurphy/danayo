> **Final 尤** splits four ways (⼜/ㄨㄛ/⼜ㄛ/ㄨ), plus a handful of smaller exceptions (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
尤 [ɨu] is the biggest final page in this project (114 characters), and it genuinely earns that size with a real four-way split — **⼜ (50), ㄨㄛ (29), ⼜ㄛ (17), and ㄨ (12)**, 108 characters between them. This isn't initial-driven the way some earlier splits turned out to be: both ⼜ and ㄨ (and both ㄨㄛ and ⼜ㄛ) mix real consonant initials and null/glide initials freely, and the same MC initial can land in either half of a pair — 讐 and 受 are both 船-initial (ʑ) yet 讐 → ㄙㄨ (plain) while 受 → ㄙ⼜ (glide). Nothing about the initial predicts which side a character falls on; this looks like the same per-character/phonetic-family assignment ("guessability") documented throughout the initials pages, just operating on the final side instead.

Beyond the four main groups, six characters sit further out:
- **否/愁/驟 → ㄚㄨ (au)** is a real, explainable minor group: all three have a genuine `-au` rime in Cantonese (fau2, sau4, zaau6), which the Dan'a'yo output tracks directly.
- **就 → ⺢ㄧ** is a singleton, plausibly tracking Korean 취's own `-wi`-shaped vowel, though this is speculative with only one data point.
- **捜 → ㄛㄨ (ou)** is a singleton close to but distinct from the main groups — plausibly a literal echo of Mandarin sōu's own `-ou`.
- **肘 → ㄨㄇ** is the one genuinely bizarre case: an unexpected nasal `-m` coda with no support anywhere in Mandarin (zhǒu), Cantonese (zaau2), or Korean (주) — none of which have any coda at all. Unexplained, the same class of anomaly as 屋三's 偪.
