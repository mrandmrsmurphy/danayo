> **Final 豪** evolved into ㄚㄨ, with a phonetic-family scatter among its exceptions (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
豪 [ɑu] is a division-1 final and converges cleanly: 81 of 85 characters land on **ㄚㄨ**, matching the Vowels table's documented winner (`au`).

The 4 exceptions cluster around one massively crowded t͡s-initial slot, ㄐㄚㄨ, which already holds 8 characters (蚤, 遭, 糟, 繰, 棗, 早, 槽, 曹). **竃** and **藻**, both also t͡s-initial, dodge that crowd via two different routes — 竃 drops the vowel entirely, landing on bare **⺢**; 藻 shifts vowel instead, landing on **ㄛㄨ**. Their shared phonetic component **喿** (also the phonetic root of 燥, 操, and 繰, all of which stay in the main ㄚㄨ group) itself dodges a separate crowded slot — the 5-member s-initial ㄙㄚㄨ (騒, 掃, 嫂, 掻, 燥) — via a third route, adding a w-glide to land on **⺢ㄨ**. Three members of the same phonetic family, three different escape strategies.

**𡿺**, an n-initial character with modest crowding pressure from 脳/悩 (2 members) on ㄋㄚㄨ, lands on **ㄨㄛ** — changing both vowel and coda.
