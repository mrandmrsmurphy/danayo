> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Initial 曉** mostly evolved into ㄏ, with small ㄍ/ㄑ/⼔ outcomes (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
曉 [x] is the 清 (voiceless) velar fricative, paired with [[聲 匣|匣]] (voiced) in the laryngeal/velar-fricative group alongside [[聲 影|影]] and [[聲 云|云]]. It's overwhelmingly regular: **ㄏ (h) for 101 of 106 characters**. Three characters — 硅, 軣, 霍 — land on **ㄍ** (a full manner shift to an unaspirated velar stop) instead of the expected fricative, and this is well-supported: both Cantonese (gwai1, gwang1, fok3) and Korean (규, 굉, 곽) show a genuine stop *g-*, not a fricative, for all three. The single **ㄑ** outcome, 蓄, is aspirated in both Cantonese (cuk1) and Korean (축). Last, **矣 → ⼔** (a null/glide reading) is fully regular too — Mandarin (yǐ), Cantonese (ji5), and Korean (의) all agree there's no initial consonant at all.
