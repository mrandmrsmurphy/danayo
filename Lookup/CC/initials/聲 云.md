> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Initial 云** mostly evolved into ø (null initial), with one ㄏ singleton (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
云 [ø] is the true "zero initial" of the laryngeal series, distinct from [[聲 影|影]] (ʔ) and [[聲 疑|疑]] (ŋ) in that it never had a real consonant even in Middle Chinese itself. Unsurprisingly, it's almost entirely null in Dan'a'yo too: **ø for 49 of 50 characters**, the same vowel-only, apostrophe-羅馬字 pattern already documented on [[聲 疑|疑]] and [[聲 影|影]]. The lone exception, **彙 → ㄏ**, is well-supported: both Mandarin (huì) and Korean (휘) show a genuine h-, even though Cantonese (wai6) stays glide-only.
