> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Initial 云** mostly evolved into ø (null initial), with one ㄏ survival (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
云's frontmatter value `ø` is a simplifying convention, not a claim that Middle Chinese itself had silence here. Modern reconstructions disagree on the exact phonetic value but agree it was a real consonant: Zhengzhang Shangfang writes 云-initial syllables with a voiced laryngeal fricative, e.g. 彙 as `/ɦʉiH/`; Baxter's romanization marks the same thing with a leading `h` (`hjw+jH` for 彙) — not aspiration, but his notation for that same murmured onset; and the deeper Old Chinese source (this vault's own OC reconstruction for 彙: `*ɢruds`) points to a uvular `*ɢ-`. What actually happened is that this onset was lost almost everywhere by the time of the daughter languages compared here — hence **ø for 49 of 50 characters**, the same vowel-only, apostrophe-羅馬字 pattern documented on [[聲 疑|疑]] and [[聲 影|影]]. **彙 → ㄏ is therefore not really an exception needing daughter-language rescue — it's the one character in this corpus that *kept* the original consonant** (as `h-` in Mandarin `huì` and Korean `휘`) rather than losing it to zero like the other 49.
