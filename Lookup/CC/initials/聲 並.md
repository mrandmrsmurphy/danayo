## CJKV Evolution
並 [b] is a 濁 (voiced) initial. The textbook Mandarin story for MC voiced obstruents is clean and tone-conditioned — 平送氣仄不送氣ˊ: characters with an original level tone (平聲) devoice to an *aspirated* initial, everything else devoices *unaspirated*. Since Dan'a'yo drops tone entirely, that conditioning can't carry over directly — instead, each character's reading was fixed either by the same MC-initial+final polling process described for [[聲 幫|幫]], or, just as often, by borrowing wholesale from an existing character that shares its phonetic component, for guessability. That second impulse is powerful but imperfect: it only holds when the exact MC final also matches. A phonetic family can still fracture once finals diverge even slightly — e.g. 皮 itself reads **ㄅㄧ** (unaspirated), yet its own derivatives 疲 and 被 (same phonetic component) read **ㄆㄧ** (aspirated), because each was independently matched to whatever existing character shared its own precise final, not necessarily to 皮 itself. The reverse also happens: 佩, 培, and 徘 share no phonetic component at all (佩, 咅, 非 respectively) but all converge on **ㄈㄛㄧ**, because they share the same final — the final, not the phonetic, is the real mechanical anchor; shared phonetics are only a common correlate of it. The net result: **ㄅ (b) for the large majority (85 of 98)**, with **ㄆ (p, 10 characters)** and **ㄈ (f, 3 characters — 佩/培/徘, a reassignment to the light-labial series)** as minority outcomes.
