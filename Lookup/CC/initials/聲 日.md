> **Initial 日** evolved into ㄋ, per a deliberate naming decision (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
日 [ȵ] is the palatal nasal, the 半齒音 ("half-dental") counterpart with no series partner, parallel to how [[聲 來|來]] stands alone among the liquids. Its Dan'a'yo outcome was fixed by a direct design decision rather than derived purely from the daughter languages: as the initial sound of 日 itself — the first character of "Japan" — it was deliberately set to **n**, tying this initial's identity to its own namesake character in a way most of the other thirty-five aren't. Whether by that design choice or by the nasal class's usual stability (matching [[聲 泥|泥]]/[[聲 娘|娘]]/[[聲 明|明]]/[[聲 疑|疑]]-when-audible), the result is fully uniform: all 51 characters land on **ㄋ (n)**, with no exceptions.
