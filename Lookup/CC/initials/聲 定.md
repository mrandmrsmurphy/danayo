## CJKV Evolution
定 [d] is a 濁 (voiced) dental stop, the exact dental counterpart of [[聲 並|並]] in the labial series — the same 平送氣仄不送氣 tone-conditioned devoicing story applies in Mandarin, and the same problem applies in Dan'a'yo: with tone gone, each character's reading was fixed individually, either by MC initial+final polling or by borrowing from a character sharing its phonetic component. The clearest example here is self-referential: 定 itself reads **ㄐㄝㄫ** — a palatalized outcome, not the plain dental ㄉ/ㄊ one might expect, closer to its Sino-Korean reading 정 (jeong) than to its Mandarin dìng or Cantonese ding6 (both plain d-). Its own derivative 錠 (which uses 定 as phonetic component) inherits that exact reading, **ㄐㄝㄫ**, unchanged — a clean case of guessability working as intended. 条 joins this same small palatalized pocket at **ㄐㄛ**, despite not sharing 定/錠's phonetic component, on the strength of its own daughter readings. Beyond this handful, the 濁→devoicing split proceeds as with 並: **ㄉ (unaspirated) for the large majority (127 of 139)**, with a smaller **ㄊ (aspirated) minority (9 characters)**.
