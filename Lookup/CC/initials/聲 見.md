> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Initial 見** mostly evolved into ㄍ, with a ㄎ minority outcome (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
見 [k] is a 清 initial — plain voiceless, neither aspirated nor voiced — the exact velar counterpart of [[聲 幫|幫]], and it shows the same pattern: no stable cross-linguistic aspiration signal, so each character's reading was fixed individually by MC initial+final polling or by phonetic-component guessability rather than by one rule. Here that produces the most lopsided split of any 清 initial documented so far: **ㄍ (unaspirated) for 284 of 301 characters (94%)**, with only **ㄎ (aspirated) as a minority (17 characters)**. The ㄎ pocket is mostly characters whose modern Mandarin reading has palatalized to j-/q- (九 jiǔ, 巾 jīn, 荊 jīng, 錦 jǐn) despite Cantonese and Korean both keeping a plain unaspirated velar (gau2/구, gan1/건, ging1/형, gam2/금) — evidence pointed the poll toward ㄎ anyway. Phonetic families hold together well within this pocket: 九, 玖, 鳩, and 軌 all share the phonetic component 九 and all land on ㄎ, matching each other exactly, unlike [[聲 定|定]]'s 皮 example — guessability here reinforces the minority outcome consistently rather than fracturing it.
