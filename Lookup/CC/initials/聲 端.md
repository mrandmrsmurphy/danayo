> [Classical Chinese](../Classical%20Chinese.md)
> **Initial 端** mostly evolved into ㄉ, with ㄊ/ㄑ/ㄐ/ㄙ minority outcomes (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
端 [t] is a 清 initial, the exact dental counterpart of [[聲 幫|幫]] and [[聲 見|見]] — the same lack of a stable cross-linguistic aspiration signal produces the same majority/minority split, **ㄉ for 56 of 80 characters** against a **ㄊ minority of 16**. But 端 adds a second, genuinely different phenomenon on top: real palatalization. Five characters — 鳥, 鵰, 彫, 貂, 釣 — share nothing in common phonetically (different phonetic components: 鳥, 鳥, 周, 召, 勺) except an identical MC final (`eu`), and all five land together on **ㄑ**, the same final-not-phonetic clustering already seen with [[聲 並|並]]'s 佩/培/徘 and [[聲 見|見]]'s 九/玖/鳩/軌. A second, smaller palatalized pocket — 弔 and 頂, with two different finals (`ek`, `eŋ`) — lands on **ㄐ**.
