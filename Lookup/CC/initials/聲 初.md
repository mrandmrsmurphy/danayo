> **Initial 初** evolved into ㄑ

## CJKV Evolution
Mandarin splits 初 by rime division into retroflex `tʂʰ-` or dental `tsʰ-`; Cantonese levels it to `tsʰ-`; Vietnamese shows the `ʂ > s` shift shared across the whole 生/莊/初/崇 block; Korean merges it with 莊 as `tɕ/tɕʰ`; and all three Sino-Japanese layers (go-on, kan-on, tō-on) agree on `s-`. **In Dan'a'yo, 初 merges with [[聲 清|清]] (精組, dental) and [[聲 昌|昌]] (章組, palatal) — the aspirated-affricate member of each of the three Middle Chinese sibilant/affricate series — into the single phoneme /c/**, since Dan'a'yo keeps the aspiration contrast among affricates but neutralizes their original place of articulation.
