> **Initial 以** evolved into y (glide)

## CJKV Evolution
以 [j] is the palatal glide/semivowel, distinct from the true zero initials ([[聲 疑|疑]], [[聲 影|影]], [[聲 云|云]]) in that it keeps an actual y- onset rather than losing the initial entirely. All 120 characters here confirm a genuine y- glide in Mandarin (yì, yǐ, yán, yè, and so on throughout); the apparent variety of bopomofo onset symbols across entries (⼶/⼜/⼄/⼘/ㄧ/ㄜ/⼔/ㄨ) is purely a matter of which vowel follows the glide, not a different consonant outcome — unlike every other initial page in this set, there's no real place/manner split to group by, just one y- onset realized before many different vowels.
