> **Initial 章** mostly evolved into ㄐ, with ㄑ/ㄊ minority outcomes (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
章 [t͡ɕ] is the 清 (plain) member of the palatal sibilant series (章組), pairing with [[聲 昌|昌]]/[[聲 禪|禪]]/[[聲 書|書]]/[[聲 船|船]]. Like [[聲 精|精]]'s dental counterpart, most of it is regular: **ㄐ for 93 of 99 characters**. Of the 5-character **ㄑ** minority, three are explained once Korean is checked rather than just Mandarin: 彰/招/燭 (zhāng/zhāo/zhú — all unaspirated in Mandarin) have Korean readings 창/초/촉, all starting with aspirated ㅊ. The remaining two, 只 and 者, have no daughter-language support either way and are simply poll-only exceptions. Last, a genuine outlier: **之 → ㄊ**, one of the most common grammatical particles in Classical Chinese, reassigned to a plain dental with no support from Mandarin (zhī), Cantonese (zi1), or Korean (지) — an unexplained one-off, not part of any pattern.
