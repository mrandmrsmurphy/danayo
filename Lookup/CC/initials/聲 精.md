> **Initial 精** mostly evolved into ㄐ, with a ㄙ minority outcome (see below)

## CJKV Evolution
精 [t͡s] is the 清 (plain) member of the dental sibilant-affricate series (精組), and unlike the other 清 initials documented so far (幫, 見, 端), its split isn't an unpredictable per-character poll — it tracks a real, regular phonological environment. Modern Mandarin palatalizes 精組 initials to `j-`/`q-`/`x-` before high front vowels/glides (the textbook 尖團 merger), and Dan'a'yo's **ㄐ majority (97 of 99)** follows that exactly. The two exceptions, **走 and 奏 (both → ㄙㄛㄨ)**, share the same non-palatalizing final (`əu`, a back vowel with no front glide) and keep the real Mandarin `z-` (zǒu/zòu) unpalatalized to match — a clean, rule-governed minority rather than an idiosyncratic one, and yet another case of two unrelated characters coinciding by final rather than phonetic component.
