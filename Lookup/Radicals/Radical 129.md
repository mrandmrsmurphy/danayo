> [[Radicals]]
> Radical 129 is 聿, a pictogram of a hand gripping a stylus or brush — the ancestor of 筆 (brush) with 竹 "bamboo" added later to specify the writing implement's material. It shows up in compounds tied to writing and to setting things down formally, as in 肇, "to begin," originally the act of opening a new undertaking with the stroke of a brush.
