## Notes
- [[涅盤]] "nirvana" + [[寂静]] "tranquility"

**Literal meaning:** “Nirvana is quiescent and still.”  
**Figurative meaning:** The ultimate state of peace beyond suffering; complete cessation of disturbance and defilement.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** niè pán jì jìng (4–2–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** nip6 pun4 zik6 zing6
- **Korean:** 열반적정
- **Japanese:** ねはんじゃくじょう（涅槃寂静）_nehan jakujō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** niết-bàn tịch tĩnh

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 涅槃寂靜
- **Simplified:** 涅槃寂静

- Often listed as one of the **Three Marks of Existence**:  
    **[[諸行無常]]**
    **[[chengyu/諸法無我]]**
    **涅槃寂静**

### Source / Origin
The phrase is a classical **Buddhist doctrinal formulation**, translating the idea that **nirvana (Sanskrit: निर्वाण, nirvāṇa)** is characterized by **complete stillness and peace**.

It appears widely in **Chinese translations of Buddhist scriptures**, including the **Āgamas** and later Mahāyāna texts. The term **寂静 (śānta / śānti)** denotes quiescence, tranquility, and the absence of agitation.

Within Buddhist philosophy, **涅槃寂静** describes the state in which the cycle of birth and death (saṃsāra) has ceased. All conditioned processes (行) have ended, and thus all suffering and disturbance are extinguished.

As part of the doctrinal triad:
- **諸行無常** — all conditioned things are impermanent
- **諸法無我** — all phenomena lack a fixed self
- **涅槃寂静** — nirvana is peace

the phrase summarizes the **goal and culmination** of Buddhist insight.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese Buddhist usage**, the phrase retains a strongly **philosophical and religious meaning**, referring specifically to nirvana rather than ordinary calmness.
- In **Japanese**, **涅槃寂静** is often encountered in doctrinal study and classical literature, emphasizing the **ultimate stillness beyond life and death**.
- In **Korean**, **열반적정** carries the same doctrinal sense, used in Buddhist teaching and texts.

Unlike many chengyu, this expression is not primarily metaphorical; it is a **technical term of Buddhist philosophy**.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
佛教認為涅槃寂静是解脫的最高境界。  
_Buddhism holds that nirvana is the highest state of liberation, characterized by perfect stillness._

**Japanese:**  
涅槃寂静は仏教における究極の安らぎを表す。  
_Nirvana represents the ultimate peace in Buddhism._

**Korean:**  
열반적정은 모든 고통이 사라진 상태를 의미한다。  
_Nirvana denotes a state in which all suffering has ceased._

### Sentiment
> **“The stillness of nirvana — peace beyond all arising and passing.”**
