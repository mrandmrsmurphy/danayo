## Literal Meaning
- [心](../characters/心%20(char).md) — heart
- [性](../characters/性.md) — soul, essential nature (here, not its more common modern sense "gender")
- [意](../characters/意.md) — mind, intention
- [力](../characters/力%20(char).md) — strength, power

Literally: "Heart, soul, mind, and strength."

## Extended Meaning
The four dimensions of total devotion commanded in the Greatest Commandment. 心 (heart, the emotional center), 性 (soul, essential nature), 意 (mind, will, intention), and 力 (strength, physical capacity) together represent the totality of human being and capability. The compression into chengyu form emphasizes completeness and integration — true love of God encompasses every aspect of personhood, not merely feeling or merely obedience alone.

## Source and Origin
Mark 12:30, Luke 10:27 (compare Deuteronomy 6:5):

> "And you shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all your strength." (Mark 12:30, ESV)

Coined as a Dan'a'yo theological idiom compressing the four nouns of the Greatest Commandment into a single four-character form, modeled on the classical Chinese convention of four-character summary expressions.

## Variants
- 全心全性全意全力 — "whole-heart, whole-soul, whole-mind, whole-strength," a fuller form spelling out the totality more explicitly.
- 心霊意力 — "heart-spirit, mind-strength," an alternate rendering substituting 霊 (spirit) for 性 (soul).
- 心性智力 — "heart-soul, wisdom-strength," a variant emphasizing the intellectual aspect of 意 more than its volitional sense.

These are alternate phrasings of the same theological content, not orthographic variants of one fixed idiom — no traditional/simplified split applies to this Dan'a'yo coinage.

## Cultural Notes
This four-character structure elegantly captures the four dimensions of total devotion commanded in the Greatest Commandment, which Jesus identified (together with wholehearted love of neighbor) as summarizing all the Law and Prophets (Matthew 22:40). Used in devotional and catechetical Chinese-language Christian writing as a compact, memorable restatement of the command, in the same register as other Dan'a'yo Biblical four-character coinages.

## Example Sentences
1. 愛神當盡心性意力，無有所留。
   "To love God one must exhaust heart, soul, mind, and strength, holding nothing back."
2. 事主之道在於心性意力之全獻。
   "The way of serving the Lord lies in the complete offering of heart, soul, mind, and strength."

## Sentiment
Devotional and comprehensive. Used to express total commitment, whole-person dedication, or the integrated nature of genuine faith.
