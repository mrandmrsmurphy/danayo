[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: zào rén xiàng xíng
- **Cantonese**: zou6 jan4 zoeng6 jing4
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: ぞうじんぞうけい
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 조인상형 (jo-in-sang-hyeong)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: tạo nhân tượng hình

### Literal Meaning
- **造** to create, make
- **人** human being
- **像** image
- **形** likeness, form

Literal: “Create man in image and likeness.”

### Figurative Meaning
A compressed expression of the doctrine that humanity is created in the image of God. It signifies dignity, representation, rationality, and covenant vocation.

### Scriptural Source
The idiom condenses **Genesis 1:26–27**:
> “Let us make man in our image, after our likeness…”

Hebrew: _בְּצַלְמֵנוּ כִּדְמוּתֵנוּ_  
“In our image, according to our likeness.”

Traditional Chinese translations typically render:
> “我們要照著我們的形像，按著我們的樣式造人。”

The Dan'a'yo form removes syntactic markers and preserves the paired theological terms 像 and 形, reflecting the dual Hebrew vocabulary.

### Example Usage
> 人之尊嚴，本於造人像形之旨。  
> Human dignity rests upon the truth that man was created in image and likeness.

### Cultural Notes
The pairing 像形 preserves the twofold Hebrew structure without collapsing them. Historically, Christian theology has debated whether “image” and “likeness” denote distinct faculties or rhetorical parallelism. Patristic writers sometimes distinguished rational capacity from moral conformity; later theologians often treated the terms as overlapping.

In contrast to surrounding ancient Near Eastern cultures, where only kings bore divine image status, Genesis universalizes the concept to all humanity. This democratization of royal dignity undergirds later doctrines of equality and moral accountability.

造 emphasizes intentional craftsmanship rather than emanation. The act is deliberate and personal.

Within Dan'a'yo, 造人像形 serves as an anthropological cornerstone. It grounds ethics, vocation, and redemption. The Fall distorts but does not erase this status, a tension developed elsewhere in biblical theology.

### Tone and Sentiment
Elevated, foundational, dignifying. It affirms human worth while implying responsibility before the Creator.
