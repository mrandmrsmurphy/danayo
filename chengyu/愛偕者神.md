[Biblical Chengyu](chengyu/Biblical%20Chengyu.md)

## Literal Meaning
- [愛 (char)](characters/愛%20(char).md) — love
- [[偕者]] — YHWH; the LORD (= [偕](characters/偕.md) "accompany" + [者 (char)](characters/者%20(char).md) "one who")
- [神 (char)](characters/神%20(char).md) — God; deity

Literally: "Love YHWH [your] God" — an imperative to love the covenantal God who accompanies.

## Extended Meaning
愛偕者神 is the Great Commandment — the love commandment embedded in the Shema, identified by Jesus as the first and greatest of all commandments. It compresses the entire covenant relationship into four characters: the action (愛, love), the personal name of the covenant God (偕者, YHWH — the one who accompanies), and the identity declaration (神, God). Together they name both what God is called and what he is, and command the proper response to both.

Unlike a doctrinal statement, 愛偕者神 is an imperative — addressed to the hearer, demanding not merely assent but orientation of the whole self toward God.

## Source and Origin
**《申命記·六章五節》** (Deuteronomy 6:5):

> וְאָהַבְתָּ אֵת יְהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ בְּכָל־לְבָבְךָ וּבְכָל־נַפְשְׁךָ וּבְכָל־מְאֹדֶךָ׃
> "You shall love YHWH your God with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your might."

This verse is the second sentence of the Shema (שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל, "Hear, O Israel") — the foundational confession of biblical faith, recited by observant Jews twice daily. Deuteronomy 6:4 declares that YHWH is one; 6:5 declares that this one God is to be loved with the entirety of one's being.

Jesus quoted this verse when asked to identify the greatest commandment:

**《馬太福音·二二章三七至三八節》** (Matthew 22:37–38):
> Ἀγαπήσεις κύριον τὸν θεόν σου ἐν ὅλῃ τῇ καρδίᾳ σου καὶ ἐν ὅλῃ τῇ ψυχῇ σου καὶ ἐν ὅλῃ τῇ διανοίᾳ σου· αὕτη ἐστὶν ἡ μεγάλη καὶ πρώτη ἐντολή.
> "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment."

The commandment is also undergirded by the reverse declaration of 《約翰一書·四章八節》 (1 John 4:8): «ὁ θεὸς ἀγάπη ἐστίν» — "God is love." The commandment to love God is grounded in the prior reality that God himself is love.

## Cultural Notes
The three divine names in Dan'a'yo — [[上帝]] (Elohim), [[天主]] (Adonai), and [[偕者]] (YHWH) — recapitulate a distinction that animated the Chinese Rites Controversy of the 17th–18th centuries: what is the proper Chinese term for the God of the Bible? Matteo Ricci's Jesuits used 天主; later Protestant missionaries debated 上帝 versus 神; 耶和華 became the phonetic standard for YHWH. Dan'a'yo rejects the phonetic route and translates: 偕者 names God by his promise — "I will be with you" — rather than by the sounds of the Hebrew.

愛偕者神 places this covenant name at the center of the supreme commandment. The choice of 偕者 rather than 天主 or 上帝 is theologically loaded: it is the personal, accompanying God — not the universal creator or the sovereign ruler — who is to be loved. This reflects a distinctively covenantal theology in which God's name is inseparable from his relational commitment.

The love commandment sits in productive tension with Confucian ethics. Confucian 仁 (benevolence, humaneness) is the central moral virtue — sometimes translated "love" — but it is directed primarily toward humans through the five relationships. The Shema's commandment orders love to God first (愛偕者神) and neighbor second. This vertical priority challenges Confucian social ethics while finding common ground with them: both traditions ground moral obligation in a comprehensive orientation of the self, not merely in discrete acts.

## Example Sentences
1. 《申命記·六章四至五節》: 「以色列啊，你要聽！耶和華我們神是獨一的主。你要盡心、盡性、盡力愛耶和華你的神。」
   Deuteronomy 6:4–5: "Hear, O Israel: YHWH our God, YHWH is one. You shall love YHWH your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might."
2. 《馬太福音·二二章三七至三八節》: 「你要盡心、盡性、盡意愛主你的神。這是誡命中的第一，且是最大的。」
   Matthew 22:37–38: "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment."

## Sentiment
Imperative and covenantal. 愛偕者神 is not a description of God but a command directed at the hearer — the foundational obligation of biblical religion, from which all other commandments derive. Its register is solemn and central; it cannot be observed from a distance.
