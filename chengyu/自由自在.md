## 自由自在

**Literal meaning:** “Free and at ease within oneself.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Unrestricted; acting freely and naturally without constraint.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** zì yóu zì zài (4–2–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** zi6 jau4 zi6 zoi6
- **Korean:** 자유자재
- **Japanese:** じゆうじざい（自由自在）_jiyū jizai_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** tự do tự tại

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 自由自在
- **Simplified:** 自由自在
- In Korean usage, often written **自由自在 → 自由自在 / 自由自在하다** conceptually as **자유자재**.

### Source / Origin
The expression has strong roots in **Buddhist vocabulary**, especially the term **自在** (Sanskrit: _īśvara_ or _svatantra_ in translation contexts), meaning **unhindered, sovereign, or unobstructed**.

In Buddhist texts, **自在** describes a state of **spiritual freedom**, where one is no longer bound by attachments or limitations. The compound **自由自在** developed in medieval Chinese Buddhist and literary usage to emphasize **complete freedom of action and being**, both inward and outward.

Over time, the phrase broadened beyond religious contexts to describe **effortless mastery or unrestrained movement**, whether physical, artistic, or mental.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 自由自在 often describes a state of **ease, naturalness, and lack of constraint**, sometimes with a relaxed or leisurely tone.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom frequently refers to **complete control or mastery**, as in handling a skill or situation freely and fluently.
- In **Korean**, **자유자재** often emphasizes **skillful control**, the ability to manipulate something freely at will.

Thus the phrase can range in meaning from **spiritual freedom** to **technical mastery**, depending on context.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他過著自由自在的生活。  
_He lives a free and unrestrained life._
**Japanese:**  
彼はその技術を自由自在に操る。  
_He can handle that technique with complete mastery._
**Korean:**  
그는 도구를 자유자재로 다룬다。  
_He handles the tools with complete freedom and control._

### Sentiment
> **“Unbound and at ease — moving and acting without constraint.”**
