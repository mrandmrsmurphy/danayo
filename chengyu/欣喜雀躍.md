## [欣](characters/欣.md)+ [喜](characters/喜%20(char).md) + [雀](characters/雀.md) + [躍](characters/躍.md)

**Literal meaning:** “Joyful and leaping like a sparrow.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To be overjoyed; to jump with excitement and delight.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** xīn xǐ què yuè (1–3–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** jan1 hei2 zoek3 joek6
- **Korean:** 흔희작약 (less common; often expressed descriptively)
- **Japanese:** きんきじゃくやく（欣喜雀躍）_kinki jakuyaku_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** hân hỉ tước dược

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 欣喜雀躍
- **Simplified:** 欣喜雀跃

### Source / Origin
The phrase combines two vivid elements:
- **欣喜** — joy, delight
- **雀躍** — “sparrow-leaping,” an image of small birds hopping energetically

The imagery appears in **classical Chinese descriptive writing**, where birds, especially sparrows, are used to depict **lively, animated movement**. The combination conveys not just happiness, but **visible, physical excitement**.

Although not tied to a single canonical passage, the expression reflects a long tradition of using **animal imagery to embody emotional states**, especially in poetry and prose of the **Han and later periods**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 欣喜雀躍 is commonly used to describe **open, exuberant happiness**, especially when the joy is difficult to contain.
- The phrase often implies **physical expression of emotion**, not just inward feeling.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom is understood but relatively literary; more common expressions are used in everyday speech    
- In **Korean**, the exact four-character form is less common, but the imagery of **jumping with joy** is readily understood.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
聽到這個好消息，大家都欣喜雀躍。  
_Hearing the good news, everyone jumped with joy._
**Japanese:**  
彼は合格の知らせに欣喜雀躍した。  
_He leapt with joy at the news of his acceptance._
**Korean:**  
그들은 좋은 소식에 매우 기뻐했다。  
_They were overjoyed at the good news._

### Sentiment
> **“Joy that leaps like a sparrow — happiness that shows itself in motion.”**
