- [東](characters/東.md) "east" + [奔](characters/奔.md) "run around" + [西](characters/西.md) "west" + [走 (char)](characters/走%20(char).md) "run"

**Literal meaning:** “Run east, run west.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To rush about everywhere; to run around busily from place to place.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** dōng bēn xī zǒu (1–1–1–3)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** dung1 ban1 sai1 zau2
- **Korean:** 동분서주
- **Japanese:** とうほんせいそう（東奔西走）_tōhon seisō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** đông bôn tây tẩu

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 東奔西走
- **Simplified:** 东奔西走
- Sometimes paired with similar expressions like **東奔西跑** (“run east and west”) or **南奔北走** (“run south and north”).

### Source / Origin
The phrase reflects a common stylistic pattern in classical Chinese where **paired directions** are used to express movement in many directions. The imagery suggests someone rushing about **without rest**, moving east and west in constant activity.

Expressions of this type appear in **Tang and Song dynasty prose and poetry**, where writers described officials, messengers, or travelers **running from place to place on urgent matters**.

Over time, the phrase became a fixed idiom describing someone who must **hurry around dealing with many tasks or obligations**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 東奔西走 often conveys the idea of **busy effort or hardship**, especially when someone must travel widely to accomplish something.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom usually refers to **running around working hard to manage affairs or solve problems**.
- In **Korean**, **동분서주** commonly describes someone **busily attending to many tasks**, often with a tone of diligence.
    

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
為了辦成這件事，他東奔西走了好幾天。  
_He ran around everywhere for several days to get this matter settled._

**Japanese:**  
彼は仕事のために東奔西走している。  
_He is running around everywhere for work._

**Korean:**  
그는 문제를 해결하려고 동분서주했다。  
_He rushed around everywhere trying to solve the problem._

### Sentiment
> **“Running east and west — constant motion in pursuit of a task.”**
