[Biblical Chengyu](chengyu/Biblical%20Chengyu.md)

## Literal Meaning
- [創](characters/創.md) — to create, originate
- [反](characters/反%20(char).md) — to rebel, turn against; fall
- [救](characters/救.md) — to rescue, redeem
- [成](characters/成%20(char).md) — to complete, consummate

Literally: "Creation, Fall, Redemption, Consummation."

## Extended Meaning
A compact expression of the four-act structure of biblical theology. Used as theological shorthand for the scriptural metanarrative: God creates a good world; humanity falls into sin and rebellion; God redeems creation through Christ's atoning work; all things are finally consummated in the new creation. Though coined in Dan'a'yo, the underlying framework reflects standard Reformed and evangelical systematic theology.

## Source and Origin

Coined as a Dan'a'yo theological idiom, compressing the biblical metanarrative into four characters — a form modeled on classical Chinese four-character summary expressions. The individual referents draw on specific biblical texts:

- **創** (Creation): Genesis 1–2 — God creates the heavens and the earth.
- **反** (Fall): Genesis 3 — humanity rebels against God; sin and death enter the world.
- **救** (Redemption): The Gospel accounts and Epistles — Christ's atoning death and resurrection.
- **成** (Consummation): Revelation 21–22 — the renewal of all things, God dwelling with his people.

> 「起初，神創造天地…」(創世記 1:1)
> "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." (Gen 1:1)
> 「神愛世人，甚至將他的獨生子賜給他們，叫一切信他的，不至滅亡，反得永生。」(約翰福音 3:16)
> "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son." (John 3:16)
> 「我來要使萬物更新！」(啟示錄 21:5)
> "Behold, I am making all things new!" (Rev 21:5)

## Standard Form
**創反救成** (Dan'a'yo standard)

Variant forms:
- 創堕救成 — using [[堕]] for "fall" (descent/ruin imagery)
- 造反贖完 — alternate vocabulary: made-rebellion-atonement-completion
- 創世反逆救贖完成 — fuller eight-character expansion

## Cultural Notes
創反救成 adapts a classical Chinese literary convention — the four-character idiom as compressed wisdom — to encode a fundamentally Western theological framework. The move is deliberately inculturating: just as Chinese thinkers expressed cosmological and ethical systems in four-character compounds, the biblical metanarrative is here cast in that same form, making it naturally memorable within a Chinese-language theological context.

The framework — creation, fall, redemption, consummation — is sometimes called the "grand narrative of Scripture" or the "four-act drama of redemption." It finds parallels in structures used by theologians such as Herman Bavinck, N.T. Wright (creation, fall, Israel, Jesus, new creation), and Graeme Goldsworthy.

## Example Sentences
1. 聖書之大義可以創反救成四字概之。
   "The great meaning of Scripture can be summarized in the four characters 創反救成."
2. 神之計画由創反救成，終必成全。
   "God's plan proceeds through creation, fall, redemption, and consummation, and will certainly be fulfilled."

## Sentiment
Theological and hopeful. Used as a concise catechetical summary of the biblical worldview, expressing confidence in God's sovereign plan over history. Tone is declarative rather than devotional — a framework for thinking, not an exclamation.
