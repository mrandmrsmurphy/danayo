## Notes

**Literal meaning:** “Sincere heart, sincere intent.”  
**Figurative meaning:** With complete sincerity; wholeheartedly; in utmost good faith.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** chéng xīn chéng yì (2–1–2–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** sing4 sam1 sing4 ji3
- **Korean:** 성심성의
- **Japanese:** せいしんせいい（誠心誠意）_seishin seii_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** thành tâm thành ý

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 誠心誠意
- **Simplified:** 诚心诚意
- Closely related: **誠心正意** (echoing classical formulations of sincerity and rectification).

### Source / Origin
The expression is rooted in **Confucian moral vocabulary**, especially the pairing of **誠 (sincerity)** and **意 (intention)**.

A key background source is the **_Great Learning_ (《大學》)**, which emphasizes:

**誠意正心** — “make the intentions sincere and rectify the mind.”

While the exact four-character form **誠心誠意** does not appear verbatim in early canonical texts, it is a **later crystallization of this Confucian ideal**, combining:

- **誠心** — a sincere heart
    
- **誠意** — sincere intention
    

The reduplication intensifies the meaning, yielding a fixed idiom that expresses **complete and undivided sincerity in both feeling and intent**.

### Cultural Notes

- In **Chinese**, 誠心誠意 is commonly used in both formal and everyday contexts to describe **genuine goodwill and wholehearted effort**.
    
- In **Japanese**, the phrase often appears in formal speech, apologies, and service contexts, emphasizing **earnestness and good faith**.
    
- In **Korean**, **성심성의** similarly conveys **devotion and sincerity**, especially in fulfilling duties or obligations.
    

The idiom carries a strongly positive moral tone, rooted in **Confucian ethics of sincerity (誠)**.

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
他誠心誠意地向大家道歉。  
_He apologized to everyone with complete sincerity._

**Japanese:**  
誠心誠意対応させていただきます。  
_We will respond with the utmost sincerity._

**Korean:**  
그는 성심성의껏 일을 했다。  
_He carried out the work with complete sincerity._

### Sentiment

> **“With a sincere heart and sincere intent — wholly genuine, without reserve.”**
