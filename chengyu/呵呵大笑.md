## 呵呵[[大笑]]

**Literal meaning:** “He-he, great laughter.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To laugh heartily or loudly; to burst into open laughter.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** hē hē dà xiào (1–1–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** ho1 ho1 daai6 siu3
- **Korean:** 가가대소
- **Japanese:** かかたいしょう（呵呵大笑）_kaka taishō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** ha ha đại tiếu

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 呵呵大笑
- **Simplified:** 呵呵大笑
- The reduplicated **[[呵呵]]** is an onomatopoetic representation of laughter.

### Source / Origin

The phrase appears in **classical Chinese narrative prose**, where writers used **呵呵** as an imitation of audible laughter. Expressions describing laughter in this form can be found in historical and literary works such as the **_Records of the Grand Historian_ (《史記》)** and later prose traditions.

In classical storytelling, laughter was often described with vivid sound imagery. The reduplicated form **呵呵** conveys the sound of an open laugh, while **大笑** (“to laugh greatly”) emphasizes the intensity.

Together the phrase came to mean **laughing loudly or heartily**, often in response to something amusing, ironic, or triumphant.

### Cultural Notes

- In **modern Chinese internet usage**, **呵呵** alone can sometimes carry a sarcastic or dismissive tone, quite different from the original sense of genuine laughter.
    
- In **classical-style writing**, however, **呵呵大笑** simply indicates **boisterous laughter**.
    
- In **Japanese and Korean**, the idiom survives mostly in literary or classical contexts rather than everyday speech.
    

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
聽到這個笑話，他不禁呵呵大笑。  
_Hearing the joke, he burst into hearty laughter._

**Japanese:**  
彼はその話を聞いて呵呵大笑した。  
_He burst out laughing when he heard the story._

**Korean:**  
그는 이야기를 듣고 가가대소했다。  
_He laughed loudly when he heard the story._

### Sentiment

> **“A full, ringing laugh — laughter that cannot be contained.”**
