**Literal meaning:** “Befriend those far away; attack those nearby.”  
**Figurative meaning:** A geopolitical strategy: form alliances with distant powers while confronting or subduing nearby rivals.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** yuǎn jiāo jìn gōng (3–1–4–1)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** jyun5 gaau1 gan6 gung1
- **Korean:** 원교근공
- **Japanese:** えんこうきんこう（遠交近攻）_enkō kinkō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** viễn giao cận công    

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 遠交近攻
- **Simplified:** 远交近攻

### Source / Origin
The strategy is recorded in the **_Records of the Grand Historian_ (《史記》)**, in the **“Biography of Fan Ju” (范雎列傳)**.

During the **Warring States period**, the strategist **Fan Ju (范雎)** advised the state of Qin on how to expand its power. He proposed that Qin should **ally with distant states** while **attacking neighboring ones**, thereby avoiding encirclement and isolating immediate rivals.

The logic is strategic:
- Distant states pose less immediate threat and can be useful allies.
- Nearby states are the primary obstacles to expansion and should be weakened or conquered first.

This policy contributed to Qin’s rise and eventual unification of China.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 遠交近攻 is a standard term in discussions of **strategy, diplomacy, and realpolitik**.
- It is often cited in analyses of both ancient and modern **international relations**.
- In **Japanese** and **Korean**, the idiom is similarly used in political or historical contexts to describe **strategic alliance-building combined with localized conflict**.

The phrase carries a neutral to analytical tone, describing a **pragmatic, interest-driven strategy** rather than a moral principle.

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
這種外交政策可以說是遠交近攻的典型。  
_This foreign policy can be described as a classic case of “ally distant, attack near.”_

**Japanese:**  
彼の戦略は遠交近攻に基づいている。  
_His strategy is based on forming distant alliances and attacking nearby rivals._

**Korean:**  
이 전략은 원교근공의 원칙을 따른다。  
_This strategy follows the principle of allying with the distant and attacking the near._

### Sentiment

> **“Ally with the distant, strike the near — strategy shaped by proximity and advantage.”**
