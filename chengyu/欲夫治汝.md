[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: yù fū zhì rǔ
- **Cantonese**: juk6 fu1 zi6 jyu5
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: よくふちじょ
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 욕부치여 (yok-bu-chi-yeo)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: dục phu trị nhữ

### Literal Meaning
- **欲** desire
- **夫** husband
- **治** rule, govern
- **汝** you

Literal: “Your desire [toward] your husband; he shall rule you.”
The phrase compresses two clauses into a single four-character structure.

### Scriptural Source
This maxim condenses **Genesis 3:16**:
> “Thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee.”

Hebrew:  
_וְאֶל־אִישֵׁךְ תְּשׁוּקָתֵךְ, וְהוּא יִמְשָׁל־בָּךְ_  
“Your desire shall be for your husband, and he shall rule over you.”

Traditional Chinese translations render:
> “你必戀慕你丈夫；你丈夫必管轄你。”

The Dan'a'yo form retains the dual dynamic of desire and rule.

### Example Usage
> 墮落之後，婚姻之中或見欲夫治汝之張力。  
> After the Fall, tension in marriage may reflect the dynamic of desire and rule.

### Cultural Notes
The Hebrew term for “desire” (_teshuqah_) appears only three times in the Old Testament, linking Genesis 3:16 and 4:7. This suggests a dynamic involving tension and mastery rather than simple romance.

治 conveys governance or administration. It need not imply cruelty, but within the curse context it signals imbalance.

Within Dan'a'yo, 欲夫治汝 functions as a sober anthropological marker. It acknowledges relational distortion while leaving room for redemption.

### Tone and Sentiment
Grave, realistic, relationally tense.
