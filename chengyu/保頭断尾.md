[Dan'a'yo Chengyu](chengyu/Dan'a'yo%20Chengyu.md)
>[!Tip] Coined as the constructive counterpart to [[舎本逐末]] — a principle for character and vocabulary inclusion as the database approaches its long tail

## Literal Meaning
- [保](../characters/保.md) — protect, preserve, guard
- [頭 (char)](../characters/頭%20(char).md) — head
- [断](../characters/断.md) — cut off, sever
- [尾 (char)](../characters/尾%20(char).md) — tail

Literally: "Guard the head, cut off the tail."

## Extended Meaning
A design principle for Dan'a'yo's own growth: protect and invest in the productive core of the language — the characters, words, and patterns that carry real cross-CJKV weight — and don't be afraid to prune, consolidate, or alias away the marginal long tail rather than forcing every last rare character into its own full entry. It is the constructive mirror of [[舎本逐末]]: where that idiom *warns against* losing sight of the fundamental while chasing the trivial, 保頭断尾 *instructs* what to do about it once the fundamentals are secure and the remaining work is mostly long-tail dregs — guard what matters, and don't hesitate to cut what doesn't carry its weight. It is invoked specifically at the point where a project's "low-hanging fruit" has been picked and what remains is statistically marginal.

## Source and Origin
Coined within <ruby>単亜語<rt>ㄉㄚㄋ·ㄚ·⼄</rt></ruby> itself (2026-06-08), in a conversation about the character database's approach to its own long tail — the point at which nearly every remaining gap is a marginal, low-frequency character whose inclusion would cost more in database bloat than it would add in expressive range. The coiner reached first for the existing classical idiom 舎本逐末 as a cautionary frame, then proposed 保頭斷尾 as a positive, actionable counterpart: not just a warning against misplaced priorities, but a rule for what to actually *do* when faced with them — protect the head (the productive core), sever the tail (the marginal remainder). The image deliberately echoes the body-as-structure metaphor already at work in [[feedback_character_inclusion_philosophy|the vault's character-inclusion judgment calls]], where, for instance, 瘠 ("thin enough to show the spine") was aliased onto its phonetic and semantic parent 脊 rather than given its own entry — itself a small act of 保頭断尾 in practice, before the principle had a name.

## Standard Form
**保頭断尾** is the Dan'a'yo standard spelling, using the Shōwa-era simplified form 断 in place of 斷 (see [[覧昭和決]]) — consistent with how Dan'a'yo spells its own coinages.
- 保頭斷尾 — the traditional-character form in which the idiom was first proposed; preserved here as an alias, since both forms are read identically

## Invocation
When a character is proposed and it has few to one word across CJKV where it is needed, it is time to invoke this _chengyu_ and make that character an alias of something close — a 代用字. The ~3000 characters at the heart of Dan'a'yo were chosen for their centrality, ubiquity, and utility. Especially if a character is just for names, the use of a reduced form, with the more obscure form relegated to a mere alias, is completely justified.

## Example Sentences
1. もう拾える低い実は拾い終えた。残るのは長い尾だけ — ここからは保頭断尾でいこう。
2. We've created every word that unlocks a productive syllable; what's left is statistically marginal — time to apply 保頭断尾 and start aliasing rather than minting.
3. 데이터베이스의 핵심은 이미 단단해. 이제부터는 보두단미의 정신으로, 변두리는 과감히 정리하자.
4. Don't mistake 保頭断尾 for laziness — it's the same judgment that produced 瘠→脊: guard the core relationships, and let the long tail fold into them.

## Sentiment
Constructive and self-directed; an internal design maxim rather than a criticism aimed outward.
Pairs with [[舎本逐末]] as warning-and-remedy: where that idiom names the failure (losing the root in pursuit of the branch), this one names the fix (hold the root, and don't be precious about the branch). Best read as encouragement to commit to a judgment call, not permission to be careless.
