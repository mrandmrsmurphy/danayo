## Notes
- [[光 (char)]]
- [[明 (char)]]
- [[正 (char)]]
- [[大 (char)]]

**Literal meaning:** “Bright, clear, upright, and great.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Open and aboveboard; honest, upright, and free of hidden motives.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** guāng míng zhèng dà (1–2–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** gwong1 ming4 zing3 daai6
- **Korean:** 광명정대
- **Japanese:** こうめいせいだい（光明正大）_kōmei seidai_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** quang minh chính đại

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 光明正大
- **Simplified:** 光明正大
- Closely related: **公明正大**, emphasizing public impartiality.

### Source / Origin

The phrase is attested in **Neo-Confucian writings of the Song dynasty**, particularly in the work of **Zhu Xi (朱熹, 1130–1200)**. In his commentary on the **_Great Learning_ (《大學章句》)**, Zhu Xi uses **光明正大** to describe the moral condition of a cultivated person whose conduct is **transparent, upright, and magnanimous**.

The components reflect core Confucian values:

- **光明** — clarity, illumination, openness
    
- **正大** — uprightness and moral greatness
    

The expression encapsulates the idea that a properly cultivated mind acts in a way that is **fit to be seen in the open**, without concealment or distortion.

### Cultural Notes

- In **Chinese**, 光明正大 often emphasizes **transparency and moral legitimacy**, especially in contrast to deceit or intrigue.
    
- In **Japanese**, the phrase is used in formal contexts to describe **fair and honorable conduct**, especially in public life.
    
- In **Korean**, **광명정대** similarly refers to actions that are **honest and beyond reproach**.
    

Compared with **公明正大**, this form leans slightly more toward **moral clarity and openness**, rather than institutional impartiality.

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
他光明正大地承認了自己的錯誤。  
_He openly and honestly admitted his mistake._

**Japanese:**  
光明正大なやり方で勝負すべきだ。  
_One should compete in an open and honorable way._

**Korean:**  
그는 광명정대한 태도로 일을 처리했다。  
_He handled the matter with openness and integrity._

### Sentiment

> **“Bright and upright — conduct that stands fully in the light, without concealment.”**
