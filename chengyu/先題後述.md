## 先題後述

### Readings
- Mandarin: xiān tí hòu shù
- Cantonese: sin¹ tai⁴ hau⁶ seot⁶
- Japanese (hiragana): せんだい こうじゅつ
- Korean (Hangul): 선제후술 (seon-je hu-sul)
- Vietnamese: tiên đề hậu thuật

### Literal structure
- [[先]]: first
- [[題]]: topic, that which is under discussion
- [後 (char)](../characters/後%20(char).md): afterward
- [[述]]: state, comment, predicate

The form is programmatic: establish the topic, then provide the statement about it.

### Interpretation
A foundational syntactic principle:

All clauses are organized as **topic → comment**, rather than subject → predicate.

### Operational meaning in Dan’a’yo
This is the primary and universal rule of word order:
1. **Clause structure**  
    Every clause, main or subordinate, is divided into:
    - 題 (topic): the frame, what is being talked about
    - 述 (comment): what is asserted about that topic
2. **Surface SVO as secondary**  
    Many clauses resemble SVO, but this is incidental:
    - when the topic aligns with the agent, SVO emerges
    - when it does not, the structure diverges from SVO without violating the rule
3. **Recursivity**  
    All embedded structures follow the same pattern:
    - relative clauses
    - complement clauses
    - adverbial clauses
    
    Each is internally topic-comment before being embedded.
1. **Interaction with [[修飾先行]]**  
    Since modifiers precede heads:
    - entire topic-comment subclauses can appear pre-nominal, linked by 之
    - the internal organization remains topic-comment even when functioning as a modifier
2. **Topic marking (implicit or structural)**  
    The system may rely on:
    - position (initial placement)
    - particles or context  
        to identify the topic, rather than fixed grammatical roles like “subject”

### Example (schematic)

- 魚 此人捕已  
    Topic: 魚 (the fish)  
    Comment: 此人 捕已 (“this man has caught [it]”)

Even if reordered for naturalness, the underlying organization remains:
- [Topic] → [what is said about it]

### Underlying principles
- **Primacy of discourse structure over argument structure**
- **Flexibility of grammatical roles** (subject, object are not primary)
- **Uniform clause architecture** across all levels

### Effect
- High adaptability in focus and emphasis
- Natural handling of topicalization without transformation
- Alignment with Classical Chinese and other topic-prominent languages, but made fully systematic    

### Concise definition
“A universal word-order rule in which every clause is structured as topic followed by comment, with apparent SVO patterns arising only when topic and agent coincide.”

### Note
By stating this as the “one and only rule,” you eliminate competing structural principles. Even head-finality (修飾先行) operates within this larger constraint: ordering is ultimately resolved at the level of topic versus comment.
