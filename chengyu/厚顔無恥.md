- [厚 (char)](characters/厚%20(char).md) "thick" + [顔](characters/顔.md) "face" + [無 (char)](characters/無%20(char).md) "without" + [恥](characters/恥.md) "shame"

**Literal meaning:** “Thick-faced and without shame.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Shameless; brazen; behaving without any sense of embarrassment or moral restraint.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** hòu yán wú chǐ (4–2–2–3)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** hau5 ngaan4 mou4 ci2
- **Korean:** 후안무치
- **Japanese:** こうがんむち（厚顔無恥）_kōgan muchi_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** hậu nhan vô sỉ

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 厚顏無恥
- **Simplified:** 厚颜无耻
- A related expression is **厚顏**, meaning “brazen” or “shameless,” used by itself.

### Source / Origin
The expression appears in **classical Chinese historical prose**, notably in **Sima Qian’s _Records of the Grand Historian_ (《史記》)**. In describing certain political figures whose behavior ignored moral standards, the historian used language emphasizing their **“thick face” and lack of shame**.

The metaphor reflects a long-standing cultural idea: a person with **“thick skin” (厚顔)** does not blush or feel embarrassment, even when acting improperly. Combined with **無恥 (“without shame”)**, the phrase intensifies the condemnation, portraying someone who has lost all sense of moral restraint.

The idiom became a standard way to criticize people who behave **brazenly or hypocritically**, especially in politics or public life.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 厚顔無恥 is a strong moral condemnation, often applied to corrupt officials or people who deny wrongdoing despite obvious evidence.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom is common in commentary or criticism, describing behavior that is **openly shameless or hypocritical**.
- In **Korean**, **후안무치** carries a similar tone of moral outrage, referring to someone who acts **without any sense of embarrassment or conscience**.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他犯了錯卻還辯解，真是厚顏無恥。  
_He made a mistake and still tried to justify it. Truly shameless._

**Japanese:**  
そんな厚顔無恥な言い訳は通らない。  
_Such a shameless excuse will not pass._

**Korean:**  
그의 행동은 후안무치하다고 비판받았다。  
_His behavior was criticized as shameless._

### Sentiment
> **“A face too thick to blush — conduct utterly devoid of shame.”**
