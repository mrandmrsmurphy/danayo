- [内](characters/内.md) "inside" + [柔 (char)](characters/柔%20(char).md) "soft" + [外](characters/外.md) "outside" + [剛](characters/剛.md) "firm"

**Literal meaning:** “Soft within, firm without.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Gentle or kind-hearted inwardly, but outwardly strong, resolute, or stern.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** nèi róu wài gāng (4–2–4–1)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** noi6 jau4 ngoi6 gong1
- **Korean:** 내유외강
- **Japanese:** ないじゅうがいごう（内柔外剛）_naijū gaigō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** nội nhu ngoại cương

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 內柔外剛
- **Simplified:** 内柔外刚
- Related expressions include **外柔内剛** (“soft outside, firm within”), which emphasizes the opposite outward posture.

### Source / Origin
The idea behind the phrase reflects a long-standing theme in **classical Chinese philosophy**, especially in discussions of moral character and governance. The combination of **inner gentleness and outer firmness** was often praised as the proper balance for a cultivated person or ruler.

Closely related formulations appear in early texts discussing character and leadership, where **柔 (softness)** is associated with compassion and humility, while **剛 (firmness)** represents strength, resolve, and moral backbone.

The pairing of these qualities echoes broader philosophical ideas found in works such as the **_Book of Changes_ (《易經》)** and later Confucian and Daoist writings, where balanced opposites are seen as essential to proper conduct.

Over time, the four-character form **内柔外剛** came to describe a personality that combines **inner kindness with outward strength and discipline**.

### Cultural Notes

- In **Chinese**, the phrase often describes someone whose **true nature is compassionate**, even though their outward demeanor may appear strict or firm.
    
- In **Korean**, **내유외강** is commonly used to praise a person who is **kind-hearted but strong in principle**.
    
- In **Japanese**, the idiom similarly conveys the ideal of **gentleness guided by strength**, often applied to leadership or personal character.
    

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他看起來嚴厲，其實內柔外剛。  
_He appears strict, but inwardly he is gentle and compassionate._

**Japanese:**  
彼女は内柔外剛の性格だ。  
_She has a character that is gentle within but firm outwardly._

**Korean:**  
그는 내유외강의 지도자로 평가받는다.  
_He is regarded as a leader who is gentle inside but firm outside._

### Sentiment
> **“Gentle in heart, yet firm in action — kindness supported by strength.”**
