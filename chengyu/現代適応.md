>[!Tip] The other of the two named pillars of [[文言現代]], the guiding motto for [[文法 - 04句法]] — the first being [[文言継承]]
[Dan'a'yo Chengyu](chengyu/Dan'a'yo%20Chengyu.md)

## Literal Meaning
- [現代](../words/現代.md) — the modern era, contemporary times
- [適応](../words/適応.md) — adaptation, to adapt, to conform to circumstances

Literally: "adaptation to the modern era."

## Extended or idiomatic sense
The principle that Dan'a'yo's grammar, however much it inherits from Literary Chinese (see [[文言継承]]), is not a museum-piece reconstruction of that classical register — it is bent back into shape as a usable everyday medium by two concrete requirements. First, **obligatory predication** (述語必須): every clause must carry an explicit verb or stative predicate; the bare nominal juxtaposition and verbless topic-comment sentences Classical Chinese freely permitted are not licensed outside the most archaic 古風体 register. Second, **fixed SVO order** (SVO語順): rather than reproducing Classical Chinese's older verb-object pattern or leaving constituent order as loose as the classics allowed, standard Dan'a'yo settles on a single, predictable subject–verb–object sequence.

現代適応 is the necessary counterweight to [[文言継承]] within [[文言現代]]'s overall balance. Where 文言継承 preserves what the classical register already did well — topic-comment structure, concision — 現代適応 supplies what that same register lacks for ordinary modern use: a guaranteed predicate in every clause, and a single word order a learner can rely on without first mastering the looser possibilities of classical syntax.

## Source and Origin
Coined within 単亜語 itself (2026-06-19), as the named companion to [[文言継承]], to give a standalone page to the second of the two components [[文法 - 04句法]] already names under its guiding motto [[文言現代]]. Built, like its companion, from two pre-existing words rather than new morphemes: [[現代]] (modern era) and [[適応]] (adaptation) — itself newly created alongside this chengyu, since it did not yet have its own entry despite being common across all five languages (Mandarin shìyìng, Japanese てきおう, Korean 적응, Vietnamese thích ứng).

## Standard Form
**現代適応** is the Dan'a'yo standard spelling, with 応 already the Shōwa-era simplified form (see [[覧昭和決]]).
- 現代適應 — the traditional-character form, preserved here as an alias

## Invocation
Invoke 現代適応 when explaining *why* a Dan'a'yo construction departs from what pure Classical Chinese would allow — most often the requirement for an overt predicate in every clause, or the insistence on SVO order where the classics would have tolerated something looser. It is the half of [[文言現代]] that says *this much must change for the language to work today*; reach for its counterpart [[文言継承]] when the point is instead about what is deliberately kept unchanged from Literary Chinese.

## Example Sentences
1. なぜDan'a'yoの文には必ず動詞があるのか — それは現代適応の要請だ。古典の省略を、現代の伝達のために制限している。
2. 어순을 SVO로 고정한 것은 현대적응의 결과다 — 옛 문언의 자유로운 어순은 현대 회화에는 맞지 않기 때문이다.
3. Dan'a'yo requires a verb in every clause not because Classical Chinese did, but because of 現代適応 — the language has to function in ordinary modern conversation, not just in the archaic register.

## Sentiment
Doctrinal and foundational, paired inseparably with [[文言継承]] as the two halves of [[文言現代]]. Neither half is "correct" without the other — 現代適応 names what had to give for Dan'a'yo to be speakable today, not a criticism of the classical tradition it departs from.
