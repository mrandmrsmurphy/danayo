## 旗幟鮮明
**Literal meaning:** “Banners and flags are bright and clear.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To take a clear and unmistakable stand; to make one’s position openly known.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** qí zhì xiān míng (2–4–1–2)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** kei4 zi3 sin1 ming4
- **Korean:** 기치선명
- **Japanese:** きしせんめい（旗幟鮮明）_kishi senmei_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** kỳ xí tiên minh

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 旗幟鮮明
- **Simplified:** 旗帜鲜明

### Source / Origin
The phrase is rooted in **military imagery from classical China**, where **flags and banners (旗幟)** were used to signal identity, command, and alignment on the battlefield.

Descriptions of **clearly visible, well-ordered banners** appear throughout historical texts such as the **_Records of the Grand Historian_ (《史記》)** and later military writings. A formation with **bright, distinct banners** indicated discipline, organization, and unmistakable allegiance.

From this imagery, the expression came to describe a person or group whose **position, principles, or loyalties are clearly declared and easily recognized**, just like banners visible from afar.

### Cultural Notes
- In **modern Chinese**, 旗幟鮮明 is frequently used in **political or ideological contexts**, meaning one should **state one’s stance clearly and without ambiguity**.
- It often carries a positive sense of **clarity, conviction, and openness**.
- In **Japanese** and **Korean**, the idiom similarly refers to **taking a clear and explicit position**, especially in public or institutional settings.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他在這個問題上態度旗幟鮮明。  
_His stance on this issue is clear and unmistakable._

**Japanese:**  
彼はその問題について旗幟鮮明な立場を取った。  
_He took a clear and explicit position on the issue._

**Korean:**  
그는 이 문제에 대해 기치선명한 입장을 밝혔다。  
_He expressed a clear and unmistakable position on this issue._

### Sentiment
> **“Banners flying bright and clear — a stance that leaves no room for doubt.”**
