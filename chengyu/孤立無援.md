# 孤立無援 / 孤立无援

**Literal meaning:** “Isolated and without aid.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To be alone with no help or support; to stand isolated, cut off from assistance — physically, socially, or morally.

---
### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** gū lì wú yuán (1–4–2–2)    
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** gu1 lap6 mou4 jyun4    
- **Korean:** 고립무원 (孤立無援) _[go-rip-mu-won]_    
- **Japanese:** こりつむえん（孤立無援）_koritsu muen_    
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** cô lập vô viện    

---
### Variants
- Traditional: 孤立無援    
- Simplified: 孤立无援    
- Related expressions:    
    - **孤軍奮鬥** — “a lone army fighting bravely.”        
    - **寡不敵眾** — “the few cannot withstand the many.”        
    - **眾叛親離** — “abandoned by all, betrayed even by kin.”        
    - **孤苦伶仃** — “utterly alone and pitiful.”        

---

### Source / Origin

The idea appears early in classical literature, though the fixed phrase **孤立無援** becomes common in **Han–Tang historical writing**.  
For example, **《史記·蘇秦列傳》** uses similar imagery to describe diplomats stranded without allies, and **《後漢書》** contains the phrase:

> 「孤立無援，勢窮力竭。」  
> _“Isolated and without aid, his power was exhausted.”_

Originally used in **military and political contexts**, the idiom later broadened to mean **any kind of helpless isolation** — emotional, moral, or intellectual.

---

### Cultural & Philosophical Meaning

- **Confucian:** Being isolated in righteousness (_道孤而行_ — “the Way is lonely, yet I walk it”) can be honorable if grounded in virtue.
    
- **Legalist / Political:** A state or person without allies is doomed.
    
- **Daoist:** The wise often stand alone because they follow the natural Way rather than the crowd.
    

Thus, _孤立無援_ can convey either **pathos or nobility** depending on context — tragic loneliness or steadfast independence.

---

### Usage Across Cultures

- **Chinese:** Used in politics, warfare, or moral reflection.
    
    - “他在改革中孤立無援，卻不曾退縮。”  
        _He was isolated in his reform efforts, yet never retreated._
        
- **Japanese:** _孤立無援 (koritsu muen)_ — “utterly isolated, with no help.” Common in literature and journalism.
    
- **Korean:** _고립무원 (gorip muwon)_ — widely used for diplomatic isolation or personal despair.
    
- **Vietnamese:** _Cô lập vô viện_ — “isolated with no assistance.”
    

---

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
他在敵人包圍中孤立無援。  
_He was surrounded by the enemy, alone and without help._

**Japanese:**  
彼は孤立無援の状況にあった。  
_He was in a state of complete isolation._

**Korean:**  
그는 고립무원의 처지에 놓였다.  
_He found himself isolated and without support._

**Vietnamese:**  
Anh ta cô lập vô viện, không ai giúp đỡ.  
_He was isolated and without anyone to help him._

---
### Sentiment

> **“Alone and cut off — yet still standing.”**

A phrase of hardship and endurance: the loneliness of the righteous or the tragic helplessness of the forsaken.  
Depending on tone, it can express pity, courage, or stoic resolve.
