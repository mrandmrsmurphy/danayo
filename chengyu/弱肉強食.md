## Notes
**Literal meaning:** “The weak are meat; the strong eat.”  
**Figurative meaning:** The strong prey on the weak; survival of the fittest; a ruthless competitive order.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** ruò ròu qiáng shí (4–4–2–2)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** joek6 juk6 koeng4 sik6
- **Korean:** 약육강식
- **Japanese:** じゃくにくきょうしょく（弱肉強食）_jakuniku kyōshoku_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** nhược nhục cường thực

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 弱肉強食
- **Simplified:** 弱肉强食

### Source / Origin
The expression is a relatively **late classical-style compound**, not traceable to a single early canonical text. It appears in **late imperial (Ming–Qing) prose** and became especially common in **modern East Asian discourse**.

The imagery is direct:
- **弱肉** — “the flesh of the weak"
- **強食** — “consumed by the strong”

The phrase gained wide circulation in the **late 19th and early 20th centuries**, particularly in China and Japan, where it was used to describe **international power politics** and social competition. It often served as a rendering or parallel to Western ideas such as **“survival of the fittest”** associated with **Social Darwinism**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **modern Chinese**, 弱肉強食 is commonly used to describe **harsh competition**, whether in nature, business, or geopolitics.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom became prominent in **Meiji-era discourse**, often used in discussions of national strength and global rivalry.
- In **Korean**, **약육강식** similarly describes a **brutal competitive environment**, sometimes with critical or cautionary overtones.

The phrase can be descriptive or critical, depending on context: it may either **acknowledge a harsh reality** or **condemn it**.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
在這個行業中，常常是弱肉強食。  
_In this industry, it is often a case of the strong preying on the weak._
**Japanese:**  
自然界は弱肉強食の世界だ。  
_The natural world is one of survival of the fittest._
**Korean:**  
그 사회는 약육강식의 논리가 지배한다。  
_That society is governed by the logic of the strong devouring the weak._

### Sentiment
> **“The weak become prey, the strong consume — a world governed by power.”**
