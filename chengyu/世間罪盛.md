[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: shì jiān zuì shèng
- **Cantonese**: sai3 gaan1 zeoi6 sing6
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: せけんざいせい
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 세간죄성 (se-gan joe-seong)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: thế gian tội thịnh

Note: 世間 is widely used in Buddhist and general CJK vocabulary meaning “the world” or “mundane realm.” The compound here is a theological construction within Dan'a'yo.

### Literal Meaning
- **世間** the world, the present age, the human sphere
- **罪** sin, transgression
- **盛** abundant, flourishing, overflowing

Literal: “In the world, sin abounds.”

### Figurative Meaning
A compact declaration of pervasive sinfulness in the human order. It emphasizes not isolated wrongdoing but systemic moral saturation.

### Scriptural Resonance
While not quoting a single verse, the phrase draws from several biblical themes:
- **Genesis 6:5** — human wickedness great upon the earth.
- **Psalm 14:3** — “There is none that doeth good.”
- **Romans 5:20** — “Where sin abounded…”
- **1 John 5:19** — “The whole world lieth in wickedness.”

The Pauline formulation in **Romans 5:20** is particularly close in structure: sin “increased” or “abounded.” Greek: _ἐπλεόνασεν ἡ ἁμαρτία_.

### Cultural Notes
The deliberate use of **[[世間]]** carries layered resonance. In Buddhist discourse, 世間 denotes the conditioned, impermanent realm of suffering. By appropriating the term, Dan'a'yo reframes it within biblical theology: the problem of the world is not merely impermanence or ignorance but moral rebellion.

**罪盛** stresses excess and proliferation. It suggests that sin is not merely present but ascendant.

The phrase can function historically, as in the pre-Flood narrative, or diagnostically, describing any age marked by moral decay. Unlike Buddhist pessimism, however, biblical theology pairs this diagnosis with redemptive intervention.

Within Dan'a'yo, 世間罪盛 stands as an anthropological and cultural assessment. It names corruption without surrendering hope.

### Example Usage
> 當世間罪盛之時，更顯救贖之需。  
> When sin flourishes in the world, the need for redemption becomes clearer.

### Tone and Sentiment
Somber, diagnostic, morally sober. It acknowledges pervasive corruption while implicitly preparing for divine remedy.
