**Literal meaning:** “As if no one were beside him.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Acting arrogantly or without regard for others; behaving as though nobody else is present.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** bàng ruò wú rén (4–4–2–2)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** bong6 joek6 mou4 jan4
- **Korean:** 방약무인
- **Japanese:** ぼうじゃくぶじん（傍若無人）_bōjaku bujin_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** bàng nhược vô nhân

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 傍若無人
- **Simplified:** 傍若无人

### Source / Origin
The expression appears in **Sima Qian’s _Records of the Grand Historian_ (《史記》)**, in the **“Biography of the Marquis of Wei (魏其武安侯列傳)”**. The text describes a person whose conduct was so arrogant that he behaved **as if no one else were present beside him**.

The phrase originally referred to a manner of **disregarding the presence or dignity of others**, implying pride or insolence. The imagery is simple: someone acts freely and boldly, ignoring everyone around him, as though he were completely alone.

Over time it developed into a fixed idiom describing **brazen, overbearing behavior**, especially by someone who assumes authority or importance.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 傍若無人 usually criticizes someone for **arrogant or inconsiderate conduct**, such as speaking loudly or behaving domineeringly in public.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom often describes someone acting **self-important or overconfident**, ignoring others around them.
- In **Korean**, 방약무인 similarly refers to someone who behaves **as though no one else matters**.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他在會議上大聲說話，傍若無人。  
_He spoke loudly at the meeting as if nobody else were there._

**Japanese:**  
彼は傍若無人な態度で周囲を困らせた。  
_His arrogant behavior troubled everyone around him._

**Korean:**  
그는 방약무인하게 행동했다。  
_He behaved as if no one else existed._

### Sentiment

> **“To act as though no one else is present — bold to the point of arrogance.”**
