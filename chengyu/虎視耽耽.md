## Literal Meaning
- [虎](characters/虎%20(char).md) — tiger
- [視](characters/視%20(char).md) — to gaze, look at
- [耽](characters/耽%20(char).md) — deep, intense (of a gaze)
- 耽 — (repeated for emphasis)

Literally: "The tiger gazes — intensely, intensely."

## Extended Meaning
To watch something with fierce, predatory covetousness — the alert stillness of a hunter who has spotted prey and is waiting for the moment to strike. Used of rivals, enemies, or powerful actors who have fixed their eye on a target and are biding their time. The repetition of 耽耽 underscores the relentlessness of the gaze: it does not waver.

## Source and Origin
From the I Ching (《易經·頤卦》), Hexagram 27 (頤, "Nourishment"), third line commentary:

> 虎視耽耽，其欲逐逐。
> "The tiger's gaze is fierce and relentless; its desires unceasing."

Hexagram 頤 concerns what one nourishes oneself on — physically and morally. The tiger image appears in the third-line commentary (六三爻辭), warning against seeking nourishment in a predatory or degraded way. Over time the phrase crystallized into a standalone idiom for any posture of dangerous, hungry watchfulness.

## Standard Form
**虎視耽耽 / 虎視眈眈**

- **虎視眈眈** — dominant modern form in Mandarin; 眈眈 (dāndān) uses the eye radical (目), making the visual intensity explicit
- **虎視耽耽** — the original I Ching orthography; 耽耽 (dāndān) uses the ear radical (耳); same pronunciation
- Dan'a'yo preserves the classical I Ching form: **耽耽**

## Cultural Notes
Used throughout the CJKV sphere to describe predatory, patient watchfulness — the posture of a powerful actor who has identified a target and is waiting for vulnerability. In Mandarin and Cantonese the idiom is distinctly menacing; it frequently appears in political commentary, business rivalry, and literary description of threatening figures.

In Japanese, こしたんたん is common across formal and informal registers and can occasionally carry a slightly less negative nuance — patient determination rather than pure predation — though the threatening undertone remains. In Korean, 호시탐탐 is a widely used loanword idiom that retains the covetous, watchful sense. In Vietnamese the Hán-Việt form is primarily literary.

## Example Sentences
1. 強国虎視耽耽，伺機而動。
   "The powerful nation watches like a tiger, waiting for its moment to strike."
2. 競争者虎視耽耽地盯著这片市場。
   "Competitors eye this market with the fierce gaze of a prowling tiger."
3. He had watched his rival's stumbles with the quiet intensity of a predator — 虎視耽耽, patient and dangerous.

## Sentiment
Strongly negative or foreboding. Describes dangerous, predatory watchfulness — typically applied to enemies, rivals, or threats waiting to strike. Never complimentary.
