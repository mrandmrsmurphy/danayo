[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: yín pán chéng shǒu
- **Cantonese**: ngan4 pun4 cing4 sau2
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: ぎんばんていしゅ
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 은반정수 (eun-ban-jeong-su)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: ngân bàn trình thủ

### Literal Meaning
- **銀** silver
- **盤** platter, tray
- **呈** to present, submit
- **首** head

Literal: “A head presented on a silver platter.”

### Figurative Meaning
A vivid reference to the martyrdom of John the Baptist. By extension, it can signify unjust execution at the whim of power, especially when truth-telling provokes hostility.

In broader metaphorical usage, it may denote delivering something decisively into another’s hands, though its primary force remains grim and judicial.

### Scriptural Source
The idiom condenses the account found in:
- **Matthew 14:6–11**
- **Mark 6:21–28**

Mark 6:28 (English)
> “And brought his head in a charger, and gave it to the damsel…”

Greek: _ἐπὶ πίνακι_ — “on a platter.”

Traditional Chinese translations render:
> “把他的頭放在盤子裡，拿來給女子。”

The Dan'a'yo form sharpens the metallic detail with 銀盤, reflecting the banquet setting and courtly excess implied in the narrative.

### Example Usage
> 忠言逆耳，終至銀盤呈首，然真理未滅。  
> Faithful speech offended power and ended in a head upon a silver platter, yet the truth did not perish.

### Cultural Notes
John’s death occurs within a royal banquet scene marked by oath, pride, and spectacle. The presentation of the head on a platter turns a prophetic life into a courtly display. The contrast between prophetic austerity and decadent power heightens the moral drama.

In Christian theology, John is the forerunner not only in preaching but in suffering. His martyrdom anticipates that of Jesus. The episode warns of political volatility and the cost of moral rebuke.

The use of 呈 emphasizes formal presentation, almost ceremonial cruelty. 銀盤 evokes wealth and luxury, intensifying the grotesque contrast between refinement and violence.

Within Dan'a'yo, 銀盤呈首 functions as a somber emblem of prophetic martyrdom and the peril of confronting corrupt authority.

### Tone and Sentiment
Grim, tragic, morally charged. It evokes injustice, prophetic courage, and the cost of truth.
