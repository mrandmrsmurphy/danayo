[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: zhāo zāi yǐn huò
- **Cantonese**: ziu1 zoi1 jan5 wo6
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: しょうさいいんか
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 초재인화 (cho-jae-in-hwa)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: chiêu tai dẫn họa

Note: [[招災]] and [[引禍]] are established two-character compounds meaning “to invite disaster” and “to bring on calamity.” Their pairing intensifies causation.

### Literal Meaning
- **招** to summon, invite
- **災** disaster
- **引** to draw in, lead
- **禍** calamity

Literal: “Invite disaster and draw calamity.”

### Figurative Meaning
A general maxim expressing that certain actions bring about ruin. In biblical framing, it often refers to rebellion against God that provokes judgment.

### Scriptural Resonance
While not drawn from a single verse, the concept pervades biblical literature:
- **Proverbs 8:36** — “He that sinneth against me wrongeth his own soul.”
- **Jeremiah 7:19** — “Do they not provoke themselves to the confusion of their own faces?    
- **Galatians 6:7** — “Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap.”

Old Testament prophetic texts frequently portray idolatry and injustice as actions that “bring” calamity upon a people.

Traditional Chinese Bible translations often use phrases such as 自招禍患 (“bring calamity upon oneself”), closely aligned with this maxim.

### Example Usage
> 背約棄義，實乃招災引禍之始。  
> To abandon covenant and righteousness is the beginning of inviting disaster.

### Cultural Notes
In classical Chinese moral discourse, 招災引禍 expresses a principle of moral causality. It does not specify divine agency; it may function almost proverbially.

In biblical theology, however, calamity is not impersonal fate. It is either the natural consequence of folly or the judicial act of a covenant God. The language of “inviting” disaster underscores human responsibility.

The doubling of 招 and 引 emphasizes active causation. Ruin is not random; it is summoned by wrongdoing.

Within Dan'a'yo, 招災引禍 can function broadly in wisdom contexts or more specifically in covenantal warning, depending on surrounding theology.

### Tone and Sentiment
Warning, causal, morally instructive. It stresses that actions have serious and often self-induced consequences.
