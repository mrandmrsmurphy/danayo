## Literal Meaning
- [鶏](../characters/鶏%20(char).md) — chicken
- [鳴](../characters/鳴%20(char).md) — cry, crow
- [狗](../characters/狗.md) — dog
- [盗](../characters/盗.md) — steal

Literally: "The crowing of a rooster and the stealing of a dog."

## Extended Meaning
People skilled only in petty tricks; tricksters or those who rely on low cunning to achieve goals. Usually negative, referring to deceitful or insignificant men — but occasionally used admiringly, for resourceful trickery that saves the day despite its low means.

## Source and Origin
From the *Records of the Grand Historian* (《史記·孟嘗君列傳》), the biography of Lord Mengchang (孟嘗君) by Sima Qian (司馬遷).

During the Warring States period, Lord Mengchang of Qi kept a large retinue of retainers, many with strange or dubious talents. When he fell out of favor in the state of Qin and was imprisoned, he escaped thanks to two of these men: one who could imitate a rooster's crow, fooling the guards into opening the gates early, and another who could steal (or bribe a guard for) a white fur pelt needed to secure passage. "Rooster-crower and dog-thief" came to symbolize those with base but useful skills — lowly people who nonetheless achieve great results through cunning.

## Standard Form
**鷄鳴狗盜** / **雞鳴狗盜** (traditional, both forms of "chicken" attested) — **鸡鸣狗盗** (simplified).

## Cultural Notes
In Chinese, usually negative — deceitful or insignificant men — though sometimes used admiringly for resourceful trickery that saves the day. In Japanese, 鶏鳴狗盗 (keimei kutō) often appears in essays as a moral warning against being "petty in ability but proud in manner." In Korean, 계명구도 is a well-known 사자성어 (four-character idiom) for "petty skill," sometimes used self-deprecatingly. In Vietnamese, *kê minh cẩu đạo* similarly refers to crafty but small-minded people — "tricksters, not gentlemen."

## Example Sentences
1. 這些人不過是鷄鳴狗盜之徒，難成大事。
   "These people are mere tricksters — the sort who crow like roosters and steal like dogs; they cannot accomplish great things."
2. 彼は鶏鳴狗盗の徒にすぎない。
   "He's nothing more than a petty trickster."
3. 그는 계명구도의 재주밖에 없다.
   "He has only the petty talents of a rooster-crower and dog-thief."

## Sentiment
Petty cunning and lowly trickery — contemptible in moral worth, yet sometimes surprisingly useful. Originally mocking the base retainers of a nobleman, the phrase has evolved into a proverb about small talents, cunning minds, and unexpected utility.
