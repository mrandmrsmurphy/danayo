[[Biblical Chengyu]]

### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: zhǔ zǎi wàn wù
- **Cantonese**: zyu2 zoi2 maan6 mat6
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: しゅさいばんぶつ
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 주재만물 (ju-jae-man-mul)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: chủ tể vạn vật

### Literal Meaning
- **主** lord, master
- **宰** to govern, rule, administer
- **万／萬** ten thousand, myriad
- **物** things, all created entities

Literal: “Lord and governor of all things.”

### Figurative Meaning
Affirms God’s absolute sovereignty over creation. It denotes comprehensive authority, providential governance, and ultimate ownership of all that exists.

### Scriptural Source
While not a direct quotation of a single verse, the phrase summarizes multiple biblical affirmations of divine sovereignty:
- **Genesis 1:1** — God as Creator of heaven and earth.
- **Psalm 24:1** — “The earth is the LORD’s, and the fullness thereof.”
- **Daniel 4:35** — “He doeth according to his will… none can stay his hand.”
- **Revelation 4:11** — Worthy because all things were created by Him.

Traditional Chinese Bible translations often use expressions such as **萬有之主** or **掌管萬有**. The Dan'a'yo form employs 宰 to stress active governance rather than mere possession.

### Example Usage
> 信者承認神為主宰万物，不獨人心，亦包括歴史與自然。  
> The faithful confess God as Lord of all things, not only of hearts, but of history and nature.

### Cultural Notes
The pairing 主宰 has long-standing philosophical resonance in classical East Asian discourse, where it can denote ultimate authority or controlling principle. In Christian theology, however, sovereignty is personal and covenantal, not impersonal fate.

万物 signals totality. Like the Hebrew merisms “heaven and earth,” it expresses universal scope. The phrase therefore functions doxologically as well as doctrinally.

In theological debates, 主宰万物 bears on questions of providence, predestination, natural law, and human freedom. Within a Dan'a'yo framework, it serves as a foundational confession: creation, history, and destiny are not autonomous.

### Tone and Sentiment
Majestic, confessional, comprehensive. It proclaims divine sovereignty with reverent assurance.
