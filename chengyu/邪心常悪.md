[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: xié xīn cháng è
- **Cantonese**: ce4 sam1 soeng4 ok3
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: じゃしんじょうあく
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 사심상악 (sa-sim-sang-ak)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: tà tâm thường ác

### Literal Meaning
- **邪** crooked, perverse, evil
- **心** heart, inner disposition
- **常** continually, constantly
- **悪／惡** evil, wicked

Literal: “The crooked heart is continually evil.”

### Figurative Meaning
A summary statement of human depravity: the persistent inclination of the human heart toward evil. It emphasizes habitual disposition rather than isolated acts.

### Scriptural Source
This idiom condenses two key passages in Genesis:
- **Genesis 6:5**
    > “Every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually.”
- **Genesis 8:21**
    > “For the imagination of man’s heart is evil from his youth.”

Traditional Chinese translations often render these verses with phrases such as:

- “人心裡所思想的盡都是惡”
- “人從幼年心裡懷著惡念”

The Dan'a'yo formulation captures the repeated biblical focus on 心 and the temporal emphasis of “continually” (常).

### Example Usage
> 洪水之前，邪心常悪；洪水之後，亦未改其本性。  
> Before the Flood the heart was continually evil; after the Flood, its nature was not fundamentally changed.

### Cultural Notes
Genesis presents a striking narrative tension. In chapter 6, the pervasive corruption of humanity precipitates the Flood. In chapter 8, after judgment has fallen, God acknowledges that the human heart remains inclined toward evil, yet promises restraint in judgment.

The repetition highlights a theological point: the Flood did not eradicate sin at its root. The problem is internal, not merely environmental. Christian theology has drawn from these verses in articulating doctrines of original sin and total depravity.

The pairing of 邪心 and 常悪 stresses habitual moral bent rather than occasional wrongdoing. The heart in biblical anthropology is the seat of will and intention, not merely emotion.

Within Dan'a'yo, this chengyu often functions as a sober diagnosis of the human condition, especially when contrasted with later themes of covenant mercy and redemption.

### Tone and Sentiment
Somber, diagnostic, theologically weighty. It conveys moral realism and underscores the depth of human fallenness.
