## Notes
- [諸 (char)](characters/諸%20(char).md) "various" + [法 (char)](characters/法%20(char).md) "law" + [[無我]] "no self"

**Literal meaning:** “All dharmas are without self.”  
**Figurative meaning:** No phenomenon possesses an independent, permanent self; all things are non-self.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** zhū fǎ wú wǒ (1–3–2–3)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** zyu1 faat3 mou4 ngo5
- **Korean:** 제법무아
- **Japanese:** しょほうむが（諸法無我）_shohō muga_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** chư pháp vô ngã

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 諸法無我
- **Simplified:** 诸法无我
- Commonly listed with:

### Source / Origin
The phrase is a standard **Buddhist doctrinal formula**, translating the Sanskrit:

**सर्वधर्माः अनात्मानः (sarva-dharmā anātmānaḥ)**  
“All phenomena are without self.”

It appears throughout **early Buddhist scriptures**, including the **Āgamas** (Chinese counterparts to the Pāli Nikāyas), and is foundational in both **Theravāda and Mahāyāna** traditions.

Here:
- **諸法 (sarva-dharmāḥ)** refers to all phenomena, whether physical or mental
- **無我 (anātman)** denies the existence of any enduring, independent self or essence

The teaching asserts that what we take to be a “self” is in fact a **composite of changing processes**, lacking permanence or autonomy.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese Buddhist thought**, 諸法無我 is a technical term central to discussions of **emptiness (空)** and dependent origination (緣起).
- In **Japanese**, the concept of **無我 (muga)** also influenced broader cultural ideas of **selflessness and egolessness**, though often in a softened or ethical sense.
- In **Korean**, **제법무아** remains a key doctrinal expression in Buddhist teaching.

Unlike many chengyu, this phrase is not metaphorical but **philosophical and analytical**, forming one of the core insights of Buddhist doctrine.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
佛教強調諸法無我，認為萬物皆無固定自性。  
_Buddhism emphasizes that all phenomena lack a fixed self-nature._

**Japanese:**  
諸法無我は仏教の基本的な教えの一つである。  
_Non-self is one of the fundamental teachings of Buddhism._

**Korean:**  
불교에서는 제법무아를 중요한 진리로 본다。  
_In Buddhism, non-self is regarded as a fundamental truth._

### Sentiment
> **“No thing possesses a self — all phenomena arise and pass without enduring essence.”**
