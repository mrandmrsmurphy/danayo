# 文言現代

## Literal meaning
“Classical language, contemporary use.”

## Extended or idiomatic sense
The principle that classical written forms and modern communicative needs are not opposites but complementary layers of a single linguistic continuom. 文言現代 affirms that a language may retain the concision, abstraction, and cultural density of classical style while functioning as a practical medium for present-day communication. In <ruby>単亜語<rt>ㄉㄚㄋ·ㄚ·⼄</rt></ruby> discourse, it names an approach that modernizes usage without vernacularizing structure.

文言現代 holds two named sub-principles in balance, set out in [[文法 - 04句法]]:
1. [[文言継承]] ("continuity with the classical written standard") — preserving topic–comment structure and concision.
2. [[現代適応]] ("adaptation to modern speech") — requiring verbs in every clause and maintaining SVO order.

**Notes on scope and tone**  
文言現代 is methodological and synthetic in scope. It rejects the binary between archaic preservation and full vernacular replacement, proposing instead a layered continuity.
