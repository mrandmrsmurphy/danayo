## Literal Meaning
- [優](../characters/優.md) — gentle, yielding
- [柔](../characters/柔%20(char).md) — soft, tender
- [不](../characters/不%20(char).md) — not
- [断](../characters/断.md) — to decide, to cut off

Literally: "Gentle and yielding, yet unable to decide."

## Extended Meaning
Indecisive; overcautious to the point of paralysis; unable to act despite reflection. The criticism is subtle: 優柔 does not originally mean weakness — it denotes softness, patience, and consideration — while 不断 marks a specific failure of resolution. The problem named is not lack of thought but excess of it: a temperament that values harmony and caution but cannot cross the threshold into decision. This is not rashness, ignorance, or cowardice — it is the failure mode of a reflective person in situations that demand commitment.

## Source and Origin
From the *Book of the Later Han* (《後漢書》), the Biography of Du Lin (杜林傳). In its original setting, the phrase functions as a character judgment, part of the Han historiographical habit of diagnosing political and moral failure through compact personal descriptors.

## Standard Form
**優柔不斷** (traditional) / **优柔不断** (simplified). A closely related but distinct idiom, **優柔寡斷** / **优柔寡断** ("gentle and yielding, wanting in decisiveness"), is often used interchangeably in modern speech and is preserved here as an alias, though it is strictly its own four-character form rather than a script variant of 優柔不斷.

## Cultural Notes
Throughout the region, the idiom describes people, leadership styles, or processes that stall because of hesitation. It appears in political commentary, management critique, and personal evaluation. The tone is negative but measured — it suggests frustration rather than moral condemnation, and often carries an implicit contrast with the decisive virtues valued in Confucian and administrative traditions: it criticizes not thoughtfulness itself, but the inability to translate judgment into action when circumstances demand it.

## Example Sentences
1. 他優柔不断，錯過了最佳時機。
   "He was so indecisive that he missed the best opportunity."
2. 彼はいつも優柔不断で、なかなか決断できない。
   "He's always so indecisive, unable to make up his mind."
3. The committee was so *yōuróu bùduàn* that the proposal died from sheer inaction, not opposition.

## Sentiment
Mildly critical, exasperated rather than contemptuous. Names a specific, recognizable failure — reflection that never resolves into action.
