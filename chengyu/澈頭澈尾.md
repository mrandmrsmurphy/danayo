- [澈](characters/澈.md) "penetrate" + [頭 (char)](characters/頭%20(char).md) "head" + [澈](characters/澈.md) "penetrate" + [尾 (char)](characters/尾%20(char).md) "tail"
**Literal meaning:** “From head to tail, completely penetrating.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Thoroughly; completely; from beginning to end without exception.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** chè tóu chè wěi (4–2–4–3)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** cit3 tau4 cit3 mei5
- **Korean:** 철두철미
- **Japanese:** てっとうてつび（徹頭徹尾）_tettō tetsubi_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** triệt đầu triệt vĩ

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 徹頭徹尾
- **Simplified:** 彻头彻尾

### Source / Origin
The expression is built from:
- **徹** — to penetrate through; to carry through completely
- **頭 / 尾** — head and tail, representing the beginning and the end

This type of construction reflects a common classical pattern where **paired body parts or endpoints** indicate totality. While not traceable to a single canonical passage, similar formulations appear in **late imperial prose**, especially in **Ming and Qing vernacular literature**, where the phrase was used to emphasize **completeness or consistency throughout**.

Over time, 徹頭徹尾 became a fixed idiom meaning **without any exception or partiality**, covering the entire extent of something.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, the phrase can be neutral (“completely”) or negative (“utterly,” as in thoroughly bad).
- In **Japanese**, 徹頭徹尾 often emphasizes **consistency of attitude or behavior from start to finish**.
- In **Korean**, **철두철미** commonly describes someone who is **meticulous and thorough**, especially in planning or execution.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他的計劃從頭到尾都是徹頭徹尾的錯誤。  
_His plan was completely wrong from beginning to end._
**Japanese:**  
彼は徹頭徹尾自分の立場を貫いた。  
_He maintained his position thoroughly from beginning to end._
**Korean:**  
그는 일을 철두철미하게 처리한다。  
_He handles work thoroughly and meticulously._

### Sentiment
> **“From head to tail — complete in every part, without exception.”**
