[Misc. Chengyu](chengyu/Misc.%20Chengyu.md)

**Literal meaning:** “To abandon oneself and cast oneself away.”  
**Extended sense:** To give up on oneself; to fall into despair or reckless self-neglect.

**Source:** From the **《孟子》**. The phrase appears in discussions of moral cultivation, where **孟子** criticizes attitudes that prevent self-improvement.

**Classical background:**  
暴 here means to expose or ruin, while 棄 means to discard. Both verbs describe acts directed at oneself. The doubling (自…自…) intensifies the idea of self-inflicted abandonment. In the Mencian context, the phrase describes people who surrender their moral potential by refusing correction or instruction.

The passage contrasts such individuals with those capable of moral growth. The fault lies not in initial ignorance but in rejecting the possibility of cultivation. For Mencius, this refusal blocks the development of innate moral tendencies.

**Modern usage:**  
Across the region, 自暴自棄 describes a state of discouragement in which a person stops trying to improve their situation or behavior. It appears in educational settings, psychological discussion, journalism, and everyday speech. The tone can be sympathetic or admonitory depending on context.

**Usage note:**  
The idiom emphasizes self-directed defeat. The obstacle is not external hardship but the decision to abandon one’s own capacity for change.
