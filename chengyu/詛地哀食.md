[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: zǔ dì āi shí
- **Cantonese**: zo3 dei6 oi1 sik6
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: そちあいしょく
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 저지애식 (jeo-ji-ae-sik)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: trở địa ai thực

### Literal Meaning
- **詛** curse, pronounce judgment upon
- **地** ground, earth
- **哀** sorrow, grief, painful hardship
- **食** eat, obtain sustenance
Literal: “The ground cursed; in sorrow eat.”

### Figurative Meaning
A compact summary of the curse upon the earth and humanity’s painful labor from it. It emphasizes both cosmic disorder and economic hardship following the Fall.

### Scriptural Source
This maxim condenses **Genesis 3:17–18**:
> “Cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life.  
> Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee…”

Hebrew:  
_אֲרוּרָה הָאֲדָמָה… בְּעִצָּבוֹן תֹּאכֲלֶנָּה_  
“Cursed is the ground… in toil you shall eat of it.”

Traditional Chinese translations render:
> “地必為你的緣故受咒詛；你必終身勞苦才能從地裡得吃的。”

The Dan'a'yo form preserves the twofold structure: curse upon the ground and sorrowful eating.

### Theological Context
Genesis 3 presents layered consequences:
- [[加哀痛産]] — intensified pain in childbirth
- [[欲夫治汝]] — relational distortion
- [[詛地哀食]] — agricultural hardship
- [[汗食帰泥]] — toil and mortality

詛地哀食 highlights that the curse extends beyond the human body to the environment itself. Creation becomes resistant.

This anticipates later biblical themes:
- Romans 8:20–22 — creation subjected to futility
- Eschatological hope of restoration

### Example Usage
> 自詛地哀食以來，人勞而不息，地亦不復如初。  
> Since the ground was cursed and eating became sorrowful, humanity labors without rest, and the earth is no longer as it was.

### Cultural Notes
The Hebrew text stresses _adamah_, the soil from which humanity was formed. The curse upon the ground mirrors the human condition. The relationship between person and earth is strained.

哀 here does not merely denote emotion but sustained hardship. The verse describes structural frustration, not episodic inconvenience.

In Dan'a'yo theology, 詛地哀食 underscores cosmic scope. The Fall affects ecology, labor, and material life. It frames history as marked by resistance until restoration.

### Tone and Sentiment
Somber, agrarian, existential. It conveys hardship woven into the fabric of fallen creation.
