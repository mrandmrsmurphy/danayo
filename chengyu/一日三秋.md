## Classical Source

The earliest textual source is the **诗经·王风·采葛** (_Book of Odes / Book of Songs_, “Airs of the States – Odes of Wei, _Gathering Kudzu_”).

Text of stanza 2:

> 彼采葛兮，  
> 一日不见，如三月兮。

Translation (Legge):

> “He gathers the kudzu;  
> One day without seeing (her)  
> Is like three months.”

## 一日三秋
### Standard Form
**一日三秋**  
No common orthographic variants. The form is consistent across Traditional and Simplified Chinese and appears identically in Japanese, Korean (Hanja), and Vietnamese literary contexts.

### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin (Pinyin):** yī rì sān qiū
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** jat1 jat6 saam1 cau1
- **Japanese (on-yomi, hiragana):** いちじつさんしゅう
- **Korean (Hangul):** 일일삼추 (il-il-sam-chu)
- **Vietnamese (Hán–Việt):** nhất nhật tam thu

### Literal Meaning
- 一 — one
- 日 — day
- 三 — three
- 秋 — autumns

Literally: “One day [is like] three autumns.”

In classical reckoning, autumn symbolizes length and maturity of time. The phrase expresses an extreme dilation of perceived time.

### Figurative Meaning
A single day feels as long as three years.  
Used to describe intense longing, especially in separation from a loved one, friend, or ruler.

It conveys emotional impatience, yearning, or anxious anticipation.

### Source and Origin
From the **_Shijing_ (詩經)**, specifically **“Cai Ge” (采葛)** in the section **Wang Feng (王風)**:

> 一日不見，如三秋兮  
> (“One day without seeing [you], it is like three autumns.”)

The chengyu condenses this poetic line into a four-character fixed expression. The imagery originates in early Zhou-period lyric poetry and reflects themes of longing and separation.


### Cultural Notes
- Strongly associated with classical poetic diction.
- Often appears in romantic or elegiac contexts.
- The emotional tone is more refined and literary than casual speech.
- While understandable in modern usage, it carries a distinctly classical resonance.

### Example Sentences
1. 別後思君，一日三秋。
2. Since she left, each day feels like _yī rì sān qiū_.
3. Awaiting news from the front, the family felt _one day like three autumns_.

### Sentiment
Deeply emotional, nostalgic, and often romantic.  
Positive in affection, but rooted in longing and absence rather than joy.
