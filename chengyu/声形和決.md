## 声形和決

### Readings
- Mandarin: shēng xíng hé jué
- Cantonese: sing¹ jing⁴ wo⁴ kyut³
- Japanese (hiragana): せいけい わけつ
- Korean (Hangul): 성형화결 (seong-hyeong hwa-gyeol)
- Vietnamese: thanh hình hòa quyết

### Literal structure
- 声: sound, phonology
- 形: form, written shape (glyph)
- 和: harmony, coordination
- 決: resolution, determination, settled principle

The compound suggests not merely coexistence but a resolved alignment between sound and form.

### Interpretation
A design principle of script presentation:

Phonetic realization (sound) and graphic form (character) are to be displayed together in a stable, unified system.

### Operational meaning in Dan’a’yo
This governs orthographic display and literacy practice:

1. **Dual-layer representation**  
    Standard presentation pairs:
    - base layer: Shinjitai kanji (semantic / morphographic)
    - annotation layer: Bopomofo (phonological) via ruby
2. **Persistent phonetic visibility**  
    Unlike Taiwanese pedagogy, phonetic annotation is not transitional. It remains a permanent feature, not a scaffold to be removed.
3. **Priority rule**  
    In cases of constraint (space, typography, etc.):
    - characters (形) take precedence
    - phonetic layer (声) may be omitted if necessary
4. **Target equilibrium**  
    The ideal state is not replacement but **co-presence**, maintaining:
    - semantic clarity via characters
    - phonological transparency via Bopomofo        

### Underlying principles
- **Bimodal literacy**: reading engages both visual-morphological and phonological channels simultaneously
- **Cross-CJKV accommodation**: since most users come from phonetic writing systems, phonology must remain explicit
- **Stability over pedagogy**: rejects the “training wheels” model of phonetic scripts

### Effect
- Reduces ambiguity in pronunciation
- Facilitates acquisition across linguistic backgrounds
- Preserves the semantic density of characters while ensuring accessibility

## Concise definition
“A principle mandating the coordinated display of phonology and character form, ideally through permanent ruby annotation, with characters taking precedence when compromise is required.”

### Note on nuance
和決 carries a slightly stronger sense than simple 和. It implies that harmony is not accidental but deliberately fixed as policy. This matches the system: not just coexistence, but enforced alignment.
