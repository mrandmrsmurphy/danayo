## Notes
- [因](characters/因.md) + [果](characters/果.md) + [報 (char)](characters/報%20(char).md) + [応 (char)](characters/応%20(char).md)

**Literal meaning:** “Cause and effect; retribution in response.”  
**Figurative meaning:** One’s actions inevitably bring corresponding results; moral causation, often with the sense of retribution.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** yīn guǒ bào yìng (1–3–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** jan1 gwo2 bou3 jing3
- **Korean:** 인과보응
- **Japanese:** いんがおうほう（因果応報）_inga ōhō_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** nhân quả báo ứng

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 因果報應
- **Simplified:** 因果报应
- Japanese commonly uses the reordered form: **因果応報**

### Source / Origin
The expression is **Buddhist in origin**, though its components draw on broader Chinese vocabulary.

- **[[因果]] (hetu–phala)** translates the Buddhist doctrine of **cause and effect**, central to karma.
- **報応 / 報應 (vipāka / karma-phala)** refers to the **resulting recompense or retribution** of actions.

These ideas appear throughout **Buddhist scriptures translated into Chinese** from the early centuries CE onward, including texts in the **Āgamas** and later Mahāyāna literature. The combined phrase 因果報應 became a standard formulation expressing the full cycle: **actions (causes) inevitably produce results (effects), often morally weighted**.

While the exact four-character compound stabilized later in vernacular and didactic literature, the concept itself is foundational to **Buddhist karmic theory**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 因果報應 often carries a strong sense of **moral retribution**, sometimes close to “what goes around comes around.”
- In **Japanese**, **因果応報** is widely used in both religious and secular contexts, sometimes with a fatalistic tone.
- In **Korean**, **인과보응** similarly emphasizes that **good and evil deeds bring corresponding consequences**.

Although rooted in Buddhism, the concept has been widely absorbed into **general East Asian moral thinking**, including Confucian-influenced ethics and popular belief.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他相信因果報應，認為善有善報，惡有惡報。  
_He believes in karmic retribution, that good is rewarded and evil punished._
**Japanese:**  
因果応報という考え方は広く知られている。  
_The idea of karmic retribution is widely known._
**Korean:**  
그는 인과보응을 믿는다。  
_He believes in moral cause and effect._

### Sentiment
> **“Every cause bears its fruit — actions return to the one who set them in motion.”**
