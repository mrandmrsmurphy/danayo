**Literal meaning:** “Flowing words and flying talk.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Rumors and gossip; unfounded talk that spreads quickly.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** liú yán fēi yǔ (2–2–1–3)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** lau4 jin4 fei1 jyu5
- **Korean:** 유언비어
- **Japanese:** りゅうげんひご（流言飛語）_ryūgen higo_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** lưu ngôn phi ngữ

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 流言飛語
- **Simplified:** 流言飞语
- Related expressions include **流言蜚語**, using 蜚 (“to fly”) in place of 飛.

### Source / Origin
The expression appears in early Chinese historical writings describing the **rapid spread of rumors and slander**. A closely related form occurs in the **_Book of Documents_ (《尚書》)** in the chapter **“Jin Teng” (金滕)**, where the phrase **流言蜚語** refers to malicious rumors circulating among the people.

The imagery is vivid:
- **流言** (“flowing words”) suggests speech that spreads like water through a population.
- **飛語 / 蜚語** (“flying talk”) suggests rumors moving swiftly through the air, carried from person to person.

Together the phrase came to describe **false or exaggerated reports that circulate widely**, often damaging reputations or causing unrest.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 流言飛語 usually refers to **baseless rumors or slanderous gossip**, particularly those spreading through a community.
    
- In **Japanese**, the phrase is commonly used in media or commentary to refer to **rumors lacking reliable evidence**.
    
- In **Korean**, **유언비어** often appears in news or legal contexts, describing **false rumors that spread through society**, sometimes with the implication of public harm.
    

### Example Usage

**Mandarin:**  
不要相信那些流言飛語。  
_Do not believe those rumors._

**Japanese:**  
流言飛語に惑わされてはいけない。  
_You must not be misled by rumors._

**Korean:**  
그 소문은 유언비어에 불과하다。  
_That rumor is nothing more than baseless gossip._

### Sentiment

> **“Words that flow and rumors that fly — talk that spreads quickly without truth.”**
