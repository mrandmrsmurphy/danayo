from 糟糠之妻不下堂

## 糟糠之妻

**Literal meaning:** “The wife of dregs and husks.”  
**Figurative meaning:** A faithful wife who shared poverty and hardship with her husband; a first wife who stood by him in difficult times.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** zāo kāng zhī qī (1–1–1–1)    
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** zou1 hong1 zi1 cai1
- **Korean:** 조강지처
- **Japanese:** そうこうのつま（糟糠の妻）_sōkō no tsuma_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** tao khang chi thê

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 糟糠之妻
- **Simplified:** 糟糠之妻
- Common phrase derived from the idiom: **糟糠之妻不下堂** (“One must not dismiss the wife who shared the days of chaff and husks”).

### Source / Origin
The expression comes from the **_Book of the Later Han_ (《後漢書》)**, in the biography of **Song Hong (宋弘)**.

According to the story, Emperor Guangwu of Han once wished to arrange for Song Hong to marry a princess after his rise to power. The emperor hinted that Song Hong could dismiss his original wife now that he had attained high status.

Song Hong replied with a famous statement:

**“貧賤之知不可忘，糟糠之妻不下堂。”**  
“Friends from humble days must not be forgotten; the wife who shared the chaff and husks must not be dismissed.”

Here **糟糠** refers to coarse food such as fermented grain residue and husks, symbolizing the **poverty and hardship** the couple endured together.

The phrase became a classic expression praising **loyalty to a spouse who shared one’s early struggles**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 糟糠之妻 often refers to a **first wife who endured hardship with her husband before his success**.
- The idiom carries strong moral weight in traditional culture, emphasizing **gratitude and fidelity**.
- In **Korean**, **조강지처** similarly refers to a wife who shared poverty with her husband and therefore deserves lasting respect.
- In **Japanese**, **糟糠の妻** appears mostly in literary or historical contexts.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他始終沒有忘記自己的糟糠之妻。  
_He never forgot the wife who shared hardship with him._

**Japanese:**  
成功しても糟糠の妻を忘れてはいけない。  
_Even after success, one must not forget the wife who shared the difficult years._

**Korean:**  
그는 조강지처를 끝까지 지켰다。  
_He remained faithful to the wife who had endured hardship with him._

### Sentiment
> **“The wife who shared the days of husks and dregs — loyalty to the one who stood beside you in poverty.”**
