[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: yún zhòu huǒ yè
- **Cantonese**: wan4 zau3 fo2 je6
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: うんちゅうかや
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 운주화야 (un-ju-hwa-ya)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: vân trú hỏa dạ

### Literal Meaning
- **雲** cloud
- **昼／晝** day
- **火** fire
- **夜** night

Literal: “Cloud by day, fire by night.”

### Figurative Meaning
A compact reference to the visible presence of God guiding Israel through the wilderness. It signifies divine guidance, protection, and covenant nearness across changing circumstances.

### Scriptural Source
This idiom condenses **Exodus 13:21–22**:

> “And the LORD went before them by day in a pillar of a cloud… and by night in a pillar of fire, to give them light…”

Also echoed in:
- **Exodus 14:19–20**
- **Numbers 9:15–23**
- **Nehemiah 9:12, 19**

Traditional Chinese translations commonly render:
> “日間在雲柱中引導他們，夜間在火柱中光照他們。”

The Dan'a'yo form strips the syntax to the dual sign: cloud–day, fire–night.

### Example Usage
> 荒野之中，神以雲昼火夜示其同在。  
> In the wilderness, God manifested his presence as cloud by day and fire by night.

### Cultural Notes
The paired imagery functions as a merism of time. Day and night together signal continuous presence. The theophany is both protective and directive.

Cloud in biblical theology often signals divine concealment and majesty; fire signals holiness and revelation. The two together balance mystery and illumination.

In later biblical reflection, this wilderness guidance becomes paradigmatic of providence. It also shapes liturgical memory in Israel’s festivals and prophetic poetry.

Within Dan'a'yo, 雲昼火夜 serves as shorthand for sustained divine guidance. It may be invoked metaphorically for steadfast presence amid uncertainty.

### Tone and Sentiment
Reassuring, majestic, covenantal. It conveys steadfast guidance through changing times.
