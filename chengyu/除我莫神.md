[[Biblical Chengyu]]
### Pronunciation
- **Mandarin**: chú wǒ mò shén
- **Cantonese**: ceoi4 ngo5 mok6 san4
- **Japanese (hiragana)**: じょがばくしん
- **Korean (Hangul)**: 제아막신 (je-a-mak-sin)
- **Vietnamese (Sino-Vietnamese)**: trừ ngã mạc thần

### Literal Meaning
- **除** except, apart from
- **我** I, me
- **莫** none, no one, nothing
- **神** god, deity

Literal: “Except me, none is God.”

### Figurative Meaning
A concise declaration of exclusive monotheism. It affirms that no other being shares divine status, authority, or essence. It excludes both rival worship and rival ontology.


### Scriptural Source
This maxim condenses several biblical affirmations:
- **Exodus 20:3** — “Thou shalt have no other gods before me.”
- **Deuteronomy 5:7** — parallel formulation.
- **Isaiah 45:5** — “I am the LORD, and there is no other; besides me there is no God.”    

Hebrew (Isaiah 45:5):  
_אֲנִי יְהוָה וְאֵין עוֹד; זוּלָתִי אֵין אֱלֹהִים_  
“I am YHWH, and there is no other; besides me there is no God.”

Traditional Chinese translations often render Isaiah 45:5 as:
> “除我以外，再沒有神。”

The Dan'a'yo form replaces 無 with 莫 to preserve classical solemnity and avoid modern lexical associations of “atheism,” while retaining existential exclusivity.

### Example Usage
> 先知之宣告曰：除我莫神，萬邦偶像皆虚。  
> The prophet proclaims: except me none is God; the idols of the nations are vain.

### Cultural Notes
In the ancient Near Eastern world, deities were commonly understood as territorial or functional. Israel’s confession was distinctive in denying not merely the legitimacy but the reality of rival gods.

The verb-like force of 莫 sharpens the proclamation. It is not merely that no others are to be worshiped; none qualifies as God. The maxim therefore bridges covenant loyalty and metaphysical uniqueness.

Historically, such declarations led surrounding cultures to accuse Jews and Christians of impiety or atheism toward the civic gods. Yet the biblical claim is not denial of deity but denial of competitors.

Within Dan'a'yo, 除我莫神 functions as a foundational confession. It pairs naturally with [[主宰万物]] and stands as a theological axis around which law, worship, and redemption turn.

### Tone and Sentiment
Absolute, prophetic, uncompromising. It carries the force of divine self-declaration and exclusive sovereignty.
