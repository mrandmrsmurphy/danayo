[Dan'a'yo Chengyu](chengyu/Dan'a'yo%20Chengyu.md)

>[!info] For this idiom in use, see [[文法 - 03文字法]].

## Literal Meaning
- [単](characters/単.md) — single, simple } 単語: a single-unit word
- [語](characters/語.md) — word, expression }
- [熟](characters/熟.md) — ripened, set, fixed } 熟語: a fixed compound expression
- [語](characters/語.md) — word, expression }

The chengyu is itself a paired compound: [[単語]] + [[熟語]]. 語 appears in both halves as the generic "word/language" element; what differentiates the two is the qualifying character — 単 (single, simple) vs. 熟 (ripened, fixed).

Literally: "Single words and compound expressions" — the two tiers of the Dan'a'yo lexicon.

## Extended Meaning
単語熟語 is a Dan'a'yo term of art naming the regulatory distinction between two classes of lexical item:

- **単語** — characters licensed to appear as free-standing one-character words (一字語). These are high-frequency, semantically stable morphemes that are phonologically distinct enough to stand alone without generating unacceptable ambiguity.
- **熟語** — characters restricted to appearing only within multi-character compounds (双字語 or longer). They may be semantically broad, phonologically colliding with other morphemes, or otherwise unsuitable as free forms.

The distinction is not merely descriptive but prescriptive: it governs whether a morpheme is *licensed* as an independent word. A character assigned to the 熟語 tier cannot surface alone — it must combine.

## Source and Origin
単語熟語 is a Dan'a'yo coinage, though both constituent terms are borrowed from established Sino-Japanese grammatical vocabulary: 単語 (たんご) is the standard Japanese word for "word" or "vocabulary item," and 熟語 (じゅくご) refers to a compound expression or set phrase — typically a multi-character Sino-Japanese word or idiom. Dan'a'yo inherits these terms and sharpens their meaning into a formal lexical distinction.

The need for such a distinction arises directly from Dan'a'yo's phonological architecture. Because Dan'a'yo systematically reduces the CJKV sound inventories to a smaller unified set of syllables, the number of homophones is high. If every character were a potential free-standing word, the resulting homophony would make the language nearly unusable in speech. The 単語/熟語 tier system resolves this by restricting which morphemes may surface as free forms — forcing disambiguation through compounding.

This parallels two historical precedents in the Sinosphere:
1. **Classical Chinese to Modern Mandarin:** Classical Chinese (文言) was largely monosyllabic; Modern Mandarin drifted toward disyllabic lexemes precisely to resolve the homophony that radical sound change had produced over the millennia.
2. **Bound vs. free morphemes:** The distinction encodes, in lexical form, the fundamental linguistic contrast between free morphemes (which can stand alone) and bound morphemes (which cannot).

The distinction is operationalized in each character file through the `boundedness` field (0–100): a character with low boundedness is a strong candidate for 単語 status; one with high boundedness is expected to appear only in 熟語 compounds.

## Cultural Notes
単語熟語 is unique among Dan'a'yo chengyu in being self-referential: it is a chengyu *about the distinction between単語 and 熟語*, and therefore about its own status as a lexical form. As a four-character compound expression, it is itself a 熟語 — a fixed set phrase — composed of two sub-words (単語 and 熟語), the second of which names the category it belongs to.

In Japanese linguistic education, 単語 and 熟語 are standard curriculum terms. 熟語 in particular encompasses two-character Sino-Japanese words (二字熟語) such as 読書 or 学習, and by extension the longer Sino-Japanese compounds that fill academic and technical vocabulary. Dan'a'yo's use of these terms imports their Japanese pedagogical familiarity and gives them a more precise, regulatory meaning.

The `boundedness` field makes the system machine-readable within the Obsidian vault: the database can identify which characters are candidates for 単語 status and which are expected to appear only as components of 熟語. This is not merely taxonomic — it is a generative constraint on the lexicon, determining which words Dan'a'yo can and cannot produce.

## Example Sentences
1. 「語」の文字は高い束縛性を持つ熟語専用字だ。単語として使うことはできない。
   "The character 語 has high boundedness — it is a 熟語-only morpheme and cannot be used as a standalone word."
2. Dan'a'yo's 単語熟語 system concentrates disambiguation into the compounding process, allowing a reduced phonological inventory without sacrificing lexical precision.
3. Every character file in the vault carries a `boundedness` score: the lower the score, the more likely the character is a 単語; the higher, the more it is confined to 熟語 compounds.

## Sentiment
Technical and taxonomic; belongs to the metalanguage of Dan'a'yo description rather than to ordinary discourse. Its register is formal and analytical — it is a term used *about* the language rather than *in* it. That it takes the form of a classical four-character compound is itself a statement: Dan'a'yo encodes its own grammar in the idiom of its own tradition.
