## 単語熟語

### Readings
- Mandarin: dān yǔ shú yǔ    
- Cantonese: daan¹ jyu⁵ suk⁶ jyu⁵
- Japanese (hiragana): たんご じゅくご
- Korean (Hangul): 단어 숙어 (dan-eo suk-eo)
- Vietnamese: đơn ngữ thục ngữ

### Literal structure
- 単語: single-unit word, a standalone lexical item
- 熟語: fixed compound, multi-character lexical unit

The binome pairs “atomic” vs “composite” vocabulary.

### Interpretation
A structural principle of the lexicon:

Certain characters are licensed as independent words (単語), while others are restricted to appearing only within compounds (熟語).

This is not merely descriptive but regulatory. It defines which morphemes may surface as free forms and which are bound.

### Functional meaning in Dan’a’yo
A constraint on lexical realization designed to control homophony:
- **単語 tier**  
    A curated subset of characters permitted to stand alone as one-character words (一字語). These are presumably high-frequency, semantically stable, and sufficiently distinct in sound.
- **熟語 tier**  
    Characters that are phonologically ambiguous or semantically too broad are disallowed as standalone forms and must combine into compounds (双字語 or longer), where disambiguation is achieved through composition.

### Underlying linguistic principle
This encodes a **free vs bound morpheme distinction**, but implemented orthographically and lexically rather than purely morphologically.

It also functions as a **phonological disambiguation strategy**:
- restrict high-collision syllables from appearing alone
- force semantic specification via compounding

Comparable in effect to:
- Classical Chinese preference for disyllabic words in later periods
- Modern Mandarin’s drift toward disyllabic lexemes to avoid homophony

### Concise definition
“A lexical regulation distinguishing standalone words and compound-only forms, used to control homophony by restricting which characters may appear independently.”

### Gloss
“単語熟語: the regulated division between free-standing characters and compound-bound characters in the lexicon.”
