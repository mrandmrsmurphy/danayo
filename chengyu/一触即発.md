[[Misc. Chengyu]]

**Literal meaning:** “At one touch, immediately erupt.”  
**Extended sense:** On the verge of explosion; in a highly volatile state where the slightest trigger will set off conflict or crisis.

**Source:** Traditionally attributed to **李開先** in 《原性堂記》. The expression belongs to late imperial prose rather than the early classical canon.

**Classical background:**  
触 denotes contact or stimulus; 即 marks immediacy; 発 indicates discharge or eruption. The sequence compresses causation into near simultaneity. There is no process described between trigger and outcome. The phrase therefore captures instability rather than violence itself.

In late Ming discourse, such language often described social tension, factional rivalry, or volatile temperament. The idiom’s structure makes it well suited to political commentary. It conveys that conditions are already primed, requiring only minimal provocation.

**Modern usage:**  
Across the region, 一触即発 is common in journalism, diplomatic reporting, and narrative prose to describe tense military standoffs, labor disputes, political crises, or interpersonal conflict. The tone is urgent but descriptive. It emphasizes imminence rather than inevitability.

**Usage note:**  
一触即発 does not describe explosion itself but the condition preceding it. Its force lies in the compression of cause and effect into a single instant.
