- [朝 (char)](characters/朝%20(char).md) "morning" + [三 (char)](characters/三%20(char).md) " three" + [暮](characters/暮.md) "evening" + [四 (char)](characters/四%20(char).md)

**Literal meaning:** “Three in the morning, four in the evening.”  
**Figurative meaning:** To change one’s words or policy capriciously; to play with numbers or wording while the substance remains the same; fickle or deceptive manipulation.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** zhāo sān mù sì (1–1–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** ziu1 saam1 mou6 sei3
- **Korean:** 조삼모사
- **Japanese:** ちょうさんぼし（朝三暮四）_chōsan boshi_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** triêu tam mộ tứ

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 朝三暮四
- **Simplified:** 朝三暮四
- A related expression is **朝四暮三**, reversing the numbers to emphasize the same idea.

### Source / Origin
The idiom comes from the Daoist text **_Zhuangzi_ (《莊子》)**, in the chapter **“Qi Wu Lun” (齊物論)**.

The story tells of a man who kept monkeys and fed them chestnuts. He announced that they would receive **three in the morning and four in the evening**, which made the monkeys angry. So he changed the wording and said **four in the morning and three in the evening**, and the monkeys were pleased.

In reality, the monkeys still received **seven chestnuts total**, but they reacted differently depending on how the distribution was presented.

Zhuangzi used the story to illustrate how people can be **misled by superficial distinctions** while failing to perceive the underlying unity of things.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese**, 朝三暮四 commonly refers to **fickleness, inconsistency, or manipulation through wording**.
- In **Japanese**, the idiom often describes someone who **changes plans or policies frequently** or behaves inconsistently.
- In **Korean**, **조삼모사** can refer either to **trickery through superficial changes** or to **foolish people who are easily deceived by appearances**.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他的政策總是朝三暮四，讓人難以信任。  
_His policies keep changing unpredictably, making him hard to trust._

**Japanese:**  
そんな朝三暮四のやり方では信頼を失う。  
_You will lose trust with such fickle tactics._

**Korean:**  
그의 계획은 조삼모사처럼 자주 바뀐다。  
_His plans change constantly like “three in the morning, four in the evening.”_

### Sentiment
> **“A change in wording that leaves the reality unchanged — a trick of appearances that fools the unwary.”**
