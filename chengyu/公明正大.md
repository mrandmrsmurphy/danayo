## [[公明]][[正大]]

**Literal meaning:** “Public, bright, upright, and great.”  
**Figurative meaning:** Fair and honorable; open and aboveboard; morally upright and impartial.

### Pronunciations
- **Mandarin:** gōng míng zhèng dà (1–2–4–4)
- **Cantonese (Jyutping):** gung1 ming4 zing3 daai6
- **Korean:** 공명정대
- **Japanese:** こうめいせいだい（公明正大）_kōmei seidai_
- **Vietnamese (Hán-Việt):** công minh chính đại

### Variants
- **Traditional:** 公明正大
- **Simplified:** 公明正大
- Closely related expressions include **光明正大** (“bright and upright”) and **公正無私** (“fair and without selfishness”).

### Source / Origin
The phrase is built from four classical moral terms:
- **[公 (char)](characters/公%20(char).md)** — public, impartial
- **[明 (char)](characters/明%20(char).md)** — clear, enlightened 
- **[正 (char)](characters/正%20(char).md)** — upright, correct
- **[大 (char)](characters/大%20(char).md)** — great or magnanimous 

Combinations of these terms appear frequently in **Confucian political and moral discourse**, describing the character of a just official or ruler. The four-character form came to represent the ideal of **transparent integrity in governance and personal conduct**.

The idiom became widespread in later imperial literature to praise people whose actions were **free from secrecy, corruption, or private motives**.

### Cultural Notes
- In **Chinese political rhetoric**, 公明正大 describes a leader who governs with **open fairness and moral clarity**.
- In **Japanese**, the phrase often refers to **fair conduct in competition, debate, or official decisions**.
- In **Korean**, 공명정대 is commonly used to praise **judges, administrators, or referees** who act with impartial integrity.

### Example Usage
**Mandarin:**  
他以公明正大的態度處理這件事情。  
_He handled the matter with fairness and integrity._

**Japanese:**  
彼は公明正大な人物として知られている。  
_He is known as a man of upright and honorable character._

**Korean:**  
그 판사는 공명정대한 판단으로 존경받는다.  
_That judge is respected for his fair and upright decisions._

### Sentiment
> **“Open, impartial, and upright — conduct that is transparent and morally beyond reproach.”**
