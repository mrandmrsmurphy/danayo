**Source and formation:**  
不可思議 is a Buddhist translation term, a calque of Sanskrit **अचिन्त्य (acintya)** and Pali **acinteyya**, meaning “inconceivable” or “imponderable.” The expression entered Sinitic vocabulary through early translations of Buddhist scriptures and became standard in medieval doctrinal and devotional literature.

It appears widely in canonical texts such as the **《妙法蓮華經》** and other Mahāyāna works, where it describes the nature of Buddhahood, cosmic scale, karmic complexity, or meditative realization.

**Classical background:**  
The compound is structurally straightforward:

- [[不可]] — not possible
- 思 — to think
- 議 — to deliberate or discuss

Together they negate the capacity of conceptual thought itself. Unlike idioms that describe something merely surprising or strange, 不可思議 signals a boundary of cognition. It marks realities that cannot be grasped through discursive reasoning.

As with many Buddhist translation compounds, the phrase fits seamlessly into four-character rhythm and was therefore easily assimilated into broader literary usage. Over time it detached from strictly doctrinal contexts.

**Modern usage:**  
Across the region, 不可思議 is widely used in both religious and secular contexts. In formal or philosophical writing it can retain its original sense of transcendence. In everyday speech it often means astonishing, mysterious, or difficult to explain. The register ranges from elevated to neutral depending on context.
