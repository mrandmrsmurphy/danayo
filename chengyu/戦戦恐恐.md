## Literal Meaning
- [戦](characters/戦.md) — to tremble; battle (appears twice)
- [恐](characters/恐.md) — fear, dread (appears twice)

Literally: "Trembling, trembling, fearful and fearful" — shaking with fear and anxiety.

## Extended Meaning
A state of extreme fear and trepidation; proceeding with the utmost caution under grave pressure or danger, in constant dread of making a fatal mistake. Often evokes the image that follows in the source text: standing at the edge of a deep abyss, treading on thin ice.

## Source and Origin

From the Classic of Poetry, 《詩經·小雅·小旻》("Lesser Elegantiae: Xiǎo Mǐn"):

> 戰戰兢兢，如臨深淵，如履薄冰。
> "Trembling, trembling, fearfully, fearfully — as if standing at the edge of a deep abyss, as if treading on thin ice."

The poem describes a minister's inner state when advising a foolish or unpredictable king — the ever-present fear of a misspoken word in a dangerous court.

## Standard Form
The classical Chinese form is **戰戰兢兢** (zhàn zhàn jīng jīng). Dan'a'yo substitutes:
- 戦 for 戰 (Japanese shinjitai simplification; aliased in [[戦]])
- 恐 for 兢 (semantic substitution; see [[恐恐]] "fearfully, with trepidation, alias: 兢兢")

## Cultural Notes
The two lines of 《小旻》 form an inseparable pair in Chinese literary memory: 戰戰兢兢 (the internal state of trembling fear) followed immediately by 如臨深淵，如履薄冰 ("as if on the brink of an abyss, as if on thin ice") — the physical image that explains why one trembles. The second line became an equally famous idiom in its own right, and together they constitute one of the oldest and most vivid expressions of existential anxiety in the Chinese tradition.

In Japanese, 戦々恐々 appears in literary and journalistic writing to describe nervous apprehension — often with a slightly deflating or comic tone that the classical original did not carry.

## Example Sentences
1. 戰戰兢兢，如臨深淵，如履薄冰。(《詩經·小雅·小旻》)
   "Trembling, trembling, fearfully, fearfully — as if at the edge of a deep abyss, as if treading on thin ice."
2. 上司之前，吾戦戦恐恐，深恐失言。
   "Before my superior, I trembled in fear, dreading a slip of the tongue."

## Sentiment
Anxious, cautious, dread-filled. Classical usage is serious — the posture of a wise person under genuine danger. Modern Japanese usage (戦々恐々) can be lighter, sometimes describing ordinary nervousness or comic dread.
