## Notes
**Form.** 核金 is [核 (char)](characters/核%20(char).md) “core; nucleus” + [金 (char)](characters/金%20(char).md) “metal,” following your rule for lanthanides.

**Etymological situation.** _Ytterbium_ is named after **Ytterby**, like yttrium, terbium, and erbium. The place-name offers no usable one-character reduction in your system, so a direct toponymic strategy is not viable.

**Motivation.** You instead select a **property-based descriptor**:

- ytterbium is notable in atomic physics for its relatively **simple, closed-shell (near “core-like”) electronic structure** among lanthanides
- it is used in **optical clocks and precision measurements**, where “core-like” behavior is advantageous
- 核 captures the idea of **centrality, nucleus, and core stability**

Thus the name encodes a **conceptual/structural property** rather than color (as with 桃金, 翠金, 褐金).

**Type of formation.**  
A **property-based designation** replacing an unusable toponym, consistent with your fallback strategy.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Meaning-based, no phonetic borrowing.
2. Uses 金 appropriately.
3. Provides a strong, high-frequency, unique symbol (核).
4. Expands your lanthanide naming beyond color into **structural/physical concepts**, adding diversity.

**Clarity and issues.**

- 核 is broad (“nucleus, core”) and not uniquely tied to ytterbium.
- The association is more **modern and abstract** than traditional color-based cues.
- However, within a fixed mapping, distinctiveness is enforced by assignment.

**Conclusion.**  
核金 is a coherent property-based solution. It departs from color naming but remains consistent with your fallback strategy, providing a clear and conceptually meaningful identifier for _ytterbium_.
