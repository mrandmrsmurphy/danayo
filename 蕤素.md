## Notes
### Form
蕤素 combines [[蕤]] with [素](characters/素.md) “element.” The suffix matches the rule for main-group elements.

### Motivation
The international name _thallium_ derives from Greek θαλλός (_thallos_) “young green shoot, sprout,” referring to the bright green spectral line observed at discovery (Crookes, 1861). 蕤 in Classical Chinese denotes luxuriant, drooping vegetation, especially the soft, pendant parts of plants. Semantically, it aligns well with the notion of fresh growth or verdant sprouting, even if not a direct botanical equivalent of “shoot.”

### Character choice
- **Semantic range:** 蕤 conveys softness, lushness, and vegetal vitality, which fits the _thallos_ imagery better than more generic 青 or 緑 would, since those are already heavily used for color.
- **Register:** It is attested in Classical Chinese and meets the frequency criterion, even if rare in modern usage.
- **Phonological uniqueness:** Your note that it has a single, stable reading (“nui”) is important for symbol use; it minimizes ambiguity when the character functions as a standalone element symbol.

**Fit within the system.**

1. Preserves the original naming logic (vegetal imagery tied to spectral color) rather than adopting a phonetic loan.
2. Avoids collision with existing color-based names (青, 紫, 茜), maintaining lexical distinctiveness across the table.
3. Provides a unique initial graph for symbol purposes.

**Comparative CJKV forms.**

- Modern Chinese: 铊 (phonetic)
- Japanese: タリウム (phonetic)
- Korean: 탈륨 (phonetic)
- Vietnamese: tali (phonetic)

As with several 19th-century elements, East Asian languages adopted phonetic transcriptions and largely lost the original semantic motivation; 蕤素 restores a meaning-based interpretation.

**Clarity and potential issues.**

- 蕤 is rare and may not be immediately recognizable to modern readers. This is offset by your controlled character set and by its role as a fixed symbol.
- Its semantic field is slightly broader than “shoot,” but still within the correct botanical domain.

**Conclusion.** 蕤素 is a defensible and internally consistent choice: it reconstructs the vegetal imagery behind _thallium_, satisfies your frequency and uniqueness constraints, and avoids conflicts within the existing naming system.
